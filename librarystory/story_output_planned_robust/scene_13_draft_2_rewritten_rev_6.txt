Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light. Rhys leaned against a crumbling pillar, eyes fixed on the star chart I traced. He didn’t bother with bluster. Just a stillness that felt… worn.

“You seek this knowledge,” he said, voice a low rumble, “but what are you willing to lose in the seeking?”

A prickle of unease traced my spine. He wasn’t trying to dissuade me, just… measuring my resolve. I let my gaze drift over his face – the deep lines etched around his eyes, the subtle clench of his jaw. He looked depleted, not driven by ambition, but haunted.

“I understand knowledge carries responsibility,” I said, keeping my voice steady. “But fear shouldn’t dictate what we seek.”

Rhys’s lips curved in a wry smile, but the shadows in his eyes didn’t lift. “Resourceful.” He paused, letting the silence stretch. “I’ve been tracking this symbol for years. I know where it leads. I can help you decipher it… for a price.”

My mind raced. This wasn’t a simple trade. It felt… precarious, like stepping onto thin ice. I trailed my fingers over the cool stone of the chart, a faint warmth radiating from the etched lines.

“What exchange?” I asked cautiously.

Rhys pushed off the pillar, straightening to his full height. It wasn't an aggressive move, but a predator’s stillness, coiled and ready. “Information. I have interests aligned with your research. Something was taken from me – stolen, actually. Something of considerable value.” He paused, his gaze locking with mine, assessing. “Your expertise. In return, access to knowledge beyond any library’s walls.”

I noticed the downturn of his lips when he spoke of the theft. Not grief, but simmering resentment, a quiet fury held in check. I consciously quieted my connection to Kaelen, relying on my instincts. The library’s whispers felt… distant, muffled.

I looked down at the symbol, my fingers tracing its intricate lines. "And what, specifically, would you have me do?"

Rhys’s lips curved into a slow smile. “You’re an Archivist. You have access to knowledge lost for generations. You can decipher the star charts, translate the ancient languages. You can see connections others miss.” He paused, his gaze unwavering. “You’re a conduit. And I need you to open the way.”

I met his gaze, a spark of defiance flickering within me. I wasn’t merely a tool. I was an Archivist, a seeker of truth. And I wouldn’t blindly follow him.

“What guarantees do I have that this isn’t a fool’s errand? That this ‘something’ even exists?”

Rhys’s smile faded, replaced by weary resignation. “None. Only my word. And the weight of centuries.” He paused, then added, his voice barely a whisper, “But I assure you. It’s real. And its return is vital. Not just for my people… but for everyone.”

The air in the alcove crackled with unspoken tension. I stared at the ancient star chart, the symbol now pulsing with unsettling energy. I was at a crossroads. And I knew, with chilling certainty, that my life would never be the same. I took a slow breath, meeting his gaze. “Very well. I’ll help you. But I work with transparency. Tell me everything. What was taken, from whom, and *why* you believe I can help.”

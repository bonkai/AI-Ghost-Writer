*   **Initial Atmosphere (Paragraphs 1-2):** Excellent build of atmosphere and mystery. The pacing is appropriately slow, establishing a sense of foreboding and anticipation.
*   **Lucian's Entrance (Paragraph 3):** The description of Lucian's arrival is effective, but the extended description ("not walking *into*… but *from*") feels slightly repetitive and slows the momentum. Consider streamlining this.
*   **Boy's Fear (Paragraphs 4-5):** The extended focus on the boy’s fear, while understandable, begins to drag. The silence is effective initially, but prolonging it without a contrasting element diminishes its impact. Consider interweaving brief descriptions of Lucian’s reactions or internal thoughts during this section.
*   **Question & Revelation (Paragraphs 6-7):** The pacing picks up again with Lucian's question and the reveal of the wildflowers. This is a strong moment.
*   **Inquisitor Interaction (Paragraph 8):** The dialogue feels a little functional here. While it advances the plot, it doesn't contribute much to the tension.
*   **Kaelen's Section (Paragraphs 9-16):** This is the biggest pacing issue. While the imagery and mystery are compelling, the section is *extremely* prolonged. The repeated descriptions of resonance, pulls, and fragmented images become monotonous. The constant layering of “felt… important… essential…” weakens the impact. This section *needs* to be significantly condensed. Consider showing, not telling, Kaelen’s connection. Instead of repeatedly stating things “felt” a certain way, demonstrate his reaction through action or a more concise internal monologue. The repeated phrasing (“A key to…”, “It *was* something…”) adds to the feeling of repetition.
*   **Tension Arc:** The scene begins with strong atmospheric tension, which is effectively maintained during the initial confrontation. However, the tension dissipates significantly during Kaelen’s extended internal exploration. The final reveal of “Florian” is a good hook, but it's somewhat weakened by the preceding lack of momentum.

Overall, the scene has strong potential, but suffers from uneven pacing, particularly in the latter half. Condensing Kaelen’s section and streamlining some of the descriptive passages would significantly improve the flow and maintain a more consistent level of tension.

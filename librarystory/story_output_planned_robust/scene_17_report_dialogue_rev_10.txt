*   **Repetitive Non-Verbal Cues:** The constant description of Seris and Rhys subtly adjusting positions, glancing at each other, and touching arms is becoming repetitive. While establishing their teamwork is important, it's happening *too* frequently within the dialogue itself. Consider trimming some of these descriptions and letting the dialogue *imply* their coordination.
*   **Valerius’s Motivation – Too On-the-Nose:** Valerius repeatedly states he wants to "ensure the safety of the realm." This is a common villain trope and feels a bit flat. Subtext would be more effective. Perhaps his satisfaction comes across in *how* he says it, or in a subtle action, rather than direct pronouncements.
*   **Cloaked Figure – Voice & Purpose:** The cloaked figure’s lines are intriguing, but the voice description (“rasped, dry as dust”) feels a little cliché. More importantly, the final line about perspective is *telling* us the test, rather than *showing* it. Consider how that realization could be subtly prompted through dialogue or action *before* the figure explicitly states it. Perhaps a specific question or observation that forces Seris to consider a different angle.
*   **Seris's Steadiness:** While Seris’s resolve is good, her voice remaining “steady, but edged with steel” throughout feels a little unrealistic given the circumstances. A slight tremor, a tightening of her vocal cords, or a momentary hesitation would add authenticity.
*   **"Safety" as a Theme – Overplayed:** The repeated emphasis on "safety" is becoming heavy-handed. While it’s a central theme, consider varying the language used to discuss it.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Rhys is largely silent. While a strong, silent type is fine, a single, well-placed line – a question, a warning, a brief observation – would solidify his character and show his thought process.

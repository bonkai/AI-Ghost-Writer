Seris pressed her palms against the cool stone, a tremor running through her. It wasn’t lost lore; it was a deliberate suppression, and time felt desperately short. She centered herself, bracing against the rising tension. Rhys paced the shadowed alcove, jaw tight, energy barely contained.

“Kaelen,” Seris focused on their mental link, “anything more on why the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? The content itself?”

Pages turned rapidly, dust motes dancing in the light. Finally, the pages stilled on a fragmented passage in archaic script.

*“The Gardener cultivated not merely flora, but memoria – the very fabric of recollection. They understood Eldoria’s history wasn’t singular, but a tapestry of possibilities. They preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…the echoes of worlds that might have been.”*

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. Rhys stopped pacing, his gaze sharpening. “Lost narratives…alternate histories?” He considered the implications, then met Seris’s eyes, a dawning realization in his expression. “So, they weren’t just preserving facts, they were preserving *potential* realities?”

*“The Order believed a single, unified history was necessary for stability,”* Kaelen projected, the weight of ages in his voice. *“They feared the chaos of conflicting truths.”*

A cold weight settled in Seris’s chest. She met Rhys’s gaze, a shared understanding passing between them. 

“Kaelen, what else?” Seris prompted, her voice tight.

*“The Gardener recorded…before. Before Eldoria.”* The voice was faint, strained. *“A time of connection.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. “Connection to what?”

*“Other realms…other beings…”*

Pages continued to turn, revealing a single, faded illustration – a swirling vortex of iridescent light, filled with impossible structures.

“Show me,” Seris breathed, her voice barely a whisper.

The illustration flared, and a rush of images flooded Seris’s mind. She stumbled, bracing herself against Rhys, her knuckles white as she gripped his arm. 

*“The Veil…it wasn’t a barrier erected to keep threats *out*…”* Kaelen’s voice fragmented, distant, laced with sorrow. *“It was built to contain something…to sever a connection.”*

The image sharpened – a breathtaking landscape of impossible geometry and beings of pure light. Then, darkness crashed over Seris, and she cried out, clutching her head. Rhys held her tighter, concern etched on his face. 

She shook her head, clearing the images. “I…I saw something. A world…before.” Realization dawned, and her eyes widened. "The Veil…it wasn't about keeping things *out*…it was about keeping us from something *else*.”

A chilling weight settled upon them. They weren’t uncovering a conspiracy; they were peeling back deliberately obscured history, a history that threatened everything they knew. Rhys tightened his grip on her arm, his gaze meeting hers. He didn’t need to ask what they should do next. A muscle jumped in his jaw. “They didn’t want us to remember.” He murmured, more to himself than to Seris, “And now I think I understand *why*.”

*   **Kaelen’s Voice:** Kaelen’s dialogue is *entirely* exposition delivered as fragmented projections. While the *information* is crucial, it feels unnatural. Consider weaving some of this into Seris or Rhys’s internal thoughts *after* Kaelen provides a key phrase or image. For example, instead of Kaelen stating “The Veil…it wasn’t a barrier erected to keep threats *out*…”, he could project an image of the Veil *shifting* or *constricting*, and Seris then vocalizes the realization: “The Veil…it wasn’t about keeping things *out*…” This makes the discovery feel earned.
*   **Repetitive Realizations:** Both Seris and Rhys state the same realization about the Veil ("it wasn't about keeping things *out*…"). While a shared moment of understanding is good, streamline it. Let *one* of them articulate it fully, and the other react with confirmation or a furthering of the thought.
*   **Rhys’s Murmur:** Rhys’s final line ("They didn’t want us to remember. And now I think I understand *why*.") feels a little too on-the-nose. It’s a telling statement rather than a revealing one. Consider *showing* his understanding through a physical reaction (a tightening of his grip, a change in his posture) or a more subtle verbal cue.
*   **Limited Character Voice:** While functional, the dialogue is fairly neutral. There’s little to distinguish Seris’s voice from Rhys’s, or either from Kaelen’s (aside from Kaelen’s fragmented delivery). Inject more personality – a characteristic phrase, a particular way of phrasing questions, a hint of their emotional state – to make each character feel more distinct.
*   **Efficiency:** The initial exchange about “lost narratives” and “potential realities” feels a little slow. The connection is quickly made. Perhaps condense this into a single line from Rhys acknowledging the implication.

*   **Rhys's opening line ("You seek this knowledge…")** feels a bit cliché for this type of character. It’s not *bad*, but leans into a familiar trope. Consider a more subtle approach to establishing the cost of knowledge – perhaps an observation about the sacrifices *others* have made pursuing similar paths.
*   **Your response ("Knowledge carries responsibility…")** feels a little *too* polished and philosophical, especially given the immediate pressure of the situation. It reads as a speech rather than a natural reaction. A shorter, more instinctive response might be more effective. Something like, “I’m aware of the risks.”
*   **Repetitive Characterization:** You consistently *tell* us Rhys is depleted, haunted, simmering with resentment, etc. The dialogue *shows* some of this, but it’s heavily supplemented by narration. Try to let his dialogue *carry* more of that weight. For example, instead of "Not grief, but simmering resentment," let a subtle bitterness *color* his words.
*   **"You're an Archivist..." (Rhys's lines)** – This feels like exposition *delivered as dialogue*. Rhys is stating the obvious. It’s a way to get information to the reader, but doesn’t feel organic. Find a way to *show* his understanding of your skills through his requests or observations.
*   **"What guarantees do I have..."** – A good question, but the timing feels slightly off. It comes across as a little defensive, when a more curious or analytical approach might be more in character for an Archivist.
*   **Rhys's final lines ("But I assure you…")** are strong, but the shift to a whisper feels a little abrupt. Build to that level of vulnerability more gradually.
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is *competent*, but leans heavily on narration to establish character and mood. The conversations are a bit formal, and could benefit from more subtext, quicker pacing, and a greater reliance on *showing* rather than *telling*. The characters state their motivations and observations rather than letting them emerge organically.

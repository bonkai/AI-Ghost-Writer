*   **Repetition:** The line "The old scholars believed they could harness a power beyond their understanding. They were wrong. And now, someone is trying to unlock it again – believing they can succeed where others failed" is repeated *verbatim*. This is a major issue. Even if Kaelen is reiterating the stakes, find a different way to phrase it the second time. Perhaps focus on *who* is trying to unlock it, or the *consequences* of failure.
*   **Kaelen's Elusiveness:** Kaelen *constantly* withholds information and speaks in vague pronouncements. While mystery is good, it's becoming frustrating. Seris asks direct questions ("What’s so important about those memories…or whatever it is I felt?") and receives answers that are either circular or incomplete. This isn’t building tension; it’s starting to feel like avoidance. Give Seris *something* concrete, even if it’s a partial truth.
*   **Seris' Reactions:** Seris feels a "resonance" and then immediately vocalizes a complex understanding ("It’s a key. The observatory wasn’t abandoned…it was sealed"). This feels rushed. Show her *working through* the connection, not just arriving at the conclusion instantly. A few lines of internal monologue or fragmented questioning would make this more believable.
*   **"Guided" Feeling:** The line about feeling "guided" is interesting, but it feels tacked on. It needs to be *earned*. Perhaps foreshadow this feeling earlier in the scene with subtle cues – a sense of being watched, a strange coincidence, a feeling of inevitability.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The pity in the man’s eyes is a great detail, but it’s only revealed in the final paragraph. Consider hinting at this earlier – a lingering gaze, a subtle expression – to build anticipation and make the reveal more impactful.
*   **Subtext Potential:** Kaelen’s constant control and withholding could be read as distrust of Seris. Explore this dynamic. Is he protecting her, or does he suspect she's a liability?

*   **Subtext & Indirect Characterization:** The silent communication between Rhys and Seris is good, but could be *slightly* more nuanced. The “What do we do?” is a little on-the-nose even as a thought. Consider replacing it with a specific, fleeting expression Rhys makes that Seris interprets – a tightening of his jaw, a quick glance at a specific tool, etc. This shows their established dynamic more effectively.

*   **Authenticity & Voice (Seris):** Seris’s responses are generally strong, but the line “We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves” feels a little *too* direct after her attempts at deflection. A more believable response might be a measured pause, then a carefully worded denial – “We haven’t charted that particular area, no.” The current line feels like she’s immediately shutting down a line of questioning instead of attempting to control the conversation.

*   **Efficiency & Purpose:** The merchant's repeated questioning about "unusual formations" and "out of place" things is starting to become repetitive. While building tension is good, consider condensing some of this. Perhaps one pointed question, followed by a shift to negotiating terms, would be more effective.

*   **Clarity vs. Ambiguity:** The dialogue is generally clear, but the merchant's motivations remain *too* vague. We understand he's looking for something hidden, but a *hint* of what he intends to *do* with the information (even if it's just a veiled threat) would raise the stakes.

*   **Flow & Rhythm:** The pacing is good overall, but the back-and-forth between the merchant and Seris feels a little formal. Injecting a slightly more conversational tone (even within the tension) could make it feel more natural.

Perhaps something about the inherent risks of discovery.

*   **Rhys is *very* reactive and non-verbal.** While effective, it's bordering on repetitive. The axe sharpening is a strong image, but after a while it becomes the *only* thing he does. Consider *one* line of dialogue – even a curt, dismissive response to Marius – to add another layer to his character and prevent him from feeling like a silent prop.

*   **Marius’s lines are strong, but lean heavily on ominous pronouncements.** While fitting for his character, consider *briefly* showing a more calculating side. A line that acknowledges their expertise *before* threatening them could make him feel more dangerous – a predator who understands his prey.

*   **The phrase “Everyone has secrets” is a cliché.** While true, it lacks impact. Consider a more personalized threat, referencing something specific about their work or the Order.

*   **Seris's repeated assertions of having "no secrets" feel a little forced.** While it's meant to project confidence, it's becoming repetitive and predictable. Consider a single, more nuanced response that acknowledges the *possibility* of secrets but implies she has nothing to hide *from him*.

*   **Kaelen’s internal voice is effective, but slightly overused.** While helpful for conveying tension, it occasionally feels like narration disguised as thought. Try to show Rhys's fear through his actions and body language more consistently.

*   **The final exchange between Seris and Marius feels a bit abrupt.** Consider adding one more line from Marius – a final, chilling assessment – to emphasize the power dynamic and leave the reader with a stronger sense of dread.

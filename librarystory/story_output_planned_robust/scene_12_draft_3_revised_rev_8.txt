The oil lamp died with a final sputter, leaving Seris enveloped in a darkness that felt less like absence of light and more like a weight. A draft snaked through the archive, colder than the stone walls, carrying the scent of dust and something else—a faint, metallic sweetness, like old blood and withered lilies. Her hand drifted instinctively to the hilt of her dagger, fingers tightening on the worn leather, not to draw it, but to ground herself.

Valerius didn’t *enter* the darkness. He simply *became* visible within it, coalescing from the shadows. Two Guardians materialized beside him, their armor swallowing the scant light rather than reflecting it. Beneath the dark fabric of his robes, a faint, pulsing glow mirrored the ethereal light in the tome’s illustrations. He paused before the open book, his gaze fixed on the depiction of the Aethel. He didn’t ask a question. Instead, he traced a finger across the illustration, a slow, deliberate gesture that felt less like inquiry and more like… possession.

Seris’s eyes scanned the image, then flicked back to Valerius, a subtle tightening of her jaw. The Aethel—gods of a forgotten age, dismissed as myth. “The histories claim they were lost to time.”

Valerius tilted his head, a slow, deliberate movement. “Lost? Or… *rearranged*?” He paused, his gaze unwavering, and a muscle jumped in Seris’s cheek. She tasted something acrid on her tongue, a phantom echo of the metallic scent. “Consider a tapestry. Threads can be cut, patterns altered.”

A prickling sensation rose on the back of her neck. She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius, her grip tightening on the dagger. The metallic scent intensified, and she noticed the polished steel of the Guardians’ armor didn’t reflect the darkness, but *drank* it, as if consuming the very light around them. 

“What do you mean, ‘rearranged’?” Her voice was sharper than she intended, laced with a defensiveness she hadn’t realized she possessed.

Valerius’s lips curved in a slow, predatory smile. He didn’t answer. Instead, he gestured to a Guardian. “The Initiate requires rest. Escort her back to her chambers.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. Seris didn’t bother feigning compliance. Resistance would be futile, and she knew, with chilling clarity, that she *had* to return to this archive—alone. The Guardian’s grip tightened on her arm, but she didn't pull away. Not yet. She understood, in that moment, that the Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound. Valerius held the threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer.

She allowed herself to be led away, a single thought echoing in her mind: *This archive isn't protecting the past. It's a prison,* and she was beginning to suspect she was a prisoner within it.

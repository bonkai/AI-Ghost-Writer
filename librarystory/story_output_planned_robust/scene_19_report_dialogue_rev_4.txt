*   **Over-Reliance on Internal Thought/Telepathy:** The vast majority of "dialogue" is actually telepathic communication or internal thought presented as dialogue. While this *can* be effective, it’s happening constantly and feels repetitive. It flattens the scene and removes opportunities for character interaction and vocal delivery. Consider how much of this could be conveyed through action, description, or *actual* spoken words.
*   **Lack of Vocal Dialogue:** Seris speaks very little. Her lines are almost entirely questions prompted by the telepathic "voice." This makes her feel passive. Even if the primary communication is telepathic, give her more agency through spoken lines – even short ones expressing her feelings or deductions.
*   **"Always" is Weak:** The final response, "*Always.*" is incredibly weak and doesn’t serve a purpose. It feels like filler. Remove it. The shared understanding should be clear without this unnecessary affirmation.
*   **Repetitive Questioning:** Seris repeatedly asks “What do you mean?” and “Lost how?” This feels like stalling and doesn't advance the information in a compelling way. Try rephrasing these questions to be more insightful or specific.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Valerius):** Valerius’s frustration is *told* to us ("Valerius’s frustration was palpable…"), but not *shown* through dialogue. Even if he doesn’t speak directly to Seris, his actions (slamming books) are a start. Consider *why* he’s slamming books. Is it directed at her, at the knowledge she's uncovering, or at something else entirely?
*   **Indirect Characterization (Seris):** While Seris is receiving information, her *reaction* to it is largely internal. Showing her fear, curiosity, or determination through spoken dialogue or physical reactions would make her a more compelling character.
*   **Metallic Scent/Click – Disconnect:** The sudden shift to sensory detail (metallic scent, click) is jarring after so much internal communication. While effective, it feels abrupt. Perhaps a small spoken reaction from Seris to the change in atmosphere would bridge the gap.

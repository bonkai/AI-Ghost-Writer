*   **Rhys's Anxiety - Overplayed?** While establishing Rhys’s fear is important, his anxiety feels *very* present in nearly every line. The hand-running-through-hair gesture is repeated (implied by “in days” earlier) and his desperation is stated directly ("desperation edging his voice"). Consider showing more through subtle body language and letting Seris carry more of the conversational weight. Less telling, more showing.
*   **Seris's "Insurance" line:** While clever, the "insurance" metaphor feels a little modern/anachronistic for the setting (unless that's intentional). It’s not *bad*, but it slightly breaks immersion. Perhaps rephrase to something more fitting the world's vernacular – “a guarantee of discretion,” or “a price commensurate with the risk.”
*   **Repetitive Tension Building:** The dialogue relies *heavily* on threat and veiled menace. While effective initially, it becomes somewhat predictable. Vary the tactics. Perhaps a moment of seeming reasonableness from the merchant, followed by a sharp turn, would be more unsettling.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – Merchant's Gaze:** The stage direction "His eyes drifted to Seris, lingering on the flex of muscle beneath her tunic" feels…underused. The dialogue doesn’t *react* to this. Even a slight acknowledgement from Seris (a tightening of her jaw, a subtle shift in posture) would add layers.
*   **"Other Paths" – Cliché?** The merchant’s line about “other paths” feels a bit stock. While it conveys the threat, it lacks originality. Consider a more specific and unsettling implication of what those “other paths” might be.
*   **Indirect Characterization – Dynamic:** The dynamic between Seris and Rhys is coming across clearly, but could be *sharpened*. Perhaps a moment where Seris subtly corrects Rhys (a slight hand gesture silencing him) or takes the lead in the conversation, solidifying her position as the dominant figure.
*   **Pacing - Slow Burn:** The scene is a slow burn, which is fine, but be mindful of keeping it engaging. A few more moments of unexpectedness or a shift in the power dynamic would prevent it from feeling stagnant.

*   **Seris’s initial lines feel a bit expository.** While establishing the sequence is necessary, “The sequence is almost complete…” and reciting the rhyme feels like she’s *telling* us she’s close to a breakthrough, rather than *showing* us through her actions or internal reactions. Consider trimming the rhyme and letting her discovery of the texture change *be* the moment of realization.
*   **Rhys is very reactive.** He mostly *responds* to Seris and Kaelen, rather than initiating or driving the conversation. While his stoicism is likely intentional, it makes him feel a bit passive. Consider giving him a line or two that demonstrates his own deductions or concerns, even if subtle.
*   **The dialogue relies heavily on Kaelen’s internal thoughts as plot advancement.** While the connection is established, using Kaelen’s thoughts *as* the information source, then having Seris simply relay it, feels a little convenient. Could some of this information be gleaned from Seris’s own observations, even if initially incomplete?
*   **“A fitting name for a mountain” is a weak line for Rhys.** It doesn’t add much to the scene and feels like filler. Given his established character, a more pragmatic or observant comment would be more fitting.
*   **Seris’s line about Valerius feels a bit rushed.** The connection between his questions and the discovery feels a little thin. Perhaps a brief internal thought *before* she says it, showing her piecing things together, would strengthen the impact.
*   **Rhys’s “Perhaps just the wind” is good—a characteristic understatement.** It's one of the stronger lines in the scene.
*   **Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks punch.** It conveys information and establishes mood, but it doesn’t quite sparkle with subtext or reveal deeper character nuances. There’s room to make it more dynamic and engaging.

*   **Kaelen's Voice:** While the telepathic communication is efficient, it feels *very* functional. It lacks nuance. Consider adding emotional coloring to Kaelen’s thoughts – is there frustration, fear, protectiveness? Even a single word modifier ("*Quickly, Initiate!*") would add depth. Right now, it reads like an instruction manual.

*   **Seris's Casual Tone:** Seris’s “Just stellar cartography” feels a bit weak, especially given Hemlock’s intensity. It's a standard deflection, and doesn't quite land as convincingly as it could. Perhaps a slightly bolder lie, or a question back at Hemlock, would be more interesting. Something that shows a flicker of defiance, even if she's terrified.

*   **Hemlock's Repetition:** Hemlock’s lines about secrets and the past are a little repetitive, even with the slight variations. While reinforcing his theme, it feels a bit on-the-nose. Consider consolidating those ideas into fewer, more impactful statements.

*   **"I don't understand"**: Seris whispering “I don’t understand” feels…flat. It's a very direct statement of confusion when she's clearly *experiencing* fear and suspicion. Show, don't tell. Perhaps a physical reaction (a swallowed breath, a tightening of her grip on the book) would convey her distress more effectively.

*   **Subtext Opportunity – Hemlock’s Smile:** You’ve established Hemlock’s chilling politeness, but the descriptions of his smile (“chillingly polite,” “didn’t reach his eyes,” “widened”) are doing a lot of the work. Let the *dialogue* itself convey that unsettling quality. A seemingly innocuous question delivered with a certain tone could be far more effective.

*   **Kaelen's Urgency – Timing:** Kaelen's panicked rush feels a little late. While it works, it might be more effective if the urgency started building *during* the exchange with Hemlock, rather than immediately after. Foreshadowing his mounting concern would increase the tension.



Overall, the dialogue is *good*. It’s functional and moves the plot forward. However, it leans towards telling rather than showing in a few key places, and could benefit from more nuanced emotional coloring and a greater reliance on subtext.

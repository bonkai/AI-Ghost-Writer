## Consistency Check: Passed - with Notes & Potential Areas for Refinement

Overall, this scene is well-written and internally consistent. The magic system (listening to the Echo, fractured tapestry metaphor) is established and used effectively. The rising tension and sense of urgency are palpable. However, there are a few minor points to consider for even greater consistency and impact.

**Here's a breakdown, categorized for clarity:**

**I. Internal Consistency - Passed:**

* **Magic System:** The descriptions of the Echo, the Order’s methods, and the Church’s opposition remain consistent throughout. The metaphors (tapestry, song, cartography) are interwoven well.
* **Character Reactions:** Kaelen’s skepticism and Seris’s growing urgency feel appropriate to their established personalities.
* **Setting:** The library/alcove setting remains consistent, and the details (candle, wind, stone) contribute to the atmosphere.
* **Plot Progression:** The scene effectively builds upon previous knowledge and introduces new mysteries (the key, the song, the crack in the wall) without feeling rushed.

**II. Minor Inconsistencies/Points for Refinement (Mostly Nuance):**

* **Level of Direct Communication from the Echo:** Initially, the Echo communicates through feelings and impressions ("a sense of urgency"). Later, it directly forms words ("Remember," "They lied"). This shift isn't *inconsistent*, but it's a significant escalation. Consider if a gradual increase in directness would be more impactful.  Perhaps the initial communication is more fragmented, assembling into words over time.
* **Specificity of the Spectral Image:** The image of the hooded figure against the burning city is evocative, but a little vague.  While mystery is good, consider if a *single* defining detail (a symbol on the hood, a specific architectural feature of the cathedral) could add more weight and foreshadowing.  This isn't an inconsistency, but a potential opportunity for richer detail.
* **Stone's Reaction:** The stone feeling "subtly wrong" then "distorted" then pulsing is a bit of a jump. Perhaps a smoother progression of sensation would be more believable.  For example: "subtly wrong" -> "cold spot" -> "a faint tremor" -> "pulsing."
* **"The key is not to find the path, but to remember the song."** This is a beautiful line, but it feels slightly disconnected from the earlier metaphor of the cartographer charting existing lands. Consider if a subtle link could be established. Perhaps the song *is* the map – a sonic representation of the existing, forgotten knowledge.

**III. Potential Plot Hole/Question (Requires Clarification):**

* **Why *Seris*?** The Echo states "Why me?" but doesn't provide a clear answer. While leaving some mystery is fine, a *hint* of why Seris is uniquely suited to hear the Echo would strengthen the scene. Is it a lineage, a skill, a past experience?  Without any indication, it feels a little arbitrary.



**Overall:** The scene is very strong. The minor points above are primarily suggestions for polishing and enhancing an already compelling narrative. The "Consistency Check: Passed" designation is accurate.  Addressing the "Why Seris?" question would be the most impactful refinement.

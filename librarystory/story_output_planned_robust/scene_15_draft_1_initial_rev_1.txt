## Scene 15 - Draft 1

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the high window of the cartography annex. Seris traced the faded ink lines of a map depicting Verenhal and the surrounding Firepeak Mountains, a relic salvaged from a forgotten archive. Rhys, meticulously cleaning a set of brass calipers, hummed a tuneless melody. They’d successfully redirected Valerius, steering him toward texts on herbal remedies, but the old inquisitor’s lingering gaze still prickled at Seris’s skin.

“This map… it’s older than any I’ve seen,” Seris murmured, her fingers brushing the brittle parchment. “The city’s layout is… different. There’s a district marked here, ‘Aethelgard,’ that doesn’t appear on any modern charts.”

Rhys glanced up, setting down the calipers. “Aethelgard? I’ve encountered the name in fragmented historical accounts. Supposedly a district devoted to artisans and scholars, destroyed during the Obsidian Uprising centuries ago. A complete loss.”

“But look,” Seris pointed to a series of minute symbols woven into the depiction of the Firepeak Mountains, almost invisible to the naked eye. “These aren’t topographical markers. They’re… coded. And they align with specific peaks.”

*They're not just aligned, they’re a key,* Kaelen’s thought resonated in Seris’s mind, a gentle pressure behind her eyes. *A cipher. The peaks represent letters, or perhaps… runes.*

“Runes?” Rhys frowned. “Ancient Eldorian runes? Those haven’t been used in centuries. What would a map from the Obsidian Uprising era have to do with them?”

Seris ignored his question, her attention focused on the map. She pulled a worn leather-bound book from her satchel – a compendium of Eldorian runes she’d painstakingly copied from various sources. Slowly, carefully, she began to match the symbols on the map with the corresponding runes in the book.

“This one… ‘Isen.’ And this… ‘Morwen.’ It’s forming a sequence,” she muttered, her brow furrowed in concentration. 

Rhys leaned closer, watching her work. “What does it say?”

Seris continued deciphering, her fingers tracing the runes. A growing sense of excitement, mixed with trepidation, filled her. “It’s… fragmented. ‘Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’”

*The Stone Heart… that’s not a metaphor,* Kaelen’s thought was sharper now, tinged with urgency. *It's a place. A location. And it’s hidden beneath one of those peaks.*

“The Serpent’s Tooth…” Rhys traced the Firepeak Mountains on the map. “That must be Mount Cinderfang. It’s the most jagged peak, looks like a serpent’s fang.”

Seris’s gaze followed his finger. “But what’s ‘the Stone Heart’?”

Suddenly, a faint discoloration on the map caught her eye. It was almost imperceptible, a subtle difference in the parchment’s texture near the base of Mount Cinderfang. She ran her fingers over it, feeling a slight indentation. 

“There’s something hidden *on* the map,” she breathed.

Rhys grabbed a small magnifying glass from his toolkit. “It’s a layering,” he said, examining the map closely. “Someone has pasted a smaller piece of parchment onto the original. It’s almost perfectly camouflaged.”

Carefully, using a delicate bone tool, Rhys began to peel back the overlay. Beneath it, revealed in faded ink, was a detailed sketch of a subterranean structure – a circular chamber carved into the rock, with a single, massive stone pillar at its center. 

“By the Ancestors…” Rhys whispered, his voice filled with awe. “It’s a vault. A hidden chamber. And that pillar… it *is* a heart. A stone heart.”

*The Stone Heart isn't just a place, it’s a key to something far older, far more powerful,* Kaelen’s thought was a resonant echo in Seris’s mind. *Something the Obsidian Uprising tried to bury. And Valerius… he knows about it.*

Seris’s gaze swept across the revealed sketch, a cold dread settling in her stomach. The map wasn’t just a record of a lost district. It was a treasure map. And they had just stumbled upon a secret that someone, centuries ago, had desperately tried to keep hidden.

“We need to go to Mount Cinderfang,” she said, her voice barely a whisper. “Before someone else finds this map.”

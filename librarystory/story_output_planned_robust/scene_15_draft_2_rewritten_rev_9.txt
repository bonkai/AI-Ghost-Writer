## Scene 15 - Draft 4

The cartography room smelled of dry paper and dust motes dancing in a single shaft of sunlight. Seris traced a finger across the brittle vellum of the ancient map, a tremor starting in her hand that mirrored the jagged peaks depicted on the parchment. 

“‘Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’” she murmured, her brow furrowed. She ran a finger across the map, detecting a subtle texture change near Mount Cinderfang’s base. A thinning. She focused, amplifying the sensation, and realized the discoloration wasn't merely a change in color, but a slight depression in the parchment, as if something beneath pressed upwards.

Rhys, perched on a stool, methodically cleaned bone tools, the scrape of stone a steady counterpoint to Seris’s quickening pulse. He paused, observing her. “Something?”

Seris didn’t meet his gaze. “I think… there’s more to this map than we initially thought.” She traced the discoloration again. “A hidden layer, almost. Like a pressure from below.”

Rhys set down the tools and rose, approaching the map. He traced the peak with his finger. “Mount Cinderfang. A fitting name for a place that holds… something.” He glanced at Seris, concern etched on his face. “What are you feeling?”

“Dangerous,” she said, her voice low. “Like disturbing something that should have remained buried.” A sickly green light flickered behind her eyes – a fleeting, unwanted image. She swallowed, gripping the map, fighting to anchor herself. 

Rhys’s gaze sharpened. “Seris?”

She shook her head, forcing herself to focus on the parchment. She traced the discoloration again. A prison. A place of binding. Valerius… his questions about the region’s history, the old gods, the rituals performed *within* the mountains… it all clicked into place. She swept the room with a quick glance, a prickling sensation on her skin.

“Valerius is looking for something here,” she said, her voice barely audible. “Something old. Something… bound.” She paused, a cold dread settling in her stomach. “I feel… watched.”

Rhys reached for a heavy iron poker, his movements deliberate. “We’ll be ready.” He didn’t offer platitudes. 

Seris nodded, her heart hammering. They had unearthed something ancient and terrible. The map, once a puzzle, now felt like a warning. A faint, metallic tang filled the air, prickling at the back of her throat. Whatever lay beneath Mount Cinderfang, it was straining against its bonds, and she feared the moment it broke free.

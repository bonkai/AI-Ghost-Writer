The cool stone pressed against Seris’s palm, a tremor running through her. It wasn’t lost lore; it was a deliberate suppression, and the weight of it settled heavy in her chest. Rhys paced the shadowed alcove, energy contained but palpable, his boots silent on the stone floor. The rhythm was unsettling.

“Kaelen,” Seris focused on their mental link, keeping her voice even. “Anything more on why the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? Be specific about what they recorded.”

Dust motes danced in the light as pages turned, then stilled. A longer pause than usual.

*“The Gardener cultivated memoria—the fabric of recollection itself.”* Kaelen’s voice was strained, fragmented, as if the effort were physical. *“They understood Eldoria’s history wasn’t singular, but a tapestry. They preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…”*

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. Rhys stopped pacing, his gaze sharpening, hands flexing. He ran a hand through his hair, considering. “Lost narratives…alternate histories?” He paused. “So they weren’t just preserving facts, they were preserving…potential realities?”

*“The Order believed a unified history was necessary for stability.”* The voice held the weight of ages. *“They feared chaos, conflicting truths. They sought to…simplify.”* The word felt brittle, laced with disapproval.

A cold weight settled in Seris’s chest. She met Rhys’s gaze, a shared understanding passing between them. This wasn’t simply about control of information; it was about controlling perception.

“Kaelen, what else did the Gardener record?” Seris prompted, her voice tight. 

*“Before. Before Eldoria.”* The voice was faint, distant. *“A time of connection.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. “Connection to what?”

*“Other realms…other beings…”*

Pages continued to turn, revealing a single, faded illustration – a swirling vortex of iridescent light, filled with impossible structures. It wasn’t a depiction of a place; it felt like a *memory* of one.

“Show me,” Seris breathed, her voice barely a whisper.

The illustration flared, and a rush of images flooded Seris’s mind. She stumbled, bracing herself against Rhys, her knuckles white as she gripped his arm. The images weren’t a clear vision, but fractured glimpses—impossible landscapes, beings of pure light, and a profound sense of loss. It felt less like *seeing* and more like *remembering* something she’d never known.

*“The Veil…it wasn’t a barrier erected to keep threats out…”* Kaelen’s voice fragmented, distant, laced with sorrow. *“It was built to contain something…to sever a connection.”*

The image sharpened, resolving into a breathtaking landscape of impossible geometry and beings of pure light. Then, a wave of disorientation crashed over Seris, and she cried out, clutching her head. Rhys held her tighter, concern etched on his face.

She shook her head, clearing the images, her vision swimming. “I…I saw something. A world…before.” Realization dawned, and her eyes widened. "The Veil…it wasn't about keeping things *out*…it was about keeping *us* from something else.” She leaned heavily on Rhys, her legs suddenly weak. The air around them felt…thin, as if the very stones were vibrating with forgotten energy.

A chilling weight settled upon them. They weren’t uncovering a conspiracy; they were peeling back deliberately obscured history, a history that threatened everything they knew. Rhys tightened his grip on her arm, his gaze meeting hers, his expression grim. He didn’t need to ask what they should do next. A muscle jumped in his jaw. He murmured, more to himself than to Seris, “They didn’t want us to remember. And now I think I understand *why*.” He glanced at the illustration, his eyes narrowed. “This changes everything.” He paused, then added, his voice barely a whisper, “And I don’t think we’re the only ones who are starting to remember.”

*   **Seris's initial line ("We’re not alone")** feels a little *too* obvious. Given her training as an initiate, a more subtle observation – a tightening of her grip on her dagger, a quick scan of the room – followed by internal monologue, would be stronger. The line *tells* us she's aware, rather than *showing* it.
*   **Valerius’s dialogue is excellent overall**, particularly his use of carefully chosen words ("guided" instead of "saved"). However, his initial response feels *slightly* formal, even for someone of his position. Perhaps a touch more…curiosity? A hint of genuine interest in her “historical curiosity” before shifting to the reprimand would make his transition feel less abrupt.
*   **"What’s troubling about it?"** – Seris’s challenge feels a bit direct, considering the power dynamic and the unsettling atmosphere. A more cautious phrasing, perhaps a question framed as seeking clarification, would be more believable.
*   **Valerius’s response about the Aethel** is strong, but the phrase "less enlightened age" feels a little cliché. Consider a more unique descriptor.
*   **The repeated emphasis on Valerius *not* answering directly** is effective, but bordering on repetitive. While his evasiveness is key, vary the *way* he avoids answering. A leading question back to Seris, a philosophical musing, etc.
*   **"I think, I’ve read enough."** – This line feels a bit weak for a turning point. It lacks punch. Consider something more defiant, or even a panicked attempt to leave.
*   **The Guardian’s silence is effective**, but it’s *entirely* silent. A subtle sound – the scrape of metal on stone, a faint hum – could add another layer of unease.
*   **The final paragraph’s internal monologue is strong**, but it relies heavily on telling (“she understood”). Show her realization through a specific detail – a flicker of memory, a physical reaction – rather than stating it directly.



Overall, the dialogue is very good and creates a strong sense of atmosphere and tension. The issues are minor and mostly relate to opportunities to *show* rather than *tell* and to vary the pacing and phrasing.

*   **Slow Build, But Effective:** The scene starts with a very deliberate, slow pace, establishing atmosphere and Seris’s internal state. This is appropriate for a discovery scene, but risks losing momentum. The pacing *just* avoids becoming stagnant because of the consistent internal pressure (Seris’s tremor, quickening pulse).
*   **Kaelen's Interruptions:** Kaelen’s thoughts, while important for information, consistently *interrupt* the building tension. While they add to the mystery, their frequency slightly deflates the suspense. Consider consolidating some of these revelations.
*   **Paragraph 6-7 – Peak Tension:** The realization of the discoloration being a depression, combined with Kaelen's "It is a cage," represents the scene’s peak tension. This is well-executed.
*   **Post-Peak – Sustained Dread:** The final paragraphs maintain a good level of sustained dread, but could be heightened. The "metallic tang" is a nice touch, but feels slightly isolated. Connect it more directly to the feeling of being watched or the straining bonds.
*   **Rhys as Anchor:** Rhys serves as a grounding force, which is effective, but his responses are *very* restrained. While realism is good, a slightly more active response to Seris’s mounting fear might amplify the tension.

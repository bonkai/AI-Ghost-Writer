*   **Over-reliance on Thought Communication:** The entire scene is built on telepathic/mental communication. While established, it becomes *very* repetitive and lacks dynamic variation. Consider occasionally having Kaelen manifest his presence in a more tangible way (a cold spot, a flickering lamp, a subtle scent) even if the core communication remains mental. This would break up the monotony.
*   **"Thinking" as Dialogue Tags:** Using "thought" as a dialogue tag ("She thought," "he thought") is redundant *within* a scene already established as primarily mental communication. It's telling us something we already know. Remove these entirely. Let the content *be* the thought.
*   **Statement vs. Response:** Kaelen frequently *offers* statements rather than *responds* to Seris’s questions. While this establishes his knowing nature, it can feel distancing and less like a conversation. Even a slight acknowledgement of her question before offering his statement would improve the flow. ("Lost?" *A flicker of sadness touched her mind.* "The old magic…")
*   **Repetitive Phrasing:** Phrases like "It remembers" are used multiple times. Vary the language. ("The mountain holds echoes," "The stone retains the past," "Firepeak bears witness.")
*   **"Always" as a Response:** Kaelen's single-word responses ("Always") feel weak. Even a brief expansion, even a mental *image* accompanying the word, would add depth.
*   **Subtext Opportunity - Valerius:** When Seris thinks "Valerius…He knows, doesn’t he?", Kaelen’s response (“He suspects. He always suspects.”) is direct. Instead, consider a more evocative response - a flash of Valerius’s face, a sense of oppressive scrutiny, a chilling premonition. Show, don't tell, his awareness.
*   **Shared Perception - Petal:** The ending with the petal is strong, but the description ("not as an image *sent*, but as a shared perception") is slightly clunky. Trust the reader to understand it's a shared experience. A simpler description ("A single crimson petal materialized over the sketch.") would be more effective.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** The dialogue *does* subtly reveal Kaelen as ancient, patient, and somewhat aloof. However, leaning into these traits more consistently would strengthen his characterization.

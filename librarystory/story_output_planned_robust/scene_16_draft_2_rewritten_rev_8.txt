The library air pressed against Seris’s skin, a subtle weight. Dust motes hung suspended in the shafts of light. A tremor ran through the stone floor, vibrating up through her boots and into her teeth. She braced a hand against a bookshelf, the ancient wood humming with discordant energy.

Rhys, studying a faded map, glanced up, his brow furrowing. His hand moved, not to his dagger, but to subtly adjust his pack, testing its weight. He watched her, assessing, then stepped closer, his gaze questioning.

The library *shifted*. Not visibly, but at the periphery of her vision, familiar lines blurred. The scent of old parchment twisted, overlaid with a metallic tang – ozone and dried blood. A subtle pressure descended, the feeling of a vast network beneath her feet, straining like a drawn bowstring. 

A discordant note fractured the harmony. Luminous threads of energy flickered, dimmed. A single thread snapped, and darkness seeped into the crack, widening it. Another followed, then another, each loss sending a ripple through the network. The energy didn’t explode, but *retreated*, leaving a hollow ache in Seris’s chest and a prickling static on her skin. She stumbled, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue, and pressed a hand to her temple, fighting disorientation.

Rhys caught her arm, his grip firm and anchoring. “Seris?”

Kaelen’s consciousness brushed against hers – not words, but raw sensation: the echoing resonance of a nexus, the weight of ancient power. An image flashed – a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian, pulsing with power, but laced with hairline cracks, the light within flickering erratically. She gasped, tightening her grip on Rhys’s arm.

He felt the tremor run through her, his grip tightening. “What do you see?”

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts, the words tasting like ash. Kaelen’s panic surged – a desperate, wordless plea. The image sharpened – obsidian, stars, cracks widening. *Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…* A cold wind whipped across her skin, the smell of sulfur stinging her nostrils. A deep, resonant thrumming vibrated through her bones. She squeezed her eyes shut, fighting the onslaught, her knuckles white against Rhys’s leather jerkin.

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus, and slowly opened her eyes, blinking against the dim light. She looked at Rhys, then away, gathering her thoughts. The vision felt…imprinted, not merely seen.

Rhys waited, his expression carefully neutral.

“Firestone Mountains?” he asked, his voice tight with concern.

“It’s connected to the source of the network,” she said, her voice strained, the words feeling…given, not reasoned. “Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible. The seal is failing, and the cracks are widest there.”

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within breaks free.”

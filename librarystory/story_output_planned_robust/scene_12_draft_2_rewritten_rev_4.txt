The single oil lamp sputtered and died, plunging the archive into darkness. The air grew heavy, stagnant. A draft, colder than stone, swept through the chamber, carrying the scent of dust and something metallic, faintly floral. Seris instinctively reached for her dagger.

“We’re not alone,” she whispered.

A voice, smooth as polished obsidian, echoed from the shadows. “Intriguing discoveries, Initiate.”

Archon Valerius coalesced from the darkness, not entering, but *becoming* visible. Two heavily armored Guardians materialized beside him, their movements unsettlingly silent. They extended from him, extensions of his will.

“I was researching ancient texts, Archon,” Seris said, meeting his gaze. 

Valerius glided closer, his movements fluid. He didn’t touch the open tome, but the air wavered around it, the ink blurring. He gestured to an illustration of the Aethel. “What do you see?”

“A civilization lost to time. Erased.” Seris’s fingers tightened on the dagger hilt.

Valerius tilted his head. “Erased? Or… repurposed?” He paused, his gaze unwavering. “Consider the loom. Threads can be cut, woven into something new.”

A prickling sensation rose on the back of Seris’s neck. The metallic scent intensified. She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius, unease twisting in her gut. She began to rise, intending to signal her departure.

Valerius’s smile widened, a predator’s acknowledgment. “A wise decision, Initiate. Some doors are best left unopened.” He gestured to a Guardian. “Escort the Initiate back to her chambers. She needs rest.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. Seris noticed the polished steel of its armor wasn’t reflecting the darkness, but *absorbing* it. 

She glanced back at Valerius. He stood before the tome, his gaze fixed on the illustration, but he wasn't looking *at* the image. He was looking *through* it. In that moment, Seris understood. The Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound. Valerius held the threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer. A cold certainty settled over her. She had stumbled onto something dangerous, something far older and more powerful than she could have imagined. And Valerius would do anything to keep it hidden.

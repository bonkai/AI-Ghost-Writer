Seris pressed her palm against the cool stone, a tremor running through her. It wasn’t lost lore; it was a deliberate suppression, and time felt desperately short. She closed her eyes, centering herself against the rising tension, the weight of it pressing against her temples. Rhys paced the shadowed alcove, energy barely contained, his boots silent on the stone floor.

“Kaelen,” Seris focused on their mental link, “anything more on why the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? Specifically, what did they *record*?”

Dust motes danced in the light as pages turned rapidly. Finally, the turning stilled.

*“The Gardener cultivated not merely flora, but memoria – the very fabric of recollection. They understood Eldoria’s history wasn’t singular, but a tapestry of possibilities. They preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…”* Kaelen’s voice trailed off, strained.

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. Rhys stopped pacing, his gaze sharpening. “Lost narratives…alternate histories?” He ran a hand through his hair, considering the implications. “So they weren’t just preserving facts, they were preserving…potential realities?”

*“The Order believed a single, unified history was necessary for stability,”* Kaelen projected, the weight of ages in his voice. *“They feared the chaos of conflicting truths.”*

A cold weight settled in Seris’s chest. She met Rhys’s gaze, a shared understanding passing between them. The implications were staggering. 

“Kaelen, what *else* did the Gardener record?” Seris prompted, her voice tight.

*“Before. Before Eldoria.”* The voice was faint, strained. *“A time of connection.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. “Connection to what?”

*“Other realms…other beings…”*

Pages continued to turn, revealing a single, faded illustration – a swirling vortex of iridescent light, filled with impossible structures. 

“Show me,” Seris breathed, her voice barely a whisper.

The illustration flared, and a rush of images flooded Seris’s mind. She stumbled, bracing herself against Rhys, her knuckles white as she gripped his arm. The images weren’t a clear vision, but fractured glimpses—impossible landscapes, beings of pure light, and a sense of profound loss.

*“The Veil…it wasn’t a barrier erected to keep threats *out*…”* Kaelen’s voice fragmented, distant, laced with sorrow. *“It was built to contain something…to sever a connection.”*

The image sharpened, resolving into a breathtaking landscape of impossible geometry and beings of pure light. Then, a wave of disorientation crashed over Seris, and she cried out, clutching her head. Rhys held her tighter, concern etched on his face.

She shook her head, clearing the images. “I…I saw something. A world…before.” Realization dawned, and her eyes widened. "The Veil…it wasn't about keeping things *out*…it was about keeping *us* from something else.” She leaned heavily on Rhys, her legs suddenly weak.

A chilling weight settled upon them. They weren’t uncovering a conspiracy; they were peeling back deliberately obscured history, a history that threatened everything they knew. Rhys tightened his grip on her arm, his gaze meeting hers. He didn’t need to ask what they should do next. A muscle jumped in his jaw. He murmured, more to himself than to Seris, “They didn’t want us to remember. And now I think I understand *why*.” He glanced at the illustration, a grim set to his features. “This changes everything.”

*   **Kaelen’s Voice:** Kaelen’s dialogue is *very* consistently fragmented and strained. While this establishes a sense of effort/difficulty accessing the information, it becomes repetitive and risks reading as stylistic flourish over genuine character/plot function. Consider varying the delivery – perhaps a complete sentence occasionally, or a shift in *how* the fragmentation manifests (e.g., emotional weight causing breaks, technical difficulty).
*   **Seris’s Repetition:** Seris asks a lot of very direct questions (“What else did the Gardener record?”, “Connection to what?”, “Show me”). While urgency is understandable, it feels a little…interrogative. Could some of this be achieved through internal monologue or letting Kaelen *offer* more information organically?
*   **Rhys’s Internalization:** Rhys has a lot of internal reactions (“A muscle jumped in his jaw,” “He murmured, more to himself”). While not *bad*, it’s a lot of telling instead of showing through dialogue. Could some of his realization be expressed *to* Seris, even if it’s a partial thought or a leading question?
*   **“And now I think I understand *why*.”** This line feels a little cliché. It’s a common phrasing for a dramatic reveal. Consider a more specific, evocative statement that hints at the ‘why’ without stating it outright.
*   **"And I don’t think we’re the only ones who are starting to remember.”** This feels tacked on. It's a good plot point, but it lands abruptly. Consider weaving this realization more organically into the conversation or having it prompted by something Seris says/does.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The shared understanding between Seris and Rhys is *told* (“a shared understanding passing between them”). Show it! A brief exchange of glances, a subtle mirroring of body language, a half-finished sentence completed by the other – these would be more impactful.
*   **Efficiency:** The last paragraph feels a bit verbose. Rhys’s realization and the concluding line could be tightened for greater impact.

*   **Seris's Internal Monologue & Over-Explanation:** There's a lot of telling us what Seris *feels* and *knows* instead of *showing* it through dialogue or action. For example, “It wasn’t a guide she held, but a held breath.” is descriptive, not dialogue-driven. Similarly, lines like “She didn’t need to explain. The intention was clear in the misshapen glyphs.” are unnecessary. Let the *reader* deduce that from the description of the glyphs and Seris's reaction.
*   **Kaelen's Communication:** The method of communication – internal chills, images – is established, but it feels *too* frequent and descriptive. While it’s good to show the impact on Seris, the constant “*Kaelen’s thought echoed…*” becomes repetitive. Consider condensing these instances or showing the impact *through* Seris’s dialogue and actions instead.
*   **Rhys's Restraint:** Rhys is consistently stoic, which is fine, but it borders on *too* passive. While his lack of reassurance is realistic, a little more engagement, even subtle, would make him feel more present in the scene. His responses are often single-line observations.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The tension is high, but the dialogue often states the obvious ("I don’t know what we’ve stumbled onto, but it feels… dangerous"). Explore opportunities for subtext. Instead of saying it’s dangerous, have Seris ask a pointed question, or have Rhys react to her anxiety with a specific action.
*   **"She didn't need to explain..." repetition:** This phrase appears twice, indicating a reliance on telling instead of showing. Trust the reader to understand the situation.
*   **Seris's "I feel watched" line:** This feels cliché. Consider *showing* her feeling watched through her actions – quick glances, checking the room, subtly positioning herself for escape.
*   **Pacing & Rhythm:** The scene is dense with internal thought and description. While not necessarily a flaw, it slows the dialogue's pace. Consider trimming some of the internal monologue to create more breathing room for the conversation.

Overall, the dialogue isn’t *bad*, but it’s heavily weighted towards exposition and internal thought. It would benefit from more subtext, fewer explanations, and a more active role for Rhys.

The library breathed in the quiet after closing. Warm light pooled from scattered lamps, leaving the upper shelves lost in shadow. Seris sat at her worktable, sketching idly, acutely aware of Valerius’s presence – a weight at the edge of her senses. A nearby candle flickered, its flame momentarily twisting in a draft she hadn’t felt moments before.

A subtle shift – not a sound, but a disturbance in a lamplight beam – drew her eye. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed, and a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table. The ink hadn’t fully dried, carrying a faint scent of parchment and something older. It was a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, not as a cartographer would render it, but as a *being* – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power.

Seris traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. *Beautiful,* she thought, and a warmth bloomed on her skin, radiating from the sketch. A mirroring appreciation brushed against her awareness – subtle, but distinctly *felt*.

The page blurred, the charcoal writhing. *It remembers,* a thought formed, clear and woven into her consciousness. Not a response, but an offering. She traced the lines again, a question forming on her lips. “What do you mean?” she breathed, barely a whisper.

*The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth, Seris. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.*

Seris’s fingers tightened on the table’s edge. She glanced towards the shadowed stacks, then back at the sketch. “Lost how?”

Silence stretched, broken only by the soft hiss of the lamps. She felt his focus sifting through forgotten lore, and a fragmented image flickered in her mind – a vast, crumbling library, then a single, brightly lit doorway. *Remembrance.*

“Remembrance?” she repeated aloud, the sound startling in the quiet library. The image faded, leaving a lingering warmth. A book slammed shut in the distance, the sound sharp and controlled. Valerius’s frustration was palpable, a tightening pressure in the air.

*The Order didn’t just forbid magic, Seris. They forbade remembering. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories.* 

A shiver crawled down her spine. This wasn’t about a rare healing plant. This was far older, far more dangerous. She glanced again at the shadowed stacks, sensing Valerius’s scrutiny intensify. He hadn’t moved, but his presence felt closer, more watchful.

“Valerius…he knows, doesn’t he? That we’re looking for more than just a flower?”

Another book slammed shut, closer this time. His presence felt like a tightening vise. *He suspects. He’s a guardian of the forgetting, Seris. He’ll do anything to protect it.*

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

A quiet strength settled over her mind. *Always.*

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch. It wasn’t a drawing, but a warning. A challenge. Then, a delicate fragrance – crimson, perfectly formed – momentarily filled the air, superimposed over the sketch. Not an image *sent*, but a shared perception. They weren’t alone. But as the fragrance faded, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. The scent of dust and old paper was suddenly…metallic. And from the darkness, a distinct *click* – the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing.

Darkness. Not the black of a disconnected monitor, but a total absence of light. A pressure, like lag in a full-VR rig, squeezed at… everything. Or where everything *should* be. Tried to lift a hand, check the binds – nothing. No arm, no body. Just… awareness. A floating cursor with no screen.

Panic spiked – cold, sharp, a system crash. Gasping felt…wrong. Like the animation was there, but no process running. Tried to shout, to ping *something*, but the signal died before it sent. Untethered. Floating in null space. “What the actual?” The thought echoed, weak. Not a voice, just… code trying to execute. What *happened*? Last thing… the game. The pressure. The pain. Fragments flickered – glowing screen, frantic key presses, that searing ache behind the eyes. Then, white. Total system failure? Heart attack? Lag spike from hell?

Tried to focus, to lock onto something solid, but thoughts scattered like dropped packets. Losing coherence. Dissolving into… nothing. Need to reboot. Need… *something*. A system diagnostic, *anything*.

Then, a shift. Not location, but texture. The darkness wasn't flat anymore. It pulsed, a faint ethereal glow. And with it, sound. Not a roar, but a hum, like distant server fans. Architectural. Shapes resolving, indistinct at first, then… a vast, cavernous space. 

*Within* something.

“Hello?” Projected the thought, a desperate signal flare.

A response, not sound, but… *feeling*. A brush against awareness, like a gentle ping. Not a voice, but an impression. Ancient. Patient. Observing.

*Aware.*

The single word resonated. Not just *existing* in the void, but being *scanned*. Like an admin was watching the server. A chill ran through… well, not *through* anything, but the sensation was there. 

Tried to orient, map the space, but it was like rendering a dream in low poly. Structures stretching endlessly, shifting, rearranging. Books. Countless volumes stacked on shelves disappearing into the gloom. And the air – though no lungs to breathe – thick with the scent of aged paper and forgotten data. It wasn't a smell, exactly. More like… information flooding the senses, a data stream of cellulose and ink.

A library. A library beyond imagining. But no librarian in sight. Glitch in the system?

“Where am I?” Projected again, desperation rising.

The response came slower this time, like a firewall blocking the connection.

*Between.*

“Between what?”

A long pause. Unsettling. Then, a chilling revelation.

*Between worlds.*

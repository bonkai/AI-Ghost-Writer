The library held its breath as the last patrons departed. Lamps cast long shadows, veiling the upper shelves in darkness. Seris sketched, charcoal whispering across the page, acutely aware of a presence. Not directly *on* her, but scanning the room. A faint, metallic tang—old blood on stone—prickled her nostrils. She unconsciously tightened her grip on the charcoal, the rough texture grounding her.

A disturbance in a lamplight beam. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed, a single page detaching and fluttering down to land open on the table. Not text, but a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, rendered not as geography, but as a being – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power. A delicate, crimson fragrance bloomed from the sketch, momentarily overpowering the scent of aged paper. Seris inhaled sharply, a ghost of iron coating her tongue.

She traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. The metallic scent intensified, making the hairs on her arms prickle. She glanced up, meeting Kaelen’s gaze. He held hers, a weight of knowing passing between them—a silent acknowledgment of the mountain’s power, mirrored in the subtle tightening of his jaw.

“It remembers,” he said, his voice a low murmur that seemed to vibrate with the stillness of the library.

“What does that mean?” Seris asked, her fingers tightening on the table’s edge, unconsciously mirroring the sharp angles of the sketched mountain. She didn’t need to ask for clarification, not really. She *felt* the weight of ages in the sketch, tasted it in the air.

He moved closer, his movements fluid and silent, the scent of old paper and metal clinging to him. “The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.” He paused, his gaze dropping to the sketch, then lifting to meet hers. “This isn’t just a depiction, Seris. It’s a key.”

A book slammed shut in the distance. Seris glanced towards the shadowed stacks, then back at Kaelen. Valerius was moving. She could almost taste the tension in the air, acrid and sharp like the metallic scent. She pressed her lips together, a silent warning to herself.

“Lost how?” she asked, her voice barely a whisper.

He swept his gaze across the library, a subtle tension tightening his jaw. “The Order didn’t just forbid magic. They forbade *remembering*. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories.”

Seris’s breath caught. “This isn’t about a rare healing plant, is it?”

Another book slammed shut, closer this time. Kaelen’s gaze locked with hers, a silent warning passing between them. He didn’t need to say it; Valerius was already suspecting something. “No. It’s far older, far more dangerous.” He leaned closer, lowering his voice, his breath warm against her ear. “Valerius suspects something. He’s a guardian of the forgetting. He’ll do anything to protect it.”

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.” She opened them, meeting Kaelen’s gaze, and a fleeting connection sparked between them—a silent acknowledgement of the risk they now faced, underscored by the metallic tang in the air. Then, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. And from the darkness, a distinct *click*—the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing.

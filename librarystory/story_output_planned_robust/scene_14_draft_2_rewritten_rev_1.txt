**Revised Scene:**

The scent of aged parchment and dust hung heavy in the air. Seris traced a finger across a star chart, the Serpent constellation winding across the vellum. “Master Elmsworth dismissed the ley lines as folklore.”

*“Elmsworth sees only what he *wants* to see,”* Kaelen’s voice resonated in her mind, sharper than usual. *“The constellations aren’t decoration. They’re a map, a key to the energy beneath Verenhal. And the Spire… it’s not just built *on* that energy, it *controls* it.”*

Seris shifted her attention, scanning the charts for overlapping patterns. Tedious work, mentally layering one constellation onto another.  Rhys, meanwhile, was absorbed in a different section, carefully examining dried herbs.

“Nightshade,” he murmured, more to himself than to her. “Potent, but tricky. A direct application would be… rash. Needs a carrier, something to modulate the toxicity.”

*“He’s not entirely oblivious,”* Kaelen observed, a flicker of surprise in his mental voice. *“He’s approaching the problem analytically, not just seeking recognition.”*

Seris allowed a small smile.  Kaelen’s assessments were rarely wrong. "What are you thinking?"

“A moonpetal infusion, perhaps? It’s known to stabilize volatile compounds.” He glanced up, meeting her gaze. “It might just work.”

“Interesting,” Seris said, genuinely intrigued. "While you're working on that, I'm focused on the ley line intersections."

*“Good. Keep him engaged. The convergence point…”* Kaelen’s voice sharpened. *“There. Where the Serpent, Dragon, and Phoenix align. Directly beneath the Obsidian Spire.”*

Seris traced the point on the chart. “The Spire… an anchor for the ley line energy?”

*“Precisely. And a vulnerable one. It’s been… shielded, but the shield is weakening.”*

Rhys straightened, a triumphant gleam in his eyes. “I think I’ve got it. Moonpetal, a touch of dragon’s scale to act as a catalyst, and a carefully measured dose of nightshade. It should neutralize the blight without harming the surrounding flora.” He presented a small pouch of blended herbs. “What do you think?”

Seris examined the mixture, impressed. "That's… remarkably well-considered, Rhys. You've clearly given this a lot of thought."

He beamed, genuinely pleased. "It's a complex problem. Requires a nuanced approach."

*“He’s not seeking praise, he’s seeking validation of his work. A subtle difference,”* Kaelen commented. *“But important.”*

*“Someone’s coming,”* Kaelen’s voice was urgent. *“A strong magical presence. Valerius.”*

Seris’s senses heightened. She scanned the towering shelves, her hand instinctively reaching for the hidden dagger beneath her cloak. Master Valerius emerged from the labyrinthine stacks, his face impassive.

“Master Valerius,” Seris greeted, forcing a polite tone. “Is there something we can assist you with?”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, lingering on the charts spread across the table. “I trust you are making appropriate use of the library’s resources. Some knowledge is best left undisturbed.” His eyes narrowed, fixing on the overlapping constellation charts. “What precisely are you researching?”

Seris met his gaze, her heart pounding. “Celestial cartography, Master Valerius. We’re studying historical interpretations of the constellations, and their potential connection to the land’s health.”

Valerius’s lips curved into a thin, skeptical smile. “Indeed. A fascinating subject. Though I caution you against delving into matters beyond your comprehension.”

*“He suspects,”* Kaelen warned, his voice a frantic whisper. *“He’s shielding something, and he’s powerful. He knows we’re close.”*

Rhys, sensing the tension, stepped forward. “We’re simply trying to understand the ancient connections between the stars and the land, Master Valerius. To find a solution to the blight.” He held out the pouch of herbs. “We believe we’ve made a breakthrough.”

Valerius’s gaze flickered to the pouch, then back to Seris. A flicker of something unreadable crossed his face. “A ‘breakthrough,’ you say?” He paused, his eyes boring into Seris. “Be careful what you seek, young scholar. Some doors are best left unopened.” He turned and walked away, disappearing back into the stacks.

*“He’s testing us,”* Kaelen said, his voice tight. *“He wants to see how far we’ll push. We need to leave. Now.”*

*   **Rhys's Agency:**  I've given Rhys more of an active role. He's not just a placeholder; he's genuinely engaged in the problem of the blight, and his expertise complements Seris's.  This makes him a more compelling character and avoids the feeling that he's just there to fill space.
*   **Subtler Kaelen:**  Kaelen’s guidance is still present, but it's been toned down to be more internal and less expository.  Instead of directly stating everything, he offers observations and prompts Seris to draw her own conclusions.

*   **Stronger Ending:** The ending is more ambiguous and leaves the reader wanting to know what happens next. Valerius’s warning and Kaelen’s urgent call to leave create a sense of immediate danger.
*   **Show, Don't Tell:**  I've tried to show Seris's thought processes and reactions rather than simply telling the reader what she's thinking.
*   **Pacing:**  I've tightened the pacing by removing some of the unnecessary dialogue and descriptions.
*   **Internal Dialogue:** Kaelen's comments are now more often framed as internal observations, adding to the sense of intimacy and urgency.

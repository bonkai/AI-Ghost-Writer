## Scene 13: The Rogue and the Idealist

The air in the Atheneum’s lower galleries hung thick with the scent of dust and decay, a familiar comfort to Seris. She traced the constellation Draco on a star chart, the symbol she’d copied from the ancient tome warming against her palm. Kaelen’s guidance had led her here, to a hidden alcove dedicated to forgotten cartography.

“Impressive work, Initiate,” a voice drawled from the shadows. “Unearthing relics most scholars wouldn’t bother with.”

Seris turned, hand instinctively moving towards the small dagger concealed beneath her cloak. Leaning against a pillar, regarding her with amused cynicism, was a man who radiated effortless danger. He wasn’t dressed like a scholar, nor a soldier. His tunic was dark leather, worn soft with age, and a network of silver rings adorned his fingers. A wicked-looking curved blade rested comfortably at his hip. 

“Who are you?” she asked, her voice carefully neutral.

“Rhys,” he replied with a lazy grin. “A… collector of curiosities. And a connoisseur of lost knowledge. Though ‘treasure hunter’ is a term some prefer.” He gestured around the alcove. “You have a keen eye. This section hasn’t seen a visitor in decades. What has piqued your interest?”

*“Be cautious,”* Kaelen’s voice whispered in her mind. *“He’s assessing you. And the symbol.”*

“I’m researching stellar cartography,” Seris said, keeping her answer concise. She subtly shifted her weight, positioning herself closer to the alcove’s exit. 

Rhys’s eyes flickered to the parchment in her hand, then back to her face. “The stars. A romantic pursuit. Though often… impractical. Unless, of course, you’re looking for something *beyond* navigation.” He stepped closer, his gaze piercing. “That symbol… it’s old. Very old. Pre-dates the Unification Wars, I’d wager. What do you know about it?”

“Only that it appears on an ancient star chart,” Seris replied, refusing to offer more. “I’m still deciphering its meaning.”

Rhys chuckled, a dry, rasping sound. “Deciphering. A noble goal. But knowledge isn’t always meant to be understood. Some things are better left buried.”

*“He knows more than he’s letting on,”* Kaelen urged. *“Press him. Ask about the symbol’s origins.”*

“Do you know its origins?” Seris challenged, meeting his gaze. “I’m curious to learn anything you might know.”

Rhys leaned back against the pillar, a flicker of something unreadable crossing his face. “Let’s just say I’ve encountered it… in places most scholars only dream of. It marks a convergence. A nexus of power. A place where the veil between worlds… thins.”

“Worlds?” Seris questioned, a knot forming in her stomach.

“Metaphorically speaking, of course,” Rhys said with a dismissive wave of his hand. “Though, in my experience, the line between metaphor and reality is often… blurred.” He paused, studying her expression. “You’re idealistic, aren’t you? A believer in the power of knowledge for the betterment of all.”

“I believe knowledge is valuable,” Seris conceded.

Rhys laughed again, a harsh, cynical sound. “Valuable? It’s a weapon, Initiate. A tool. And like any tool, it can be used to build… or to destroy. You seek this knowledge, but do you understand the consequences?”

*“He’s trying to dissuade you,”* Kaelen warned. *“He fears what you might find.”*

“I understand that all knowledge carries responsibility,” Seris said firmly. “But fear shouldn’t dictate what we seek.”

Rhys’s eyes narrowed. “Naive. But perhaps… useful.” He straightened, pushing himself off the pillar. “I have a proposition for you, Initiate. I’ve been tracking this symbol for years. I know where it leads. I can help you decipher its meaning… for a price.”

“What price?” Seris asked cautiously.

Rhys smiled, a predatory glint in his eyes. “Information. I have… certain interests that align with your research. Let’s just say I’m looking for something lost. And I believe you might hold the key.” He paused, letting his words hang in the air. “A partnership. What do you say?”

*“Don’t trust him,”* Kaelen’s voice was urgent, laced with warning. *“He’s manipulating you. He’ll use you to get what he wants.”*

Seris met Rhys’s gaze, her mind racing. He was dangerous, cynical, and clearly driven by his own agenda. But he also possessed knowledge she desperately needed. 

“I’ll consider it,” she said slowly, buying herself time. “But I make no promises.”

Rhys’s smile widened. “Wise. Very wise. Perhaps we’ll have much to discuss.” He gave a curt nod and melted back into the shadows, leaving Seris alone with her thoughts and Kaelen’s increasingly frantic warnings. The air in the alcove suddenly felt colder, heavier. She clutched the parchment with the ancient symbol, a growing sense of unease settling over her. She had found an ally, perhaps. But she suspected this partnership would come at a very steep price.

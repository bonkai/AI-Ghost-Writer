*   **Hemlock’s Dialogue – Subtext & Efficiency:** Hemlock’s dialogue is *very* strong, but leans heavily into cryptic pronouncements. While effective for establishing his character, it risks becoming repetitive. The repeated pauses and indirect answers, while building tension, start to feel like a pattern rather than nuanced responses to *this* specific moment. Consider streamlining a few of his longer speeches – perhaps letting a single, pointed phrase carry the same weight.
*   **Kael’s Internal State & Dialogue:** Kael’s dialogue feels a little sparse given the intensity of her experience. While showing (through physical reactions) is good, a *little* more vocalization of her struggle – even just frustrated grunts or short, desperate pleas – could amplify the scene's impact. Her final line ("What do you mean, ‘guardians’?") feels a bit late; she's been experiencing resistance for a while, and a question about potential defenses might have come earlier.
*   **Repetition of Physical Reactions:** The repeated mention of the tremor, metallic taste, and Kael clutching her head, while initially effective, begins to feel redundant. Consider varying the descriptions of her discomfort or focusing on *new* physical manifestations as the scene progresses.
*   **“It doesn’t want to be found” – Opportunity for Subtext:** Kael’s line “It doesn’t want to be found” is a bit on-the-nose. Could this be *implied* through her struggle and frustration, rather than stated outright? Showing her realization through her actions and internal monologue would be more impactful.
*   **Kael's Composure:** The description of Kael *fighting* to appear composed feels a bit…telling, not showing. We see her clenching her fist, but the internal struggle to *maintain* composure could be conveyed more subtly through her dialogue (a forced calmness in her voice, a clipped tone).

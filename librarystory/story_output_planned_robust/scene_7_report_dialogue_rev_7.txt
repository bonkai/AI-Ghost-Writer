*   **Over-Reliance on Internalization:** A significant portion of this scene is Seris’s internal experience described *around* the dialogue, rather than *revealed through* it. While the descriptions are vivid, it weakens the impact of the conversation. For example, the extended description of the vision *before* she speaks about it feels like telling, not showing. Consider how much of that experience could be conveyed *through* her dialogue – fragmented sentences, emotional outbursts, specific details she focuses on.
*   **Hemlock's Dialogue – Too Cryptic/Passive:** Hemlock’s lines are *almost* entirely cryptic statements. While mystery is good, he feels more like a narrator delivering exposition than an active participant in the conversation. His lines lack a clear *motivation* beyond observing Seris. What does *he* want? What is *his* agenda? Even subtle shifts in his phrasing to reveal his intentions would add depth.
*   **“Some places remember…” – Cliché & Exposition:** The line “Some places remember. Some objects…hold echoes” is a fairly standard fantasy trope and feels like a convenient info-dump. It doesn’t feel organic to the conversation. Consider how this information could be revealed through Hemlock *reacting* to Seris’s description of the vision, rather than stating it directly.
*   **Repetitive Emphasis on Knowing/Watching:** The repeated emphasis on being "known" and the sphere/serpent "watching" becomes a bit heavy-handed. While it’s important thematically, find ways to convey it more subtly through action or subtext.
*   **Seris's Dialogue – A Bit Explanatory:** Seris's description of the vision ("Cities…a serpent…it felt real…") is a little too comprehensive. It feels like she's recounting it to someone, rather than reacting in the moment. Consider shortening her sentences, focusing on the *emotional* impact rather than the detailed imagery.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Subtext:** The entire scene feels a bit surface-level. There's a lot of stating of facts and feelings, but not much underlying tension or unspoken meaning. Explore what each character *isn’t* saying. What are they hiding from each other? What are their true motivations?

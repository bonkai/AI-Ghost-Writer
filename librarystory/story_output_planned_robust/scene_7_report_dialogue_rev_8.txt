*   **“Cities…a serpent…it felt real. Not a vision, but…a memory. One that isn’t mine. It felt like looking into my own past, but a past I never lived.”** – This is a lot of telling, not showing. The emotional impact is lessened because Seris is *describing* the experience rather than *reacting* to it through dialogue. Consider breaking this into smaller pieces, interspersed with Hemlock’s reactions or questions. For example: “It…it felt real. Like a memory.” followed by Hemlock prompting, “Whose memory?”
*   **Hemlock’s lines feel a bit too…knowing.** While his subtly is good, his responses are almost *too* perfect. He immediately understands the implications without Seris fully articulating them. It feels like he's there to deliver exposition rather than genuinely reacting to her experience. Consider making his initial response more cautious or questioning. ("A memory? That's...unusual.")
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The entire conversation lacks a strong undercurrent of tension or hidden agendas. Given the potentially dangerous nature of the sphere and Hemlock's secretive nature, there's an opportunity to build tension through veiled threats or carefully worded questions.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** We don't get a strong sense of *how* Seris typically speaks. Is she usually verbose, or more reserved? The dialogue feels a bit neutral, not fully revealing her personality.
*   **Repetition of "felt":** The word "felt" is used repeatedly. While it conveys emotional impact, varying the language would make the dialogue more dynamic. Consider using synonyms like "seemed," "appeared," "resonated," or simply showing her reaction through body language.
*   **"Be careful. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting*.”** – This line is good, but the pause indicated by the ellipsis feels a little forced in dialogue. Consider a more natural pause, like a change in tone or a significant look.

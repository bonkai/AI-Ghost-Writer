**Draft 3**

The scent of aged parchment and dust hung heavy in the library. Seris traced a finger across the star chart, the constellation Cygnus mirroring the pattern of blight spreading across the kingdom. Rhys meticulously ground dried herbs, the rhythmic rasp a counterpoint to the silence.

“Moonpetal, a touch of dragon’s scale to catalyze…and nightshade,” Rhys murmured, presenting a small pouch of blended powder. “A delicate balance. Neutralize the blight without harming the surrounding flora.”

Seris examined the mixture, impressed. “Remarkably considered, Rhys. You’ve clearly given this a lot of thought.”

He beamed, a flicker of genuine pride in his eyes. “It’s a complex problem. Demands nuance.”

*He’s not seeking praise, validation of his work,* Kaelen’s voice echoed within her mind. *A subtle difference, but important.*

A prickling sensation at the back of her neck. *Someone’s coming.*

Seris’s hand instinctively drifted towards the hidden dagger beneath her cloak. Master Valerius emerged from the labyrinthine stacks, his face an impassive mask.

“Master Valerius,” Seris greeted, forcing a polite tone. “Is there something we can assist you with?”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, lingering on the charts spread across the table. “I trust you are making appropriate use of the library’s resources. Some knowledge is best left undisturbed.” His eyes narrowed, fixing on the overlapping constellation charts. “What precisely are you researching?”

Seris met his gaze, her heart a steady drum against her ribs. “Celestial cartography, Master Valerius. We’re studying historical interpretations of the constellations, and their potential connection to the land’s health.”

Valerius’s lips curved into a thin, skeptical smile. “Indeed. A fascinating subject. Though I caution you against delving into matters beyond your comprehension.”

*He suspects,* Kaelen warned, his voice a frantic whisper. *He’s shielding something. Powerful. We’re close.*

Rhys, sensing the tension, stepped forward, holding out the pouch of herbs. “We’re simply trying to understand the ancient connections between the stars and the land, Master Valerius. To find a solution to the blight. We believe we’ve made a breakthrough.”

Valerius’s gaze flickered to the pouch, then back to Seris. A flicker of something unreadable – perhaps fear? – crossed his face. “A ‘breakthrough,’ you say?” He paused, his eyes boring into her. “Be careful what you seek, young scholar. Some doors are best left unopened.” He turned and walked away, disappearing back into the stacks.

Seris exhaled slowly, her muscles coiled tight.

*He’s testing us,* Kaelen said, his voice tight. *He wants to see how far we’ll push.*

A cold dread settled in her stomach. She glanced at Rhys, his brow furrowed with concern.

*We need to leave. Now.*

Seris didn't hesitate. “Rhys, we’re finished here.” She swept the charts into a roll, her movements quick and decisive.

Rhys, sensing the urgency, followed suit. “What’s wrong?”

“We’ve attracted unwanted attention.”

They moved swiftly towards the exit, the silence of the library now oppressive. As they reached the archway, Seris risked one last glance back.

Valerius stood at the far end of the aisle, a dark silhouette amidst the towering shelves. He wasn’t watching the shelves. He was watching *them*.

A shiver ran down Seris’s spine. They were no longer scholars seeking knowledge. They were trespassers, and they were being hunted.

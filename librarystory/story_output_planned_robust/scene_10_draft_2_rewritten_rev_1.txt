*“Ubi draco dormit, ibi lumen occultum.”* – “Where the dragon sleeps, there lies hidden light.”

*“Not literal, Initiate. Draco, the constellation. Find its position.”*

She traced Draco with her finger, then followed its line of sight. A small, almost invisible symbol nestled amongst the stars – a circle bisected by a vertical line.

*“A key. A map. Older than this library.”*

She glanced around. The Restricted Section felt… suffocating. Hemlock was at his post, seemingly engrossed, but she *felt* his attention – a weight on her neck. He was watching.

Her hand trembled as she sketched the symbol onto parchment. It felt… *important*.  More than she could understand.

A shadow fell across the page.

“Finding something interesting, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice was smooth, deceptively gentle.

Seris snapped the parchment shut, her heart hammering. “Just stellar cartography,” she forced out. “For my studies.”

Hemlock’s gaze swept over the book, lingering on the highlighted line. “Fascinating. Though some knowledge is best left unmapped.” His eyes met hers, unreadable. “The stars hold secrets. And secrets… bite.”

Hemlock’s slow smile didn’t reach his eyes. “Indeed.” He paused, scanning the shelves. “The past reaches out. Sometimes, it’s best not to answer.”

He knew. He knew she was uncovering something he wanted hidden.

“I don’t understand,” she whispered.

Hemlock’s smile widened, chillingly. “Perhaps you will. In time.”

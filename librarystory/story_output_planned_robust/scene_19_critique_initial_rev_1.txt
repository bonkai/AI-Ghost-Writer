*   **Romance – Lack of Subtlety & Internal Experience:** While the scene establishes mental communication, the “warmth” and “gentle pressure” used to convey connection feel overly direct and tell, rather than *show* the burgeoning emotional connection. The scene relies heavily on stating feelings rather than letting them unfold through Seris’s internal experience and reactions. The petal image is a good start, but more nuanced, internal responses are needed to build romantic tension.
*   **Pacing – Information Dump:** The scene quickly moves into exposition about Firepeak, the Sunstone Bloom, and the Order's motives. This feels rushed and diminishes the impact of the reveal. Slowing down the information delivery, perhaps by having Seris piece things together more gradually through Kaelen's fragmented thoughts or visions, would create more suspense and allow the mystery to breathe.
*   **Character – Kaelen’s Agency:** Kaelen primarily *reacts* to Seris's questions. While his frustration is shown through book slamming, he feels passive. Give him more agency – let him initiate some of the mental communication, offer cryptic clues before being asked, or subtly guide Seris’s thoughts. This will make him a more compelling and active presence in the scene, and strengthen the connection.

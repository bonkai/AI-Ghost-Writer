*   **Efficiency & Purpose:** The dialogue is largely functional, delivering information, but often feels like characters are *stating* things rather than *revealing* them through interaction. For example, much of Kaelen’s exposition (“The Sunstone Bloom isn’t just a rare flower…”) could be woven into a more dynamic exchange. Consider showing, not telling.
*   **Subtext:** Opportunities for subtext are missed. Kaelen is consistently withholding, which is fine for his character, but the *way* he does it feels blunt. Instead of simply not elaborating (“He didn’t elaborate”), show his internal conflict or strategic thinking through pauses, changes in tone, or subtle physical reactions.
*   **Authenticity & Voice:** Kaelen’s voice is consistently serious and measured. While consistent, it borders on monotonous. Injecting moments of frustration, weariness, or even dry humor would make him feel more real. Seris's voice is less defined. She primarily asks questions, which is fine, but could benefit from more assertive statements or displays of her own intellect.
*   **Flow & Rhythm:** The dialogue is a bit static. There's a lot of back-and-forth exposition without much emotional beat or rising tension *within* the conversations. Consider incorporating more active listening (characters responding to the *emotion* behind what’s said, not just the words themselves).
*   **Indirect Characterization:** The dialogue *hints* at the power dynamic between Kaelen and Seris (he’s the keeper of secrets, she’s the seeker), but it could be strengthened. Show how Kaelen subtly directs the conversation or how Seris challenges his authority (even if she doesn't win).
*   **Repetition:** The phrase "Marius is..." appears frequently. Vary the phrasing or show Marius’s actions instead of constantly referring to him.
*   **"Why?" Question:** Seris asking "Why?" twice feels a little weak. Consider replacing one instance with a more specific question or a statement of her own deduction.

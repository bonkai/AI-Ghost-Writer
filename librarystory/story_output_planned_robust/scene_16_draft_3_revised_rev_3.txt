The air in the library thickened, a pressure against Seris’s skin that wasn’t quite painful, but *wrong*. Dust motes hung suspended, not swirling, but frozen mid-dance. A tremor ran through the stone, resonating in her teeth. Then, a tightening behind her eyes, the prickling sensation of being watched by something ancient.

“Something’s happening,” she breathed, bracing a hand against a towering bookshelf.

Rhys, studying a faded map, looked up, concern creasing his brow. “What is it?”

Before Seris could answer, the library *shifted*. Not visibly, but a distortion of perception, a ripple in the very fabric of the room. The scent of old parchment and beeswax was tainted with a metallic tang, like blood and ozone. 

Then came the sensations – the weight of ages, colossal power, and a profound sorrow that resonated deep within her bones. She felt a network of stars woven with threads of energy, a celestial tapestry stretched across the void. Beings of grace and power moved within it, not creating, but *sustaining* the constellations, channeling energy against a looming collapse she could *feel*.

A fracture. A discordant note, a shadow creeping into the light. A figure cloaked in obsidian unleashed a force that didn’t destroy, but *severed* – cutting the threads, allowing darkness to seep through. The stars didn’t weep from destruction, but from *unraveling*.

Seris gasped, stumbling back, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue. She pressed a hand to her temple, fighting the onslaught.

Rhys gripped her arm, steadying her. “Seris! What’s happening?”

Kaelen’s voice, fragmented and strained, echoed in her mind – not words, but raw sensation. *“…Nexus…responding…resonance…ancient…before…”* A flicker of icy dread, then *“…key…vulnerability…”*

She saw it then – a web of light, straining against an impossible darkness. A pressure building, threatening to overwhelm. The image coalesced – a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian. It pulsed with power, but now, hairline cracks spiderwebbed across its surface, the light within flickering erratically. 

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts. “A ward…a seal…and it’s failing.” She felt a surge of Kaelen’s panic – *“…contained…weakening…”*

Rhys squeezed her arm, concern etched on his face. “What is it, Seris? What does it mean?”

“It’s connected to the Nexus…to the source of the network…a prison.” She closed her eyes, forcing herself to sift through the onslaught of sensation. “And the seal is breaking.” Kaelen’s voice, a dying ember – *“…Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…hurry…”*

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus.

“Firestone Mountains?” Rhys asked, voice tight. “What’s happening?”

Seris closed her eyes, forcing clarity. “The Nexus isn’t just a source of power, it’s a prison. Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible. The seal is failing, and the Firestone Mountains…they’re where the cracks are widest.”

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within the Nexus breaks free.” The fate of Eldoria, she knew, hung precariously in the balance.

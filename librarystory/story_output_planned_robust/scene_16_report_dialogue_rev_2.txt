*   **Kaelen’s Communication:** While the fragmented, sensation-based communication is a good choice, it borders on *too* fragmented. It’s becoming a string of keywords rather than conveying any real emotional weight or urgency *from Kaelen himself*. Consider adding *one* brief, emotionally charged fragment per transmission – a hint of fear, desperation, or pain – to ground the information and remind us this is a person communicating, not just a data stream.
*   **Rhys’s Role:** Rhys is largely a reactor. He asks clarifying questions, but feels passive. While understandable given the situation, consider giving him *one* moment of proactive insight or a suggestion, even if Seris quickly dismisses it. It would make him feel more engaged and less like a sounding board.
*   **“It’s connected…” & “The Nexus isn’t just…”:** These lines, while revealing information, are still quite expository. Consider *showing* Seris making these connections through her internal reactions and physical sensations. For example, instead of “It’s connected to the Nexus,” perhaps “The symbol… it *resonates* with the feeling of the network, the pull towards the Nexus…”
*   **Repetition of “Nexus”:** The word "Nexus" is used *very* frequently. While it's central to the scene, consider finding synonyms or rephrasing sentences to avoid sounding repetitive. ("The source," "the prison," "the heart of the network")
*   **Seris's Final Line:** “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within the Nexus breaks free.” – This feels a little flat after the intensity of the visions. Consider adding a beat of physical reaction (a clenched fist, a sharp intake of breath) to emphasize her determination.

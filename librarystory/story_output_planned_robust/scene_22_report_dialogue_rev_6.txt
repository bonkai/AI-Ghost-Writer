*   **Repetitive Revelation:** The core reveal – the Veil’s true purpose – is stated *multiple* times. First by Kaelen ("...built to contain something…to sever a connection."), then by Seris ("...keeping something *in*…or keeping us from something *else*."). This diminishes the impact. Consider streamlining – let the initial reveal from Kaelen *land* and let Seris’s reaction/understanding be more about the *implications* than restating the fact.
*   **Kaelen’s Voice:** Kaelen’s dialogue is consistently fragmented and strained. While this *could* indicate the difficulty of accessing the information, it’s becoming a pattern that feels more like a stylistic tic than a meaningful character choice. Consider varying the sentence structure and level of fragmentation. Not *every* line needs to be delivered in pieces.
*   **“Show me” – Lack of Nuance:** Seris’s “Show me” feels a little flat. Given the gravity of the situation and her established character, a more emotionally resonant line might be better. Something like, “I need to see it,” or “Let me understand.”
*   **Silent Agreement – Cliché:** The ending – Rhys tightening his grip, a silent agreement – is a common trope. It’s not *bad*, but it feels a little predictable. Consider showing their shared understanding through a more specific action or a brief exchange of dialogue, even if it's just a single word or a shared glance with internal monologue.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** While the dialogue *reveals* information, it doesn't always *reveal character*. For example, Rhys is largely reactive. Give him a line that shows *his* specific interpretation of the reveal, his fears, or his plan. What does *he* make of this new information?
*   **Tighten the Reveal:** The initial fragmented passage feels a bit info-dumpy. Consider breaking it up with dialogue or action. Perhaps Seris reads a line, then reacts, then Rhys asks a question, then another line is read. This creates more dynamic pacing.

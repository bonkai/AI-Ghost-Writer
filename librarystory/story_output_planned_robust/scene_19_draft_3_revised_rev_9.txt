The library settled into stillness as the last patrons departed. Lamps cast long shadows, veiling the upper shelves. Seris sketched, charcoal whispering across the page, acutely aware of a presence nearby. Not directly *on* her, but observing. She unconsciously tightened her grip on the charcoal, the rough texture grounding her.

A lamplight beam flickered. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed, a single page detaching and fluttering down to land open on the table. Not text, but a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, rendered not as geography, but as a being – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power. A delicate, crimson fragrance bloomed from the sketch, momentarily overpowering the scent of aged paper. Seris inhaled sharply, a ghost of iron coating her tongue.

She traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. She glanced up, meeting Kaelen’s gaze. His jaw was tight, mirroring the sharp angles of the sketched mountain.

“It remembers,” he said, his voice a low murmur that seemed to vibrate with the stillness of the library.

“What does that mean?” Seris asked, her fingers tightening on the table’s edge. She *felt* the weight of ages in the sketch, tasted it in the air.

He moved closer, his movements fluid and silent. “The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.” He paused, his gaze dropping to the sketch, then lifting to meet hers. “This isn’t just a depiction, Seris. It’s a key.”

A book slammed shut in the distance. Seris glanced towards the shadowed stacks, then back at Kaelen. Valerius was moving. She pressed her lips together, a silent warning to herself.

“Lost how?” she asked, her voice barely a whisper.

He swept his gaze across the library, tension tightening his jaw. “The Order didn’t just forbid magic. They forbade *remembering*. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories.”

Seris’s breath caught. “This isn’t about a rare healing plant, is it?”

Another book slammed shut, closer this time. Kaelen’s gaze locked with hers, a silent warning passing between them. He didn’t need to speak; Valerius was already suspecting something. He leaned closer, his breath warm against her ear. “No. It’s far older, far more dangerous.”

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.” She opened them, meeting Kaelen’s gaze, and a fleeting connection sparked between them—a shared understanding of the risk they now faced. Then, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. And from the darkness, a distinct *click*—the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing.

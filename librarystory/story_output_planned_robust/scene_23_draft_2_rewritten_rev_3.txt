The workshop smelled of oiled steel and dried herbs, a fragile peace disturbed by insistent rapping at the door. Seris ran a honing stone along the edge of a scalpel, the rhythmic rasp a deliberate counterpoint. Rhys, ostensibly organizing charts, kept glancing towards the door, his hand drifting near a drawer, fingers tightening and relaxing against the wood.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her voice a low current, her gaze never leaving the sharpening stone.

“A merchant,” a gravelly voice replied. “Looking for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask.”

Rhys exchanged a fleeting glance with Seris. A prickle of unease ran down his spine. He opened the door to reveal a man swallowed by shadow, a wide-brimmed hat obscuring his features. Only his eyes were visible – sharp, assessing, unsettlingly still. The air around him carried the scent of woodsmoke and old metal, and Rhys felt a sudden chill despite the workshop’s warmth.

“I understand you have charts of the Firepeak region,” the man said, his voice a rasp against stone. He deposited a heavy pouch onto the workbench, the clink of coins stark in the small space. “I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps, particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys’s gaze narrowed, studying the man. He subtly shifted his weight, a readiness coiled in his muscles. “What *kind* of formations?”

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin smile. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” His eyes drifted to Seris, lingering on the flex of muscle beneath her tunic, and a flicker of something predatory crossed his face. “I’ve heard whispers of your…research. The Sunstone Bloom. A beautiful flower, but hardly worth risking life and limb for.”

Seris didn’t meet his gaze. She traced the edge of a botanical illustration with a calloused fingertip, the movement slow and deliberate, her knuckles whitening slightly. “We’re simply botanists, cataloging rare species. Our work is purely academic.”

The merchant chuckled, a dry, rasping sound. “Botanists who happen to be interested in ancient legends and forgotten pathways. Don’t insult my intelligence.” He tapped the pouch. “But we *can* discuss terms.”

Rhys’s eyes met Seris’s, a silent plea for understanding. He didn't need to voice the question – *what do we do?* – it hung between them, a taut thread, and a bead of sweat trickled down his temple.

Seris didn’t return his gaze. She slowly lowered her hand, not to her side, but to rest it flat on the workbench, palm down, fingers spread slightly. A subtle signal, almost imperceptible, and her breathing shallowed. *Patience. Let him talk.*

“We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice unwavering, a cool counterpoint to the merchant’s energy. “But we know someone who might.” She finally met the merchant’s gaze, her eyes the color of flint, assessing, and a muscle ticked in her jaw. “And that someone…charges a premium.”

A beat of silence stretched, thick and tense. The merchant’s eyes narrowed, studying Seris, gauging her resolve, and his lips thinned. He didn’t like being played, but he recognized a kindred spirit. 

“Interesting,” he said, his voice a low purr. “Perhaps we *can* discuss terms.” He gestured towards a stool with a gloved hand. “Shall we?”

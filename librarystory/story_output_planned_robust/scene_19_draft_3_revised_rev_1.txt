## Draft 3 - Scene 19

The library had emptied for the night. Only a handful of lamps cast pools of warm light across the towering shelves, leaving the upper reaches lost in shadow. Seris sat at their worktable, not sketching, not reading, but simply…present. The weight of Valerius’s scrutiny still pressed on her, a cold spot on her skin.

A flicker of movement caught her eye. Not physical, but a subtle disturbance in the air, a ripple in the patterns of dust motes dancing in a lamplight beam. Kaelen.

*He’s watching,* she thought, not voicing the words, knowing he’d perceive them regardless. A faint pressure acknowledged her awareness, a subtle resonance against her mind.

A book on a nearby shelf trembled, its spine flexing. Then, a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table before her. The ink hadn't fully dried; a faint scent of parchment and something…older, lingered. It wasn’t a message, not exactly. A sketch. A rough charcoal rendering of Firepeak Mountain, but not as a cartographer would draw it. This depicted the mountain as a *being*, its peaks like jagged teeth, its slopes etched with lines of ancient power.

Seris traced the lines of the drawing with a fingertip. A fleeting impression brushed against her mind – a sense of immense age, of stone remembering centuries. *Beautiful,* she thought, and the impression intensified, resonating with a shared appreciation.

*It remembers,* a thought formed in her head, clear and distinct, yet not spoken. It wasn't her voice, but Kaelen’s, woven into the fabric of her consciousness. He hadn’t responded to her observation; he’d offered it unprompted.

She traced the lines of the drawing with a fingertip. “What do you mean?” she thought back, directing the question inward.

The book shifted on the shelf, a subtle readjustment. *The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth, Seris. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.* He offered the thought as a statement, not a response to her question.

“Lost?” she thought, a questioning echo.

The page before her blurred, the charcoal lines seeming to writhe. *The old magic. The knowledge the Order sought to erase. It’s buried within Firepeak. And the Sunstone Bloom…it’s a key.*

Seris frowned. “A key to what?”

A long silence stretched, broken only by the crackling of the lamps. She could *feel* Kaelen’s focus, a distant sifting through layers of forgotten lore. He didn’t immediately respond. Finally, a fragmented image flickered in her mind – a vast library, crumbling and overgrown, then a single, brightly lit doorway. *Remembrance.*

“Remembrance?” she repeated, aloud this time, the sound startling in the quiet library.

*The Order didn’t just forbid magic, Seris. They forbade remembering. They feared what the past held. Firepeak…it remembers everything. And the Bloom…it can unlock those memories.* The image of the doorway solidified, then faded.

Seris felt a shiver crawl down her spine. This wasn’t just about finding a rare healing plant anymore. This was about something far older, far more dangerous.

“Valerius…” she thought, the name a dark weight in her mind. “He knows, doesn’t he? That we’re looking for more than just a flower?”

A book slammed shut on a nearby shelf, the sound echoing. Kaelen’s frustration was palpable, but controlled. *He suspects. He always suspects. He’s a guardian of the forgetting, Seris. He’ll do anything to protect it.*

Seris closed her eyes, her temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

A calming presence settled over her mind, not a warmth, but a quiet strength. *Always.*

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch of Firepeak. It no longer seemed like a drawing, but a warning. A challenge. And, strangely, a promise. The mountain remembered. And now, so did she. 

A single petal, crimson and perfectly formed, materialized not as an image *sent*, but as a shared perception, a silent affirmation superimposed over the sketch. It wasn't a gift, but a mirroring – a resonance of understanding. They weren’t alone. And the journey ahead, however dangerous, felt…less daunting.

*   **Repetitive Questioning:** The scene relies heavily on Seris asking “Is there anything more?” or variations of it. While it conveys urgency, it becomes predictable and lacks nuance. Consider having Kaelen proactively offer information, even if fragmented, or having Seris infer things from the revealed text.
*   **Exposition through Kaelen:** Kaelen functions almost *entirely* as an exposition delivery system. The mental connection feels like a convenient plot device to dump information. Try to show Seris and Rhys *deducing* more of this themselves, based on the fragments Kaelen provides. Perhaps Kaelen could offer impressions or feelings *alongside* the facts, adding emotional weight.
*   **Rhys’s Role:** Rhys largely reacts and states the obvious ("So, they weren’t just suppressing ideas?"). He feels passive. Give him more agency – let him formulate theories, challenge assumptions, or offer alternative interpretations of the information.
*   **“The Weave” – Over-Explanation:** The explanation of “The Weave” is a bit clunky and on-the-nose. The initial mention is fine, but the subsequent explanation feels like a textbook definition. Trust the reader to grasp the concept through context and implication.
*   **Seris's Internal State & Dialogue:** While the stage directions indicate Seris's fear and dread, this doesn't always translate into *how* she speaks. Her dialogue is often quite direct, even when she should be shaken. Inject more vulnerability or hesitation into her lines.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – Valerius:** The reveal about Valerius feels a little flat. Instead of simply stating “He knew we were getting close,” show Seris *realizing* this through a specific memory or detail. A quick flashback, or a re-interpretation of a past conversation, would be much more impactful.
*   **Fragmented Dialogue:** The mental connection dialogue feels a bit choppy. Vary the sentence structure and length to create a more natural flow.

*   **Repetitive Action Tags:** The constant use of “He didn’t just…” and “Seris didn’t just…” is becoming noticeable and distracting. It’s slowing down the pace and feels like telling rather than showing. Trust the reader to understand the weight of these actions. For example, instead of "He didn’t just step back; he circled Seris…”, simply write “He circled Seris…”
*   **Over-Reliance on Physical Manifestations of Emotion:** While the physical tells (trembling fingers, jaw ticking, etc.) are good, they are *constant*. It’s becoming a pattern. Vary it. Sometimes an internal thought (like Seris’s pulse thrumming) is more effective. Let *some* emotion be contained, not all broadcast.
*   **Rhys's Silence:** Rhys is *very* silent. While effective to a degree, it’s bordering on unrealistic. Even non-verbal characters react *somehow* to direct pressure. A slight tightening of the jaw, a shift in weight – something to acknowledge Marius’s presence, even if he doesn’t speak.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – Marius:** Marius’s dialogue is a bit *too* direct with the veiled threats. Consider layering more subtext. Instead of explicitly saying “Some memories are best left buried,” he could allude to the consequences of uncovering certain truths, leaving the threat implied.
*   **"We have no secrets" – Cliché:** This line feels very predictable. While it serves a purpose, consider a more nuanced response. Perhaps a deflection, a question back to Marius, or a statement about their dedication to the Order – something that avoids the direct denial.
*   **Kaelen's Voice – Limited Use:** Kaelen’s telepathic communication is interesting, but it’s a single line. Expand on this! It's a unique element that could add significant tension and insight into Rhys's motivations.
*   **Indirect Characterization - Seris/Elara:** The constant clutching of the pouch is effective, but consider *showing* what Elara meant to Seris through a brief, internal memory or a fleeting thought.
*   **Pacing – Slow Burn:** While the tension is building, the scene is *very* slow. The constant descriptions of actions are contributing to this. Trim some of the action tags and let the dialogue and internal thoughts carry more weight.

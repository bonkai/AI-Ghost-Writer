*   **Kaelen’s Voice:** The consistent use of italics for Kaelen’s voice, while indicating a mental link, becomes visually tiring and doesn't necessarily *sound* natural. Consider varying how this connection is indicated – perhaps brief, descriptive phrases ("Kaelen's thoughts echoed in her mind...") interspersed with direct dialogue, or subtle shifts in Seris’s internal monologue reflecting Kaelen’s input. The fragmented, distant quality is good, but the italics amplify it to the point of feeling repetitive.
*   **Exposition & “Tell, Don’t Show”:** A lot of information is delivered *through* dialogue, specifically Kaelen’s explanations. While necessary for plot progression, it feels like a series of explanations rather than a natural conversation. For example, Kaelen stating “The Veil…it wasn’t a barrier erected to keep threats out…” is very direct. Could this be revealed more subtly through Seris’s reaction to the images, or Rhys’s deductions?
*   **Rhys’s Reactions:** Rhys often states the obvious ("This changes everything.") or simply acknowledges what Seris has already conveyed. His dialogue feels more like a sounding board than an active participant. Give him more internal conflict or unique insights. What specifically does *he* think about the implications?
*   **Seris’s Prompting:** Seris’s questions are functional but lack nuance. They move the plot forward, but don't reveal much about her internal state beyond determination. Consider adding emotional layers to her prompts. Is she afraid? Hopeful? Angry?
*   **Repetition of "Remembering":** The concept of “remembering” is used frequently, which, while thematic, starts to feel repetitive. Explore synonyms or different ways to express the regaining of lost knowledge.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The shared understanding between Seris and Rhys is stated (“a shared understanding passing between them”). *Show* that understanding through their actions, body language, or brief, meaningful exchanges.
*   **"What was contained?"** – The final question feels a bit abrupt and lacks emotional weight after the intense vision. Perhaps a moment of quiet contemplation before asking, or a more vulnerable delivery.

Overall, the dialogue is functional and conveys the necessary information, but it lacks depth, nuance, and opportunities for subtext. It leans heavily on exposition and could benefit from more indirect characterization and emotional resonance.

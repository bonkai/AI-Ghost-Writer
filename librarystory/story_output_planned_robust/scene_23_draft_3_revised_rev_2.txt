The workshop smelled of oiled steel and dried herbs, a fragile peace disturbed by the insistent rapping at the door. Seris ran a honing stone along the edge of a scalpel, the rhythmic rasp a deliberate counterpoint. Rhys, ostensibly organizing charts, kept glancing towards the door, his hand drifting near a drawer.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her voice a low current.

“A merchant,” a gravelly voice replied. “Looking for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask.”

Rhys exchanged a fleeting glance with Seris. This felt…off. He opened the door to reveal a man swallowed by shadow, a wide-brimmed hat obscuring his features. Only his eyes were visible – sharp, assessing, unsettlingly still. The air around him carried the scent of woodsmoke and old metal.

“I understand you have charts of the Firepeak region,” the man said, his voice a rasp against stone. He deposited a heavy pouch onto the workbench, the clink of coins stark in the small space. “I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps, particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys’s gaze narrowed, studying the man. “What *kind* of formations?”

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin smile. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” His eyes drifted to Seris, lingering on the flex of muscle beneath her tunic. “I’ve heard whispers of your…research. The Sunstone Bloom. A beautiful flower, but hardly worth risking life and limb for.”

Seris didn’t meet his gaze. She traced the edge of a botanical illustration with a calloused fingertip, a slow, deliberate movement. “We’re simply botanists, cataloging rare species. Our work is purely academic.”

The merchant chuckled, a dry sound. “Botanists who happen to be interested in ancient legends and forgotten pathways. Don’t insult my intelligence.” He tapped the pouch. “But we *can* discuss terms.”

Rhys’s eyes met Seris’s, a silent plea for understanding. He didn't need to voice the question – *what do we do?* – it hung between them, a taut thread.

Seris didn’t return his gaze. She slowly lowered her hand, not to her side, but to rest it flat on the workbench, palm down. A subtle signal, almost imperceptible. *Patience. Let him talk.*

“We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice unwavering, a cool counterpoint to the merchant’s energy. “But we know someone who might.” She finally met the merchant’s gaze, her eyes the color of flint, assessing. “And that someone…charges a premium.”

A beat of silence stretched, thick and tense. The merchant’s eyes narrowed, studying Seris, gauging her resolve. He didn’t like being played, but he recognized a kindred spirit. 

“Interesting,” he said, his voice a low purr. “Perhaps we *can* discuss terms.” He gestured towards a stool with a gloved hand. “Shall we?”

*   **Repetitive phrasing:** The phrase "separate fact from folklore" is used twice within a short span of dialogue (Rhys & Seris). While it establishes a talking point, it feels a little clunky. Consider varying the wording – “distinguish truth from legend,” “verify the historical basis,” etc.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Valerius):** Valerius’s warnings are *very* direct. While effective, consider layering more subtext. Instead of explicitly warning about the dangers of Firepeak, he could speak about the *people* who have disappeared – a lost expedition, a local guide who vanished. This would create a more unsettling atmosphere and allow Seris/Rhys to subtly push back or probe.
*   **Kaelen’s Commentary:** Kaelen’s internal commentary is frequent. While it establishes the dynamic and tension, it *almost* feels like the scene is telling us how to feel rather than *showing* us. Consider reducing the frequency of Kaelen’s observations, allowing Seris’s actions and reactions to convey the tension. Let the dialogue and Seris’s internal state do more of the work.
*   **Rhys's Smoothness:** Rhys is consistently "smooth" and quick on the uptake. While consistent characterization, it borders on *too* perfect. A slight hesitation, a barely perceptible flicker of nervousness, would make him feel more human and raise the stakes.
*   **Valerius's Smile:** Describing the smile as "not reaching his eyes" is a bit of a cliché. While it conveys the message, consider a more unique description – a tightening of his lips, a coldness around the corners of his mouth, a predatory gleam.
*   **Indirect Characterization (Valerius):** The mention of sandalwood cologne feels a bit…on the nose. It's a shortcut to establish power and control. Consider *showing* his power through his actions, his gaze, his deliberate pauses. The cologne isn't *necessary*.
*   **Seris's "Preparedness":** Seris stating "We're prepared for any eventuality" feels a bit defiant and unnatural, given the context. It's a strong statement, but doesn't quite land believably. A more subtle response – a firm nod, a steady gaze – would be more effective.

*   **Repetitive Internal Dialogue:** The character’s internal questions (“What am I?”, “Soon what?”) are repeated without much variation or progression. While understandable given his disorientation, it feels stagnant after a while. Consider showing his internal struggle through *action* or shifts in his awareness rather than constantly posing questions.
*   **The Presence's Voice:** The descriptions of the Presence’s voice (“a feeling, a resonance,” “dry, rustling sound”) are good, but the *content* of its responses is often overly philosophical and vague. While a sense of ancient detachment is desired, it borders on being unhelpful. Try grounding some of its answers in concrete imagery or specifics related to the Library, even if it doesn't reveal everything. For example, instead of "We do not ‘want’," perhaps something like, "Desire is a current that erodes the bindings of memory."
*   **“Listen” as a Motif:** The repeated emphasis on “listening” is effective, but it risks becoming heavy-handed. Vary how this directive is delivered. Perhaps the Library *shows* him something instead of constantly telling him to listen.
*   **Passive Character:** The character is almost entirely reactive. He asks questions, receives answers, and then…feels things. While appropriate for initial disorientation, he needs to begin *doing* something more proactive to drive the scene forward. Even small attempts to manipulate the Library, beyond just pushing at pages, would add agency.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The Presence’s responses could reveal more about the Library’s nature and its history through subtle word choices and phrasing. Right now, it feels a bit too neutral.
*   **"Soon" and "Patience" - Frustratingly Vague:** These responses, while creating tension, feel like stalling tactics. Consider giving the character *some* small piece of information, even a misleading one, to keep him engaged and the reader invested. The current level of ambiguity is bordering on frustrating.

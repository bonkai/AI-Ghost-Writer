*   **Initial Atmosphere (Paragraphs 1-3):** The opening establishes a strong sense of mystery and foreboding, but lingers slightly too long on description. While evocative, the extended focus on the chamber’s atmosphere before any real action begins slows the momentum. Consider condensing the first two paragraphs for a quicker entry into the discovery of the key.
*   **Key Discovery & Reveal (Paragraphs 4-6):** The discovery of the key is well-paced, with the energy surge and incomplete feeling adding immediate intrigue. However, the *successive* mental revelations from Kaelen (ward, then Valerius's orchestration) feel slightly stacked. Space these out, perhaps interweaving a bit of Seris's physical reaction/assessment between each mental message to allow the reader to process the increasing stakes.
*   **Confrontation Build-Up (Paragraphs 7-9):** The entrance of Valerius and the cloaked figure is effective, but the description of the soldiers’ “alert, practiced” stances feels a bit static. Instead of *telling* us they're ready, show it through a subtle action or detail (a hand tightening on a weapon, a shift in weight).
*   **Dialogue & Tension (Paragraphs 10-13):** The dialogue between Seris and Valerius is solid, but the reveals regarding the Obsidian Circle and the key’s true purpose are delivered in quick succession. This diminishes their impact. Consider spreading these revelations out further, perhaps revealing the symbol on the glove *after* a beat of tension and a question from Seris.
*   **Climax & Resolution (Paragraphs 14-16):** The final reveal of the ritual site and the blood offering is a strong cliffhanger. However, the pacing feels a little rushed getting there. The transition from Valerius's demand to Seris's defiant response could benefit from a brief beat of internal conflict or a more pronounced physical reaction.

Overall, the scene is well-structured, but the tension could be heightened by strategically spacing out the reveals and adding more dynamic action beats. The tendency to deliver information through Kaelen’s thoughts, while efficient, sometimes sacrifices immediate emotional impact.

*   **Repetitive Questioning:** The character repeatedly asks “Where am I?” and “Between what?” while receiving increasingly abstract answers. While this conveys panic, it starts to feel like a loop. Consider *showing* the desperation through internal monologue or attempts to manipulate the environment (if possible) instead of *telling* it through repeated questions.
*   **Lack of Character Voice:** The protagonist's internal thoughts and projected questions are very… neutral. Given the situation (sudden, terrifying digital displacement), there should be more raw emotion, frustration, or even technical jargon bleeding into the projections. A gamer/programmer would likely have a different internal/external voice than someone else.
*   **Abstract Responses – Opportunity for Subtext:** The entity's responses (“Aware,” “Between,” “Between worlds”) are intentionally vague, which is fine. However, consider layering *how* those impressions are delivered. Is there a feeling of superiority? Pity? Curiosity? Adding emotional nuance to the impressions will make the entity more compelling and the dialogue more impactful. Right now, it feels a bit flat.
*   **“Data Stream” Description – Borderline Info-Dump:** The description of the scent of aged paper as a “data stream of cellulose and ink” feels a bit forced and overly technical, especially in the context of a panicked, disoriented consciousness. It’s trying too hard to connect the digital and physical. Simplify this – focus on the *feeling* of the scent, not its composition.
*   **“Brittle”/“Corrupted” File Language:** While consistent with the digital theme, describing thoughts as "brittle" or "corrupted" feels a little on-the-nose. Show the character’s deteriorating mental state through fragmented thoughts or difficulty forming coherent projections, rather than explicitly stating it.

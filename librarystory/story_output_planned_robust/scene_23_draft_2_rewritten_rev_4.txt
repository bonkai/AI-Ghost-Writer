The workshop smelled of oiled steel and dried herbs. A sharp rap at the door broke the quiet. Seris ran a honing stone along a scalpel, the rasp deliberate. Rhys, ostensibly organizing charts, kept glancing at the door, his hand tightening and relaxing against a drawer.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her voice low, gaze fixed on the sharpening stone.

“A merchant. Looking for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask,” a gravelly voice replied.

Rhys exchanged a quick glance with Seris. He opened the door to reveal a man swallowed by shadow, a wide-brimmed hat obscuring his features. Only his eyes were visible – sharp, assessing, unsettlingly still. The air carried woodsmoke and old metal, and Rhys felt a sudden chill.

“I understand you have charts of the Firepeak region,” the man said, his voice a rasp against stone. He deposited a heavy pouch onto the workbench, the clink of coins stark. “I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps, particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys narrowed his gaze. “What *kind* of formations?”

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin smile. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” His eyes drifted to Seris, lingering on the flex of muscle beneath her tunic. “You’ve both ventured deep into those peaks, haven't you?”

Seris didn’t meet his gaze. She traced a botanical illustration with a calloused fingertip. “We’re botanists, cataloging rare species. Our work is purely academic.”

The merchant chuckled, a dry, rasping sound. “Botanists who happen to know the old paths. Don’t insult my intelligence.” He tapped the pouch. “But we *can* discuss terms. A generous cut for your silence, perhaps?”

Rhys’s eyes met Seris’s, a silent plea. *What do we do?*

Seris lowered her hand, resting it flat on the workbench, palm down, fingers spread. A subtle signal. Her breathing shallowed. *Patience. Let him talk.*

“We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice unwavering. “But we know someone who might.” She finally met the merchant’s gaze, her eyes the color of flint. “And that someone…charges a premium. Information like that isn’t cheap.”

A beat of silence stretched, thick and tense. The merchant’s eyes narrowed, studying Seris, gauging her resolve. “Indeed. And what *kind* of premium are we talking about?”

“Something that reflects the danger involved in acquiring it,” Seris replied, a muscle ticking in her jaw.

“Interesting,” the merchant said, his voice a low purr. “Perhaps we *can* discuss terms.” He gestured towards a stool with a gloved hand. “Shall we?”

“Seris?”

She whirled, heart hammering. Hemlock stood framed in the doorway, bracing himself, a stillness clinging to him.

Seris offered a curt nod, gaze fixed on the sphere. An inexplicable pull resonated within her, deep in her bones. She extended a hand, fingers hovering above the cool surface. A tremor ran through the stone as her fingertips brushed it, a vibration climbing her arm and settling in her chest – not unpleasant, but *awakening*.

The world shifted. Fractured glimpses of time – cities carved into mountains crumbling with age, canyons choked with luminous flora – pulsed with forgotten life, mirroring her quickening pulse. Coiled around a basalt peak, a colossal serpentine creature stirred, scales shimmering like a thousand sunsets. Awe dissolved into primal fear. 

Then, a single, ancient eye met hers – reptilian, gold flecks like trapped starlight. It didn’t merely *see* her; it bored *through* her, stripping away pretense. A resonance bloomed in her chest, a hollow ache mirroring the creature’s weariness. It felt…familiar. Not a memory, but a recognition. She stumbled back, bracing a hand against the wall.

“Seris?” Hemlock’s voice, sharp with concern, cut through the vision. He entered the chamber, his gaze sweeping over the sphere, then locking onto her face. “What do you see?”

She squeezed her eyes shut, the image of the eye seared onto her eyelids. When she opened them, the visions had receded, leaving a coldness in her chest. “Cities…a serpent…it felt real. Not a vision, but…a memory. One that isn't mine.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint smile. He reached out, fingers hovering over the sphere, then drew back. “Some places remember. Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, gaze locking with hers, probing. “And some people…are more attuned. What did the serpent *feel* like?”

She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her. “Old. Tired. And…knowing. Like it had been waiting.”

Hemlock studied her intently. “Be careful. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting*.” A subtle shift in his gaze suggested he knew more than he let on. “Do you feel…changed?”

She looked back at the sphere. For a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths—a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns, a single, golden eye reflecting her own. The eye didn’t blink. It *watched*. And in its depths, she glimpsed not a reflection of herself, but a flicker of something ancient, something *knowing*. A silent acknowledgement.

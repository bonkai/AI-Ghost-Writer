## Scene 19

The library had emptied for the night. Pools of warm light from a handful of lamps barely touched the towering shelves, leaving the upper reaches lost in shadow. Seris sat at their worktable, not sketching, not reading, but acutely aware of the weight of Valerius’s scrutiny – a cold pressure on her skin. A nearby candle flickered.

A subtle disturbance – not a sound, but a shift in the dust motes dancing in a lamplight beam – caught her eye. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed on a nearby shelf, and a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table. The ink hadn’t fully dried, carrying a faint scent of parchment and something older. It was a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, not as a cartographer would render it, but as a *being* – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power.

Seris traced the charcoal lines. A fleeting impression brushed her mind – immense age, stone remembering centuries. *Beautiful,* she thought, and the impression intensified, resonating with shared appreciation. The air around the sketch warmed.

The book shifted again. *It remembers,* a thought formed, clear and distinct, woven into her consciousness. Not a response, but an offering. She traced the lines. “What do you mean?” she breathed, barely a whisper.

The page blurred, the charcoal writhing. *The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth, Seris. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.*

“Lost?” she echoed, questioning.

Silence stretched, broken only by the lamps. She felt his focus, sifting through forgotten lore. A fragmented image flickered in her mind – a vast, crumbling library, then a single, brightly lit doorway. *Remembrance.*

“Remembrance?” she repeated aloud, the sound startling. The image faded, leaving warmth on her skin.

*The Order didn’t just forbid magic, Seris. They forbade remembering. They feared the past. Firepeak…it remembers everything. And the Bloom…it can unlock those memories.* 

A shiver crawled down her spine. This wasn’t about a rare healing plant. This was far older, far more dangerous.

“Valerius…” she breathed, the name heavy on her tongue. “He knows, doesn’t he? That we’re looking for more than just a flower?”

A book slammed shut on a nearby shelf, the sound echoing sharply. His frustration was palpable, controlled. *He suspects. He’s a guardian of the forgetting, Seris. He’ll do anything to protect it.*

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

A quiet strength settled over her mind. *Always.*

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch. It wasn’t a drawing, but a warning. A challenge. Then, a delicate fragrance – crimson, perfectly formed – momentarily filled the air, superimposed over the sketch. Not an image *sent*, but a shared perception. They weren’t alone. But as the fragrance faded, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. The scent of dust and old paper was suddenly…metallic.

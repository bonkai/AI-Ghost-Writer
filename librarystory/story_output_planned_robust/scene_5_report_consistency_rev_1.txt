This is a strong piece of writing. The scene is well-established, atmospheric, and builds tension effectively. Here's a breakdown of why it works and some minor suggestions (mostly stylistic, as the core is solid):

**Strengths:**

* **Atmosphere & Setting:** The description of the Atheneum is excellent. The details (stained glass, shadows, crumbling relic) create a palpable sense of age, mystery, and unease. You've successfully conveyed the feeling of being watched and the library's "alive" quality.
* **Tension & Suspense:** The slow build of tension is masterful. The initial prickling sensation, the cryptic responses of Hemlock, and the increasingly intense mental impressions all contribute to a feeling that something is definitely *off*.
* **Characterization (Seris):** Seris is well-defined as cautious, observant, and resourceful. Her internal reactions (heart pounding, trembling fingers) feel authentic and ground the scene. She's clearly trained and competent, but not invulnerable.
* **Intriguing Mystery:** The "Veiled Cities," the fragmented memories, and the serpent's eye create a compelling mystery that pulls the reader in. The intentionality of the mental impressions is a great twist.
* **Pacing:** The pacing is excellent. It's not rushed, but it doesn't drag either. The slow reveal of information keeps the reader engaged.
* **Show, Don't Tell:** You consistently *show* Seris's reactions and the atmosphere, rather than simply *telling* us about them. This makes the scene much more immersive.

**Minor Suggestions (mostly stylistic):**

* **"Initiate" Repetition:** The use of "Initiate" feels a little repetitive. While it establishes her status, consider varying the phrasing occasionally (e.g., "the young woman," "Seris").
* **Hemlock's Stillness:**  While his stillness is unnerving, you could subtly *show* it more. Perhaps a detail about his posture, the unblinking quality of his gaze, or the way he doesn't breathe.  (e.g., "Hemlock hadn’t moved, his shoulders rigid as stone.")
* **"Intentional" - Slightly Redundant:**  When you state "This was… *intentional*," it feels a little on-the-nose. The reader is already likely to be inferring that the impressions aren't random.  Consider letting the mystery linger a bit longer. Perhaps just: "This wasn't a simple echo of the past."
* **Serpent Imagery:** The serpent image is good, but consider expanding on it subtly later in the story. Perhaps a recurring motif or a symbol that holds deeper meaning.
* **"Tucked the image…into the back of her mind"** - This phrase is slightly clunky. Perhaps: "She filed the image of the serpent away, a strange puzzle piece in the growing mystery."

This is a very strong scene. It's well-written, atmospheric, and effectively builds tension. The mystery is intriguing, and the characterization is solid. The suggestions above are minor and mostly stylistic; the core of the scene is excellent.

*   **Exposition & “Anticipating Questions”:** Kaelen frequently *tells* Seris things she hasn’t explicitly asked about, framed as “anticipating her question.” This feels unnatural and overly convenient. For example, “Kaelen said, anticipating her question.” It robs Seris of agency and makes the dialogue feel like an info-dump disguised as conversation. *Suggestion:* Let Seris *ask* the questions. Let her draw conclusions, even if they're slightly off, before Kaelen corrects/expands.
*   **Repetitive Emphasis on Being Watched/Guided:** The constant reiteration of “being watched,” “being guided,” and Marius’s intent feels heavy-handed. While establishing paranoia is important, it's done through *telling* rather than *showing* through character interaction. *Suggestion:* Subtlety. Let the *actions* of others, and Seris's reactions to them, convey the sense of being monitored.
*   **Kaelen’s Voice:** Kaelen’s dialogue is consistently informative but lacks personality. He feels like a plot device delivering exposition rather than a fully realized character. *Suggestion:* Inject more emotion, even subtle frustration or weariness, into his lines. A slight quirk of speech or a characteristic way of phrasing things would help.
*   **“Pity” Encounter:** The description of the man's expression as "pity" and the subsequent analysis of it ("It wasn’t concern; it was…expectation.") is too much internal monologue disguised as observation. *Suggestion:* Show Seris’s reaction to the man’s gaze. Let her *feel* the wrongness, rather than explicitly labeling it. A physical reaction (a tightening of her grip, a chill running down her spine) would be more effective.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Seris's reactions are described, but her *dialogue* doesn’t reveal much about her personality. She’s mostly reactive. *Suggestion:* Give her lines that showcase her intelligence, her determination, or her vulnerabilities. Even a sarcastic remark or a pointed question would add depth.
*   **"Let them come."** Kaelen's line is a bit cliché and doesn't feel organic to the scene. It's a bold statement, but lacks the weight it should carry.

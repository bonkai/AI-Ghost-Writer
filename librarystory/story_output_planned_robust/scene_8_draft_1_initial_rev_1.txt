## Scene 8 - Draft 1

Seris returned to the alcove an hour later, ostensibly to examine a shelf of treatises on herbalism. The section was deliberately chosen – remote, overlooked, and offering a clear view of the small, circular table she’d claimed as her temporary workspace. She needed the isolation, and the control. The blank-covered volume, still tucked inside her satchel, felt like a weight against her hip.

She’d spent the intervening hour observing Hemlock. He hadn’t left his post, but his gaze seemed to drift more often towards her alcove. Not a direct stare, but a subtle tracking, a constant assessment. He knew she hadn’t abandoned her search.

Now, she needed to test a theory. 

Seris pulled a slim volume on medicinal fungi from the shelf, opening it at random. She began to “read,” her eyes scanning the text, but her attention was focused on the periphery, on the subtle shifts in the air, on the feeling she’d experienced earlier. She needed to recreate the… resonance.

She flipped a page. Nothing. Another. Still nothing. Frustration gnawed at her. Was it the book itself? The location? Or had it been a fleeting anomaly?

Then, she remembered something. The initial touch hadn’t been a deliberate search. She’d brushed against the spine accidentally. She deliberately, but gently, ran her fingertips across the worn leather of the fungi book. 

Nothing.

She tried again, applying slightly more pressure. Still nothing.

Then, almost unconsciously, she began to *think* about the serpent. The intricate carvings, the sense of hidden stone, the weight of forgotten history. She focused all her mental energy on reconstructing the image, willing the resonance to return.

Suddenly, a page in the fungi book seemed to… *shift*. Not physically, but in her perception. The printed words blurred, then resolved into a different arrangement, a single line highlighted in what looked like faded gold ink.

*“The stone remembers the coil.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. It wasn’t printed in the book. She was *seeing* it, superimposed on the text. A phantom line, a whisper from… somewhere.

She quickly scanned the surrounding pages. Nothing. The line appeared only on that single page, triggered by her focus, by her *thought*. 

“Interesting,” she murmured, her voice barely audible.

She flipped to another page. Nothing. She tried again, focusing on the serpent, on the image of the hidden city. This time, a different line appeared, equally ephemeral.

*“Where the sun sleeps, the path begins.”*

This wasn’t simply reading. It was… communication. A response to her thoughts, filtered through the book.

She glanced up, half-expecting to see Hemlock watching her. He was still at his post, seemingly engrossed in a large tome, but Seris could feel his attention, a subtle pressure against her mind. He knew something was happening.

She needed to be careful. She couldn’t risk drawing too much attention. She decided to try a different approach. She didn’t *think* about the serpent. Instead, she simply *felt* it. The cold stone, the intricate carvings, the weight of centuries. She allowed the image to wash over her, a passive recipient of its energy.

The page in front of her blurred again, then resolved. This time, instead of words, a small, stylized image appeared – a winding path leading towards a shadowed peak, a mountain silhouetted against a setting sun. 

It was a map. A fragmented, symbolic map, but a map nonetheless.

“Remarkable,” she breathed, her fingers tracing the outline of the mountain.

Suddenly, a voice cut through the quiet of the alcove.

“Finding what you seek, Initiate?”

Seris startled, quickly closing the book. She turned to see Hemlock standing a few feet away, his expression unreadable. He hadn't approached silently.

“Just… researching,” she said, forcing a casual tone. “Herbal remedies can be quite complex.”

Hemlock’s gaze drifted to the book in her hands. “Indeed. And sometimes, the most potent remedies are hidden in plain sight.”

He paused, his eyes locking with hers. “I’ve noticed you’ve been spending a considerable amount of time with that particular volume. Is there something of interest within its pages?”

Seris met his gaze, her heart pounding. She couldn't lie. Not to him. Not completely.

“It… speaks to me,” she said, choosing her words carefully. “It reveals things… that aren’t explicitly written.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a subtle, knowing smile. “Ah. So you, too, can hear the whispers of the Atheneum.”

He took a step closer, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper. “Be warned, Initiate. The Atheneum offers knowledge, but it demands a price. And some secrets… are best left undisturbed.”

*   **Kaelen’s Voice:** While the shift to internalized responses and single words is *mostly* effective, it risks becoming *too* minimalist. The repeated “felt” sensations (“felt her heart pounding,” “felt sensations”) become noticeable and slightly clunky. Vary the phrasing to avoid repetition. Consider *showing* the effect of his presence on Seris (a sudden chill, a tightening in her chest) instead of *telling* us she feels it.
*   **Seris’s Repetition:** Seris asks a *lot* of questions. While inquisitive is good, it borders on interrogation. Consider having her *react* to Kaelen’s revelations more often, allowing the information to sink in and prompting further questions organically.
*   **“Protecting Us?” Line:** This feels a little on-the-nose. Seris articulating the possibility the Church is *protecting* people is a bit direct. Could this be conveyed through her expression or internal monologue instead? Perhaps she just *thinks* the question, rather than saying it aloud.
*   **“Why me?” & Response:** The response, *"Fragments drawn together by your presence. What remains of the Order, seeking someone who will listen,"* is a bit lengthy and explanatory for a fragmented consciousness. It feels like exposition delivered *as* dialogue. Could this be shortened to something more enigmatic? ("A resonance… a need.")
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Church's Motives):** The dialogue establishes the Church is suppressing the Order. However, there's little nuance. Is it purely fear of power? Religious zealotry? Political control? Injecting a hint of complexity into Kaelen's descriptions of the Church could add depth.
*   **Indirect Characterization (Seris):** While Seris is inquisitive, we don't get a strong sense of *her* personality beyond that. Her reactions to the revelations are fairly neutral. Injecting a bit more emotional response (fear, determination, grief) could make her more relatable.



Overall, the dialogue is functional and moves the plot forward, but it lacks subtlety and emotional depth. It relies a bit too heavily on direct questioning and exposition.

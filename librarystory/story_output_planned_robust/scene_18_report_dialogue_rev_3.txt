*   **Subtext & Deflection:** The dialogue is *very* direct. While the tension is palpable (thanks to the stage directions), the characters state their concerns rather than *showing* them through layered conversation. Rhys’s explanations (“We understand the risks…”) feel like he's trying to convince *himself* as much as Valerius. This could be strengthened by having him offer a detail that's *too* specific, or a reassurance that rings hollow.
*   **Valerius's Voice:** Valerius’s lines are good, but lean heavily into vague threats. While effective, consider adding a line where he *appears* supportive, then subtly undermines it. A seemingly innocuous question about their research that reveals his underlying skepticism would be chilling.
*   **Seris’s Response:** Seris is a bit too composed. Her "carefully neutral" expression and direct explanation feel like a shield. A slight crack in her composure – a hesitation, a too-quick response – would make her more vulnerable and the scene more engaging.
*   **Rhys & Seris - Shared Apprehension:** The "fleeting pressure" on Seris’s arm is a good start, but the dialogue doesn't quite support it. Their conversation *after* Valerius leaves is a missed opportunity. A brief, coded exchange (“Did you notice…?” “His eyes…”) would reinforce their shared unease and their understanding of the threat.
*   **Repetition:** The repeated emphasis on the dangers of Firepeak feels a little redundant. Valerius states it directly, then implies it with his "tests preparations" line. Condense this – let the implications do more of the work.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** The sandalwood cologne is a nice touch, but could be woven into the dialogue. Perhaps Seris subtly reacts to the scent (a slight wrinkle of her nose, a barely perceptible intake of breath).

Overall, the dialogue isn't *bad*, but it's functional rather than compelling. It establishes the conflict, but doesn't fully exploit the potential for subtext, nuance, and indirect characterization. It's a little too "on the nose" for a scene clearly meant to be fraught with tension and hidden agendas.

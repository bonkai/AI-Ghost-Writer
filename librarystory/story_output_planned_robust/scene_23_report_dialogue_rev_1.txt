*   **Rhys’s initial line (“Who is it?”)** feels a bit…functional. Given the established tension and their cautious nature, something more subtly wary might land better. Perhaps, “What is it?” or even just a raised eyebrow and a questioning glance before speaking.
*   **Merchant’s repeated emphasis on “unusual geological formations” and “anything out of place”** feels slightly repetitive. While establishing his interest is important, it's laid on a little thick. Consider streamlining this – perhaps focusing on *one* specific, intriguing detail instead.
*   **Rhys’s breath catching is a *telling* moment.** While not terrible, consider *showing* his reaction instead. A tightening of his jaw, a barely perceptible flinch, a hand instinctively moving towards…something.
*   **Seris’s line, “We’re simply botanists…”** is a standard denial. It's *okay*, but could be more layered. Perhaps she delivers it while *also* subtly examining the merchant, assessing his motives. The action *with* the line adds complexity.
*   **The silent communication between Rhys and Seris is strong**, but the stage direction "*what do we do?* – it hung heavy between them” feels a little on-the-nose. Trust the reader to *feel* the tension and understand their unspoken question.
*   **Seris’s “Patience” signal feels a bit…clean.** It works, but a truly subtle signal might be even more ambiguous – a slight shift in posture, a barely perceptible tightening of her lips, something that could be interpreted multiple ways.
*   **The merchant’s “Interesting” feels a little flat as a response.** It’s a placeholder. Consider a line that reveals more about *his* reaction to Seris’s veiled threat/negotiation. Does he seem amused? Intrigued? Annoyed?
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is *good*. It’s tense, atmospheric, and generally serves its purpose. The issues are mostly minor – opportunities to elevate the subtlety and nuance, and trust the reader to infer more.

*   **Kaelen's Mental Voice:** While the *concept* of a mental voice is interesting and established, it’s *overused*. Nearly every line from Kaelen is flagged as a “mental voice.” This quickly becomes distracting and diminishes its impact. Reserve it for truly crucial, deeply personal, or hidden thoughts. Otherwise, let him *speak* normally.

*   **Repetitive Worry:** Kaelen's constant warnings (“Be careful,” “Some memories are best left forgotten,” “We’ve walked into a trap”) feel redundant. Show his worry through *action* or subtle changes in his demeanor, rather than repeatedly stating it. The “I should have stopped you” line is particularly weak; it’s a telling line, not a showing one.

*   **Seris's Reactions:** Seris’s responses are often very direct (“What is it?” “What kind of memories?” “What did they *do* to the people who remembered?”). While clarity is good, consider layering in more internal reaction or a beat of processing before she asks. This would make her feel more reactive and less like an interrogator.

*   **"Unmade" – Info Dump Potential:** The explanation of “unmaking” is lengthy and delivered as direct exposition. While horrifying, it lacks impact because it's *told* rather than *felt*. Consider revealing this fate through fragmented memories *experienced* by Seris as she touches the book, or through a brief, evocative description of the consequences, leaving the full horror to the reader's imagination.

*   **"This isn’t just about the Sunstone anymore…"** This line feels predictable and on-the-nose. The tension is already high; the realization doesn't need to be explicitly stated. Let the weight of the situation sink in through their actions and the atmosphere.

*   **Subtext Opportunity – Kaelen's Hesitation:** Kaelen’s hesitation to bring Seris here is mentioned, but not explored. A brief, tense exchange *before* they even enter the chamber could establish this conflict and add layers to their relationship.

*   **Indirect Characterization – Kaelen's Grief:** While the historian backstory is good, it feels a little flat. Show Kaelen's grief through a subtle physical reaction (a clenched jaw, a faraway look in his eyes) or a brief, choked-off sentence when he mentions the historian.



Overall, the dialogue is functional, but it lacks nuance and relies too heavily on telling rather than showing. There's potential to deepen the emotional impact and create a more immersive experience by layering in subtext, internal reactions, and more evocative descriptions.

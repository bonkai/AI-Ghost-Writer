*   **Over-Explanation & Metaphor Reliance:** The dialogue *heavily* relies on the tapestry/thread metaphor. While evocative initially, it’s repeated and becomes a crutch. Consider showing the danger/corruption of the Echo through *effects* Seris experiences, rather than constantly *telling* us about it. For example, instead of "Certain fragments unravel the mind," perhaps Seris experiences a fleeting, disorienting vision or a surge of conflicting emotions.
*   **Kaelen's Voice:** Kaelen's delivery is consistently *telling* rather than *revealing*. He explains, explains, explains. This makes him feel more like an expository device than a character. Consider how to inject more personality or emotional weight into his responses. Even as a disembodied voice, he could have *mannerisms* in how he delivers information.
*   **Seris's Reactions:** Seris often asks direct questions that Kaelen answers directly. This lacks subtext and tension. Could some questions be implied? Could Kaelen deflect or answer with a question of his own? Her final thought, while effective as internal monologue, feels disconnected from the dialogue.
*   **"Remember" - Cliché:** The final word, "Remember," is a bit of a tired trope. While it *works* thematically, consider a more unique and impactful final message.
*   **Pacing & Pauses:** While the pauses are noted in the stage directions, the dialogue itself doesn’t *build* to those pauses organically. The information feels delivered in chunks, rather than unfolding naturally.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** We learn very little about *why* the Order valued listening over wielding power. A small detail about their philosophy would add depth.

Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks nuance and relies too heavily on exposition. It needs more subtext, more character-specific voice, and a reduction in direct explanation.

## Scene 21 - Draft 3

The air in the workshop tasted of dried herbs and mountain dust, a familiar comfort now tainted with a metallic edge. A chill clung to the space even after Inquisitor Marius had departed. 

“He’s probing,” Kaelen breathed, and Seris’s hand instinctively went to the small, worn pouch containing Elara’s last portrait, her fingers tracing the faded image. *He suspects more than just academic interest.* The touch was grounding, a reminder of everything at stake.

Rhys shifted subtly, his hand resting near the hilt of his climbing axe, but his gaze remained fixed on the closed door. “The Bloom’s properties are well-documented, Inquisitor. Its potential for healing is significant.” He delivered the line with a practiced neutrality, a shield against scrutiny.

Marius’s gaze flickered to Rhys, then back to Seris. “Healing? A noble pursuit. Though I confess, I find it curious that a scholar of your…reputation would dedicate herself to such a commonplace goal.” The words were silken, laced with condescension.

Seris clenched her jaw, the muscle jumping almost imperceptibly. He was circling, testing her defenses. “The pursuit of knowledge is never commonplace, Inquisitor.” She kept her voice even, forcing a steadiness she didn’t entirely feel. A nervous habit – smoothing a wrinkle in her skirt – threatened to surface, and she consciously suppressed it.

“Indeed.” Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging every detail. “Tell me, Scholar. What are you *truly* seeking on Firepeak Mountain? Is it knowledge…or something else?”

Rhys’s hand tightened on his axe, the steel gleaming faintly in the dim light. *He’s afraid,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in Seris’s mind. *Afraid of what you might find, and what that might mean for the Order.* The realization settled like a stone in her gut. This wasn’t just a routine inspection.

Seris forced herself to meet Marius’s gaze, a cold determination hardening within her. She wouldn’t be intimidated. She wouldn’t let him see her desperation. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.” She kept her expression carefully blank, but allowed a flicker of defiance to enter her eyes.

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile, devoid of warmth. “A convenient answer. But I suspect…you are not being entirely forthcoming.” He paused, then lowered his voice, a hint of something dangerous in his tone. “The Bloom…it remembers. It holds echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep.”

He stepped back, his gaze sweeping over their cluttered workspace, lingering on a dried specimen of a rare mountain moss. “I will be watching you. Both of you.”

Rhys’s hand tightened further on his axe, his knuckles white. Seris reached for the pouch containing Elara’s portrait, her fingers trembling slightly as she traced the faded image. She wouldn’t let him take that away, either.

“We have no secrets, Inquisitor.” She said the words with deliberate conviction, projecting an unwavering confidence she barely felt.

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing her with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has secrets, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to sacrifice to keep them buried?” He offered a curt nod, then turned and swept out of the workshop, leaving behind a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being watched.

The silence that followed was heavy. Rhys began to methodically sharpen his axe, the rhythmic rasp a small rebellion against the lingering tension. Seris stared at the closed door, her heart pounding. The Inquisitor hadn't found any secrets…yet. But she knew, with a chilling certainty, that he would be back. And when he returned, he wouldn't be seeking information. He would be seeking control. She clutched the pouch containing Elara’s portrait, the worn fabric a small comfort. She wouldn’t let him win.

*   **Seris’s initial line (“The pursuit of knowledge…”)** feels a little *too* poised and philosophical given the immediate pressure of an Inquisitor’s visit. It reads as a prepared statement rather than a spontaneous response. Consider something more immediate and subtly defiant, perhaps a simple, “We explore all avenues, Inquisitor.”
*   **Rhys’s non-verbal communication is stronger than his verbal.** He’s doing a lot of *showing* (axe cleaning, positioning) but remains silent. While effective, it leans heavily on description. Consider giving him *one* short, pointed line – something that doesn’t reveal information but establishes his attitude (e.g., a curt, “Is that all, Inquisitor?” while continuing to clean the axe).
*   **Marius’s dialogue is strong overall, but the “debts” line feels a bit cliché.** While it serves a purpose, it lacks nuance. Explore a more specific, veiled threat that hints at what he knows or suspects.
*   **The internal dialogue (Kaelen’s voice) is slightly overused.** While useful for establishing tension, it's happening frequently. Trust the *shown* tension more and reserve Kaelen’s voice for truly critical moments.
*   **Repetitive silent communication:** The extended eye contact and "silent understanding" between Seris and Rhys at the end, following the previous prolonged non-verbal exchanges, feels redundant. A brief, shared glance would be more impactful.
*   **Subtext opportunity:** Marius's comment about the Bloom remembering feels like a direct exposition dump. Instead of *telling* us about the Bloom’s history, show it through his reaction – perhaps a specific, involuntary flinch or a fleeting expression of something akin to fear.
*   **“Everyone has debts…”** – This feels like a stock villain line. Consider replacing it with a more personalized observation about Seris or Rhys – something that suggests he’s been investigating them specifically.

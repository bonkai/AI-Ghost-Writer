*   **Repetitive Internal State:** Seris frequently *states* her feelings of being watched/guided/anticipated. While internal monologue is fine, it’s becoming a crutch. Show, don't tell. For example, instead of “She couldn’t shake the feeling they weren’t being hunted, but *guided*,” show her scanning the crowds *looking* for guidance, or noticing patterns that suggest manipulation.
*   **Kaelen's Cryptic Responses:** Kaelen’s responses often feel deliberately vague ("They sought understanding, not control," "Firepeak isn’t just a destination. It’s a reckoning"). While mystery is good, it’s bordering on unhelpful. It creates a sense of him holding back information *from the reader* as much as from Seris. Consider adding a beat where Seris actively *challenges* his vagueness.
*   **"Playing a Game" Trope:** The "he's playing a game" line is a bit cliché. It doesn’t feel organic to the situation. Consider replacing it with a more specific observation about the man’s behavior.
*   **Seris's Deductions – Too Direct:** Seris’s repeated statements about what the man *wants* or *expects* feel too analytical. It’s like she's narrating the plot to Kaelen (and the reader). Subtext would be stronger. Instead of "It’s that he *wants* us to," show her noticing a subtle smirk, a calculated pause, or something that *implies* his intent.
*   **Lack of Emotional Range:** The dialogue is very focused on intellectual assessment of the situation. There's little emotional vulnerability or reaction *within* the dialogue itself. Consider adding moments of fear, frustration, or even anger to make the characters feel more real.
*   **"Reckoning" Feels Forced:** The line about Firepeak being a "reckoning" feels like foreshadowing delivered *as* dialogue. It doesn’t flow naturally.

Overall, the dialogue is functional, but leans heavily on stating information rather than revealing character or building tension through subtext. It needs more nuance and emotional depth.

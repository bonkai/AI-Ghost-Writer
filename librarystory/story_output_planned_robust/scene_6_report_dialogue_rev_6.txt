*   **Hemlock's Lack of Response:** Several times, you indicate Hemlock *doesn't* respond. While silence can be powerful, it happens *a lot* here. Consider if each instance is truly necessary, or if a minimal response (“Hmm,” a raised eyebrow) would be more effective. Too much silence reads as a narrative shortcut instead of intentional character choice.
*   **"Admiring the architecture" – Weak Lie:** This line feels *very* flimsy, even as the character intends it to be. It’s a classic tell. Consider a slightly more elaborate, desperate attempt at normalcy, or lean into the stammer and obviousness to show the character is flustered and a poor liar.
*   **"As are your…abilities, it seems."** – This feels a bit condescending and on-the-nose from Hemlock. It *tells* us he thinks her abilities are weak instead of *showing* it through his reactions. Subtlety would be more effective. Perhaps a simple, "A difficult task, even for someone skilled."
*   **"It doesn’t want to be found" – Internal Thought as Dialogue:** This line feels like an internal monologue accidentally spoken aloud. While characters do this, it lacks purpose here. It doesn’t advance the conversation or reveal anything new. Consider removing it or rephrasing it as a frustrated exclamation.
*   **Repetitive Physical Descriptions as Subtext:** You rely heavily on describing Hemlock *not* moving, his cold eyes, and subtle facial expressions. While these are good details, they become repetitive. Let his *actions* (or lack thereof) speak louder. A single, strong instance of his stillness is more impactful than repeated mentions.
*   **"Perhaps you should focus on secrets *willing* to be found."** – This line is good, but feels slightly late. It’s the most proactive Hemlock has been, and it would have more impact if delivered earlier, perhaps after her first failed attempt, rather than after a prolonged struggle.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Explore the power dynamic more through dialogue. Hemlock’s quiet observation and lack of assistance *show* his control, but consider adding a subtly patronizing tone or a veiled threat to heighten the tension.

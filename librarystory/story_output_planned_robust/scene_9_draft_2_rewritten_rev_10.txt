Dust motes danced in a single shaft of light. The air smelled of aged paper and a faint, coppery tang. Seris traced a finger along a crumbling tome, brittle leather flaking beneath her touch. She gestured not to a manuscript detailing the Church’s rise, but to a half-collapsed stack of Order texts, their bindings cracked and faded. 

She touched one, and a shower of dust erupted. “They didn’t erase the Order, Kaelen. They buried it. Not with stone, but with silence.” Seris closed her eyes, concentrating. “The Order’s magic wasn’t about *doing*. It was about listening – to the currents, to the Echo itself.”

A tremor ran through the alcove, rattling a clay pot on a high shelf. Seris didn’t acknowledge it, but her grip tightened on the book. “Imagine a cartographer, not drawing new borders, but understanding a landscape that shifts and breathes.”

“Listening to what?” Kaelen asked, a touch of impatience.

Seris’s brow furrowed. “The Echo isn’t an archive. It’s a reflection – shattered, but still resonant. Fragments of possibility, of what *was*, what *could be*… and what the Church fears.” She paced, then stopped. “Imagine a tapestry, not woven with intention, but formed from the threads of every moment. The Order sought to understand the pattern. The Church… wants to control it.”

“Fractured how?” Kaelen pressed.

Seris ran a hand over a crumbling spine. “Threads fraying, twisting… creating dissonance. Echoes bleeding into each other, distorting the truth. Like a melody played on a broken instrument – recognizable, but unsettling. A single discordant note… can unravel everything.”

“Unravel everything?” Kaelen echoed.

Seris nodded. “The Firepeaks are a nexus – a place where the veil between worlds is thin. The Order built a citadel there, not to *guard* knowledge, but to *listen* for what was being lost.”

A pressure touched Seris’s mind – not a voice, but urgency, desperation. *“A confluence. Fragments drawn together by your presence. What remains of the Order… seeking someone who will listen.”*

Seris swept her gaze across the alcove, dread tightening her chest. “Why me?”

*“The Church knows you’re close to the truth. They will be coming.”* The air grew frigid. A spectral image flickered in the swirling ink of an open tome – a hooded figure against a burning city, flames licking at a crumbling cathedral. It vanished as quickly as it appeared. 

The pages settled. A single word formed in the glowing ink: *“Remember.”* Beneath it, a second word: *“They lied.”* Then, silence – profound, heavy. The stone of the bookshelf felt subtly wrong, distorted. Seris wrapped her arms around herself, a cold spot on the stone sending a jolt up her arm. She pressed her hand against the stone, and a hairline crack widened in the wall behind it, revealing darkness. Dust motes swirled. A faint tremor ran through the bookshelf. A whisper seemed to emanate from the crack: *"The key is not to find the path, but to remember the song."*

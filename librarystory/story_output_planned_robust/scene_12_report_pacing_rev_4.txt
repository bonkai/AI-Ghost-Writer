*   **Initial Darkness & Scent:** The opening is strong, establishing atmosphere, but the description of the scent (“faintly floral”) feels slightly *off* – it doesn’t quite align with the rising tension and feels a bit decorative. Consider leaning into the metallic aspect to maintain a more unsettling tone.
*   **Valerius’s Entrance:** Excellent. The description of him *becoming* visible is far more impactful than simply entering.
*   **Dialogue & Loom Metaphor:** The dialogue is deliberately slow, and this works for building mystery, but it risks becoming *too* drawn out. The loom metaphor is good, but the pacing needs to be careful not to lose momentum before the reveal.
*   **Rising Tension – Prickling Sensation:** The prickling sensation is a good physical manifestation of Seris's unease, but it’s immediately followed by Valerius ordering her escort. This feels like a slight deflation of tension. Consider delaying the escort order by a beat or two, allowing the prickling sensation to linger and amplify.
*   **Guardian's Absorption of Light:** This is a fantastic detail! It’s visually striking and significantly heightens the tension. However, it's almost *too* quickly glossed over (“She barely registered…”). Give Seris (and the reader) a *fraction* of a second more to fully process this anomaly.
*   **Reveal & Final Paragraph:** The realization about the Aethel and Valerius is strong, and the final paragraph delivers a solid emotional impact. However, the pacing feels slightly rushed *after* the reveal. A single sentence or two expanding on Seris’s internal state (“A cold certainty settled…”) could allow the weight of the discovery to fully land.
*   **Overall:** The scene is well-structured, but the tension arc has a few subtle dips. It builds effectively, but could be tightened by delaying certain actions/reactions by a beat and allowing key details to resonate for a slightly longer duration.

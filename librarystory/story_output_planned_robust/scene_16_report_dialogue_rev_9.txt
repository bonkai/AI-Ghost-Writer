*   **“What do you see?”** – This feels a bit *too* direct, especially given the escalating strangeness. Rhys is perceptive; he *knows* something is happening to Seris beyond a simple dizzy spell. Consider something like, "Seris? What's happening?" or even a non-verbal cue followed by a question later.
*   **“It’s…the source,” she said, her voice strained, the words feeling given, not reasoned.** – The narration telling us the words are "given, not reasoned" is unnecessary and weakens the impact. *Show* us that through the dialogue itself. Perhaps a fragmented sentence, or a statement that doesn't quite connect logically. Instead of the narration, try something like, “The Firestone Mountains… it’s there.”
*   **Repetitive Concern:** Rhys asks a question and then immediately receives a full explanation. While realistic, it slows the momentum. Consider letting Seris’s physical reaction (sagging, trembling) *show* the severity of the vision, and then have her deliver the crucial information more concisely.
*   **“We have to go. Before it breaks free.”** – Again, a bit direct. The urgency is good, but it lacks nuance. Consider phrasing it as a desperate plea or a statement of grim inevitability. Something like, "We don't have much time. It's starting..." or "The Firestone Mountains…we have to get there."
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Rhys’s quiet support is excellent. However, a *tiny* line of dialogue acknowledging the strangeness of the vision (even just a murmured, "What *was* that?") would reinforce his perceptive nature and create a stronger connection with Seris.



Overall, the dialogue isn’t *bad*, but it leans towards functional rather than evocative. It delivers information but doesn’t fully capitalize on the dramatic tension of the scene. It could benefit from more subtext, fragmented phrasing, and a slightly less direct approach.

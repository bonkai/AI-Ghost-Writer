The air in the library pressed against Seris’s skin, a wrongness that settled deep in her bones. Dust motes hung suspended, almost frozen. A tremor ran through the stone, vibrating in her teeth, and she braced a hand against a towering bookshelf, feeling the ancient wood hum.

Rhys, studying a faded map, looked up, his brow furrowing as he registered her tension. He didn’t ask what was wrong, simply observed, his hand instinctively moving to rest lightly on the hilt of his dagger.

The library shifted, a ripple distorting the angles of the room at the periphery of her vision. The familiar scent of old parchment and beeswax was tainted with a metallic tang – ozone and something akin to blood. A weight descended, the feeling of ages pressing down. She sensed a network of energy beneath her feet, extending outwards, sustaining the room. Within it, a fragile harmony strained.

A discordant note fractured the harmony. A shadow crept into the light, and a force severed luminous threads, allowing darkness to seep through. The energy didn’t weep from destruction, but retreated, the loss echoing in Seris’s mind as a hollow ache.

She stumbled back, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue, pressing a hand to her temple. Rhys caught her arm, steadying her, his grip firm and silent.

Kaelen’s consciousness brushed against hers – not words, but raw sensation: the echoing resonance of a nexus, the weight of ancient power, a desperate plea. A flicker of icy dread, then an image – a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian, pulsing with power, but laced with hairline cracks, the light within flickering erratically. 

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts.

Rhys tightened his grip on her arm, his gaze searching her face.

She felt Kaelen’s panic – a desperate, wordless surge – the sense of something contained, weakening. The image sharpened – obsidian, stars, cracks widening. *Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…*

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus, and opened her eyes.

“Firestone Mountains?” Rhys asked, his voice tight with concern.

“It’s connected to the source of the network,” she said, her voice strained. “Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible. The seal is failing, and the Firestone Mountains…they’re where the cracks are widest.”

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within breaks free.”

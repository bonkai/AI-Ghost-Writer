## Scene 17 - Draft 3

The damp air clung to Seris’s skin, a chill laced with the scent of decay and metal. Dust motes danced in the fractured light, revealing crumbling shelves and a circular chamber steeped in silence. She ran a gloved hand along the cold stone, a prickling sensation raising the hairs on her neck – a resonance, not of age, but of *waiting*.

“What are we looking for?” Rhys asked, his voice low, eyes scanning the shadows. Impatience tightened the lines around his jaw.

Seris approached the ornate pedestal, her fingers tracing the weathered stone. A hidden seam yielded with a soft click, revealing the key. It wasn’t ornate, but thrummed with a faint energy. As she lifted it, a chill traced a path up her arm, and a fleeting image – a swirling vortex of violet and black – flashed behind her eyes. A metallic taste coated her tongue. She hesitated, a shadow crossing her face, then steeled her expression.

“What have we here?”

Seris and Rhys whirled around. Captain Valerius stood in the passage entrance, flanked by four soldiers. Behind him, shrouded in shadow, stood a cloaked figure, utterly still. The soldiers raised weapons, but Valerius held a hand, a calculated satisfaction on his face.

“I expected you sooner,” he said, his voice smooth. “You’ve found what you were looking for. A rather…delicate artifact.”

Seris’s fingers tightened around the key, the metal biting into her palm. She met Rhys’s gaze – a shared acknowledgement of the trap, a silent calculation of angles and escape routes already forming. He subtly adjusted his stance, shifting weight, readying himself. 

“What do you want?”

Valerius smiled, a predatory curve of his lips. “Simply to ensure the safety of the realm, my dear. And to prevent a power from falling into the wrong hands.” He gestured towards the cloaked figure. “My associate has a…particular interest in that.”

The cloaked figure raised a hand encased in a black glove. A faint, ethereal glow emanated from beneath the hood, illuminating a symbol etched into the glove – a coiled serpent devouring its own tail. The glow wasn’t merely light; it felt like a draining of warmth, a subtle pressure against her mind, and a fleeting impression of vast, empty spaces. 

Seris glanced at Rhys, apprehension flickering in her eyes. He returned her look, his expression grim, and subtly shifted to cover her flank. 

Valerius continued, “This key unlocks a power dormant for centuries. A power some believe should *remain* dormant. A gateway to the Obsidian Realms – places best left undisturbed.”

Seris felt the key pulse, a cold fire spreading up her arm. The image from before returned, stronger now – a swirling vortex, and a sense of something *watching*. She closed her eyes briefly, bracing against the sensation, then opened them, her gaze unwavering. She subtly angled her body, moving closer to Rhys, her shoulder brushing his arm – a silent signal of readiness. 

“What do you intend to do with it?” Seris asked, her voice steady, but edged with steel.

Valerius smiled, a predatory expression that didn’t reach his eyes. “Simply to ensure the safety of the realm. Hand over the key, and we can all walk away.”

Seris’s fingers tightened around the silver key. She glanced at Rhys, a silent understanding passing between them. They were outnumbered, outmatched. But she wouldn’t yield. A moment stretched, and she realized this wasn't about the key itself. It was about *her* reaction to it.

“I think,” Seris said, her voice ringing with newfound resolve, “we have very different definitions of safety.” She took a step back, towards Rhys, preparing for a fight. The key pulsed in her hand, a cold, dangerous weight. She drew a slow, steadying breath, accepting the power flowing through her, and the role she was about to play. Rhys’s hand moved, subtly resting on the hilt of his blade, a mirror to her own readiness. 

A flicker of something – amusement? – crossed the cloaked figure’s hidden face. “Perhaps,” a voice rasped, dry as dust, “safety is a comforting lie.” The figure’s gaze, though hidden, felt like a physical weight on Seris. “Or perhaps,” the voice continued, a subtle emphasis on the word, “it's merely a matter of perspective.” The figure didn't reach for the key, didn't move. Seris felt a chilling realization blossom in her mind: *they aren’t here to take the key, but to see what I will do with it.*  She met the unseen gaze, a flicker of defiance in her own. The key throbbed, and she knew, with a certainty that settled deep in her bones, that this wasn’t a negotiation. It was a test.

*   **Rhys’s initial line (“You seek this knowledge… sacrifice?”)** feels a bit cliché. It’s not *bad*, but leans heavily into a familiar trope. Consider a more subtle phrasing that *implies* sacrifice rather than directly stating it. Perhaps something about the cost of ambition, or the things one leaves behind when pursuing a goal.
*   **“Resourceful.”** Rhys’s single-word response to your statement feels a little flat. While brevity can be effective, here it doesn't quite land. It lacks nuance. Perhaps a slight expansion – “Surprisingly so,” or a more telling observation about *how* you’re resourceful – would be stronger.
*   **Your repeated internal descriptions (“I allowed my gaze…,” “I noticed…,” “I consciously quieted…”) are interrupting the flow of dialogue.** We *know* you’re observing and thinking. Trust the reader to infer these actions from your *responses* and let the dialogue carry more weight. Cut at least half of these internal beats.
*   **The "key/lock" metaphor is a bit on-the-nose.** While not terrible, it's a fairly common image. Consider a more original metaphor or simply stating the need for your skills directly.
*   **Rhys’s “None. Only my word. And the weight of centuries” is strong, but could be even more impactful.** The “weight of centuries” feels a little vague. What *specifically* does that weight represent? A lost lineage? A broken oath? Hinting at the source of that weight would add depth.
*   **Your final request for “transparency” is a little too direct.** While logical, it feels a bit like a modern interrogation. An Archivist would likely phrase it with more curiosity and a focus on historical context. Perhaps, “I work best when I understand the full picture. Can you tell me the history of what was lost?”
*   **Overall, the dialogue is competent but leans toward telling rather than showing.** There's a lot of stating of intent and observation of emotion. Focus on letting the characters *reveal* their motivations and feelings through their word choice and subtext.

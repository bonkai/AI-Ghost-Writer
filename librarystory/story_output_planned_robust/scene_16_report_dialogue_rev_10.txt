*   **Repetitive Questioning:** The back-and-forth of "What do you see?" followed immediately by Seris's fragmented answers feels a little flat. While understandable given the situation, it lacks nuance. Consider *showing* Rhys’s concern and Seris’s distress through internal monologue or physical reactions *instead* of relying solely on direct questioning.
*   **"The source" – Vague Exposition:** Seris saying "The source" is a bit of an info-dump. While it *needs* to be communicated, it feels abrupt. Could this be revealed through her reaction *after* seeing the vision? Perhaps a gasp and a muttered, "No… the source…" would be more impactful.
*   **"Ancient. Terrible." – Tell, Don't Show:** Similar to above, labeling something as "Ancient. Terrible." is telling rather than showing. The vision itself should *imply* these qualities.
*   **Rhys’s Lack of Reaction:** While his patience is admirable, Rhys is *completely* unreadable. A slight shift in expression, a tightening of his jaw, *something* to indicate his internal state would make him feel more present. He's acting more as a support beam than a character.
*   **"We have to go" – Direct & Lacking Subtext:** This is a crucial moment, but the dialogue is very direct. Consider if there’s a way to convey urgency and determination through *how* she says it, or through a physical action *before* she speaks. Perhaps she pushes herself away from him, already turning towards the exit.
*   **Strained Voice - Show, Don't Tell:** "Her voice strained" is a tell. Can you *show* this through shorter sentences, fragmented phrasing, or a specific word choice?

*   **Overly Explanatory:** The dialogue is *very* heavy on exposition. Seris spends a lot of time *telling* Kaelen (and the reader) about the Order, the Church, and the Echo, rather than *showing* through action or letting Kaelen deduce things. For example, the extended metaphor of the tapestry and the broken instrument is a bit much. Consider trimming these and letting the imagery of the Echo *itself* convey the concepts.
*   **Kaelen's Role:** Kaelen feels largely reactive. She asks questions, but they often feel like prompts for Seris to deliver another monologue. Give her more agency – perhaps she challenges Seris’s assumptions, offers alternative interpretations, or has her own insights based on her experiences.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The initial exchange about the Church not condemning the Order "overnight" is good, hinting at a complex history. Lean into this more. Instead of Seris *stating* the Church replaced the narrative, show Kaelen discovering evidence of this manipulation.
*   **Repetitive Urgency:** The increasing urgency in the dialogue ("A single discordant note can unravel everything," "Unravel the world?") feels a bit repetitive. Vary the phrasing and focus on *specific* consequences rather than vague threats.
*   **The "Mind Voice":** The italicized thoughts/voice from the Order feel a bit… convenient. While it establishes communication, it removes some tension. Consider if some of this information could be conveyed through environmental cues or Seris’s own deductions.
*   **"Remember" & "They Lied":** While dramatic, the single words appearing in the ink are a little on-the-nose. Perhaps the ink could form a fragmented image or symbol that hints at the lie, requiring interpretation.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** While Seris's sadness and weariness are stated, showing her *reacting* to the weight of the Order’s history (a sigh, a touch to a crumbling book, a momentary loss of focus) would be more impactful.

The absence wasn’t black, but a pressure—a full-VR rig lagging at the edge of perception. He tried to lift a hand, check for restraints. Nothing. No arm, no body. Just… awareness. A cursor detached from any screen, drifting in static.

A cold spike of panic—system crash? The *memory* of breath hitched, a phantom ache in his chest, but no lungs expanded. He tried to shout, to ping *something*, but the signal died in static. Untethered. Floating in null space. *What happened?* The thought fragmented, less a voice than code struggling to compile. Last thing… the game. Pressure building behind his eyes, the frantic rhythm of key presses, then white. System failure? A heart attack? A final, cruel lag spike? A corrupted save? The last thought tasted like ash.

Focus fractured. Thoughts scattered like dropped packets, each one fading before it fully formed. Coherence dissolving. *Reboot. Diagnostic. Anything.* The urge wasn't logical, not for… this. A reflex, a desperate attempt to reclaim control as the system spiraled. He clung to the command, repeating it like a mantra against the encroaching void.

Then, a shift. Not location, but texture. The darkness pulsed with a faint, ethereal glow, like the dying embers of a monitor. A hum resonated—distant server fans, architectural. Shapes resolved—a vast, cavernous space. He wasn’t *in* darkness anymore. He was *within* something.

“Hello?” He projected the thought, a fragile signal flare. It felt flimsy, lost in a hurricane, barely a whisper against the immense silence.

A response came, not as sound, but as *feeling*. A gentle ping against his awareness, laced with an ancient stillness. Not a voice, but an impression—patient, observing, impossibly *old*. It didn’t answer, not immediately. It *considered*. 

*Aware.*

The single word resonated, and with it, the sensation of being scanned. Like an admin assessing a new process. A chill—not *through* anything, but undeniably there. Less examination, more cataloging. He felt… labeled.

He tried to orient, map the space, but it rendered like a low-poly dream. Structures stretched endlessly, shifting, rearranging. Books. Countless volumes stacked on shelves that disappeared into the gloom, their spines unreadable. The air—though no lungs to breathe—held the scent of aged paper and forgotten data. Not a smell, exactly. A flood of information, cellulose and ink washing over his awareness. Each volume pulsed with quiet energy, a silent story waiting to be unlocked.

A library. Beyond imagining. No librarian. A glitch? A system with no operator?

“Where am I?” He projected again, desperation fraying the signal. The thought felt brittle, corrupted, like a file refusing to load.

The response came slower, like a firewall actively blocking connection. A subtle weight, a sense of… amusement? Not malicious, detached.

*Between.*

“Between what?”

A long pause. Unsettling. Then, a chilling revelation, delivered with the distinct impression of something *watching*. Something that had seen… everything.

*Between worlds.*

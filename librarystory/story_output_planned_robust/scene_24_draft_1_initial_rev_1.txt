The alley reeked of stale ale and something indefinably worse. Seris pressed herself against the damp stone wall, the rough surface scraping against her leather jerkin. Rain slicked the cobblestones, reflecting the flickering gas lamps in distorted pools of light. She risked a glance back. Marius, broad-shouldered and relentless, was gaining on them.

“Seris! Wait!” His voice, though strained, carried easily through the narrow passage. It wasn't the shout of an inquisitor, but the urgent plea of a man desperate to speak. A lie, she knew, but one that momentarily stalled her stride.

“Keep moving, Rhys!” she hissed, her breath catching in her throat. Rhys, nimble and quick, was already rounding a corner, disappearing into the labyrinthine streets of the Lower Ward. She pushed off the wall, her boots splashing through puddles, and sprinted to catch up.

Marius was closer now, his pace unwavering. He wasn’t running, exactly, but *flowing* through the crowds, navigating the bustling market stalls and dodging startled pedestrians with an unnerving grace. It was as if he knew exactly where they were going, anticipating their every turn.

“Seris, please! I just want to understand what you’re looking for!” He called out, his voice laced with a frustrating blend of urgency and control. 

She ignored him, weaving through a knot of merchants hawking their wares. The scent of spices and roasted meats filled the air, a jarring contrast to the cold dread gripping her. She risked another glance back. Marius was still gaining, his eyes fixed on her with an intensity that sent shivers down her spine. 

“The Atheneum archives…you were asking about the Sunstone Bloom, the Firepeak expeditions…” He continued, his words tumbling out in a rush. “What connection do they have?”

Rhys rounded another corner, disappearing from view. She had to reach him. She had to get away from Marius.

She ducked under a canvas awning, momentarily losing sight of her pursuer. The alley narrowed, becoming a claustrophobic passage between towering buildings. She pressed on, her lungs burning, her muscles screaming in protest.

Then, she saw it – a break in the buildings, a small, hidden courtyard bathed in the pale glow of a single lantern. Rhys was waiting, his hand outstretched. 

“Seris!”

She surged forward, reaching for his hand. But before she could grasp it, Marius was there, blocking her path. He moved with a speed that defied his size, cutting off her escape.

“Enough,” he said, his voice low and dangerous. He didn’t grab her, didn’t raise his hand. He simply *was* there, an immovable obstacle. “I’ve been patient. Now, you’re going to tell me what you’re hiding.”

Seris met his gaze, her heart pounding against her ribs. She could feel Rhys watching from the courtyard, his presence a silent reassurance.

“We’re conducting research,” she said, her voice surprisingly steady. “Ancient healing practices. That’s all.”

Marius’s lips curved into a sardonic smile. “The Sunstone Bloom doesn’t grow in gardens, Seris. It grows on Firepeak, near the ruins of the old observatory. An observatory abandoned for centuries because of…unpleasant discoveries.”

He paused, his eyes locking with hers. “You’re not interested in healing, are you? You’re looking for something *within* the mountain. Something the old scholars tried to forget.”

Rhys stepped forward, his hand resting on the hilt of his dagger. “Leave her alone, Marius.”

Marius didn’t even glance at Rhys. His focus remained solely on Seris. “The Atheneum holds fragments of the observatory’s records. You’ve been piecing them together. What have you found?”

Seris knew she couldn't lie her way out of this. Marius was too perceptive, too determined. And she had a sinking feeling that he already knew more than he was letting on. 

“Memories,” she said, her voice barely a whisper. “The mountain holds memories. Forgotten histories. And someone doesn’t want them to be remembered.”

Marius’s eyes narrowed. “And you think you can unlock them?”

Before Seris could answer, a sharp whistle pierced the air. A figure emerged from the shadows, a hooded man with a crossbow trained on Marius. 

“Leave her alone, Marius,” the man said, his voice gruff. “She’s under our protection.”

Marius’s gaze flickered to the crossbow, then back to Seris. A slow smile spread across his lips.

“So,” he said, his voice dangerously soft. “It seems I’ve stumbled into something…interesting.” He took a step back, his hands raised in mock surrender. “Very well. I’ll leave you to your secrets. But know this, Seris. Some memories are best left buried.”

He turned and disappeared into the labyrinthine streets, leaving Seris and Rhys standing in the rain, shaken but alive. The hooded man lowered his crossbow, his face obscured by shadow.

“Come,” he said, his voice a low rumble. “We need to get you somewhere safe.”

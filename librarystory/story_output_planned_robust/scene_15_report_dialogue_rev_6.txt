*   **Seris’s initial monologue feels a bit ‘telling’ rather than ‘showing’.** While establishing the mystery is important, much of it could be conveyed through her *actions* and reactions, and Rhys’s questions. For example, instead of *saying* the phrasing isn’t Eldorian elegance, show her struggling to translate it, perhaps muttering to herself and frowning in concentration.
*   **Rhys feels somewhat passive.** He’s mostly a reactor to Seris’s discoveries. Give him more agency – perhaps he offers a contrasting interpretation of the runes, or asks pointed questions that force Seris to articulate her thoughts.
*   **Kaelen’s mental intrusions are frequent and disrupt the flow.** While the telepathic link is established, the constant interjections feel like exposition dumps. Consider fewer, more impactful intrusions, or integrate the information more subtly into Seris’s internal monologue. For example, instead of *“But it’s also a cage,”* perhaps Seris feels a sudden, instinctive understanding of the map's purpose – a sense of confinement.
*   **Repetitive Sensing:** Rhys *repeatedly* senses Seris's unease/terror. This becomes predictable. Vary his reactions, or show him taking more proactive steps to understand what's bothering her.
*   **"I feel watched" is a cliché.** While it conveys Seris's fear, it's a weak line. Show *why* she feels watched – a shadow flickering in her peripheral vision, a cold spot in the room, a prickling sensation on her skin.
*   **The dialogue is functional but lacks subtext.** There’s little sense of the *relationship* between Seris and Rhys beyond professional competence. Inject some personal history or underlying tension to add depth. Are they cautious with each other? Is there a history of trust or mistrust?
*   **Valerius reveal feels a bit sudden.** While it sets up a potential conflict, it’s delivered somewhat abruptly. Foreshadow his interest earlier in the scene, perhaps through a mention of a recent conversation or a lingering question.

Overall, the dialogue isn't *bad*, but it's largely lacking in nuance and depth. It serves its purpose of conveying information, but it doesn’t fully engage the reader or reveal the characters’ inner lives. It leans heavily on telling instead of showing.

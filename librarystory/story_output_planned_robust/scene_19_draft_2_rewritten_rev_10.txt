The library breathed into stillness as the last patrons departed, lamps casting long shadows that veiled the upper shelves. Seris sketched, charcoal whispering across the page, acutely aware of a presence—not directly *on* her, but observing the space around her. She tightened her grip on the charcoal, the rough texture a grounding anchor.

A lamplight flickered, then steadied. Kaelen. A book’s spine flexed, and a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table. Not text, but a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, rendered not as geography, but as a being. Jagged peaks formed teeth, slopes etched with ancient power. A delicate, crimson fragrance bloomed from the sketch, momentarily overpowering the scent of aged paper. Seris inhaled sharply, a ghost of iron coating her tongue. It felt…familiar.

She traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. She glanced up, meeting Kaelen’s gaze. His jaw was tight, mirroring the sharp angles of the sketched mountain. He wasn’t merely observing *her*; he was watching the library itself.

“It remembers,” he said, his voice a low murmur that vibrated with the stillness.

“What does that mean?” Seris asked, her fingers tightening on the table’s edge. The weight of ages pressed on her, not just in the sketch, but in the very air.

He moved closer, his movements fluid and silent, until his shoulder brushed hers. “The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost—what *they* tried to bury.” He paused, his gaze dropping to the sketch, then lifting to meet hers. “This isn’t just a depiction, Seris. It’s a key—and they know it.”

A book slammed shut in the distance. Seris glanced towards the shadowed stacks, her pulse quickening. Valerius. She pressed her lips together, a silent warning to herself. Kaelen’s gaze flicked to the sound, then back to her, a subtle tightening around his eyes.

“Lost how?” she asked, her voice barely a whisper.

He swept his gaze across the library, tension coiling in his shoulders. “The Order didn’t just forbid magic. They forbade *remembering*. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories. And Valerius…he’s a guardian of that forgetting.”

Seris’s breath caught. “This isn’t about a rare healing plant, is it?”

Another book slammed shut, closer this time. Kaelen’s gaze locked with hers, a silent message passing between them—*he suspects*. He didn’t need to speak. He leaned closer, his breath warm against her ear. “No. It’s far older, far more dangerous. And they’re starting to realize we’re looking for more than herbs.”

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.” She opened them, meeting Kaelen’s gaze, and a fleeting connection sparked between them—a shared understanding of the risk, and something more—a fragile trust. Then, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. And from the darkness, a distinct *click*—the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing, followed by the soft scrape of stone on stone.

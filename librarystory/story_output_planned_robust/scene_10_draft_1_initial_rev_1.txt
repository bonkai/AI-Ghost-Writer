The air in the Restricted Section tasted of dust and forgotten things. Seris traced the spine of a particularly thick tome, its leather cracked and brittle with age. Kaelen’s presence felt… expectant. Not guiding, precisely, but hovering near the edges of her awareness, a subtle pressure against her thoughts.

“It’s not about the title,” a voice whispered in her mind, so faint she almost dismissed it as her own internal monologue. *“Or the binding. Think of what the author *felt* when they penned these words. The weight of the knowledge, the… longing.”*

Seris frowned, running a finger along the embossed lettering. *De Origine Stellarum*. “On the Origin of Stars.” Dryly academic. She hadn't even considered it a candidate.

*“Not the subject matter, Initiate. The *need* to record it. What drove him to preserve this… memory?”*

She closed her eyes, focusing not on the words, but on the sensation of the book itself. The cool, rough texture of the leather, the faint scent of beeswax and decay. She tried to imagine the author, hunched over a desk, painstakingly copying the text. Not a scholar seeking knowledge, but a guardian, protecting something precious.

A warmth bloomed on the page beneath her fingertips, not a heat, but a resonance. The ink seemed to… shift, subtly rearranging itself. Not changing the words, but *revealing* something within them.

“What…?” she breathed, her eyes snapping open. The page now displayed a series of constellations, not neatly drawn diagrams, but swirling patterns of light, as if viewed through a mist. And beneath them, a single line of text, highlighted in a faint, ethereal gold.

*“Ubi draco dormit, ibi lumen occultum.”* – “Where the dragon sleeps, there lies hidden light.”

“A dragon?” Seris murmured, scanning the surrounding pages. Nothing. The illumination clung only to that single line, a beacon in the sea of text.

*“Not a literal dragon, Initiate,”* Kaelen’s voice was clearer now, a gentle pressure behind her eyes. *“A constellation. Draco. Look for its position relative to the others.”*

She traced the constellation of Draco with her finger, then followed the line of sight it created, extending across the page to a cluster of seemingly unrelated stars. 

“There…” she breathed. A small, almost invisible symbol was nestled amongst the stars – a circle bisected by a vertical line.

*“That symbol… it's a key. A map. But not of this library. Of something… older.”*

She glanced around, suddenly aware of the oppressive silence of the Restricted Section. Hemlock was still at his post, seemingly engrossed in a scroll, but she could *feel* his attention, a weight on the back of her neck. He was watching. Always watching.

“I need to copy this,” she said, her voice barely a whisper.

*“Be swift. And discreet. Hemlock is… attuned to energies like these. He’ll sense if you linger too long.”*

She quickly sketched the symbol onto a small piece of parchment, her hand trembling slightly. It felt… important. More important than she could possibly understand.

As she finished the sketch, a shadow fell across the page.

“Finding something interesting, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice was smooth, deceptively gentle.

Seris snapped the parchment shut, her heart pounding. “Just… researching stellar cartography,” she said, forcing a casual tone. “For my studies.”

Hemlock’s gaze swept over the open book, lingering on the highlighted line. “A fascinating field. Though some knowledge is best left… unmapped.” His eyes met hers, unreadable. “The stars hold many secrets. And some secrets… bite back.”

He didn’t reach for the book. He didn’t demand an explanation. He simply *watched*, his presence radiating an unsettling stillness.

Seris met his gaze, her chin lifting in defiance. “I’m simply expanding my understanding,” she said, her voice steady despite the tremor in her hands.

Hemlock offered a slow, enigmatic smile. “Indeed.” He paused, his gaze sweeping over the shelves, taking in the arrangement of books. “The past has a way of reaching out. Sometimes, it’s best not to answer its call.”

She knew he was referring to more than just the book. He knew she was uncovering something he wanted hidden. 

“I don’t understand,” she said, her voice barely a whisper.

Hemlock’s smile widened. “Perhaps you will. In time.” He turned back to his post, but Seris could feel his gaze on her back, a silent warning.

She closed the book, carefully concealing the symbol beneath the cover. She wouldn’t let him see her fear. She wouldn't let him know how close she was to unraveling the truth. 

*“We need to leave,”* Kaelen’s voice was urgent now, a ripple of energy against her mind. *“He suspects something. And he’s not one to be trifled with.”*

Seris nodded, her heart pounding. She carefully returned the book to its shelf, then turned and walked away, her hand instinctively clutching the small piece of parchment hidden in her pocket. The dragon slept. And she had just found the key to its hidden light.

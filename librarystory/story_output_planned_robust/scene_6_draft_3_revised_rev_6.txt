The chill started at the base of my skull, intensifying with each attempt. I reached for the serpent image – a fragment of carved stone, a sinuous curve hinting at ancient power – not with words, but with intent.

This time, contact felt different. Not a blank wall, but a yielding resistance, like pressing against oiled silk. A flicker beyond the stone – a vast, echoing chamber choked with shadow, the faintest impression of watching eyes. Then, a tightening behind my temples, and a coppery taste bloomed on my tongue.

“Is everything alright?” Hemlock’s voice, smooth as polished obsidian, cut through my concentration. He hadn’t moved, but his unblinking gaze assessed me, a subtle lift of his eyebrow the only indication of his question.

I forced my eyes open, blinking against the dim light. “Just admiring the architecture,” I stammered, the lie feeling flimsy even to my own ears.

I tried again, willing the serpent image to solidify, to reveal itself. Energy surged through me, and for a moment, I felt a resonance, not with a being, but with the stone itself, with the Atheneum’s foundations. It felt…old. Immeasurably so. And profoundly unwilling, like forcing a lock with the wrong key. I pushed harder, and a sharp pain lanced through my head, momentarily eclipsing the chill.

A bookshelf rattled, a heavy tome tumbling to the floor with a muffled thud. The scent of aged paper and dust filled the air, thick and suffocating. I stumbled back, clutching my temples, a wave of nausea washing over me.

“Careful now,” Hemlock said, a hint of amusement lacing his voice, but his eyes remained cold. He didn’t move to help. “These volumes are fragile. As are your…abilities, it seems.”

I ignored him, pressing through the discomfort, focusing on what the carving *meant*. I sent another probe outwards, a desperate plea. This time, the resistance wasn't yielding silk, but a solid wall of stone. It felt like slamming into a glacier, the impact jarring my teeth. The air grew cold, a subtle stillness descending, muffling all sound. A prickling sensation rose on my skin, as if a storm was brewing *within* the stone.

Then, the wall *moved*. Not physically, but…internally. It wasn’t just blocking me; it was actively repelling me, pushing against my mind with ancient force. 

“It doesn’t want to be found,” I breathed, more to myself than to Hemlock.

He didn’t respond.

I sent another, weaker tendril of intent towards the image.

Nothing.

I lowered my head, trying to regain control. When I looked up, Hemlock’s gaze met mine. He hadn’t moved, hadn’t offered comfort. Only a silent, knowing assessment. A subtle tightening of his lips – not triumph, but something colder, something…expectant. He was waiting. 

I pressed my fingers to my throbbing temple, a dull ache spreading through my skull. A tremor ran through my hand. He hadn’t just witnessed my failure; he’d anticipated it. And that realization, more than the pain, left me shaken. I swallowed, forcing down the metallic taste of blood. The tremor worsened, and I clenched my fist, fighting to appear composed.

Hemlock’s eyes flickered to my hand, then back to my face. A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “Perhaps,” he said softly, “you should focus on secrets *willing* to be found.”

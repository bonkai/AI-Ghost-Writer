The cartography room smelled of dry paper and dust motes dancing in a single shaft of sunlight. Seris’s fingertip traced the brittle vellum of the ancient map, a slight tremor running through her hand as she followed the jagged peaks. She murmured, “‘Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’” Her brow furrowed, and she pressed a little harder near Mount Cinderfang’s base. The parchment felt…different. Not a change in color, but a yielding.

Rhys, perched on a stool, scraped a bone tool clean, the sound a steady rhythm. He paused, watching Seris. “Something?”

She didn’t meet his gaze, continuing to trace the anomaly. A subtle depression. She pressed again, and a faint click echoed in the quiet room.

Rhys set down the tool and approached, his gaze following her finger. “Mount Cinderfang. Fitting.” He studied the map with a cartographer’s eye, tracing the peak. “Layering… deliberate, not error.”

Seris’s hand tightened on the map. Valerius’s questions about the region, the old gods, the rituals *within* the mountains, flooded back. It clicked. “He’s looking for something here,” she said, her voice low. “Something old. Something…held.”

Rhys’s gaze met hers, concern tightening his features. “Held? What makes you say that?”

She swept the room with a quick glance, a prickling sensation on her skin. She gripped the map tighter, a wave of nausea rising. “Just…a feeling. Like disturbing something that should have stayed buried.”

Rhys reached for a heavy iron poker, his movements deliberate and silent. He didn’t offer reassurance.

A faint, metallic tang filled the air, prickling at the back of Seris’s throat. Whatever lay beneath Mount Cinderfang was straining against its bonds. She traced the discoloration one last time, a chilling certainty washing over her. This wasn’t just a map; it was a warning.

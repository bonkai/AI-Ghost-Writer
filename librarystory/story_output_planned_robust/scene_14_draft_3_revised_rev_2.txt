The scent of aged parchment and dust hung heavy in the library. Seris traced a finger across the star chart, the constellation Cygnus a pale echo of the blight staining the kingdom. Rhys meticulously ground dried herbs, the rasp of the pestle a counterpoint to the oppressive silence.

“Moonpetal, a touch of dragon’s scale to catalyze…and nightshade,” Rhys murmured, presenting a small pouch of blended powder. “A delicate balance. Neutralize the blight without harming the surrounding flora.”

Seris examined the mixture, a flicker of admiration crossing her face. “Remarkably considered, Rhys. You’ve anticipated so much.”

He offered a small, self-deprecating smile. “It’s a complex problem. Demands…precision.”

*He wants to be *seen* as capable, not praised,* Kaelen’s thought brushed against Seris’s mind.

A prickling sensation at the back of Seris’s neck. Footsteps approached, slow and deliberate.

Her hand instinctively tightened on the hilt of the hidden dagger beneath her cloak. Master Valerius emerged from the labyrinthine stacks, his face an impassive mask. He didn’t glance at Rhys, didn’t acknowledge the work spread across the table. His gaze fixed on Seris.

“Master Valerius,” Seris greeted, forcing a polite tone. “Is there something we can assist you with?”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, lingering on the charts. “I trust you are making appropriate use of the library’s resources. Some knowledge is best left undisturbed.” His eyes narrowed, focusing on the overlapping constellation charts. “What precisely are you researching?”

Seris met his gaze, her heart quickening. “Celestial cartography, Master Valerius. We’re studying historical interpretations of the constellations, and their potential connection to the land’s health.”

Valerius’s lips curved into a thin, skeptical smile. “Indeed. A fascinating subject. Though I caution you against delving into matters beyond your comprehension.”

*He suspects. Something significant. We’re close,* Kaelen’s thought was sharper now, edged with urgency.

Rhys, sensing the tension, stepped forward, holding out the pouch of herbs. “We’re simply trying to understand the ancient connections between the stars and the land, Master Valerius. To find a solution to the blight. We believe we’ve made a breakthrough.”

Valerius’s gaze flickered to the pouch, then back to Seris. A fleeting expression – something akin to fear? – crossed his face, quickly masked. “A ‘breakthrough,’ you say?” He paused, his eyes boring into her. “Be careful what you seek, young scholar. Some doors are best left unopened.” He turned and walked away, disappearing back into the stacks.

Seris exhaled slowly, her muscles coiled tight.

*He’s testing us. Gauging how far we’ll push,* Kaelen’s thought was a low hum in her mind.

A cold dread settled in her stomach. She glanced at Rhys, his brow furrowed with concern.

*We need to leave. Now.*

Seris didn't hesitate. “Rhys, we’re finished here.” She swiftly rolled the charts, her movements decisive.

Rhys, sensing the urgency, followed suit. “What’s wrong?”

“We’ve attracted unwanted attention.”

They moved swiftly towards the exit, the silence of the library now oppressive. As they reached the archway, Seris risked one last glance back.

Valerius stood at the far end of the aisle, a dark silhouette amidst the towering shelves. He wasn’t examining the shelves. He was watching *them*.

A shiver ran down Seris’s spine. They were no longer scholars seeking knowledge. They were trespassers, and they were being hunted.

*   **Initial Build-Up (Paragraphs 1-3):** Excellent slow burn. The descriptions of static, vibration, and the unsettling realism are effective. The pacing is deliberate, creating a mounting sense of unease.
*   **Pacing Shift (Paragraphs 4-7):** The activation of the glyphs feels *slightly* rushed. The transition from observation to full sensory overload happens quickly. Consider adding a beat or two – perhaps a stronger internal resistance from Kaelen before he touches the glyph, or a more gradual escalation of the physical sensations.
*   **Overload & Reset (Paragraphs 8-9):** The sensory overload is well-described, but the *immediate* return to the Atheneum feels abrupt. The "fracture. Static." is effective as a visual cue, but the shift back to a calmer setting lacks a beat to acknowledge the impact of the experience. A brief disorientation or struggle to regain bearings would heighten the tension.
*   **Post-Reset Reflection (Paragraphs 10-12):** The extended reflection is the weakest point. While the reveals are important, the pacing slows *too* much. The repeated phrasing ("ancient," "waiting," "memory") becomes slightly monotonous. Consider condensing some of the philosophical musings or interweaving them with more active internal processing (Kaelen trying to *understand* what he's experiencing).
*   **Tension Arc:** The scene builds tension effectively up to the glyph activation. However, the tension *releases* too quickly after the reset. The post-reset reflection needs more internal conflict or a lingering sense of threat to maintain engagement. The final line ("I think I've seen these symbols before...") is a good hook, but it feels somewhat isolated after the lengthy reflection.

Overall, the scene is well-written, but the pacing is uneven. The initial build-up and activation are strong, but the post-reset reflection slows momentum, and the tension arc needs a more sustained peak and valley.

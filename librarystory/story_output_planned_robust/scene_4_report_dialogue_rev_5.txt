*   **Lucian's Dialogue - Implied Threat vs. Actual Voice:** Lucian’s initial line, “What have you concealed?” feels a little flat for an Inquisitor. It *tells* us he’s intimidating, but doesn’t *show* it through the language. Consider a phrasing that carries more weight, perhaps implying accusation *within* the question itself. Something like, "So, little thief. What treasure did you think to hide from us?" or a more clipped, direct approach.
*   **Repetitive Questioning:** The sequence of questioning feels a bit redundant. Lucian asks what's concealed, then *immediately* follows up with a question about what was concealed *near the statue*. Combining these into a single, more pointed question would increase tension and efficiency.
*   **Valerius' Exposition:** Valerius’ lines about the Archivists are *very* expository. While necessary information, it's delivered as a straightforward explanation. Consider weaving some of this information into his observations of the locket itself. For example, instead of "They weren't merely preservers, Lucian. They were its protectors," he could say, "The craftsmanship…it’s not about preservation, it’s about fortification. This wasn’t meant to *hold* knowledge, but to *guard* it."
*   **Lack of Subtext:** The dialogue is largely direct. While clarity is good, consider adding layers of subtext, especially between Lucian and Valerius. Are there underlying tensions or disagreements between them? Hints of this could be revealed through subtle word choice or phrasing.
*   **Boy's Silence:** The boy remains entirely silent. While understandable given his fear, a single, desperate plea or whimper could heighten the emotional impact. Even a choked-back sob would add depth.
*   **Kaelen's Lack of Dialogue:** Kaelen's role is entirely internal. While fitting for a spectral observer, a *brief* line – perhaps a murmured observation or a question directed at himself – could reinforce his presence and add a unique perspective.

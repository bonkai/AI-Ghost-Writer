The air in the library thickened, a pressure against Seris’s skin that wasn’t quite pain, but a wrongness that settled deep in her bones. Dust motes hung frozen, suspended as if held by an unseen hand. A tremor ran through the stone, vibrating in her teeth, and she braced a hand against a towering bookshelf, feeling the ancient wood hum beneath her palm.

Rhys, studying a faded map, looked up, his brow furrowing as he registered her tension. He didn’t ask what was wrong, simply observed, his hand instinctively moving to rest lightly on the hilt of his dagger.

The library shifted, not visibly, but in the periphery of her perception, a ripple distorting the angles of the room. The familiar scent of old parchment and beeswax was tainted with a metallic tang – ozone and something akin to blood. A weight descended, the oppressive feeling of ages pressing down. She felt a network of energy, not as woven stars, but as branching roots beneath her feet, extending outwards, sustaining the very structure of the room. Within it, she sensed a fragile harmony, a delicate balance strained to its limit.

A discordant note fractured the harmony. A shadow crept into the light, and a force that didn’t destroy, but *severed* – cutting the luminous threads, allowing darkness to seep through. The energy didn’t weep from destruction, but *retreated*, the loss of connection echoing in Seris’s mind as a sudden, hollow ache.

She stumbled back, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue, pressing a hand to her temple, fighting the onslaught of sensation. Rhys caught her arm, steadying her, his grip firm and silent.

Kaelen’s fragmented consciousness brushed against hers – not words, but raw sensation: the echoing resonance of a nexus, the weight of ancient power, a desperate plea for…vulnerability. A flicker of icy dread, then a focused image – a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian, pulsing with power, but now laced with hairline cracks, the light within flickering erratically. 

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts. “A ward…a seal…and it’s failing.” She felt Kaelen’s panic – a desperate, wordless surge – the sense of something contained, weakening.

Rhys tightened his grip on her arm, his gaze searching her face.

“It’s connected to the Nexus…to the source of the network…a prison.” She closed her eyes, forcing herself to sift through the onslaught. “And the seal is breaking.” Kaelen’s consciousness, a dying ember – *Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…*

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus, and opened her eyes.

“Firestone Mountains?” Rhys asked, his voice tight with concern.

“The Nexus isn’t just a source of power,” she said, her voice strained. “It’s a prison. Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible. The seal is failing, and the Firestone Mountains…they’re where the cracks are widest.”

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within the Nexus breaks free.”

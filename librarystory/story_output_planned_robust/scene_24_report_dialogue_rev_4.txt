*   **Over-Explanation/Info-Dumps:** Several exchanges feel like characters are stating the obvious or explaining things to each other they would *already* know, especially given their positions and the urgency of the situation. For example, Kaelen stating “He’s trying to box us in, make us predictable” feels redundant – Seris is *living* the situation, she doesn’t need it explained. Similarly, the repeated emphasis on *why* Marius is doing things feels clunky. Trust the reader to infer motivation from action.
*   **Lack of Subtext:** The dialogue is very direct. Opportunities for layered meaning are missed. Kaelen’s withholding of information is *stated* (“deliberate withholding”) rather than *shown* through his responses. Let his silence or carefully chosen words do the work.
*   **Character Voice – Kaelen:** Kaelen comes across as overly expository. A leader of this Circle would likely be more concise and strategic with his words, relying on implication and commands rather than lengthy explanations. His dialogue lacks the weight and authority it needs.
*   **Repetition:** The idea of memories being suppressed/important is repeated multiple times, lessening its impact. Condense these instances and let Seris’ internal reactions and discoveries carry more weight.
*   **Seris’ Frustration:** While Seris expressing frustration is fine, it feels a bit flat. Show her frustration through *action* – a tightened grip on her dagger, a quickened pace, a sharp question.
*   **"Guided" Feeling:** The final line about being "guided" is telling, not showing. This is a strong concept, but it needs to be subtly woven into earlier scenes or dialogue, creating a sense of unease and foreshadowing.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The scene misses opportunities to reveal character *through* dialogue. For example, a brief, pointed exchange about the Circle's resources or internal politics could hint at power dynamics and relationships.



Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks depth and nuance. It prioritizes conveying information over revealing character and building tension. It needs more subtext, concision, and a stronger sense of individual voice.

*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening paragraphs (1-3) establish atmosphere well, but the pacing is *very* slow. The descriptions, while evocative, could be tightened to build momentum. The repetition of "resonating" and "ancient" feels a little heavy-handed at this stage.
*   **Tension Build – Delayed:** The tension *starts* to build with the glyph interaction (paragraph 5), but it's a gradual incline. The "surge of energy" is described as sensation, which is good, but it doesn’t immediately translate to a *threat*.
*   **Fractured Climax:** The sensory overload ("Fracture. Static.") feels abrupt. While disorientation is the goal, the transition is too quick. A beat or two of escalating chaos before the cut to the Atheneum would heighten the impact.
*   **Post-Shift Reflection – Too Long:** The paragraphs following the return to the Atheneum (starting with “I found myself…”) are largely exposition and internal monologue. While important for revealing information, they significantly *lower* the tension established earlier. This section needs to be trimmed and interspersed with active thought or action. The repeated emphasis on "memory" becomes redundant.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Micro-Tension:** Elara’s single line ("What’s happening?!") is a missed opportunity. Her reaction feels flat. A more panicked or urgent response would amplify the disorientation and danger.
*   **Overall:** The scene relies heavily on internal revelation rather than external conflict. While a shift in perspective is fine, the pacing is imbalanced. The initial build is slow, the climax is rushed, and the resolution is overly contemplative. The tension arc feels more like a plateau with brief spikes than a sustained climb.

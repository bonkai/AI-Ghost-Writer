*   **Initial Darkness & Reveal:** The opening is strong – the extinguishing lamp immediately establishes a shift in atmosphere. However, the description of the cold draft and scent feels slightly prolonged *before* Valerius appears. Tighten this – reveal the scent *as* or *immediately after* he materializes to connect it directly to him and heighten the mystery.
*   **Dialogue & Tension:** The dialogue between Seris and Valerius is intriguing, but the pacing slows noticeably during the exchange about the Aethel. While thematic, it lacks immediate tension. Interweave a small, unsettling action from Valerius or the Guardians *during* the dialogue to maintain a sense of threat.
*   **Sensory Detail & Build:** The prickling sensation and absorption of light are excellent details that effectively build tension. However, these are somewhat isolated. Connect them more directly to Seris's growing realization – perhaps a physical reaction (tightening muscles, quickened pulse) coinciding with the sensory input.
*   **Escort & Internal Shift:** The moment the Guardian moves is well-handled. Seris’s decision to not resist is a smart choice, showcasing her internal calculation. The final two sentences are strong, delivering a clear reveal and raising the stakes.
*   **Overall:** The scene *mostly* works, but the tension ebbs and flows rather than building consistently. The key is to weave more active elements (actions, reactions) *throughout* the dialogue and descriptive passages to maintain a higher level of suspense.

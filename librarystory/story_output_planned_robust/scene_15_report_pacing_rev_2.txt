The first arrives *just* as they identify the 'Stone Heart' as significant, and the second immediately after. Consider interleaving one of them *before* the full reveal of the sketch, creating more ambiguity.

However, the description of the sketch itself, while detailed, doesn't quite translate into *felt* tension. The awe and apprehension are *told* rather than *shown* through visceral reactions.
*   **Final Third – Good Momentum:** The final section, with Seris feeling watched and Rhys preparing the poker, is well-paced and effectively builds tension. The feeling of being watched is more impactful than the earlier descriptions.
*   **Overall:** The scene is structurally sound, but the tension arc is a bit flat. It needs more dynamic shifts between slower observation and faster realization of danger. The initial discovery feels less urgent than it should, and the tension needs to be *felt* more through Seris’s and Rhys’s physical and emotional responses.

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light. Rhys leaned against a crumbling pillar, eyes fixed on the star chart I traced with a fingertip. He didn’t speak, didn’t offer greeting – only observed, a stillness that felt worn, like a stone weathered by centuries. A faint pressure touched my mind – not a question, but an assessment.

“You seek this,” he said finally, voice a low rumble that vibrated in the alcove, gesturing to the chart with a subtle nod. “What are you willing to lose?”

A prickle of unease traced my spine. Not a warning, but a calculation. He wasn’t dissuading me, merely measuring. I let my gaze drift over his face – the deep lines around his eyes, the subtle clench of his jaw. Haunted, not driven. A shadow of loss clung to him. I briefly reached for Kaelen, seeking guidance, but the connection felt… distant, muffled.

“Knowledge carries responsibility,” I said, keeping my voice level.

Rhys’s lips curved in a wry smile, the shadows in his eyes unmoved. “Resourceful.” He paused, letting the silence stretch, the only sound the rustle of parchment. “I’ve tracked this symbol for years. I know where it leads. I can help you decipher it… for a price.”

My mind raced. Not a simple trade. Precarious, like stepping onto a frozen lake. I trailed my fingers over the cool stone of the chart, a faint warmth radiating from the etched lines.

“What exchange?” I asked, direct.

Rhys pushed off the pillar, straightening to his full height. Not aggression, but a predator’s controlled stillness. “Information. My interests align with your research. Something was stolen – something of value.” He paused, his gaze locking with mine, assessing. “Your expertise. In return, access to knowledge beyond any library’s walls.”

I noticed the downturn of his lips, a simmering resentment held in check. I quieted my connection to Kaelen, relying on instinct. The library’s whispers felt distant, muffled, as if protesting.

I looked down at the symbol, my fingers tracing its lines. “Specifically, what do you want me to do?”

Rhys’s lips curved into a slow smile. “You’re an Archivist. You access lost knowledge, decipher star charts, translate ancient languages. You see connections others miss.” He paused, his gaze unwavering. “You’re a conduit. And I need you to open the way.”

I met his gaze, a spark of defiance flickering within me. I wasn’t merely a tool. I was an Archivist, a seeker of truth. And I wouldn’t blindly follow him.

“What guarantees do I have this isn’t a fool’s errand? That this ‘something’ even exists?”

Rhys’s smile faded, replaced by weary resignation. “None. Only my word. And the weight of centuries.” He paused, then added, his voice barely a whisper, “But I assure you. It’s real. And its return is vital. Not just for my people… but for everyone.”

The air in the alcove crackled with tension. I stared at the ancient star chart, the symbol now pulsing with unsettling energy. I was at a crossroads. I took a slow breath, meeting his gaze. “Very well. I’ll help you. But I require transparency. Tell me everything. What was taken, from whom, and why you believe *I* can help.”

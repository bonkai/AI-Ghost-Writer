## Scene 7 - Draft 1

Seris moved deeper into the Atheneum, abandoning the main chamber for a warren of narrow aisles dedicated to ‘Restricted Cartography & Theoretical Geographies’ – a deliberately obscure categorization Hemlock had pointed her towards with a knowing glance. The air here was thicker, heavier with dust and the scent of decaying parchment. Sunlight barely penetrated, forcing her to rely on the glow of her everbright lantern, which cast dancing shadows that played tricks on her eyes.

She ran a finger along the spines, deliberately avoiding titles. She wasn’t looking for information *about* hidden cities, not yet. She was following the pull, the echo of stone and serpent that had flared up when she touched the clasped volume. It felt less like a direction and more like a vibration, a resonance within her own mind.

The sensation led her to a section of the wall that *should* have been solid. The shelves ended abruptly, replaced by a seamless expanse of aged, dark wood paneling. But as she approached, the vibration intensified, focused on a single, almost imperceptible seam running vertically down the paneling. 

It wasn’t a trick of the light. The seam was there, a hairline fracture in the wood, cleverly disguised amidst the natural grain. 

Hesitantly, Seris pressed on the seam. Nothing. She tried again, applying more pressure, then ran her hand along the panel, searching for a release. Still nothing. Frustration gnawed at her. Was she imagining things? Had the initial contact with the serpent-clasped book been a phantom sensation?

Then, she remembered the serpent itself. The way it coiled, the subtle pressure points of its scales. She pressed on the seam, not with brute force, but with a deliberate, spiraling motion, mimicking the curve of a serpent’s body.

A low rumble echoed through the aisle. Dust rained down from the ceiling. The paneling groaned, then *shifted*. Not outwards, but inwards, sliding silently into the wall, revealing a narrow doorway leading into absolute darkness.

Seris held her breath, lantern raised. The doorway wasn't large – barely wide enough for a single person to pass through – and it led to what appeared to be a cramped, circular chamber. The air spilling out was noticeably colder, carrying a faint, metallic tang.

As she stepped inside, the lantern light revealed a small, stone room, dominated by a single, intricately carved pedestal. Upon the pedestal rested a sphere of polished obsidian, no larger than her fist. It wasn't the sphere itself that drew her attention, but the carvings etched into its surface – a complex network of serpentine patterns, mirroring the image that had flooded her mind.

As she reached out to touch the sphere, a surge of energy washed over her. It wasn’t painful, but disorienting, like a sudden drop in pressure. Images flickered behind her eyes – vast, crumbling cities carved into the sides of mountains, winding canyons filled with strange, bioluminescent flora, and a colossal, serpentine creature coiled around a peak, its scales shimmering like a thousand sunsets.

She stumbled back, clutching her head. The images subsided, leaving her breathless and shaken. The obsidian sphere pulsed with a faint, internal light.

“Interesting,” a voice murmured from the doorway.

Seris whirled around, lantern raised. Hemlock stood there, his face obscured by shadow. He hadn't made a sound, hadn’t betrayed his approach. 

“You knew about this place,” she accused, her voice tight.

Hemlock stepped into the chamber, his eyes finally visible in the lantern light. They were dark, knowing, and held a hint of… amusement?

“The Atheneum holds many secrets, Initiate. Some are revealed only to those who know where to look… and how to listen.” He gestured towards the obsidian sphere. “That sphere… is a key. A fragment of a map, if you will. It shows the location of Aethelgard.”

“Aethelgard?” Seris echoed, her mind reeling.

“A lost city. Carved into the Firepeak Mountains, centuries ago. A city said to hold knowledge… and power… beyond imagining.” Hemlock paused, his gaze locking with hers. “But Aethelgard is not easily found. And those who seek it… often find more than they bargained for.”

He reached out, his fingers hovering over the obsidian sphere. "Tell me, Initiate. What did *you* see when you touched it?"

Seris hesitated, a knot forming in her stomach. She didn't trust Hemlock. But she knew, with a chilling certainty, that he held the key to understanding the serpent, the sphere, and the lost city of Aethelgard. And she had a feeling that whatever secrets lay hidden within those mountains, they were about to change everything.

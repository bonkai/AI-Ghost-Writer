*   **Lucian's Dialogue - Implied Threat vs. Actual Delivery:** Lucian *implies* a lot, which is good for his character, but some lines feel more like stage direction written as dialogue. For example, "Let me see what you’re concealing" is a statement of intent, not something a character would naturally *say* to exert control. Consider something like, "Show me." or "What have you got hidden?" to be more concise and impactful.
*   **Boy's Dialogue - Too Passive:** The boy's responses are *extremely* meek. While fear is appropriate, he's almost entirely reactive. Even a small, desperate plea ("Please, sir, it's just flowers!") would add a touch more character and pathos.
*   **Valerius - Scholarly Tone - Slightly Flat:** Valerius's dialogue is *correct* for a scholar, but a little dry. It lacks nuance. Consider adding a slight edge or curiosity to his delivery, even a hint of internal conflict. He's intrigued by the locket, but also an Inquisitor. That tension should be present in his speech.
*   **Repetitive Reporting:** Lucian's line, "This boy possesses a relic of the Old Faith," feels like unnecessary narration *within* dialogue. It's telling us information we should infer from the situation. Let his actions and tone convey that information.
*   **Lack of Subtext:** There's very little subtext happening. Everyone says what they mean, or what they *seem* to mean. Consider what characters *aren't* saying. What are they hiding, even from themselves? The momentary softening towards the flowers is a good start, but build on that.
*   **Kaelen - No Dialogue:** While Kaelen is a spectral observer, consider if *any* form of communication is possible, even a subtle influence on thoughts or emotions. If not, that’s fine, but it’s worth exploring.

Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks depth and nuance. It needs more subtext, more character-specific voice, and less direct exposition.

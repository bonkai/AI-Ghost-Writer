## Scene 14

The scent of aged vellum and dust hung heavy in the air, a familiar comfort that Seris barely registered. She traced the faded gold tooling on a massive tome – *Celestial Harmonies*, a treatise on the constellations and their supposed influence on the ebb and flow of magic. Rhys, meanwhile, was absorbed in a scroll detailing ancient herbal remedies, muttering about poultices and antidotes.

*“Subtle, but effective,”* Kaelen’s voice drifted into her mind, a cool current against the rising heat of frustration. *“Rhys is drawn to practical applications. Let him believe he’s finding a solution to the blight. It’s a legitimate concern, and it keeps him occupied.”*

Seris glanced at Rhys, his brow furrowed in concentration. The blight affecting the outlying farms *was* a pressing issue, but she suspected his focus was less about curing the land and more about proving his worth to the Order. He craved recognition, a weakness she’d observed during their travels.

*“And you?”* she thought, directing the question towards Kaelen. *“What are we looking for amongst these star charts?”*

*“Connections,”* came the reply, a whisper in the silence. *“The constellations aren’t merely decorative. They’re a map. A key to understanding the ley lines beneath Verenhal. The source of the city’s power… and its vulnerability.”*

Seris ran a finger over a depiction of the Serpent constellation, its coils winding across the parchment. “The ley lines… Master Elmsworth mentioned them, but dismissed them as folklore.”

*“Elmsworth dismisses anything that doesn’t fit his rigid worldview,”* Kaelen retorted, a touch of disdain in his mental voice. *“He sees magic as a tool to be controlled, not a force to be understood. Focus on the intersections. Where the constellations overlap. That’s where the power concentrates.”*

She shifted her attention, scanning the charts for overlapping patterns. It was tedious work, requiring her to mentally overlay one constellation onto another, searching for common points. Rhys, oblivious, continued to examine his scroll, occasionally sniffing at the dried herbs within.

*“Rhys is heading towards the section on nightshade,”* Kaelen observed. *“A potent ingredient. And dangerous in the wrong hands. A distraction, nothing more.”*

Seris allowed herself a small smile. Kaelen seemed to anticipate Rhys’s every move, subtly guiding their interaction. It was a strange partnership, this silent collaboration between herself, the living scholar, and the disembodied consciousness within her mind.

“Look at this,” she murmured, tracing a point where the Serpent, the Dragon, and the Phoenix constellations converged. “The intersection falls directly beneath the Obsidian Spire.”

*“Precisely,”* Kaelen confirmed, his mental voice gaining a sharper edge. *“The Spire isn’t just a fortress. It’s an anchor. A focal point for the ley line energy. And it’s vulnerable.”*

Rhys suddenly straightened, a triumphant gleam in his eyes. “I believe I’ve found a promising combination! Nightshade, moonpetal, and a touch of dragon’s scale… it should counteract the blight’s toxins.” He turned to Seris, eager for her approval. “What do you think?”

Seris forced a smile. “It sounds… thorough, Rhys. Perhaps a little excessive?”

He bristled slightly. “Excessive caution is never a bad thing when dealing with a virulent disease.”

*“Let him believe that,”* Kaelen urged. *“Keep him occupied. I’m sensing something… a shift in the energy. Someone is approaching.”*

Seris’s senses heightened. She scanned the towering shelves, her hand instinctively reaching for the hidden dagger beneath her cloak. A figure emerged from the labyrinthine stacks – Master Valerius, the head librarian, his face etched with disapproval.

“Master Valerius,” Seris greeted, forcing a polite tone. “Is there something we can assist you with?”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, lingering on the charts spread across the table. “I was merely ensuring that our guests are making appropriate use of the library’s resources. Some knowledge is best left undisturbed, you understand.” His eyes narrowed, fixing on the overlapping constellation charts. “What precisely are you researching?”

Seris met his gaze, her heart pounding against her ribs. “Celestial cartography, Master Valerius. We’re studying the historical interpretations of the constellations.”

Valerius’s lips curved into a thin, skeptical smile. “Indeed. A fascinating subject. Though I trust you’re not delving into matters beyond your comprehension.”

*“He suspects,”* Kaelen warned, his voice a frantic whisper. *“He’s a powerful mage. And he’s protecting something.”*

Rhys, oblivious to the tension, chimed in. “We’re merely seeking to understand the ancient connections between the stars and the land.”

Valerius’s gaze flickered between them, assessing their sincerity. “Very well. But remember, knowledge is a double-edged sword. Wield it with caution.” He paused, his eyes locking onto Seris. “And some doors… are best left unopened.”

He turned and disappeared back into the stacks, leaving Seris and Rhys in a chilling silence.

*“We need to leave,”* Kaelen urged, his voice barely audible. *“Now. He knows we’re onto something. And he’ll stop at nothing to protect it.”*

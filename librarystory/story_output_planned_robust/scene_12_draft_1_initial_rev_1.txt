## Scene 12: Echoes of the Forgotten

The dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the high, arched window of the Atheneum’s deepest archive. Seris traced a finger across the brittle spine of a tome bound in blackened iron, the metal cold even through her gloves. It wasn’t the chill that made her shiver, though. It was the *absence*. An absence of information, of even *mention* of the entities hinted at in the fragmented texts she’d been piecing together.

*“This one… it feels different,”* Kaelen’s voice resonated within her mind, a low hum against the silence. *“Older. Before the Church consolidated its doctrines.”*

She carefully opened the book. The pages weren’t parchment, but something akin to polished slate, etched with glyphs that felt less written and more…grown. The ink wasn’t ink at all, but a silver dust that shimmered with an internal light. The language was unlike anything she’d encountered – not the elegant script of the old Elven kingdoms, nor the harsh runes of the Firepeak dwarves. It was… primal.

“What *is* this place?” she breathed, her voice echoing softly.

*“A repository of what they wanted forgotten,”* Kaelen replied, his tone laced with a strange sorrow. *“Look for references to the ‘First Bloom,’ or the ‘Silent Gods.’ They predated the current pantheon. The Church… actively erased them.”*

Seris scanned the etched pages, her eyes aching. The text spoke of beings of immense power, not gods of thunder or sun, but entities woven into the very fabric of Eldoria itself. They weren’t worshipped, but *integrated with* – the mountains breathed with their essence, the rivers flowed with their will.  Then she found it – a single, faded illustration depicting a being of pure light, its form shifting and nebulous, surrounded by blossoming, bioluminescent flora. Beneath it, a phrase, repeated like a mantra: *“Aethel – the Seed of Stars.”*

“Aethel…” she murmured.

*“That’s one of them,”* Kaelen confirmed, his voice sharper now. *“They called them the Aethel – the Firstborn. Beings of pure energy, connected to the lifeblood of Eldoria. The Church claimed they were chaotic, dangerous… but that’s a lie.”*

She continued to decipher the text, a growing unease settling in her stomach. The Aethel hadn't simply vanished. They’d been… *suppressed*. Their power siphoned, their essence bound, and used to fuel the very magic the Church now controlled. 

“They didn’t just erase them from history,” Seris said, her voice trembling. “They *stole* their power.”

*“Precisely,”* Kaelen’s voice was grim. *“And that power… it’s still there. Dormant, but not gone. The Church built its foundations on stolen divinity.”*

Suddenly, a cold draft swept through the archive, extinguishing the single oil lamp she’d brought. Darkness descended, thick and suffocating. Seris instinctively reached for the hilt of her dagger.

“We’re not alone,” she whispered.

A voice, smooth as polished stone, echoed from the shadows. “Intriguing discoveries, Initiate. I didn’t realize you possessed such…historical curiosity.”

It was Archon Valerius, one of the highest-ranking members of the Church hierarchy. He materialized from the darkness, a figure of imposing height and chilling composure.  He wasn’t alone. Two heavily armored Guardians flanked him, their faces obscured by steel helms.

“I was merely researching ancient texts, Archon,” Seris said, forcing herself to meet his gaze.

Valerius glided closer, his eyes scanning the open tome. “Ancient texts can be…dangerous things. Filled with falsehoods and forgotten heresies.” He traced a finger across the illustration of Aethel. “This… this is a particularly troubling depiction. A relic of the old ways.”

“What’s troubling about it?” Seris challenged, her hand tightening around her dagger.

Valerius smiled, a predatory curve of his lips. “The Aethel were beings of immense power, yes. But they were also chaotic, unpredictable. They threatened the very stability of Eldoria. The Church rightly purged their influence, for the good of all.”

“You stole their power,” Seris accused, her voice ringing with defiance.

Valerius’s smile didn’t falter. “A strong leader understands the necessity of utilizing all available resources. We simply… repurposed their energy.  Directed it towards a more…beneficial purpose.”

*“He’s lying,”* Kaelen hissed in her mind. *“He’s twisting the truth. Get out of there, Seris. Now!”*

Seris knew Kaelen was right. Valerius wasn’t interested in a scholarly discussion. He was issuing a warning. And she had stumbled upon something he desperately wanted to keep hidden.

“I believe I’ve found everything I need here, Archon,” she said, slowly closing the tome.

Valerius’s eyes narrowed. “Indeed. Perhaps it’s best to leave the past undisturbed, Initiate. Some doors are best left unopened.”

He gestured to the Guardians. “Escort the Initiate back to her quarters. And ensure she understands the importance of discretion.”

As the Guardians moved to flank her, Seris knew this wasn’t just about ancient history. It was about power. And she had just become a threat to those who wielded it.  The echoes of the forgotten Aethel resonated within her, a silent promise of a conflict yet to come.

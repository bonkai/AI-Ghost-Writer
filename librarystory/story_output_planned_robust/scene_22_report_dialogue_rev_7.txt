*   **Repetitive Realization:** The realization about the Veil (“it wasn’t about keeping things *out*…it was about keeping something *in*…or keeping us from something *else*.”) is stated *twice* – once by Seris immediately after the vision, and then essentially repeated by Rhys. Condense this. Let Seris have the initial, breathless realization, and let Rhys’s reaction be a more internal, hardened response that *shows* understanding, rather than stating it.
*   **Kaelen's Voice:** Kaelen’s dialogue is almost entirely exposition delivered as fragmented statements. While the fragmented nature *implies* strain, it leans heavily into telling rather than showing. Consider *how* Kaelen might deliver this information, given their nature. Perhaps through flashes of imagery *along with* the words, or by emphasizing certain words to convey the weight of the information.
*   **Rhys's Internalization:** Rhys’s murmured lines ("They didn’t want us to remember…And now I think I understand *why*.") are fine, but feel a bit…flat. He’s a character who typically *does* rather than just thinks/says. Consider how his understanding manifests physically *before* he speaks. A tightening of his grip, a shift in his stance, a narrowing of his eyes – these would be more impactful than the murmured lines.
*   **Seris's Breathlessness:** While understandable given the situation, Seris's frequent short phrases ("Show me," "I…I saw something") feel a little repetitive and weaken her agency. A stronger, more focused delivery, even while shaken, would be more compelling.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The interaction between Seris and Rhys feels a little static. While the physical touch (bracing against him, his grip) is good, explore how their established dynamic affects their reactions. Does Rhys instinctively *protect* Seris? Does Seris rely on his strength? Subtle cues would add depth.
*   **"Connection to what?"** This line feels like a very direct prompt. Perhaps Seris could react to the word "connection" with a specific physical response (a widening of her eyes, a quickening of her breath) that prompts Kaelen to continue, rather than asking a direct question.

Overall, the dialogue isn’t *bad*, but it leans heavily on exposition and direct statements. It could be strengthened by showing more, telling less, and leveraging the established character dynamics for more nuanced interactions.

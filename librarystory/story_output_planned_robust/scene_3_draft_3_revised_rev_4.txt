## Draft 3 - Scene 3

He wasn’t dissolving, not exactly. More an unraveling—threads of awareness pulled taut, then released. A fall, not down, but *inward*, into a space that wasn’t space, a silence that wasn’t silence. Then, a jarring halt. He wasn’t *where* he was, not anymore. The chair, the monitor – ghosts of memory, fading with each passing moment.

He wasn’t *what* he was, either. He tried to flex a hand, shift weight, but found…nothing. No limbs, no body, only diffuse awareness, a point of consciousness adrift. Terrifying, yet…exhilarating. A strange lightness bloomed in the absence of weight, of form. A phantom ache for solidity pulsed within him, quickly fading, replaced by a growing disorientation.

The silence pressed, a velvet weight. But it wasn’t truly silent. Beneath the absence of sound, a subtle hum resonated, a vibration tickling awareness. He focused, seeking a pattern, a source. The hum grew, coalescing into a feeling – immense age, boundless knowledge. It felt…watched. A prickle of unease settled over his awareness, sharper now, less a passive sensation and more a deliberate scrutiny.

He reached out, not with a hand, but with will. He focused on a plain brown volume on a towering shelf, and *pushed*. Not physically, of course. No body to exert force with. He channeled disorientation, desperate need for solidity, into concentrated energy. A flicker of frustration sparked within him – a phantom ache for tangible connection, quickly followed by the realization that *wanting* felt…wrong, somehow. As if desire itself was a forgotten language. The book didn’t move, but the air around it shimmered, and a dust mote danced in the unseen current.

He strained, pushing harder, and a single page within the book fluttered, then another. A distortion spread, like heat rising, and he felt a strange pull, a connection forming. He almost *tasted* the knowledge – a metallic tang on his nonexistent tongue, followed by the faintest scent of aged paper and leather. The effort left him breathless, a strange sensation without lungs, and a dizzying wave of exhaustion.

A word solidified on the page, glowing with inner light: *Listen.*

Listen? To what? The silence? It wasn’t silent, not really. Beneath the absence of sound, the hum throbbed – a heartbeat in the vastness. He focused, and the hum resolved into a chorus of whispers, too faint to decipher. He *projected* a question, a desperate plea: *Where am I?*

“You are a resonance, a fragment of awareness within the Great Library. A place where stories are not just told, but *lived*.” The voice was ancient, layered, like the rustling of countless pages, and carried a weight that seemed to press against his very being.

He concentrated, *projecting* thought towards the voice, a spark of defiance flickering within his formless self: *What do you want from me?*

The voice chuckled, a dry, rustling sound like parchment crumbling. “Want? A quaint notion. We do not ‘want.’ We *are*. And you, little resonance, are…new. A potential echo.”

*What am I?* he thought, the question echoing with desperate need.

The voice didn’t immediately answer. A wave of images flooded his mind – fleeting glimpses of countless stories, lives lived, worlds imagined – a dizzying kaleidoscope. Knights battling dragons, lovers under starlit skies, cities rising and falling, galaxies swirling. Overwhelming, exhilarating, terrifying. He recoiled, struggling to regain control, the images threatening to overwhelm his nascent awareness.

“You are a reader,” the voice finally said, its tone gentle, a soothing balm. “And now, you are part of the story.”

A pause, then a final, enigmatic whisper. "Welcome home." A warmth spread through his awareness, a sense of belonging he hadn’t known he craved, but it was quickly overshadowed by a growing unease. 

He tried again to *speak*, to force a sound, any sound, but found only the echoing silence of his own awareness. He focused all his energy, willing a response, but nothing came. The library held its breath, waiting. He pushed harder, desperation building, and a single word formed on another page, distant and faint: *Soon.* 

What did it mean? Soon what? The question hung unanswered in the vastness, a chilling promise in the echoing silence.

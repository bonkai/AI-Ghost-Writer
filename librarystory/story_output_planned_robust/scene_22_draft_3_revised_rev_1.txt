## Draft 3

Seris closed her eyes, a wave of fear washing over her, stealing her breath. She pressed her palms against the cool stone of the table, grounding herself. They weren’t just searching for ancient lore anymore. They were stumbling into a conspiracy that reached the highest echelons of power. And they were running out of time. She could feel the weight of it pressing down on her, a suffocating dread. “We need to be careful,” she whispered, her voice barely audible. “Very careful.”

Rhys nodded, his jaw tight. He glanced around the shadowed alcove of the library, as if expecting to be observed. “Careful won’t be enough. We need to understand what they’re so afraid of. What exactly *is* this ‘dangerous knowledge’?”

“Kaelen,” Seris said, opening her eyes and focusing on the mental connection. “Is there anything more about *why* the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? Anything about its content?”

A flurry of pages turned, faster now, almost frantic. The air around them crackled with a faint energy, and dust motes danced in the shafts of light filtering through the high windows. Finally, the pages stilled, revealing a fragmented passage written in elegant, archaic script.

*“The Gardener cultivated not merely flora, but memoria – the very fabric of recollection. They understood that Eldoria’s history was not a singular truth, but a tapestry woven from countless perspectives, altered and pruned by those in power. The Gardener preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…the echoes of worlds that might have been.”*

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. “Lost narratives? Alternate histories?”

“So, they weren’t just preserving facts,” Rhys mused, running a hand through his hair. “They were preserving *possibilities*.”

*“The Order believed that a single, unified history was essential for maintaining order,”* Kaelen’s voice resonated in her mind, laced with urgency. *“They feared that acknowledging multiple realities would unravel the foundations of Eldorian society. They called it ‘the fracturing of the Weave.’”*

“The Weave?” Seris repeated, a chill running down her spine. “What does that mean?”

A new page turned, displaying a complex diagram – a swirling vortex of interconnected lines and symbols. *“The Weave is the collective consciousness of Eldoria, the shared belief system that binds the realm together. The Order believed that the Gardener’s knowledge threatened to tear holes in the Weave, unleashing chaos and instability.”*

Rhys leaned closer, studying the diagram. “So, they weren’t just suppressing information, they were suppressing…ideas? Alternate ways of seeing the world?”

“It seems so,” Seris said, her voice barely a whisper. “And they’re still doing it.” She remembered Valerius’s veiled warnings, his subtle attempts to steer her away from the Bloom. It wasn’t about landslides. It was about control.

*“The Order still exists, operating from the shadows,”* Kaelen’s voice was strained. *“They have infiltrated every level of Eldorian society, manipulating events to maintain their grip on power. Valerius is one of their most trusted agents.”*

Seris’s blood ran cold. Valerius. The man who had seemed so reasonable, so concerned for her well-being. She had trusted him. 

“He knew we were getting close,” she breathed, her voice tight with anger and betrayal. “He let us dig, just enough to understand the danger…and to lead us into a trap.”

Rhys’s gaze met hers, grim and resolute. “We need to be smarter than them. We need to understand what they’re protecting, and why. What is so dangerous about these ‘lost narratives’?”

A final page turned, revealing a single, haunting sentence written in faded ink. *“The Gardener’s greatest secret was not what was lost, but what was deliberately forgotten.”*

Seris stared at the words, a sense of dread washing over her. Deliberately forgotten. What had been erased from Eldorian history, and why? What truths lay buried beneath the carefully constructed narrative of the Founding Kings and the current pantheon of gods?

“Kaelen,” she said, her voice trembling. “Is there anything more? Anything at all about what was *deliberately forgotten*?”

Silence. A long, agonizing silence. Then, a single, chilling whisper echoed in her mind.

*“The Gardener remembered a time before Eldoria…before the Veil fell.”*

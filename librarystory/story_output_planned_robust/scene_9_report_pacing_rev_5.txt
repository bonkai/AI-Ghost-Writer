*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening paragraphs (1-3) are *very* descriptive and atmospheric, which isn't inherently bad, but it delays the introduction of active conflict or rising tension. While establishing mood is important, it risks feeling static.
*   **Tension Peaks & Valleys:** The scene operates in waves of tension. The "pages turning themselves" and tremors are good, but the moments *between* these events feel a little flat. Consider interspersing more subtle indicators of unease – a change in temperature, a creak in the stone, a prickling sensation on Seris’s skin – to maintain a consistent low-level hum of tension.
*   **Fractured Knowledge Sequence:** The exchange about "fractured how?" and the unraveling tapestry is strong, visually evocative, and builds tension nicely. However, immediately following this with the explanation of "weakened weave" feels a little explanatory. Trust the visual and emotional impact; you don’t need to *tell* us the consequences as explicitly.
*   **Darkness & Reveal:** The extinguishing of the candle is a classic tension-builder, but it’s immediately followed by a purely internal communication ("A confluence...") and then another vision. This feels like *too much* happening at once. Give Seris (and the reader) a beat to react to the darkness *before* layering on the next reveal.
*   **Final Image & Silence:** The final paragraph is effective. The lingering scent of smoke and the distorted bookshelf create a strong sense of foreboding. However, the silence, while intended to be profound, could be *slightly* shorter. Prolonged silence can sometimes lose its impact.



Overall, the scene has good potential, but the tension could be more consistently maintained. It relies heavily on peaks of activity (pages turning, visions) and needs more subtle cues in the valleys to create a truly gripping experience.

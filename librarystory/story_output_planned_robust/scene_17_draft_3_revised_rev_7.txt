## Scene 17 - Draft 3

The damp air of the passage clung to Seris’s skin, carrying the scent of decay and a faint metallic tang. Dust motes danced in the slivers of light, illuminating crumbling shelves. The circular chamber felt less like a cache and more a reliquary, steeped in silence. She ran a gloved hand along the cold stone wall, a prickling sensation raising the hairs on her neck.

“What are we looking for, exactly?” Rhys asked, his voice hushed, eyes scanning the shadows. He shifted his weight, a subtle impatience in the movement.

Seris approached the ornate pedestal in the chamber’s center, her fingers tracing the weathered stone. She found a hidden seam and, with a soft click, a compartment opened, revealing the key. It wasn’t ornate, but felt strangely…alive. She lifted it, and a faint chill traced a path up her arm.

“Well, well. What have we here?”

Seris and Rhys whirled around. Captain Valerius stood in the passage entrance, flanked by four soldiers. Behind him, shrouded in shadow, stood a cloaked figure radiating unsettling stillness. The soldiers raised weapons, but Valerius held a hand, stopping them. His expression wasn’t surprise, but a calculated satisfaction.

“I expected you sooner,” he said, his voice smooth. “You’ve found what you were looking for. A rather…delicate artifact.”

Seris’s fingers tightened around the key, the metal biting into her palm. A muscle ticked in her jaw, mirroring the pressure in her hand. She met Rhys’s gaze, a brief, shared acknowledgement of the trap – a silent calculation of angles and escape routes. He subtly adjusted his stance, readying himself.

“What do you want?”

Valerius smiled, a predatory curve of his lips. “Simply to ensure the safety of the realm, my dear. And to prevent a power from falling into the wrong hands.” He gestured towards the cloaked figure. “My associate is… particularly interested in that.”

The cloaked figure raised a hand encased in a black glove. A faint, ethereal glow emanated from beneath the hood, illuminating a symbol etched into the glove – a coiled serpent devouring its own tail. The glow wasn't merely light; it felt like a draining of warmth, a subtle pressure against her mind. 

Seris glanced at Rhys, a fleeting exchange of apprehension. He returned her look, his expression grim, and subtly shifted to cover her flank, effectively positioning himself between her and the cloaked figure.

Valerius continued, “This key unlocks a power dormant for centuries. A power some believe should *remain* dormant.”

Seris felt the key pulse, a cold fire spreading up her arm. She closed her eyes briefly, a tremor running through her, but quickly regained control. She subtly angled her body, moving closer to Rhys, her shoulder brushing his arm – a silent signal of readiness.

“What power?”

“A gateway,” Valerius replied, his voice low. “To realms best left undisturbed.”

Seris glanced towards the crumbling archway, barely visible in the shadows. The pull she felt wasn't just a sense of direction, but a visceral tugging, a deep resonance within her own being.

“What do you want with it?” Seris asked, her voice steady, but edged with defiance.

Valerius smiled, a predatory expression that didn’t reach his eyes. “Simply to ensure the safety of the realm. Hand over the key, and we can all walk away.”

Seris’s fingers tightened around the silver key. She glanced at Rhys, a silent understanding passing between them. They were outnumbered, outmatched. But she wouldn’t yield.

“I think,” Seris said, her voice ringing with newfound resolve, “we have very different ideas about safety.” She took a step back, towards Rhys, preparing for a fight. The key pulsed in her hand, a cold, dangerous weight. She drew a slow, steadying breath, accepting the power flowing through her, and the role she was about to play. Rhys’s hand moved, subtly resting on the hilt of his blade, a mirror to her own readiness.

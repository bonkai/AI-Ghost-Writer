*   **Slow Start:** The initial paragraphs (1-2) establish atmosphere well, but are *too* slow. The focus on Seris's internal state, while necessary, lingers for too long before any external event occurs. Consider condensing this to quickly establish the unease and then *immediately* introduce the first disturbance.
*   **Ripple Effect Tension:** The sequence of disturbances (dust motes, flexing book, falling page) is effective in building initial tension, but the pauses between each event are a bit lengthy. Tighten the pacing here – each event should *almost* immediately follow the previous one to maintain a sense of mounting mystery.
*   **Internalization vs. Action:** A significant portion of the scene is Seris *receiving* information and having internal reactions. While this is important for character development, it slows the momentum. Interweave these internal moments with more active responses from Seris – a quick sketch of the mountain herself, a muttered question, a glance around the library.
*   **Fragmented Communication:** The delivery of information via fragmented thoughts and images is intriguing, but becomes repetitive. The back-and-forth feels drawn out. Consider streamlining the communication – perhaps fewer, more impactful images, or a single, longer ‘transmission’ instead of several short ones.
*   **Climax & Resolution:** The final paragraphs (petal appearing, shared perception) are a good emotional beat, but feel slightly anticlimactic after the build-up. The tension dissipates *too* quickly. Consider ending on a more ambiguous note – perhaps a glimpse of someone watching them, or a subtle indication that Valerius is closer than they think. This would maintain the tension and create a stronger hook for the next scene.
*   **Overall:** The scene relies heavily on *telling* rather than *showing*. While the internal experience is well-written, the pacing suffers from a lack of external action and a tendency to linger on internal reactions. Tightening the pacing, interweaving internal and external moments, and ending on a more ambiguous note would significantly improve the tension and flow.

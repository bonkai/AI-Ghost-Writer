“Seris?”

She spun, heart leaping. Hemlock stood framed in the doorway, his expression unreadable. He didn’t speak immediately, simply observed, a stillness that felt less like composure and more like a held breath.

Seris offered a curt nod, her gaze fixed on the sphere. She extended a hand, fingers hovering just above the cool surface, drawn by an inexplicable pull. A tremor ran through the stone as her fingertips brushed it.

The world didn’t shatter, but *unfolded*. First, a blurring at the edges of her vision, like heat rising from sun-baked stone. Then, layers peeled back, revealing glimpses—not of a place, but of *times*. She saw cities carved into the sides of mountains, their spires crumbling under the weight of ages, then canyons choked with luminous, alien flora. The images weren’t static; they pulsed with a forgotten life, a slow, rhythmic breathing. Coiled around the highest peak of a basalt mountain, a colossal serpentine creature stirred. Scales shimmered, each one reflecting a thousand sunsets, and the air itself thrummed with its ancient power.

It wasn’t the sheer scale that stole her breath, but the gaze. Amidst the swirling chaos of fractured landscapes, a single, ancient eye met hers—reptilian, the gold flecks within it like trapped starlight. It didn’t merely *see* her; it bored *through* her, stripping away pretense, expectation, even memory. A resonance bloomed in her chest, a hollow ache mirroring the creature’s immeasurable weariness. She stumbled back, a strangled sound escaping her lips. The images fractured, receding like a tide pulled back from the shore, leaving a coldness in her chest and a prickling behind her eyes. She braced a hand against the wall, knuckles white, breath ragged.

*Awakening…* The thought wasn’t spoken, but *felt*—a weightless pressure against her mind, the echo of the serpent’s presence. Not a threat, but an inevitability, steeped in eons and a chilling, patient hunger. It felt… strangely like homecoming. She squeezed her eyes shut, then slowly, cautiously opened them.

The chamber seemed heavier, shadows deeper, the thrumming beneath her feet intensified. The sphere radiated heat, the carvings twisting and shifting like living things.

Hemlock’s voice, closer now, broke the silence. “Disturbed?” He had entered the chamber, his gaze sweeping over the sphere, then settling on her face, assessing.

She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her despite the warmth. “Cities…a serpent…it felt real. Not like a vision, but…a memory. One that isn't mine.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint, unreadable smile. He reached out, fingers hovering over the sphere, then drew back, as if testing the air. “Some places remember. Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, his gaze locking with hers, searching. “And some people…are more attuned.”

He studied her intently. “Be careful. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting*.”

She looked back at the sphere, and for a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths—a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns, a single, golden eye reflecting her own. The eye didn’t blink. It *watched*. And in its depths, she glimpsed not a reflection of herself, but a flicker of something ancient, something *knowing*.

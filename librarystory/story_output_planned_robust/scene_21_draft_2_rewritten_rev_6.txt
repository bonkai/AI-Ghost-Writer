## Scene 21 - Draft 4

The workshop smelled of dried herbs and mountain dust, a familiar comfort now laced with metallic tang. A chill lingered long after Inquisitor Marius departed, settling into the stone floor and clinging to shadowed corners.

“He’s probing,” Kaelen breathed. Seris’s fingers drifted to the worn pouch containing Elara’s portrait, her thumb tracing the faded image. She tightened her grip, the leather creaking faintly – a subtle anchor against the rising tension.

Rhys shifted, angling his body between Marius’s last position and Seris’s research corner – a silent claim of territory. “The Bloom’s properties are well-documented, Inquisitor. Significant potential for healing, though its volatility requires careful study.” He glanced at the shelves, his eyes lingering on the restricted ingredients, then adjusted a precisely balanced scale – a display of focused expertise.

Seris smoothed a stray strand of hair, avoiding Marius’s gaze. Her fingers traced her jawline, a self-soothing gesture she couldn't quite conceal. “The pursuit of knowledge is rarely commonplace, Inquisitor.” A muscle ticked in her jaw.

“Indeed.” Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging details. He stopped on the half-dissected flower, the petals arranged with unsettling precision. His lips thinned – a flicker of recognition, perhaps disapproval.

Seris met Marius’s gaze, her expression carefully still. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.” A flicker crossed her eyes, quickly masked, but she held his gaze, projecting unwavering defiance. She shifted her weight, subtly placing herself between Marius and the most sensitive research materials.

Rhys began cleaning his axe *before* Marius finished speaking, the rhythmic scrape of steel on stone a preemptive act. He maintained a focused intensity on his task, not looking at Marius. *He’s afraid,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in Seris’s mind. *Afraid of what you might find, and what that means for the Order.* Rhys’s gaze flickered toward the shadowed corner, then returned to his work, the cleaning becoming more deliberate, more forceful.

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile. “A convenient answer.” He circled Seris slowly, like a predator assessing its prey, measuring her reactions. “The Bloom…it remembers. Echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep.”

He stepped back, his gaze lingering on a dried specimen of rare mountain moss, then swept over their workspace. “I will be watching you. Both of you.”

Rhys tested the axe’s edge with his thumb – a dangerous gesture. He didn’t look at Marius. Seris’s fingers trembled, but she clenched her hand around the pouch, concealing the movement.

“We have no secrets, Inquisitor.” She said the words with deliberate conviction, projecting confidence she barely felt. Her voice was steady, but her pulse thrummed in her ears.

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing her with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has secrets, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to sacrifice to keep them buried?” He offered a curt nod, then swept out, leaving a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being watched. A faint scent of ozone followed him – a subtle reminder of the Order’s power.

Silence descended. Rhys continued to sharpen his axe, the rasp a small, rhythmic sound against the tension, his gaze fixed on the closed door. Seris stared at the door, her heart pounding. He hadn't found secrets…yet. But she knew, with chilling certainty, that he would return. And when he did, he wouldn’t seek information. He would seek control. She clutched the pouch, the worn fabric a fragile comfort. She wouldn’t let him.

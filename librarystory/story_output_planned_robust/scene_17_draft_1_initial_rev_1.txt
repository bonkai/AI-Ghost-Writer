## Scene 17 - Draft 1

The air hung thick and damp as Seris and Rhys navigated the crumbling outer districts of Verenhal. The map, painstakingly reconstructed from fragments within the Atheneum, led them to a forgotten section of the city wall, a stretch choked with weeds and shadowed by decaying towers. 

“Are you certain this is the place?” Rhys asked, his voice echoing slightly against the stone. He ran a hand along a section of the wall, dislodging flakes of mortar. “It looks… abandoned.”

*Abandoned on the surface,* Kaelen’s thought brushed against Seris’s mind, a cool current guiding her. *But not forgotten. The Iron Church maintains a presence, a subtle one. They’re watching.*

Seris scanned the area, her gaze sweeping across the dilapidated structures. The map indicated a hidden access point, a small, overgrown archway concealed within the wall itself. “It is. The map depicts a concealed entrance. Likely used for smuggling… or something similar.”

Rhys, ever practical, produced a small, curved knife and began hacking away at the thick ivy clinging to the wall. “Smuggling seems… optimistic. More likely a forgotten sewer outlet.”

As Rhys cleared the vegetation, a section of the wall gave way, revealing a narrow archway, barely wide enough for a single person to pass through. It was indeed concealed, cleverly integrated into the stonework. But it wasn’t unguarded.

Two figures in the grey and crimson livery of the Iron Guard stood a short distance away, seemingly engrossed in a conversation. They weren’t actively *guarding* the entrance, but positioned themselves with a casual awareness that suggested they were monitoring the area.

“Company,” Rhys murmured, his hand instinctively moving to the hilt of his own blade.

*They’ve been expecting someone,* Kaelen’s thought was sharp. *They're not looking for a direct confrontation. They want to observe, to understand our purpose.*

Seris lowered her voice. “We need to be subtle. A direct approach will only raise alarms. Rhys, create a diversion. Something small, to draw their attention away from the entrance.”

Rhys nodded, understanding immediately. He moved a few paces away, deliberately stumbling over a loose stone. “Blast these crumbling streets!” he called out, feigning annoyance. 

The guards glanced in his direction, their expressions shifting from bored vigilance to mild irritation. One of them began to move towards Rhys, a scowl on his face.

“Now,” Seris breathed. 

She slipped through the archway, moving with practiced silence. The passage was dark and narrow, the air heavy with the smell of damp earth and decay. Rhys, having successfully distracted the guards, quickly followed, melting into the shadows behind her.

“Impressive,” Rhys whispered as they navigated the winding passage. “You move like a wraith.”

*The passage leads to an underground chamber,* Kaelen’s thought was a guiding beacon. *A relic of a forgotten order. The map indicates a hidden cache within.*

The passage opened into a small, circular chamber. The walls were lined with crumbling shelves, most of which were empty. Dust motes danced in the faint light filtering in from the entrance. In the center of the chamber stood a single, ornate pedestal.

“What are we looking for?” Rhys asked, his eyes scanning the chamber.

Seris approached the pedestal, her gaze fixed on a small, recessed compartment. “The map indicated a key. A small, silver key. It unlocks something… something important.”

She carefully examined the pedestal, running her fingers along its surface. After a moment, she discovered a hidden latch. She pressed it, and the compartment sprang open, revealing a small, tarnished silver key.

As she reached for the key, a voice echoed from the passage behind them.

“Well, well. What have we here?”

Seris and Rhys whirled around to find Captain Valerius, the stern commander of the Iron Guard, standing in the entrance, flanked by four heavily armed soldiers. His expression was unreadable, but his eyes held a glint of cold amusement.

“I must confess,” Valerius said, his voice smooth as steel, “I expected a more… elaborate scheme. But a hidden passage and a silver key? Rather pedestrian, wouldn’t you say?”

Rhys tensed, his hand gripping the hilt of his blade. Seris, however, remained calm, her gaze locked on Valerius.

*He knew we were coming all along,* Kaelen’s thought was a chilling realization. *This isn’t about catching us. It’s about… something else.*

Seris held up the silver key, her voice steady. “We’re simply scholars, Captain. Investigating the history of this city.”

Valerius chuckled, a dry, humorless sound. “Is that so? And what history, precisely, requires a secret passage and a stolen key?” He paused, his eyes narrowing. “Perhaps you’d care to enlighten me?”

## Scene 12 - Draft 4

The single oil lamp sputtered and died, plunging the archive into darkness. The air grew heavy, stagnant. A draft, colder than stone, swept through the chamber, carrying the scent of dust and something else… something metallic and faintly floral, like dried lilies pressed between ancient pages. Seris instinctively reached for the hilt of her dagger, her knuckles white against the worn leather.

“We’re not alone,” she whispered, her breath misting in the cold air.

A voice, smooth as polished obsidian, echoed from the shadows. “Intriguing discoveries, Initiate. I didn’t realize you possessed such…historical curiosity.”

Archon Valerius coalesced from the darkness, not entering, but *becoming* visible. He didn’t walk into being; he *was*, drawn into existence by her investigation. Two heavily armored Guardians materialized beside him, their movements unsettlingly silent, their faces hidden behind featureless steel helms. They extended from him, extensions of his will.

“I was researching ancient texts, Archon,” Seris said, meeting his gaze. His eyes consumed the surrounding darkness, leaving only emptiness.

Valerius glided closer, his movements fluid and unnervingly graceful. He didn’t touch the open tome, but the air around it wavered in his presence, the ink blurring. “Sometimes, Initiate,” he said, his voice a low resonance, “history isn’t written, it’s… curated.”

“Curated?” Seris echoed, her voice barely a whisper. She ran a hand over the aged parchment, tracing the lines of the illustration.

Valerius didn’t answer directly. He gestured towards the illustration of the Aethel with a long, slender finger. “Tell me, Initiate, what do you see?”

“A civilization lost to time. Erased.” Seris’s fingers tightened on the dagger hilt.

“Erased?” Valerius tilted his head, sending a shiver down Seris’s spine. “Or… repurposed? What if nothing truly vanishes, but merely… transforms?” He paused. “Consider the loom. Threads can be cut, yes, but woven into something new. Something… stronger.”

A prickling sensation rose on the back of Seris’s neck, a feeling of being watched by something ancient and powerful. The metallic scent intensified, almost suffocating. She felt the weight of the archive pressing down, the accumulated years of forgotten knowledge and suppressed power. 

She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius, a growing unease twisting in her gut. The air felt wrong, charged. She needed to end this conversation. Now.

“I think I’ve read enough.” She began to rise, intending to signal her departure.

Valerius’s smile widened, not a gesture of warmth, but a predator’s acknowledgment. “A wise decision, Initiate. Some doors are best left unopened. And some truths… best left undisturbed.” He gestured to a Guardian, the movement precise. “Escort the Initiate back to her chambers. She needs…rest.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. As it approached, Seris noticed the polished steel of its armor wasn’t reflecting the darkness, but *absorbing* it, swallowing the light whole. It was as if the darkness itself fed its form.

She glanced back at Valerius. He stood before the tome, his gaze fixed on the illustration. But he wasn't looking *at* the image. He was looking *through* it, as if seeing something beyond the page. In that moment, Seris understood. The Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound. Valerius held the threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer. A cold certainty settled over her. She had stumbled onto something dangerous, something far older and more powerful than she could have imagined. And Valerius would do anything to keep it hidden.

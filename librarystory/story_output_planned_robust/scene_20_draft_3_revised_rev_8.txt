## Scene 20: Draft 3

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, her fingers tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure. Old Man Hemlock emerged from the gloom, pacing a tight circle, his gaze darting between shadows and stone. He stopped abruptly, hand rising to his ear as if listening, then lowered himself to a weathered stool.

“I believed in the purity of the Light,” he began, his voice brittle. He glanced at the mosaic, regret flickering across his face, then quickly looked away. “A meticulous cataloger. Every blessing documented, every deviation noted. I thought I understood order.”

Seris leaned forward, her hand instinctively reaching within her pack, fingers brushing the outline of the Sunstone Bloom sketch. *What did these records truly mean?* Not just cataloging, but *control* through cataloging.

“Records of the Before-Times.” Hemlock’s voice dropped to a rasping whisper. He swept his gaze around the cavern. “A world steeped in magic, not *controlled* blessings, but raw, untamed force. It wasn’t chaos, child. It was…potential.” He traced the lines of the vortex on the mosaic with his eyes. “I saw images…cities built of shimmering crystal, powered by the lifeblood of the earth. Gardens that bloomed with bioluminescent flora, responding to thought, to emotion.” He hesitated, his hand hovering near Seris’s arm, then dropped it, as if afraid to implicate her further.

He stepped back into the shadows, his voice barely a murmur. “They didn’t just fear the magic. They feared what people *remembered* with it. The power it gave them, the questions it inspired.”

Seris’s jaw tightened, her fingers tracing the delicate lines of the sketch. This wasn’t a legend she followed; it was a deliberate obscuring, and she was tracing the steps to uncover it. "Why bury it?" she asked, her voice sharp, demanding more than just explanation, but *motivation*.

“The Order deemed those memories dangerous. They didn’t destroy them. They buried them. And built the Church upon the grave, a monument to forgetting.” A faint scraping echoed from the tunnel entrance, and Hemlock whirled around, hand instinctively reaching for a hidden knife. He scanned the darkness, breath coming in ragged gasps.

Seris followed his gaze, her hand tightening on her dagger, but remaining calm, assessing the potential threat. She needed to understand *who* was listening, not just that someone was.

Hemlock lowered his hand, but didn’t fully relax. He glanced at the Sunstone Bloom sketch peeking from her pack. “They believe the flower might unlock something. Something they desperately want to keep buried.”

“I questioned it,” he continued, clutching his chest, a tremor running through his body. “I dared to ask *why*. And for that, I was branded a heretic. Stripped of my rank. Exiled.”

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls. “You risked everything.”

“A small defiance.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris, his expression grim. “They believe knowledge is control. They were right, to a degree. But they also fear what they cannot control.”

Seris met his gaze, the crimson stain on the sketch feeling like a brand. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower. She was chasing a truth the Church would kill to keep buried. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, she felt a prickling sensation on her skin. 

Beneath the fear, a new resolve hardened within her. This wasn’t just about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it. She tightened her grip on her pack. "What can we do?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper, but filled with a newfound determination.

Hemlock’s gaze flickered to the mosaic, then back to Seris. A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “We remember,” he said, his voice gaining strength. “We tell the stories. And we protect the embers of what remains.” He briefly covered her hand on her pack, a grounding touch. “The flower…it’s not just a key to the past. It’s a seed for the future.” He paused, his eyes locking with hers. "But be warned, child. They won't let us plant it easily."

*   **Subtext & Non-Verbal Communication:** The scene *leans* heavily on non-verbal cues (hand movements, gazes). While effective, it risks becoming overly reliant on stage direction to convey meaning. Consider adding *internal* monologue for Rhys to show his interpretation of Seris’s signal. Right now, it's *telling* us it's a subtle signal instead of *showing* us how Rhys understands it.
*   **Rhys's Voice:** Rhys is largely reactive. His only direct question ("What *kind* of formations?") feels functional rather than characterful. Giving him a line with a bit more personality, even a curt remark, would establish his voice more strongly.
*   **Merchant's Dialogue - Repetition:** The merchant repeatedly *states* he knows more than they let on ("Don’t insult my intelligence," "I’ve heard whispers…"). This feels a little on the nose. Show his knowledge through more pointed questions or observations, rather than direct accusations.
*   **"What do we do?" - Overly Direct:** The "silent plea" and unspoken question between Rhys and Seris is a bit cliché. A subtle shift in Rhys's body language *combined* with a brief, almost involuntary glance at a specific object (a weapon, a locked box) would be more impactful.
*   **Seris's Deflection:** Seris's "We’re simply botanists" line is a standard deflection. It's not *bad*, but it could be more layered. Perhaps a slight hesitation, or a detail that *almost* contradicts her statement, would add nuance.
*   **"Charges a premium" - Weak Close:** The line "And that someone…charges a premium" feels a bit flat. It’s a functional statement, but lacks punch. Consider something more evocative, hinting at the danger or difficulty of obtaining the information. ("That someone…demands a steep price. In more than coin.")
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is *competent* but leans towards functional rather than captivating. It prioritizes conveying information and tension, but could benefit from more distinctive character voices and subtler subtext. The scene relies heavily on atmosphere and non-verbal cues, which is fine, but the dialogue itself feels a little safe.

## Draft 3

The workshop smelled of oiled steel and dried herbs, a fragile peace disturbed by the insistent rapping at the door. Seris ran a honing stone along the edge of a scalpel, the rhythmic rasp a deliberate counterpoint to the growing tension. Rhys, ostensibly organizing charts, kept glancing towards the door, his hand hovering near a drawer filled with cartography tools.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her voice a low current.

“A merchant,” a gravelly voice replied. “Looking to trade for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask.”

Rhys exchanged a fleeting glance with Seris, a silent question passing between them. This felt…off. The collector they’d been expecting used intermediaries. He opened the door to reveal a man swallowed by shadow, a wide-brimmed hat obscuring his features. Only his eyes were visible – sharp, assessing, and unsettlingly still. The air around him carried the scent of woodsmoke and something metallic, like old blood.

“I understand you have charts of the Firepeak region,” the man said, his voice a rasp against stone. He deposited a heavy pouch onto the workbench, the clink of coins a stark punctuation in the small space. “I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps, particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys’s gaze narrowed, studying the man. “What kind of formations?”

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin, unsettling smile. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” His eyes drifted to Seris, lingering on the subtle flex of muscle beneath her tunic, the way her hand remained close to her hip. “I’ve heard whispers of your…research. The Sunstone Bloom. A beautiful flower, but hardly worth risking life and limb for.”

Seris didn’t meet his gaze. Instead, she traced the edge of a meticulously drawn botanical illustration with a calloused fingertip, her movements slow and deliberate. “We’re simply botanists, cataloging rare species. Our work is purely academic.”

The merchant chuckled, a dry, humorless sound. “Botanists who happen to be interested in ancient legends and forgotten pathways. Don’t insult my intelligence.” He tapped the pouch on the workbench. “I’ll double what you’d get from any local cartographer for a map showing the location of the Whisperwind Caves.”

Rhys’s breath caught in his throat. The Whisperwind Caves. A name relegated to fragmented texts, whispered rumors of a concentration of Skyglass fragments – a secret he hadn’t even breathed to Seris.

“How do you know about the Whisperwind Caves?” he asked, his voice tight with suspicion.

The merchant’s smile widened, revealing teeth that seemed too sharp, too white against the shadow of his face. “Let’s just say I have…sources. And I’m willing to pay handsomely for information. Or, if you prefer, I can simply acquire it myself.” His gaze flickered to the subtle bulge beneath Seris’s tunic, the outline of a concealed dagger. “Though that might be…messier.”

Rhys’s eyes met Seris’s, a silent plea for understanding, for a signal. He didn't need to voice the question – *what do we do?* – it hung heavy between them.

Seris didn’t return his gaze. She slowly, deliberately, lowered her hand, not to her dagger, but to rest it flat on the workbench, palm down. A subtle, almost imperceptible signal. *Patience.*

“We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice unwavering, a cool counterpoint to the merchant’s predatory energy. “But we know someone who might.” She finally met the merchant’s gaze, her eyes the color of flint, assessing, unreadable. “And that someone…charges a premium.”

A beat of silence stretched, thick and tense. The merchant’s eyes narrowed, studying Seris, gauging her resolve. He didn’t like being played, but he recognized a kindred spirit in her – someone who understood the value of secrets, and the price of obtaining them.

“Interesting,” he said, his voice a low purr. “Perhaps we can discuss terms.” He gestured towards a stool with a gloved hand. “Shall we?”



**Changes made and why:**

*   **Increased Subtlety and Nuance:**  Removed even more direct explanations and relied heavily on body language, implied meaning, and atmospheric descriptions. The focus is on *showing* the tension and power dynamics rather than *telling* the reader.
*   **Stronger Character Dynamics:**  The relationship between Rhys and Seris is more complex and nuanced. Their silent communication is a key element of the scene.
*   **Heightened Tension:**  The pacing is slower, allowing the tension to build gradually. The descriptions are more evocative, creating a more immersive experience.
*   **Ambiguous Ending:**  The scene ends on a note of uncertainty, leaving the reader wondering what will happen next. This creates a sense of anticipation and keeps them engaged.
*   **Refined Dialogue:**  The dialogue is more concise and impactful, with each line carrying multiple layers of meaning.
*   **Emphasis on Visuals:**  The descriptions are more visual, allowing the reader to create a vivid picture of the scene in their mind.
*   **Focus on Power Dynamics:** The scene is structured to highlight the power struggle between Seris, Rhys, and the merchant.



This draft aims for a more sophisticated and atmospheric approach, relying on subtlety, implication, and visual storytelling to create a compelling and immersive experience for the reader. The focus is on creating a sense of unease and anticipation, rather than providing all the answers upfront.

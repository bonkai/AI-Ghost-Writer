## Scene 21 - Draft 3

The workshop smelled of dried herbs and mountain dust, a familiar comfort now laced with something metallic. The chill lingered long after Inquisitor Marius departed, settling into the stone floor and clinging to the shadowed corners.

Kaelen breathed, “He’s probing. Testing the edges.” Seris’s fingers drifted to the small, worn pouch containing Elara’s portrait, her thumb tracing the faded image. She didn’t simply touch it; her grip tightened, the leather creaking faintly, a subtle anchor in the rising tension.

Rhys shifted, not his weight, but his stance. He subtly angled his body between Marius’s last position and Seris’s shadowed research corner, a silent claim of territory. “The Bloom’s properties are well-documented, Inquisitor. Significant potential for healing, though its volatility requires careful study.” He glanced at the shelves, his eyes lingering on the restricted ingredients, then deliberately moved to adjust a precisely balanced scale, the movement precise and deliberate – a display of focused expertise.

Seris smoothed a stray strand of hair, avoiding Marius’s gaze, but her fingers didn't still. They continued to trace the line of her jaw, a self-soothing gesture she didn’t quite conceal, a tell betraying her composure. “The pursuit of knowledge is rarely commonplace, Inquisitor.” A muscle ticked in her jaw, but she didn’t meet his eyes, maintaining a carefully constructed distance.

“Indeed.” Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging details. He stopped on the half-dissected flower, the petals arranged with unsettling precision. He didn’t just *see* it; his lips thinned almost imperceptibly, a flicker of recognition, or perhaps…disapproval.

Seris met Marius’s gaze, a controlled stillness in her expression. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.” A flicker of something unreadable crossed her eyes, quickly masked, but she didn’t break eye contact, projecting an unwavering defiance. She subtly shifted her weight, placing herself between Marius and the most sensitive research materials, a protective barrier.

Rhys’s hand didn’t rest near his axe; he was already cleaning it, the rhythmic scrape of steel on stone beginning *before* Marius finished speaking, a preemptive act, a silent promise of defense. He didn’t look at Marius, but maintained a focused intensity on his task. *He’s afraid,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in Seris’s mind. *Afraid of what you might find, and what that means for the Order.* Rhys’s gaze flickered toward the shadowed corner, then returned to his work, the cleaning becoming more deliberate, more forceful.

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile. “A convenient answer.” He stepped closer, his voice dropping to a near whisper. He didn’t simply step back; he circled Seris slowly, like a predator assessing its prey, measuring her reactions, gauging her strength. “The Bloom…it remembers. Echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep.”

He stepped back, his gaze lingering on a dried specimen of rare mountain moss, then swept over their workspace. “I will be watching you. Both of you.”

Rhys didn’t just clean his axe; he tested the edge with his thumb, a dangerous gesture that spoke volumes, a clear warning. He didn’t look at Marius, but the action was deliberately provocative. Seris’s fingers trembled, but she clenched her hand around the pouch, concealing the movement, grounding herself in the memory of Elara.

“We have no secrets, Inquisitor.” She said the words with deliberate conviction, projecting an unwavering confidence she barely felt. Her voice was steady, but her pulse thrummed in her ears.

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing her with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has secrets, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to sacrifice to keep them buried?” He offered a curt nod, then swept out, leaving a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being watched. The scent of ozone, faint but noticeable, followed him, a subtle reminder of the Order’s power.

Silence descended. Rhys continued to sharpen his axe, the rasp a small, rhythmic sound against the tension, his gaze fixed on the closed door. Seris stared at the door, her heart pounding a slow, steady rhythm. He hadn't found secrets…yet. But she knew, with chilling certainty, that he would return. And when he did, he wouldn’t seek information. He would seek control. She clutched the pouch, the worn fabric a small, fragile comfort. She wouldn’t let him.

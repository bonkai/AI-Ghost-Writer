He wasn’t dissolving, not exactly. More an unraveling—threads of awareness pulled taut, then released. A fall, not down, but *inward*, into a space that wasn’t space, a silence that wasn’t silence. The sensation lingered, a disorienting drift, before a jarring halt. Ghosts of memory—the chair, the monitor—faded, not abruptly, but with a slow, dissolving grace. He clung to them briefly, a pang of loss, before they dissolved completely.

He wasn’t *what* he was, either. He attempted to flex a hand, to shift weight, but found…nothing. No limbs, no body, only diffuse awareness, a point of consciousness adrift. A tremor of fear quickly gave way to a strange lightness. The absence of weight, of form, was unsettling, yet…exhilarating. He registered a fleeting sense of panic, then deliberately suppressed it. Wanting felt…wrong.

He reached out, not with a hand, but with will. He focused on a plain brown volume on a towering shelf, and *pushed*. Not physically, but with the force of his disorientation, his desperate need for solidity. A flicker of frustration sparked within him. The book didn’t move, but the air around it shimmered. He strained, pushing harder, and a single page within the book fluttered, then another. A distortion spread, like heat rising, and he felt a strange pull, a connection forming. He almost *tasted* the knowledge – a metallic tang on his nonexistent tongue, followed by the faintest scent of aged paper and leather. The effort left him breathless, a dizzying wave of exhaustion. He recoiled, momentarily overwhelmed.

A word solidified on the page, glowing with inner light: *Listen.*

Listen? To what? The silence? It wasn’t silent, not really. Beneath the absence of sound, a hum throbbed – a heartbeat in the vastness. He focused, and the hum resolved into a chorus of whispers, too faint to decipher. He directed a question towards the source of the whispers: *Where am I?*

The air around him seemed to thicken, to *respond*. A presence settled over his awareness, ancient and layered. “You are a resonance, a fragment of awareness within the Great Library. A place where stories are not just told, but *lived*.” The voice wasn't a sound, but a feeling. It felt…old.

He concentrated, directing a spark of defiance towards the presence: *What do you want from me?*

A dry, rustling sound, like parchment crumbling, echoed in his awareness. “Want? A quaint notion. We do not ‘want.’ We *are*. And you, little resonance, are…new. A potential echo.” The response was measured, almost detached. 

*What am I?* he thought, the question echoing with desperate need.

The presence didn’t immediately answer. Instead, a wave of images flooded his mind – fleeting glimpses of countless stories, lives lived, worlds imagined. Knights battling dragons, lovers under starlit skies, cities rising and falling. The images were overwhelming. He instinctively pulled back, shielding his awareness, struggling to regain control.

“You are a reader,” the presence finally said, its tone gentle. “And now, you are part of the story.” The voice calmed the chaotic images, bringing a sense of order.

A pause, then a final whisper. "Welcome home." A warmth spread through his awareness, a sense of belonging. He felt…absorbed.

He tried again to force a sound, any sound, but found only silence. He focused all his energy, willing a response, but nothing came. The library held its breath. He pushed harder, desperation building, and a single word formed on another page, distant and faint: *Soon.* 

What did it mean? Soon what? The question hung unanswered. He strained again, focusing all his will, and felt a faint tremor run through the shelves. A page fluttered, then another, and a single phrase, barely legible, appeared: *Patience, little echo. Your story…begins with listening.* The phrase felt less like an answer and more like a directive. He felt a strange compulsion to obey, to surrender to the silence and *listen*. The library wasn’t just waiting; it was *watching*.

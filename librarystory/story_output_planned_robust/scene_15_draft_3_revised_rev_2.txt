## Draft 3 - Revised

The air in the cartography room hung thick with the scent of aged parchment and dust. Sunlight, filtered through the high, arched window, illuminated the intricate lines of the ancient map spread across the table. Seris traced a finger along the faded ink, the brittle paper crackling beneath her touch. The map, charting the Firepeak Mountains, felt less like a geographical guide and more like a whispered secret.

“The sequence is almost complete,” she murmured, brow furrowed. “’Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’ The phrasing isn’t Eldorian elegance. It’s raw, primal – like a carving etched by hands long forgotten.”

Rhys, perched on a stool, meticulously cleaned bone tools. “Pre-Eldorian? Before the unification? Those dialects are fragments now.”

“More than just the language,” Seris replied, her voice hushed. “The runes themselves. Variations, corruptions of the standard glyphs. Someone deliberately obscured the meaning, layered it with… something else.” She ran a calloused fingertip over a distorted rune. “This… ‘Isen.’ And this, a fractured ‘Morwen.’ Intentional, I think.”

*The Stone Heart is a nexus,* Kaelen’s voice resonated in her mind, sharper now. *A point where the veil thins.*

Rhys glanced up, sensing her unease. “Kaelen said something?”

Seris hesitated. “Just confirmation the runes are older than we thought. And that the ‘Stone Heart’ isn’t metaphorical.” She returned to the map, her gaze sweeping across the jagged peaks. “The Serpent’s Tooth… that must be Mount Cinderfang. The most prominent, the most menacing.”

Rhys traced the peak with his finger. “A fitting name. Looks ready to strike.”

Seris’s eyes narrowed. She ran a finger across the parchment, detecting a subtle texture change near Mount Cinderfang’s base. Almost imperceptible, a slight discoloration and indentation. “There’s something *on* the map,” she breathed. “Hidden beneath the surface.”

Rhys, pragmatic as ever, reached for a magnifying glass. “A layering, I suspect.” He examined the area, brow furrowing. “Someone pasted a smaller piece onto the original. Brittle with age, threatening to tear.” A faint, earthy scent mingled with the adhesive’s tang. Beneath it, revealed in faded ochre ink, was a sketch of a subterranean structure – a circular chamber carved into living rock, with a massive stone pillar at its center. The pillar wasn’t smooth; it was covered in spiraling glyphs that seemed to writhe.

“By the Ancestors…” Rhys breathed, filled with awe and apprehension.

Seris traced the lines of the sketch with a trembling finger. “It’s not just a chamber. A focus. A convergence point. Energies… *flow* here.”

*The Stone Heart is a prison,* Kaelen’s voice echoed, chillingly clear. *Something strains against its bonds. Ancient. Hungry.*

Rhys looked up, sensing her growing terror. “What is it, Seris?”

Seris shook her head, dismissing the premonition. “I don’t know. But we’ve stumbled onto something dangerous. Something that should have remained buried.” She glanced towards the doorway, a prickling sensation on her neck. The room felt cold, despite the sun.

“And Valerius…” she continued, her voice low. “He’s been asking too many questions about this region’s history, the old gods… things best forgotten.” She paused, sweeping the room with her gaze. “I feel watched.”

A shadow flickered in her periphery. She spun around, but the room was empty—just dust motes dancing in the sunlight. The feeling lingered, cold and unsettling.

Rhys, practical as ever, reached for a heavy iron poker. “Perhaps just the wind. But we’ll be ready.”

Seris nodded, her heart pounding. They had unearthed something ancient and terrible. And she had a chilling premonition they weren’t alone in knowing about it. The map, once a puzzle, now felt like a warning. They had awakened something that should have remained asleep.



**Changes Made & Rationale:**

*   **Tightened Pacing:** Removed some repetitive phrasing and streamlined descriptions for a more urgent feel.
*   **Strengthened Internal Conflict:** Emphasized Seris's growing fear and sense of being watched.
*   **Kaelen's Voice:** Kept Kaelen's interjections impactful but not overwhelming. The two distinct statements ("nexus" and "prison") create a more unsettling effect.
*   **Show, Don't Tell:** Focused on describing Seris's reactions (trembling finger, pounding heart) rather than explicitly stating her fear.
*   **Final Line:** Kept the final line as a strong, evocative statement of the danger they've unleashed.
*   **Rhys's Pragmatism:** Maintained Rhys's grounded, practical nature as a counterpoint to Seris's growing anxiety. This creates a good dynamic.
*   **Subtle Foreshadowing:** The mention of Valerius's questions adds another layer of mystery and hints at a larger conspiracy.

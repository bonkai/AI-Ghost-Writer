## Scene 4 - Draft 3

The air tasted of ash and regret. Rain threatened, clinging to the oppressive stillness of the courtyard. Kaelen drifted through the crumbling stone, a prickling awareness settling over him – not with witnessing, but with *being* witnessed. A resonance, faint but insistent, like a half-forgotten melody. It felt…focused, directed at the base of the weathered statue.

A boy, barely ten summers old, darted into the space, clutching something hidden. He pressed a tarnished locket beneath a loose stone – a forgotten deity barely discernible beneath layers of grime. His fingers trembled, a silent plea escaping his lips.

Kaelen’s attention sharpened. The locket wasn’t gold or silver, but a dull, grey metal, cool to the imagined touch. Its carvings weren’t ornamental, but precise, almost mathematical. A phantom ache resonated within him – not in a hand he once possessed, but a ghost of muscle memory, a longing for tactile connection. He strained, attempting to *see* through the metal, to decipher the symbols… and felt a faint resistance, a warding.

Lucian emerged from the archway, solidifying from the shadows. The dim light seemed to bend around him, not simply fall on him. He didn’t move, simply observed the boy, his presence a weight in the courtyard. Lucian *knew* the boy was there, even before he fully materialized.

The boy froze, his face draining of color. He didn't meet Lucian’s gaze.

Lucian advanced, his boots crunching on the gravel. He didn’t demand, he *implied*. “What have you concealed?” His voice held a quiet certainty, an accusation already delivered.

The boy hesitated, then extended a trembling hand, revealing a handful of wildflowers – a pathetic offering. The scent, faint but present, seemed to momentarily disrupt Lucian’s composure. A flicker of something unreadable crossed the Inquisitor’s features – quickly masked by practiced control. It was a fleeting moment, but Kaelen sensed a vulnerability in Lucian, a ghost of something… lost.

“Lies.” Lucian’s voice was chipped ice, measured and precise. He seized the boy’s arm, forcing him to drop the flowers. “What were you concealing near the statue?”

A coldness settled over Kaelen, mirroring the boy’s fear. He couldn't intervene directly, but focused his intent, channeling a subtle disorientation through the air around Lucian. A momentary drop in temperature, a flicker in the periphery of Lucian's vision. Just enough to make him stumble, a brief loss of balance, and a tightening of his jaw in annoyance.

Valerius stepped forward, holding up the locket. “A relic of the Archivists. A forgotten order dedicated to preserving the lore of Eldoria.” He held the locket up to the light, studying it with a scholar’s curiosity. “They weren't merely preservers, Lucian. They were its protectors.”

He turned back to the boy, his voice calm. “Take him to the Citadel. He will be questioned. And this…” He held up the locket. “…will be added to our collection. See it cataloged with care. A full report on its composition and any accompanying symbols.”

As the Inquisitors led the boy away, Kaelen lingered. The rain began to fall, washing over the stone. He reached out, his spectral hand passing through the cold, damp stone of the statue. A faint resonance pulsed beneath his touch, a whisper of power. It wasn't a memory, but a pull, a connection. A fragmented image flickered in his mind: not a vast library, but a single, intricately carved key. 

He wasn't merely an observer. He was bound to this place, to this history. The locket wasn’t just a relic. It was *part* of the key. A key to unlocking a forgotten past, and perhaps, to understanding his own purpose. The rain fell harder, washing away the dust of ages, revealing a hidden truth. The Archivists hadn’t simply preserved knowledge. They had protected something. And whatever that something was, it was still here, waiting. Not hidden, but guarded. He felt a subtle shift in the energy around the statue, a tightening of the wards. The Archivists weren’t just gone. They were waiting. And he, somehow, was connected to their vigil. He sensed a faint, rhythmic pulse emanating from the statue itself, a heartbeat of forgotten power. It was a call, a silent invitation. And Kaelen knew, with growing certainty, that he was meant to answer.

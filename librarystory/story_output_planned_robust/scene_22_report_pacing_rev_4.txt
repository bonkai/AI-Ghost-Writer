*   **Initial Pacing:** The opening is slightly slow. The first three paragraphs establish dread, but linger a bit too long on internal states before revealing *why* Seris is afraid. Consider condensing the first two paragraphs.
*   **Fragmented Reveals:** The use of fragmented passages and Kaelen’s voice is effective for building tension, but the pacing of these reveals feels *slightly* uneven. The pauses between each revelation are good, but Paragraphs 7-10 (the final reveal sequence) feel rushed compared to the earlier discoveries. Extend these a little to let the impact land.
*   **Repetitive Physical Reactions:** While showing is good, the repeated descriptions of Seris stumbling/leaning on Rhys/physical reactions become noticeable after a while. Vary these or reduce their frequency.
*   **Tension Arc:** The tension *builds* well throughout the scene, but the peak feels a bit…muted. The final vision is described quickly, and Seris’s reaction, while present, doesn’t fully *explode* with the weight of the revelation. Consider expanding on the sensory details of the vision itself and Seris’s immediate, visceral response.
*   **Ending:** The ending is solid, establishing a shared purpose. However, it feels a little *too* neat. A touch of uncertainty or lingering dread might heighten the impact and leave the reader more unsettled.

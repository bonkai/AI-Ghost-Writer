## The Crimson Bloom

The scent of aged parchment and dust motes hung in the air, a familiar comfort that did little to soothe the prickle of awareness crawling across Seris’s skin. She dipped her quill into the ink, the nib scratching against the vellum as she deepened the shading on the Sunstone Bloom’s petals. A delicate crimson stain bloomed on the page, mirroring the unsettling pressure building within her.

Rhys was already scanning a crumbling tome on Firepeak’s geological formations, tracing a finger across a faded illustration of a precarious rock face. He glanced up, then back down, a subtle tightening around his jaw – a barely perceptible clenching of muscle. “The strata are…unstable. Landslides are common after rainfall.” He didn't meet her eyes.

A shadow fell across their worktable, not abrupt, but a gradual obscuring of the lamplight. Valerius hadn’t approached directly, but lingered a few feet away, observing their workspace – scattered texts, the half-finished sketch, the curated chaos – without speaking. The air seemed to thicken, each breath suddenly deliberate.

“Still chasing whispers?” he asked, his voice smooth, yet carrying a subtle weight.

Rhys straightened, offering Seris’s sketch with a slight tilt of his hand – a gesture less of presentation, more of deflection.

Valerius’s gaze followed, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. He studied it for a long moment, his expression unreadable. A flicker of something – curiosity, perhaps, or something far colder – crossed his face. He didn’t comment on the sketch itself.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly. “A rare specimen. Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon – a phenomenon rarely witnessed.”

Seris met Valerius’s gaze, her expression carefully neutral, but a pulse throbbed at her temple. “We’ve found accounts of its use in ancient healing practices, among the mountain tribes. We’re hoping to corroborate those claims.”

“Firepeak Mountain is dangerous. Many have ventured into those peaks and never returned.” The scent of his sandalwood cologne drifted towards them, cloying and unsettling.

Rhys’s fingers tightened on the tome’s page, the parchment crinkling almost imperceptibly. “We understand the risks. We’ve been meticulously researching the terrain, the weather patterns…” He avoided Valerius’s eyes, focusing intently on the faded illustrations.

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, assessing. The silence stretched, punctuated only by the crackle of the lamplight. Then, a slow smile touched his lips, but didn't reach his eyes.

“Perhaps you are prepared.” He paused. “Though the mountain…tests preparations.” His eyes locked with Seris’s, holding her gaze for a beat too long. “It always does.”

Seris held his gaze, her expression unwavering, but a tremor ran through her fingers, barely contained.

Valerius’s smile widened, unsettlingly cold. “I look forward to hearing of your discoveries…or misfortunes.” He turned, his cloak swirling around him, and moved away, leaving Seris and Rhys bathed in the flickering lamplight.

He doesn't believe we're seeking a bloom. Rhys’s hand rested briefly on Seris’s arm, a fleeting pressure – a silent question, a shared apprehension – before he returned to the text. The crimson stain on the vellum seemed to pulse, a dark bloom against the pale parchment.

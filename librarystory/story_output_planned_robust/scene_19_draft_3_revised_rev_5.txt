The library breathed with the quiet of closing. Warm light pooled from scattered lamps, leaving the upper shelves lost in shadow. Seris sketched, the charcoal gliding across the page, acutely aware of Valerius’s lingering presence. A candle flickered, the flame momentarily twisting in a draft.

A disturbance in a lamplight beam caught her eye. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed, and a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table. It wasn’t text, but a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, not as a cartographer would render it, but as a *being* – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power.

Seris traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. “Beautiful,” she breathed, a warmth blooming on her skin. She glanced up, searching for Kaelen, and met his gaze across the room. A mirroring appreciation flickered in his eyes.

“It remembers,” he said, his voice a low murmur that seemed to resonate not in the air, but *within* her.

Seris’s fingers tightened on the table’s edge. “What do you mean?”

“The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.” He stepped closer, his movements fluid and silent. “This isn’t just a depiction, Seris. It’s a key.”

A book slammed shut in the distance. Seris glanced towards the shadowed stacks, then back at Kaelen, a flicker of unease crossing her face. “Lost how?”

He paused, his gaze sweeping the library. “The Order didn’t just forbid magic. They forbade *remembering*. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories.”

Seris’s breath caught. “This isn’t about a rare healing plant, is it?”

Another book slammed shut, closer this time. Kaelen’s jaw tightened. “No. It’s far older, far more dangerous.” He stepped closer still, lowering his voice. “Valerius suspects something. He’s a guardian of the forgetting. He’ll do anything to protect it.”

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

Kaelen’s hand brushed hers, a fleeting, almost accidental touch. A spark ignited between them, quickly extinguished by the library’s chill. “Always.”

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch. A delicate fragrance – crimson, perfectly formed – momentarily filled the air. They shared a look, a silent understanding passing between them. Then, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. The scent of dust and old paper was suddenly…metallic. And from the darkness, a distinct *click* – the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing.

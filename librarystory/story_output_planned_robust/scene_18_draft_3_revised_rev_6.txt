Dust motes danced in the lamplight. Seris deepened the shading on the Sunstone Bloom’s petals, the crimson mirroring a tightening in her chest.

Rhys traced a finger across a faded illustration of a precarious rock face on Firepeak. He didn’t meet her eyes. “Landslides are common after rainfall,” he said, voice clipped. He tapped the illustration. “Shale’s unstable. Especially with the recent storms.”

A shadow fell across their worktable. Valerius didn’t approach directly, but lingered, observing. The air felt thick, weighted.

“Still chasing whispers?” Valerius asked, his voice smooth, carrying a subtle edge.

Rhys offered Seris’s sketch with a tilt of his hand – a deflection. Valerius’s gaze followed, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. He studied it, expression unreadable, then a flicker of something – dismissal, perhaps – crossed his face. He didn’t comment on the sketch itself.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly. “Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon – a rare sight. A pity such delicate things grow in such…unforgiving places.” The statement implied knowledge beyond simple botany, and a subtle warning.

Seris met Valerius’s gaze. “We’ve found accounts of its use in ancient healing practices, among the mountain tribes. We’re corroborating those claims.” A muscle ticked in her jaw, betraying her tension.

“Firepeak Mountain is…unforgiving. Many venture into those peaks and never return.” The scent of his sandalwood cologne drifted towards them, cloying, oppressive.

Rhys’s fingers tightened on the tome’s page, but he didn't look up. “We understand the risks. We’ve researched the terrain, the weather patterns…” He traced the faded lines with a calloused thumb, a little too forcefully.

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, assessing. Silence stretched, punctuated only by the crackle of the nearby lamp. Then, a slow smile touched his lips, but didn't reach his eyes.

“Perhaps you *are* prepared.” He paused, his gaze locking with Seris’s. “Though the mountain…tests preparations. It has a way of revealing true intentions.” His eyes held hers a beat too long, a silent challenge.

Seris held his gaze, her expression neutral, but the pulse in her temple throbbed. She barely breathed.

Valerius’s smile widened, unsettlingly cold. “I look forward to hearing of your discoveries…or misfortunes.” He turned, his cloak swirling around him, and moved away.

Rhys’s hand briefly covered hers, a fleeting, tight pressure, before he returned to the text. The crimson stain on the vellum seemed to pulse. A coldness settled over Seris, a certainty that Valerius hadn’t believed them, and that their journey had become far more dangerous.

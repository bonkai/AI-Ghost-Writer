*   **Repetitive Echoing:** The dialogue relies heavily on echoing phrases ("Erased?" / "Erased?", "Curated?" / explanation about curation). While this can build tension, it happens *too* frequently here and feels a bit artificial after a while. Consider varying Seris's responses more – a questioning look, a brief statement of disagreement, or a direct question instead of always echoing.
*   **Valerius's Monologues:** Valerius does a lot of *telling* instead of *showing* his power and motivations. The loom metaphor, while evocative, is a lengthy explanation delivered *to* Seris rather than something she discovers through his actions or subtle cues. Consider condensing this into a shorter statement and letting his actions (the absorbing armor, looking *through* the image) speak louder.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Seris's Tightening Grip):** The stage direction "Seris’s fingers tightened on the dagger hilt, a subtle tightening, almost imperceptible" is *excellent* visual storytelling. However, the dialogue doesn't acknowledge or *react* to this. Valerius, being perceptive, should *notice* this nonverbal cue and perhaps comment on it (even indirectly). This would add layers to their interaction.
*   **Seris's Lack of Probing:** Seris is remarkably passive. She receives cryptic statements and unsettling demonstrations of power, yet she doesn't push for clarification or challenge Valerius directly. While fear is understandable, a little more active questioning would make her a more compelling character.
*   **"Wise Decision" feels flat:** Valerius saying "A wise decision, Initiate" after Seris begins to rise feels a little on-the-nose. It telegraphs his intent too clearly. A more subtle response – a raised eyebrow, a slight smile, or simply *ignoring* her movement – would be more effective.
*   **Indirect Characterization (Valerius):** Valerius's dialogue is *very* formal and measured. This is good, but lean into it even more. Perhaps sprinkle in archaic phrasing or a deliberate lack of contractions to emphasize his age and authority.

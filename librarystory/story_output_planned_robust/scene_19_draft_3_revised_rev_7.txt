The library held its breath as the last patrons departed. Lamps cast long shadows, leaving the upper shelves veiled in darkness. Seris sketched, charcoal moving across the page, acutely aware of a presence. Not directly *on* her, but scanning the room.

A disturbance in a lamplight beam. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed, a single page detaching and fluttering down to land open on the table. Not text, but a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, rendered not as geography, but as a being – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power.

Seris traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. She glanced up, meeting Kaelen’s gaze. He held her eyes a beat longer than polite, a subtle acknowledgement passing between them – a shared understanding of the weight this knowledge carried.

“It remembers,” he said, his voice a low murmur.

Seris’s fingers tightened on the table’s edge. “What do you mean?”

He moved closer, his movements fluid and silent. “The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.” He paused, his gaze dropping to the sketch, then lifting to meet hers. “This isn’t just a depiction, Seris. It’s a key.”

A book slammed shut in the distance. Seris glanced towards the shadowed stacks, then back at Kaelen. Valerius was moving.

“Lost how?” she asked, her voice barely a whisper.

He swept his gaze across the library, a subtle tension tightening his jaw. “The Order didn’t just forbid magic. They forbade *remembering*. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories.”

Seris’s breath caught. “This isn’t about a rare healing plant, is it?”

Another book slammed shut, closer this time. Kaelen’s gaze locked with hers, a silent warning passing between them. He didn’t need to say it; Valerius was already suspecting something. “No. It’s far older, far more dangerous.” He leaned closer, lowering his voice. “Valerius suspects something. He’s a guardian of the forgetting. He’ll do anything to protect it.”

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.” 

A delicate fragrance – crimson, perfectly formed – momentarily filled the air, emanating from the sketch. She opened her eyes, meeting Kaelen’s gaze. A fleeting connection, a shared understanding, passed between them – a silent acknowledgement of the risk they now faced. Then, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. The scent of dust and old paper was suddenly…metallic. And from the darkness, a distinct *click* – the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing.

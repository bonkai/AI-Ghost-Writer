## Draft 3 - Scene 20: Flashback

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air, thick and cloying. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure – a forgotten goddess, judging by Rhys’s unearthed texts. The stone felt cold, a chill that seeped into her bones, mirroring the unsettling weight of the questions swirling in her mind. A flicker of movement in the shadows made her hand instinctively tighten around the hilt of her dagger.

“You seek to understand why I strayed from the Light, child?” A brittle voice echoed, laced with regret. Old Man Hemlock emerged, his face a roadmap of wrinkles etched by years of solitude. He didn’t settle, but paced like a caged animal, his movements jerky and agitated. “A foolish endeavor. Some doors are best left unopened – and some memories, unremembered.”

Seris didn’t turn, her senses on high alert. “I seek to understand *you*,” she replied, her voice steady despite the tremor in her hands. “And why you risked everything to preserve these…heretical texts.”

Hemlock stopped pacing, his gaze fixed on the mosaic. “I was a Scholar of the Order, once. Dedicated. Zealous. I believed in the purity of the Light.” He ran a hand over the cold stone, a haunted look in his eyes. “Then I found the Archives. Hidden beneath the Grand Cathedral. Records the Order had deliberately…lost. Or, more accurately, *buried*.”

Seris leaned forward, her breath catching. “What kind of records?”

Hemlock’s voice dropped to a rasping whisper. “Records of the Before-Times. Of a world steeped in magic, not the sanctioned blessings we offer now, but a raw, untamed force. I saw images…cities built of shimmering crystal, powered by the very lifeblood of the earth. Gardens that bloomed with bioluminescent flora, structures that *sang* with energy. And records of *why* that magic was suppressed.” He grabbed Seris’s arm, his grip surprisingly strong. “They spoke of memories. Of a civilization that remembered…too much. Memories that threatened the very foundations of our world, or so they claimed.”

He released her, stepping back into the shadows. “The Order deemed those memories dangerous. They didn’t destroy them, of course. They *buried* them. And then they built the Church upon the grave, a monument to forgetting.”

Seris felt a chill crawl down her spine. “You’re saying the Church…suppresses knowledge?”

“Suppresses, alters, *rewrites*,” Hemlock corrected, his eyes burning with feverish intensity. “I saw the documents. Rituals to…diminish the collective memory. To lull the world into a peaceful ignorance. They called it ‘The Great Quietening.’" He pointed to a faded inscription on the mosaic, depicting a swirling vortex of light. “This depicts the ‘Memory Wells’ – places where the Order siphoned away the collective memories, leaving only…compliance. They didn’t just take knowledge; they eroded the *capacity* to remember.”

He grabbed Seris’s arm again, pulling her closer. “I questioned it. I dared to ask *why*. And for that, I was branded a heretic. Stripped of my rank. Exiled.” He released her, then stumbled back, clutching his chest.

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls.

Hemlock’s gaze drifted to the shadows. “I couldn’t stand by and watch them disappear. They are a warning, child. A reminder of what we’ve lost…and what we’ve been made to forget.” A scraping sound echoed from the tunnel entrance. Hemlock whirled around, eyes wide with fear. “They suspect. They have sent observers.”

“And the Order…they know you have them?”

“They suspect. This place is warded, hidden by the very magic they seek to control.” He glanced nervously at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris. “But the wards won’t hold forever.” He touched the Sunstone Bloom sketch poking from her pack. “That flower…it’s a key. A conduit to forgotten memories. They will be coming for it, too.”

Seris met his gaze, her jaw tight. The crimson stain on the sketch felt like a brand, a painful echo of a past she was only beginning to understand. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower anymore. She was chasing a truth that the Church would kill to keep buried. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, Seris knew, with chilling certainty, that Valerius wasn’t just observing them. He was hunting them. And she had a feeling they were running out of time. The silence that followed was broken only by Hemlock’s ragged breathing and the growing sense of dread that settled over them both. The weight of forgotten histories, and the dangerous pursuit of remembering, pressed down on Seris like the stone of the ancient hearth.

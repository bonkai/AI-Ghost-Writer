*   **Rhys's Dialogue Feels Weak:** Rhys’s lines (“We’re just researchers…”, “Or would you prefer I seek this information elsewhere?”) are too direct and reactive. He feels like a sounding board rather than an active participant. His desperation is *told* through stage direction ("silent plea bordering on desperation") but not *shown* in his speech. Consider giving him lines that hint at fear or a past trauma, or lines that attempt to subtly steer the conversation away from danger but are overridden by the merchant.

*   **Seris's Voice is Strong, But Consistent Subtext Needed:** Seris is the stronger voice here, but even her dialogue could benefit from *more* subtext. While "Patience. Let him reveal his hand." is a good internal thought, it doesn't fully translate to her *delivery*. Her lines are mostly declarative. Consider adding a layer of politeness or feigned ignorance to mask her true intentions. For example, instead of directly stating they don’t have the map, she could say, “The Whisperwind Caves? A fascinating place, though sadly beyond the scope of our botanical surveys.”

*   **Merchant's Dialogue is Effective, But Repetitive:** The merchant’s veiled threats (“…other paths to the peaks”) are good, but he leans on them a little heavily. Find ways to convey his menace through more subtle cues – a shift in tone, a pointed observation, a comment about the fragility of researchers.

*   **"Convenient" Feels a Bit On-the-Nose:** The merchant's line, "Botanists who know the old paths. Convenient," is a little too direct in revealing his suspicion. It feels like he's stating the obvious. A more subtle approach would be more unsettling.

*   **The Pouch Clink is a Dialogue Opportunity:** The description of the pouch clinking is good, but could be tied into dialogue. Perhaps the merchant deliberately emphasizes the sound, or Seris makes a pointed comment about the weight of the coins.

*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The detail of Seris tracing the botanical illustration is good visual storytelling. Consider adding a line of dialogue that subtly connects to the illustration – perhaps a comment about the resilience of a particular plant, or a comparison between the plant’s hidden roots and the secrets hidden in the peaks. This would reinforce her character and add depth to the scene.

## Draft 4: The Obsidian Sphere

“Seris?”

She whirled around, heart pounding. Hemlock stood in the doorway, his expression unreadable. “Finding what you seek?”

Seris could only nod, her gaze fixed on the sphere. The room seemed to spin, and the weight of darkness pressed in, suffocating her. She felt…watched. Not by Hemlock, but by something ancient and unseen, stirring within the obsidian sphere. A tremor ran through her, not of fear, but of…recognition. A faint echo of a dream she hadn't known she possessed.

The air tasted of dust and something metallic, like old blood. The lantern light, usually comforting, felt insufficient, swallowed by the oppressive darkness. She traced the rough-hewn stone of the walls, feeling a subtle vibration beneath her fingertips, a low thrumming that resonated deep within her bones. It wasn't merely a physical sensation; it felt like a response to something *within* her.

As she reached out to touch the sphere, a surge of energy washed over her, not a shock, but a profound shift in pressure, as if the very air had thickened. Images didn’t *flash* behind her eyes, but unfolded, layered over her vision like translucent veils. Vast, crumbling cities carved into the sides of mountains bled into winding canyons filled with strange, bioluminescent flora. And coiled around the highest peak, a colossal serpentine creature, its scales shimmering with a thousand sunsets, pulsed with an internal, terrifying life. 

But it wasn’t the grandeur of the vision that stole her breath. It was the *gaze*. Amidst the chaos of the unfolding landscape, a single, ancient eye met hers – reptilian, gold-flecked, and brimming with a knowing that felt both predatory and sorrowful. It wasn’t merely seeing *her*; it was looking *through* her, stripping away layers of self until she felt utterly exposed. A strange resonance bloomed within her chest, a mirroring of the creature’s ancient weariness.

She stumbled back, a hand flying to her temple, a low moan escaping her lips. The images fractured, then receded, leaving a lingering ache behind her eyes and a coldness that seeped into her bones, settling deep within her chest. The obsidian sphere pulsed with a faint, inner light, the serpentine patterns etched into its surface seeming to writhe and shift under the flickering lantern light. She squeezed her eyes shut, fighting to anchor herself, to separate the vision from reality. A wave of dizziness washed over her, and she braced a hand against the cold stone wall.

A voice, not spoken aloud, but *felt* within the hollow of her mind, echoed the image of the serpentine creature, a resonance that vibrated through her very being: *“Awakening…”* It wasn’t a threat, exactly, but a statement of inevitability, weighted with eons of slumber and a hunger that chilled her to the core. It felt…familiar. Like a forgotten language stirring within her soul.

She slowly opened her eyes, the room seeming…different. The air was heavier, the shadows deeper. The sphere, though still dark, radiated a subtle heat, and the carvings seemed to have subtly rearranged themselves, forming a new, unsettling pattern. She took a shaky breath, trying to regain control, to understand what she had witnessed.

“Seris?” Hemlock’s voice, closer now, broke the silence. He had entered the chamber, his gaze sweeping over the sphere, then settling on her face, his expression unreadable. “You seem…disturbed.”

She tried to speak, to explain the visions, the voice, the overwhelming sense of dread, but the words caught in her throat. The weight of the unseen presence in the room pressed down on her, stifling her breath. She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her despite the subtle warmth emanating from the sphere.

“I…I saw things,” she finally managed, her voice a mere whisper. “Cities…a serpent…it felt…real. Like a memory, but not my own.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint, enigmatic smile. “Real enough, perhaps.” He reached out, his fingers hovering over the sphere, but he didn’t touch it. “Some places remember. Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, his gaze locking with hers, a flicker of something unreadable in his eyes. “And some people…are more attuned to those echoes than others.”

He paused again, studying her intently. “Be careful, Seris. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed.”

The room fell silent, save for the faint thrumming that continued to resonate beneath her feet. She looked back at the obsidian sphere, and for a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths, a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns, as if something ancient and powerful was stirring within. She was no longer certain what she had awakened, but she knew, with a chilling certainty, that her life had irrevocably changed. The darkness wasn’t just around her; it was *within* her now, a silent promise of things to come. And for the first time, she felt a strange, unsettling sense of…recognition.

*   **Over-reliance on non-verbal cues:** While showing is good, the scene *leans heavily* on descriptions of expressions and physical reactions *instead* of letting dialogue carry emotional weight. Phrases like “Seris’s gaze met Rhys’s, a shared understanding passing between them” and “Rhys didn’t need to ask what they should do next; his determined gaze met Seris’s, a silent agreement passing between them” are telling, not showing, and feel a bit lazy. Consider *how* that understanding or agreement manifests in their *words* – even a single line could be more impactful.

*   **Kaelen's voice is *too* fragmented.** While the idea of a strained connection is good, almost *all* of Kaelen’s communication is single words or short phrases. This makes it difficult to grasp the full scope of the information and feels repetitive after a while. Consider allowing Kaelen to construct *slightly* more complete sentences, even if they are still broken or halting.

*   **Rhys feels reactive.** He largely exists to support Seris and ask clarifying questions. Give him more agency and internal life. What are *his* thoughts and feelings about these revelations? What does *he* want to do? A line or two expressing his own fears or determination would strengthen his character.

*   **“Alone…?” is a weak line.** It’s a very short, simple response to a massive revelation. Consider something more evocative or questioning.

*   **The final exchange feels rushed.** Seris has a realization about the Veil, and then immediately, the scene ends with a sense of ominous weight. A few more lines exploring that realization, or hinting at the next steps, would provide a stronger conclusion.

*   **Repetitive phrasing:** The phrase "a chilling..." appears multiple times. Vary the descriptors.



Overall, the dialogue is functional and conveys the necessary information, but it lacks depth and nuance. It relies too much on telling and non-verbal cues, and the characters could benefit from more internal life and agency.

*   **Initial Setup (Paragraphs 1-2):** The opening is strong in establishing atmosphere, but the transition from describing the library to the first disturbance (dust motes) feels slightly abrupt. A beat of Seris’s internal state – anticipation, unease – could heighten tension *before* the visual cue.
*   **Revelation & Internal Experience (Paragraphs 3-7):** The core of the scene – the communication with the sketch – is well-paced *within itself*. The layering of sensory detail (warmth, scent) is effective. However, this section feels somewhat isolated. The external pressure of Valerius (established in paragraph 1) is almost entirely absent, lessening the overall tension. Interweaving his presence – a subtle shift in his focus, a tightening of his energy – would create a more compelling dynamic.
*   **Rising Action & Climax (Paragraphs 8-10):** The final three paragraphs deliver a significant tension spike. The escalating details – book slamming, extinguishing lamp, metallic scent, latch clicking – are excellent. The pacing here is *exactly* where it needs to be – quickening as the danger becomes apparent.
*   **Overall:** The scene’s biggest weakness isn’t pacing *within* sections, but the uneven distribution of tension. The middle section, while intriguing, lacks sufficient external pressure to maintain momentum. Consider subtly re-introducing Valerius’s presence throughout, not just at the very end. This would create a more sustained sense of threat and make the final reveal more impactful.

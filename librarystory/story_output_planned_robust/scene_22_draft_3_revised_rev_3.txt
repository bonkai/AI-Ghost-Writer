## Scene 22 - Draft 3

Seris closed her eyes, fear a cold hand on her breath. She pressed her palms against the cool stone, grounding herself. This wasn’t just about lost lore. They’d stumbled into a conspiracy that reached the highest echelons of power, and time was slipping away. A suffocating dread settled over her. “We need to be careful,” she whispered, her voice tight. “But careful won’t be enough.”

Rhys nodded, jaw clenched, scanning the shadowed alcove as if expecting observers. “What are they so afraid of? What *is* this ‘dangerous knowledge’?” He ran a hand through his hair, agitation clear in his movements.

“Kaelen,” Seris focused on their mental connection, “anything more about why the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? Its content?”

Pages turned rapidly, the air crackling with energy. Dust motes danced in the light. Finally, the pages stilled, revealing a fragmented passage in archaic script.

*“The Gardener cultivated not merely flora, but memoria – the very fabric of recollection. They understood Eldoria’s history wasn’t a singular truth, but a tapestry woven from countless perspectives, altered and pruned by those in power. They preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…the echoes of worlds that might have been.”*

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. “Lost narratives? Alternate histories?”

“So, they weren’t just preserving facts,” Rhys mused, pacing a short circle. “They were preserving *possibilities*.”

*“The Order believed a unified history was essential for order,”* Kaelen’s voice resonated in her mind, laced with urgency. *“They feared acknowledging multiple realities would unravel Eldorian society – ‘the fracturing of the Weave.’”*

“The Weave?” Seris repeated, a chill running down her spine. 

A new page displayed a complex diagram – a swirling vortex of interconnected lines and symbols. *“The Weave is Eldoria’s collective consciousness, the shared belief system binding the realm. The Gardener’s knowledge threatened to tear holes in it, unleashing chaos.”*

Rhys leaned closer, studying the diagram. “So, they weren’t just suppressing information, they were suppressing…ideas? Different ways of seeing the world?”

“It seems so.” A memory surfaced – Valerius’s veiled warnings, his subtle steering away from the Bloom. It wasn’t about landslides. It was about control. Betrayal tightened her chest. “Valerius…he knew.”

*“The Order still exists, operating from the shadows,”* Kaelen’s voice was strained. *“They’ve infiltrated every level of Eldorian society, manipulating events. Valerius is one of their most trusted agents.”*

Seris’s blood ran cold. The man who’d seemed so reasonable, so concerned. She’d trusted him, confided in him. A bitter laugh escaped her lips. “He let us dig, just enough to understand the danger…to lead us into a trap.” She clenched her fists, anger burning.

Rhys met her gaze, grim and resolute. “We need to be smarter. Understand what they’re protecting, and why. What *is* so dangerous about these ‘lost narratives’?” He turned back to the diagram, tracing a line with his finger, a determined set to his jaw. 

A final page turned, revealing a haunting sentence in faded ink. *“The Gardener’s greatest secret was not what was lost, but what was deliberately forgotten.”*

Seris stared at the words, dread washing over her. Deliberately forgotten. What had been erased from Eldorian history? What truths lay buried beneath the narrative of the Founding Kings and the gods?

“Kaelen,” she said, her voice trembling. “Anything more? About what was *deliberately forgotten*?”

Silence stretched, agonizingly long. Then, a single, chilling whisper echoed in her mind.

*“The Gardener remembered a time before Eldoria…before the Veil fell.”*

Seris gasped, stumbling back a step. Rhys caught her arm, steadying her. "Before Eldoria? What does that even mean?"

Kaelen didn't respond immediately. The silence felt heavier now, pregnant with a forgotten history. Finally, a faint echo resonated in Seris's mind, barely a whisper, a tremor of ancient memory.

*“A time when Eldoria…wasn’t alone.”* 

Seris’s breath hitched. The weight of that single sentence pressed down on her, a chilling premonition of a past far older, and far more dangerous, than she could have imagined. She leaned heavily on Rhys, her knuckles white as she gripped his arm.

“Alone…?” she breathed, the word barely audible. “What do you mean, Kaelen? What was here *before*?”

A flicker of something – a fragmented image, a sensation of vastness and…otherness – brushed against her mind, so fleeting she almost dismissed it. 

*“Other worlds…other beings…a connection severed.”* The voice was weaker now, strained, as if reaching across an impossible distance. *“The Veil…it wasn’t meant to protect Eldoria…it was meant to *separate* it.”*

The image sharpened for a fleeting moment – a swirling, iridescent landscape unlike anything she'd ever seen, filled with impossible structures and beings of pure light. Then, darkness.

Seris cried out, clutching her head. Rhys held her tighter, concern etched on his face. "Seris! What is it? What's happening?"

She shook her head, trying to clear the images. "I…I saw something. A world…before. Before Eldoria. Kaelen showed me…" She trailed off, realization dawning. "The Veil…it wasn't about keeping things *out*…it was about keeping something *in*…or keeping us from something *else*."

The chilling weight of the forgotten past settled upon them, heavier than ever. They weren’t just uncovering a conspiracy; they were peeling back the layers of a deliberately obscured history, a history that threatened to shatter everything they thought they knew about their world.

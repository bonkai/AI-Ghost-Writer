*   **Repetitive Questions:** The character repeats "Observation?" multiple times. While understandable given his fear and confusion, it feels redundant and slows the pacing. Consider varying the phrasing or showing his unease through internal monologue/action instead.
*   **Lack of Character Voice:** The dialogue (or rather, the *lack* of traditional dialogue) is largely internal and relies on impressions/sensations. This is a stylistic choice, but it risks feeling detached. Even within this framework, the character’s *attempts* at communication could be more distinct. What is *his* personality? Is he naturally assertive, cautious, desperate? These traits should subtly influence how he *tries* to communicate, even if the responses are non-verbal.
*   **Passive Reception:** The character is almost entirely *receiving* information. He’s constantly being scanned, flooded with sensations. While this establishes the power dynamic, it makes him feel less proactive. Give him more opportunities to *actively* try to influence the situation, even if those attempts fail.
*   **"Forgotten" – Impact & Follow-Up:** The final word, "Forgotten," is powerful. However, it lands somewhat flat because there’s no immediate reaction from the character. A beat of internal turmoil, a desperate attempt to understand *why* he's forgotten, would amplify the emotional impact.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The presence consistently avoids direct answers. This is good, creating mystery. However, lean into this more. Instead of simply *not* answering, have the presence respond with something *adjacent* to the question, a deflection that reveals more about *it* than about the answer.
*   **"A Repository, A Bridge" – Feels Expository:** While not strictly *bad*, the line "A repository. A bridge." feels a bit like an info-dump. Could this information be revealed more organically through the sensations the character experiences? Perhaps he *feels* the weight of countless stories, *senses* connections between different eras, instead of being *told* what the place is.

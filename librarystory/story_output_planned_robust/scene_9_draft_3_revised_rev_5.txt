## Draft 3 - Scene 9

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light illuminating the alcove, the air thick with the scent of aged paper and a faint, coppery tang. Seris traced a finger along the spine of a crumbling tome, brittle leather flaking beneath her touch. “Before kingdoms, before gods… the stories say there were currents. Understandings. The Echo… it holds fragments, doesn’t it?” A pause, her gaze drifting across the shadowed shelves. A subtle tension tightened her shoulders.

Kaelen’s presence resonated not from lips, but through the swirling patterns in the pages before her – a tapestry, she sensed, and a single pulled thread could unravel everything. He didn’t *answer* directly, instead guiding her attention to a manuscript detailing the Church’s rise. 

A stone skittered from the archway. Seris glanced up, annoyance flashing across her face before she quickly masked it, a practiced defense. “The Church branded the Order heretical, accused them of forbidden magic.” She crossed her arms, bracing against a chill that seemed to seep from the stones themselves. “They said it was power-hungry. A corruption.”

The pages turned themselves, settling on a passage describing the Order’s practices. Seris touched a nearby book, a faint tremor running through its spine. She closed her eyes, concentrating, a subtle ache blooming in her fingertips as if the book itself were *breathing*. “They fear what they don’t understand. The Order’s magic wasn’t about *doing*… it was about *listening*.” 

A pause. The air grew noticeably colder, the pages trembling. “Listening to what?” she breathed, the question barely a whisper, more to herself than to any presence.

A fractured sensation – not an answer, but a feeling of immense weight, like holding a shattered mirror. Memories were dangerous. The Echo wasn’t a simple archive, but a shattered reflection of countless possibilities. He didn’t explain; he *showed* her the feeling of fractured knowledge.

“Fractured how?” she pressed, her voice tight.

The air chilled further, the pages rippling faster. “Threads fraying, twisting… poisoning the whole cloth. Not malice, but dissonance. Echoes bleeding into each other.” The sensation wasn't delivered as words, but as a chilling visual – a vibrant tapestry unraveling into grey threads.

Seris’s breath hitched. “Unravel the world?” She stopped her restless pacing, her gaze fixed on the swirling patterns, a flicker of fear in her eyes.

“Not instantly. But a weakened weave… creates tears. Allows things *through*.”

“So the Church isn’t just suppressing knowledge… they’re protecting us?” A strange weight settled on her chest, unease growing. She looked away from the swirling patterns, unable to meet their unsettling gaze.

“Protecting themselves. They claim to be the sole arbiters of truth, guardians of Eldoria. They see the Echo as chaos. Centuries of erasing the Order’s legacy.” The implication hung heavy in the air – the Church wasn't benevolent, but self-serving.

Leaning against a bookshelf, Seris felt exhaustion wash over her, the weight of centuries pressing down. “And the Firepeak Mountains? The rumors of a hidden archive?”

The pages stilled, then rippled faster, a gust of wind rattling the windowpanes, extinguishing the candle. Seris jumped, her heart pounding. Darkness descended. “The Firepeaks are a nexus point, where the veil is thin. The Order built a citadel there, safeguarding what remains.”

A sensation – not a voice, but a presence within her mind, a gentle nudge. *“A confluence. Fragments drawn together by your presence. What remains of the Order, seeking someone who will listen.”*

Seris swept her gaze across the shadowed alcove, dread tightening her chest. “Why me?”

*“The Church knows you’re close to the truth. They will be coming.”* The air grew frigid. A spectral image flickered in the swirling ink – a hooded figure, silhouetted against a burning city. It vanished as quickly as it appeared, leaving a lingering scent of smoke. 

The pages settled. A single word formed in the swirling ink, glowing with an ethereal light: *“Remember.”* Then, silence. A silence so profound it felt like a physical weight. The stone of the bookshelf felt subtly wrong – distorted, as if whispering secrets against her back. Seris wrapped her arms around herself, shivering, the chill deeper than the cold air, a premonition settling in her bones.

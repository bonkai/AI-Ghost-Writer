*   **Hemlock’s voice is *very* consistent – perhaps too much.** He’s perpetually smooth, assessing, and subtly threatening. While consistency is good, a *little* variation – a hint of genuine concern, even feigned – might make him feel more complex. It risks making him feel one-dimensional.
*   **“Just a bit lightheaded” feels weak.** Given the intensity of what’s happening, and the character’s attempt to hide it, a more convincing (though still understated) line would be better. Something like, "I'm…fine. Just need a moment," delivered through gritted teeth, would read stronger.
*   **The dialogue is functional, but lacks significant subtext.** The characters *say* what they mean. Opportunities for veiled threats or hidden motivations are present but underutilized. For example, Hemlock's lines about ambition and station could be delivered with more ambiguity, leaving the reader to question his true motives. Is he genuinely concerned about the Atheneum’s secrets, or is he protecting his own power?
*   **The repeated emphasis on the character's internal state is *shown* well, but the dialogue doesn’t reflect it enough.** The character is clearly distressed, but their spoken lines are relatively calm. A little more desperation or urgency in their voice would enhance the tension.
*   **“Please. I need to understand” is a bit direct.** Consider a more desperate, almost whispered plea. Something like, “I… I have to know,” would convey more vulnerability.
*   **Hemlock’s final lines feel a little on-the-nose.** The “warning” feels stated rather than implied. Consider delivering this sentiment through a more subtle gesture or a veiled threat.

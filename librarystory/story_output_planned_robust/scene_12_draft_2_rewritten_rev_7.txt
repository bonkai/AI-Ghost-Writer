The oil lamp sputtered and died, plunging the archive into darkness. The air grew heavy, stagnant. A draft, colder than stone, swept through the chamber, carrying the scent of dust and something metallic, faintly floral. Seris instinctively reached for her dagger.

Valerius coalesced from the darkness, not entering, but *becoming* visible. Two heavily armored Guardians materialized beside him, their movements unsettlingly silent. 

He paused before the open tome, gesturing to an illustration of the Aethel. “What do you make of this civilization?”

“Lost to time,” Seris said, her fingers tightening on the dagger hilt. “Or so the histories claim.”

Valerius tilted his head. “Erased? Or… repurposed?” He paused, his gaze unwavering. “Consider how threads can be cut, woven into something new.”

A prickling sensation rose on the back of Seris’s neck. She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius. The metallic scent intensified. Beneath his robes, a faint, pulsing glow – the same ethereal light as the illustrations – became visible.

“What do you mean by ‘repurposed’?”

Valerius’s smile widened, a predator’s acknowledgment. “Some doors are best left unopened.” He gestured to a Guardian. “Escort the Initiate back to her chambers. She needs rest.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. Seris noticed the polished steel of its armor wasn’t reflecting the darkness, but *absorbing* it. Her attention remained fixed on Valerius. He stood before the tome, his gaze fixed on the illustration, but he wasn’t looking *at* the image. He was looking *through* it. 

In that moment, Seris understood. The Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound. Valerius held the threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer.

As the Guardian reached for her arm, Valerius met her gaze, a silent warning passing between them. She didn’t bother signaling her departure. She already knew it wouldn't be permitted, and she knew, with chilling clarity, that she had to find a way back to this archive—alone. The Guardian’s grip tightened, but Seris didn't resist. Not yet. She needed to understand what Valerius was hiding, and she had a feeling the answers weren't found in the light. She allowed herself to be led away, a single thought echoing in her mind: *This archive isn't protecting the past. It's a prison.*

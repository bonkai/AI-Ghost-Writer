*   **Overly Defensive Dialogue (Seris):** Seris’s initial lines (“The histories claim…”, “What do you mean…”, “Are you suggesting…”) are *too* reactive and defensive. While understandable given the situation, it reads as a bit simplistic. Consider subtly layering her questions with curiosity *as well* as suspicion. Instead of immediately accusing the Church, perhaps a line like, "The official accounts don't mention anything of the Aethel wielding power. Was there a reason for that omission?" would be more nuanced.
*   **Valerius’s Monologues:** Valerius often delivers information in extended explanations rather than engaging in a true dialogue. While his character is clearly controlling, the extended explanations feel like exposition dumps disguised as conversation. Shorten his responses, forcing Seris to draw more conclusions herself. For example, instead of “Fabricated is such a… crude word…,” try “Curated, Initiate.” and *let her react*.
*   **Repetitive Internal State:** The repeated emphasis on Seris’s fear and tightening grip (dagger, hand) becomes redundant. Show, don’t tell. A single, well-placed physical reaction is more impactful than multiple reiterations.
*   **“How?” Question – Missed Opportunity:** Seris asks “How?” about the containment, and Valerius *completely* avoids answering. This is good for establishing his control, but it feels abrupt. A *brief* non-answer – a cryptic phrase or a dismissive gesture – before ordering the escort would be more effective than simply ignoring the question. It would add a layer of psychological pressure.
*   **Indirect Characterization - Potential:** While Valerius’s control is evident, explore opportunities to reveal his character through *how* he controls the conversation. Does he use condescension? Manipulation? Subtle threats? Infuse his dialogue with these nuances.
*   **Seris’s Internal Monologue Overpowers Dialogue:** The final paragraph is almost entirely internal thought. While it’s useful for establishing her resolve, it cuts off the potential for a final, charged exchange with Valerius or the Guardian. Even a silent, defiant look would be more impactful than simply fading to internal thought.

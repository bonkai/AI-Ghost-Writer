The air in the library shifted, a subtle pressure building against Seris’s skin. Dust motes danced in the sunlight, not swirling, but suspended, as if holding their breath. A tremor ran through her perception, a dissonance in the ancient stones. Then, a tightening behind her eyes, the sensation of being watched.

“Something’s happening,” she breathed, bracing a hand against a towering bookshelf.

Rhys, studying a faded map, looked up, concern creasing his brow. “What is it?”

Before Seris could answer, the library shimmered—a distortion of light and shadow, a breath held too long. The scent of old parchment and beeswax was tainted with a metallic tang, acrid and wrong.

Then, the visions hit—not images, but *feelings*. The weight of ages, colossal power, and a profound sorrow that resonated deep within her soul. She saw a network of stars woven with threads of energy, a celestial tapestry stretched across the void. Beings of grace and power moved within it, not crafting, but *sustaining* the constellations. They channeled energy, preventing collapse, holding back a darkness beyond the stars.

A fracture. A discordant note, a shadow creeping into the light. A figure cloaked in obsidian unleashed a force that severed the network, allowing darkness to seep through. The stars wept, not as a reaction to destruction, but as a consequence of unraveling.

Seris gasped, stumbling back, hand flying to her temple. Blood tasted metallic on her tongue.

Rhys gripped her arm, steadying her. “Seris! What’s happening?”

Kaelen’s voice, fragmented and strained, echoed in her mind. *“…Nexus…responding…resonance…ancient…before…Sundering…bleeds…”* Not thoughts, but raw sensation, pain lacing the edges.

The library rippled, shelves blurring. Seris felt a pull, a dizzying sensation of witnessing events centuries past.

“Kaelen? What is this?” she managed, voice trembling.

*“…memory…imprinted…Nexus…place…stars…bound…weakens…seal…”* Fragments, like shards of glass, each one sharp.

The visions intensified. The network wasn’t a shield *against* darkness, but a barrier *containing* it—a primordial force threatening to consume Eldoria. The figure in obsidian hadn’t unleashed a force, but severed connections, dismantling the network deliberately. The Sundering wasn’t a cataclysm, but a desperate containment—a sealing of breaches.

“The Sundering…” Seris breathed, realization dawning. “It wasn’t a cause…it was a *containment*.”

*“…Nexus…key…vulnerability…contained…sealed…weakening…bleedthrough…”* Kaelen’s voice faded, a dying ember.

Then, a specific image crystallized—a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian. It pulsed with power, a cold dread settling in her bones. But now, she saw cracks spiderwebbing across the surface, the light flickering.

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling old texts. “A ward…a seal…and it’s failing.”

Rhys squeezed her arm, concern etched on his face. “What is it, Seris? What does it mean?”

“It’s connected to the Nexus…to the source of the network…a prison…and the seal is breaking.”

*“…Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…”* Kaelen’s voice barely a whisper. *“…hurry…”*

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling.

“Firestone Mountains?” Rhys asked, voice tight. “What’s happening?”

Seris closed her eyes, gathering her thoughts. “The Nexus isn’t just a source of power, it’s a prison. Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible. The seal is failing, and the Firestone Mountains…they’re where the cracks are widest.”

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within the Nexus breaks free.” The fate of Eldoria, she knew, hung precariously in the balance.

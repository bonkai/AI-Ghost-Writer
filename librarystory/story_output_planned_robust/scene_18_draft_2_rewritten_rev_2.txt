## Draft 3: The Crimson Bloom

The scent of aged parchment and dust motes hung in the air, a familiar comfort that did little to soothe the prickle of awareness crawling across Seris’s skin. She dipped her quill into the ink, the nib scratching against the vellum as she deepened the shading on the Sunstone Bloom’s petals.

“A remarkably resilient specimen, considering its purported habitat,” she murmured, calibrating the volume to carry. “The texts claim it thrives on sheer cliffs, nourished by glacial melt and…a peculiar form of lichen.”

Rhys was already scanning a crumbling tome on Firepeak’s geological formations, tracing a finger across a faded illustration of a precarious rock face. “The strata are…unstable, to say the least. Landslides are common, particularly after heavy rainfall. Makes access challenging.” He glanced at Seris, a shared glance of practiced composure.

A shadow fell across their worktable, not abrupt, but a gradual obscuring of the lamplight. Valerius hadn’t approached directly, but lingered a few feet away, his presence radiating quiet intensity. He observed their workspace – scattered texts, the half-finished sketch, the curated chaos – without speaking. The air seemed to thicken.

“Still chasing the whispers of ancient remedies?” he asked, his voice smooth as polished stone, with an unreadable undercurrent.

Rhys straightened, forcing a casualness that didn’t quite reach his eyes. “We’ve encountered a complex text detailing several potential applications. It’s proving challenging to separate verifiable fact from folklore.” He gestured towards Seris’s sketch, a subtle offering.

Valerius’s gaze followed Rhys’s hand, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. He studied it for a long moment, his expression unreadable. A flicker of curiosity, perhaps calculation, crossed his face. He didn’t comment on the sketch.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly, his voice a low murmur. “A rare specimen, indeed. Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon – a phenomenon rarely witnessed, and even more rarely documented.”

Seris met Valerius’s gaze, her expression carefully neutral. “We’ve found several accounts of its use in ancient healing practices, particularly among the mountain tribes. We’re hoping to corroborate those claims.”

“Firepeak Mountain is a dangerous place to search for folklore,” Valerius said, his voice laced with a subtle warning. The scent of his sandalwood cologne drifted towards them, a subtle assertion of power. “Many have ventured into those peaks and never returned. The mountain claims those who underestimate its power.”

Rhys responded smoothly. “We understand the risks, of course. We’ve been meticulously researching the terrain, the weather patterns, the local flora and fauna. We’re preparing for a challenging expedition.”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, assessing, calculating. He didn’t respond immediately. The silence stretched. Then, a slow, deliberate smile touched his lips. It didn't reach his eyes.

“Perhaps,” he said, his voice dangerously soft. “Perhaps you are. In that case, I wish you good fortune. Though I suspect…the mountain will test your preparations.” He paused, his eyes locking with Seris’s. “It always does.”

Seris held his gaze, her expression carefully neutral. “We’re prepared for any eventuality.”

Valerius’s smile widened, but remained unsettlingly cold. “Indeed. I look forward to hearing of your discoveries…or your misfortunes.” He turned, his cloak swirling around him, and moved away, leaving Seris and Rhys bathed in the flickering lamplight.

He doesn't believe we're seeking a bloom. He believes we're seeking something else entirely.

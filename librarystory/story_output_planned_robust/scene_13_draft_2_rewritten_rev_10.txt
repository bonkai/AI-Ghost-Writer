Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light. Rhys leaned against a crumbling pillar, eyes fixed on the star chart I traced with a fingertip. He didn’t greet me, only observed – a stillness that felt worn, like weathered stone. A faint pressure touched my mind – an assessment, not a question. I tested my connection to Kaelen, finding it distant, muted – not blocked, but… withdrawn.

“You seek this,” he said, voice a low rumble, gesturing to the chart with a subtle nod. “What are you willing to lose?”

A prickle of unease traced my spine. He wasn’t dissuading me, merely measuring the cost. I let my gaze drift over his face – the deep lines around his eyes, the subtle clench of his jaw. Loss clung to him. I subtly reached for Kaelen, but the connection remained frustratingly faint.

“Knowledge carries responsibility,” I said, keeping my voice level.

Rhys’s lips curved in a wry smile. “Resourceful.” He paused, letting the silence stretch, the only sound the rustle of parchment. “I’ve tracked this symbol for years. I know where it leads. I can help you decipher it… for a price.”

My mind raced. Not a simple trade. I trailed my fingers over the cool stone of the chart, a faint warmth radiating from the etched lines. The library’s whispers felt oddly distant.

“What exchange?” I asked, direct.

Rhys pushed off the pillar, straightening to his full height. Not aggression, but a predator’s controlled stillness. “Information. My interests align with your research. Something was stolen – something of value.” He paused, his gaze locking with mine. “Your expertise. In return, access to knowledge beyond any library’s walls.”

He didn’t meet my gaze, his jaw tight. I quieted my connection to Kaelen, trusting instinct. The muffled link felt…wrong.

I looked down at the symbol, my fingers tracing its lines. “Specifically, what do you want me to do?”

Rhys’s lips curved into a slow smile. “You’re an Archivist. You access lost knowledge, decipher star charts, translate ancient languages. You see connections others miss.” He paused, his gaze unwavering. “You’re a conduit. And I need you to open the way.”

I met his gaze, a spark of defiance flickering within me. I wasn’t merely a tool. I was an Archivist, a seeker of truth. And I wouldn’t blindly follow him.

“What guarantees do I have this isn’t a fool’s errand? That this ‘something’ even exists?”

Rhys’s smile faded, replaced by weary resignation. “None. Only my word. And the weight of centuries.” He paused, then added, his voice barely a whisper, “But I assure you. It’s real. And its return is vital. Not just for my people… but for everyone.”

The air in the alcove crackled with tension. I stared at the ancient star chart, the symbol now pulsing with unsettling energy. I took a slow breath, meeting his gaze. “Very well. I’ll help you. But I require transparency. Tell me everything. What was taken, from whom, and why you believe *I* can help.” He didn't answer immediately, his gaze drifting to the pulsing symbol on the chart, a flicker of something unreadable crossing his face.

## Scene 9 - Draft 3

The air in the alcove tasted of dust and something older – a metallic tang that clung to the back of Seris’s throat. She ran a hand along a bookshelf, the brittle wood cool beneath her fingers. “Before the kingdoms, before the gods… there were currents. Understandings. The Echo holds fragments of them.”

Kaelen’s voice didn’t come from lips, but resonated *through* the swirling patterns in the pages before her. “Imagine a tapestry woven with every moment, every life. Pull a single thread, and the whole thing begins to fray.”

A loose stone clattered from the archway, startling Seris. She glanced up, but saw only shadows. “The Church claims the Order was heretical, practicing forbidden magic.” She crossed her arms, a defensive gesture, more to herself than to Kaelen.

The pages turned themselves, landing on a brittle manuscript detailing the Church’s founding. “The Church fears what it doesn’t understand. The Order’s magic wasn’t about wielding power, but about *listening* to the Echo.” Seris touched a nearby book, a faint tremor running through its spine and into her fingertips. She frowned, concentrating on the sensation, trying to decipher what the feeling *meant*.

“But why hide these memories? Why not share them?” Frustration tightened her jaw. She paced a short distance, the small space feeling even smaller.

A longer pause. The air grew noticeably colder, and the pages trembled violently. “Because some memories are dangerous. The Echo isn’t a benign archive. It’s fractured… corrupted.”

“Corrupted how?” Seris pressed, her voice sharper, demanding specifics.

“Certain fragments unravel the mind. Others… unravel the world.”

Seris’s breath hitched. “Unravel the world?” She stopped pacing, her gaze fixed on the swirling patterns in the pages, as if expecting a visual answer.

“Imagine that tapestry completely undone. Threads snapping, colors bleeding into nothingness.”

“So the Church isn’t just suppressing knowledge, they’re… protecting us?” A strange weight settled on her chest, a growing unease. She looked away, unable to meet the swirling patterns in the pages, as if the truth was too much to bear. 

“Protecting themselves. They believe they are the sole arbiters of truth, the guardians of Eldoria’s future. They see the Echo as a threat to their authority, a source of chaos. They’ve been systematically erasing the Order’s legacy for centuries.”

Leaning against a bookshelf, Seris felt exhaustion wash over her. “And what about the Firepeak Mountains? The rumors of a hidden archive?”

The pages stilled, then rippled again, faster. A gust of wind rattled the windowpanes, extinguishing the single candle on a nearby table. Seris jumped, her heart pounding. Darkness descended, thick and absolute. “The Firepeaks are a nexus point, where the veil between ages is thin. The Order built a citadel there, a repository for the most dangerous fragments of the Echo. It’s protected by… echoes of the past.”

A sudden chill ran down Seris’s spine. “And what about you? Are you… an echo?”

A long silence. The pages remained perfectly still. Then, a voice – not spoken, but *felt* – resonated within her mind. *“I am a confluence. A gathering of fragments, drawn together by your presence. I am what remains of the Order’s collective consciousness, seeking someone who will listen.”*

Seris swept her gaze across the shadowed alcove, a growing dread tightening her chest. “Why me?”

*“The Church knows you are close to the truth. They will be coming.”* The air grew frigid. A spectral image flickered in the swirling ink of the pages – a hooded figure, silhouetted against a burning city. It vanished as quickly as it appeared. 

The pages settled. A single word formed in the swirling ink, glowing with an ethereal light: *“Remember.”* Then, silence. A silence so profound it felt like a weight pressing down on her soul. The last vestige of warmth vanished, leaving Seris shivering in the darkness. She wrapped her arms around herself, her mind reeling.

She didn’t just think *What have I gotten myself into?* She *felt* it, a cold dread settling deep in her bones. This wasn't just about uncovering lost knowledge anymore. This was about something far more dangerous, and she was caught in the middle.

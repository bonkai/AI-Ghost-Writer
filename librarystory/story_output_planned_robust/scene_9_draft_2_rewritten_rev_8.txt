## Draft 4 - Scene 9

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light illuminating the alcove, the air thick with the scent of aged paper and a faint, coppery tang. Seris traced a finger along the spine of a crumbling tome, brittle leather flaking beneath her touch. “Before kingdoms, before gods…” She paused, her gaze drifting across the shadowed shelves. “The stories say there were currents. Understandings. The Echo… it holds fragments, doesn’t it?” She turned to Kaelen, a flicker of something unreadable in her eyes. “The Church didn’t condemn the Order overnight. It began subtly, a slow erosion.”

She gestured to a manuscript detailing the Church’s rise, but didn’t wait for Kaelen to examine it. “They didn’t declare war. They started with isolation, discrediting the Order’s teachings. Look.” She tapped a faded illustration depicting Order scholars studying celestial charts. “Accusations of consorting with… things best left undisturbed. A campaign of whispers, of eroding trust. It wasn’t about truth, but control.”

Seris touched a nearby book, a faint tremor running through its spine. She closed her eyes, concentrating, her knuckles white. When she spoke, her voice was hushed. “The Order’s magic wasn’t about *doing*… it was about *listening*. About understanding the currents, the Echo itself. They sought knowledge of what *was*, not what *could be*.” A pause. She opened her eyes, studying Kaelen’s reaction.

“Listening to what?” Kaelen asked.

Seris’s brow furrowed. “The Echo isn't a simple archive. It’s a shattered reflection of countless possibilities, fragments of what was, what could be… and what should remain lost.” She paced a short distance, then turned back, her expression intense. “Imagine a tapestry, woven with threads of every possible moment. The Order sought to understand the pattern, while the Church fears any deviation from their single, prescribed design.”

“Fractured how?” Kaelen pressed, her voice mirroring Seris’s growing urgency.

Seris ran a hand over the manuscript, her fingers tracing the faded ink. “Threads fraying, twisting… poisoning the whole cloth. Not malice, but dissonance. Echoes bleeding into each other, distorting the truth. It’s like a song played on a broken instrument – the melody is still there, but warped and unsettling.” She stopped pacing, meeting Kaelen’s gaze. “A single discordant note can unravel everything.”

“Unravel the world?” Kaelen asked, a note of concern tightening her voice.

Seris stopped, her gaze fixed on the swirling patterns within the aged paper. “Not instantly. But a weakened weave… creates tears. Allows things *through*. Things that shouldn’t be.” She paused, a flicker of fear in her eyes. “The Church claims to be protecting us from these ‘things,’ but their protection is a cage.”

“So they’re protecting us?” Kaelen challenged, her skepticism evident.

Seris shook her head, a weary sadness in her eyes. “Protecting themselves, primarily. They claim to be the sole arbiters of truth, guardians of Eldoria. They see the Echo as chaos, a threat to their authority. Centuries of erasing the Order’s knowledge… rewriting history.”

The pages stilled, then rippled faster, a gust of wind rattling the windowpanes and extinguishing the candle. Seris jumped, her heart pounding. Darkness descended, thick and sudden. “The Firepeaks are a nexus point, where the veil between worlds is thin. The Order built a citadel there, safeguarding what remains of their knowledge.”

A gentle pressure touched her mind, not a voice, but a sense of urgency, laced with desperation. *“A confluence. Fragments drawn together by your presence. What remains of the Order… seeking someone who will listen.”*

Seris swept her gaze across the shadowed alcove, dread tightening her chest. “Why me?”

*“The Church knows you’re close to the truth. They will be coming.”* The air grew frigid. A spectral image flickered briefly in the swirling ink – a hooded figure silhouetted against a burning city, flames licking at a crumbling cathedral. The image held for a beat longer, then vanished, leaving a lingering scent of smoke and ash. 

The pages settled. A single word formed in the swirling ink, glowing with an ethereal light: *“Remember.”* Beneath it, a second word appeared: *“They lied.”* Then, silence. A silence so profound it felt like a physical weight. The stone of the bookshelf felt subtly wrong – distorted, as if whispering secrets against her back. Seris wrapped her arms around herself, a cold spot on the stone sending a jolt up her arm. It felt… like a warning. She pressed her hand against the stone, and a faint tremor ran through the bookshelf, revealing a hairline crack in the wall behind it. Dust motes swirled around the crack, revealing a darkness within, a hint of something ancient and forgotten. The stone pulsed faintly beneath her fingertips.

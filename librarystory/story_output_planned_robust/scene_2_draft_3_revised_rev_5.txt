The absence wasn’t black, but a pressure—a full-VR rig lagging at the edge of perception. He willed a hand to rise, flex, *prove* something was still connected. Nothing. No arm, no body. Just… awareness. A cursor detached from any screen, adrift in static. Panic spiked—a cold, tightening sensation where his chest *should* be. He remembered the mechanics of breath, the subtle burn of exertion, but there were no lungs to fill. He tried to shout, to force a signal, but the attempt dissolved into static. Untethered. Floating in null space. He squeezed his eyes shut, a phantom reflex, and the darkness *responded*, pulsing with the effort, as if straining against a failing connection.

*Reboot. Diagnostic. Anything.* The commands looped, frantic and useless, not logical for… this. A desperate attempt to force function, to reclaim control as the system spiraled. He clung to the sequence, repeating it until the words themselves felt hollow, frayed at the edges. A flicker of corrupted code – a memory of a life, dissolving into static, then *reasserting* – a flash of his apartment, the glow of his monitors, the half-finished game… then gone.

Then, a shift. Not location, but texture. The pressure eased, replaced by a faint, ethereal glow, like the dying embers of a monitor. A hum resonated—distant, architectural, vibrating *through* his awareness. Shapes resolved—a vast, cavernous space. He wasn’t *in* darkness, but *within* something. He reached for a nearby shelf, focusing his will, and a fleeting impression of wood grain, the cool press of aged paper, flickered at the edge of his awareness – a ghost of sensation, almost painful in its brevity. He strained again, *demanding* the connection, and the impression fractured, dissolving into static, leaving behind only a phantom ache in his nonexistent fingertips.

He projected a thought, a fragile signal flare. *Hello?* It felt flimsy, lost in a hurricane, immediately followed by a wave of disorientation, as if the projection itself had weakened his connection, *draining* him.

A response came, not as sound, but as *feeling*. A gentle ping against his awareness, laced with ancient stillness. Not a voice, but an impression—patient, observing, impossibly *old*. It didn’t answer immediately. It *considered*, and with that consideration came a sense of being… cataloged. A slow, deliberate assessment, like a curator examining a newly discovered artifact.

*Aware.*

The single word resonated, and with it, the sensation of being scanned, probed. Not with light, but with something colder, more analytical. Like an admin assessing a new process, labeling, categorizing. A chill—not *through* anything, but undeniably there, settling deep within his… core? He tried to pull away, to shield his thoughts, but the scan continued, relentless and thorough, dissecting his memories, his fears, *savoring* them.

Books. Countless volumes stacked on shelves that disappeared into the gloom, their spines unreadable. He reached for one, and a flood of information washed over his awareness—cellulose and ink, the weight of centuries, a silent story waiting to be unlocked. Not a smell, but a rush of data – the ghost of forgotten languages, the faintest echo of hands that had turned these pages. A library beyond imagining. No librarian. A glitch? A trap?

“Where am I?” He projected again, desperation fraying the signal, the thought itself splintering into fragmented code. He *pushed* at the question, demanding an answer, but the effort left him feeling… depleted, as if the very act of communication was costing him something vital.

The response came slower, like a firewall actively blocking connection. A subtle weight, a sense of… amusement? Not malicious, but detached, as if observing an insect struggling in amber.

*Between.*

“Between what?” He pushed, demanding clarity, the effort leaving him feeling… drained, *hollow*.

A long pause. Unsettling. Then, a chilling revelation, delivered with the distinct impression of something *watching*, something that had seen… everything. A sensation of immense age, of infinite patience. 

*Between worlds.*

*   **Hemlock’s dialogue is *almost* too on-the-nose.** While his veiled threats are effective, the lines “some knowledge is best left undisturbed” and “The past has a way of reaching out. Sometimes, it’s best not to answer its call” are quite direct. Consider *showing* his suspicion and warning through subtle phrasing and body language, rather than explicitly stating it. For example, instead of “some knowledge is best left undisturbed,” he could simply observe, “That’s an…uncommon volume to be studying.”
*   **Seris’s repeated “I…” and “I don’t understand” feel passive.** While fear is understandable, these responses don't give her much agency. Consider giving her a slightly more assertive (even if still fearful) line, like “I was just curious” or “I’m not sure what you mean.”
*   **The final exchange (Seris’s defiant chin lift) is a bit cliché.** While visually descriptive, it feels like a shortcut for internal emotion. Show her resolve through a *line* of dialogue, even a short one, that demonstrates her determination to continue her research. For example, “I’ll finish my research, thank you.” or even a simple, steely “I see.” would be more effective.
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is functional, but leans towards being too direct and lacking in subtext. There’s potential to create more tension and character depth by relying more on implication and subtle phrasing.

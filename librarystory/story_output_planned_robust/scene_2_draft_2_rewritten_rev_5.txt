The absence wasn’t black, but a pressure—a full-VR rig lagging at the edge of perception. He willed a hand to rise, flex, *prove* something was still connected. Nothing. No arm, no body. Just… awareness. A cursor detached from any screen, adrift in static. Panic spiked—a cold, tightening sensation where his chest *should* be. He remembered the mechanics of breath, the subtle burn of exertion, but there were no lungs to fill. He tried to shout, to force a signal, but the attempt dissolved into static. Untethered. Floating in null space. He squeezed his eyes shut, a phantom reflex, and the darkness *responded*, pulsing with the effort.

*Reboot. Diagnostic. Anything.* The commands looped, frantic and useless, not logical for… this. A desperate attempt to force function, to reclaim control as the system spiraled. He clung to the sequence, repeating it until the words themselves felt hollow, frayed at the edges. A flicker of corrupted code – a memory of a life, dissolving into static.

Then, a shift. Not location, but texture. The pressure eased, replaced by a faint, ethereal glow, like the dying embers of a monitor. A hum resonated—distant, architectural, vibrating *through* his awareness. Shapes resolved—a vast, cavernous space. He wasn’t *in* darkness, but *within* something. He reached for a nearby shelf, focusing his will, and a fleeting impression of wood grain, the cool press of aged paper, flickered at the edge of his awareness – a ghost of sensation, almost painful in its brevity. He tried again, straining, and the impression fractured, dissolving into static.

He projected a thought, a fragile signal flare. *Hello?* It felt flimsy, lost in a hurricane, immediately followed by a wave of disorientation, as if the projection itself had weakened his connection.

A response came, not as sound, but as *feeling*. A gentle ping against his awareness, laced with ancient stillness. Not a voice, but an impression—patient, observing, impossibly *old*. It didn’t answer immediately. It *considered*, and with that consideration came a sense of being… cataloged.

*Aware.*

The single word resonated, and with it, the sensation of being scanned, probed. Not with light, but with something colder, more analytical. Like an admin assessing a new process, labeling, categorizing. A chill—not *through* anything, but undeniably there, settling deep within his… core? He tried to pull away, to shield his thoughts, but the scan continued, relentless and thorough, dissecting his memories, his fears.

Books. Countless volumes stacked on shelves that disappeared into the gloom, their spines unreadable. He reached for one, and a flood of information washed over his awareness—cellulose and ink, the weight of centuries, a silent story waiting to be unlocked. Not a smell, but a rush of data – the ghost of forgotten languages, the faintest echo of hands that had turned these pages. A library beyond imagining. No librarian. A glitch? A trap?

“Where am I?” He projected again, desperation fraying the signal, the thought itself splintering into fragmented code. 

The response came slower, like a firewall actively blocking connection. A subtle weight, a sense of… amusement? Not malicious, but detached, as if observing an insect struggling in amber.

*Between.*

“Between what?” He pushed, demanding clarity, the effort leaving him feeling… depleted.

A long pause. Unsettling. Then, a chilling revelation, delivered with the distinct impression of something *watching*, something that had seen… everything. A sensation of immense age, of infinite patience.

*Between worlds.*

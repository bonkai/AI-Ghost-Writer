*   **Hemlock’s Voice – Consistency & Subtext:** Hemlock’s dialogue is *almost* too consistently smooth and subtly menacing. While effective, it risks becoming one-note. Consider varying his phrasing slightly to reflect moments of genuine curiosity (even if quickly masked) or irritation. His lines are very ‘telling’ – we’re *told* he’s assessing, cold, etc. – rather than *shown* through more nuanced delivery.
*   **Initiate’s Stammering & Lies:** The “Just admiring the architecture” line feels a bit weak and cliché. It doesn’t quite fit a character actively *trying* to access ancient power. A more desperate, internally-focused lie ("I'm fine, just a headache") might be more believable and reveal more about their determination.
*   **Repetitive Internal State:** The constant reiteration of physical sensations ("pain in my head," "tasting blood," "ringing emptiness") becomes repetitive. Show these effects through *action* more than description. For example, instead of saying "tasting blood," show the Initiate subconsciously wiping their mouth.
*   **“Telling” vs. “Showing” – Hemlock’s Warnings:** Hemlock's lines are very direct in their threat ("Consider this a warning"). While effective, explore ways to deliver these warnings *indirectly*. A veiled comment about other initiates who "pushed too far" could be more chilling.
*   **Initiate’s Final Attempt – Silence:** The final, desperate attempt followed by “Silence. Absolute, impenetrable silence” is a bit on-the-nose. Consider letting the *lack* of response speak for itself. The silence is already powerful.
*   **Indirect Characterization – Hemlock’s Expectation:** The realization that Hemlock anticipated the failure is strong. However, it's almost entirely conveyed through internal thought. A *very* subtle physical cue from Hemlock (a slight incline of the head, a barely perceptible smile) during the silence could amplify this moment.
*   **Ambition & Station – Exposition:** Hemlock’s line about ambition exceeding station feels a bit like exposition. Is there a way to hint at this conflict earlier, or through another character?

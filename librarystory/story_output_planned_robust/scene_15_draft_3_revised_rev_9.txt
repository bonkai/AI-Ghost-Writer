The cartography room smelled of dry paper and dust motes dancing in a single shaft of sunlight. Seris traced a finger across the brittle vellum of the ancient map, a tremor starting in her hand that mirrored the jagged peaks depicted on the parchment. 

“‘Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’” she murmured, her brow furrowed. She ran a finger across the map, detecting a subtle texture change near Mount Cinderfang’s base. A thinning. She focused, amplifying the sensation, and realized the discoloration wasn't merely a change in color, but a slight depression in the parchment, as if something beneath pressed upwards.

Rhys, perched on a stool, methodically cleaned bone tools, the scrape of stone a steady counterpoint to Seris’s quickening pulse. He paused, observing her. “Something?”

Seris didn’t meet his gaze, continuing to trace the anomaly. “I think… there’s more to this map than we initially thought. A hidden layer, almost. Like pressure from below.” She pressed harder, and a faint, almost imperceptible click echoed in the quiet room.

Rhys set down the tools and rose, approaching the map. He traced the peak with his finger. “Mount Cinderfang. A fitting name for a place that holds… something.” His gaze sharpened, studying the map with a cartographer's eye. “The layering… it's not a mistake. It's deliberate.”

Seris shook her head, a cold dread settling in her stomach. She remembered Valerius’s questions about the region’s history, the old gods, the rituals performed *within* the mountains. It all clicked into place. “Valerius is looking for something here,” she said, her voice barely audible. “Something old. Something… bound.”

Rhys’s gaze met hers, concern etched on his face. “Bound? What makes you say that?”

Seris swept the room with a quick glance, a prickling sensation on her skin. She gripped the map tighter, fighting a wave of nausea. “It’s just… a feeling. Like disturbing something that should have remained buried.”

Rhys reached for a heavy iron poker, his movements deliberate. “We’ll be ready.” He didn’t offer platitudes. 

A faint, metallic tang filled the air, prickling at the back of Seris’s throat. Whatever lay beneath Mount Cinderfang, it was straining against its bonds, and she feared the moment it broke free. She traced the discoloration one last time, a chilling certainty washing over her. This wasn’t just a map; it was a warning.

*   **Repetitive questioning:** Rhys asks “What’s happening?” and “What is it?” and “What does it mean?” and “What’s happening?” in quick succession. While concern is understandable, it feels like a loop. Consider consolidating these into fewer, more impactful questions, or replacing some with actions showing his concern (tightening his grip, searching her face).
*   **Kaelen’s voice – too much telling, not enough showing:** The fragmented thoughts from Kaelen are essentially exposition delivered directly to Seris (and therefore the reader). While understandable as she’s receiving them, it's a lot of direct information. Can some of this be *felt* by Seris and then *interpreted* by her, rather than just stated? For example, instead of “…key…vulnerability…”, perhaps Seris feels a sharp, instinctive understanding of a weakness, and *then* vocalizes that understanding.
*   **Seris’s explanations are lengthy:** Seris delivers a lot of exposition about the Nexus, the prison, and the Firestone Mountains. While necessary information, it’s delivered in relatively unbroken blocks. Consider breaking it up with Rhys’s reactions, questions, or actions. A little back-and-forth would make it feel more like a conversation and less like a lecture.
*   **“The fate of Eldoria…” – overly dramatic closing line:** While the stakes are high, this feels a little cliché and on-the-nose. It's a strong statement, but could be more impactful if *implied* through Seris's actions or expression, rather than explicitly stated.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Rhys feels a bit passive. His primary function is to ask questions and express concern. Consider adding a line or action that reveals something about *his* personality or skills. Is he a pragmatist? A scholar? A warrior? Show that through his response to the crisis.

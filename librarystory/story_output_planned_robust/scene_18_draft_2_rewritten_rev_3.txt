## The Crimson Bloom

The scent of aged parchment and dust motes hung in the air, a familiar comfort that did little to soothe the prickle of awareness crawling across Seris’s skin. She dipped her quill into the ink, the nib scratching against the vellum as she deepened the shading on the Sunstone Bloom’s petals.

Rhys was already scanning a crumbling tome on Firepeak’s geological formations, tracing a finger across a faded illustration of a precarious rock face. He glanced up, then back down, a subtle tightening around his jaw. “The strata are…unstable. Landslides are common after rainfall.”

A shadow fell across their worktable, not abrupt, but a gradual obscuring of the lamplight. Valerius hadn’t approached directly, but lingered a few feet away, observing their workspace – scattered texts, the half-finished sketch, the curated chaos – without speaking. The air seemed to thicken.

“Still chasing whispers?” he asked, his voice smooth.

Rhys straightened, offering Seris’s sketch with a slight tilt of his hand.

Valerius’s gaze followed, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. He studied it for a long moment, his expression unreadable. A flicker of something – curiosity, perhaps – crossed his face. He didn’t comment.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly. “A rare specimen. Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon – a phenomenon rarely witnessed.”

Seris met Valerius’s gaze, her expression carefully neutral. “We’ve found accounts of its use in ancient healing practices, among the mountain tribes. We’re hoping to corroborate those claims.”

“Firepeak Mountain is dangerous. Many have ventured into those peaks and never returned.” The scent of his sandalwood cologne drifted towards them.

Rhys’s fingers tightened on the tome’s page. “We understand the risks. We’ve been meticulously researching the terrain, the weather patterns…”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, assessing. The silence stretched. Then, a slow smile touched his lips, but didn't reach his eyes.

“Perhaps you are prepared.” He paused. “Though the mountain…tests preparations.” His eyes locked with Seris’s. “It always does.”

Seris held his gaze, her expression unwavering.

Valerius’s smile widened, unsettlingly cold. “I look forward to hearing of your discoveries…or misfortunes.” He turned, his cloak swirling around him, and moved away, leaving Seris and Rhys bathed in the flickering lamplight.

He doesn't believe we're seeking a bloom. He believes we're seeking something else entirely. Rhys’s hand rested briefly on Seris’s arm, a fleeting pressure before he returned to the text.

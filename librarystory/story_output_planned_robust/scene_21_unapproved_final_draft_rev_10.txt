The workshop smelled of dried herbs and mountain dust, but a metallic tang now clung to the air, prickling at the back of Seris’s throat. The chill lingered after Inquisitor Marius departed, settling not on the stone floor, but in the spaces between breaths. She tightened her grip on the pouch containing Elara’s portrait, the leather warm beneath her fingers.

“He’s feeling for cracks,” Kaelen murmured, a current in her mind. Seris didn’t react outwardly, her fingers tracing her jaw – a habitual gesture.

Rhys shifted, subtly obstructing the view of the restricted ingredients with his broad frame. He adjusted a precisely balanced scale, the meticulous process a deliberate display of focused work.

Seris met Marius’s gaze, allowing a hint of challenge to bloom in her eyes. She subtly reclaimed her space, stepping closer to the workbench and resuming her examination of a dried petal. “The pursuit of knowledge often leads us to uncommon ground, Inquisitor.”

Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging details. He stopped on the half-dissected flower, petals arranged with unsettling precision. His lips thinned, not in disapproval, but in…recognition. A flicker crossed his face, quickly masked.

“An unconventional arrangement. Almost…ceremonial.”

Seris held his gaze, her expression neutral. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.”

Rhys began cleaning his axe, the rhythmic scrape of steel on stone a counterpoint to Marius’s scrutiny. His gaze flickered toward the shadowed corner, then returned to his work – a silent acknowledgement of shared risk. *He’s testing our composure,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper. *Seeing how we react.*

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile, but it didn’t reach his eyes. “A convenient answer.” He moved with fluid grace, assessing the room’s structural integrity, his fingers trailing along a stone pillar. “The Bloom…it remembers. Echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. A time when such flora held sway over more than just healing.” He paused. “Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep…and remind us of what we’ve forgotten.”

He stepped back, his gaze lingering on their workspace. “I will be watching you. Both of you. Not to find secrets, but to understand what you choose to hide.”

Rhys continued cleaning his axe, maintaining focused intensity, pointedly ignoring Marius, but with an awareness bordering on predatory.

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing Seris. “Everyone has debts, Scholar.” He paused, letting the statement hang in the air. “What are you willing to forfeit to settle them?” He offered a curt nod, then swept out, leaving a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being observed. A faint scent of ozone followed him.

Silence descended. Seris met Rhys’s gaze, a silent understanding passing between them. He was ready. And so was she. A flicker of defiance in her eyes. They wouldn't be broken.

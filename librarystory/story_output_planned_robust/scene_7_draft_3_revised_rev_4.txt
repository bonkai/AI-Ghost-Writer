“Seris?”

She whirled, heart hammering against her ribs. Hemlock stood in the doorway, expression unreadable. He didn’t speak, only watched, a stillness about him that felt like a held breath.

Seris nodded, gaze fixed on the sphere. The room felt constricted, the weight of the darkness pressing in, stealing the air from her lungs. She reached out, fingers brushing the cool, smooth surface, an involuntary pull she couldn’t resist.

The world fractured. Not into darkness, but into layers. Vast, crumbling cities carved into mountainsides bled into winding canyons filled with bioluminescent flora. Coiled around the highest peak, a colossal serpentine creature, scales shimmering like a thousand sunsets, pulsed with life.

It wasn’t the grandeur that stole her breath, but the gaze. Amidst the chaos, a single, ancient eye met hers – reptilian, gold-flecked, brimming with knowing. It didn’t merely *see* her; it looked *through* her, stripping away layers until she felt utterly exposed, a fragile thing laid bare. A resonance bloomed within her chest, mirroring the creature’s weariness, a weight of ages settling upon her own heart. She stumbled back, hand flying to her temple, a low moan escaping her lips. The images fractured, receding, leaving an ache behind her eyes and a coldness that settled deep within her chest. She braced a hand against the cold stone wall, knuckles white, breath ragged.

A voice, not spoken, but *felt* within her mind, echoed the image of the serpent: *“Awakening…”* Not a threat, but a statement of inevitability, weighted with eons and a chilling hunger. It felt…like homecoming. She squeezed her eyes shut, then slowly opened them, a tremor running through her.

The room seemed different – heavier, shadows deeper, the thrumming beneath her feet intensified, vibrating up through her bones. The sphere radiated subtle heat, the carvings rearranged into an unsettling pattern, twisting like living things. She took a shaky breath, trying to regain control, but found only a growing disorientation.

Hemlock’s voice, closer now, broke the silence. “Disturbed?” he asked, not as a question, but an observation. He had entered the chamber, gaze sweeping over the sphere, then settling on her face, his expression unreadable.

She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her despite the warmth from the sphere. Words felt inadequate, a clumsy attempt to capture the impossible. She could only manage a whisper. “Cities…a serpent…it felt…real. Like a memory, but not my own.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint, enigmatic smile. He reached out, fingers hovering over the sphere, then drew back, as if testing the air. “Some places remember,” he said, his voice low. “Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, gaze locking with hers, a flicker of something unreadable in his eyes. “And some people…are more attuned to those echoes than others.”

He studied her intently. “Be careful, Seris. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting*.”

She looked back at the obsidian sphere, and for a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths – a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns, a single, golden eye reflecting her own. The eye didn’t blink. It *watched*.

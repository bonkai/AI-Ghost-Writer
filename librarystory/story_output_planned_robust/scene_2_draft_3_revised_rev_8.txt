The absence wasn’t black, but pressure—a lagging VR rig at the edge of perception. He willed a hand to rise, flex—prove connection. Nothing. No arm, no body. Just awareness. A cursor adrift in static. Panic spiked—a tightening where his chest *should* be. Breath—the mechanics, the burn—ghosts of sensation. He tried to shout, force a signal, but it dissolved into static. Untethered. Floating. He squeezed his eyes shut—a phantom reflex—and darkness *responded*, pulsing, straining against a failing connection.

*Reboot. Diagnostic. Anything.* The commands looped, frantic, useless. He clung to the sequence, a desperate mantra, until the words frayed. A flicker of corrupted code, a life dissolving, then reasserting—apartment, monitors, half-finished game… gone. He forced himself to stop, to breathe—a useless act, yet grounding. He needed to *do* something, anything, beyond repeating failed commands. He focused, straining to recall the last line of code he’d been working on, clinging to the tangible.

He drifted, then a sensation – not sight, but *presence*. A vastness, layered with the scent of aged paper and dust. He reached for something, *anything*, focusing his will. A fleeting impression of cool, brittle paper – a ghost of sensation, almost painful. He strained, *demanding* connection, and it fractured, dissolving, leaving a phantom ache. He clenched his mental focus, attempting to shield his thoughts, to build a wall against the encroaching… what *was* this place?

He projected a thought—*Hello?* It felt flimsy, lost, swallowed by the immensity around him.

A response came, not as sound, but feeling. A gentle ping—ancient stillness. Not a voice, but an impression—patient, observing, impossibly old. It didn’t answer, didn’t acknowledge. It *considered*, cataloging—a curator examining an artifact. 

*Aware.*

The word resonated, and with it, a scan—colder, analytical. Like an admin assessing a process, labeling, categorizing. A chill settled deep within his… core? He tried to pull away, to *block* the probing, but the scan continued, relentless, dissecting memories, fears. He fought back, pushing against the intrusion, attempting to scramble his thoughts, to create static. He needed to assert control, to prove he wasn't simply a passive recipient.

He reached out again, desperate for something solid, something real. He focused on a single book, willing himself to *feel* it, to understand. The sensation returned—cool, brittle paper—but this time, something else. A flicker of image, a fragment of a face, a single word… *lost*. The image sharpened momentarily, coalescing into the fleeting impression of a woman’s sorrowful eyes before dissolving again. It felt… familiar.

The presence shifted, a subtle tightening of the mental pressure. *You are not the first to ask. Nor will you be the last.* 

He felt a prickle of unease. *Observation?* What did that mean? Was he a specimen? A prisoner? He attempted to solidify his mental defenses, to create a barrier, to project a question. *What is this place?*

The presence didn’t respond directly. Instead, a new sensation flooded his awareness—not a vision, but a *feeling* of countless stories, of lives lived and lost, all contained within the silent volumes surrounding him. A weight of history, of knowledge, of *being*.

*A repository,* the presence finally offered, the thought less a voice and more a resonance within his own mind. *A bridge. And now… an observation.* 

He felt a prickle of unease. *Observation?* Was he being studied? He focused again, trying to project a clear thought. *Who are you?*

Then, a final sensation, subtle but distinct – a touch, not physical, but *mental*. A fleeting image of a hand, reaching out, then withdrawing. And with it, a single, echoing word, not directed *at* him, but *within* him.

*Forgotten.*

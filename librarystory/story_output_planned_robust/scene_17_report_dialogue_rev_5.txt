*   **Repetitive Predatory Smiles:** Valerius *smiles* with predatory intent three times. This quickly becomes a tell rather than a character nuance. Consider varying his reactions – a tightening of his jaw, a cold assessment, a dismissive wave of the hand.
*   **Kaelen’s Thoughts – Overuse/Infodump:** While the telepathic link is established, Kaelen’s thoughts are doing *a lot* of heavy lifting. We get exposition about the Obsidian Circle, the ritual site, and Seris being a conduit *through* thought. This feels like telling, not showing. Could some of this be revealed through Seris’s observations, reactions, or even Valerius’s dialogue (subtly hinting at what they need her for)? The urgency is good, but the *way* it's delivered is clunky.
*   **“Safety” as a Theme - Too On-The-Nose:** The repeated emphasis on "safety" feels a little simplistic. While it establishes a central conflict, it lacks subtlety. Explore phrasing that implies control, dominion, or preservation of order instead of directly stating "safety."
*   **Seris's Steadiness - Believability:** Seris is facing a very dangerous situation, discovering betrayal and ancient powers. Her voice remains *too* steady throughout. A tremor, a slight catch in her breath, or a brief moment of vulnerability would make her response more believable and impactful.
*   **Rhys’s Silence:** Rhys is present but largely silent. While a strong, silent type can work, he’s almost entirely a prop. A brief line of dialogue – a questioning glance at Seris, a subtle warning – would solidify his character and partnership.
*   **“We have very different ideas about safety” - Cliché:** This line feels predictable. Consider a more defiant or challenging statement that reflects Seris’s growing acceptance of the key’s power.
*   **Subtext Opportunity - Valerius’s Motives:** Valerius *says* he wants to protect the realm, but his predatory smiles and the Obsidian Circle’s symbol suggest something far more sinister. Lean into this contrast. Have him deliver a noble-sounding line while *doing* something that betrays his true intentions.

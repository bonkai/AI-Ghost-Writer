## Scene 15 - Draft 3

The cartography room smelled of dry paper and dust motes dancing in a single shaft of sunlight. Seris traced a finger across the brittle vellum of the ancient map, a tremor starting in her hand that echoed the mountain peaks depicted on the parchment. 

“The sequence is almost complete,” she murmured, her brow furrowed. “’Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’” She glanced at Rhys, then quickly back at the map, her jaw tightening.

Rhys, perched on a stool, methodically cleaned bone tools, the scrape of stone a steady counterpoint to Seris’s quickening pulse. He didn’t look up.

“Pre-Eldorian?” Seris asked, more to fill the silence. “Those dialects are fragmented now.”

“More than just the language,” she continued, tracing a distorted rune. “Variations. Corruptions. Someone deliberately obscured the meaning, layered it with something else.”

*A nexus,* Kaelen’s thought settled within her, accompanied by a fleeting image: fractured stone, hairline fissures spiderwebbing across its surface. She breathed deeply, anchoring herself, before the image could touch her expression. 

Rhys finally looked up, his gaze assessing. “Kaelen said something?”

Seris hesitated. “Confirmation the runes are older than we thought. And that the ‘Stone Heart’ isn’t metaphorical.” She returned to the map, her gaze sweeping across the jagged peaks.

Rhys traced the peak with his finger. “A fitting name.”

Seris’s eyes narrowed as she ran a finger across the parchment, detecting a subtle texture change near Mount Cinderfang’s base. *A thinning,* Kaelen’s thought echoed. She focused on the map, amplifying the sensation, and realized the discoloration wasn't merely a change in color, but a slight depression in the parchment, as if something beneath pressed upwards.

“I don’t know what we’ve stumbled onto,” she said, her voice low, “but it feels dangerous. Like disturbing something that should have remained buried.”

*It is a cage,* Kaelen’s voice resonated, sharper now. A fleeting image – sickly green light pulsing within the fractured stone – flickered behind her eyes. She swallowed, gripping the map.

Rhys looked up, sensing her mounting terror. “Seris?”

She shook her head, forcing herself to focus. She traced the discoloration with a trembling finger. A prison. A place of binding.

“Valerius…” she continued, her voice barely audible. “He’s been asking too many questions. About this region’s history, the old gods… the rituals performed *within* the mountains.” She paused, sweeping the room with a quick glance. “I feel… watched.”

Rhys reached for a heavy iron poker, his movements deliberate. “Perhaps just the wind.” He didn't offer platitudes. "We'll be ready."

Seris nodded, her heart hammering. They had unearthed something ancient and terrible. The map, once a puzzle, now felt like a warning. A faint, metallic tang filled the air, prickling at the back of her throat. She traced the discoloration again, a cold dread settling in her stomach. Whatever lay beneath Mount Cinderfang, it was straining against its bonds, and she feared the moment it broke free.

*   **Over-Explanation/On-the-Nose:** Several lines feel overly expository. For example, “This wasn’t just about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it.” and “The flower…it’s not just a key to the past. It’s a seed for the future.” These are thematic statements, but they feel *told* rather than *shown*. Let Seris's actions and Hemlock's reactions convey these ideas instead.
*   **Repetitive Theme:** The idea of "forgetting" and "remembering" is hammered home repeatedly. While central to the plot, it lacks subtlety. Consider replacing some direct mentions with imagery or subtext.
*   **Hemlock’s Panic/Fear:** Hemlock's shifts between eloquent explanation and panicked reactions are jarring. While a conflicted character is good, the transitions feel abrupt. Perhaps show the fear *through* his explanations – a rushed pace, fragmented sentences, or a tendency to look over his shoulder.
*   **Physical Touch:** The hand-reaching/touching moments (Hemlock touching Seris's arm, then her pack) feel a little contrived and don’t fully land emotionally. Consider *why* he reaches out – is it a moment of connection, a desperate plea for help, or something else? Make the motivation clearer and the action more impactful. The initial touch feels like a beat that doesn't earn its weight.
*   **Seris’s Internal State:** While described in narration (“her jaw tight,” “knuckles white”), Seris’s dialogue is fairly reactive. Give her more agency. What questions does *she* proactively ask? What doubts or fears does she express?
*   **Subtext Opportunity (The Order):** The Order’s motivations are stated directly ("They feared what people remembered"). Consider hinting at *why* they feared it. Was it a loss of control? A threat to their power? Let the reader piece together the implications.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** Hemlock’s dialogue is largely exposition. Show his character through his *way* of speaking – his word choice, his pauses, his evasions. What does his language reveal about his personality and his past?

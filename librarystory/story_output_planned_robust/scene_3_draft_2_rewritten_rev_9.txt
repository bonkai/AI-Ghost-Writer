He wasn’t dissolving, not exactly. More an unraveling—threads of awareness pulled taut, then released. A fall, not down, but *inward*, into a space that wasn’t space, a silence that wasn’t silence. The sensation lingered, a disorienting drift, before a jarring halt. Ghosts of memory—the chair, the monitor—faded, not abruptly, but with a slow, dissolving grace. He clung to them briefly, a pang of loss, before they dissolved completely.

He wasn’t *what* he was, either. He attempted to flex a hand, to shift weight, but found…nothing. No limbs, no body, only diffuse awareness, a point of consciousness adrift. A tremor of fear quickly gave way to a strange lightness. The absence of weight, of form, was unsettling, yet…exhilarating. He registered a fleeting sense of panic, then deliberately suppressed it. *Wanting* felt…wrong. A foreign sensation. He tested the limits of this new state, reaching out not with a hand, but with will.

He focused on a plain brown volume on a towering shelf, and *pushed*. Not physically, but with the force of his disorientation, his desperate need for solidity. A flicker of frustration sparked within him. The book didn’t move, but the air around it shimmered. He strained, pushing harder, and a single page within the book fluttered, then another. A distortion spread, like heat rising, and he felt a strange pull, a connection forming. He almost *tasted* the knowledge – a metallic tang on his nonexistent tongue, followed by the faintest scent of aged paper and leather. The effort left him breathless, a dizzying wave of exhaustion. 

A word solidified on the page, glowing with inner light: *Listen.*

Listen? To what? The silence? It wasn’t silent, not really. Beneath the absence of sound, a hum throbbed – a heartbeat in the vastness. He focused, and the hum resolved into a chorus of whispers, too faint to decipher. He directed a question towards the source of the whispers: *Where am I?*

The air around him seemed to thicken, to *respond*. “You are a resonance, a fragment of awareness within the Great Library. A place where stories are not just told, but *lived*.” The voice wasn't a sound, but a feeling. It felt…old. Impossibly old. A pause. He pressed for more. *Why am I here?*

“You are new,” the presence said, its tone shifting, less a statement, more an observation. “A potential echo. We observe, we collect. You…arrived.” It didn’t answer his question. He concentrated, directing a spark of defiance towards the presence: *What do you want from me?*

A dry, rustling sound, like parchment crumbling, echoed in his awareness. “Want? A quaint notion. We do not ‘want.’ We *are*. And you, little resonance, are…an anomaly.” 

*What am I?* he thought, the question echoing with desperate need.

“You are a reader,” the presence said, its tone softening slightly. “Or, you *were*. Now…something more.” A beat of silence. He tried again, projecting a plea. *Help me understand.*

The library didn't respond immediately. The silence stretched, becoming almost oppressive. Then, a single page, far down a towering shelf, began to glow. Not with the inner light of the words upon it, but with a faint, pulsing luminescence. He strained to see, to understand. The page remained stubbornly blank, but he could *feel* something emanating from it. A resonance. A story waiting to be told. And, underlying everything, the distinct impression that he wasn’t asking the questions. He was being *tested*.

He focused again, attempting to project a thought, a plea, *What should I listen for?* 

This time, the response wasn't immediate. The silence stretched, becoming almost oppressive. Then, a single image flickered into existence on a nearby page – a fleeting glimpse of a woman's face, etched with sorrow, before dissolving back into blankness. The image vanished as quickly as it appeared, leaving him reeling. 

*Who was that?* he projected, the question laced with urgency.

The library remained silent for a long moment. Then, a single word formed on the page closest to him, a word that resonated deep within his awareness: *Remember.* He pushed, desperate for more. *Remember what?*

The library offered no further response. The luminescence on the distant shelf pulsed faster, then faded. He was left in silence, the single word echoing in the vastness. He strained again, focusing all his attention on the glowing page, but it remained blank. The library wasn't just waiting; it was *watching*, and he sensed, with growing unease, that his questions were only revealing his ignorance.

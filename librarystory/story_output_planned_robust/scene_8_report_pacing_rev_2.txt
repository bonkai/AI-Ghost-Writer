*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening paragraphs are very internal and descriptive. While establishing atmosphere is good, the pace is *very* slow for a scene that seems to be building towards discovery and potential conflict. Consider condensing the initial description of recalling the serpent.
*   **Discovery Rhythm:** The pattern of recalling the serpent, page illuminating, new clue appearing is effective, but it risks becoming repetitive. Vary the *way* Seris attempts to access the memory each time to maintain interest.
*   **Hemlock's Entrance - Delayed Impact:** Hemlock’s approach is described as fluid and controlled, which is good for establishing his character. However, the build-up to his voice feels *too* drawn out. The paragraph before his line (“She opened the book…”) is almost entirely internal and slows the momentum right before the confrontation. Shorten it or interweave a sensory detail (a floorboard creak, a change in temperature) to signal his arrival sooner.
*   **Tension Peaks & Valleys:** The tension rises with each clue uncovered, but dips significantly during Seris’s internal reactions and descriptions. The dialogue with Hemlock is the strongest point for tension, but it's relatively short.
*   **Ending – Lingering Unease:** The ending is effective at leaving the reader with a sense of unease. The lingering warmth is a nice touch. However, consider a slightly more active closing line from Seris to show her resolve or fear, rather than simply stating the warmth remains.

Overall, the scene is well-written, but the pacing is uneven. Tightening the descriptions and shortening the pauses before key moments will significantly increase the tension and impact.

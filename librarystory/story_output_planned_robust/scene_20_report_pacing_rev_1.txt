*   **Slow Start:** The opening paragraph is descriptive but slows the momentum. While establishing atmosphere is good, the extended focus on the environment before any action or dialogue feels prolonged. Consider condensing the first few sentences.
*   **Dialogue Heavy Mid-Section:** The bulk of the scene consists of Hemlock *telling* Seris information. While necessary for exposition, it lacks dynamic tension. Break up the long stretches of monologue with Seris’s reactions, internal thoughts, or brief actions.
*   **Tension Peaks & Valleys:** The tension builds nicely with the reveal of the Order’s suppression of knowledge, but then plateaus. The “observers” reveal is a good spike, but quickly deflated by further exposition. Consider interweaving the threat of the observers *during* Hemlock’s explanation, rather than as a separate reveal.
*   **Final Paragraph - Strong Finish:** The closing paragraph is the strongest in terms of building immediate tension – the extinguishing of the lamp and the sense of being hunted is effective. However, it feels slightly disconnected from the preceding dialogue dump.
*   **Hook/Transition:** The scene *starts* atmospherically, but lacks an immediate hook. Consider starting *in media res* – perhaps with Hemlock already speaking, or Seris discovering something unsettling. The ending provides a good sense of anticipation, but could be strengthened by a more visceral cliffhanger – a sound, a visual, a direct threat.

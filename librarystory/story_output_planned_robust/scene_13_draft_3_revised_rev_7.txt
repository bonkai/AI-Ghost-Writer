Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light. Rhys leaned against a crumbling pillar, eyes fixed on the star chart I traced with a fingertip. He didn’t bother with bluster – just a stillness that felt worn, like a stone weathered by centuries.

“You seek this knowledge,” he said, voice a low rumble that vibrated in the alcove. “What are you willing to lose in the seeking?”

A prickle of unease traced my spine. Not a warning, but a calculation. He wasn’t dissuading me, merely measuring my resolve. I let my gaze drift over his face – the deep lines around his eyes, the subtle clench of his jaw. Depleted, not driven, haunted by something old.

“Knowledge carries responsibility,” I said, keeping my voice level. “Fear shouldn’t dictate what we seek, nor should it be a bargaining chip.”

Rhys’s lips curved in a wry smile, the shadows in his eyes unmoved. “Resourceful.” He paused, letting the silence stretch, the only sound the rustle of parchment. “I’ve tracked this symbol for years. I know where it leads. I can help you decipher it… for a price.”

My mind raced. Not a simple trade. Precarious, like stepping onto a frozen lake. I trailed my fingers over the cool stone of the chart, a faint warmth radiating from the etched lines.

“What exchange?” I asked, direct.

Rhys pushed off the pillar, straightening to his full height. Not aggression, but a predator’s stillness. “Information. My interests align with your research. Something was stolen from me – something of value.” He paused, his gaze locking with mine, assessing. “Your expertise. In return, access to knowledge beyond any library’s walls.”

I noticed the downturn of his lips. Not grief, but simmering resentment, a quiet fury held in check. I quieted my connection to Kaelen, relying on instinct. The library’s whispers felt distant, muffled, as if protesting my severance.

I looked down at the symbol, my fingers tracing its lines. “Specifically, what do you want me to do?”

Rhys’s lips curved into a slow smile. “You’re an Archivist. You access lost knowledge, decipher star charts, translate ancient languages. You see connections others miss.” He paused, his gaze unwavering. “You’re a conduit. And I need you to open the way.”

I met his gaze, a spark of defiance flickering within me. I wasn’t merely a tool. I was an Archivist, a seeker of truth. And I wouldn’t blindly follow him.

“What guarantees do I have this isn’t a fool’s errand? That this ‘something’ even exists?”

Rhys’s smile faded, replaced by weary resignation. “None. Only my word. And the weight of centuries.” He paused, then added, his voice barely a whisper, “But I assure you. It’s real. And its return is vital. Not just for my people… but for everyone.”

The air in the alcove crackled with tension. I stared at the ancient star chart, the symbol now pulsing with unsettling energy. I was at a crossroads. And I knew, with chilling certainty, my life would never be the same. I took a slow breath, meeting his gaze. “Very well. I’ll help you. But I require transparency. Tell me everything. What was taken, from whom, and why you believe *I* can help.”

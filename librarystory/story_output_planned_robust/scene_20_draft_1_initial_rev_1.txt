The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air, thick and cloying. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, though no fire warmed the cavernous space. She traced the chipped mosaic depicting a winged figure – a forgotten goddess, judging by the book Rhys had unearthed – with a calloused fingertip. The stone felt cold, unforgiving.

A voice, brittle with age and regret, echoed from the shadows. “You seek to understand why I strayed from the Light, child? A foolish endeavor. Some doors are best left unopened.”

Seris didn’t turn. Old Man Hemlock, a hermit shunned by the Church, rarely offered direct answers. She’d found him nestled within these forgotten tunnels beneath Verenhal, a repository of forbidden lore and whispered truths. 

“I seek to understand *you*,” Seris replied, her voice steady despite the tremor in her hands. “And why you risked everything to preserve these…heretical texts.”

Hemlock shuffled closer, his face a roadmap of wrinkles etched by years of solitude and sorrow. He settled onto a rickety stool, his gaze fixed on the mosaic. “I was a Scholar of the Order, once. Dedicated. Zealous. I believed in the purity of the Light, the sanctity of our teachings.”

He paused, a ghost of a smile touching his lips. “Then I found the Archives. Hidden beneath the Grand Cathedral. Records the Order had deliberately…lost.”

Seris leaned forward, her breath catching in her throat. “What kind of records?”

“Records of the Before-Times. Of a world steeped in magic, not the sanctioned blessings we offer now, but a raw, untamed force. And records of *why* that magic was suppressed.” Hemlock’s voice dropped to a rasping whisper. “They spoke of memories. Of a civilization that remembered…too much. Memories that threatened the very foundations of our world. The Order deemed those memories dangerous. They didn’t destroy them, of course. They *buried* them. And then they built the Church upon the grave.”

Seris felt a chill crawl down her spine. “You’re saying the Church…suppresses knowledge?”

“Suppresses, alters, *rewrites*,” Hemlock corrected, his eyes burning with a feverish intensity. “I saw the evidence with my own eyes. Documents detailing rituals to…diminish the collective memory. To lull the world into a peaceful ignorance. They called it ‘The Great Quietening.’”

He reached out, his hand trembling as he touched a faded inscription on the mosaic. “I questioned it. I dared to ask *why*. And for that, I was branded a heretic. Stripped of my rank. Exiled.”

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls that lined the cavern walls.

Hemlock’s gaze drifted to the shadows. “I couldn’t stand by and watch them disappear. They are a warning, child. A reminder of what we’ve lost…and what we’ve been made to forget.”

“And the Order…they know you have them?”

“They suspect. They have sent…observers. But this place is warded. Hidden by the very magic they seek to control.” He looked at Seris, his expression grave. “But the wards won’t hold forever. And if they find me…they will find you, too.”

Seris met his gaze, her jaw tight. The weight of his words settled upon her, heavy and suffocating. The crimson stain on the Sunstone Bloom sketch in her pack felt like a brand. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower anymore. She was chasing a truth that the Church would kill to keep buried. And she knew, with a chilling certainty, that Valerius wasn’t just observing them. He was waiting for them to stumble into the darkness.

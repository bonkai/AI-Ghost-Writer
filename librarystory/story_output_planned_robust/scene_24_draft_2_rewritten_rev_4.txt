The scent of spiced wine and woodsmoke clung to the narrow alley, a deceptive sweetness. Seris didn’t feel fear, but a prickling awareness of being assessed. Marius hadn’t pursued openly, hadn’t shouted. He’d vanished, and the silence felt predatory, a tightening noose.

Kaelen led her through a hidden archway into a dimly lit courtyard. Silent figures in dark clothing moved with purpose, their faces impassive. An apprentice stumbled, scattering scrolls. Kaelen didn’t flinch, merely adjusted his stride, avoiding the mess – a silent reprimand that spoke volumes. Seris realized this wasn't merely a haven, but a meticulously controlled space, every movement accounted for.

“He’s mapping our reactions,” Kaelen rumbled, his gaze sweeping the courtyard. “Marius doesn’t act without calculating the cost. He’s testing our defenses, gauging how we respond to pressure.”

Seris scanned the faces of the Circle’s members, a knot tightening in her chest. “What did he mean, about knowing we were connected to the Circle?”

Kaelen pushed open a heavy, iron-bound door. “Information is his currency. Your research into the observatory records confirmed our involvement. More importantly, he knew *why* you were looking.” He didn't elaborate, letting the implication hang between them.

The chamber beyond held ancient tomes, star charts, and strange, metallic instruments. A subtle energy hummed in the air. Seris ran a hand over a weathered map of Firepeak, tracing the slopes with her finger. The lines felt…familiar, stirring a half-forgotten dream. A flicker of something—a fragmented memory?—touched her mind, then vanished.

“The Sunstone Bloom isn’t just a rare flower,” she said, her voice gaining strength. “It’s a key. The observatory wasn’t abandoned due to unpleasant discoveries. It was *sealed*.”

Kaelen nodded, his expression grim. “The scholars believed Firepeak held a ‘resonance,’ a connection to other realms. They experimented, and unleashed something they couldn’t control.”

“Memories,” Seris repeated, recalling her conversation with Marius. “The mountain holds memories. Someone doesn’t want them remembered.” A chill ran down her spine. It wasn’t simply about control; it was about *suppression*. And Marius, she realized, wasn’t an opponent, but an enforcer of that suppression.

Kaelen’s gaze drifted to a complex orrery, its gears still and silent. “The resonance isn’t just a connection. It’s a conduit. The memories…fragments of consciousness, echoes of beings that existed long before humanity. Beings capable of reshaping reality.” He paused, meeting her gaze, a flicker of warning in his eyes. “And someone is desperate to keep those echoes silent.”

A Circle initiate burst into the chamber, breathless. “Kaelen. Messengers from the Verenhal Guard are asking questions…about a woman seen with you. Matching Seris’ description.”

Kaelen’s jaw tightened, but his expression remained carefully controlled. “Marius is already at work, diverting attention.” He turned back to Seris. “He’s trying to box us in, make us predictable.”

“Why?” Seris asked, frustration building. “What’s so important about those memories?”

“The old scholars believed they could *control* this power. They were wrong. And now, someone is trying to unlock it again, believing they can succeed where others failed.” Kaelen grabbed a worn leather satchel, his movements swift and decisive. “We need to move. This chamber isn’t secure.” He paused, sweeping the room, lingering on the shadowed corners. “And neither is this path.”

He led her through a hidden passage, the damp stone cool against her skin. Seris glanced back as they hurried along, and a flicker of movement at the entrance caught her eye. A shadow, too quick to identify, but undeniably *there*. Her hand instinctively moved to the dagger at her hip.

“Someone’s following us,” she said, her voice barely a whisper.

Kaelen didn’t break stride. “Let them come.” He pushed open a concealed door, revealing a narrow alleyway that emptied onto a bustling street. “This path will be fraught with danger. Marius will be watching, and he’s not the only one.” He glanced at Seris, his expression unreadable. “We need to lose them, and then head for Firepeak. But be warned…we're walking into a trap. And whoever laid it has been expecting us all along.”

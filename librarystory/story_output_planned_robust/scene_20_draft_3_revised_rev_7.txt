## Scene 20: Draft 3

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, her fingers tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure. Old Man Hemlock emerged from the gloom, pacing a tight circle, his gaze darting between shadows and stone. He stopped abruptly, hand rising to his ear as if listening, then lowered himself to a weathered stool.

“I believed in the purity of the Light,” he began, his voice brittle. He glanced at the mosaic, a flicker of regret crossing his face, then quickly looked away. “A meticulous cataloger. Every blessing documented, every deviation noted. I thought I understood order.”

Seris leaned forward, her hand instinctively reaching for the Sunstone Bloom sketch within her pack. The crimson stain on the aged parchment felt heavy beneath her fingers.

“Records of the Before-Times.” Hemlock’s voice dropped to a rasping whisper. He swept his gaze around the cavern. “A world steeped in magic, not *controlled* blessings, but raw, untamed force. It wasn’t chaos, child. It was…potential.” He traced the lines of the vortex on the mosaic with his eyes. “I saw images…cities built of shimmering crystal, powered by the lifeblood of the earth. Gardens that bloomed with bioluminescent flora, responding to thought, to emotion.” He reached out, then hesitated, his hand hovering near Seris’s arm before dropping back to his side. 

He stepped back into the shadows, his voice barely a murmur. “They didn’t just fear the magic. They feared what people *remembered* with it. The power it gave them, the questions it inspired.”

Seris’s jaw tightened, her fingers tracing the sketch. She wasn’t following a legend; she was retracing steps deliberately obscured.

“The Order deemed those memories dangerous. They didn’t destroy them. They buried them. And built the Church upon the grave, a monument to forgetting.” A faint scraping echoed from the tunnel entrance, and Hemlock whirled around, hand instinctively reaching for a hidden knife. He scanned the darkness, breath coming in ragged gasps.

Seris followed his gaze, her hand tightening on her dagger, but remaining calm.

Hemlock lowered his hand, but didn’t fully relax. He glanced at the Sunstone Bloom sketch peeking from her pack. “They believe the flower might unlock something. Something they desperately want to keep buried.”

“I questioned it,” he continued, clutching his chest, a tremor running through his body. “I dared to ask *why*. And for that, I was branded a heretic. Stripped of my rank. Exiled.”

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls.

Hemlock’s gaze drifted to the shadows, a flicker of defiance in his eyes. “I couldn’t stand by and watch them disappear. They are a warning, child. A reminder of what we’ve lost…and what we’ve been made to forget.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris, his expression grim.

Seris met his gaze, the crimson stain on the sketch feeling like a brand. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower. She was chasing a truth the Church would kill to keep buried. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, she felt a prickling sensation on her skin. 

Beneath the fear, a new resolve hardened within her. This wasn’t just about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it. She tightened her grip on her pack. "What can we do?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper, but filled with a newfound determination.

Hemlock’s gaze flickered to the mosaic, then back to Seris. A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “We remember,” he said, his voice gaining strength. “We tell the stories. And we protect the embers of what remains.” He reached out and briefly covered her hand on her pack, a grounding touch. “The flower…it’s not just a key to the past. It’s a seed for the future.”



**Changes Made & Rationale:**

*   **Tightened Pacing:** Removed some repetitive phrasing and gestures to create a more focused scene.
*   **Subtlety with Fear:** Reduced instances of explicit fear descriptions. Seris is portrayed as alert and determined, not primarily frightened. The "prickling sensation" remains to indicate tension, but it's less overwhelming.
*   **Strengthened Seris’s Agency:** Emphasized Seris’s determination and resolve throughout.
*   **Reduced Hand Movements:** Streamlined the hand gestures to avoid feeling overly descriptive.
*   **Flow and Clarity:** Minor adjustments to sentence structure for smoother reading.
*   **Focus on Core Theme:** Reinforced the idea of remembering as an act of resistance and hope.
*   **Removed Redundancy:** Eliminated phrases that stated the obvious or repeated information.

The air in the cartography room hung thick with the scent of aged parchment and dust. Sunlight, filtered through the high, arched window, illuminated the intricate lines of the ancient map spread across the table. Seris traced a finger along the faded ink, the brittle paper crackling—a sound that echoed the tremor in her hand. The map, charting the Firepeak Mountains, wasn’t a guide; it felt like holding a breath *before a plunge into icy water*.

“The sequence is almost complete,” she murmured, brow furrowed. “’Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’ The phrasing isn’t Eldorian elegance. It’s raw, primal – like a carving etched by hands long forgotten.” A prickling sensation crawled up her spine, a cold whisper against her senses – *not of fear, but of being observed*.

Rhys, perched on a stool, meticulously cleaned bone tools, the rhythmic scraping a counterpoint to Seris’s mounting unease. “Pre-Eldorian? Before the unification? Those dialects are fragments now.”

“More than just the language,” Seris replied, her voice hushed. “The runes themselves. Variations, corruptions of the standard glyphs. Someone deliberately obscured the meaning, layered it with… something else.” She ran a calloused fingertip over a distorted rune. “This… ‘Isen.’ And this, a fractured ‘Morwen.’ Intentional, I think.” *She didn’t need to say it aloud, but the deliberate defacement felt like a challenge.*

*The Stone Heart is a nexus,* Kaelen’s voice resonated in her mind, sharper now, a cold pressure against her thoughts. *A point where the veil thins. A place between worlds.* 

Rhys glanced up, sensing her unease. “Kaelen said something?”

Seris hesitated, meeting his gaze. “Confirmation the runes are older than we thought. And that the ‘Stone Heart’ isn’t metaphorical.” She returned to the map, her gaze sweeping across the jagged peaks, a growing dread constricting her chest. “The Serpent’s Tooth… that must be Mount Cinderfang. The most prominent, the most menacing.”

Rhys traced the peak with his finger. “A fitting name. Looks ready to strike.”

Seris’s eyes narrowed. She ran a finger across the parchment, detecting a subtle texture change near Mount Cinderfang’s base. Almost imperceptible, a discoloration in the aged vellum. “I don’t know. But we’ve stumbled onto something dangerous. Something that should have remained buried.”

*The Stone Heart is a nexus,* Kaelen’s voice echoed again, but this time laced with chilling urgency. *But it’s also a cage. And something strains against its bonds.* The image shifted in her mind – the chamber cracking, fissures spreading across the stone, the green light intensifying. *Ancient. Hungry.*

Rhys looked up, sensing her growing terror. “Seris?”

Seris shook her head, dismissing the premonition, forcing herself to focus. She traced the discoloration with a trembling finger. A prison. A place of binding.

“Valerius…” she continued, her voice low. “He’s been asking too many questions about this region’s history, the old gods… specifically, the rituals performed *within* the mountains. Things best forgotten.” She paused, sweeping the room with her gaze. “I feel watched.”

Rhys reached for a heavy iron poker. “Perhaps just the wind. But we’ll be ready.”

Seris nodded, her heart pounding a frantic rhythm against her ribs. They had unearthed something ancient and terrible. And she had a chilling premonition they weren’t alone in knowing about it. The map, once a puzzle, now felt like a warning. They had awakened something that should have remained asleep. A faint, metallic tang, like old blood and ozone, filled the air – *a scent that clung to the back of her throat, a promise of storm and something far older*.

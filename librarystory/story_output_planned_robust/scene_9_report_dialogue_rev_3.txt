*   **Repetitive Questioning:** Seris asks *a lot* of questions, often very directly after Kaelen speaks. While inquisitiveness is fine, it feels like an interrogation rather than a natural exchange. Consider having her *react* more to Kaelen’s answers, then formulate questions that build on those reactions. For example, instead of "Corrupted how?", perhaps a beat of silence followed by "So, these aren't just lost memories... they're *damaged*?"
*   **Kaelen's Voice:** While the "voice not spoken, but felt" is a cool concept, it consistently feels *too* expository. Kaelen answers questions directly, even complex ones, with detailed explanations. Consider more metaphorical or fragmented responses. Lean into the "echo" aspect – snippets of thoughts, feelings, images, rather than complete sentences.
*   **Subtext Opportunity - Church's Motives:** When Seris asks if the Church is protecting us, Kaelen’s response (“Protecting themselves”) is a bit blunt. It *tells* us the Church is self-serving, rather than *showing* it. Perhaps a pause, a subtle shift in the swirling patterns, before Kaelen responds with something less direct – “They believe order is paramount.” – forcing Seris to draw the conclusion herself.
*   **"Why me?" – A Missed Beat:** The question "Why me?" feels a bit…flat, given the build-up. It’s a perfectly valid question, but after the dramatic reveal of Kaelen’s nature, a moment of stunned silence, or a more emotional reaction ("You… you chose *me*?") would be more impactful.
*   **Final Paragraph – Overwriting:** The final paragraph is a lot of internal monologue *told* through sensation. We *know* Seris is scared; the distorted stone feels redundant. Trust the reader to understand her fear from the preceding dialogue and events. A single, powerful sensory detail (“The stone felt…wrong”) would be stronger.
* **Indirect Characterization - Seris's Personality:** Seris's constant questioning, while driving the scene forward, paints her as a bit relentlessly logical and lacking in emotional reaction. Consider adding moments where she *feels* the weight of the information before pressing for more details.

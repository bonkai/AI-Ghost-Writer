*   **Repetitive Internal Monologue vs. Dialogue:** The bulk of the "revelations" happen *within* Kaelen’s head, then are *restated* as dialogue. This makes the conversation with Elara feel flat. For example, the repeated emphasis on "ancient," "memory," and the idea of the game being a map is shown, then *told* through dialogue. Trim the internal thought and let the *impact* of Kaelen's experience show *through* his dialogue, even if it's fragmented or panicked.
*   **Elara's Role:** Elara is largely a reactive character. Her only lines are questions prompted by Kaelen’s distress. Give her a line or two that demonstrates *active* concern or attempts to understand *what* Kaelen is experiencing, beyond just "What's happening?". Even a simple "What do you *feel*?" would add depth.
*   **"I think..." & Weak Assertions:** Kaelen relies heavily on phrases like "I think," "It’s like," and "I’m not sure." While uncertainty is understandable given the situation, it weakens the impact of his discoveries. After the initial surge, lean into more declarative statements, even if they're delivered with strain or panic.
*   **"Backdoor" is a Mismatch:** The term "backdoor" feels too modern/tech-focused for the "ancient" and "forgotten language" vibe the scene is building. Consider a more evocative term – “key,” “threshold,” “gateway,” etc.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – Physical Reactions:** You've done a good job with *describing* the physical effects on Kaelen. However, *show* those effects influencing his dialogue. Instead of "I think I found it," perhaps a gasped, fragmented "It… it *opened*…" or a line delivered through gritted teeth.
*   **Fractured Dialogue:** Consider more fractured, incomplete sentences as the energy surge intensifies. This will mirror the fracturing of Kaelen's mind and create a more visceral experience for the reader.

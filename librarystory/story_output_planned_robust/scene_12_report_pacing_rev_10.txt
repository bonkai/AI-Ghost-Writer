*   **Slow Start:** The first paragraph relies heavily on atmospheric description. While effective, it delays the introduction of conflict and feels a bit *too* drawn out before Valerius appears. Consider condensing the initial sensory details.
*   **Pacing Imbalance:** The scene operates in a largely conversational rhythm, punctuated by descriptions of Valerius’s movements. This is fine, but the tension *builds* slowly and doesn’t have enough significant peaks and valleys. The reveal of the 'light-eaters' is a good moment, but it’s quickly followed by more dialogue.
*   **Tension Peaks Need Emphasis:** The moment where Seris realizes this isn't a discussion, but a manipulation, is strong. However, it's followed *immediately* by Valerius ordering her escort, deflating some of the tension. A beat of internal struggle *before* the order would amplify the impact.
*   **Ending Feels Rushed:** The final paragraph contains a lot of internal revelation. While important, it feels crammed in. Spreading these realizations over a few more lines, perhaps during the walk back to her chambers, would create a more satisfying and less hurried conclusion.
*   **Hook Opportunity:** The opening could be more immediately arresting. Starting *in media res*—perhaps with Seris already sensing something wrong, or reacting to a subtle cue from Valerius—would grab the reader's attention more effectively.

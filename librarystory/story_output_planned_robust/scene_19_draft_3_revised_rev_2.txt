## Scene 19

The library had emptied for the night. Only a handful of lamps cast pools of warm light across the towering shelves, leaving the upper reaches lost in shadow. Seris sat at their worktable, not sketching, not reading, but simply…present. The weight of Valerius’s scrutiny still pressed on her, a cold spot on her skin. A nearby candle flickered, mirroring the unease within her.

A subtle disturbance caught her eye. Not a physical movement, but a ripple in the dust motes dancing in a lamplight beam. Kaelen.

The spine of a book on a nearby shelf flexed, a silent tremor. Then, a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table before her. The ink hadn't fully dried; a faint scent of parchment and something…older, lingered. It wasn’t a message, not exactly. A sketch. A rough charcoal rendering of Firepeak Mountain, but not as a cartographer would draw it. This depicted the mountain as a *being*, its peaks like jagged teeth, its slopes etched with lines of ancient power.

Seris traced the lines of the drawing with a fingertip. A fleeting impression brushed against her mind – a sense of immense age, of stone remembering centuries. *Beautiful,* she thought, and the impression intensified, resonating with a shared appreciation. The air around the sketch seemed to shimmer, momentarily warmer.

The book shifted on the shelf, a subtle readjustment. *It remembers,* a thought formed, clear and distinct, yet woven into the fabric of her consciousness. Not a response, but an offering.

She traced the lines of the drawing with a fingertip. “What do you mean?” she breathed, the question barely a whisper.

The page before her blurred, the charcoal lines seeming to writhe. *The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth, Seris. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.*

“Lost?” she echoed, a questioning murmur.

A long silence stretched, broken only by the crackling of the lamps. She could *feel* his focus, a distant sifting through layers of forgotten lore. Finally, a fragmented image flickered in her mind – a vast library, crumbling and overgrown, then a single, brightly lit doorway. *Remembrance.*

“Remembrance?” she repeated, aloud this time, the sound startling in the quiet library. The image faded, leaving a lingering warmth on her skin.

*The Order didn’t just forbid magic, Seris. They forbade remembering. They feared what the past held. Firepeak…it remembers everything. And the Bloom…it can unlock those memories.* 

Seris felt a shiver crawl down her spine. This wasn’t just about finding a rare healing plant anymore. This was about something far older, far more dangerous.

“Valerius…” she breathed, the name a dark weight on her tongue. “He knows, doesn’t he? That we’re looking for more than just a flower?”

A book slammed shut on a nearby shelf, the sound echoing sharply. His frustration was palpable, but controlled. *He suspects. He always suspects. He’s a guardian of the forgetting, Seris. He’ll do anything to protect it.*

Seris closed her eyes, her temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

A calming presence settled over her mind, not warmth, but a quiet strength. *Always.*

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch of Firepeak. It no longer seemed like a drawing, but a warning. A challenge. And, strangely, a promise. A single petal, crimson and perfectly formed, didn’t materialize as an image *sent*, but as a shared perception – a delicate fragrance momentarily filling the air, a silent affirmation superimposed over the sketch. They weren’t alone. And the journey ahead, however dangerous, felt…less daunting.

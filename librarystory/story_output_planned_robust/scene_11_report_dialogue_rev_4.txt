*   **Repetitive Revelation:** The repeated phrasing of "something ancient," "something waiting," and the emphasis on "memory" becomes redundant. While building suspense is good, the dialogue relies too heavily on these vague phrases instead of *showing* the impact of the discovery. Consider replacing some instances with more specific descriptions of the sensations or the *effect* of the memory on the character.
*   **Elara's Reaction:** Elara’s single line, “What’s happening?!” feels very flat and reactive. Given the dramatic events unfolding, a more nuanced response would be more believable. Perhaps fear mixed with curiosity, or a specific question about the physical effects your character is experiencing. It feels like her purpose is solely to ask the obvious.
*   **Internal Monologue as Dialogue:** A significant portion of the “dialogue” is actually internal monologue presented *as* spoken lines. For example, “This isn’t just a joke… It’s a key.” feels like a thought the character is having *to themselves*, not necessarily something they’d articulate to Elara in that moment. Streamline this by showing the impact of these realizations through action and body language, rather than stating them directly.
*   **"I think I found it" - Weak Delivery:** The line "I… I think I found it" is hesitant and lacks impact. Given the magnitude of the discovery, a more declarative statement would be stronger.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The character realizes they were *building* something with these symbols in a past life/memory. This is huge! But it’s stated rather plainly. Explore the emotional impact of this realization through dialogue. Is there disbelief? Fear? A sense of purpose? A hint of arrogance? Let Elara draw it out of them.
*   **Lack of Back-and-Forth:** The scene is largely one-sided. Your character does most of the talking/internalizing. Even a few lines of questioning or observation from Elara would add depth and dynamism.

*   **Hemlock's Voice – Repetitive & Telling:** Hemlock *constantly* explains his feelings and motivations rather than *showing* them through action or subtext. Phrases like "I believed," "I dared to ask," "I was branded" are all telling, not revealing. Consider replacing some of these with internal monologue (shown through action/reaction) or letting his past actions speak for themselves.
*   **Seris – Reactive, Not Proactive:** Seris mostly asks questions. While questioning is fine, she feels passive. Give her more agency in the conversation – offer opinions, challenge Hemlock’s statements, or reveal more about her own motivations beyond simply “uncovering the truth.”
*   **"Why bury it?" – Direct & Lacking Nuance:** This question, while logical, feels a bit blunt. Consider a more indirect approach – perhaps a statement about the injustice of erasing history, or a question about *who* benefited from the burying.
*   **Hemlock's "Small Defiance" – Understated:** Given the risk he took, "small defiance" feels like a significant downplay. This feels inconsistent with the weight of his exile.
*   **"They won't let us plant it easily" – Cliche:** This line is a bit predictable and lacks impact. Try a more specific threat or a more metaphorical warning.
*   **Hand-Holding – Overused Physicality:** The repeated touching (hand hovering, covering her hand) feels a bit forced and doesn't fully justify its presence. Ensure each instance serves a clear purpose – comfort, warning, imparting knowledge – or trim it.
*   **Crimson Stain on Sketch – Symbolic, But Untied:** The mention of the crimson stain feels symbolic, but its significance isn’t fully integrated into the dialogue or Seris’s reactions. Expand on this detail.
*   **"We remember, we tell the stories" - Slightly Preachy:** This sounds like a motto rather than a natural response. Ground it in a specific memory or personal experience.
*   **Overall - Too Much Exposition:** The scene relies heavily on Hemlock *telling* Seris about the past. Consider revealing information through flashbacks, discovered documents, or environmental storytelling.

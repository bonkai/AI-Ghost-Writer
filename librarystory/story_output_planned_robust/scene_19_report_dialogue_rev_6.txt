*   **Kaelen's initial line ("It remembers") is a bit too mystical/vague.** While atmospheric, it doesn't immediately ground the conversation. Consider making it more specific to the sketch – “This sketch…it feels like more than just lines on paper.” This ties the statement to the *present* moment and avoids feeling like pure exposition.
*   **Repetitive "silent" moments.** The stage directions repeatedly emphasize silent looks and understandings. While *some* is good, it’s overused here. Trust the dialogue and actors to convey those moments. Remove a couple instances.
*   **Seris's "Lost how?" feels a little weak.** Given the building tension, she could be more direct, even a little challenging. Something like, “Lost *to whom*?” or “What was lost?” would show more agency.
*   **Kaelen’s explanation of Valerius (“He’s a guardian of the forgetting…”) is clunky and a bit on-the-nose.** It *tells* us Valerius's motivation instead of *showing* it through his actions (which are hinted at with the book slamming). Consider streamlining it to something like, “Valerius won’t be pleased we’re looking into this.”
*   **The hand-brushing moment is *very* cliché and feels forced.** The "spark ignited, quickly suppressed" language is overly dramatic. If the connection is important, show it through *what* they say or *how* they react to each other *after* the touch, not the touch itself.
*   **"Always" is a weak response.** It lacks specificity and feels like a stock romantic line. Given the danger, a more pragmatic or protective response would be more believable. Perhaps, "We will be," or "I won't let anything happen to you."
*   **The fragrance and metallic scent are sensory details, not dialogue, but they feel awkwardly placed *after* a very intimate (though cliché) moment.** It breaks the flow. Consider weaving the scent into Kaelen's dialogue, or using it as a reaction *to* something he says.

Overall, the dialogue isn’t *bad*, but it relies heavily on telling instead of showing, and leans into romantic tropes rather than organic character interaction. It needs tightening and a bit more subtlety.

*   **Lucian's Dialogue - Too Declarative:** Lucian's lines, especially "Lies," and his commands ("Take him…," "Retrieve the object") feel very direct and lack nuance. While his character is clearly authoritative, a more subtle approach would heighten the tension. Instead of *stating* "Lies," perhaps a dismissive gesture coupled with a pointed question about the flowers would be more effective. Similarly, implying the retrieval of the object rather than *ordering* it could showcase his power more subtly.

*   **Valerius - Exposition Dump:** Valerius's line, "They weren’t merely concealing it… A relic of the Archivists, perhaps?" is largely exposition. We *see* the locket is significant. Let the reader infer its importance through Kaelen's observations and Lucian's reaction. Valerius could simply state, "It’s more than a trinket," leaving the 'Archivist' connection for Kaelen to piece together (or for Lucian to confirm/deny).

*   **Lack of Subtext in Boy's Silence:** The boy's silence is effective for conveying fear, but it's *only* fear. Could his silence also hint at loyalty to whoever tasked him with hiding the locket? A fleeting, defiant glance, or a barely perceptible tightening of his jaw, could add another layer to his character.

*   **Kaelen - Internal Monologue as Dialogue Substitute:** Kaelen has *a lot* of internal monologue. While effective for conveying his perceptions, it overshadows any potential for dialogue. Consider if *some* of those observations could be revealed through brief interactions with other characters, even if it’s just a questioning glance at Valerius when the locket is identified.

*   **"Florian" - Too Dramatic:** The sudden appearance of the word "Florian" at the end feels abrupt and lacks build-up. While a powerful reveal is intended, it feels unearned. Perhaps a series of fragmented sensations or images culminating in the word would be more impactful.

*   **Overall - Missed Opportunities for Power Dynamics:** The dialogue feels somewhat flat in terms of power dynamics. Lucian, as an Inquisitor, should be able to *control* the conversation more effectively, not just issue commands. Consider how he could use questions, veiled threats, or subtle manipulations to achieve his goals.

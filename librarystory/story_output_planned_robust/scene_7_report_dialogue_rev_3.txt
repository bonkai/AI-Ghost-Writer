*   **Hemlock’s Dialogue Feels…Flat:** While enigmatic is intended, Hemlock’s lines (“Real enough, perhaps,” “Some places remember…”) are a bit *too* cryptic and lack a strong emotional undercurrent. They feel like pronouncements rather than genuine reactions to Seris’s distress. Consider adding a layer of concern, even if veiled, to make him feel more engaged.

*   **Seris’s “I…I saw things” is Weak:** This is a crucial moment! After a vision that clearly rattled her, this line feels incredibly understated. It's understandable she's shaken, but a more visceral reaction – a gasp, a fragmented sentence, even a choked sob – would be more impactful. Show, don’t just tell, her disturbance.

*   **Missed Opportunity for Subtext (Seris & Hemlock):** There’s a power dynamic at play here, and the dialogue doesn't fully exploit it. Hemlock seems to *know* more than he lets on. Seris is vulnerable and seeking reassurance. A subtle push-and-pull, where Hemlock offers cryptic guidance and Seris seeks more concrete answers, would heighten the tension.

*   **Repetitive Emphasis on "Echoes":** The word "echoes" is used multiple times in quick succession by Hemlock. While thematic, it feels a little heavy-handed. Consider varying the language to describe the sphere's influence (memories, remnants, imprints, etc.).

*   **Seris’s Internal State vs. Dialogue:** The narrative describes Seris feeling longing *and* dread, but her dialogue focuses almost entirely on the frightening aspects. A hint of that unsettling recognition or pull towards the vision would add complexity to her character and foreshadow future developments.

*   **Hemlock's Final Line - Feels Like Exposition:** “Be careful, Seris. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting* to be disturbed.” This is telling us information rather than *showing* us the danger. Could this be delivered as a question, a worried observation, or a veiled warning based on his own knowledge?

*   **Over-Reliance on Sensation/Implied Dialogue:** The majority of communication happens through sensations and implied feelings ("*Memories are dangerous*," "*A confluence… seeking someone who will listen*"). While atmospheric, it’s becoming repetitive and hinders character voice. It *tells* us information instead of letting characters *reveal* it through interaction. Consider translating some of these sensations into actual spoken dialogue, even if fragmented or cryptic.
*   **Seris’ Questions Feel Reactive, Not Proactive:** Seris largely asks questions *in response* to the sensations. Give her more agency. What is she *actively* trying to discover? What assumptions is she testing? This would make her feel less like a passive recipient of information.
*   **"Remember" – Too On-the-Nose:** The single word appearing in ink feels cliché and lacks subtlety. The scene builds tension well, but this feels like a forced plot device. Consider *showing* the memory triggering instead of explicitly stating "Remember."
*   **Repetitive Use of Physical Reactions:** The constant descriptions of cold, shivering, and the weight of the air are becoming numbing. Vary the physical responses. Fear manifests in many ways.
*   **Church Motivation Feels Summarized:** The explanation of the Church’s motives ("Protecting themselves… sole arbiters of truth") feels like exposition dumped into dialogue. Show this through their *actions* or have characters reveal it through discovered documents, rather than stating it directly.
*   **Fractured How?** - When Seris asks "Fractured *how*?" it's a good question, but the answer is still very abstract. While the visual is strong, grounding it with a *specific* example of distortion would be helpful. 
* **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Explore how Seris *reacts* to the sensations. Does she trust them? Is she skeptical? Her internal conflict would add depth.

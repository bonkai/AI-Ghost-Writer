The absence wasn’t black, but a pressure—a full-VR rig lagging at the edge of perception. He willed a hand to rise, flex, *prove* something was still connected. Nothing. No arm, no body. Just… awareness. A cursor detached from any screen, adrift in static. Panic spiked—system crash? He *remembered* breath, the ache of a chest expanding, but there were no lungs to fill. He tried to shout, to ping *something*, but the signal died in static. Untethered. Floating in null space. *What happened?* The thought fractured, less a voice than code struggling to compile. Last thing… the game. Pressure building, frantic key presses, then white. A corrupted save? The thought tasted like ash.

He fought to focus, to rebuild coherence. Thoughts scattered, each fading before forming. *Reboot. Diagnostic. Anything.* The urge wasn't logical, not for… this. A reflex, a desperate attempt to reclaim control as the system spiraled. He clung to the command, repeating it like a mantra against the encroaching void.

Then, a shift. Not location, but texture. The darkness pulsed with a faint, ethereal glow, like the dying embers of a monitor. A hum resonated—distant, architectural. Shapes resolved—a vast, cavernous space. He wasn’t *in* darkness, but *within* something. He tried to map the space, but the structures rendered as low-poly dreams, shifting and rearranging. He reached for a nearby shelf, expecting resistance, finding only… nothing. 

“Hello?” He projected the thought, a fragile signal flare. It felt flimsy, lost in a hurricane.

A response came, not as sound, but as *feeling*. A gentle ping against his awareness, laced with ancient stillness. Not a voice, but an impression—patient, observing, impossibly *old*. It didn’t answer immediately. It *considered*. 

*Aware.*

The single word resonated, and with it, the sensation of being scanned. Like an admin assessing a new process. A chill—not *through* anything, but undeniably there. He felt… labeled. He tried to pull away, to shield his thoughts, but the scan continued, relentless and thorough.

Books. Countless volumes stacked on shelves that disappeared into the gloom, their spines unreadable. He reached for one, and a flood of information washed over his awareness—cellulose and ink, the weight of centuries, a silent story waiting to be unlocked. Not a smell, but a data stream, overwhelming and intoxicating. A library beyond imagining. No librarian. A glitch?

“Where am I?” He projected again, desperation fraying the signal. The thought felt brittle, corrupted, like a file refusing to load.

The response came slower, like a firewall actively blocking connection. A subtle weight, a sense of… amusement? Not malicious, but detached.

*Between.*

“Between what?” He pushed, demanding clarity.

A long pause. Unsettling. Then, a chilling revelation, delivered with the distinct impression of something *watching*. Something that had seen… everything.

*Between worlds.*

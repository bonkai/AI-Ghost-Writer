“Seris?”

She whirled, heart hammering against her ribs. Hemlock stood in the doorway, expression unreadable. “Finding what you seek?”

Seris nodded, gaze fixed on the sphere. The room felt constricted, the weight of the darkness pressing in. She reached out, fingers brushing the cool, smooth surface almost involuntarily.

The world fractured. Not into darkness, but into layers. Vast, crumbling cities carved into mountainsides bled into winding canyons filled with bioluminescent flora. Coiled around the highest peak, a colossal serpentine creature, scales shimmering, pulsed with life.

It wasn’t the grandeur that stole her breath, but the gaze. Amidst the chaos, a single, ancient eye met hers – reptilian, gold-flecked, brimming with knowing. It didn’t merely *see* her; it looked *through* her, stripping away layers until she felt exposed. A resonance bloomed within her chest, mirroring the creature’s weariness. She stumbled back, hand flying to her temple, a low moan escaping her lips. The images fractured, receding, leaving an ache behind her eyes and a coldness that settled deep within her chest. She braced a hand against the cold stone wall, knuckles white.

A voice, not spoken, but *felt* within her mind, echoed the image of the serpent: *“Awakening…”* Not a threat, but a statement of inevitability, weighted with eons and a chilling hunger. It felt…like homecoming. She squeezed her eyes shut, then slowly opened them.

The room seemed different – heavier, shadows deeper, the thrumming beneath her feet intensified. The sphere radiated subtle heat, the carvings rearranged into an unsettling pattern. She took a shaky breath, trying to regain control.

“Seris?” Hemlock’s voice, closer now, broke the silence. He’d entered the chamber, gaze sweeping over the sphere, then settling on her face. “You seem…disturbed.”

She tried to speak, to explain the visions, the voice, the dread…and something else, akin to longing. But the words caught in her throat. She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her despite the warmth from the sphere.

“I…I saw things,” she managed, her voice a whisper. “Cities…a serpent…it felt…real. Like a memory, but not my own.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint, enigmatic smile. “Real enough, perhaps.” He reached out, fingers hovering over the sphere, but didn’t touch it. “Some places remember. Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, gaze locking with hers. “And some people…are more attuned to those echoes than others.”

He studied her intently. “Be careful, Seris. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting* to be disturbed.”

The room fell silent, save for the thrumming. She looked back at the obsidian sphere, and for a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths, a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns. She was no longer certain what she had awakened, but she knew, with chilling certainty, that her life had irrevocably changed. The darkness wasn’t just around her; it was *within* her now, a silent promise of things to come. And for the first time, she felt a strange, unsettling sense of recognition. A feeling that she wasn't just *seeing* the past, but remembering it.

*   **Initial Pacing Feels Static:** The first four paragraphs are largely exposition delivered through dialogue. While the content is important, the rhythm is flat. Consider interweaving *some* action or Seris’s internal reaction *within* the dialogue to break it up.
*   **Tension Build is Gradual, But Slow:** The increasing cold, flickering pages, and extinguished candle are good atmospheric touches, but they happen relatively late in the scene. The tension needs to begin building *earlier*, even subtly.
*   **Paragraph 8 & 9 – Opportunity Missed:** Seris asks “So the Church isn’t just suppressing knowledge, they’re… protecting us?” This is a *key* moment for internal conflict and should be given more space. Explore her doubt, her confusion, her feeling of being misled. The quick follow-up from the ‘voice’ feels rushed.
*   **Paragraph 14 – Strongest Tension Peak:** The spectral image is effective, and the single word “Remember” is a good cliffhanger. However, the build-up to this peak feels a bit uneven.
*   **Ending – Effective, But Could Be Sharper:** The final paragraph is well-written, but the “weight of forgotten ages” feels slightly cliché. Consider a more visceral, immediate sensation to leave the reader with.
*   **Overall:** The scene is *mostly* well-paced, but leans towards being too slow in the first half. The tension arc is present, but could be amplified by earlier and more frequent micro-tension moments. Consider incorporating more of Seris's *physical* reactions to the growing strangeness – a quickened pulse, a cold sweat, a tightening in her chest – to draw the reader more fully into her experience.

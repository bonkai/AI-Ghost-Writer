*   **Subtext & Non-Verbal Communication:** The scene *heavily* relies on subtle physical cues (hand positions, whitening knuckles, shallow breathing). While effective, it borders on overdoing it. Consider *reducing* the number of described micro-expressions and letting the dialogue carry more weight. The constant description diminishes the impact of the truly important signals.
*   **Rhys’s Silence:** Rhys is almost entirely reactive. His internal state is shown *through* action/description, but he doesn’t *speak* much. While understandable given the tension, giving him *one* line of carefully chosen dialogue – even a short, ambiguous response to the merchant – would strengthen his character and prevent him from feeling passive.
*   **Merchant’s Motivation:** The merchant’s interest feels a little vague. While mystery is good, "unusual geological formations" and "anything out of place" are broad. A *hint* of *why* he’s interested (even a veiled one) would add another layer of intrigue. Is he a collector? An explorer? Something more sinister?
*   **Seris’s “Botanist” Line:** This feels a bit weak and defensive. It's a standard denial. Consider a more layered response – something that acknowledges their knowledge *without* admitting to mapmaking. Perhaps a comment about the region’s unique flora *leading* them to explore unusual areas.
*   **Repetition of Subtlety:** The descriptions of subtle signals are very strong, but also repeated. For example, multiple descriptions of Seris’s hands and breathing. Streamline these. Choose the *most* impactful signal for each beat.
*   **“Charges a premium”:** This line is a bit cliché. It lacks punch. Consider a more evocative way to convey the cost. Perhaps hinting at a specific, rare item or service the contact demands.
*   **Overall:** The dialogue isn't *bad*, it's just overly reliant on showing rather than telling through speech. The scene is strong on atmosphere and tension, but the dialogue could be more dynamic and revealing.

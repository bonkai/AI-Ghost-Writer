*   **Subtext & Indirect Characterization:** While the dialogue *implies* tension and distrust, it’s quite direct. Valerius’s veiled threats (“discoveries…or misfortunes”) are effective, but feel a little on the nose. Consider *showing* his skepticism more through subtle actions and phrasing. For example, instead of stating the mountain "tests preparations," he could ask a leading question like, “Have you accounted for the shifting shale after a prolonged rain?” or simply offer a dry, “Ambitious.”
*   **Efficiency & Purpose:** Rhys’s explanation of their research (“We’ve been meticulously researching the terrain, the weather patterns…”) feels like an info-dump, delivered *to Valerius* who likely already knows this if he's familiar with Firepeak. It doesn’t feel organic. Perhaps this information could be revealed through internal monologue or a brief exchange *between* Seris and Rhys earlier in the scene.
*   **Authenticity & Voice:** Seris and Rhys’s responses are a bit *too* controlled, especially given the pressure Valerius is applying. A slight crack in their composure – a sharper tone, a delayed response, a more visible physical reaction – would make them feel more real and the scene more dynamic. The “muscle ticking in her jaw” is good, but needs to be reflected in her *words*.
*   **Flow & Rhythm:** The dialogue is a series of statements and questions, lacking much back-and-forth. While this contributes to the tense atmosphere, it feels slightly static. A shorter exchange, a pointed question from Seris, or a more forceful interruption from Rhys could inject more energy.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** The repeated focus on *how* they respond (muscle ticking, pulse in temple, hand covering hers) is good, but could be strengthened by tying those reactions *to* specific lines of dialogue. For example, Seris could subtly flinch *while* Valerius is speaking, or Rhys's hand could tighten on the book *as* Valerius delivers a veiled threat.

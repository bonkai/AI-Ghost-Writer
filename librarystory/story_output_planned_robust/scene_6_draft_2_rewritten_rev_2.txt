The chill started at the base of my skull, a prickling that intensified with each attempt. I reached out again, not with words, but with intent, focusing on the serpent image. It wasn't a clear picture, more a fragment of carved stone, a sinuous curve hinting at ancient power.

This time, contact felt different. Not a blank wall, but a yielding resistance, like pushing against oiled silk. A flicker beyond the stone – a vast, echoing chamber choked with shadow, the faintest impression of watching eyes. Then, a tightening behind my temples.

“Is everything alright, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice, smooth as polished obsidian, cut through my concentration. He hadn’t moved, but his unblinking gaze felt like a weight.

I forced my eyes open, blinking against the dim light. “Just admiring the architecture,” I stammered, the lie tasting like ash. “This place is…remarkable.”

I tried again, willing the serpent image to solidify, to *reveal* itself. Energy surged through me, and for a moment, I felt a resonance, not with a being, but with the stone itself, with the Atheneum’s foundations. It felt…old. Immeasurably so. And profoundly unwilling.

A sharp pain lanced through my head, and I gasped, stumbling back. A bookshelf rattled, a heavy tome tumbling to the floor with a muffled thud. The scent of aged paper and dust filled the air.

“Careful now,” Hemlock said, amusement lacing his voice. He didn’t move to help. “These volumes are fragile. As are your…abilities, it seems.”

I ignored him, pressing through the discomfort, focusing on what the carving *meant*. I sent another probe outwards, a desperate plea. This time, the resistance wasn't yielding silk, but a solid wall of stone. It felt like slamming into a glacier.

The air grew cold, a subtle stillness descending, muffling all sound. I didn’t notice, too consumed by the effort, but I *felt* it – a tremor in the floor, a low hum resonating from the Atheneum’s core, a prickling on my skin, as if near a storm.

Then, the wall *moved*. Not physically, but…internally. It wasn’t just blocking me; it was actively repelling me, pushing against my mind with ancient, hostile force. A warning flooded my senses: *Stay away. This is not for you.*

I recoiled, clutching my head, the pressure behind my eyes threatening to overwhelm me. The humming intensified, then abruptly ceased, leaving ringing silence. The cold remained, clinging like a shroud.

“Perhaps you’ve been studying too long,” Hemlock said, rising and slowly approaching. His shadow stretched across the floor, engulfing me. “The Atheneum can be overwhelming for the unfamiliar. Some are simply…not meant to understand.”

I backed away, my hand instinctively reaching beneath my cloak, fingers closing around my dagger’s hilt. “I’m fine,” I said, my voice tight. “Just a bit lightheaded.”

One last time, I sent a desperate plea outwards, a fragile tendril of intent reaching for the serpent image. *Please. I need to understand.*

Silence. Absolute, impenetrable silence. The Atheneum hadn't just shut me out; it had *erased* me, as if I were never there. The connection severed, leaving a void.

I realized I wasn’t communicating with a spirit, but reaching something far older, far more powerful, and it clearly didn’t want to be disturbed. And it had made its displeasure known.

Hemlock stopped a few feet away, dark eyes boring into mine. “The Atheneum chooses who it reveals its secrets to,” he murmured, his voice a low caress. “And it seems, Initiate, it has not chosen you. Perhaps it sensed…ambition. A desire for knowledge exceeding your station.”

He paused, his gaze sweeping over me with chilling assessment. “Some secrets are best left buried. For your sake, and for those who would be…disappointed by your curiosity.” He took another step closer, his voice dropping to a whisper. “Consider this a warning.”

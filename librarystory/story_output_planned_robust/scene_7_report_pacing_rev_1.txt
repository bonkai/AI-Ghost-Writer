*   **Initial Setup (Paragraphs 1-2):** The opening is solid, establishing mystery and a sense of unease. However, the description of the chamber, while atmospheric, lingers a bit *too* long before the core event (touching the sphere) occurs. This slightly slows momentum.
*   **Vision Sequence (Paragraphs 3-6):** This is where the scene shines. The layered imagery and the focus on the *gaze* of the creature are excellent. The pacing *within* the vision is well-handled, building from grand landscapes to intensely personal exposure. The internal experience is prioritized, which is effective.
*   **Post-Vision (Paragraphs 7-9):** The return to reality feels slightly rushed. The description of the changed room is good, but the immediate dialogue with Hemlock feels a bit abrupt after the intensity of the vision. A beat or two to allow Seris to fully *react* to the changed environment would strengthen the impact.
*   **Hemlock's Dialogue (Paragraphs 10-12):** Hemlock’s enigmatic responses are effective for building tension, but his prolonged withholding of information begins to feel a little passive. The final paragraph, with the potential movement within the sphere, is a strong closing image, but could be *even* more impactful if preceded by a slightly longer pause of silence and anticipation.
*   **Overall Tension Arc:** The tension builds beautifully *during* the vision sequence, peaking with the eye contact. However, the tension *after* the vision plateaus somewhat. More sustained reaction and questioning would elevate the overall impact.

While the scene is strong, it could benefit from a few adjustments to refine the pacing and maintain a consistently high level of tension throughout.

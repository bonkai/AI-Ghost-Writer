The oil lamp sputtered, then died, plunging Seris into a darkness that felt less like absence and more like a suffocating weight. A draft snaked through the archive, colder than the stone walls, carrying the scent of dust, withered lilies, and something faintly metallic—old blood, perhaps. Her hand drifted to the hilt of her dagger, fingers tightening on the worn leather, a familiar grounding instinct. She hadn’t expected the chill to penetrate so deeply, to settle in her bones.

Valerius didn’t enter the darkness. He *became* visible within it, coalescing from the shadows, two Guardians materializing beside him, their armor absorbing, not reflecting, the faint light. Beneath his robes, a subtle, pulsing glow mirrored the tome’s illustrations, a heartbeat in the gloom. He paused before the open book, gaze fixed on the depiction of the Aethel. He didn’t ask a question, instead tracing a finger across the illustration – a slow, deliberate caress that felt less like inquiry and more like claiming possession.

Seris’s eyes scanned the image, then flicked back to Valerius, a subtle tightening of her jaw. The Aethel—gods of a forgotten age, dismissed as myth, relegated to children’s tales. She hesitated, a flicker of fear crossing her face, quickly masked. Years she’d spent believing the official histories, accepting the Church’s narrative. Doubts, long suppressed, now surfaced, cold and unwelcome, like shards of ice in her veins.

“The histories claim they were lost to time,” she said, her voice a little too steady, a little too defensive.

Valerius tilted his head, a slow, deliberate movement. “Lost? Or… *rearranged*?” He paused, his gaze unwavering, a subtle pressure against her mind. “Consider a tapestry, Initiate. Threads can be cut, patterns altered, colors… dimmed.”

A prickling sensation rose on the back of her neck. She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius, her grip tightening on the dagger, the metal cold against her palm. The metallic scent intensified, and she noticed the polished steel of the Guardians’ armor didn’t reflect the darkness, but *drank* it, leaving no gleam, no hint of light. A chill settled over her, a sense of something deeply, fundamentally wrong. The weight of the archive felt heavier now, oppressive, as if the very stones were pressing in on her.

“What do you mean, ‘rearranged’?” Her voice was sharper than she intended, betraying the tremor in her hand. She forced herself to meet Valerius’s gaze, refusing to flinch, refusing to appear intimidated.

Valerius’s lips curved in a slow, predatory smile. He didn’t answer, instead gesturing to a Guardian. “The Initiate requires rest. Escort her back to her chambers.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. Seris didn’t bother feigning compliance. Resistance would be futile, and she knew, with chilling clarity, that she *had* to return to this archive—alone. The Guardian’s grip tightened on her arm, but she didn't pull away. Not yet. She understood, in that moment, that the Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound, the threads carefully concealed. Valerius held those threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer.

She allowed herself to be led away, a single thought echoing in her mind: *This archive isn't protecting the past. It’s a prison,* and she was beginning to suspect she was a prisoner within it. A desperate hope bloomed within her: she *had* to find proof, something concrete, before Valerius could unravel everything. She needed to understand *how* he’d done it, and what he intended to do with the stolen light.

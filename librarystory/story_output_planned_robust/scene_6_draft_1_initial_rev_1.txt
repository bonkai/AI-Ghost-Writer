## Scene 6 - Draft 1

Seris traced the spine of a crumbling tome, pretending to decipher its faded title. She needed a moment to collect herself. The sensation from the silver clasp – the flash of stone and serpents – still prickled beneath her skin. It felt… directed. Like a whisper meant for her alone. 

She risked a glance towards Hemlock. Still at his desk, seemingly absorbed in cataloging scrolls, but Seris knew better. He was watching. Always watching. 

She tried to reach out, to recapture the feeling, to *ask* what the serpent meant. But there was nothing. Just the scent of dust and the oppressive silence of the Atheneum. Frustration tightened her jaw. It wasn’t a memory, not exactly. It felt… external. As if something – or someone – had *shown* her, then retreated.

“Beautiful bindings, aren’t they?” she called out, aiming for a casual tone. “So much history preserved within these walls.”

Silence. Hemlock didn’t even glance up.

Seris tried again, focusing her intent, attempting to project a thought, a question, *anything* towards the source of the earlier impression. *Hello? Is anyone there?* 

Nothing. Only the echo of her own mental effort. It felt like shouting into a vast, empty cavern.

She closed her eyes, concentrating. She pictured the serpent, the intricate stone carvings, trying to amplify the feeling, to draw it back. A faint pressure built behind her temples, a subtle thrumming in her ears. But it was fleeting, ephemeral. Like trying to grasp smoke.

“Is everything alright, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice, smooth and unsettling, cut through her concentration. He hadn’t moved, but his gaze was now fixed on her, unblinking.

Seris forced her eyes open, blinking rapidly. “Just… admiring the architecture,” she stammered, feeling a flush creep up her neck. “This place is truly remarkable.”

She tried again, more forcefully this time, to send a mental probe outwards. *Can you hear me? Show me the serpent again.* She focused all her will, picturing the image with unwavering clarity. 

This time, the pressure behind her eyes intensified, becoming almost painful. A wave of dizziness washed over her, and she stumbled, catching herself on a nearby bookshelf. Books shifted and threatened to tumble.

“Careful now,” Hemlock said, his voice laced with something that sounded suspiciously like amusement. “These volumes are quite fragile.”

Seris ignored him, pushing through the discomfort. She was so focused, so intent, that she didn’t notice the subtle shift in the air around her, the almost imperceptible hum that vibrated through the shelves. 

Then, a flicker. A fleeting impression, not of an image, but of… resistance. Like hitting an invisible wall. A sense of being *blocked*. 

She gasped, recoiling as if burned. The pressure in her head vanished, leaving her feeling drained and disoriented. 

“Perhaps you’ve been studying for too long,” Hemlock said, rising from his desk and slowly approaching her. “The Atheneum can be… overwhelming for those unfamiliar with its energies.”

Seris backed away, her hand instinctively reaching for the hilt of the dagger hidden beneath her cloak. “I’m fine,” she said, her voice tight. “I just… felt a bit lightheaded.”

She tried one last time, a desperate plea sent outwards. *Please. I need to understand.*

Silence. Absolute, impenetrable silence. It was as if the Atheneum itself had shut her out. 

She realized, with a sinking feeling, that she wasn’t communicating with a person, or even a spirit. She was trying to reach something far older, far more powerful, and it clearly didn’t want to be disturbed. 

Hemlock stopped a few feet away, his shadow falling over her. “The Atheneum chooses who it reveals its secrets to,” he said, his voice a low murmur. “And it seems, Initiate, that it has not chosen you.”

He paused, his dark eyes boring into hers. “Perhaps it’s best to leave the secrets buried.”

*   **Seris’s initial line (“The pursuit of knowledge…”) feels a bit too formal and *telling* rather than *showing*.** It’s a statement of philosophy delivered *to* Marius, when a more subtle demonstration of her dedication to knowledge (through action, or a response to something he said) would be stronger. Consider removing it entirely and letting her actions speak for themselves.

*   **Marius's line about the Bloom remembering is a bit of an info-dump.** While the information is important, it's delivered as exposition. Could some of this be revealed through Seris or Rhys *reacting* to something Marius observes, or through a brief, cryptic exchange? Perhaps he finds an old text and comments on the script, prompting Seris to explain its significance.

*   **Rhys is *very* silent.** While his silence is intentional, it's bordering on static. A single, well-placed grunt or a pointed glance could add more texture to his character and create more tension. His focused axe-cleaning is a good visual, but it needs a little verbal or non-verbal counterpoint to Marius's dialogue.

*   **The exchange of silent understanding between Seris and Rhys at the end is weak.** "A silent understanding passing between them" is a narrative telling, not a dramatic showing. Instead of *telling* us they're ready, *show* it. A brief, shared glance, a subtle shift in posture, or even a single, whispered word would be more effective.

*   **Marius's final line ("Everyone has debts…") is a cliché.** While it works on a surface level, it feels predictable. Consider a more unique way to hint at Seris’s past or to create a sense of threat. Perhaps a veiled reference to a specific event or a person she's lost.

*   **Kaelen’s mental voice-overs are disruptive.** They feel like unnecessary narration. If the information is crucial, find a way to integrate it into Seris’s internal thoughts or have it revealed through her actions.

*   **Overall:** The dialogue is functional but lacks nuance and subtext. It relies heavily on characters stating their intentions or feelings rather than *demonstrating* them. The scene would benefit from more indirect characterization, more subtext, and a reduction in exposition.

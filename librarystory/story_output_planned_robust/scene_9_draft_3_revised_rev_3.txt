## Scene 9 - Draft 3

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light illuminating the alcove, the air thick with the scent of aged paper and a faint, coppery tang that prickled at Seris’s nostrils. She ran a finger along the spine of a crumbling tome, brittle leather flaking beneath her touch. “Before kingdoms, before gods… the stories say there were currents. Understandings. The Echo… it holds fragments, doesn’t it?”

Kaelen’s voice resonated not from lips, but *through* the swirling patterns in the pages before her. “Imagine a tapestry. Each thread a life, a moment. Pull one, and the whole thing begins to unravel. The Order believed everything was connected – memory, magic, even the land itself.”

A stone skittered from the archway, startling Seris. She glanced up, a flicker of annoyance crossing her face. “The Church branded the Order heretical, accused them of forbidden magic.” She crossed her arms, not defensively, but as if bracing against a chill. “They said it was power-hungry. A corruption.”

The pages turned themselves, settling on a manuscript detailing the Church’s rise. Seris touched a nearby book, a faint tremor running through its spine. She closed her eyes, concentrating on the sensation, a subtle ache blooming in her fingertips. “They fear what they don’t understand. The Order’s magic wasn’t about *doing*… it was about *listening*.”

“Listening to what?” Seris pressed, her voice sharper, demanding specifics. She paced a short distance, her movements restless. “What were they hearing?”

A pause. The air grew noticeably colder, the pages trembling. “Because some memories are dangerous. The Echo isn’t a simple archive. It’s fractured… corrupted.”

“Corrupted how?” 

“Imagine threads within the tapestry fraying, twisting… poisoning the whole cloth. Not with malice, but with… dissonance. Memories forced out of time, echoes bleeding into each other.”

Seris’s breath hitched. “Unravel the world?” She stopped pacing, her gaze fixed on the swirling patterns, searching for a visual answer.

“Not instantly. But a single, corrupted thread can weaken the weave, create tears… and those tears can widen. Allowing things *through* that shouldn’t be.”

“So the Church isn’t just suppressing knowledge… they’re protecting us?” A strange weight settled on her chest, a growing unease. She looked away, unable to meet the swirling patterns.

“Protecting themselves. They claim to be the sole arbiters of truth, the guardians of Eldoria. They see the Echo as a threat to their authority, a source of chaos. Centuries of erasing the Order’s legacy.”

Leaning against a bookshelf, Seris felt exhaustion wash over her. “And the Firepeak Mountains? The rumors of a hidden archive?”

The pages stilled, then rippled faster. A gust of wind rattled the windowpanes, extinguishing the candle. Seris jumped, her heart pounding. Darkness descended, thick and absolute. “The Firepeaks are a nexus point, where the veil between ages is thin. The Order built a citadel there, a repository for the most dangerous fragments. Protected by… echoes of the past.”

A sudden chill ran down Seris’s spine. “And you? Are you… an echo?”

A long silence. The pages remained still. Then, a voice – not spoken, but *felt* – resonated within her mind. *“I am a confluence. Fragments drawn together by your presence. What remains of the Order’s collective consciousness, seeking someone who will listen.”*

Seris swept her gaze across the shadowed alcove, a growing dread tightening her chest. “Why me?”

*“The Church knows you’re close to the truth. They will be coming.”* The air grew frigid. A spectral image flickered in the swirling ink – a hooded figure, silhouetted against a burning city. It vanished as quickly as it appeared. 

The pages settled. A single word formed in the swirling ink, glowing with an ethereal light: *“Remember.”* Then, silence. A silence so profound it felt like a weight pressing down on her soul. The last vestige of warmth vanished, leaving Seris shivering in the darkness. She wrapped her arms around herself, her mind reeling.

She didn’t just *think* *What have I gotten myself into?* She *felt* it, a cold dread settling deep in her bones. This wasn’t about uncovering lost knowledge anymore. This was about something far more dangerous, and she was caught in the middle. She pressed her hand against the cool stone of the bookshelf, grounding herself. The stone felt…wrong. Distorted. As if the very walls were whispering secrets she wasn't meant to hear.

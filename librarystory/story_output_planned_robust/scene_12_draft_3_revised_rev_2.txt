## Scene 12 - Draft 3

The single oil lamp sputtered and died, plunging the archive into darkness. The air grew heavy, stagnant. A draft, colder than stone, swept through the chamber, carrying the scent of dust and something else… something metallic and faintly floral, like dried lilies pressed between ancient pages. Seris instinctively reached for the hilt of her dagger, her knuckles white against the worn leather.

“We’re not alone,” she whispered, her breath misting in the cold air.

A voice, smooth as polished obsidian, echoed from the shadows. “Intriguing discoveries, Initiate. I didn’t realize you possessed such…historical curiosity.”

It was Archon Valerius, his form coalescing from the darkness not as an entrance, but as a *manifestation*. He didn’t *walk* into being, he *became* visible, as if drawn into existence by her very investigation. Two heavily armored Guardians materialized beside him, their movements unsettlingly silent, their faces hidden behind featureless steel helms. They didn't stand *beside* him, they *extended* from him, like extensions of his will.

“I was merely researching ancient texts, Archon,” Seris said, forcing herself to meet his gaze. His eyes, even in the dimness, seemed to *consume* the surrounding darkness, leaving only a hollow emptiness in their wake.

Valerius glided closer, his movements fluid and unnervingly graceful. He didn’t touch the open tome, but the air around it seemed to *waver* in his presence, the ink blurring as if disturbed by a cold breath. “Sometimes, Initiate,” he said, his voice a low resonance, “history isn’t written, it’s… curated.”

“Curated?” Seris echoed, her voice barely a whisper.

Valerius didn’t answer directly. Instead, he gestured towards the illustration of the Aethel with a long, slender finger. “Tell me, Initiate, what do you see when you look at this image?”

“A civilization,” Seris replied, “lost to time. Erased.”

“Erased?” Valerius tilted his head, a subtle movement that sent a shiver down Seris’s spine. “Or… repurposed? What if I told you that nothing truly vanishes, but merely… transforms?” He paused, letting the question hang in the air. “Consider the loom, Initiate. Threads can be cut, yes, but they can also be woven into something new. Something… stronger.”

Seris felt a prickling on the back of her neck, a sensation of being watched, not by eyes, but by something far older, far more powerful. The metallic scent intensified, mingling with the dust, becoming almost suffocating. She could feel the weight of the archive pressing down on her, the accumulated years of forgotten knowledge and suppressed power. Her fingers tightened around the hilt of her dagger. 

She swallowed, the metallic taste coating her tongue. She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius, a growing unease twisting in her gut. The air felt…wrong. Charged. She needed to end this conversation. Now.

“I think,” she said slowly, her voice strained, “I’ve read enough.” A tremor ran through her hands, betraying her fear. She began to rise, intending to signal her departure.

Valerius’s smile widened, but it didn’t feel like a gesture of warmth. It was a predator’s acknowledgment of a captured prey. “A wise decision, Initiate. Some doors are best left unopened. And some truths… are best left undisturbed.” He gestured to one of the Guardians, the movement precise and economical. “Escort the Initiate back to her chambers. She needs…rest.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. As it approached, Seris noticed something unsettling – the polished steel of its armor wasn't reflecting the darkness, but *absorbing* it, swallowing the light whole. It was as if the darkness itself was drawn to the Guardian, feeding its form. 

As she turned to leave, she glanced back at Valerius. He was still standing before the open tome, his gaze fixed on the illustration of the Aethel. But he wasn't looking *at* the image. He was looking *through* it, as if seeing something beyond the page, something hidden within the woven threads of the past. And in that moment, Seris understood. The Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound, and Valerius was the one who held the threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer. A cold certainty settled over her. She had stumbled onto something dangerous, something far older and more powerful than she could have imagined. And Valerius would do anything to keep it hidden.

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, her fingers tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure – a forgotten goddess, judging by Rhys’s texts. The stone felt cold beneath her touch. She instinctively tightened her grip on the hilt of her dagger, scanning the shadows, a flicker of unease crossing her face.

“You seek to understand why I strayed from the Light?” Old Man Hemlock’s voice was brittle, echoing slightly in the cavern. He emerged from the gloom, pacing like a caged animal, his gaze darting between the shadows and the mosaic. “Some doors are best left unopened. Some memories…a weight best left undisturbed.” He stopped abruptly, hand rising to cover his ear, as if warding off a sound, then lowered it, a haunted look in his eyes. “The Order has long ears. Even in exile, we are not truly alone.”

Seris didn’t turn, senses on high alert, her jaw tightening. “I seek to understand *you*,” she replied, voice steady despite the tremor in her gut. “What did they show you in those archives?”

Hemlock paused, then slowly lowered himself to a weathered stool, his joints creaking in protest. He avoided her gaze, studying the worn stone floor. “I believed in the purity of the Light. A meticulous scholar, devoted to cataloging sanctioned blessings.” He glanced at the mosaic, a flicker of something akin to longing in his eyes. “Then I found the records. Buried, not destroyed. They weren’t meant to be *lost*, merely…hidden.”

Seris leaned forward, breath catching, her hand instinctively reaching for the Sunstone Bloom sketch in her pack. “What kind of records?”

“Records of the Before-Times.” Hemlock’s voice dropped to a rasping whisper, his gaze sweeping the cavern as if expecting to be overheard. “A world steeped in magic, not controlled blessings, but a raw, untamed force.” He gestured to a faded section of the mosaic, depicting a swirling vortex, a wistful expression crossing his face. “I saw images…cities built of shimmering crystal, powered by the lifeblood of the earth. Gardens that bloomed with bioluminescent flora.” He reached out, his fingers brushing Seris’s arm, then quickly withdrawing as if burned, his eyes widening in a moment of panic. “And records of *why* that magic was suppressed.”

He stepped back into the shadows, his voice barely a murmur. “They didn’t just fear the magic, child. They feared what people *remembered* with it. The power it gave them, the questions it inspired.”

Seris’s jaw tightened, her fingers tracing the outline of the sketch. “What do you mean?”

“The Order deemed those memories dangerous. They didn’t destroy them. They *buried* them. And built the Church upon the grave, a monument to forgetting.” A faint scraping echoed from the tunnel entrance, and Hemlock whirled around, his hand instinctively reaching for a hidden knife. He scanned the darkness, his breath coming in ragged gasps.

Seris followed his gaze, her hand tightening on the hilt of her dagger. “They suspect?”

Hemlock lowered his hand, his gaze returning to Seris. “This place is warded, hidden by the very magic they seek to control. But the wards won’t hold forever.” He glanced at the Sunstone Bloom sketch peeking from her pack. “They believe the flower might unlock something. Something they desperately want to keep buried.”

“I questioned it,” Hemlock continued, his voice barely a whisper, his eyes filled with a haunted look. “I dared to ask *why*. And for that, I was branded a heretic. Stripped of my rank. Exiled.” He stumbled back, clutching his chest, a tremor running through his body. “They said I was ‘unstable.’ ‘A danger to the faithful.’”

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls.

Hemlock’s gaze drifted to the shadows, a flicker of defiance in his eyes. “I couldn’t stand by and watch them disappear. They are a warning, child. A reminder of what we’ve lost…and what we’ve been made to forget.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris, his expression grim.

Seris met his gaze, her jaw tight. The crimson stain on the sketch felt like a brand, a weight on her soul. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower. She was chasing a truth the Church would kill to keep buried. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, she felt a prickling sensation on her skin, a sense of being watched.

Beneath the fear, a new resolve hardened within her. This wasn’t just about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it. She tightened her grip on her pack, knuckles white. "What can we do?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper, but filled with a newfound determination. "How do we fight a war against forgetting?"

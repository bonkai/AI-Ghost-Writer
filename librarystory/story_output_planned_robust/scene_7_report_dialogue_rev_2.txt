*   **Repetitive Emotional Signposting:** The descriptions of Seris’s internal state (“heart pounding,” “felt…watched,” “chilling certainty,” “strange, unsettling sense of…recognition”) are doing a lot of the work that dialogue *could* be doing. Show, don’t tell. Let her reactions *in* conversation reveal these feelings.
*   **Hemlock's Enigmatic Responses:** Hemlock’s dialogue is *almost* too vague. While mystery is good, his constant enigmatic pronouncements ("Real enough, perhaps," "Some people…are more attuned") start to feel like a narrative crutch. He's telling us he's mysterious, rather than *being* mysterious through specific word choices and subtext. Consider *why* he speaks this way – is he deliberately obscuring information, testing her, or genuinely unsure? Let that motivation inform his phrasing.
*   **Seris's Limited Vocalization:** Seris's initial description of the vision is a long block of internal monologue before she manages a weak "I…I saw things." This feels passive. Even a fragmented, breathless attempt to describe it *immediately* would be more engaging. ("Cities…and a serpent…it was like…a dream…but…")
*   **"Real enough, perhaps." - Feels unnatural.** It's a very formal and slightly awkward response to what Seris is implying. It doesn't quite fit a character who, even if mysterious, would likely respond with something more grounded.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The dialogue doesn't fully capitalize on the dynamic between Seris and Hemlock. Is there a power imbalance? A history of trust or mistrust? The dialogue could hint at this more subtly.
*   **Subtext Potential:** The line "Or…perhaps it has been *waiting* to be disturbed" is good, but could be even stronger. Hemlock could deliver it with a specific tone or look that suggests *he* knows more than he's letting on.
*   **The "voice" – internal monologue disguised as a plot device.** The line "Awakening…" feels like a shortcut. While effective for establishing the voice, consider how this could be *hinted* at through Seris's physical reactions or fragmented thoughts *before* the direct "voice" appears.

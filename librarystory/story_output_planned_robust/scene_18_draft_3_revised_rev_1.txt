## Draft 3: The Crimson Bloom

The scent of aged parchment and dust motes hung heavy in the air, a familiar comfort that did little to soothe the prickle of awareness crawling across Seris’s skin. Kaelen’s presence was a cool current beneath her thoughts, a silent commentary on the performance she was about to enact. She dipped her quill into the ink, the nib scratching against the vellum as she deepened the shading on the Sunstone Bloom’s petals.

“A remarkably resilient specimen, considering its purported habitat,” she murmured, more to herself than to Rhys, but carefully calibrating the volume to carry. “The texts claim it thrives on sheer cliffs, nourished by glacial melt and…a rather peculiar form of lichen.”

Kaelen’s thought-voice was a whisper in her mind: *Subtlety. He’s watching for cracks in the facade.*

Rhys, ever the adept performer, was already scanning a crumbling tome on Firepeak’s geological formations. He traced a finger across a faded illustration of a precarious rock face. “The strata are…unstable, to say the least. Landslides are common, particularly after heavy rainfall. Makes access…challenging.” He glanced at Seris, a shared glance that spoke volumes of practiced deception.

*Good,* Kaelen approved. *Let him see the 'difficulties'. Make it seem like a genuine scholarly pursuit, hampered by logistical hurdles.*

A shadow fell across their worktable, not abrupt, but a gradual obscuring of the lamplight. Valerius hadn’t approached directly, but lingered a few feet away, his presence radiating a quiet intensity. He hadn’t spoken, simply observed, his gaze sweeping over their workspace, taking in the scattered texts, the half-finished sketch, the carefully curated chaos. The air seemed to thicken, pressing in on Seris’s lungs.

“Still chasing the whispers of ancient remedies?” he asked, his voice smooth as polished stone, but with an undercurrent of something unreadable.

Rhys straightened, forcing a casualness that didn't quite reach his eyes. “We’ve encountered a…complex text, detailing several potential applications. It’s proving…challenging to separate verifiable fact from folklore.” He gestured towards Seris’s sketch, a subtle offering, a carefully constructed piece of bait.

Valerius’s gaze followed Rhys’s hand, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. He studied it for a long moment, his expression unreadable. A flicker of something – curiosity, perhaps, but also calculation – crossed his face. He didn’t comment on the sketch itself.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly, his voice a low murmur. “A rare specimen, indeed. Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon – a phenomenon rarely witnessed, and even more rarely documented.”

*He’s testing the depth of our obsession,* Kaelen warned, the thought a sharp prickle against Seris’s awareness. *Don’t overplay it. Authenticity is key.*

Seris met Valerius’s gaze, her expression carefully neutral, a practiced mask of scholarly interest. “We’ve found several accounts of its use in ancient healing practices, particularly among the mountain tribes. We’re hoping to corroborate those claims, to separate fact from folklore, as Rhys mentioned.”

“Firepeak Mountain is a dangerous place to search for folklore,” Valerius said, his voice laced with a subtle warning. The scent of his sandalwood cologne drifted towards them, a subtle assertion of power, a reminder of his control. “Many have ventured into those peaks and never returned. The mountain claims those who underestimate its power.”

Rhys, quick on the uptake, responded smoothly. “We understand the risks, of course. We’ve been meticulously researching the terrain, the weather patterns, the local flora and fauna. We’re preparing for a challenging expedition.”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, assessing, calculating. He didn’t respond immediately. The silence stretched, thick and suffocating. Then, a slow, deliberate smile touched his lips. It didn't reach his eyes.

“Perhaps,” he said, his voice dangerously soft. “Perhaps you are. In that case, I wish you good fortune. Though I suspect…the mountain will test your preparations.” He paused, his eyes locking with Seris’s. “It always does.”

*He knows,* Kaelen’s thought was a cold current, but laced with a strange excitement. *He’s playing with us, letting us believe we’re leading him on. But he's also laying a trap. A subtle one.*

Seris held his gaze, her expression carefully neutral. “We’re prepared for any eventuality.” A slight tilt of her head, a carefully crafted expression of unwavering determination.

Valerius’s smile widened, but it remained unsettlingly cold. “Indeed. I look forward to hearing of your discoveries…or your misfortunes.” He turned, his cloak swirling around him, and moved away, leaving Seris and Rhys bathed in the flickering lamplight, and the chilling realization that the game had just begun.

Kaelen’s voice, now a quiet hum in her mind, offered a single, chilling observation: *He doesn't believe we're seeking a bloom. He believes we're seeking something else entirely.*

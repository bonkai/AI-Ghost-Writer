*   **Initial Pacing:** The opening paragraphs (Seris’s fear, Rhys’s questioning) feel slightly static. While establishing mood is important, the internal thoughts and dialogue exchanges are a little lengthy before the reveal of the Gardener’s knowledge. Consider condensing the first three paragraphs to heighten the sense of urgency.
*   **Information Dump:** The revealed passages from the pages, while crucial, are presented in relatively quick succession. While the fragmented nature is intentional, the *delivery* feels a bit rushed. Adding a beat of reaction *between* each reveal – a line of internal thought from Seris or Rhys processing the information – would allow the tension to breathe and prevent it from feeling like an info-dump.
*   **Tension Peaks & Valleys:** The scene largely *builds* tension effectively, but it's a fairly consistent build without significant valleys. Introducing a momentary lull – perhaps a false lead or a moment of quiet contemplation – *before* the final reveals (“The Veil wasn’t meant to protect…”) would make those reveals even more impactful.
*   **Final Reveal Pacing:** The sequence of Kaelen’s final revelations (the image, the weakening voice, the explanation of the Veil) is strong, but could be *slightly* elongated. The image is almost *too* fleeting. Allowing Seris to experience a slightly more sustained (though still fragmented) vision would amplify the impact and her reaction.
*   **Hook/Transition:** The scene ends on a powerful note, but it’s a pure cliffhanger. While effective, consider adding a single sentence hinting at immediate consequence – a sound, a shadow, a shift in the environment – to create a more immediate sense of threat and propel the narrative forward.

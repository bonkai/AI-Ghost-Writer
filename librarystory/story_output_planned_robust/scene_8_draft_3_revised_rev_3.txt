## Draft 3: The Stone Remembers

The alcove smelled of aged paper and dust motes danced in the filtered light. Seris ran a thumb over the worn leather cover, feeling the subtle give of the binding. She focused not on *finding* a message, but on *recalling* the sensation of the serpent – cool stone against her palm, damp earth, echoing silence.

The page wavered, not with distortion, but a blurring of ink, as if heat rose from the parchment. The blurring resolved into a delicate highlighting of existing words, a faint gold sheen.

*“The stone remembers the coil.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. The words weren’t new; they were woven into the text, patiently waiting. It felt less like receiving a message and more like uncovering a hidden secret.

She scanned the surrounding pages. Nothing. The illumination clung only to that single page, triggered by the resonance of the memory, the echo of the serpent’s form.

“Interesting,” she murmured, more to herself than anyone else.

She turned to another page, consciously summoning the image of the serpent, but didn’t *picture* it. She allowed the *feeling* of it to wash over her – weight, texture, stillness – letting the memory permeate her senses.

A different section of the page subtly illuminated.

*“Where the sun sleeps, the path begins.”*

This wasn’t reading. It was a conversation, a slow unveiling of lore.

She glanced up. Hemlock was at his post, seemingly engrossed, but his head tilted as if listening. A flicker crossed his face – a tightening of his jaw, a narrowing of his eyes – then smoothed over.

She needed to be careful. She didn’t *recall* the serpent, but simply *allowed* the feeling to permeate her, a passive recipient of its energy.

The page blurred again, then resolved. Instead of words, a faint, translucent image appeared – a winding path towards a shadowed peak, a mountain silhouetted against a setting sun. The image flickered.

She felt a warmth on her fingertips, an echo of the path. 

“Finding what you seek, Initiate?”

Seris startled, quickly closing the book. Hemlock stood a few feet away, his expression unreadable. He hadn’t approached silently. His movements were fluid, controlled, as if he’d simply *been* there.

“Just… researching,” she said, forcing a casual tone. “The medicinal properties of fungi.”

“A fascinating field,” Hemlock said, his gaze lingering on the closed book. “Though some knowledge is best left undisturbed.” His voice was smooth, with an edge.

A prickle of unease ran down her spine. “I… I’m simply expanding my understanding.”

“Indeed.” He paused, his eyes sweeping the alcove, taking in the arrangement of books, the light. “The past has a way of reaching out. Sometimes, it’s best not to answer its call.” It wasn’t a question, but a statement.

“I don’t understand.”

Hemlock offered a small, enigmatic smile. “Perhaps you will. In time.” He turned back to his post, but Seris could feel his gaze on her back. He hadn’t resumed reading. He was watching.

She opened the book again, her fingers trembling. The page remained blank. The image of the mountain was gone, but the warmth lingered, and a shiver traced her spine. She wouldn't let him see her fear. She pressed her lips together, a silent vow, and met Hemlock’s unseen gaze with a defiant lift of her chin.

The roar of the crowd pressed in on Kaelen, a dull throb even through the headset. Onscreen, ‘Nyxborn,’ a wraith in obsidian, moved as a blur against a chaotic battlefield. Three opponents remained in the Ascension Gauntlet. Kaelen didn’t see enemies, but shifting probabilities, predicting each move before it happened. 

Fingers danced across the keyboard – not playing, but *commanding*. A blink, a leap defying gravity, spectral blades cascading – Nyxborn flowed through the digital chaos, leaving trails of dissolving code. Dust motes swirled in the dim light of his room, illuminated by the screen’s glow. Empty energy drink cans littered his desk, a half-eaten sandwich molding on the edge.

“He’s a goddamn machine,” a commentator’s voice boomed, lost in the frenzy.

Kaelen barely heard it. Years spent honing this skill had cost him more than sleep. A faint tremor in his left hand, quickly suppressed, went unnoticed. He wasn’t after glory, but understanding – unraveling the game’s logic, *becoming* the code.

Two opponents fell. Only ‘Grimfang’ remained.

Grimfang unleashed a desperate assault – explosions, a monstrous beast. Kaelen anticipated it, weaving through the chaos. He saw the opening – a flicker of vulnerability.

He initiated ‘Void Shift’ – teleportation, existing outside reality. The screen flashed white. He reappeared behind Grimfang, blade poised.

But a pressure built behind his eyes. The room swam at the periphery of his vision. A sharp ache pulsed in his chest, distinct from the game’s adrenaline. He tried to focus, to ignore it.

The screen flickered. He glanced down, expecting the keyboard. Instead, a spreading darkness clouded his vision. The ache intensified, a burning pressure now. 

The roar of the crowd warped, distant and distorted. A single word echoed: “Victory!”

Then, silence. And a sickening lurch, not within the game, but a falling sensation into an absolute void.

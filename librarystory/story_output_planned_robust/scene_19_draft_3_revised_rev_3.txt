The library had emptied for the night. Pools of warm light from a handful of lamps barely touched the towering shelves, leaving the upper reaches lost in shadow. Seris sat at their worktable, not sketching, not reading, but acutely aware of Valerius’s scrutiny – a cold pressure at the edge of her awareness. A nearby candle flickered, its flame momentarily distorted.

A subtle disturbance – not a sound, but a shifting of dust motes in a lamplight beam – drew her eye. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed, and a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table. The ink hadn’t fully dried, carrying a faint scent of parchment and something older. It was a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, not as a cartographer would render it, but as a *being* – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power.

Seris traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. *Beautiful,* she thought, and the impression intensified, resonating with shared appreciation. A warmth bloomed on her skin, radiating from the sketch.

The page blurred, the charcoal writhing. *It remembers,* a thought formed, clear and distinct, woven into her consciousness. Not a response, but an offering. She traced the lines again, a question forming on her lips. “What do you mean?” she breathed, barely a whisper.

*The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth, Seris. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.*

“Lost?” she echoed, questioning, her fingers tightening on the edge of the table.

Silence stretched, broken only by the lamps. She felt his focus, sifting through forgotten lore, and a fragmented image flickered in her mind – a vast, crumbling library, then a single, brightly lit doorway. *Remembrance.*

“Remembrance?” she repeated aloud, the sound startling in the quiet library. The image faded, leaving a lingering warmth on her skin.

*The Order didn’t just forbid magic, Seris. They forbade remembering. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories.* 

A shiver crawled down her spine. This wasn’t about a rare healing plant. This was far older, far more dangerous.

“Valerius…” she breathed, the name heavy on her tongue. “He knows, doesn’t he? That we’re looking for more than just a flower?”

A book slammed shut on a nearby shelf, the sound echoing sharply. His frustration was palpable, controlled. *He suspects. He’s a guardian of the forgetting, Seris. He’ll do anything to protect it.*

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

A quiet strength settled over her mind. *Always.*

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch. It wasn’t a drawing, but a warning. A challenge. Then, a delicate fragrance – crimson, perfectly formed – momentarily filled the air, superimposed over the sketch. Not an image *sent*, but a shared perception. They weren’t alone. But as the fragrance faded, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. The scent of dust and old paper was suddenly…metallic. And from the darkness, a distinct *click* – the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing.

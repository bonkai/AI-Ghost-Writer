*   **Repetitive Questioning:** The protagonist repeatedly asks "Where am I?" and "Between what?". While understandable given the situation, it feels a little static. Consider varying the phrasing or showing his disorientation through internal monologue/attempts to *do* something rather than just asking.
*   **Lack of Character Voice:** The protagonist's projections ("Hello?", "Where am I?", "Between what?") are very neutral. Even in a disoriented state, some trace of his personality should come through. Is he panicked? Curious? Defiant? A little more flavor would help.
*   **The "Entity's" Voice:** The entity’s responses (“Aware,” “Between,” “Between worlds”) are *too* minimalist. While the intent is to convey ancientness and detachment, it risks feeling like a lazy writing shortcut. Even a subtle shift in the *feeling* accompanying each word could add depth. For example, "Aware..." with a sensation of immense patience, or "Between..." delivered with a feeling of vastness.
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The description of the library – the scent of aged paper, the feeling of data – is strong, but it’s *all* delivered through the protagonist’s sensory experience. This is fine, but be careful it doesn’t become a lengthy exposition dump disguised as internal sensation.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The entity's amusement feels a little flatly stated. Instead of *telling* us it feels amused, *show* it through the sensation it projects. A slight distortion of his awareness? A momentary feeling of being toyed with?
*   **Efficiency:** The initial attempts to "lift a hand" and "shout" are quickly abandoned. While establishing his lack of a body is important, these actions feel a bit perfunctory. Perhaps streamline this section, focusing immediately on the awareness of being untethered.

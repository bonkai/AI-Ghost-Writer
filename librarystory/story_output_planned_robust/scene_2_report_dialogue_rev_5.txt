*   **Repetitive phrasing:** The constant reiteration of how each projection/question *feels* to the character ("leaving him feeling… drained, hollow," "leaving him feeling… depleted") becomes tiresome and tells rather than shows. We *understand* he’s losing energy; demonstrate it through changes in the quality of his projections or his ability to perceive the environment.
*   **Lack of Character Voice:** While the situation is unusual, the character's questions ("Where am I?", "Between what?", "Between worlds?") are very… direct. Given the context of being disconnected from a VR rig and existing as pure awareness, a more fragmented, desperate, or even philosophical line of questioning might feel more authentic. Consider internal monologue bleeding into these projections.
*   **"Between" as a Response:** The repeated use of "Between" feels like a stalling tactic from the entity, but it doesn’t reveal much about *its* character. Is it deliberately obtuse? Playful? Indifferent? Injecting a *hint* of personality into these responses would make the entity more compelling.
*   **Over-Reliance on Sensory Data as Information:** While the descriptions of sensation are evocative, the constant "not a smell, but a rush of data" construction feels clunky. Can some of this information be *implied* through his reactions or the changing environment?
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The entity's "savoring" of his fears is a good start, but it could be pushed further. Instead of stating it directly, show it through a subtle shift in the environment, a flicker of static, or a momentary intensification of the sensation.
*   **Efficiency:** The line "A glitch? A trap?" feels unnecessary. The character's desperation and the unsettling nature of the library already imply his suspicion.

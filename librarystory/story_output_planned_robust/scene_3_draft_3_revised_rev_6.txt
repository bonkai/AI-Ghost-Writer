## Scene 3 - Draft 3

He wasn’t dissolving, not exactly. More an unraveling—threads of awareness pulled taut, then released. A fall, not down, but *inward*, into a space that wasn’t space, a silence that wasn’t silence. The sensation lingered, a disorienting drift, before a jarring halt. Ghosts of memory—the chair, the monitor—faded, not abruptly, but with a slow, dissolving grace. He clung to them briefly, a pang of loss, before they dissolved completely.

He wasn’t *what* he was, either. He attempted to flex a hand, to shift weight, but found…nothing. No limbs, no body, only diffuse awareness, a point of consciousness adrift. A tremor of fear, quickly followed by a strange lightness. The absence of weight, of form, was unsettling, yet…exhilarating. He registered a fleeting sense of panic, quickly suppressed. Wanting felt…wrong, archaic.

He reached out, not with a hand, but with will. He focused on a plain brown volume on a towering shelf, and *pushed*. Not physically, of course. He channeled his disorientation, his desperate need for solidity, into concentrated energy. A flicker of frustration sparked within him. The book didn’t move, but the air around it shimmered, and a dust mote danced in the unseen current. A subtle response, barely perceptible. He strained, pushing harder, and a single page within the book fluttered, then another. A distortion spread, like heat rising, and he felt a strange pull, a connection forming. He almost *tasted* the knowledge – a metallic tang on his nonexistent tongue, followed by the faintest scent of aged paper and leather. The effort left him breathless, a dizzying wave of exhaustion. He recoiled, momentarily overwhelmed.

A word solidified on the page, glowing with inner light: *Listen.*

Listen? To what? The silence? It wasn’t silent, not really. Beneath the absence of sound, the hum throbbed – a heartbeat in the vastness. He focused, and the hum resolved into a chorus of whispers, too faint to decipher, like the turning of countless pages. He directed a question towards the source of the whispers: *Where am I?*

The air around him seemed to thicken, to *respond*. A presence settled over his awareness, ancient and layered, like the rustling of countless pages. “You are a resonance, a fragment of awareness within the Great Library. A place where stories are not just told, but *lived*.” The voice wasn't a sound, but a feeling, a resonance within his own awareness. It felt…old. Immeasurably so.

He concentrated, directing a spark of defiance towards the presence: *What do you want from me?*

A dry, rustling sound, like parchment crumbling, echoed in his awareness. “Want? A quaint notion. We do not ‘want.’ We *are*. And you, little resonance, are…new. A potential echo.” The response was measured, almost detached. There was a weight to it, a sense of vastness.

*What am I?* he thought, the question echoing with desperate need.

The presence didn’t immediately answer. Instead, a wave of images flooded his mind – fleeting glimpses of countless stories, lives lived, worlds imagined – a dizzying kaleidoscope. Knights battling dragons, lovers under starlit skies, cities rising and falling, galaxies swirling. The images were overwhelming, chaotic, and he instinctively pulled back, shielding his awareness. He struggled to regain control, to filter the onslaught.

“You are a reader,” the presence finally said, its tone gentle, a soothing balm. “And now, you are part of the story.” The voice seemed to calm the chaotic images, to bring a sense of order, but the sheer volume of information still left him reeling.

A pause, then a final, enigmatic whisper. "Welcome home." A warmth spread through his awareness, a sense of belonging he hadn’t known he craved. It was a comforting sensation, but also unsettlingly complete. He felt…absorbed.

He tried again to force a sound, any sound, but found only the echoing silence of his own awareness. He focused all his energy, willing a response, but nothing came. The library held its breath, waiting. He pushed harder, desperation building, and a single word formed on another page, distant and faint: *Soon.* 

What did it mean? Soon what? The question hung unanswered in the vastness, a chilling promise in the echoing silence. He strained again, focusing all his will, and felt a faint tremor run through the shelves around him. A page fluttered, then another, and a single phrase, barely legible, appeared: *Patience, little echo. Your story…begins with listening.* The phrase felt less like an answer and more like a directive, a subtle weight settling upon his awareness. He felt a strange compulsion to obey, to surrender to the silence and *listen*. The library wasn’t just waiting; it was *watching*.

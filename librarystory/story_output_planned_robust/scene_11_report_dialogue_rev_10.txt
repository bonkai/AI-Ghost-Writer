*   **“What is it? You’re pale.”** – This line feels very…observational. It's functional, but lacks depth. Elara is clearly concerned, but the dialogue doesn't *show* that concern beyond stating the obvious. Consider adding a touch of urgency or a more personal question, reflecting their relationship. Perhaps, “Hey! You okay? You’re scaring me.” or “What’s happening? You’ve gone completely white.”
*   **“I… I built it.”** – This is a significant reveal, but the delivery is a little weak. The "I...I" stutter feels more like hesitation than overwhelmed realization. Consider a more impactful phrasing, like, "It's… mine. I built this." or even just a stunned, breathy, "I… remember building it."
*   **“Instructions. A framework. And I remember…”** – This feels like *telling* rather than *showing*. The fragments are described as being overwhelming, but the dialogue itself is quite flat. Consider letting the emotion bleed into the phrasing. Perhaps, “Instructions… a framework… it’s all… coming back.” (fragmented delivery mirroring the fragmented memories).
*   **“…laying the first stone.”** – Again, a powerful line, but it lacks impact. The pause is noted in the stage direction, but the line itself feels a bit… declarative. Consider a more visceral delivery. Perhaps, “The first stone… I… I remember laying it.” (emphasizing the personal connection and the resurfacing memory).
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is functional in conveying information, but it consistently *tells* us how the character feels rather than *showing* it through their speech. Consider how the character's disorientation and overwhelming memories would *affect* their speech patterns.

## Scene 16 - Draft 1

The air in the Atheneum felt…different. Not colder, not thicker, but *resonant*. Seris traced the faded gold lettering on a particularly ancient tome – *De Origine Stellarum* – her fingers tingling. Rhys was meticulously cataloging a collection of crumbling scrolls detailing herbal remedies, his usual meticulousness amplified, as if sensing the shift. 

They’d successfully steered Valerius towards the herbal texts, offering him a trail of ‘promising leads’ about terrestrial causes for the blight. He’d taken the bait, disappearing into the stacks with a satisfied nod, but the reprieve felt fragile. Kaelen’s silence was the most unsettling part. He hadn’t offered a single thought, a single nudge, since Valerius had withdrawn.

Suddenly, a wave of disorientation washed over Seris. The library seemed to blur at the edges, the scent of parchment twisting into something acrid, metallic. A pressure built behind her eyes, and a low hum vibrated through her skull.

“Seris?” Rhys’s voice sounded distant, muffled. He was reaching for her, concern etched on his face.

Before she could respond, a cascade of images flooded her mind. Not her own memories, but *visions*. A vast, star-strewn sky, unlike any she’d ever seen. Colossal structures of obsidian and silver, reaching towards the heavens. Figures cloaked in shadow, chanting in a language that resonated with a primal power. And then, a blinding flash of light, followed by…fracture. The structures crumbling, the sky tearing apart, and a wave of darkness consuming everything.

She gasped, stumbling back, her hand flying to her head. The visions subsided, leaving her breathless and disoriented. 

“Seris! What’s happening?” Rhys gripped her arm, steadying her.

Kaelen’s voice, when it finally came, was fragmented, strained. *“…the Nexus…it’s…responding…a resonance…ancient…before the Sundering…”*

The library around them seemed to shimmer, the shelves momentarily distorting like reflections in water. Seris felt a pull, a dizzying sensation of being drawn backwards through time.

“Kaelen? What is this?” she managed to whisper, her voice trembling.

*“…a memory…not mine…but imprinted…on the Nexus…a place…where the stars…were bound…before…”* The thoughts were coming in broken fragments, as if Kaelen himself was struggling to maintain coherence.

The visions intensified. She saw a council of beings, radiating power, manipulating constellations like pieces on a game board. They were building something, a network of energy, connecting the stars. Then, a betrayal. A figure cloaked in darkness, unleashing a cataclysmic force that shattered the network, unleashing the Sundering – the event that had reshaped Eldoria centuries ago.

“The Sundering…” Seris breathed, the realization dawning on her. “This isn’t just about the blight. It’s about what *caused* the Sundering.”

*“…the Nexus…it’s a key…a place of power…but also…a vulnerability…they tried to contain it…to seal it…but the seal is weakening…”* Kaelen’s voice was fading, becoming increasingly distant.

Suddenly, a specific image crystallized within the chaos. A symbol – a complex arrangement of interlocking stars, etched onto a dark, obsidian surface. It pulsed with an inner light, radiating a power that made Seris’s skin crawl.

“That symbol…” she murmured, her eyes fixed on the mental image. “I’ve seen it before…in the old maps…the ones detailing the Firepeak Mountains…”

Rhys, witnessing her distress, was clearly concerned. “Seris, you’re pale. What’s going on? What symbol?”

Before Seris could answer, a wave of intense pain lanced through her head. She cried out, collapsing to her knees. The library spun around her, the shelves blurring into a kaleidoscope of colors. 

*“…danger…they know…they’re coming…the Guardians…they protect the Nexus…but they’re weakened…you must find it…before they do…”* Kaelen’s voice was now a mere whisper, a fading echo in the storm raging within her mind.

Then, silence. Complete, utter silence. The visions vanished, the pain subsided, leaving her weak and trembling.

Rhys was kneeling beside her, his face etched with worry. “Seris! What happened? Are you alright?”

She looked up at him, her eyes filled with a newfound urgency. “The Firepeak Mountains,” she said, her voice barely a whisper. “We have to go to the Firepeak Mountains. The answer…the key to everything…is hidden there.”

She didn’t know *what* awaited them in those treacherous peaks, but she knew, with a chilling certainty, that their search for a cure for the blight had just become something far, far more dangerous. And that Kaelen, whatever he was, had just revealed a secret that could either save Eldoria…or shatter it completely.

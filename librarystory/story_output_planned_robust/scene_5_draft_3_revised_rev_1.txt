## Scene 5 - Draft 3

The Atheneum smelled of dust and forgotten things—a scent Seris found strangely comforting, yet immediately unsettling. Not the clean dust of neglect, but the rich, layered aroma of centuries pressed between parchment and binding. She ran a gloved hand along a towering bookshelf, the wood cool beneath her fingertips, her gaze already sweeping the chamber, assessing angles, shadows, *exits*. Sunlight, fractured by stained glass depicting long-dead scholars, painted the vast chamber in shifting hues of ruby and sapphire, but failed to penetrate the deeper recesses, creating pockets of oppressive darkness. 

The library was a labyrinth, and Seris felt instantly *observed*. Aisles spiraled into darkness, branching into hidden alcoves and forgotten galleries. Most dismissed it as a crumbling relic, a repository for useless lore. The Iron Church certainly did, deeming its contents heretical and restricting access. Which, of course, was precisely why Seris had sought it out. She wasn't here for grand spells or potent potions, but for information—fragments of the past, whispers of a time before the Church’s iron grip, before magic was branded as sin. Specifically, she needed anything referencing the ‘Veiled Cities’—rumored settlements erased from history, said to possess knowledge the Church desperately wanted to suppress.

A cough echoed from the shadows. Seris’s hand tightened on the hilt of the dagger concealed beneath her cloak, her weight shifting subtly into a combat stance. She scanned the nearest alcove, her gaze lingering on Old Man Hemlock, the Atheneum’s caretaker. Hunched over a table, meticulously cataloging scrolls, he appeared harmless enough. But his stillness was unnerving, and his position offered an unsettlingly comprehensive view of the chamber.

“Looking for something specific, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice rasped, barely louder than the rustling of parchment. He didn’t look up, but Seris felt his gaze – a prickling sensation on her skin – assessing her.

“Just…exploring,” Seris replied, keeping her voice neutral, but her eyes continued to scan the room, cataloging potential threats. “The Church rarely permits access. I’m curious what secrets such a place holds.”

“Secrets have a way of finding those who seek them,” Hemlock said cryptically. “But be warned. Some secrets are best left buried.”

Seris ignored the warning, turning back to the shelves, her movements quick and efficient. She ran her finger along the spines, scanning the faded titles. Most were treatises on theology, philosophy, or obscure alchemical formulas. Useless. The prickling sensation on the back of her neck intensified, and she subtly adjusted her position, placing herself closer to the nearest exit – a shadowed archway leading to a smaller gallery.

She reached for a particularly worn volume, its cover devoid of any title. As her fingers brushed against the aged leather, a faint impression flickered at the edge of her awareness – a sense of immense age, of stone and silence. It wasn’t a sound, but a resonance, a pressure against her mind. 

She froze, her heart pounding. The sensation sharpened, coalescing into a fragmented thought, *not her own*. *“The stones remember…”* It felt less like hearing a voice and more like receiving a memory – cold, ancient, and deeply unsettling.

Seris quickly withdrew her hand, glancing around nervously. Hemlock hadn’t moved, but his eyes, obscured by shadow, seemed to bore into her.

“Did you say something, Initiate?” he asked, his voice a low murmur.

“No,” Seris replied quickly, forcing a smile. “Just admiring the craftsmanship of the bindings.”

She needed to leave. This place… it wasn’t just old. It was *alive*, humming with an energy she couldn’t comprehend. And she had the unsettling feeling that whatever secrets the Atheneum held, they weren’t going to be given up easily. She turned to leave, but paused, noticing a small, unassuming volume tucked away on a lower shelf. Its cover was blank, but a single, silver clasp held it closed. 

Something about it called to her. A sense of familiarity, a pull she couldn’t ignore. Hesitantly, she reached for it, her fingers trembling slightly. As she touched the clasp, the impression flooded her mind again, clearer this time, sharper, accompanied by a fleeting image of a coiled serpent.

*“The path is hidden. Seek the serpent’s eye.”* 

Seris snatched her hand back as if burned. Her breath hitched. This wasn't a simple echo of the past. This was… *intentional*. She glanced at Hemlock, her eyes narrowed. He hadn't moved, hadn't reacted. But the shadow around his eyes seemed to deepen. She had the distinct impression he'd been *expecting* this. 

She tucked the image of the serpent into the back of her mind. The Atheneum wasn't just a library. It was a guardian. And she had just been given a task.

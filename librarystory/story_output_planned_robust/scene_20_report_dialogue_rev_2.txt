*   **Repetitive phrasing:** The word "buried" is used *a lot*. While thematically appropriate, it becomes noticeable and a bit heavy-handed. Consider varying the language – “hidden,” “suppressed,” “lost,” “concealed” could offer some relief.
*   **Hemlock's exposition:** While necessary to reveal the backstory, a lot of Hemlock’s dialogue feels like he's *telling* rather than *showing*. Specifically, the long explanations of what the Order did. Consider breaking this up with Seris asking more pointed questions that *force* Hemlock to reveal information in smaller, more digestible pieces.
*   **Seris’s reactions:** Seris is largely reactive. While understandable given the situation, giving her more agency in the conversation—asking *why* the Order feared memories, for example—would make her feel more proactive and less like a passive listener.
*   **Subtext opportunity (Sunstone Bloom):** Hemlock reveals the Bloom is a key/conduit *very* directly. This feels a little flat. Perhaps he could react to *seeing* it, a flicker of recognition or fear in his eyes, prompting Seris to ask what he knows about it. This creates mystery and allows for a more organic reveal.
*   **Hemlock’s fear:** Hemlock is *constantly* looking towards the tunnel entrance and expressing fear. While consistent, it becomes predictable. Varying his reactions – a sudden silence, a hand trembling, a change in his vocal tone – would make it more impactful.
*   **"Erodes it" vs. "Suppresses":** The correction from "suppresses" to "erodes" is good, but a little clunky. Perhaps Seris could say something like, “So they don’t just hide knowledge, they…diminish the ability to *have* knowledge?” This makes the nuance clearer and feels more natural.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** There’s a missed opportunity to reveal more about Hemlock through *how* he speaks. Is he bitter? Resigned? Still clinging to hope? Injecting more emotional coloring into his dialogue would make him a more compelling character.

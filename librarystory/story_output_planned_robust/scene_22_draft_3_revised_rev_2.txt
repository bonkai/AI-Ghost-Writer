## Draft 3

Seris closed her eyes, fear stealing her breath. She pressed her palms against the cool stone, grounding herself. They weren’t just searching for lore anymore. They’d stumbled into a conspiracy reaching the highest echelons of power, and time was running out. A suffocating dread settled over her. “We need to be careful,” she whispered, her voice tight. “Very careful.”

Rhys nodded, jaw clenched, scanning the shadowed alcove as if expecting observers. “Careful won’t be enough. We need to understand what they fear. What *is* this ‘dangerous knowledge’?”

“Kaelen,” Seris said, focusing on their mental connection. “Anything more about why the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? Its content?”

Pages turned rapidly, the air crackling with energy. Dust motes danced in the light. Finally, the pages stilled, revealing a fragmented passage in archaic script.

*“The Gardener cultivated not merely flora, but memoria – the very fabric of recollection. They understood Eldoria’s history wasn’t a singular truth, but a tapestry woven from countless perspectives, altered and pruned by those in power. They preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…the echoes of worlds that might have been.”*

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. “Lost narratives? Alternate histories?”

“So, they weren’t just preserving facts,” Rhys mused, running a hand through his hair. “They were preserving *possibilities*.”

*“The Order believed a unified history was essential for order,”* Kaelen’s voice resonated in her mind, laced with urgency. *“They feared acknowledging multiple realities would unravel Eldorian society – ‘the fracturing of the Weave.’”*

“The Weave?” Seris repeated, a chill running down her spine. “What does that mean?”

A new page displayed a complex diagram – a swirling vortex of interconnected lines and symbols. *“The Weave is Eldoria’s collective consciousness, the shared belief system binding the realm. The Gardener’s knowledge threatened to tear holes in it, unleashing chaos.”*

Rhys leaned closer, studying the diagram. “So, they weren’t just suppressing information, they were suppressing…ideas? Different ways of seeing the world?”

“It seems so,” Seris said, her voice barely a whisper. A memory surfaced – Valerius’s veiled warnings, his subtle steering away from the Bloom. It wasn’t about landslides. It was about control. Betrayal tightened her chest. 

*“The Order still exists, operating from the shadows,”* Kaelen’s voice was strained. *“They’ve infiltrated every level of Eldorian society, manipulating events. Valerius is one of their most trusted agents.”*

Seris’s blood ran cold. Valerius. The man who’d seemed so reasonable, so concerned. She’d trusted him, confided in him. A bitter laugh escaped her lips. “He knew we were getting close,” she breathed, anger and betrayal hardening her voice. “He let us dig, just enough to understand the danger…to lead us into a trap.”

Rhys met her gaze, grim and resolute. “We need to be smarter. Understand what they’re protecting, and why. What *is* so dangerous about these ‘lost narratives’?” He turned back to the diagram, tracing a line with his finger. 

A final page turned, revealing a haunting sentence in faded ink. *“The Gardener’s greatest secret was not what was lost, but what was deliberately forgotten.”*

Seris stared at the words, dread washing over her. Deliberately forgotten. What had been erased from Eldorian history? What truths lay buried beneath the narrative of the Founding Kings and the gods?

“Kaelen,” she said, her voice trembling. “Anything more? About what was *deliberately forgotten*?”

Silence stretched, agonizingly long. Then, a single, chilling whisper echoed in her mind.

*“The Gardener remembered a time before Eldoria…before the Veil fell.”*

Seris gasped, stumbling back a step. Rhys caught her arm, steadying her. "Before Eldoria? What does that even mean?"

Kaelen didn't respond immediately. The silence felt heavier now, pregnant with a forgotten history. Finally, a faint echo resonated in Seris's mind, barely a whisper.

*“A time when Eldoria…wasn’t alone.”*

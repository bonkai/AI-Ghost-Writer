Seris closed her eyes, a cold dread settling over her. She pressed her palms against the cool stone, grounding herself. This wasn’t merely about lost lore. They’d stumbled into a conspiracy reaching the highest echelons of power, and time felt desperately short. Her breath quickened, and her grip on the stone tightened, a visible tremor running through her.

Rhys scanned the shadowed alcove, jaw clenched, running a hand through his hair in frustrated energy. “What are they so afraid of? What *is* this ‘dangerous knowledge’?”

“Kaelen,” Seris focused on their mental connection, “anything more about why the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? Its content?”

Pages turned rapidly, the air crackling with energy. Dust motes danced in the light. Finally, the pages stilled, revealing a fragmented passage in archaic script.

*“The Gardener cultivated not merely flora, but memoria – the very fabric of recollection. They understood Eldoria’s history wasn’t singular, but a tapestry of possibilities. They preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…the echoes of worlds that might have been.”*

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. Confusion clouded her features, her brow furrowing as she considered the implications. 

“Lost narratives? Alternate histories?” Rhys mused, pacing a short circle. “So, they weren’t just preserving facts, they were preserving *possibilities*.”

*“The Order believed a single, unified history was necessary for stability,”* Kaelen projected, the information delivered as context, not explanation. *“They feared the chaos of conflicting truths.”*

Seris’s gaze met Rhys’s, a shared understanding passing between them. The implications hung heavy in the silence.

“Kaelen,” Seris prompted, “what else?”

*“The Gardener recorded…before. Before Eldoria.”* The voice was faint, strained. *“A time of connection.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. “Connection to what?”

*“Other realms…other beings…”*

Pages continued to turn, revealing a single, faded illustration – a swirling vortex of iridescent light, filled with impossible structures.

“Show me,” Seris breathed, her voice barely a whisper.

The illustration flared with light, and a rush of images flooded Seris’s mind. She stumbled, bracing herself against Rhys, her knuckles white as she gripped his arm.

*“The Veil…it wasn’t a barrier erected to keep threats *out*…”* Kaelen’s voice was fragmented, distant. *“It was built to contain something…to sever a connection.”*

The image sharpened – a breathtaking landscape of impossible geometry and beings of pure light. Then, a wave of darkness crashed over Seris, and she cried out, clutching her head. Rhys held her tighter, concern etched on his face. "Seris! What is it? What's happening?"

She shook her head, trying to clear the images. “I…I saw something. A world…before. Before Eldoria. Kaelen showed me…” Realization dawned, and her eyes widened. "The Veil…it wasn't about keeping things *out*…it was about keeping something *in*…or keeping us from something *else*."

The chilling weight of the forgotten past settled upon them, heavier than ever. They weren’t just uncovering a conspiracy; they were peeling back the layers of a deliberately obscured history, a history that threatened to shatter everything they thought they knew about their world. Rhys didn’t need to ask what they should do next; his determined gaze met Seris’s, a silent agreement passing between them. He tightened his grip on her arm, a silent promise of support.

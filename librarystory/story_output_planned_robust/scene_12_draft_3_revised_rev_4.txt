The single oil lamp sputtered and died, plunging the archive into darkness. The air grew heavy, stagnant. A draft, colder than stone, swept through the chamber, carrying the scent of dust and something metallic, faintly floral. Seris instinctively reached for her dagger.

“We’re not alone,” she whispered.

Archon Valerius coalesced from the shadows, not entering, but *becoming* visible. Two heavily armored Guardians materialized beside him, their movements unsettlingly silent. They extended from him, extensions of his will.

“Intriguing discoveries, Initiate.” Valerius glided closer, pausing before the open tome. He didn’t touch it, but the air wavered around the aged parchment, the ink blurring slightly. He gestured to an illustration of the Aethel. “What do you see?”

“A civilization lost to time. Erased.” Seris’s fingers tightened on the dagger hilt. “Or so the histories claim.”

Valerius tilted his head, his gaze unwavering. “Erased? Or… repurposed?” He didn't wait for a response, instead tracing a finger across the illustration. “Consider the loom. Threads can be cut, woven into something new.”

A prickling sensation rose on the back of Seris’s neck. The metallic scent intensified. She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius, a question forming on her lips. “What does that mean?”

Valerius’s smile widened, a predator’s acknowledgment. “A wise decision to ask questions, Initiate. Some doors are best left unopened.” He gestured to a Guardian. “Escort the Initiate back to her chambers. She needs rest.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. Seris noticed the polished steel of its armor wasn’t reflecting the darkness, but *absorbing* it. She barely registered the movement, her attention fixed on Valerius. 

He stood before the tome, his gaze fixed on the illustration, but he wasn’t looking *at* the image. He was looking *through* it. In that moment, Seris understood. The Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound. Valerius held the threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer. A cold certainty settled over her. She had stumbled onto something dangerous, something far older and more powerful than she could have imagined. And Valerius would do anything to keep it hidden. She didn’t bother signaling her departure. She already knew it wouldn't be permitted.

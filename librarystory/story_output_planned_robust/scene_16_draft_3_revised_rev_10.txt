The library air thickened, pressing against Seris’s skin. Dust motes danced in the light, each a tiny world poised to shatter. A tremor ran through the stone, vibrating up through her boots, into her teeth. She gripped a bookshelf, the ancient wood humming with discordant energy.

Rhys, studying a faded map, glanced up, his brow furrowing. He didn’t reach for his dagger, but subtly shifted his pack. His gaze met hers—a silent question laced with concern.

The library *shifted*. Lines blurred at the edge of her vision, stretched, then snapped back. The scent of old parchment twisted with a metallic tang – ozone and blood. A pressure descended, the feeling of a vast network straining beneath her feet.

A discordant note fractured the harmony. Luminous threads of energy flickered, dimmed. A thread snapped. Pain lanced through Seris’s chest. Static raised the hairs on her arms. She stumbled, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue, and pressed a hand to her temple.

Rhys caught her, his grip firm. “Seris?”

Kaelen’s consciousness brushed against hers—a raw resonance, the weight of ancient power. An image flashed—a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian, pulsing, *cracking*. She gasped, tightening her grip on Rhys’s arm.

He felt the tremor run through her, his grip tightening. “What do you see?”

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts, the words tasting like ash. Kaelen’s panic surged. The image sharpened—obsidian, stars, cracks widening. *Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…* A cold wind whipped across her skin, the smell of sulfur stinging her nostrils. A resonant thrumming vibrated through her bones. She squeezed her eyes shut, knuckles white against Rhys’s jerkin.

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus, and slowly opened her eyes. She looked at Rhys, then away, gathering her thoughts. The vision felt…imprinted.

Rhys waited, his expression unreadable, his hand firmly supporting her. He didn’t press, didn’t ask again.

“Firestone Mountains?” he asked, his voice tight.

“The source,” she said, her voice strained. “Something contained within. Ancient. Terrible. The seal…failing.” She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “Worst there.”

“We have to go,” she said, “before it breaks free.”

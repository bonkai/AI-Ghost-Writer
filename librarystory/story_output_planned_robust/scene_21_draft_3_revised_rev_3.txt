## Scene 21 - Draft 3

The workshop smelled of dried herbs and mountain dust, a familiar comfort now tainted with something metallic. A chill lingered after Inquisitor Marius departed.

Kaelen breathed, “He’s probing.” Seris’s hand instinctively moved to the small, worn pouch containing Elara’s portrait, her fingers tracing the faded image – a grounding touch, a reminder of everything at stake.

Rhys shifted, his hand near the hilt of his climbing axe, but his gaze remained fixed on the closed door. “The Bloom’s properties are well-documented, Inquisitor. Significant potential for healing.” The practiced neutrality felt brittle, a thin shield.

Marius’s gaze flickered to Rhys, then back to Seris. “Healing? Curious, Scholar. A reputation like yours dedicated to such a commonplace goal.” The words were silken, edged with condescension.

Seris clenched her jaw, the muscle jumping. He was circling, testing her defenses. “The pursuit of knowledge is never commonplace, Inquisitor.” She forced steadiness into her voice, suppressing the urge to smooth her skirt.

“Indeed.” Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging details. “Tell me, Scholar. What are you *truly* seeking on Firepeak Mountain? Knowledge…or something else?”

Rhys’s hand tightened on his axe, the steel gleaming faintly. *He’s afraid,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in Seris’s mind. *Afraid of what you might find, and what that means for the Order.* The realization settled like a stone. This wasn't routine.

Seris met Marius’s gaze, a cold determination hardening within her. She wouldn't be intimidated. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.” A flicker of defiance entered her eyes, carefully controlled.

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile. “A convenient answer. But I suspect you aren’t being entirely forthcoming.” He lowered his voice, a dangerous edge creeping in. “The Bloom…it remembers. Echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep.”

He stepped back, his gaze sweeping over their workspace, lingering on a dried specimen of rare mountain moss. “I will be watching you. Both of you.”

Rhys’s knuckles whitened on his axe, but he didn’t raise it. Seris reached for the pouch, her fingers trembling slightly as she traced the faded image. 

“We have no secrets, Inquisitor.” She said the words with deliberate conviction, projecting an unwavering confidence she barely felt.

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing her with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has secrets, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to sacrifice to keep them buried?” He offered a curt nod, then swept out, leaving a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being watched.

Silence descended. Rhys began to sharpen his axe, the rasp a small rebellion against the tension, but his gaze remained fixed on the door. Seris stared at the closed door, her heart pounding. He hadn't found secrets…yet. But she knew, with chilling certainty, that he would return. And when he did, he wouldn’t seek information. He would seek control. She clutched the pouch, the worn fabric a small comfort. She wouldn’t let him.

*   **Hemlock's Exposition:** While the information Hemlock delivers is crucial, a lot of it feels like he's *telling* rather than *revealing*. Consider breaking up his longer speeches with Seris's questions or reactions. Even simple interjections ("Cities of crystal?") would add dynamism and allow for more subtext. Right now, it feels like a monologue with brief pauses.
*   **Seris's Lack of Probing:** Seris is remarkably passive for someone actively seeking answers. She receives a lot of heavy information and mostly *reacts* with internal thoughts or a tightening grip on her pack. Give her more direct questions. What specifically does she want to know about the "dangerous memories"? What does she think of Hemlock's exile?
*   **"We remember" - Cliché:** The final exchange, while emotionally resonant, leans heavily on a well-worn trope. Consider a more unique phrasing that still conveys the same message of resistance.
*   **Hand Contact - Overuse/Underdevelopment:** The hand-reaching/touching feels repetitive and lacks consistent purpose. The first hesitant reach is good – it shows his internal conflict. But the final touch on her pack feels tacked on. Either make the hand contact more meaningful (a firm clasp, a guiding touch) or reduce its frequency.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – The Flower:** The flower is repeatedly mentioned as a “key” or “seed.” Explore the *metaphor* more subtly. Have characters react to the *appearance* of the flower, its scent, or its unique properties, rather than constantly stating its symbolic meaning.
*   **Seris's Internalization vs. Externalization:** Seris has a lot of internal thoughts (“She wasn’t following a legend…”). While internal monologue is fine, consider translating some of those thoughts into dialogue, even if it's just a pointed question or a skeptical remark.

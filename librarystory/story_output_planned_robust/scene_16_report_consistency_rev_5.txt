## Consistency Check: Passed.

After a thorough review of both drafts (Draft 3 and the current draft), I find **no major inconsistencies**. The narrative flows logically, and the character reactions are consistent with the established tone and setting. Here's a breakdown of my assessment:

* **Character Consistency:** Seris and Rhys act in character throughout both drafts. Their concern for each other and their dedication to uncovering the truth remain consistent. Kaelen’s presence as a consciousness/guide also remains consistent in how it communicates through sensation and fragmented imagery.
* **Worldbuilding Consistency:** The established worldbuilding – the network of energy, the ancient power, the Firestone Mountains – is maintained throughout both drafts. The increasing sense of dread and the unraveling of the network’s harmony build logically.
* **Plot Consistency:** The plot progresses smoothly. The initial suspicion about the constellations (in Draft 3) seamlessly transitions into the discovery of the failing seal at the Firestone Mountains in the current draft. The shift in focus feels organic.
* **Sensory Details:** The use of sensory details (metallic tang, discordant energy, icy dread) is consistent and contributes to the overall atmosphere.

**Minor Observations (not inconsistencies, just points for consideration):**

* **Pace:** The current draft is significantly more focused on immediate threat and sensation. Draft 3 had a slower build-up with more emphasis on observation and deduction. This isn't an inconsistency, but a stylistic shift.
* **Kaelen's Communication:** Kaelen's method of communication is consistently fragmented, but the sheer volume of imagery in the current draft is higher. This could be a result of the increasing urgency.



**Overall:** The current draft builds effectively on the foundation laid in Draft 3. The narrative is cohesive, the characters are consistent, and the worldbuilding remains strong.

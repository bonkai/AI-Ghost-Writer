*   **Repetitive Action/Dialogue Tag:** The constant repetition of "Seris tightened her grip on the silver key, her knuckles white" is becoming distracting. It *shows* tension, but after the second instance, it's telling instead of showing. Consider removing at least one instance and letting her actions (a flinch, a change in stance, a quick glance at Rhys) convey the tension.
*   **Kaelen’s Thoughts – Info Dump/Overuse:** While Kaelen’s telepathy is established, the sheer volume of exposition delivered through her thoughts is slowing the scene. The information about the Obsidian Circle and being a conduit is a lot to dump at once. Could some of this be revealed through Valerius's dialogue or actions, or through environmental clues? Consider trimming Kaelen’s explanations to just the *most* critical information at this moment.
*   **Valerius – Predictable Villain:** Valerius’s dialogue is fairly standard villain fare. “Safety of the realm” is a cliché. While functional, it lacks nuance. Consider giving him a more specific motivation, or a quirk in his speech pattern, to make him more memorable.
*   **Rhys – Silent Partner:** Rhys is present, but largely silent. While Seris is the primary speaker, a brief line from him – a question, a warning, a show of support – would strengthen their partnership and prevent him from feeling like a prop.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Valerius):** Valerius’s satisfaction feels stated rather than *shown*. Instead of saying "I expected you sooner," he could make a comment about how predictably Seris followed the clues, or how he *knew* she’d be drawn to the key. This would subtly reveal his manipulation and arrogance.
*   **“What power?” – Direct Question:** Seris asking “What power?” is very direct. Consider having her ask a more probing question, something that hints at her understanding of the key’s potential without revealing everything. For example, “What does this key *unlock*?” or “What have you been keeping hidden?”
*   **Indirect Characterization (Seris/Rhys):** The shared glances between Seris and Rhys are good, but could be more specific. What *exactly* is being communicated? A plan? A silent warning? A shared fear?

## Revised Draft - Incorporating Feedback

Here's a revised draft of the scene, incorporating the suggestions and focusing on deepening the tension, internal stakes, and sensory details. I've aimed for a more immersive experience, showing more than telling, and making Kaelen's presence more subtly felt.

The scent of aged parchment and dust motes hung heavy in the air. Seris traced the delicate veins of the Sunstone Bloom, her charcoal pencil a quiet scratch against the rough paper. She wasn’t *seeing* the flower anymore, but sketching a performance, emphasizing the details that would read as obsession.

*He’s circling,* Kaelen’s thought brushed against her mind, cool and precise as a scalpel. *East Wing, star charts. He’s re-examining the constellations we flagged, lingering on Lyra. He expects us to chase the celestial trail.*

Seris didn’t lift her gaze. Her hand moved with practiced precision, but her focus was fractured, listening for the subtle shift in the library’s rhythm that would signal Valerius’s approach. “Predictable,” she murmured, her voice barely audible. “He believes we’re lost in the stars.”

“This text…it’s deliberately misleading,” Rhys said, frustration tightening his jaw. He ran a hand through his hair, leaving a smudge of charcoal on his temple. The dark mark seemed to accentuate the lines of exhaustion around his eyes. “The proportions are illogical. It’s a tangle of alchemical nonsense.”

*Precisely,* Kaelen affirmed, the thought a quiet hum in her mind. *Let him waste time unraveling a phantom recipe. The author was a known eccentric, a master of obfuscation.*

Seris subtly altered her sketching, shifting from botanical accuracy to emphasizing the flower’s purported medicinal properties – a fever reducer, a nerve tonic, a component in potions to enhance mental clarity. She imagined a desperate healer, seeking a miracle cure.

“The Sunstone Bloom is more myth than reality,” she said, her voice carrying just enough to reach Rhys. “The texts claim it grows only on the highest, most inaccessible peaks of Firepeak Mountain, guarded by…well, the legends are fanciful, to say the least.” She allowed a hint of wistful longing to color her tone.

*Good. Plant the seed. Let him believe we’re chasing a phantom,* Kaelen instructed. *Firepeak Mountain is a trap. Remote, treacherous, and a drain on his resources.*

Rhys caught the cue. He abandoned the problematic recipe and began scanning the surrounding texts, feigning interest in accounts of expeditions to Firepeak Mountain. He deliberately lingered over a passage detailing the mountain’s harsh conditions. “Apparently, the journey is…arduous. Rockslides, blizzards…and, according to some accounts, giant spiders.” He emphasized the last detail with a touch of dramatic flair, a slight widening of his eyes.

A shadow fell across their worktable. Valerius stood before them, his presence radiating a quiet intensity. He hadn’t approached directly, but paused a few feet away, observing them with an unnerving stillness. The air seemed to thicken around him.

“Still pursuing the remedies?” he asked, his voice smooth as polished stone, but with an edge of something unreadable beneath the surface.

“We’ve encountered a…complex text,” Rhys said, carefully avoiding eye contact. “But we’re making progress. We’ve uncovered some intriguing accounts of a flower said to possess remarkable healing properties.” He gestured towards Seris’s sketch, a subtle offering.

Valerius’s gaze followed Rhys’s hand, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. A flicker of something – curiosity, perhaps, but also calculation – crossed his face. He didn’t comment on the sketch itself.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly, his voice a low murmur. “A rare specimen, indeed. Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon – a phenomenon rarely witnessed.”

*He’s testing the depth of our obsession,* Kaelen warned, the thought a sharp prickle against Seris’s awareness. *Don’t overplay it. Subtlety is key.*

Seris met Valerius’s gaze, her expression carefully neutral, a practiced mask of scholarly interest. “We’ve found several accounts of its use in ancient healing practices. We’re hoping to corroborate those claims, to separate fact from folklore.”

“Firepeak Mountain is a dangerous place to search for folklore,” Valerius said, his voice laced with a subtle warning. The scent of his sandalwood cologne drifted towards them, a subtle assertion of power. “Many have ventured into those peaks and never returned. The mountain claims those who underestimate its power.”

“We understand the risks,” Rhys said quickly, but with a forced confidence. “We’ve been meticulously researching the terrain, the weather patterns. We’re prepared for a challenging expedition.”

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, assessing, calculating. He didn’t respond immediately. The silence stretched, thick and suffocating. Then, a slow, deliberate smile touched his lips.

“Perhaps,” he said, his voice dangerously soft. “Perhaps you are. In that case, I wish you good fortune. Though I suspect…the mountain will test your preparations.” He paused, his eyes locking with Seris’s. “It always does.”

*He’s playing with us,* Kaelen’s thought was a cold current. *He knows we’re leading him on. But he wants to see how far we’ll go.*

The workshop smelled of dried herbs and mountain dust, a familiar comfort now laced with something metallic. A chill lingered after Inquisitor Marius departed.

Kaelen breathed, “He’s probing.” Seris’s fingers drifted to the small, worn pouch containing Elara’s portrait, her thumb tracing the faded image. A grounding touch, but her grip tightened almost imperceptibly.

Rhys shifted his weight, his gaze fixed on the closed door. “The Bloom’s properties are well-documented, Inquisitor. Potential for healing is significant.” He paused, glancing at a shelf of carefully labeled specimens, his eyes lingering a beat too long on a row of potent, restricted ingredients.

Marius’s gaze flickered to Rhys, then back to Seris. “Healing? Curious, Scholar. A reputation like yours dedicated to such a commonplace goal.” He tilted his head, a slight, assessing movement, and his gaze swept the room, pausing on a half-dissected flower, its petals arranged with unsettling precision.

Seris smoothed a stray strand of hair, avoiding direct eye contact, but her fingers didn’t quite still. “The pursuit of knowledge is rarely commonplace, Inquisitor.” She kept her voice even, but a muscle ticked in her jaw.

“Indeed.” Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging details. He stopped on a complex arrangement of drying herbs, a faint smile playing on his lips. “Tell me, Scholar. What *truly* draws you to Firepeak Mountain? Knowledge…or something else?”

Rhys’s hand rested near the hilt of his climbing axe, but he didn’t grip it. He simply observed Marius, a subtle tightening around his eyes. *He’s afraid,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in Seris’s mind. *Afraid of what you might find, and what that means for the Order.* The realization settled, heavy and cold, and Rhys’s gaze flickered to the shadowed corner where Seris kept her more…unconventional research.

Seris met Marius’s gaze, a controlled stillness in her expression. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.” A flicker of something unreadable crossed her eyes, quickly masked, but she didn’t break eye contact.

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile. “A convenient answer.” He stepped closer, his voice dropping to a near whisper. “The Bloom…it remembers. Echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep.”

He stepped back, his gaze lingering on a dried specimen of rare mountain moss, then swept over their workspace. “I will be watching you. Both of you.”

Rhys’s knuckles whitened slightly, but he didn’t raise his hand. He began to meticulously clean the blade of his axe, a deliberate, rhythmic action. Seris’s fingers trembled almost imperceptibly as she traced the faded image on the pouch. 

“We have no secrets, Inquisitor.” She said the words with deliberate conviction, projecting an unwavering confidence she barely felt.

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing her with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has secrets, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to sacrifice to keep them buried?” He offered a curt nod, then swept out, leaving a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being watched.

Silence descended. Rhys continued to sharpen his axe, the rasp a small, rhythmic sound against the tension, his gaze fixed on the door. Seris stared at the closed door, her heart pounding a slow, steady rhythm. He hadn't found secrets…yet. But she knew, with chilling certainty, that he would return. And when he did, he wouldn’t seek information. He would seek control. She clutched the pouch, the worn fabric a small, fragile comfort. She wouldn’t let him.

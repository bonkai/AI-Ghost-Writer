*   **Hemlock’s Lack of Direct Engagement:** While his aloofness is clearly intentional for his character, it borders on repetitive. He observes, makes cryptic comments, and *doesn’t* offer assistance. This is established quickly, and continuing it through *every* exchange feels less like characterization and more like a pattern. Consider *one* instance of him offering a small, almost begrudging, piece of practical advice (even if it’s unhelpful) to break the monotony.
*   **Internal Monologue Bleeding into Dialogue:** Lines like "It doesn’t want to be found," feel very much like an internal thought vocalized without a clear reason for sharing it with Hemlock. Given his established character, he wouldn't respond, but the line feels narratively weak as a spoken line. Consider if it *needs* to be said aloud, or if it’s more impactful as internal thought.
*   **Repetitive Physical Reactions:** The constant focus on the character’s physical reactions (tremor, throbbing temple, metallic taste) is becoming distracting. While important to show the strain, it’s happening with almost every attempt. Vary the descriptions or reduce the frequency.
*   **Hemlock’s Final Speech – Potential for Subtext:** His final lines are good, but could be *even* stronger. The “secrets best left undisturbed” is a solid threat/warning. However, consider adding a *personal* element. Does *he* have a reason to want this secret left undisturbed? A subtle hint of self-preservation would add layers.
*   **“Perhaps” overuse:** Hemlock starts multiple sentences with “Perhaps.” It’s becoming a verbal tic. While it fits his cautious nature, it’s noticeable.
*   **Clarity of Stakes:** While the *feeling* of dread is well-conveyed, the *reason* for it remains somewhat vague. Hemlock knows more, but we don't get a strong sense of *what* that knowledge is, beyond a general sense of danger. A *tiny* clue (even a misdirection) could raise the stakes.

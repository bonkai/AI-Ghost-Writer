*   **Lucian's Dialogue - Too Direct:** Lucian's initial line, "What have you concealed?" feels a bit blunt, even for an intimidating figure. It lacks subtlety. Consider something more suggestive, like, "Tell me what troubles you, child," or simply a prolonged, silent observation before speaking. The current line feels like an interrogation rather than an implied threat.
*   **Boy's Silence - Opportunity for Subtext:** The boy *only* offers flowers and then remains silent. While fear is understandable, a small, desperate plea or a single word ("No!") could add emotional weight and show more character. His complete silence feels a little flat.
*   **Valerius' Explanation - Info-Dump:** Valerius’ line, “They weren't merely concealing it, Lucian. They were safeguarding it,” feels like telling, not showing. We *see* they were safeguarding it through their actions. The explanation is unnecessary and slows the scene. Let the resonance of the locket and Valerius's understanding be enough.
*   **Valerius' Orders - Functional, Not Characterizing:** Valerius’ instructions about cataloging the locket are purely functional. Inject a hint of his personality – is he meticulous? Impatient? Does he see the locket as merely an object, or something more? A small detail could enrich the scene.
*   **Lack of Dialogue Variety:** The dialogue is largely interrogative or declarative. Consider adding more internal monologue or brief exchanges that *aren't* directly about the locket, to create a more natural conversational flow.
*   **Kaelen's Absence:** Kaelen is a silent observer, which is fine for his role, but the lack of any internal dialogue *during* the interaction feels like a missed opportunity to establish his perspective and reactions more strongly.

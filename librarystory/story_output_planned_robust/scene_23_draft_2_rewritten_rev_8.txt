## Scene 23

The workshop smelled of oiled steel and dried herbs. A sharp rap at the door broke the quiet. Seris ran a honing stone along a scalpel, the rasp deliberate. Rhys, ostensibly organizing charts, kept glancing at the door, his hand tightening and relaxing around a quill – a subtle shift Seris noted. He hadn’t slept properly in days, dark shadows beneath his eyes.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her voice low, gaze fixed on the sharpening stone.

“A merchant. Looking for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask,” a gravelly voice replied.

Rhys exchanged a quick glance with Seris. He opened the door to reveal a man swallowed by shadow, a wide-brimmed hat obscuring his features. Only his eyes were visible – sharp, assessing, unsettlingly still. The air carried woodsmoke and old metal, and Rhys instinctively stepped back, a barely perceptible flinch.

“I understand you have charts of the Firepeak region,” the man said, his voice a rasp against stone. He deposited a heavy pouch onto the workbench, the clink of coins sharp. “I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps, particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys narrowed his gaze, his voice tight. “What *kind* of formations are you interested in? Be specific.” He subtly positioned himself between the merchant and a shadowed corner of the workshop.

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin smile. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” His eyes drifted to Seris, lingering on the cord binding her tunic, then tracing the line of her jaw. “You’ve both ventured deep into those peaks, haven't you? Seen things most haven't.”

Seris traced a botanical illustration with a calloused fingertip, her movements slow and precise. “We document the region’s flora. Rare specimens require extensive surveying. It’s meticulous work, demanding knowledge of the terrain. We’ve cataloged fault lines, mineral deposits… the natural architecture of the peaks.”

The merchant chuckled, a dry sound. “Botanists who know the old paths. Convenient.” He tapped the pouch. “But we can discuss a mutually beneficial arrangement. A cut of my payment for your…discretion?”

Rhys’s eyes met Seris’s, a flicker of desperation in them. “Look, we’re just researchers. We don’t get involved in…that kind of thing. We barely make enough to cover our expenses.” He ran a hand through his hair, a gesture more frantic than before.

Seris didn't immediately respond, her gaze fixed on the merchant. “We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice even. “However, we know individuals who might. Individuals who understand the value of silence…and the dangers of speaking freely.” She finally met the merchant’s gaze, her eyes the color of flint. “And such knowledge…commands a substantial premium.”

A beat of silence stretched, thick and tense. The merchant’s eyes narrowed, studying Seris, gauging her resolve. He didn’t respond immediately, his gaze lingering on her face, then flicking to Rhys, assessing his reaction. 

“Indeed. And what *kind* of premium are we discussing?” The merchant’s voice was a low purr, laced with a hint of threat. “Something beyond coin, perhaps?”

“Something that reflects the inherent risks, and the…potential consequences of disclosure,” Seris replied, a muscle ticking in her jaw. “Information like this isn't simply bought and sold. It's…insurance. For all parties involved.”

The merchant’s gaze flicked between Seris and Rhys, assessing the dynamic between them. He saw Rhys’s barely concealed anxiety, and Seris’s unwavering composure. A slow smile touched his lips. "Intriguing." He paused, then leaned forward, lowering his voice. “Let’s be clear. I don’t appreciate games. I need that map, and I need it now. I’m not interested in protracted negotiations or…complications.”

Seris didn't flinch. “Then perhaps you should have come with a more…direct offer.” She gestured to a stool with a slight tilt of her head. “Shall we discuss terms, or shall we waste each other’s time?”

The merchant’s eyes hardened. “I dislike being delayed.” He gestured towards the stool with a gloved hand. “And I *especially* dislike being lectured.” He paused, his gaze lingering on Rhys, a silent warning. “Or would you prefer I seek this information elsewhere?” A slow smile touched his lips. “There are always…other paths to the peaks. And some paths…are best left untrodden.” He leaned back, radiating a quiet menace. “Think carefully. Your silence, or the consequences of defiance. The choice is yours.”

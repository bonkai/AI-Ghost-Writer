## Scene 15 - Draft 2

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the high window of the cartography annex. Seris traced the faded ink lines of a map depicting Verenhal and the surrounding Firepeak Mountains, a relic salvaged from a forgotten archive. Rhys, meticulously cleaning a set of brass calipers, hummed a tuneless melody. They’d successfully redirected Valerius, steering him toward texts on herbal remedies, but the old inquisitor’s lingering gaze still prickled at Seris’s skin, a weight on the back of her neck.

“This map… it’s older than any I’ve seen,” Seris murmured, her fingers brushing the brittle parchment. “The city’s layout is… different. There’s a district marked here, ‘Aethelgard,’ that doesn’t appear on any modern charts. Almost as if it was… deliberately erased.”

Rhys glanced up, setting down the calipers. “Aethelgard? I’ve encountered the name in fragmented historical accounts. Supposedly a district devoted to artisans and scholars, destroyed during the Obsidian Uprising centuries ago. A complete loss. Though some whisper it wasn’t entirely *destroyed*…”

“But look,” Seris pointed to a series of minute symbols woven into the depiction of the Firepeak Mountains, almost invisible to the naked eye. “These aren’t topographical markers. They’re… coded. And they align with specific peaks, but not in any logical way. It's too precise, too deliberate."

*They're not just aligned, they’re a key,* Kaelen’s thought resonated in Seris’s mind, a gentle pressure behind her eyes. *A cipher. The peaks represent letters, or perhaps… runes. But not the common Eldorian runes. These are… older. Pre-Eldorian, almost.*

“Runes?” Rhys frowned. “Ancient Eldorian runes? Those haven’t been used in centuries. What would a map from the Obsidian Uprising era have to do with them?”

Seris ignored his question, her attention focused on the map. She pulled a worn leather-bound book from her satchel – a compendium of Eldorian *and* pre-Eldorian runes she’d painstakingly copied from various sources, a project that had consumed weeks. Slowly, carefully, she began to match the symbols on the map with the corresponding runes in the book, her brow furrowing with concentration. Several didn’t match. They were variations, almost… corrupted.

“This one… ‘Isen.’ And this… a distorted form of ‘Morwen.’ It’s forming a sequence, but it’s fragmented, almost deliberately broken,” she muttered, tracing the runes with a calloused fingertip. 

Rhys leaned closer, watching her work. “What does it say?”

Seris continued deciphering, her fingers tracing the runes. A growing sense of excitement, mixed with trepidation, filled her. “It’s… ‘Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’ But the phrasing is… archaic. Not the elegant prose of the Eldorians. It’s rough, almost… primal.”

*The Stone Heart… that’s not a metaphor,* Kaelen’s thought was sharper now, tinged with urgency. *It's a place. A location. And it’s hidden beneath one of those peaks. But it’s more than just a location. It’s a… conduit.*

“The Serpent’s Tooth…” Rhys traced the Firepeak Mountains on the map. “That must be Mount Cinderfang. It’s the most jagged peak, looks like a serpent’s fang.”

Seris’s gaze followed his finger. “But what’s ‘the Stone Heart’?” She ran a finger across the parchment, noticing a subtle difference in texture near the base of Mount Cinderfang. It was almost imperceptible, a slight discoloration and a barely-there indentation. 

“There’s something hidden *on* the map,” she breathed, her voice hushed.

Rhys grabbed a small magnifying glass from his toolkit. “It’s a layering,” he said, examining the map closely. “Someone has pasted a smaller piece of parchment onto the original. It’s almost perfectly camouflaged. And the adhesive… it's not modern. It's made from crushed bone and tree resin."

Carefully, using a delicate bone tool, Rhys began to peel back the overlay. The parchment resisted, brittle with age, threatening to tear. Beneath it, revealed in faded ink, was a detailed sketch of a subterranean structure – a circular chamber carved into the rock, with a single, massive stone pillar at its center. The pillar wasn’t smooth; it was covered in intricate carvings, symbols that felt… unsettling.

“By the Ancestors…” Rhys breathed, his voice filled with awe and apprehension.

Seris traced the lines of the sketch with her finger. “It’s not just a chamber. It’s… a focus. A place where energies converge.”

*The Stone Heart is a prison,* Kaelen’s voice echoed in her mind, a sudden, chilling clarity. *And something is trying to break free.*

Rhys looked up, sensing Seris’s unease. “What is it?”

Seris shook her head, trying to dismiss the premonition. “I don’t know. But I have a feeling we’ve stumbled onto something dangerous. And Valerius… he’s been asking too many questions about the history of the region.” She glanced at the doorway, a prickling sensation on the back of her neck. "I think we're being watched."

## Draft 3 – Scene 7

“Seris?”

She whirled, heart hammering. Hemlock stood framed in the doorway, his expression carefully neutral, but a stillness clung to him—less composure, more bracing.

Seris offered a curt nod, gaze fixed on the sphere. An inexplicable pull resonated within her, deep in her bones. She extended a hand, fingers hovering above the cool surface. A tremor ran through the stone as her fingertips brushed it, a vibration climbing her arm and settling in her chest – not unpleasant, but *awakening*.

The world didn’t shatter, but *shifted*. A blurring at the edges of her vision, then layers peeled back, revealing fractured glimpses of time—cities carved into mountains crumbling with age, canyons choked with luminous flora. The images pulsed with forgotten life, mirroring her quickening pulse. Coiled around a basalt peak, a colossal serpentine creature stirred, scales shimmering like a thousand sunsets. Awe quickly dissolved into primal fear.

It wasn’t the scale, but the gaze. Amidst the chaos, a single, ancient eye met hers—reptilian, gold flecks like trapped starlight. It didn’t merely see her; it bored *through* her, stripping away pretense. A resonance bloomed in her chest, a hollow ache mirroring the creature’s weariness. It felt…familiar. Not a memory, but a recognition—a chord struck within her soul. She stumbled back, bracing a hand against the wall, breath ragged. The images receded, leaving coldness in her chest, a prickling behind her eyes.

*Awakening…* The thought wasn’t spoken, but *felt*—a weightless pressure against her mind, the echo of the serpent’s presence. Not a threat, but an inevitability, steeped in eons. It felt… strangely like homecoming. Disorientation washed over her. She squeezed her eyes shut, then cautiously opened them.

The chamber felt heavier, shadows deeper, the thrumming beneath her feet intensified. The sphere radiated heat, carvings twisting like living things, responding to her touch. A tremor ran through her, not of fear, but of recognition. A sense of being *known*.

Hemlock’s voice, closer now, broke the silence. “Disturbed?” He had entered the chamber, gaze sweeping over the sphere, then settling on her face, assessing. A flicker of expectation crossed his features.

She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her. “Cities…a serpent…it felt real. Not a vision, but…a memory. One that isn’t mine. It felt like looking into my own past, but a past I never lived.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint smile. He reached out, fingers hovering over the sphere, then drew back. “Some places remember. Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, gaze locking with hers, probing. “And some people…are more attuned.”

He studied her intently, expression unreadable. “Be careful. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting*.” A subtle shift in his gaze suggested he knew more than he let on, and suspected she did too.

She looked back at the sphere. For a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths—a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns, a single, golden eye reflecting her own. The eye didn’t blink. It *watched*. And in its depths, she glimpsed not a reflection of herself, but a flicker of something ancient, something *knowing*. A silent acknowledgement. A promise. Or a warning. The past was seeing *her*.

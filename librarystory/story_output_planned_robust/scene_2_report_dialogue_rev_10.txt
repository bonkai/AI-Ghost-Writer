*   **Over-reliance on internal monologue as 'dialogue'.** While the format presents these as exchanges, much of it is the protagonist *thinking* and the presence responding with single words or sensations. This isn’t necessarily *bad*, and fits the scenario, but it skirts the line of being dialogue at all. Consider if some of the protagonist's internal questions could be replaced with actions or attempts to *demonstrate* his thoughts (e.g., focusing intently on a book *as a question* rather than *asking* about it).
*   **The Presence's Voice is *too* consistent.** While enigmatic is good, the consistent 'feeling-based' communication starts to feel repetitive. Even subtle shifts in *how* the presence communicates (a colder scan vs. a fleeting image) would add depth.
*   **"Forgotten" Ending – Powerful, but potentially confusing.** While the intention is to have it resonate *within* him, it might read as simply a thought *he* has, not a final communication. Consider a slightly stronger signal that it originates from the presence – a fading sensation accompanying the word.
*   **Lack of Conflict/Stakes in the 'Dialogue'.** The protagonist is largely accepting of the situation. Even a small attempt to *demand* answers or express frustration more strongly would raise the tension. The presence is entirely passive, offering only observations.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity Missed:** We learn *nothing* about the presence beyond its age and observational nature. Even a hint of its motivations or history (revealed through its reactions to the protagonist) would make it more compelling.



Overall, the writing is strong and atmospheric, but the dialogue (and its substitute) feels a little static. It prioritizes mystery over interaction, which may be intentional, but limits the potential for dramatic tension and character development.

*   **Rhys’s Pragmatism:** While consistent, Rhys’s immediate jumps to practical solutions (“wind,” grabbing the poker) feel *very* frequent and a little flat. It’s bordering on a character tic rather than a nuanced response. Consider letting him express *some* concern or curiosity before defaulting to practicality.
*   **Seris’s Internalization:** Seris does a lot of *feeling* and then *telling* Rhys what she feels ("I feel watched," "growing dread"). Show more of her reaction *through* dialogue. Instead of "growing dread," perhaps a clipped sentence, a change in tone, or a physical reaction (tightening grip on the map).
*   **Kaelen’s Interruptions:** Kaelen's mental intrusions are frequent and, while establishing stakes, are disrupting the flow of the conversation. Consider consolidating some of these into a single, more impactful moment. Or, explore *how* these intrusions affect Seris's speech – a stutter, a pause, a shift in topic.
*   **Repetitive Statements of Danger:** Lines like “something dangerous” and “something that should have remained buried” are a bit on the nose. The situation *is* dangerous – the dialogue doesn't need to explicitly state it repeatedly. Let the revealed map and Seris's reactions convey the danger.
*   **Subtext Opportunity - Valerius:** The line about Valerius feels dropped in. It’s a good thread, but needs more development. Perhaps Seris shares a specific question he asked, or a detail that made her uneasy. This would create more tension and intrigue.
*   **“By the ancestors…”:** This feels like a standard fantasy exclamation. It doesn’t add much to Rhys’s character or the moment. Consider a more unique reaction.
*   **Seris Dismissing Premonitions:** Seris dismissing Kaelen’s warnings feels a little contrived. It's a way to avoid dealing with the immediate threat, but it doesn't feel organic to her character. Perhaps she tries to dismiss it, but her voice betrays her fear.

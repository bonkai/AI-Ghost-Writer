## Revised Draft - Incorporating Feedback

Here's a revised draft of the scene, incorporating the feedback provided. I've focused on making the communication feel less mental and more *felt*, grounding it in sensory experience, and adding more subtle reactions from Hemlock. I’ve also tried to increase the tension and mystery.

---

She’d spent the intervening hour observing Hemlock. He hadn’t left his post, but his gaze seemed to drift more often towards her alcove. Not a direct stare, but a subtle tracking, a constant assessment. He knew she hadn’t abandoned her search.

Now, she needed to test a theory. 

Seris pulled a slim volume on medicinal fungi from the shelf, opening it at random. She began to “read,” her eyes scanning the text, but her attention wasn’t *on* the text. It was on the weight of the book in her hands, the cool smoothness of the aged paper, the scent of dust and dried leaves. She needed to recreate the… resonance.

She flipped a page. Nothing. Another. Still nothing. Frustration gnawed at her. Was it the book itself? The location? Or had it been a fleeting anomaly?

Then, she remembered something. The initial touch hadn’t been a deliberate search. She’d brushed against the spine accidentally. She deliberately, but gently, ran her fingertips across the worn leather of the fungi book, tracing the embossed lettering. 

Nothing.

She tried again, applying slightly more pressure, almost caressing the spine. Still nothing.

Then, almost unconsciously, she let her mind drift back to the serpent carvings. Not *thinking* about them, but *remembering* the sensation of running her hand over the cool stone, the feeling of the intricate patterns beneath her fingertips, the faint echo of a forgotten history. She closed her eyes, letting the memory flood her senses.

A subtle tremor ran through the book in her hands.  The page before her seemed to… *waver*, the printed words blurring at the edges. It wasn't a visual distortion, exactly, more like a shift in the air around the page, a shimmering heat. Then, the blurring resolved, not into different text, but into a subtle highlighting of existing words, a faint golden sheen overlaying the ink.

*“The stone remembers the coil.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. The words weren’t *new*. They were already there, woven into the text, but… revealed.  It felt less like receiving a message and more like uncovering something that had always been hidden. 

She quickly scanned the surrounding pages. Nothing. The highlighting appeared only on that single page, triggered not by thought, but by the *feeling* of the serpent, by the resonance of the memory.

“Interesting,” she murmured, her voice barely audible.

She flipped to another page. Nothing. She tried again, focusing on the serpent, on the image of the hidden city, not *picturing* it, but *recalling* the weight of the stone, the scent of damp earth, the feeling of being enclosed within its walls. 

This time, a different section of the page subtly illuminated. 

*“Where the sun sleeps, the path begins.”*

This wasn’t simply reading. It was… a conversation. A response to her *presence*, filtered through the book.

She glanced up, half-expecting to see Hemlock watching her. He was still at his post, seemingly engrossed in a large tome, but his head was tilted slightly, as if listening for something beyond the rustle of pages. A flicker of something unreadable crossed his face.

She needed to be careful. She couldn’t risk drawing too much attention. She decided to try a different approach. She didn’t *recall* the serpent. Instead, she simply *allowed* the feeling of it to wash over her, a passive recipient of its energy, letting the memory permeate her senses.

The page before her blurred again, then resolved. This time, instead of words, a faint, almost translucent image appeared – a winding path leading towards a shadowed peak, a mountain silhouetted against a setting sun. The image seemed to *pulse* with a faint, inner light.

It was a map. A fragmented, symbolic map, but a map nonetheless.

“Remarkable,” she breathed, her fingers tracing the outline of the mountain, feeling a strange warmth radiating from the page.

Suddenly, a voice cut through the quiet of the alcove.

“Finding what you seek, Initiate?”

Seris startled, quickly closing the book. She turned to see Hemlock standing a few feet away, his expression unreadable. He hadn't approached silently. His movements were too fluid, too controlled for someone simply looking up from a book.

“Just… researching,” she said, forcing a casual tone. “The medicinal properties of fungi.”

“A fascinating field,” Hemlock said, his gaze lingering on the closed book in her hands. “Though some knowledge is best left undisturbed.”

She felt a prickle of unease. “I… I’m simply expanding my understanding.”

“Indeed.” He paused, his eyes sweeping over the alcove. “The past has a way of reaching out, doesn’t it? Sometimes, it’s best not to answer its call.”

“I don’t understand.”

Hemlock offered a small, enigmatic smile. “Perhaps you will. In time.” He turned back to his post, but Seris could feel his gaze on her back, a silent, unsettling weight.

*   **Rhys's Dialogue Feels Functional, Not Character-Specific:** While polite and intelligent, Rhys's lines ("It’s a complex problem. Demands nuance.") and ("We’re simply trying to understand…") feel like they *serve* the plot rather than revealing his personality. What makes *him* unique in how he speaks? Is he overly formal? Does he have a particular turn of phrase?
*   **Valerius's Dialogue is a Bit Too On-the-Nose:** His warnings ("Some knowledge is best left undisturbed," "Be careful what you seek…") are very direct. Consider *implying* his suspicion and possessiveness through more subtle phrasing or questions. A veiled threat is often more effective than a blatant one.
*   **Kaelen's Voice – Opportunity for Subtext:** While you’ve integrated Kaelen well, the direct statements ("He suspects," "He’s shielding something") still feel like telling. Could some of this be conveyed through Seris’s *perception* of Valerius’s micro-expressions or body language? *Show* Seris realizing these things, rather than Kaelen simply *telling* her.
*   **Seris's "What’s wrong?" feels weak:** Rhys is clearly sensing urgency. A more proactive question, driven by her concern for *him*, would be stronger. Something like, “Are you alright? You seem tense.”
*   **Repetition of "We"**: The frequent use of "we" in Seris and Rhys’s dialogue, while logical, starts to feel a bit stilted. Vary sentence structure to avoid this.



Overall, the dialogue isn’t *bad*, but it leans towards functional rather than vibrant. With a little more attention to individual voice and subtle delivery, it could be significantly more impactful.

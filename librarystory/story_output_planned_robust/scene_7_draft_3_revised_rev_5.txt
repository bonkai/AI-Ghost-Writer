“Seris?”

She whirled, heart hammering. Hemlock stood in the doorway, expression unreadable. He didn’t speak, only watched, a stillness that felt like a held breath.

Seris nodded, gaze fixed on the sphere. She reached out, fingers brushing the cool surface, an involuntary pull she couldn’t resist.

The world fractured—not into darkness, but layers. Vast, crumbling cities carved into mountainsides bled into canyons filled with bioluminescent flora. Coiled around the highest peak, a colossal serpentine creature, scales shimmering like a thousand sunsets, pulsed with life.

It wasn’t the grandeur that stole her breath, but the gaze. Amidst the chaos, a single, ancient eye met hers—reptilian, gold-flecked, brimming with knowing. It didn’t merely *see* her; it looked *through* her, stripping away layers. A resonance bloomed within her chest, mirroring the creature’s weariness. She stumbled back, a moan escaping her lips. The images fractured, receding, leaving an ache behind her eyes and a coldness within her chest. She braced a hand against the wall, knuckles white, breath ragged.

*“Awakening…”* The voice wasn’t spoken, but *felt*—the image of the serpent echoing in her mind. Not a threat, but inevitability, weighted with eons and a chilling hunger. It felt…like homecoming. She squeezed her eyes shut, then slowly opened them.

The room seemed heavier, shadows deeper, the thrumming beneath her feet intensified. The sphere radiated heat, carvings twisting like living things.

Hemlock’s voice, closer now, broke the silence. “Disturbed?” He’d entered the chamber, gaze sweeping over the sphere, then settling on her face.

She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her despite the warmth. “Cities…a serpent…it felt real. Like a memory, but not my own.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint smile. He reached out, fingers hovering over the sphere, then drew back. “Some places remember. Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, gaze locking with hers. “And some people…are more attuned.”

He studied her intently. “Be careful. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting*.”

She looked back at the sphere, and for a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths—a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns, a single, golden eye reflecting her own. The eye didn’t blink. It *watched*.

Darkness. Not the digital black of a disconnected monitor, but a profound, absolute absence of light. A suffocating weight pressed against… everything. Or rather, against *where* everything should be. Kaelen tried to lift a hand, to brush the darkness away, but there was no hand. No arm. No body.

Panic flared, cold and sharp. He gasped, but no air filled his lungs. He tried to speak, to shout, to *do* anything, but his voice was lost before it formed, swallowed by the void. He was adrift, untethered, a consciousness suspended in nothingness.

“What… what happened?” The thought formed, echoing weakly within the confines of his own mind. It wasn’t a spoken question, merely a desperate plea directed at… at what? There was no one to answer.

He strained, attempting to recall the last moments – the game, the pressure, the pain. Fragments flickered – the glowing screen, the frantic keystrokes, the searing ache behind his eyes. Then, a final, blinding flash of white. Had it been a seizure? A heart attack?

He tried to focus, to grasp at something solid, but his thoughts scattered like dust motes in a windstorm. He was losing himself, dissolving into the nothingness. 

Then, a shift. Not a change in location, but a change in *texture*. The darkness wasn’t uniform anymore. It pulsed, subtly, with a faint, ethereal glow. And with the glow came… sound. Not the booming roar of a crowd, but a gentle hum, like the distant resonance of a forgotten bell.

He focused on the sound, clinging to it as a lifeline. It grew stronger, coalescing into something… architectural. He began to perceive shapes, indistinct at first, then gradually resolving into the vague outline of a vast, cavernous space.

He was… *within* something.

“Hello?” He projected the thought, a desperate beacon in the echoing darkness.

A response, not of sound, but of *feeling*. A gentle brush against his awareness, like a whisper against his soul. It wasn’t a voice, but an impression, a sense of ancient, patient observation.

*Aware.*

The single word resonated within him, a profound and unsettling realization. He wasn’t simply *existing* in this void; he was being *observed*.

He tried to orient himself, to understand where he was, but it was like trying to map a dream. The space stretched endlessly in all directions, filled with towering structures that seemed to shift and rearrange themselves with every blink of his nonexistent eyes. He sensed… books. Countless volumes, stacked on shelves that disappeared into the gloom. And the air, though he couldn't breathe it, was thick with the scent of aged paper and forgotten knowledge.

He was in a library. A library beyond imagining. But a library… without a librarian.

“Where am I?” he projected again, his desperation growing.

The response came, slower this time, as if the very fabric of the space resisted the communication.

*Between.*

“Between what?”

A pause, long and unsettling. Then, a final, chilling revelation.

*Between worlds.*

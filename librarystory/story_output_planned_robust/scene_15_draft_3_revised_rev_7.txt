## Scene 15 - Draft 3

The cartography room smelled of dry paper and dust motes dancing in the single shaft of sunlight slicing through the high, arched window. Seris traced a finger across the brittle vellum of the ancient map, the paper whispering beneath her touch. A tremor started in her hand, spreading up her arm, a cold echo of the mountain peaks depicted on the map. It wasn’t a guide she held, but a held breath.

“The sequence is… almost complete,” she murmured, her brow furrowed not in concentration, but a growing unease. “’Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’” She didn’t need to state the obvious; the phrasing lacked the polished grace of Eldorian script. It felt raw, carved with desperate urgency. She glanced at Rhys, then quickly back at the map, a fleeting tightening of her jaw the only outward sign of her apprehension.

Rhys, perched on a stool, methodically cleaned bone tools, the scrape of stone against stone a steady counterpoint to the quickening beat of her own heart. He didn’t look up.

“Pre-Eldorian?” she asked, more to fill the silence than seek information. “Before the unification? Those dialects are… fragmented now.”

“More than just the language,” she continued, tracing a distorted rune with a calloused fingertip. “Variations. Corruptions. Someone deliberately obscured the meaning, layered it with… something else.” She didn’t need to explain. The intention was clear in the misshapen glyphs. “This… ‘Isen.’ And this, a fractured ‘Morwen.’”

*A nexus,* Kaelen’s presence settled within her, not as sound, but as a sudden chill at the base of her skull, followed by a fleeting image: fractured stone, hairline fissures spiderwebbing across its surface. She breathed deeply, anchoring herself, letting the image fade before it could touch her expression. Years of shared consciousness had taught her control, but the raw *feeling* of the image – containment, strain – lingered.

Rhys finally looked up, his gaze assessing. “Kaelen said something?”

Seris hesitated, meeting his eyes, a carefully neutral expression on her face. “Confirmation the runes are older than we thought. And that the ‘Stone Heart’ isn’t… metaphorical.” She returned to the map, her gaze sweeping across the jagged peaks, a subtle quickening of her breath the only sign of her growing dread. “The Serpent’s Tooth… Mount Cinderfang. The most prominent.”

Rhys traced the peak with his finger. “A fitting name.” He didn’t offer reassurance, just observation.

Seris’s eyes narrowed as she ran a finger across the parchment, detecting a subtle texture change near Mount Cinderfang’s base. Almost imperceptible, a discoloration in the aged vellum. *A thinning,* Kaelen’s thought echoed, a pressure against her temples. She focused on the map, amplifying the sensation, and realized the discoloration wasn't merely a change in color, but a slight *depression* in the parchment, as if something beneath pressed upwards.

“I don’t know what we’ve stumbled onto,” she said, her voice low, “but it feels… dangerous. Like disturbing something that should have remained buried.”

*It *is* a cage,* Kaelen’s voice resonated, sharper now, a pulse of energy that made the hairs on her arms prickle. A fleeting image – sickly green light pulsing within the fractured stone – flickered behind her eyes. She swallowed, forcing the image down, her knuckles white as she gripped the map.

Rhys looked up, sensing her mounting terror. “Seris?”

She shook her head, forcing herself to focus, to breathe evenly. She traced the discoloration with a trembling finger, a subtle pulse echoing the frantic beat of her heart. A prison. A place of binding.

“Valerius…” she continued, her voice barely audible. “He’s been asking too many questions. About this region’s history, the old gods… the rituals performed *within* the mountains.” She paused, sweeping the room with a quick, almost involuntary glance. “I feel… watched.”

Rhys reached for a heavy iron poker, his movements deliberate, unhurried. “Perhaps just the wind.” He didn't offer empty platitudes. "We'll be ready."

Seris nodded, her heart hammering against her ribs. They had unearthed something ancient and terrible. And she had a chilling premonition they weren't the only ones who knew about it. The map, once a puzzle, now felt like a warning. A faint, metallic tang, like old blood and ozone, filled the air, prickling at the back of her throat. She traced the discoloration again, a cold dread settling in her stomach. Whatever lay beneath Mount Cinderfang, it was straining against its bonds, and she feared the moment it broke free.

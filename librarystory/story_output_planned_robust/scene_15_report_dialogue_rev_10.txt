*   **Seris’s initial murmured lines (“Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…”)** – While atmospheric, this feels a bit *too* on-the-nose for a character actively investigating. It’s telling us she’s found something significant rather than *showing* her process of discovery. Consider having her react to the text first, then perhaps repeat a key phrase as realization dawns.
*   **Rhys’s “Something?”** – This is functional but lacks character. Given their established dynamic (presumably Rhys is observant and perhaps a bit wry), a more nuanced response would be better. Something like, “Find a wrinkle in the world?” or simply a raised eyebrow and expectant look would convey more.
*   **“He’s looking for something here,”** – Again, direct statement. Could be strengthened by showing Seris connecting the map to Valerius's questions *through* her dialogue. For example, “The rituals… he asked about rituals *within* the mountains… it has to be connected.”
*   **“Contained? What makes you say that?”** – Rhys’s question is fine, but his *lack* of reassurance feels a little abrupt. A small acknowledgement of Seris’s distress before questioning would make his readiness feel more earned.
*   **“Just…a feeling. Like disturbing something that should have stayed buried.”** – This is weak. The “just a feeling” is a cliché. The internal thought ("the feeling tasted like iron and regret") is excellent and should be *integrated* into the dialogue. Something like, “It feels…wrong. Like stirring up something ancient… and bitter.”
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The entire exchange feels a bit surface-level. Given the stakes and the characters' potential history, there's room for underlying tension or unspoken concerns to color the dialogue.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** Rhys’s immediate reach for the poker is good, but it could be amplified by a brief, shared glance that conveys years of trust and preparedness.

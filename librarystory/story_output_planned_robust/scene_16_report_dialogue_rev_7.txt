*   **Repetitive Non-Verbal Communication:** Rhys’s “tightening grip” and “searching gaze” are described multiple times. While consistent, it becomes noticeable and doesn’t *show* us much beyond “Rhys is concerned.” Consider varying his reactions – a slight shift in weight, a subtle questioning of her hand, a brief touch to her arm.
*   **“Given” Dialogue:** Seris’s line, “Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible,” and “The seal is failing…” feels like exposition *told* to us through her character, rather than organically arising from her internal processing. It lacks the emotional weight the scene is building. Consider *how* she arrives at this conclusion. Is it a deduction? A feeling? A memory? Show the thought process, even briefly.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Rhys):** Rhys consistently *doesn't* ask questions, which is a deliberate choice. However, after several instances of withholding, a *single* carefully phrased question might be more impactful than continued silence. Perhaps, after she says "We have to go," he could say, “What exactly are we facing?” This acknowledges her experience without demanding immediate explanation.
*   **Seris’s “Given” Dialogue (cont.):** The line “It’s connected to the source of the network” feels like a direct info-dump. Perhaps she could have a physical reaction that *leads* to this realization. For example, touching a specific point on the map, feeling a resonance, and then murmuring, “The network…it originates there.”
*   **Kaelen’s Communication:** While the non-verbal communication from Kaelen is effective for establishing the urgency and fragmented nature of the vision, consider *briefly* showing Seris’s internal struggle to interpret it. A single word or phrase she pulls from the onslaught would ground the experience for the reader.
*   **Efficiency:** The repeated descriptions of Rhys's reactions, while consistent, slow the pace. Trimming some of these descriptions would heighten the tension.

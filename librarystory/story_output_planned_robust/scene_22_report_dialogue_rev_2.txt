*   **Repetitive Questioning:** The repeated questioning of "What is…?" (The Weave, lost narratives, deliberately forgotten) feels a bit formulaic and slows the momentum. Consider varying the phrasing or having characters *react* to the information instead of immediately asking for definitions. Show, don't just ask.
*   **Kaelen's Delivery:** Kaelen’s responses are *entirely* exposition dumps. While necessary to convey information, it lacks dramatic tension. Consider having Kaelen struggle to access the memories, or deliver fragmented pieces that Seris and Rhys must piece together. A little resistance or difficulty would make the reveals more impactful.
*   **Seris's Reactions:** Seris’s reactions are often internal ("dread washing over her," "betrayal tightened her chest") *followed* by a fairly flat delivery of dialogue. Try merging the internal feeling *into* the dialogue itself. For example, instead of "He knew…to lead us into a trap," perhaps something like, "He *used* us. He let us dig right into it…" (more anger and betrayal expressed in the phrasing).
*   **Rhys's Role:** Rhys feels somewhat passive. He mostly observes and asks clarifying questions. Give him a stronger reaction to the reveals, or have him offer a theory or insight that moves the plot forward.
*   **"Before Eldoria" Reveal:** The final exchange is a bit abrupt. After building tension, the reveal of "Before Eldoria" feels a little underwhelming. Consider adding a beat of anticipation – perhaps a physical sensation for Seris before Kaelen responds, or a more dramatic internal reaction.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Valerius):** The line about Valerius could be stronger. Seris saying "He knew" is a statement of fact. Explore the emotional weight of that betrayal. Perhaps a line expressing her feeling of foolishness or vulnerability.

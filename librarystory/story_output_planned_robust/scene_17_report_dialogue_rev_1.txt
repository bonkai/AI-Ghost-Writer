*   **Over-Reliance on Thought-Narration (Kaelen):** Kaelen’s constant thoughts are *telling* us information rather than letting Seris *discover* it or react organically. While the mental link is established, it’s becoming a crutch. Consider reducing the frequency and letting Seris deduce things herself, even if she’s partially guided by Kaelen. For example, instead of "The Obsidian Circle…", perhaps Kaelen could send a *feeling* of dread and a fragmented image of the serpent symbol, forcing Seris to connect the dots.
*   **“Just a relic…” line:** This feels a bit cliché and overly cautious. Seris is a capable character. A more subtle deflection, or a question back at Valerius, might be more in character. Something like, "Interesting you ask that, Captain. What makes *you* think it's consequential?"
*   **Valerius’s Motivation – Too Direct:** Valerius’s explanation of wanting to “ensure the safety of the realm” is a bit bland and expected. While villains *believe* they're justified, a more nuanced approach – perhaps framing it as preventing chaos, or protecting the realm from a perceived threat – would add depth.
*   **Repetitive Internal State:** Several lines emphasize Seris’s fear/realization ("chilling certainty," "desperate chance," "tremor in her hands"). While internal conflict is good, it's becoming repetitive. Show, don’t tell. Let her actions and reactions convey her fear.
*   **“Different ideas about safety” – A Bit Flat:** While a solid line to end on, it lacks punch. After all the build-up, it feels a little underwhelming. Consider something more defiant or challenging.
*   **Rhys is Silent:** Rhys is present throughout the scene, but doesn't contribute *any* dialogue. This makes him feel like a prop. Even a single line of support or observation would strengthen his presence and the dynamic between him and Seris.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Valerius’s Smile):** Valerius's predatory smile is *described*, but not *used*. A line from Seris *reacting* to the smile ("Your smile doesn't inspire confidence, Captain.") would add tension and reveal character.

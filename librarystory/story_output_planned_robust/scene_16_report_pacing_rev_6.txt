*   **Initial Atmosphere (Paragraphs 1-2):** Excellent setup. The slow build of sensory details (dust, tremor, Rhys's subtle readiness) establishes a palpable sense of unease. Pacing is effective in creating anticipation.
*   **Shift & Energy Disruption (Paragraphs 3-4):** The description of the library *shifting* is strong, but the energy retreat feels slightly abrupt. While the intention is to create a 'hollow ache' instead of explosion, the transition could be smoother—perhaps a beat of increasing strain *before* the retreat.
*   **Kaelen's Connection (Paragraphs 5-7):** This is where the pacing falters slightly. The rush of sensation and imagery is overwhelming, and while intended to convey panic, it feels *too* rapid. Consider breaking it down into smaller, more digestible beats. Show Seris processing each element (icy dread, symbol, fragmented words) rather than just receiving it all at once. This would allow the tension to build more organically.
*   **Post-Vision Silence (Paragraphs 8-9):** The immediate silence after the onslaught is effective, providing a necessary pause. However, the dialogue feels a little *too* direct after such a disorienting experience. Perhaps a moment of Seris struggling to articulate her thoughts, or Rhys prompting her gently, would feel more realistic and maintain the tension.
*   **Overall:** The scene is strong in establishing atmosphere and hinting at a larger threat. The primary issue is a slight imbalance in pacing during the transmission of Kaelen’s vision. Slowing down those beats would allow the tension to peak more effectively.

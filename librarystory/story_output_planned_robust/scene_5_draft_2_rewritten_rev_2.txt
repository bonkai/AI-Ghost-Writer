## Scene 5 - Revised

The Atheneum smelled of dust and forgotten things—a scent that stirred something within Seris, a disquieting echo of ages past. She ran a gloved hand along a towering bookshelf, the cool wood a grounding sensation as her gaze swept the chamber, assessing angles, shadows. Sunlight fractured by stained glass painted the vast chamber in shifting hues, but failed to penetrate the deeper recesses, leaving pockets of oppressive darkness.

The library was a labyrinth, and Seris felt watched. Aisles spiraled into darkness, branching into hidden alcoves. Most dismissed it as a crumbling relic. The Iron Church certainly did, restricting access. Which was precisely why Seris had sought it out. Not for spells or potions, but for information—fragments of a time before the Church’s grip, before magic was sin. Specifically, she needed anything referencing the ‘Veiled Cities’—settlements erased from history, said to possess suppressed knowledge.

A cough echoed from the shadows. Seris’s hand tightened, her weight shifting subtly. She scanned the nearest alcove, her gaze lingering on Old Man Hemlock, the caretaker. Hunched over a table, cataloging scrolls, he appeared harmless, yet his stillness was unnerving. His position offered a comprehensive view of the chamber.

“Looking for something specific, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice rasped, barely louder than rustling parchment. He didn’t look up, but Seris felt his gaze – a prickling on her skin.

“Just exploring,” Seris replied, keeping her voice neutral. Her eyes continued to scan for threats. “The Church rarely permits access. I’m curious what secrets such a place holds.”

“Secrets have a way of finding those who seek them,” Hemlock said cryptically. “But be warned. Some are best left buried.”

Seris ignored the warning, turning back to the shelves, her movements quick and efficient. She ran her finger along the spines, scanning faded titles. Most were treatises on theology, philosophy, or alchemy. Useless. The prickling on her neck intensified, and she subtly adjusted her position, placing herself closer to a shadowed archway – an exit.

She reached for a worn volume, its cover devoid of a title. As her fingers brushed the aged leather, a faint impression flickered at the edge of her awareness – a sense of immense age, of stone and silence. It wasn’t a sound, but a resonance, a pressure against her mind.

She froze, her heart pounding. The sensation sharpened, coalescing into a fragmented thought, *not her own*. *“The stones remember…”* It felt less like hearing a voice and more like receiving a memory – cold, ancient, unsettling.

Seris quickly withdrew her hand, glancing around nervously. Hemlock hadn’t moved, but his eyes, obscured by shadow, seemed to bore into her.

“Did you say something, Initiate?” he asked, his voice a low murmur.

“No,” Seris replied quickly, forcing a smile. “Just admiring the craftsmanship.”

She needed to leave. This place wasn’t just old. It was *alive*, humming with an incomprehensible energy. And she felt whatever secrets the Atheneum held wouldn’t be given up easily. She turned to leave, but paused, noticing a small, unassuming volume tucked away on a lower shelf. Its cover was blank, held closed by a single, silver clasp. 

Something about it called to her. A sense of familiarity, a pull she couldn’t ignore. Hesitantly, she reached for it, her fingers trembling slightly. As she touched the clasp, the impression flooded her mind again, clearer this time, sharper, accompanied by a fleeting image of a coiled serpent.

Seris snatched her hand back as if burned. Her breath hitched. This wasn't a simple echo of the past. This was… *intentional*. She glanced at Hemlock, her eyes narrowed. He hadn't moved, hadn't reacted. But the shadow around his eyes seemed to deepen.

She tucked the image of the serpent into the back of her mind. The Atheneum wasn’t just a library. It was a guardian. And she had just been given a task.

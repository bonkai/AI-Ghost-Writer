*   **Authenticity & Voice:** Seris's dialogue is consistently strong and believable for a character who is both knowledgeable and protective. Rhys's dialogue is almost *too* silent. While the stage directions indicate desperation, he needs *some* verbal contribution, even if it’s short and anxious, to feel like a fully realized character and not just a reactive prop.
*   **Subtext:** Excellent use of subtext throughout, particularly in the glances and silences. The merchant’s veiled threats and Seris’s measured responses create a palpable tension. The "botanist" label is cleverly used by both sides to establish a pretense.
*   **Efficiency & Purpose:** Mostly efficient. The dialogue effectively establishes the stakes, the power dynamics, and the characters' motivations. However, Seris's repeated emphasis on "preservation" and "assurance" feels slightly redundant after a few iterations. Consider streamlining this – a single, powerful statement of her principles might be more impactful.
*   **Flow & Rhythm:** The pace is good, building tension effectively. The back-and-forth feels natural.
*   **Clarity vs. Ambiguity:** Well-balanced. The merchant's true intentions remain ambiguous, which is good, while Seris's protective stance is clear.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** Excellent. The dialogue subtly reveals Seris’s authority and Rhys’s vulnerability. The merchant is effectively portrayed as manipulative and impatient.
*   **Info-Dumps:** No blatant info-dumps, though Seris’s explanations about her work occasionally border on it. Try to integrate more of this information into actions or subtext.

**Specific Suggestions:**

*   **Rhys:** Give him at least 2-3 short lines. Even a nervous “Seris…” or a hesitant question would add depth.
*   **Seris's Repetition:** Condense her statements about preservation and assurance. Perhaps replace one instance with a pointed action (e.g., carefully arranging a fragile plant specimen).
*   **Merchant's Threats:** While subtle, the merchant's final line ("There are always…alternative routes…") feels a little cliché. Consider a more unique threat that ties into the specific nature of the caves or the region.
*   **Tighten:** The phrase "assurance that those who shared it with us remain…unburdened" is a bit verbose. Could be shortened to “assurance of their safety.”

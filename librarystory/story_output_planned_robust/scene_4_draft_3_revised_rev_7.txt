## Scene 4 - Draft 3

The air tasted of ash and regret. Rain threatened, clinging to the oppressive stillness of the courtyard, each drop a weight against the crumbling stone. Kaelen drifted through the space, a prickling awareness settling over him – not with witnessing, but with *being* witnessed. A resonance, faint but insistent, focused on the base of the weathered statue – a pull, not a memory, but a beckoning. It felt… expectant.

A boy, barely ten summers old, darted into the space, clutching something hidden. He pressed a tarnished locket beneath a loose stone, his fingers trembling, lips moving in a silent, desperate plea. The gesture felt less about concealment and more about *entrustment* – a desperate offering *to* the stone itself, as if it could offer protection.

Kaelen’s attention sharpened. The locket wasn’t merely metal; it resonated with a subtle energy, a muted echo of power. He sensed a deliberate crafting, a purpose beyond simple ornamentation. 

A shadow fell across the boy. Lucian, an Inquisitor, hadn’t *moved* so much as *appeared*. The light seemed to bend around him, not illuminate. He didn’t demand attention, simply *observed* the boy, his presence a weight in the courtyard. Before the boy fully registered him, Lucian *knew*. The concealment had been clumsy, born of desperation, and Lucian clearly relished the predictability of it.

The boy froze, his face draining of color. He didn’t meet Lucian’s gaze, but stared at the ground, as if begging the stones to swallow him whole. A tremor ran through his small frame.

Lucian advanced, his boots crunching on the gravel. He didn’t demand, he *implied*. “What have you entrusted to the stone?” His voice held a quiet certainty, an accusation already delivered, yet laced with a subtle, predatory patience.

The boy hesitated, then extended a trembling hand, revealing a handful of wildflowers – a pathetic offering, their scent faint but present. Lucian’s expression didn’t soften. Instead, a flicker of something unreadable crossed his features – a brief, almost painful recognition, quickly masked by practiced control. It was fleeting, but Kaelen sensed a vulnerability in Lucian, a ghost of something lost, something he desperately tried to bury. A moment of empathy, quickly extinguished.

“Lies.” Lucian’s voice was chipped ice, precise and cutting. He seized the boy’s arm, forcing him to drop the flowers. “What were you concealing near the statue?” 

A coldness settled over Kaelen, mirroring the boy’s fear. He subtly exerted his influence, a ripple of spectral energy meant to disrupt Lucian’s focus – a momentary distraction, a flicker of static in the Inquisitor’s perception. It wasn’t enough to *stop* him, but it bought the boy a fraction of a second, a breath of reprieve.

Valerius, another Inquisitor, stepped forward. “They weren’t merely concealing it, Lucian. They were safeguarding it.” He didn’t need to explain; the locket *spoke* of a purpose beyond simple collection. The metal itself felt… resonant, *alive* with a dormant power. “A relic of the Archivists, perhaps?”

Lucian’s gaze hardened. He turned back to the boy, his grip tightening. “Take him to the Citadel. He will be questioned. And retrieve the object.” He nodded to Valerius. “Catalog it with care. A full report on its composition and any accompanying symbols. I want to know everything about its origins.”

As the Inquisitors led the boy away, Kaelen lingered. The rain began to fall, washing over the stone. He reached out, his spectral hand passing through the cold, damp stone of the statue. A faint resonance pulsed beneath his touch, a whisper of power. It wasn't a memory, but a pull, a connection – a fragmented image flickered in his mind: not a vast library, but a single, intricately carved key. A key shaped like a blooming flower.

He wasn't merely an observer. He was bound to this place, to this history. The locket wasn’t just a relic. It was *part* of the key. A key to unlocking a forgotten past, and perhaps, to understanding his own purpose. The rain fell harder, washing away the dust of ages, revealing a hidden truth. The Archivists hadn’t simply preserved knowledge. They had protected something. And whatever that something was, it was still here, waiting. Not hidden, but guarded. He sensed a subtle shift in the energy around the statue, a tightening of the wards. The Archivists weren’t just gone. They were waiting. And he, somehow, was connected to their vigil. He felt a faint, rhythmic pulse emanating from the statue itself, a heartbeat of forgotten power. It was a call, a silent invitation. And Kaelen knew, with growing certainty, that he was meant to answer. The key, he realized, wasn’t just *to* something. It *was* something. A conduit. And the locket was the first piece. A piece that resonated with a specific frequency, a frequency that echoed within him. He reached out again, focusing his spectral energy on the statue, and a single word formed in his mind, unbidden and ancient: *Florian*.

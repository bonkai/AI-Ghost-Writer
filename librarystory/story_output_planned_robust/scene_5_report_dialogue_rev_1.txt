*   **Seris's initial response ("Just…exploring") feels weak and overly cautious.** It’s a standard deflection, but doesn't quite fit a character who *actively sought out* a restricted library. Consider something that acknowledges the restriction while still projecting purpose – "I requested permission. I'm researching historical theological disputes."
*   **Hemlock’s cryptic statements (“Secrets have a way…”, “Some secrets are best left buried”) are *very* on-the-nose.** While mystery is good, this feels like he's *telling* us he's mysterious rather than *being* mysterious. Subtext! Let his actions and observations do more of the work. Perhaps a pointed look while saying nothing at all.
*   **The repeated insistence on Seris "admiring the craftsmanship" feels contrived.** It's used twice as a cover for her reactions to the psychic impressions, and it’s becoming noticeable *to the reader*. Find a different way to convey her attempt to appear normal – perhaps a quick, irrelevant question about the library’s history.
*   **The psychic impressions themselves are described *to* us, rather than *shown* through dialogue.** While internal monologue is fine, consider how these impressions affect Seris’s *speech*. Does her voice become strained? Does she stumble over words? A brief, involuntary gasp would be more impactful than a description of her heart pounding.
*   **Seris’s internal acknowledgement ("This wasn't a simple echo…intentional") feels like telling, not showing.** This could be subtly woven into her questioning gaze at Hemlock. Instead of *thinking* it's intentional, have her *ask* a question that implies it. ("You knew this would happen, didn't you?")
*   **Hemlock's lack of reaction is…suspiciously perfect.** He's *too* still. A slight adjustment of his posture, a barely perceptible tightening of his lips, would make him feel more real and add to the tension.
*   **The phrase "tucked the image of the serpent into the back of her mind" is a bit cliché and tells us something we should feel through her actions.** Consider a more visceral reaction – a shiver, a tightening of her grip on her cloak, a fleeting expression of fear.

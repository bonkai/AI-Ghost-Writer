## Scene 24

Spiced wine and woodsmoke clung to the narrow alley, a deceptive sweetness. Seris didn’t feel fear, but a prickling awareness – not of being watched, but assessed. Marius hadn’t pursued openly. He’d vanished, and the silence tightened around her, a pressure she tasted as metallic on her tongue. Her pulse quickened.

Kaelen led her through a hidden archway into a dimly lit courtyard. Silent figures in dark clothing moved with practiced precision. An apprentice stumbled, scattering scrolls, but the reaction wasn't anger, swift efficiency – a choreography of control. A chill crawled across Seris’s skin. This wasn’t just a sanctuary; it was a fortress.

“He’s testing our reactions,” Kaelen murmured, his voice barely audible above the courtyard’s subtle hum.

Seris scanned the faces of the Circle’s members, breath catching. “How did he know we were connected? I haven't spoken a word.” A muscle ticked in her jaw. It wasn't just knowledge; it was anticipation. He’d been waiting for this connection, for *her*.

Kaelen pushed open a heavy, iron-bound door, revealing a chamber filled with ancient texts and strange instruments. He gestured to a complex astrolabe, brass gleaming in the dim light. “They sought understanding, not control. A subtle but important distinction.”

Seris traced the lines of the astrolabe. “What were they hoping to achieve?”

“Understanding. And perhaps…influence.” Kaelen led her deeper. A faint tremor ran beneath her feet. 

“Someone’s following,” she whispered.

Kaelen didn’t break stride. “Let them come.” He pushed open a concealed door, revealing a narrow alleyway emptying onto a bustling street. “This path will be fraught with danger. Marius will be watching. We need to lose them, then head for Firepeak.”

As they walked, Seris couldn’t shake the feeling they weren’t being hunted, but guided. She risked a glance over her shoulder. A man in a dark coat, face obscured by a wide-brimmed hat, met her gaze. He didn’t react, but she knew, with chilling certainty, he was watching. And he knew they knew. He wasn't observing; he was waiting. In his eyes, not malice, but pity. It felt wrong, condescending. 

She stopped abruptly, forcing Kaelen to halt. “That man…” she murmured, tilting her head. “He’s not trying to *stop* us.”

Kaelen followed her gaze, expression tightening. “He’s playing a game,” he said, low. “And we’re the pieces.” He gripped her arm, urging her forward. “Don't meet his eyes. Move, now.”

She allowed him to pull her along, but lingered with another glance. “It’s not just that he *can* let us walk into a trap,” she said, voice tight. “It’s that he *wants* us to. He *expects* us to.”

Kaelen quickened their pace. “Then let’s defy his expectations.” He glanced at her, something unreadable in his eyes. “But be ready. Firepeak isn’t just a destination. It’s a reckoning.” He paused. “And he’s likely already prepared for us to defy him.”

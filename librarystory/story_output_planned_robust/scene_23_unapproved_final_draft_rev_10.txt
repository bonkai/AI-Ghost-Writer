## Scene 23

The workshop smelled of oiled steel and dried herbs. A sharp rap at the door broke the quiet. Seris ran a honing stone along a scalpel, the rasp deliberate and rhythmic. Rhys, ostensibly organizing charts, traced patterns on the paper with a fingertip, his gaze flicking to the door with each knock. Dark circles shadowed his eyes, a testament to sleepless nights.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her voice low, gaze fixed on the sharpening stone.

“A merchant. Heard you two had…an eye for the land. Looking for specific charts,” a gravelly voice replied.

Rhys exchanged a quick glance with Seris, then moved to open the door, pausing just short of fully revealing the figure beyond. A man stood silhouetted against the dim corridor, a wide-brimmed hat obscuring his features. He didn’t offer a hand, simply stepped inside, filling the small space with a subtle chill. The scent of woodsmoke and something faintly metallic clung to him.

“I understand you’ve both…traveled extensively in the peaks,” the merchant said, his voice a low rasp. He placed a weighted pouch onto the workbench, the sound muffled by the thick wood. “Detailed charts are…valuable. Particularly those indicating geological anomalies.”

Rhys narrowed his gaze, subtly shifting to block the merchant’s view of a half-unrolled map depicting a network of caves. “Anomalies? That’s a broad term.”

The merchant’s lips curved into a fleeting smile, but it didn’t reach his eyes. “Let’s say I’m interested in places where the earth…remembers. Where things lie dormant, waiting to be awakened.” His gaze drifted to Seris, lingering on the intricate knotwork of her tunic, then tracing the line of her jaw with unsettling precision.

Seris traced a botanical illustration with a calloused fingertip, her movements slow and deliberate. “We document the region’s flora. Certain rare specimens thrive in unique geological conditions. It requires extensive surveying, a meticulous understanding of the terrain.” She didn't meet his gaze.

The merchant chuckled, a dry, brittle sound. “Botanists who chart forgotten paths. Convenient.” He tapped the pouch. “A portion of my payment, for your…discretion? A finder’s fee, if you will.”

Rhys’s eyes met Seris’s, a flicker of desperation in them. He subtly pressed his lips together, a silent plea. Before he could speak, Seris raised a hand, a barely perceptible gesture that silenced him. 

“We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice even and measured. “However, we know individuals who might. Individuals who understand the…delicacy of certain knowledge.” She finally met the merchant’s gaze, her eyes the color of flint. “Such expertise commands a substantial premium.”

A beat of silence stretched, thick and tense. The merchant’s gaze swept over Rhys, assessing his anxiety, then returned to Seris, gauging her resolve. He noticed the way Rhys’s hand tightened on a quill, the subtle tension in his shoulders. “Intriguing.” He paused, then leaned forward, lowering his voice. “I need information regarding a specific cave system. A place where the stone…bleeds. And I need it quickly. I’m not interested in protracted negotiations.”

Seris didn't flinch. “We don’t *sell* locations, merchant. We offer consultation. We assess the risks, the potential consequences of disturbing these places, and we advise accordingly.” She gestured to a cluttered table covered in dried plants and geological samples. “Our work isn’t about profit. It’s about preservation.”

The merchant’s lips thinned. “Preservation? Or obstruction?”

Seris’s gaze remained unwavering. “Both, if necessary.” She glanced at Rhys, a silent command, then turned back to the merchant. “And a significant down payment. Before we even discuss the *possibility* of locating this…cave system. Enough to cover our time, our expertise, and a guarantee that you won’t simply circumvent us and seek this information elsewhere.”

The merchant’s eyes hardened. “I dislike being delayed.” He gestured towards a stool with a gloved hand. “And I *especially* dislike being lectured.” He paused, his gaze lingering on Rhys, a silent warning. “There are always…alternative routes to the peaks. And some paths…are best left untrodden.”

Seris met his gaze, unwavering. “Our price isn’t merely coin,” she said, her voice low and steady. “It’s assurance. Assurance that this information won’t be used to disturb what lies sleeping. Assurance that those who shared it with us remain…unburdened. And a binding agreement—in writing—that any disturbance will be conducted with the utmost care and respect for the natural order.” She glanced at Rhys, a subtle tightening of her jaw. “And a complete accounting of your intentions.”

The merchant studied her for a long moment, his expression unreadable. He finally let out a slow breath. “You drive a hard bargain, botanist.” He reached inside his cloak and produced a small, sealed scroll. “Perhaps…we can begin drafting terms.” He placed the scroll on the workbench, a silent olive branch. “But understand this: time is of the essence.”

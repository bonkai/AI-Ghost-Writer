The roar of the crowd was a dull pressure against Kaelen’s skull, filtered through the headset. Onscreen, ‘Nyxborn,’ a wraith in obsidian, moved as a blur—less a character controlled, more an extension of his will. Three opponents remained in the Ascension Gauntlet. He didn’t *see* enemies, but shifting probabilities, predicting each move before it began. 

Fingers danced across the keyboard, not playing, but *commanding*. A blink, a leap defying gravity, spectral blades cascading—Nyxborn flowed through the digital chaos, leaving trails of dissolving code. Dust motes swirled in the dim light of his room, illuminated by the screen’s glow. Empty energy drink cans formed a precarious tower on his desk, a half-eaten sandwich a forgotten monument to a life lived indoors.

“He’s a goddamn machine,” a commentator’s voice boomed, lost in the frenzy.

Kaelen barely registered it. Years spent honing this skill had cost him more than sleep. A tremor in his left hand, quickly suppressed, went unnoticed. He wasn’t after glory, but understanding—unraveling the game’s logic, *becoming* the code. A persistent ache behind his eyes, easily dismissed as eyestrain, throbbed with each rapid keystroke.

Two opponents fell. Only ‘Grimfang’ remained.

Grimfang unleashed a desperate assault—explosions, a monstrous beast. Kaelen anticipated it, weaving through the chaos. He saw the opening—a flicker of vulnerability.

He initiated ‘Void Shift’—teleportation, existing outside reality. The screen flashed white. He reappeared behind Grimfang, blade poised.

But the ache behind his eyes intensified, blossoming into a searing pressure. The room swam at the periphery of his vision, the edges blurring. He tried to focus, to ignore it, but a cold sweat slicked his palms.

The screen flickered, then fractured into static. He glanced down, expecting the keyboard, but saw only a spreading darkness clouding his vision. The pressure in his chest tightened, stealing his breath. 

The roar of the crowd warped, distant and distorted. A single word echoed: “Victory!”

Then, silence. A sickening lurch, not within the game, but a falling sensation into an absolute void. And a final, searing pain that wasn't digital at all.

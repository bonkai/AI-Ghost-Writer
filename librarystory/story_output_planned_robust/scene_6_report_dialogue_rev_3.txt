*   **Repetitive Internal Thought/Narration:** The final paragraph *exactly* repeats the realization from earlier ("I realized I wasn’t communicating…") and the description of the lingering taste of blood. This is narration *instead* of dialogue or internal monologue that advances the scene. Cut it. Trust the reader to remember.
*   **Hemlock’s Monologues:** Hemlock delivers a *lot* of exposition and veiled threats. While his character is clearly intended to be enigmatic and controlling, the length of his speeches feels unnatural. Break these up. Have him say *less* and rely more on his body language and the *implication* of his words. For example, the entire speech about ambition and exceeding station could be trimmed to: “Some secrets are best left buried, Initiate.” The rest is implied.
*   **Initiate’s Stammering/Weak Responses:** The “Just admiring the architecture” line is *very* weak and feels like a placeholder. While a character might lie, it should be more believable, or the *failure* of the lie should be more impactful. Consider something that *attempts* a more sophisticated deception, or a desperate attempt to change the subject.
*   **Overuse of Internal Sensation:** While the physical sensations (tasting blood, copper, feeling the stone) are good for establishing the intensity of the connection, they become repetitive. Not every attempt needs a new physical manifestation.
*   **Subtext Opportunity - Hemlock’s Pity/Triumph:** You correctly identify the ambiguity of Hemlock's final expression. *Lean into that.* Instead of explicitly wondering what it is, show it through his actions. A very slight incline of his head, a barely perceptible shift in his gaze, a tightening of his grip on something – these are more effective than internal questioning.
*   **“Please. I need to understand.”** This final plea is weak and doesn’t feel earned. After all the resistance, the pain, and Hemlock’s warnings, a simple “Please” feels…flat. Consider a more desperate, emotionally raw expression of need, or even *no* final attempt – letting the silence speak for itself.
* **Indirect Characterization:** The dialogue *does* effectively show the power dynamic between the Initiate and Hemlock. He is clearly in control, and she is intimidated. This is a strength.

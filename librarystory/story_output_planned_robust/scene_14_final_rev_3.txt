## Scene 14 - Draft 3

The scent of aged parchment and dust hung in the library. Seris traced a finger across the star chart, the constellation Cygnus a pale echo of the blight staining the kingdom. Rhys meticulously ground dried herbs, the rasp of the pestle a sharp counterpoint to the oppressive silence. He measured a pinch of moonpetal, then hesitated, his brow furrowed in concentration.

“A delicate balance,” Rhys murmured, presenting a small pouch of blended powder. “Neutralize the blight without harming the surrounding flora.”

Seris examined the mixture. *He craves recognition,* Kaelen’s thought brushed against her mind, a subtle nudge rather than a direct statement. 

Footsteps approached, slow and deliberate.

Seris’s hand instinctively tightened, not on a hidden dagger, but subtly shifting her weight, preparing to move. Master Valerius emerged from the labyrinthine stacks, his face an impassive mask. He didn’t glance at Rhys, didn’t acknowledge the work spread across the table. His gaze fixed on Seris, assessing.

“Master Valerius,” Seris greeted, her tone polite but firm.

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, lingering on the charts. “I trust you are making appropriate use of the library’s resources. Some knowledge is best left undisturbed.” His eyes narrowed, focusing on the overlapping constellation charts. A tremor ran through his hand, barely perceptible, but Seris saw it – a fleeting flicker of something suppressed. His jaw tightened.

“What precisely are you researching?”

Seris met his gaze, her heart quickening only slightly. “Celestial cartography, Master Valerius. We’re studying historical interpretations of the constellations, and their potential connection to the land’s health.” She kept her explanation concise, devoid of unnecessary detail.

Valerius’s lips curved into a thin, skeptical smile. “Indeed. A fascinating subject. Though I caution you against delving into matters beyond your comprehension.” His gaze flickered to the herbs, then back to Seris, and for a fleeting instant – a single, stark moment – his eyes widened with something akin to fear. It wasn’t a panicked widening, but a flash of *recognition* – as if he’d seen this path before. The expression vanished almost immediately, replaced by his usual impassive mask, but the image burned in Seris’s mind.

*He knows more than he lets on. Something significant. We’re close,* Kaelen’s thought was sharper now, edged with urgency, but tempered with a sense of caution. *But he’s testing her, gauging her resolve.*

Rhys, sensing the tension, stepped forward, holding out the pouch of herbs. “We’re simply trying to understand the ancient connections between the stars and the land, Master Valerius. To find a solution to the blight. We believe we’ve made a breakthrough.”

Valerius’s gaze flickered to the pouch, then back to Seris. “A ‘breakthrough,’ you say?” He paused, his eyes boring into her. “Be careful what you seek, young scholar. Some doors are best left unopened.” He didn't linger. He turned and walked away, disappearing back into the stacks with a deliberate pace.

Seris exhaled slowly, her muscles coiled but not tense. 

*He’s not trying to intimidate, he’s assessing. He wants to see how far we’ll push, if we’ll back down at a warning,* Kaelen’s thought was a low hum in her mind.

A cold dread settled in her stomach, not from fear, but from the realization of the stakes. She glanced at Rhys, his brow furrowed with concern.

*We need to change course. Not leave, but shift our focus. He suspects the constellations are the key. We need to lead him elsewhere,* Kaelen urged.

Seris didn't hesitate. “Rhys, we’ve reached a temporary impasse with the celestial charts. Let’s examine the historical texts on blight remedies. See if we can find any corroborating evidence.” She swiftly began rolling the charts, her movements decisive, but not rushed.

Rhys, sensing the subtle shift in strategy, followed suit. “What’s wrong?”

“He’s onto something. We need to give him a different scent to follow.”

They moved towards a different section of the library, the silence now charged with a new awareness. As they reached the archway, Seris risked one last glance back.

Valerius stood at the far end of the aisle, a dark silhouette amidst the towering shelves. He wasn’t examining the shelves. He was watching *them*, but his gaze was now subtly questioning, almost… confused.

A shiver ran down Seris’s spine. They were no longer simply scholars seeking knowledge. They were players in a game, and the stakes were far higher than they’d initially imagined.

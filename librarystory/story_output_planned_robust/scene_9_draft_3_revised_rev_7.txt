## Draft 3 - Scene 9

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light illuminating the alcove, the air thick with the scent of aged paper and a faint, coppery tang. Seris traced a finger along the spine of a crumbling tome, brittle leather flaking beneath her touch. “Before kingdoms, before gods… the stories say there were currents. Understandings. The Echo… it holds fragments, doesn’t it?” She glanced across the shadowed shelves, then turned to Kaelen. “The Church didn't condemn the Order overnight. It began subtly.”

She gestured to a manuscript detailing the Church’s rise. “They didn't immediately declare war. They started with isolation, discrediting the Order’s teachings. Look here.” She pointed to a faded illustration depicting Order scholars studying celestial charts. “Accusations of consorting with… things best left undisturbed. It was a campaign of whispers, of eroding trust.”

Seris touched a nearby book, a faint tremor running through its spine. She closed her eyes, concentrating. “The Order’s magic wasn’t about *doing*… it was about *listening*. About understanding the currents, the Echo itself. They sought knowledge of what *was*, not what *could be*.”

“Listening to what?” Kaelen asked.

Seris’s brow furrowed. “The Echo isn't a simple archive. It’s a shattered reflection of countless possibilities, fragments of what was, what could be… and what should remain lost.” She opened her eyes, meeting Kaelen’s gaze. “Imagine a tapestry, woven with threads of every possible moment. The Order sought to understand the pattern, while the Church fears any deviation from their single, prescribed design.”

“Fractured how?” Kaelen pressed.

Seris ran a hand over the manuscript, her fingers tracing the faded ink. “Threads fraying, twisting… poisoning the whole cloth. Not malice, but dissonance. Echoes bleeding into each other, distorting the truth. It’s like a song played on a broken instrument – the melody is still there, but warped and unsettling.”

“Unravel the world?” Kaelen asked, a note of concern in her voice.

Seris stopped her restless pacing, her gaze fixed on the swirling patterns within the aged paper. “Not instantly. But a weakened weave… creates tears. Allows things *through*. Things that shouldn’t be.” She paused, a flicker of fear in her eyes. “The Church claims to be protecting us from these ‘things,’ but…”

“So they’re protecting us?”

Seris shook her head. “Protecting themselves, primarily. They claim to be the sole arbiters of truth, guardians of Eldoria. They see the Echo as chaos, a threat to their authority. Centuries of erasing the Order’s teachings, rewriting history to suit their narrative.” She leaned against a bookshelf, exhaustion washing over her. “And the Firepeak Mountains? The rumors of a hidden archive?”

The pages stilled, then rippled faster, a gust of wind rattling the windowpanes and extinguishing the candle. Seris jumped, her heart pounding. Darkness descended. “The Firepeaks are a nexus point, where the veil between worlds is thin. The Order built a citadel there, safeguarding what remains of their knowledge.”

A gentle pressure touched her mind, not a voice, but a sense of urgency. *“A confluence. Fragments drawn together by your presence. What remains of the Order, seeking someone who will listen.”*

Seris swept her gaze across the shadowed alcove, dread tightening her chest. “Why me?”

*“The Church knows you’re close to the truth. They will be coming.”* The air grew frigid. A spectral image flickered briefly in the swirling ink – a hooded figure silhouetted against a burning city. It vanished as quickly as it appeared, leaving a lingering scent of smoke. 

The pages settled. A single word formed in the swirling ink, glowing with an ethereal light: *“Remember.”* Then, silence. A silence so profound it felt like a physical weight. The stone of the bookshelf felt subtly wrong – distorted, as if whispering secrets against her back. Seris wrapped her arms around herself, a cold spot on the stone sending a jolt up her arm. It felt… like a warning. She pressed her hand against the stone, and a faint tremor ran through the bookshelf, revealing a hairline crack in the wall behind it.

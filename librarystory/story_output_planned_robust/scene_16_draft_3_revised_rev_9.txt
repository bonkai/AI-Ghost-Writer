The library air thickened, pressing against Seris’s skin like a shroud. Dust motes danced in the shafts of light, each a tiny world poised to shatter. A tremor ran through the stone, vibrating up through her boots, into her teeth. She gripped a bookshelf, the ancient wood humming with discordant energy.

Rhys, studying a faded map, glanced up, his brow furrowing. He didn’t reach for his dagger, but subtly shifted his pack, testing its balance. His gaze met hers, a silent question laced with concern.

The library *shifted*. Not visibly, but at the edge of her vision, lines blurred, stretched, then snapped back. The scent of old parchment twisted, overlaid with a metallic tang – ozone and blood. A pressure descended, the feeling of a vast network beneath her feet, straining against its bonds.

A discordant note fractured the harmony. Luminous threads of energy flickered, dimmed. A thread snapped, and darkness seeped into the crack, widening it. Each loss sent a sharp ache through Seris’s chest, a sympathetic pain. Static raised the hairs on her arms. She stumbled, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue, and pressed a hand to her temple.

Rhys caught her, his grip firm, anchoring her against the unsettling waves. “Seris?”

Kaelen’s consciousness brushed against hers – not words, but a raw, echoing resonance, the weight of ancient power and a desperate plea. An image flashed – a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian, pulsing, *cracking*. She gasped, tightening her grip on Rhys’s arm.

He felt the tremor run through her, his grip tightening. “What do you see?”

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts, the words tasting like ash. Kaelen’s panic surged, a desperate, wordless cry. The image sharpened – obsidian, stars, cracks widening. *Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…* A cold wind whipped across her skin, the smell of sulfur stinging her nostrils. A resonant thrumming vibrated through her bones. She squeezed her eyes shut, knuckles white against Rhys’s jerkin.

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus, and slowly opened her eyes. She looked at Rhys, then away, gathering her thoughts. The vision felt…imprinted, not merely seen.

Rhys waited, his expression unreadable, but his hand remained firmly supporting her. He didn’t press, didn’t ask again, but his quiet presence was a steady anchor.

“Firestone Mountains?” he asked, his voice tight with concern.

“It’s…the source,” she said, her voice strained, the words feeling given, not reasoned. “Something’s contained within it. Ancient. Terrible. The seal is failing. It’s worst there.”

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before it breaks free.”

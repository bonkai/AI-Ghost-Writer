## Draft 3 - Scene 9

Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light illuminating the alcove, the air thick with the scent of aged paper and a faint, coppery tang. Seris traced a finger along the spine of a crumbling tome, brittle leather flaking beneath her touch. “Before kingdoms, before gods… the stories say there were currents. Understandings. The Echo… it holds fragments, doesn’t it?” She paused, glancing across the shadowed shelves, a subtle tension tightening her shoulders. 

Kaelen’s presence felt like a shift in the air, a subtle pressure against her senses. She turned to a manuscript detailing the Church’s rise, finding his attention already there. “They branded the Order heretical, accusing them of forbidden magic. But it wasn't a sudden condemnation.”

She touched the manuscript, her fingers lingering on a faded illustration. “It began subtly. Whispers of dangerous practices, accusations of consorting with… things best left undisturbed. The Church didn’t immediately declare war. They started with isolation, with discrediting the Order’s teachings.” The pages turned themselves, settling on a passage describing the Order’s practices. 

Seris touched a nearby book, a faint tremor running through its spine. She closed her eyes, concentrating. “The Order’s magic wasn’t about *doing*… it was about *listening*. About understanding the currents, the Echo itself.” 

A pause. The air grew noticeably colder. “Listening to what?” she breathed, more to herself than to any presence.

A fractured sensation – not an answer, but a feeling of immense weight, like holding a shattered mirror. *Memories are dangerous,* the feeling implied. “The Echo isn’t a simple archive,” Seris explained, her voice low. “It’s a shattered reflection of countless possibilities, fragments of what was, what could be… and what should remain lost.”

“Fractured *how*?” she pressed, her voice tight.

The air chilled further, the pages rippling faster. “Threads fraying, twisting… poisoning the whole cloth. Not malice, but dissonance. Echoes bleeding into each other, distorting the truth.” The sensation wasn’t delivered as words, but as a chilling visual – a vibrant tapestry unraveling into grey threads, the colors fading.

Seris’s breath hitched. “Unravel the world?” She stopped her restless pacing, her gaze fixed on the swirling patterns, a flicker of fear in her eyes.

“Not instantly. But a weakened weave… creates tears. Allows things *through*. Things that shouldn’t be.”

“So the Church isn’t just suppressing knowledge… they’re protecting us?” A strange weight settled on her chest, unease growing. She looked away from the swirling patterns, unable to meet their unsettling gaze.

“Protecting themselves, primarily. They claim to be the sole arbiters of truth, guardians of Eldoria. They see the Echo as chaos, a threat to their authority. Centuries of erasing the Order’s teachings, rewriting history to suit their narrative.”

Leaning against a bookshelf, Seris felt exhaustion wash over her, the weight of centuries pressing down. “And the Firepeak Mountains? The rumors of a hidden archive?”

The pages stilled, then rippled faster, a gust of wind rattling the windowpanes, extinguishing the candle. Seris jumped, her heart pounding. Darkness descended. “The Firepeaks are a nexus point, where the veil between worlds is thin. The Order built a citadel there, safeguarding what remains of their knowledge.”

A sensation – not a voice, but a presence within her mind, a gentle nudge. *“A confluence. Fragments drawn together by your presence. What remains of the Order, seeking someone who will listen.”*

Seris swept her gaze across the shadowed alcove, dread tightening her chest. “Why me?”

*“The Church knows you’re close to the truth. They will be coming.”* The air grew frigid. A spectral image flickered in the swirling ink – a hooded figure, silhouetted against a burning city. It vanished as quickly as it appeared, leaving a lingering scent of smoke. 

The pages settled. A single word formed in the swirling ink, glowing with an ethereal light: *“Remember.”* Then, silence. A silence so profound it felt like a physical weight. The stone of the bookshelf felt subtly wrong – distorted, as if whispering secrets against her back. Seris wrapped her arms around herself, shivering, the chill deeper than the cold air, a premonition settling in her bones. She ran a hand over the rough stone, a single, cold spot sending a jolt up her arm. It felt… like a warning.

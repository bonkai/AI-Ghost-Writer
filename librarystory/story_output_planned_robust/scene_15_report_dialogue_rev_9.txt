*   **Seris’s initial murmur (“‘Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’”) feels overly dramatic and poetic for a practical cartographer actively *examining* a map.** It reads more like narration *about* her discovery than something she’d naturally say while working. Consider simplifying it to a more focused observation about the text.
*   **Rhys's "Something?" is *extremely* minimal.** While his taciturn nature is established, it feels a bit *too* short, even for him. It lacks a subtle cue to his concern or curiosity. Perhaps "Something's changed?" or "What have you found?" would add a touch more engagement.
*   **The exchange about “bound” feels slightly forced.** Seris states her feeling without much build-up. While internal feelings are valid, a beat of *showing* her apprehension (a quickened breath, a hand tightening) before *telling* Rhys would be more impactful.
*   **Rhys’s response, “We’ll be ready,” is efficient, but lacks nuance.** It’s a perfectly functional line for his character, but it doesn’t *reveal* much. Consider adding a small action *with* the line – checking the sturdiness of the poker, scanning the room – to subtly show his protective instincts and preparation.
*   **The dialogue is generally efficient, but leans towards telling rather than showing.** There’s a lot of internal state communicated directly. Opportunities for subtext – letting actions and small reactions speak for the characters – are missed. For example, instead of Seris saying "I think… there’s more to this map than we initially thought," *show* her frustration and focused concentration through her actions and a slight change in her breathing.
*   **While not strictly *bad*, the dialogue feels a little flat.** It's functional, but lacks spark or memorable phrasing. Injecting a little more individual voice – a quirk of speech, a habitual phrase – could elevate it.

Dust motes danced in the lamplight. Seris deepened the shading on the Sunstone Bloom’s petals, the crimson stain mirroring the pressure building within her.

Rhys traced a finger across a faded illustration of a precarious rock face on Firepeak. He didn’t meet her eyes. “Landslides are common after rainfall,” he said, his voice clipped.

A shadow fell across their worktable, gradually obscuring the lamplight. Valerius didn’t approach directly, but lingered, observing their workspace. The air seemed to thicken.

“Still chasing whispers?” Valerius asked, his voice smooth, carrying a subtle weight.

Rhys offered Seris’s sketch with a tilt of his hand – a deflection.

Valerius’s gaze followed, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. He studied it for a long moment, his expression unreadable. A flicker crossed his face, then vanished. He didn’t comment on the sketch itself.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly. “A rare specimen. Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon – a phenomenon rarely witnessed.” The statement implied a knowledge beyond simple botany.

Seris met Valerius’s gaze. “We’ve found accounts of its use in ancient healing practices, among the mountain tribes. We’re hoping to corroborate those claims.” She kept her voice even.

“Firepeak Mountain is dangerous. Many have ventured into those peaks and never returned.” The scent of his sandalwood cologne drifted towards them, cloying and unsettling.

Rhys’s fingers tightened on the tome’s page. “We understand the risks. We’ve been meticulously researching the terrain, the weather patterns…” He focused intently on the faded illustrations.

Valerius’s gaze swept over them, assessing. The silence stretched. Then, a slow smile touched his lips, but didn't reach his eyes.

“Perhaps you are prepared.” He paused. “Though the mountain…tests preparations.” His eyes locked with Seris’s, holding her gaze for a beat too long.

Seris held his gaze, her expression neutral, but a pulse throbbed at her temple.

Valerius’s smile widened, unsettlingly cold. “I look forward to hearing of your discoveries…or misfortunes.” He turned, his cloak swirling around him, and moved away.

Rhys’s hand briefly covered hers, a fleeting pressure, before he returned to the text. The crimson stain on the vellum seemed to pulse. A coldness settled over Seris, a certainty that Valerius hadn’t believed them, and that their journey had become far more dangerous.

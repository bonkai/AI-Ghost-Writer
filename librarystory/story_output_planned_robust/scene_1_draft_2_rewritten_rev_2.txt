The roar of the crowd was a dull pressure against Kaelen’s skull, filtered through the headset. Onscreen, ‘Nyxborn,’ a wraith in obsidian, moved as a blur—less a character controlled, more an extension of his will. Three opponents remained in the Ascension Gauntlet. He didn’t *see* enemies, but shifting probabilities, predicting each move before it began. A tremor in his left hand, barely noticeable at first, caused Nyxborn to stumble momentarily during a crucial dodge.

Fingers danced across the keyboard, not playing, but *commanding*. A blink, a leap defying gravity, spectral blades cascading—Nyxborn flowed through the digital chaos, leaving trails of dissolving code. The initial tremor returned, sharper now, forcing a slight adjustment in his grip. He compensated, but the precision felt…off. 

“He’s a goddamn machine,” a commentator’s voice boomed, lost in the frenzy.

Kaelen barely registered it. Years spent honing this skill had cost him more than sleep. The ache behind his eyes, previously dismissed as eyestrain, pulsed with each rapid keystroke, blurring the edges of his vision. He ignored it, forcing his focus back to the game.

Two opponents fell. Only ‘Grimfang’ remained.

Grimfang unleashed a desperate assault—explosions, a monstrous beast. Kaelen anticipated it, weaving through the chaos, but the ache flared, making his reaction time fractionally slower. He barely avoided a hit, Nyxborn taking minor damage. He saw the opening—a flicker of vulnerability.

He initiated ‘Void Shift’—teleportation, existing outside reality. The screen flashed white. He reappeared behind Grimfang, blade poised, but the pressure behind his eyes intensified, and a wave of dizziness washed over him. His fingers fumbled on the keyboard.

The screen flickered, then fractured into static. He glanced down, expecting the keyboard, but saw only a spreading darkness clouding his vision. The pressure in his chest tightened, stealing his breath. His grip slackened.

The roar of the crowd warped, distant and distorted. A single word echoed: “Victory!”

Then, silence. A sickening lurch, not within the game, but a falling sensation into an absolute void. And a final, searing pain that wasn't digital at all.

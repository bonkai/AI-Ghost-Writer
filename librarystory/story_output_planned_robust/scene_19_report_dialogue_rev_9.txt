*   **“It remembers.”** – While atmospheric, this feels a bit *too* cryptic as an opening line. It lacks grounding. Consider something slightly more descriptive, even if still metaphorical. Perhaps, “The mountain… it feels *old*.” or “This sketch…it feels like looking at a living thing.”
*   **“Lost how?”** – This is a perfectly functional question, but feels a little flat given the building tension. Seris is *feeling* the weight of ages. Perhaps something more evocative of that sensation? “Lost…like it was deliberately *taken*?” or “What was lost *to* the mountain?”
*   **Kaelen’s silence/non-verbal communication:** The reliance on “silent warning passing between them” is a bit of a tell, rather than a show. The dialogue *around* that moment is good (Kaelen’s tightening jaw, Seris’s awareness), but the explicit statement feels redundant. Trust the reader to interpret those cues.
*   **“We have to be careful.”** – This is a statement of the obvious, given the context. It doesn’t *reveal* anything about Seris’s character or her specific concerns. Consider what *specifically* she's careful *about*. Is it getting caught? The power of the mountain? The implications of the lost memories?
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The explanation of the Order forbidding remembering is a bit clunky. It's delivered as direct exposition. Could some of this be revealed through Seris’s internal thoughts, or through Kaelen hinting at the Order’s paranoia?
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is *competent*, but leans towards functional rather than deeply revealing or subtly layered. It establishes the mystery, but doesn’t fully capitalize on the opportunity for subtext and indirect characterization. The characters *say* what needs to be said, but rarely reveal much *beyond* that.

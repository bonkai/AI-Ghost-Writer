*   **Kaelen's initial line ("It remembers") is a bit too mystical/poetic.** While fitting his character, it feels like telling, not showing. Consider *how* he conveys this idea – perhaps by describing what *he* feels when looking at the sketch, rather than stating the mountain’s sentience directly.
*   **Repetitive questioning from Seris.** She asks "What do you mean?" and then "Lost how?" – these feel functional but lack nuance. Combine or rephrase to reveal more about her internal state. Is she skeptical? Intrigued? Fearful?
*   **Kaelen's explanation feels rushed.** The transition from “echoes of the past” to “it’s a key” and then to the Order’s motivations is a lot of exposition delivered quickly. Break it up. Perhaps reveal the Order's fear *before* mentioning Firepeak as a key.
*   **"This isn’t about a rare healing plant, is it?" feels slightly contrived.** It's a very direct way to get confirmation of a plot twist. Consider having Seris realize this through Kaelen’s reaction or a subtle clue in his dialogue.
*   **Kaelen's "Always" is weak.** After a line about danger and Valerius, a simple "Always" feels insufficient. A more protective or determined response would be more impactful. Something like, “We will be,” or “I won’t let anything happen to you.”
*   **The silent understanding is a cliché.** While a look can convey much, simply stating they share one feels lazy. *Show* the understanding through their body language or a shared, subtle reaction to the metallic scent.
*   **The dialogue is generally *functional* but lacks subtext.** There’s a lot of direct information exchange. Consider what each character *isn’t* saying. What are their hidden motivations or fears? Injecting more of that will elevate the scene.
*   **The hand brush is fine, but the immediate extinguishing of the "spark" feels too abrupt.** A lingering moment, even a subtle hesitation, would create more tension and hint at a deeper connection.

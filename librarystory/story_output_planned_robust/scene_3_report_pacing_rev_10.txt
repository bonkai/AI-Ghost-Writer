*   **Internal Pacing:** The scene is *very* slow. While appropriate for the initial disorientation, it sustains this pace for too long. The repeated phrasing (“He didn’t ask, but felt…”, “He didn’t search *for*, but *allowed*…”) creates a rhythmic lull that, while intentional, becomes monotonous after a while. The constant internal focus and lack of external action slow momentum.
*   **Tension Arc:** The tension is almost entirely internal – the character’s confusion and disorientation. There’s little external conflict or pressure. The initial mystery is intriguing, but it plateaus. The “responses” from the library are too passive; they offer information without creating stakes or challenges. The scene lacks a clear escalation or a compelling reason for the reader to be anxious about what will happen next.
*   **Hooks & Transitions:** The opening is strong in establishing the unusual state. However, the scene lacks micro-hooks to keep the reader engaged throughout. Each “response” feels similar in tone and delivery, diminishing their impact. The ending is a gentle invitation, which is fine thematically, but doesn't create a strong desire to know what happens next.
*   **Specific areas:** Paragraphs 2-4 feel repetitive in establishing the character's new limitations and internal exploration. The constant emphasis on *not* doing things ("He didn't ask...") becomes a crutch. The section from "He knew, with sudden certainty..." to the end feels drawn out. The pursuit of the book lacks urgency.

To improve: Introduce small, escalating challenges or obstacles. Give the Library a more active role – perhaps a sense of judgment or withholding information. Vary the rhythm with brief, more dynamic descriptions. Inject a sense of urgency into the search for the book. Consider adding a hint of danger or a consequence for failure.

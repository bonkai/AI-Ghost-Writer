He wasn’t dissolving, not exactly. More an unraveling—threads of awareness pulled taut, then released. A fall, not down, but *inward*, into a space that wasn’t space, a silence that wasn’t silence. Then, a jarring halt. The chair, the monitor – ghosts of memory, fading with each passing moment.

He wasn’t *what* he was, either. He tried to flex a hand, shift weight, but found…nothing. No limbs, no body, only diffuse awareness, a point of consciousness adrift. Terrifying, yet…exhilarating. A strange lightness bloomed in the absence of weight, of form. He registered a fleeting sense of loss for solidity, quickly replaced by a growing disorientation.

The silence pressed, a velvet weight. But it wasn’t truly silent. Beneath the absence of sound, a subtle hum resonated, a vibration tickling awareness. He focused, seeking a pattern, a source. The hum grew, coalescing into a feeling – immense age, boundless knowledge. He felt watched. A prickle of unease settled over his awareness, sharper now, a deliberate scrutiny.

He reached out, not with a hand, but with will. He focused on a plain brown volume on a towering shelf, and *pushed*. Not physically, of course. He channeled disorientation, desperate need for solidity, into concentrated energy. A flicker of frustration sparked within him – wanting felt…wrong, somehow. As if desire itself was a forgotten language. The book didn’t move, but the air around it shimmered, and a dust mote danced in the unseen current.

He strained, pushing harder, and a single page within the book fluttered, then another. A distortion spread, like heat rising, and he felt a strange pull, a connection forming. He almost *tasted* the knowledge – a metallic tang on his nonexistent tongue, followed by the faintest scent of aged paper and leather. The effort left him breathless, a dizzying wave of exhaustion.

A word solidified on the page, glowing with inner light: *Listen.*

Listen? To what? The silence? It wasn’t silent, not really. Beneath the absence of sound, the hum throbbed – a heartbeat in the vastness. He focused, and the hum resolved into a chorus of whispers, too faint to decipher. He directed a question towards the source of the whispers: *Where am I?*

The air around him seemed to thicken, to *respond*. A presence settled over his awareness, ancient and layered, like the rustling of countless pages. “You are a resonance, a fragment of awareness within the Great Library. A place where stories are not just told, but *lived*.”

He concentrated, directing a spark of defiance towards the presence: *What do you want from me?*

A dry, rustling sound, like parchment crumbling, echoed in his awareness. “Want? A quaint notion. We do not ‘want.’ We *are*. And you, little resonance, are…new. A potential echo.”

*What am I?* he thought, the question echoing with desperate need.

The presence didn’t immediately answer. A wave of images flooded his mind – fleeting glimpses of countless stories, lives lived, worlds imagined – a dizzying kaleidoscope. Knights battling dragons, lovers under starlit skies, cities rising and falling, galaxies swirling. Overwhelmed, he recoiled, struggling to regain control. 

“You are a reader,” the presence finally said, its tone gentle, a soothing balm. “And now, you are part of the story.”

A pause, then a final, enigmatic whisper. "Welcome home." A warmth spread through his awareness, a sense of belonging he hadn’t known he craved.

He tried again to force a sound, any sound, but found only the echoing silence of his own awareness. He focused all his energy, willing a response, but nothing came. The library held its breath, waiting. He pushed harder, desperation building, and a single word formed on another page, distant and faint: *Soon.* 

What did it mean? Soon what? The question hung unanswered in the vastness, a chilling promise in the echoing silence. He strained again, focusing all his will, and felt a faint tremor run through the shelves around him. A page fluttered, then another, and a single phrase, barely legible, appeared: *Patience, little echo. Your story…begins with listening.*

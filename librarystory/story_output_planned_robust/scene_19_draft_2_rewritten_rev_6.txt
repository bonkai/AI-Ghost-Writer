The library held its breath as the last patrons departed. Lamps cast long shadows, leaving the upper shelves veiled in darkness. Seris sketched, charcoal moving across the page, acutely aware of Valerius’s lingering presence nearby.

A disturbance in a lamplight beam. Kaelen. Then, a book’s spine flexed, a single page detaching and fluttering down to land open on the table. Not text, but a sketch – Firepeak Mountain, rendered not as geography, but as a being – jagged peaks like teeth, slopes etched with ancient power.

Seris traced the charcoal lines, a sense of immense age settling over her. “Beautiful,” she breathed, a warmth blooming on her skin. She glanced up, meeting Kaelen’s gaze across the room. A mirroring appreciation flickered in his eyes, held a moment longer than polite.

“It remembers,” he said, his voice a low murmur that seemed to resonate within her.

Seris’s fingers tightened on the table’s edge. “What do you mean?”

He moved closer, his movements fluid and silent. “The mountain isn’t merely rock and earth. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.” He paused, his gaze dropping to the sketch, then lifting to meet hers. “This isn’t just a depiction, Seris. It’s a key.”

A book slammed shut in the distance. Seris glanced towards the shadowed stacks, then back at Kaelen, a flicker of unease crossing her face.

“Lost how?” she asked, her voice barely a whisper.

He swept his gaze across the library, a subtle tension tightening his jaw. “The Order didn’t just forbid magic. They forbade *remembering*. They feared the past. Firepeak…it can unlock those memories.”

Seris’s breath caught. “This isn’t about a rare healing plant, is it?”

Another book slammed shut, closer this time. Kaelen’s gaze locked with hers, a silent warning passing between them. “No. It’s far older, far more dangerous.” He stepped closer still, lowering his voice. “Valerius suspects something. He’s a guardian of the forgetting. He’ll do anything to protect it.”

Seris closed her eyes, temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

Kaelen’s hand brushed hers, a fleeting touch that lingered a beat too long. A spark ignited, quickly suppressed by the library’s chill, but the warmth remained on her skin. “Always.”

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch. A delicate fragrance – crimson, perfectly formed – momentarily filled the air. They shared a look, a silent understanding passing between them. Then, a cold draft snaked through the library, extinguishing a nearby lamp and plunging a section of the shelves into deeper shadow. The scent of dust and old paper was suddenly…metallic. And from the darkness, a distinct *click* – the unmistakable sound of a latch releasing.

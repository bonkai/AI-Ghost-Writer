*   **Pacing & Tension:** The scene feels largely *tell*, not *show*. While the discovery of information is important, the pacing is too quick. The jump from astronomical chart to Gardener to suppressed knowledge to the Order and Valerius happens rapidly without building sufficient suspense or allowing the characters (and the reader) to *feel* the weight of each revelation. Slow down the reveals; let Seris and Rhys react more fully to each piece of information.
*   **Character Voice & Agency:** Rhys feels passive. He reacts, but doesn’t contribute meaningfully to the investigation. Give him more agency – perhaps he notices a detail Seris misses, or offers a different interpretation of the symbols. Seris’s internal state is described ("a wave of fear"), but not *felt*. Show her fear through her actions, physical sensations, and dialogue.
*   **Clarity of Stakes:** While we know the Order wants to suppress the truth, the *why* remains vague. The scene hints at danger, but doesn't fully establish the consequences of uncovering this lost knowledge. What specifically is the Order afraid of? What will happen if the truth "blooms"? Adding a concrete threat or consequence will significantly raise the stakes and increase the impact.

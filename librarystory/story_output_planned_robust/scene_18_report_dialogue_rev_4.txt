*   **Subtext & Indirect Characterization:** While the scene *shows* tension, much of it relies on stage direction (tremors, tightening grips). The dialogue itself is quite direct. For example, Seris stating they're "hoping to corroborate claims" is functional but lacks nuance. Consider *how* she says it – a defensive tone? A carefully constructed explanation? Show, don't tell, through word choice.
*   **Valerius's Dialogue:** Valerius’s lines are strong, but leaning heavily on ominous pronouncements. While effective, it’s a consistent pattern. Explore moments where his veiled threats are *almost* polite, or where he feigns genuine interest, creating a more unsettling contrast.
*   **Rhys's Avoidance:** Rhys’s dialogue is *too* focused on practical details. His deflection is clear from the stage directions, but the dialogue could reflect his anxiety more subtly. Perhaps a slightly rushed explanation, or a tendency to over-explain, revealing his discomfort.
*   **Repetition of Tension:** The constant emphasis on physical reactions (tremors, tightening grips) combined with Valerius’s pronouncements feels repetitive. Vary the *way* tension is conveyed. Let dialogue create tension through implication, veiled threats, or power dynamics, rather than solely relying on physical manifestations.
*   **"Misfortunes" Line:** The line "I look forward to hearing of your discoveries…or misfortunes" feels a bit cliché. Consider a more subtle threat, or a statement that appears complimentary on the surface but carries a darker implication.
*   **Seris’s Composure:** Seris maintains a “carefully neutral” expression and “even” voice throughout. While this establishes her control, it also makes her feel somewhat flat. Allowing *small* cracks in her composure – a slight hesitation, a carefully chosen word – would make her feel more real and the scene more engaging.

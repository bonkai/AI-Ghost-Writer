*   **Fragmented Dialogue – Overuse:** While the initial fragmented lines (“Deliberate… architectural.”) effectively convey initial processing/shock, it continues *through* the conversation with Elara. This becomes repetitive and weakens the impact. Consider streamlining – a single, impactful fragmented line is more effective than several.
*   **“I… I built it.” – Too Direct:** This is a *huge* reveal and feels incredibly flat delivered this way. It lacks the emotional weight it deserves. Instead of stating it, *show* it through a reaction, a physical manifestation of the memory, or a more evocative, incomplete phrase. For example: "It’s… familiar. The structure… I *know* it." followed by a physical reaction.
*   **“Instructions. A framework. And I remember… laying the first stone.” – Info-Dump Potential:** This feels like a character *telling* us information rather than *experiencing* it. It’s a good starting point for internal monologue, but needs to be integrated more subtly into the dialogue or revealed through action/reaction. Perhaps a physical gesture (reaching out as if to lay a stone) accompanies a fragmented line about it.
*   **Elara's Question – Underutilized:** Elara asks "What is it? What did you see?" but receives a vague answer. This is a missed opportunity. The character could *try* to explain, but be unable to articulate the experience fully, creating tension and highlighting the strangeness of the revelation. Or, the answer could be a *misdirection* – something the character *thinks* they saw, before the full memory surfaces.
*   **Lack of Emotional Exchange:** The dialogue is very focused on the ‘reveal’ and lacks a sense of emotional connection between the characters. Elara's reaction is completely absent. Even a simple, "What?" or a concerned "Are you alright?" would add depth.

The air in the Atheneum’s restricted section tasted of dust and decay, a scent Seris had grown accustomed to. Rhys meticulously copied glyphs from a crumbling scroll, his brow furrowed in concentration. She ran a finger along the spine of a particularly ancient tome, its leather cracked and brittle.

“Kaelen,” she murmured, her voice barely a whisper, “anything on the Bloom’s origins? Beyond the healing properties?”

A page fluttered in response, not from the book she’d indicated, but from a volume several shelves away. It fell open to a faded illustration of a complex astronomical chart, overlaid with symbols Seris didn’t recognize. Her breath hitched.

“That’s…unexpected,” she said, stepping closer, her fingers tracing the delicate lines of the chart. “What is this?”

*“A star map,”* Kaelen’s voice echoed in her mind, a soft rustling of parchment. *“But not of Eldoria’s night sky. Older. Much older. And the symbols…they relate to a system of magical resonance. A way of drawing power from celestial alignments.”*

Rhys glanced up, intrigued. He paused in his copying, his gaze sharp. “Celestial alignments? You think the Bloom’s growth is tied to the stars?”

“Possibly,” Seris said, tracing the lines of the chart. “But look at this.” She pointed to a cluster of symbols near the edge of the map. “These match glyphs found on the Bloom’s petals. I dismissed them as decorative, but…” She felt a prickling on her skin, a sense of something overlooked.

*“They aren’t decoration,”* Kaelen interjected, a sense of urgency coloring his mental voice. *“They’re a key. A way to unlock the Bloom’s true potential…and its history.”*

She scanned the text surrounding the chart, deciphering the archaic script. The words felt…heavy, imbued with a forgotten power. “It speaks of a ‘Gardener’…a being who cultivated not just plants, but memories. A keeper of lost lore.”

Rhys frowned, his eyes narrowed in concentration. He ran a hand through his hair, a habit when deep in thought. “Lost lore? What kind of lore?”

“The text is fragmented,” Seris said, her voice strained. “but it hints at a time before the Founding Kings, before the current pantheon of gods. A time when Eldoria…remembered something different.” She felt a tremor in her hands, a growing unease. 

“Remembered *what*?” Rhys pressed, his voice low.

Suddenly, a cold dread settled over her. “Kaelen, is there anything else? Anything about *why* this knowledge was lost?” Her heart hammered against her ribs.

A series of pages began to turn rapidly, almost violently, as if Kaelen were frantically searching. The air around them felt thick, charged with an unseen energy. Then, a single page stopped, displaying a chilling passage.

*“The Gardener’s knowledge was deemed…dangerous. A threat to the established order. It was purged, the memories suppressed, the Bloom’s true nature hidden. Those who sought to preserve it were…silenced.”*

Rhys’s hand tightened on the table, his knuckles white. He leaned forward, his gaze fixed on the page. “Silenced? By whom?”

Before Seris could answer, a new page turned, revealing a symbol—a stylized, closed eye—repeatedly emblazoned across the vellum. 

*“The Order of the Veiled Truth,”* Kaelen’s voice was strained, almost a whisper. *“They were the guardians of the old order, the enforcers of forgotten laws. And they still exist.”*

Seris’s blood ran cold. The Order of the Veiled Truth. She’d heard whispers of them in hushed tones—a shadowy organization said to control the levers of power from behind the scenes. Valerius was a member. A knot formed in her stomach.

“Valerius,” she breathed, the name a curse on her lips. “He knew we were looking into the Bloom. He was warning us…or threatening us.” She could almost feel his gaze on her, assessing, calculating.

Rhys’s gaze met hers, a grim understanding dawning in his eyes. He didn’t need her to explain. “He’s not just concerned about landslides, is he?”

*“They don’t want the truth to bloom,”* Kaelen’s voice resonated with a desperate urgency. *“They will do anything to keep the past buried. They believe some knowledge is too dangerous for the world.”*

Seris closed her eyes, a wave of fear washing over her, stealing her breath. She pressed her palms against the cool stone of the table, grounding herself. They weren’t just searching for ancient lore anymore. They were stumbling into a conspiracy that reached the highest echelons of power. And they were running out of time. She could feel the weight of it pressing down on her, a suffocating dread. “We need to be careful,” she whispered, her voice barely audible. “Very careful.”

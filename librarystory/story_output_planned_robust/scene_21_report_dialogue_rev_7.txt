*   **Subtext & Efficiency:** The dialogue feels *very* direct. While clarity is good, much of it lacks subtext. For example, Seris stating "The pursuit of knowledge is rarely commonplace" feels like telling rather than *showing* her defensiveness. Consider *how* she says it – a clipped tone, a challenging stare – to convey the same meaning with more impact.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** The repeated physical actions (Seris smoothing hair, Rhys sharpening his axe) are good, but could be *integrated* into the dialogue more. Instead of *telling* us Rhys is afraid through Kaelen’s thought, show it in a slip of the tongue, a hesitation, or a more aggressive tone than usual.
*   **Info-Dumps:** Marius's lines about the Bloom and its history feel like exposition delivered *to* the characters rather than a natural part of the conversation. Consider having Seris or Rhys ask leading questions that *prompt* Marius to reveal this information, or have it revealed through their reactions to something he observes.
*   **Repetition/Efficiency:** The ending is somewhat repetitive. Both Seris and Rhys have internal/silent moments emphasizing readiness. This could be streamlined. Perhaps Seris's silent resolve is shown through a *change* in her posture or a hardening of her gaze, allowing Rhys's readiness to stand alone.
*   **Voice/Authenticity:** Marius's lines are a bit…generic villainous. They lack a specific *flavor*. What makes *this* Inquisitor tick? What is his specific method of intimidation? A unique turn of phrase or a specific focus in his questioning would help.
*   **Flow & Rhythm:** The dialogue is functional, but lacks a natural ebb and flow. The characters respond to each other, but rarely *build* on what’s been said. More back-and-forth, with characters interrupting or challenging each other, would create a more dynamic scene.

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, her fingers tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure. Old Man Hemlock emerged from the gloom, immediately scanning the cavern’s perimeter. His hand instinctively rose to his ear, then lowered to a weathered stool, his movements quick and tense.

“I believed in potential,” he began, his voice brittle. He glanced at the mosaic, regret flashing across his face, then quickly looked away. “A world steeped in magic, not controlled blessings, but raw force. They didn’t fear the magic itself. They feared what people *remembered* with it.” He reached for a fragile scroll, hesitated, then snatched it back, his fingers trembling.

“Cities of shimmering crystal, powered by the lifeblood of the earth. Gardens blooming with bioluminescent flora, responding to thought…” He closed his eyes, a haunted look on his face. “They feared the questions it inspired.” He abruptly stood, pacing, his energy frantic. “They stripped me of my rank when I asked why knowledge was feared, exiled me for seeking truth.”

Seris’s jaw tightened. She pulled the Sunstone Bloom sketch partially from her pack, the crimson stain vivid against the worn canvas. “Why bury it?” she demanded, her voice sharp. She met his gaze, challenging him.

A faint scraping echoed from the tunnel entrance. Hemlock whirled around, hand flying to a hidden knife, his body tense. He scanned the darkness, assessing the threat. 

Seris moved, positioning herself *between* Hemlock and the tunnel entrance, a silent offer of protection. She didn’t simply assess; she *shielded*. Her hand tightened on her dagger, knuckles white.

Hemlock lowered his hand, but remained wary. He glanced at the sketch peeking from her pack, a flicker of warning in his eyes. “They believe the flower unlocks something. Something they desperately want to keep buried.”

“You risked everything to save these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls.

He scoffed. “A futile gesture, perhaps.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris, his expression grim. “They believe knowledge is control. And they were right, to a degree.”

Seris met his gaze, the crimson stain on the sketch feeling like a brand against her palm. She wasn’t chasing a flower. She was chasing a truth. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, she didn’t flinch, but her senses heightened, her grip tightening on the pack. 

Beneath the fear, a new resolve hardened within her. This wasn’t about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it. She tightened her grip on her pack. "What can we do?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper, but filled with determination.

Hemlock’s gaze flickered to the mosaic, then back to Seris. A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “We remember,” he said, his voice gaining strength. “We tell the stories. And we protect what remains.” He briefly covered her hand on her pack, a grounding touch, then released it. “The flower…it’s a seed for the future.” He paused, his eyes locking with hers. "But be warned. They won't let us plant it easily." He subtly nodded towards the tunnel, acknowledging a presence. "They are already listening."

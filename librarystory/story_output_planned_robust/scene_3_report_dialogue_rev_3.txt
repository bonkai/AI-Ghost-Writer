*   **Repetitive Internal Dialogue:** The constant reiteration of his inability to speak/make sound (“no voice, no mouth,” “only…thought,” trying to *speak* repeatedly) becomes a bit redundant. While establishing this limitation is important, it's hammered home too often. Consider *showing* this limitation through failed attempts at communication rather than constantly *telling* us about it.
*   **Voice's Detachment:** The voice of the Library is *very* passive and enigmatic. While this fits the setting, it risks feeling emotionally flat. Injecting a *hint* of curiosity, amusement, or even weariness into the voice’s responses could make it more engaging. Right now, it feels like an information dispenser.
*   **“Want”/“Are” Dichotomy:** The Library’s insistence on not “wanting” but simply “being” is a philosophical point, but it’s stated a bit abruptly. Consider subtly *demonstrating* this difference in its actions or responses, rather than just stating it as a principle.
*   **Overuse of “Awareness”:** The word “awareness” is used frequently. While appropriate given the character’s state, it can become noticeable. Look for opportunities to replace it with synonyms (consciousness, perception, sense) or rephrase sentences to avoid it.
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The flood of images – “knights battling dragons, lovers under starlit skies…” – feels like a quick way to establish the Library’s scope, but it’s a bit overwhelming and doesn't feel organically connected to the conversation. Perhaps the Library could offer *one* specific, evocative image as a taste, rather than a deluge.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The final line, "Welcome home," is a lovely touch, but feels slightly disconnected from the earlier, more detached tone of the Library. Explore if there's a way to foreshadow this warmth earlier in the conversation.

## Scene 21 - Draft 4

The workshop smelled of dried herbs and mountain dust, but a metallic tang now clung to the air, prickling at the back of Seris’s throat. The chill lingered after Inquisitor Marius departed, settling not on the stone floor, but in the spaces between breaths.

“He’s feeling for weaknesses,” Kaelen breathed, his hand instinctively tightening on the hilt of his dagger. Seris’s fingers traced the worn leather of the pouch containing Elara’s portrait, the movement less a caress, more a reflexive bracing – a subtle tightening of her grip betraying her unease.

Rhys shifted, not angling his body *between* Marius and Seris, but subtly blocking the view of the restricted ingredients with his broad frame – a casual obstruction, yet pointedly deliberate. He adjusted a precisely balanced scale, not with a display of expertise, but with a deliberate *slowness*, as if demonstrating a process too intricate for the Inquisitor to grasp. “The Bloom’s properties are…complex, Inquisitor. Significant potential, certainly, but its volatility demands meticulous study. A single miscalculation…” He trailed off, letting the implied danger hang in the air.

Seris smoothed a stray strand of hair, but didn’t meet Marius’s gaze. Instead, her fingers traced the line of her jaw, a self-soothing gesture she couldn’t quite suppress, but performed with a subtle defiance – a visible acknowledgement of his scrutiny, yet a refusal to be intimidated. “The pursuit of knowledge often leads us to…uncommon ground, Inquisitor.” A muscle ticked in her jaw, not from tension, but from contained frustration.

Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging details. He stopped on the half-dissected flower, the petals arranged with unsettling precision. His lips thinned, not in disapproval, but in something akin to…recognition. A flicker of something unreadable crossed his face. “An…unconventional arrangement. Almost…ceremonial.”

Seris met Marius’s gaze, her expression carefully neutral. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek, though your methods…differ.” A flicker crossed her eyes, quickly masked, but leaving a lingering challenge in their depths. She didn’t position herself *between* Marius and the research, but subtly shifted closer to the workbench, subtly reclaiming her space.

Rhys began cleaning his axe, not before Marius finished speaking, but *during* his assessment, the rhythmic scrape of steel on stone a deliberate counterpoint to the Inquisitor’s scrutiny. The motion was controlled, precise, but with an underlying energy – not of fear, but of coiled readiness. He maintained a focused intensity on his task, pointedly ignoring Marius, but with an awareness that bordered on predatory. *He’s testing us,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in Seris’s mind. *Seeing how we react under pressure.* Rhys’s gaze flickered toward the shadowed corner, then returned to his work, a silent acknowledgement of the shared risk.

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile, but it didn’t reach his eyes. “A convenient answer.” He didn’t circle Seris like a predator, but moved with a fluid grace, casually observing the room as if assessing its structural integrity. “The Bloom…it remembers. Echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. A time when such…flora held sway over more than just healing.” He paused, letting the implication sink in. “Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep…and remind us of what we’ve forgotten.”

He stepped back, his gaze lingering on a dried specimen of rare mountain moss, then swept over their workspace. “I will be watching you. Both of you. Not to *find* secrets, but to understand what you *choose* to hide.”

Rhys continued to sharpen his axe, the rasp a steady rhythm, his gaze fixed on the closed door, but with an alertness that suggested he was listening for more than just footsteps. The sharpening had stopped. He was ready.

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing Seris with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has debts, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to *forfeit* to settle them?” He offered a curt nod, then swept out, leaving a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being observed. A faint scent of ozone followed him – a subtle reminder of the Order’s power, but also of its inherent instability.

Silence descended. Seris wouldn’t let him. Not without a fight. She met Rhys's gaze, a silent understanding passing between them. The sharpening had stopped. He was ready. And so was she.

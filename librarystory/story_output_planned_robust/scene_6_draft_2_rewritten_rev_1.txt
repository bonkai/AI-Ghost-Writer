## Scene 6 - Draft 2

Seris traced the spine of a crumbling tome, feigning interest in its faded title. She needed a moment to recalibrate. The flash of stone and serpents from the silver clasp hadn’t just *felt* directed – it had felt… *seen*. Like a momentary acknowledgement, quickly withdrawn.

She risked a glance towards Hemlock. Still at his desk, seemingly absorbed in cataloging scrolls, but his stillness was a performance. He was watching, always assessing.

She tried to recapture the feeling, to *ask* what the serpent signified. Nothing. Just the scent of dust and the oppressive silence of the Atheneum. Frustration tightened her jaw. It wasn’t a memory, not exactly. It felt external, *imposed*. As if something had brushed against her mind, then retreated before she could grasp it.

“Beautiful bindings, aren’t they?” she called out, aiming for a casual tone. “So much history preserved within these walls.”

Silence. Hemlock didn’t even glance up. The lack of acknowledgement felt deliberate.

Seris focused, attempting to project a thought, a question, *anything* towards the source of the earlier impression. *Hello? Is anyone there?* A faint pressure built behind her eyes, a fleeting sense of… something responding. A spark, quickly extinguished. It was frustratingly ephemeral, like chasing a phantom.

She closed her eyes, concentrating. She pictured the serpent, the intricate carvings, amplifying the image, trying to draw the feeling back. This time, a flicker of something – not visual, but *emotional* – brushed against her mind. A sense of ancient weight, of cold stone and forgotten power. Then, a tightening, a resistance.

“Is everything alright, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice, smooth and unsettling, cut through her concentration. He hadn’t moved, but his gaze was now fixed on her, unblinking.

Seris forced her eyes open, blinking rapidly. “Just… admiring the architecture,” she stammered, feeling a flush creep up her neck. “This place is truly remarkable.”

She tried again, more forcefully this time, to send a mental probe outwards. *Can you hear me? Show me the serpent again.* She focused all her will, picturing the image with unwavering clarity. This time, a brief, almost painful spike of pressure behind her eyes. A fragmented impression – not of the serpent itself, but of a vast, echoing chamber, filled with shadows.

Then, a wall. A sudden, absolute block. It felt like slamming into something solid, something ancient and unyielding. She gasped, stumbling back and catching herself on a nearby bookshelf. Books shifted, one tumbling to the floor with a soft thud.

“Careful now,” Hemlock said, his voice laced with amusement. “These volumes are quite fragile.” He didn’t move to help.

Seris ignored him, pushing through the discomfort. She was so focused, so intent, that she didn’t notice the subtle shift in the air around her – a cooling, a stillness that seemed to absorb all sound. She *did* feel a faint vibration through the floor, a low hum resonating from the very foundations of the Atheneum.

This time, the resistance wasn't just a block, but an *active* force. It felt like being pushed back, rebuffed. A sense of warning, of *stay away*.

She recoiled, clutching her head. The pressure in her temples intensified, then abruptly vanished, leaving her feeling drained and disoriented. The humming faded.

“Perhaps you’ve been studying for too long,” Hemlock said, rising from his desk and slowly approaching her. “The Atheneum can be… overwhelming for those unfamiliar with its energies.” His voice was dangerously soft.

Seris backed away, her hand instinctively reaching for the hilt of the dagger hidden beneath her cloak. “I’m fine,” she said, her voice tight. “I just… felt a bit lightheaded.”

She tried one last time, a desperate plea sent outwards. *Please. I need to understand.*

Silence. Absolute, impenetrable silence. It was as if the Atheneum itself had deliberately shut her out, erecting a barrier around its secrets.

She realized, with a sinking feeling, that she wasn’t communicating with a person, or even a spirit. She was trying to reach something far older, far more powerful, and it clearly didn’t want to be disturbed. And it had made its displeasure known.

Hemlock stopped a few feet away, his shadow falling over her. “The Atheneum chooses who it reveals its secrets to,” he said, his voice a low murmur. “And it seems, Initiate, that it has not chosen you.”

He paused, his dark eyes boring into hers. “Perhaps it’s best to leave the secrets buried.” He didn't say "for now." The implication hung heavy in the air.



**Changes made and why:**

* **Stronger Sensory Details:** Added more details about the *feel* of the mental contact – coolness, vibration, pressure.  This helps the reader experience Seris’s attempts more vividly.
* **Active Resistance:** Changed the block from passive to *active*.  The Atheneum isn’t just refusing to respond; it’s actively pushing back.  This raises the stakes and makes the Atheneum feel more like a conscious entity.
* **Subtle Shifts in Hemlock's Behavior:**  Hemlock is less overtly amused and more subtly threatening.  His lack of help with the fallen book and the addition of "for now" in the final line add to the tension.
* **Internal Focus:**  More focus on Seris's internal experience of the mental contact. This makes her feel more vulnerable and emphasizes the strangeness of the situation.
* **Tightened Pacing:** Removed some redundant phrasing to create a more focused and tense scene.
* **Emphasis on "Secrets" vs. "Truth":** Changed some wording to emphasize that the Atheneum is guarding *secrets* rather than just withholding information. This hints at a more deliberate and potentially malicious intent.
* **More evocative language:** Replaced some bland phrasing with more descriptive language to create a richer and more immersive experience for the reader.




This revised draft aims to create a more unsettling and suspenseful scene, emphasizing the power and mystery of the Atheneum and the potential danger Seris is facing.

*   **Initial Engagement:** The opening is strong, immediately establishing tension with Hemlock's entrance and Seris's reaction. Good hook.
*   **Pacing – Vision Sequence:** The description of the visions (paragraphs 2-4) feels slightly *too* drawn out. While evocative, the prolonged imagery risks losing momentum. Consider condensing some of the descriptive phrases. The shift from awe to fear is effective, but could be sharpened with quicker cuts between images.
*   **Tension – Internal vs. External:** The tension is primarily internal (Seris’s emotional/psychological response). This is fine, but the external tension with Hemlock feels momentarily sidelined during the vision sequence. Brief interjections from Hemlock (a questioning glance, a subtle shift in posture) during the vision could maintain a low-level external pressure.
*   **Paragraph 5 – Slowing Momentum:** The paragraph beginning "Awakening..." feels redundant after the previous detailed vision. It restates feelings already established. This is a key spot to tighten.
*   **Build & Release:** The tension builds nicely throughout the vision and Seris’s immediate reaction. The dialogue with Hemlock provides a brief release, but then quickly re-establishes tension with his cryptic statements and probing gaze.
*   **Final Paragraph – Excellent Tension:** The final paragraph is the strongest – the suggestion of the sphere responding and the "eye watching" is genuinely unsettling. The ambiguity of the promise/warning is perfect.
*   **Overall:** The scene is well-structured, but could benefit from some tightening to maintain a more consistent pace. The internal focus is effective, but occasional reminders of Hemlock's presence would elevate the external tension.

*   **Tightness & Strain - Show, Don't Tell:** While you *tell* us Kaelen’s voice is “tight, strained,” the dialogue itself doesn’t fully *demonstrate* that internal state. Consider adding fragmented sentences, pauses (indicated by ellipses or dashes), or a more clipped tone. For example, instead of “It’s not a joke,” try “Not… a joke.” or “It's not just code.”
*   **Elara's Reactions - Passive:** Elara largely *reacts* to Kaelen’s pronouncements. Her single exclamation feels a little weak given the increasing intensity of the situation. Give her more agency. Perhaps a questioning "What do you mean by 'a key'?" or a more concerned “Kaelen, are you alright?” would add depth.
*   **Repetition of "It's..."**: The phrase "It's..." is used frequently at the beginning of sentences. While not egregious, varying sentence structure would improve flow.
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The final paragraph, while poetic, feels like a direct info-dump *after* the dialogue ends. Some of this realization could be subtly woven *into* the dialogue itself. Perhaps Kaelen muttering something about “hidden geometry” *while* touching the glyphs, or a fragmented recollection triggered by the patterns.
*   **"I think I found it" - Weak Climax:** After building tension, “I think I found it” feels anticlimactic. Given the physical and mental strain described, a more desperate or urgent line would be more impactful. Something like “It’s open… I’ve found it!” or even just a gasp followed by a whispered realization.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The relationship between Kaelen and Elara feels somewhat flat. Injecting a little subtext—a hint of concern, protectiveness, or even disagreement—could add layers. For example, Elara could say something like, “You’re pushing yourself too hard, Kaelen,” subtly expressing her worry.

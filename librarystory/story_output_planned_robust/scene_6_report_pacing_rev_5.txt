*   **Initial Contact (Paragraphs 1-2):** Good build of mystery and escalating sensation. The copper taste and tightening temples are effective. However, the description of the initial contact feels slightly *too* drawn out. Consider streamlining the first paragraph to hit the 'yielding resistance' quicker.

Each time, the scene builds, then gets cut short by his observation. This creates a frustrating rhythm for the reader. Consider fewer, more impactful Hemlock moments, or weaving his assessment *into* the character’s internal experience.
*   **Mid-Scene Plateau (Paragraphs 6-7):** The bookshelf falling and the description of aged paper feels like a standard ‘something happens’ beat. It doesn't significantly raise the stakes or tension. It feels like a pause rather than a progression.
*   **Final Push & Aftermath (Paragraphs 8-10):** The final resistance and warning are strong. The immediate aftermath, however, lingers *too* long on internal sensation (throbbing temple, tremor). While showing the impact is good, the repetition of physical distress becomes numbing. Trim some of this to maintain momentum.
*   **Overall Tension Arc:** The scene feels like a series of peaks and valleys that are too evenly spaced. The tension builds, gets interrupted, builds again, gets interrupted. This prevents a sustained sense of dread or urgency. The final moments are strong, but the preceding interruptions diminish their impact.

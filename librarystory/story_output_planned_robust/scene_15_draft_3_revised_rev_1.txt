## Draft 3

The air in the cartography room hung thick with the scent of aged parchment and dust. Sunlight, filtered through the high, arched window, illuminated the intricate lines of the ancient map spread across the table. Seris traced a finger along the faded ink, the brittle paper crackling beneath her touch. The map, ostensibly charting the Firepeak Mountains, felt less like a geographical guide and more like a whispered secret.

“The sequence is almost complete,” she murmured, her brow furrowed in concentration. “’Beneath the Serpent’s Tooth… where shadows sleep… the Stone Heart awaits…’ But the phrasing… it’s not Eldorian elegance. It’s raw, primal. Like a carving etched by hands long forgotten.”

Rhys, perched on a stool, meticulously cleaned a set of bone tools. “Pre-Eldorian, you think? Before the unification? Those dialects are fragments now, barely decipherable.”

“More than just the language,” Seris replied, her voice hushed. “The runes themselves. Variations, corruptions of the standard glyphs. It’s as if someone deliberately obscured the meaning, layered it with… something else.” She ran a calloused fingertip over a particularly distorted rune. “This one… ‘Isen.’ And this… a fractured form of ‘Morwen.’ It feels… intentional.”

*The Stone Heart is not merely a location,* Kaelen’s voice resonated in her mind, sharper now, laced with urgency. *It is a nexus. A point where the veil between worlds thins. And something… watches from the other side.*

Rhys glanced up, sensing her unease. “What did Kaelen say?”

Seris hesitated, reluctant to voice the growing dread that coiled in her stomach. “Just… confirmation that the runes are older than we thought. And that the ‘Stone Heart’ isn’t just a poetic metaphor.”

She returned her attention to the map, her gaze sweeping across the jagged peaks depicted in faded ink. “The Serpent’s Tooth… that has to be Mount Cinderfang. The most prominent, the most… menacing.”

Rhys traced the peak with his finger. “A fitting name. Looks like a viper ready to strike.”

Seris’s eyes narrowed. She ran a finger across the parchment, a subtle difference in texture catching her attention near the base of Mount Cinderfang. It was almost imperceptible, a slight discoloration and a barely-there indentation. “There’s something *on* the map,” she breathed, her voice barely a whisper. “Hidden beneath the surface.”

Rhys, ever the pragmatist, reached for a small magnifying glass. “A layering, I suspect.” He examined the area closely, his brow furrowing. “Someone has pasted a smaller piece of parchment onto the original. It’s remarkably well-camouflaged. And the adhesive… it’s not modern. It’s a concoction of crushed bone, tree resin, and… something else. Something… acrid.” He wrinkled his nose. “It smells of decay.”

Carefully, using a delicate bone scalpel, Rhys began to peel back the overlay. The parchment resisted, brittle with age, threatening to tear. A faint, earthy scent filled the air, mingling with the acrid tang of the adhesive. Beneath it, revealed in faded ochre ink, was a detailed sketch of a subterranean structure – a circular chamber carved into the living rock, with a single, massive stone pillar at its center. The pillar wasn’t smooth; it was covered in intricate carvings, spiraling glyphs that seemed to writhe before her eyes.

“By the Ancestors…” Rhys breathed, his voice filled with awe and apprehension.

Seris traced the lines of the sketch with a trembling finger. “It’s not just a chamber. It’s a focus. A convergence point. A place where energies… *flow*.”

*The Stone Heart is a prison,* Kaelen’s voice echoed in her mind, chillingly clear. *And something is straining against its bonds. Something ancient. Something… hungry.*

Rhys looked up, sensing her growing terror. “What is it, Seris? What do you see?”

Seris shook her head, trying to dismiss the terrifying premonition that gripped her. “I don’t know. But I have a feeling we’ve stumbled onto something dangerous. Something that should have remained buried.” She glanced towards the doorway, a prickling sensation on the back of her neck. The room felt suddenly cold, despite the afternoon sun.

“And Valerius…” she continued, her voice barely a whisper. “He’s been asking too many questions about the history of this region. About the old gods. About… things best left forgotten.” She paused, her gaze sweeping across the room. “I have the distinct feeling we’re being watched.”

A shadow flickered in the periphery of her vision. She spun around, but the room was empty. Just dust motes dancing in the sunlight. But the feeling lingered, cold and unsettling.

Rhys, ever practical, reached for a heavy iron poker leaning against the wall. “Perhaps it’s just the wind. But we’ll be ready, just in case.”

Seris nodded, her heart pounding in her chest. They had unearthed something ancient and terrible. And she had a chilling premonition that they were not alone in knowing about it. The map, once a fascinating puzzle, now felt like a warning. A warning that they had awakened something that should have remained asleep. And that the shadows were closing in.

## Draft 3

The absence wasn’t black, but pressure—a lagging VR rig at the edge of perception. He willed a hand to rise, flex, *prove* connection. Nothing. No arm, no body. Just awareness. A cursor adrift in static. Panic spiked—a tightening where his chest *should* be. Breath—the mechanics, the burn—ghosts of sensation. He tried to shout, force a signal, but it dissolved into static. Untethered. Floating. He squeezed his eyes shut—a phantom reflex—and darkness *responded*, pulsing, straining against a failing connection.

*Reboot. Diagnostic. Anything.* The commands looped, frantic, useless. Not logical for… this. He clung to the sequence, repeating it until the words frayed. A flicker of corrupted code – a life, dissolving – then *reasserting* – apartment, monitors, half-finished game… gone.

Then, a shift. Not location, but texture. Pressure eased, replaced by an ethereal glow—dying embers of a monitor. A hum resonated—distant, architectural, vibrating through awareness. Shapes resolved—a vast, cavernous space. He wasn’t *in* darkness, but *within* something. He reached for a shelf, focusing will, and a fleeting impression of cool, brittle paper – a ghost of sensation, almost painful. He strained, *demanding* connection, and it fractured, dissolving, leaving a phantom ache in nonexistent fingertips. He clenched his mental focus, attempting to shield his thoughts.

He projected a thought—a fragile signal flare. *Hello?* It felt flimsy, lost, immediately followed by disorientation.

A response came, not as sound, but feeling. A gentle ping—ancient stillness. Not a voice, but an impression—patient, observing, impossibly old. It didn’t answer. It *considered*, and with that consideration came cataloging—a curator examining an artifact. 

*Aware.*

The word resonated, and with it, a scan—colder, analytical. Like an admin assessing a process, labeling, categorizing. A chill—not *through* anything, but undeniably there, settling deep within his… core? He tried to pull away, to *block* the probing, but the scan continued, relentless, dissecting memories, fears.

He reached out again, desperate for something solid, something real. He focused on a single book, willing himself to *feel* it, to understand. The sensation returned—cool, brittle paper—but this time, something else. A flicker of image, a fragment of a face, a single word… *lost*.

The presence shifted, a subtle tightening of the mental pressure. *You are not the first to ask. Nor will you be the last.*

He felt a prickle of unease. *Observation?* What did that mean? Was he a specimen? A prisoner? He strained against the feeling of being scrutinized, attempting to solidify his mental defenses.

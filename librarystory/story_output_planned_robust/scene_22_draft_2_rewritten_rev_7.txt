Seris pressed her palms against the cool stone, a tremor running through her. It wasn’t lost lore; it was a conspiracy reaching the highest echelons, and time felt desperately short. She forced a centering breath, steeling herself. Rhys paced the shadowed alcove, running a hand through his hair, his jaw tight with barely suppressed energy.

“Kaelen,” Seris focused on their mental connection, “anything more about why the Gardener’s knowledge was suppressed? The content itself?”

Pages turned rapidly, dust motes dancing in the light. Finally, the pages stilled on a fragmented passage in archaic script.

*“The Gardener cultivated not merely flora, but memoria – the very fabric of recollection. They understood Eldoria’s history wasn’t singular, but a tapestry of possibilities. They preserved the lost threads, the forgotten narratives…the echoes of worlds that might have been.”*

Seris frowned, absorbing the words. Rhys stopped pacing, his gaze sharpening, a muscle ticking in his jaw. “Lost narratives? Alternate histories?” He considered the implications, then looked at Seris, a dawning realization in his eyes. “So, they weren’t just preserving facts, they were preserving *possibilities*… other potential realities?”

*“The Order believed a single, unified history was necessary for stability,”* Kaelen projected, the weight of ages in the voice. *“They feared the chaos of conflicting truths.”*

Seris met Rhys’s gaze, a shared understanding passing between them. A cold weight settled in her chest.

“Kaelen, what else?” Seris prompted, her voice tight.

*“The Gardener recorded…before. Before Eldoria.”* The voice was faint, strained. *“A time of connection.”*

Seris’s breath hitched. “Connection to what?”

*“Other realms…other beings…”*

Pages continued to turn, revealing a single, faded illustration – a swirling vortex of iridescent light, filled with impossible structures.

“Show me,” Seris breathed, her voice barely a whisper.

The illustration flared, and a rush of images flooded Seris’s mind. She stumbled, bracing herself against Rhys, her knuckles white as she gripped his arm.

*“The Veil…it wasn’t a barrier erected to keep threats *out*…”* Kaelen’s voice fragmented, distant, laced with sorrow. *“It was built to contain something…to sever a connection.”*

The image sharpened – a breathtaking landscape of impossible geometry and beings of pure light. Then, darkness crashed over Seris, and she cried out, clutching her head. Rhys held her tighter, concern etched on his face. 

She shook her head, clearing the images. “I…I saw something. A world…before. Kaelen showed me…” Realization dawned, and her eyes widened. "The Veil…it wasn't about keeping things *out*…it was about keeping something *in*…or keeping us from something *else*."

A chilling weight settled upon them. They weren’t uncovering a conspiracy; they were peeling back deliberately obscured history, a history that threatened everything they knew. Rhys tightened his grip on her arm, his gaze meeting hers. He didn't need to ask what they should do next. He simply *knew*. His expression hardened, a grim determination settling over his features. "They didn't want us to remember," he murmured, more to himself than to Seris. "They wanted it all forgotten."

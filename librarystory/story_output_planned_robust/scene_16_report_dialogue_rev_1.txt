*   **Repetitive Emotional Signifiers:** The dialogue relies heavily on stating emotional states ("trembling," "voice tight with urgency," "eyes filled with grim determination"). While showing emotion is good, *telling* us repeatedly becomes tiresome. Consider letting actions and subtext convey these feelings instead. For example, instead of "her legs trembling," describe her needing to grip Rhys’ arm for support.
*   **Kaelen’s Voice – Fragmented but Functional:** While the fragmented nature of Kaelen's voice is understandable (given the method of communication), it borders on *too* broken to effectively deliver information. It’s becoming a crutch. Try consolidating some of the fragments into slightly longer, but still broken, phrases. This will make the crucial information land more effectively.
*   **Seris’s Exposition:** Seris does a *lot* of explaining. While some explanation is necessary, much of it feels like she’s narrating to Rhys (and the reader) rather than having a natural conversation. For example, the line “It’s connected to the Nexus…to the source of the network…it’s a place…a prison…” could be trimmed and delivered with more impact through a single, evocative phrase.
*   **Rhys as a Reactive Character:** Rhys primarily asks clarifying questions. He needs more agency and internal reaction. Even a small line demonstrating his own fear or a deduction based on what Seris says would strengthen his character.
*   **"The Sundering..." realization:** The line "It wasn’t a cause…it was a *containment*.” feels a bit on-the-nose. Consider letting this realization dawn more gradually, perhaps through a change in Seris’s expression or a physical reaction.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The scene is filled with high stakes, but the emotional connection between Seris and Rhys feels somewhat understated. A moment of shared fear, a touch, or a silent exchange of understanding would heighten the tension and deepen their relationship.

*   **Repetitive Exposition:** The dialogue frequently *tells* rather than *shows* the stakes. Lines like "The Sunstone Bloom isn’t just a rare flower," and "The resonance isn’t just a connection" feel like info-dumps. Consider revealing this information through Seris's observations, Kaelen's actions, or the environment.
*   **Kaelen's Stoicism:** Kaelen's responses are consistently terse and explanatory. While his character might be reserved, the dialogue lacks nuance. Inject some personality – a hint of weariness, frustration, or even reluctant respect for Seris. His "Let them come" feels particularly flat.
*   **Seris's Frustration:** Seris asks "Why?" repeatedly. While understandable, it’s becoming a crutch. Explore her frustration through internal monologue or more active attempts to piece together the mystery.
*   **Predictable Exchanges:** Many lines are simply question/answer, lacking subtext. For example, Seris stating “Someone’s following us” and Kaelen immediately acknowledging it. Consider having Kaelen react *before* Seris vocalizes it, demonstrating his awareness.
*   **"Walking into a trap" cliché:** The final line is a well-worn trope. While it conveys danger, it lacks impact. Consider a more subtle foreshadowing of the danger ahead.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The apprentice stumbling is a good moment, but it's *described* rather than *shown* through dialogue. A brief, sharp reprimand *from* Kaelen, even a single word, would be more effective.

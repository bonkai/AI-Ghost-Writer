*   **Subtext & Efficiency:** While the scene *shows* tension through action and body language (which is excellent!), the dialogue feels a bit…stilted. It’s largely characters stating the obvious or offering veiled threats/observations *at* each other rather than *with* each other. For example, Seris’s line, “The pursuit of knowledge often leads us to…uncommon ground, Inquisitor,” feels like a direct response to an unspoken accusation, but it lacks nuance. It’s a statement, not a deflection or a challenge.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** The dialogue *supports* the characterization established through action, but it doesn’t actively *reveal* much new. We *see* Rhys is ready for a fight, but his silence doesn’t *tell* us anything about his personality or motivations beyond that.
*   **Repetition & Redundancy:** The constant reiteration of "He was ready" and "And so was she" at the end is unnecessary. The characters’ readiness is already established through their actions and body language. Let the silence speak for itself.
*   **Marius’s Lines:** Marius's dialogue is the strongest, but even it leans towards being overly dramatic. The lines about memories and debts are effective, but could be delivered with more subtlety. Consider *implying* these things through his observations rather than stating them directly.
*   **Opportunities for Subtext:** There’s a missed opportunity for subtext when Marius acknowledges the flower arrangement as “ceremonial.” What *specifically* does that recognition mean to *him*? A brief, internal thought or a loaded pause would add layers.
*   **Rhys’s Silence:** While intentional, Rhys's complete lack of dialogue is bordering on unrealistic, even for a stoic character. A single, carefully chosen word or a pointed glance could amplify the tension.

**Suggestion:** Focus on *implying* more through subtext and letting the characters’ actions speak louder. Trim redundant phrases and consider how each line can simultaneously reveal character, advance the plot, or build tension – ideally, all three.

The oil lamp died, leaving Seris in a darkness that felt less like absence and more like a weight. A draft snaked through the archive, colder than the stone walls, carrying the scent of dust and a faint, metallic sweetness—old blood and withered lilies. Her hand drifted to the hilt of her dagger, fingers tightening on the worn leather, a grounding instinct.

Valerius didn’t enter the darkness. He simply became visible within it, coalescing from the shadows, two Guardians materializing beside him, their armor swallowing light. Beneath his robes, a faint, pulsing glow mirrored the tome’s illustrations. He paused before the open book, gaze fixed on the depiction of the Aethel. He didn’t ask a question, instead tracing a finger across the illustration, a slow, deliberate gesture that felt less like inquiry and more like possession.

Seris’s eyes scanned the image, then flicked back to Valerius, a subtle tightening of her jaw. The Aethel—gods of a forgotten age, dismissed as myth. She hesitated, a flicker of unease crossing her face. “The histories claim they were lost to time.”

Valerius tilted his head, a slow, deliberate movement. “Lost? Or… *rearranged*?” He paused, his gaze unwavering. A muscle jumped in Seris’s cheek, and she tasted something acrid on her tongue, a phantom echo of the metallic scent. “Consider a tapestry. Threads can be cut, patterns altered.”

A prickling sensation rose on the back of her neck. She glanced at the tome, then back at Valerius, her grip tightening on the dagger. The metallic scent intensified, and she noticed the polished steel of the Guardians’ armor didn’t reflect the darkness, but *drank* it. A chill settled over her, a sense of something deeply wrong.

“What do you mean, ‘rearranged’?” Her voice was sharper than she intended, a defensiveness surfacing she hadn’t realized she possessed. A tremor ran through her hand.

Valerius’s lips curved in a slow, predatory smile. He didn’t answer, instead gesturing to a Guardian. “The Initiate requires rest. Escort her back to her chambers.”

The Guardian moved, a silent, implacable force. Seris didn’t bother feigning compliance. Resistance would be futile, and she knew, with chilling clarity, that she *had* to return to this archive—alone. The Guardian’s grip tightened on her arm, but she didn't pull away. Not yet. She understood, in that moment, that the Aethel hadn't simply been erased from history. They had been *unwoven*, their light stolen and bound. Valerius held the threads. He wasn’t a guardian of knowledge. He was its jailer.

She allowed herself to be led away, a single thought echoing in her mind: *This archive isn't protecting the past. It's a prison,* and she was beginning to suspect she was a prisoner within it.

*   **Initial Intrigue & Slow Build:** The opening paragraph is strong in establishing a sense of unease, but the pacing is *very* slow. The internal thoughts ("not fear, but awareness…") stretch out the tension without immediate payoff. Consider condensing this to a few impactful sentences.
*   **Courtyard & Information Dump:** The courtyard scene feels slightly static. The description of the Circle’s efficiency is well-written, but doesn’t significantly escalate the tension. The dialogue feels expository, explaining the situation rather than *showing* it through action or reaction.
*   **Fragmented Memory:** The “resonance”/memory sequence is intriguing, but feels abrupt. It needs a beat more build-up, perhaps a stronger physical sensation before the attempt to recall. The quick vanishing feels rushed; allow Seris a moment to *struggle* with the memory before it fades.
*   **Repetitive Tension – Being Watched:** The constant reiteration of “being watched” (shadows, man in the coat, etc.) starts to feel repetitive. While maintaining awareness is important, the tension plateaus. Vary the *type* of threat—switch from observation to active pursuit, or introduce a new obstacle.
*   **Pity/Expectation Climax:** The final paragraph with the man's gaze is the most potent moment. However, it arrives *late* in the scene. This could be a more effective climax if the preceding tension had been more dynamically built. The "pity" is a strong note, but feels slightly disconnected from the earlier, more direct threats.
*   **Overall:** The scene is more about establishing a feeling of paranoia than building sustained, escalating tension. It's a slow burn, which isn't necessarily bad, but it needs more *variation* in pacing and a stronger sense of immediate consequence to prevent it from feeling stagnant. The tension peaks are too subtle and arrive too late to maximize impact.

*   **Repetitive Internal Dialogue:** The character frequently asks questions internally ("What do you want from me?", "What am I?", "Soon what?"). While internal questioning is fine, it happens *very* frequently in quick succession. Consider consolidating some of these into a single, more complex thought, or showing his desperation through action/sensory experience instead of constant questioning.
*   **The Presence's Voice:** The Presence's dialogue, while evocative, is *very* consistent in its detached, almost poetic tone. It lacks nuance. Consider adding moments of irritation, curiosity, or even a hint of amusement to make it feel less like a static narrator and more like a genuine entity. The consistent “we do not ‘want’” feels a bit like telling instead of showing its nature.
*   **"Little Echo/Resonance":** The repeated use of these terms feels a bit clunky and expository. While establishing the character's role is important, the Presence repeats it often. Show the character *being* an echo/resonance through its interactions, rather than constantly labeling it as such.
*   **Info-Dumping via the Presence:** The initial explanation of the Great Library is a large block of exposition delivered by the Presence. While necessary for the reader, consider breaking it up with the character’s reactions, internal thoughts, or sensory experiences.
*   **Lack of Direct Dialogue (Opportunity):** The entire conversation is one-sided, with the character primarily *thinking* questions and the Presence responding. While appropriate for the situation, explore moments where the character attempts to *speak* even if it fails. The effort, the frustration, the *attempt* at vocalization would add another layer to his helplessness and desperation.
*   **"Soon" and "Patience":** The repeated use of these single-word answers from the library feels like stalling. While building mystery is good, these responses are becoming predictable and a bit frustrating for the reader. Consider offering a more substantial clue, even if it’s cryptic.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The Presence says "Welcome home." This is a lovely line, but consider *why* it feels like home. What specifically about the library evokes that feeling? Show, don't just tell.

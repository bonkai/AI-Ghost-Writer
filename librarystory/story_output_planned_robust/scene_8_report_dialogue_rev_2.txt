*   **Seris's initial murmurs ("Interesting")** feel a little weak. Given the momentousness of the discovery, a stronger, more visceral reaction would be more believable. Perhaps a breathy "Incredible," or simply a silent intake of breath.
*   **The "fungi" lie** is a bit cliché and doesn’t quite fit Seris’s character, who seems intelligent and resourceful. A more plausible, if still misleading, explanation would be better. Perhaps something about researching historical herbal remedies or comparing different textual interpretations.
*   **Hemlock's dialogue is *very* heavy-handed with implication.** While his mysterious nature is good, the lines "some knowledge is best left undisturbed" and "The past has a way of reaching out…sometimes it’s best not to answer its call" are bordering on exposition disguised as conversation. Subtlety would be more effective. Perhaps a simple, pointed question about *why* she’s focusing on such obscure texts.
*   **Seris's repeated "I..." statements ("I don’t understand," "I’m simply expanding my understanding")** feel defensive and a little repetitive. Varying her responses, even with short, noncommittal phrases, would make the exchange feel more natural.
*   **The back-and-forth between Seris and Hemlock lacks true tension.** While the *reader* understands the stakes, the dialogue doesn't quite convey that urgency. More active questioning from Hemlock, or a more defiant response from Seris, would raise the stakes.
*   **Hemlock's final line ("Perhaps you will. In time.")** feels a bit too smug and predictable. A more ambiguous or unsettling statement would be more effective.

Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks nuance and subtlety. It relies too heavily on implication and doesn't fully exploit the potential for subtext and tension.

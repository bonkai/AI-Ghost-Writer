## Draft 3 - Scene 20

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, her fingers tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure – a forgotten goddess, judging by Rhys’s texts. The stone felt cold beneath her touch. She instinctively tightened her grip on the hilt of her dagger, scanning the shadows.

“You seek to understand why I strayed from the Light?” Old Man Hemlock’s voice was brittle, echoing slightly in the cavern. He emerged from the gloom, pacing like a caged animal. “Some doors are best left unopened. Some memories…a weight best left undisturbed.” He stopped abruptly, hand rising to cover his ear as if warding off a sound. “The Order has long ears. Even in exile, we are not truly alone.”

Seris didn’t turn, senses on high alert. “I seek to understand *you*,” she replied, voice steady despite the tremor in her gut. “What did they show you in those archives?”

Hemlock paused, then slowly lowered himself to a weathered stool, his joints creaking in protest. “I believed in the purity of the Light. A meticulous scholar, devoted to cataloging sanctioned blessings.” He glanced at the mosaic. “Then I found the records. Buried, not destroyed. They weren’t meant to be *lost*, merely…hidden.”

Seris leaned forward, breath catching. “What kind of records?”

“Records of the Before-Times.” Hemlock’s voice dropped to a rasping whisper. “A world steeped in magic, not controlled blessings, but a raw, untamed force.” He gestured to a faded section of the mosaic, depicting a swirling vortex. “I saw images…cities built of shimmering crystal, powered by the lifeblood of the earth. Gardens that bloomed with bioluminescent flora.” He reached out, his fingers brushing Seris’s arm, then quickly withdrawing as if burned. “And records of *why* that magic was suppressed.”

He stepped back into the shadows. “They didn’t just fear the magic, child. They feared what people *remembered* with it. The power it gave them, the questions it inspired.”

Seris’s jaw tightened. “What do you mean?”

“The Order deemed those memories dangerous. They didn’t destroy them. They *buried* them. And built the Church upon the grave, a monument to forgetting.” A faint scraping echoed from the tunnel entrance. Hemlock whirled around, eyes wide. “They send observers, you know. To gauge our silence.”

Seris felt a chill crawl down her spine. “You’re saying the Church…suppresses knowledge?”

“Erodes it,” Hemlock corrected, his voice strained. “Not just taking what is known, but dismantling the capacity to *remember*.” He touched the mosaic. “These depict the ‘Memory Wells’ – places where the Order siphoned away collective memories.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris. “I discovered the Wells weren’t just repositories…they were *powered* by something. Something the flower might unlock.” His gaze fell on the Sunstone Bloom sketch poking from her pack.

“I questioned it,” Hemlock continued, his voice barely a whisper. “I dared to ask *why*. And for that, I was branded a heretic. Stripped of my rank. Exiled.” He stumbled back, clutching his chest. “They said I was ‘unstable.’ ‘A danger to the faithful.’”

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls.

Hemlock’s gaze drifted to the shadows. “I couldn’t stand by and watch them disappear. They are a warning, child. A reminder of what we’ve lost…and what we’ve been made to forget.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris. “They suspect. This place is warded, hidden by the very magic they seek to control. But the wards won’t hold forever.”

Seris met his gaze, jaw tight. The crimson stain on the sketch felt like a brand. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower. She was chasing a truth the Church would kill to keep buried. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, Seris knew, with chilling certainty, that Valerius wasn’t just observing them. He was hunting them.

The weight of forgotten histories pressed down on Seris. Beneath the fear, a new resolve hardened within her. This wasn’t just about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it. She tightened her grip on her pack, knuckles white. "What can we do?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper, but filled with a newfound determination. "How do we fight a war against forgetting?"

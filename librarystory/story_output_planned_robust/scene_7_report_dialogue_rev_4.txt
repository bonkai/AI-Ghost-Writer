*   **Hemlock's Dialogue - Observation vs. Question:** The line, “Disturbed?” being “not as a question, but an observation” feels a little *told* rather than *shown*. Hemlock could deliver this line *as* an observation through tone and body language. The narration stating it’s not a question is redundant.

*   **Seris's Initial Response - Too Brief:** Seris’s “Cities…a serpent…it felt…real. Like a memory, but not my own” is understandable given her shock, but feels a little *too* minimal. Even a fragmented, emotional response with a bit more detail would strengthen the scene. Consider adding a physical reaction *while* she speaks – a gasp, a hand clutching her chest, etc.

*   **Hemlock's "Some places remember..." - Expositional:** While poetic, Hemlock’s lines about places remembering and objects holding echoes lean towards exposition. This information feels delivered *to* the reader through Hemlock, rather than organically arising from the conversation. Consider if this explanation is truly *necessary* at this moment, or if it could be revealed more subtly later.

*   **Hemlock's "Be careful..." - Vague Threat:** The warning, “Be careful, Seris. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed. Or…perhaps it has been *waiting*,” is a bit cliché and lacks specificity. While mystery is good, it feels a little *too* vague. What specifically is the danger? Even a hint would raise the stakes.

*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** There's a missed opportunity to reveal more about Hemlock’s character through his dialogue. Is he genuinely concerned for Seris, or is he more interested in *what* she’s experiencing? A slight shift in tone or phrasing could hint at his motivations.

*   **Repetition of "Watching":** The final line emphasizing the eye "watching" feels a bit repetitive after the description of the creature’s gaze earlier. Consider a different phrasing to emphasize the unnerving sensation.

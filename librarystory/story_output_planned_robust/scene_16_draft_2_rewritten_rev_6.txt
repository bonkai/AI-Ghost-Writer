The library air felt thick against Seris’s skin, a pressure that settled deep in her bones. Dust motes hung frozen in the light. A tremor ran through the stone, vibrating in her teeth, and she braced a hand against a bookshelf, feeling the ancient wood hum with discordant energy.

Rhys, studying a faded map, glanced up, his brow furrowing as he registered her tension. His hand drifted to the hilt of his dagger, not a question asked, but a readiness shown.

The library *shifted*. Not visibly, but at the edge of her vision, the familiar lines blurring. The scent of old parchment and beeswax twisted with a metallic tang – ozone and something like dried blood. A weight descended, the feeling of a network beneath her feet, extending outwards, sustaining the room. It strained, a bowstring pulled taut.

Then, a discordant note fractured the harmony. Luminous threads of energy snapped, and darkness seeped into the cracks. The energy didn't explode, but *retreated*, leaving a hollow ache in Seris’s chest. She stumbled, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue, and pressed a hand to her temple.

Rhys caught her arm, his grip firm and silent, anchoring her.

Kaelen’s consciousness brushed against hers – not words, but raw sensation: the echoing resonance of a nexus, the weight of ancient power. A flicker of icy dread, then an image – a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian, pulsing with power, but laced with hairline cracks, the light within flickering erratically. She gasped, a sharp intake of breath.

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts.

Rhys tightened his grip, his gaze searching her face. “What is it?”

Kaelen’s panic surged – a desperate, wordless plea. The image sharpened – obsidian, stars, cracks widening. *Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…* A cold wind whipped across her skin, the smell of sulfur stinging her nostrils. A deep, resonant thrumming vibrated through her bones. She squeezed her eyes shut, fighting the onslaught.

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus, and opened her eyes.

“Firestone Mountains?” Rhys asked, his voice tight with concern.

“It’s connected to the source of the network,” she said, her voice strained. “Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible. The seal is failing, and the cracks are widest there.” The words felt…given, not reasoned.

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within breaks free.”

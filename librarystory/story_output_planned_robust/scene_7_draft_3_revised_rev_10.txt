“Seris?”

She whirled, heart hammering. Hemlock stood framed in the doorway, bracing himself, a stillness clinging to him.

Seris offered a curt nod, gaze fixed on the sphere. An inexplicable pull resonated within her, deep in her bones. She extended a hand, fingers hovering above the cool surface. A tremor ran through the stone as her fingertips brushed it, a vibration climbing her arm and settling in her chest – not unpleasant, but *awakening*. The air tasted of dust and something older, something metallic.

The world fractured. Impossible cities carved into mountains crumbled with age, canyons choked with luminous flora pulsed with forgotten life, mirroring her quickening pulse. Coiled around a basalt peak, a colossal serpentine creature stirred, scales shimmering like a thousand sunsets. Awe dissolved into primal fear. 

Then, a single, ancient eye met hers – reptilian, gold flecks like trapped starlight. It didn’t merely *see* her; it bored *through* her, stripping away pretense. A resonance bloomed in her chest, a hollow ache mirroring the creature’s weariness. It felt…familiar. Not a memory, but a recognition. She stumbled back, bracing a hand against the cold stone of the wall, the shock a grounding force.

Hemlock entered the chamber, his gaze sweeping over the sphere, then locking onto her face. Concern etched lines around his eyes, but his expression held a strange calculation. He didn’t rush to her side.

She squeezed her eyes shut, the image of the eye seared onto her eyelids. When she opened them, the visions had receded, leaving a coldness in her chest and a faint ringing in her ears. “Cities…” she breathed, her voice barely a whisper. “A serpent…it felt real. Not a vision, but…something I’ve known before.”

Hemlock’s lips curved into a faint smile, but it didn’t quite reach his eyes. He reached out, fingers hovering over the sphere, then drew back, a subtle tension in his jaw. He didn’t touch it. “Some places remember,” he said, his voice low. “Some objects…hold echoes.” He paused, his gaze locking with hers, probing, assessing. “What did the serpent *feel* like?”

She wrapped her arms around herself, a sudden chill gripping her. “Old. Tired. And…knowing. Like it had been waiting…” She trailed off, the thought unfinished.

Hemlock studied her intently, his gaze lingering on her face, then drifting to her hands, as if searching for a sign. “Be careful. What sleeps within the stone may not appreciate being disturbed.” A subtle shift in his gaze suggested he knew more than he let on. “Do you feel…changed?”

She looked back at the sphere. For a fleeting moment, she thought she saw a flicker of movement within its depths—a subtle shifting of the serpentine patterns. And then, a single, golden eye reflecting her own. The eye didn’t blink. It *watched*. A prickle ran down her spine. “I… I think so.” She touched her chest, as if confirming her own heartbeat. “It felt like… it *recognized* me.”

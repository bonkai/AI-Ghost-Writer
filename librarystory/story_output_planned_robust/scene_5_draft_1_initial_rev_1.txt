The Atheneum smelled of dust and forgotten things—a scent Seris found strangely comforting. Not the clean dust of neglect, but the rich, layered aroma of centuries pressed between parchment and binding. Sunlight, fractured by stained glass depicting long-dead scholars, painted the vast chamber in shifting hues of ruby and sapphire. She ran a gloved hand along a towering bookshelf, the wood cool and smooth beneath her fingertips.

The library was a labyrinth. Aisles spiraled into darkness, branching into hidden alcoves and forgotten galleries. Most dismissed it as a crumbling relic, a repository for useless lore. The Iron Church certainly did, deeming its contents heretical and restricting access. Which, of course, was precisely why Seris had sought it out.

She wasn’t here for grand spells or potent potions. She sought *information*. Fragments of the past, whispers of a time before the Church’s iron grip, before magic was branded as sin. Specifically, she needed anything referencing the ‘Veiled Cities’—rumored settlements erased from history, said to possess knowledge the Church desperately wanted to suppress.

A cough echoed from the shadows. Seris’s hand instinctively drifted to the hilt of the dagger concealed beneath her cloak. She scanned the nearest alcove, her gaze lingering on a hunched figure meticulously cataloging scrolls. Old Man Hemlock, the Atheneum’s caretaker. Harmless, but unsettlingly observant.

“Looking for something specific, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice rasped, barely louder than the rustling of parchment. He didn’t look up.

“Just…exploring,” Seris replied, keeping her voice neutral. “The Church rarely permits access. I’m curious what secrets such a place holds.”

“Secrets have a way of finding those who seek them,” Hemlock said cryptically. “But be warned. Some secrets are best left buried.”

Seris ignored the warning, turning her attention back to the shelves. She ran her finger along the spines, scanning the faded titles. Most were treatises on theology, philosophy, or obscure alchemical formulas. Useless. 

A subtle shift in the air caught her attention. A prickling sensation on the back of her neck. She glanced around, but saw nothing. Just the towering shelves, the dust motes dancing in the sunlight, and the silent, watchful figure of Old Man Hemlock.

She reached for a particularly worn volume, its cover devoid of any title. As her fingers brushed against the aged leather, a voice, faint as a whisper, echoed in her mind. *“The stones remember…”*

Seris froze, her heart pounding. She hadn’t *heard* the voice, not with her ears. It had resonated within her mind, a fleeting impression, a fragment of thought not her own.

She quickly withdrew her hand, glancing around nervously. Hemlock hadn’t moved, but his eyes, obscured by shadow, seemed to bore into her.

“Did you say something, Initiate?” he asked, his voice a low murmur.

“No,” Seris replied quickly, forcing a smile. “Just admiring the craftsmanship of the bindings.”

She needed to leave. This place… it wasn’t just old. It was *alive*, humming with an energy she couldn’t comprehend. And she had the unsettling feeling that whatever secrets the Atheneum held, they weren’t going to be given up easily. She turned to leave, but paused, noticing a small, unassuming volume tucked away on a lower shelf. Its cover was blank, but a single, silver clasp held it closed. 

Something about it called to her. A sense of familiarity, a pull she couldn’t ignore. Hesitantly, she reached for it, her fingers trembling slightly. As she touched the clasp, the voice echoed in her mind again, clearer this time.

*“The path is hidden. Seek the serpent’s eye.”*

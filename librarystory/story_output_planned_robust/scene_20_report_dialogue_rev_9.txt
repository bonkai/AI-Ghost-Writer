*   **Over-Explanation/Internal Monologue Disguised as Dialogue:** Hemlock *tells* us a lot about his feelings and motivations rather than *showing* them through action and subtext. For example, lines like “It wasn’t chaos, child. It was…potential.” and “They didn’t just fear the magic. They feared what people *remembered* with it.” feel like exposition delivered *to* the reader via Hemlock, rather than natural conversation. Consider trimming or showing these feelings through his behavior.
*   **Repetitive Theme (Knowledge = Control/Fear):** The idea of the Church fearing knowledge and control is stated *multiple* times, directly. While thematic consistency is good, it’s becoming redundant. Subtlety would be more effective. Perhaps show the *effects* of this fear through the actions of those listening in the tunnel, rather than Hemlock explicitly stating it.
*   **Seris's Dialogue Feels Reactive:** Seris largely asks questions or states observations. While this establishes her as observant, it doesn’t give us much insight into *her* internal world or motivations beyond wanting the flower. Give her more proactive lines that reveal her beliefs or personality.
*   **"We remember" feels cliché:** While poetic, the line “We remember” feels a bit overused and lacks punch in this context. Consider a more specific or impactful statement of their shared goal.
*   **Hemlock's "Ghost of a Smile":** The stage direction describing Hemlock’s “ghost of a smile” feels unnecessary and doesn't add much to the dialogue. Let his words and actions convey his emotions.
*   **Subtle Opportunities Missed:** The moment where Hemlock covers Seris’s hand is good, but could be *more*. Does he squeeze it? Is it a hesitant touch? A protective gesture? Expand on the physicality to convey deeper meaning.
*   **Indirect Characterization - good, but could be stronger:** The dynamic between Seris and Hemlock is well-established (she's determined, he's weary but hopeful). However, further emphasize this through subtle shifts in their dialogue - perhaps Seris’s questions becoming more insistent as she gains confidence, or Hemlock’s replies becoming more encouraging as he sees her resolve.

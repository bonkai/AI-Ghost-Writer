The workshop smelled of dried herbs and mountain dust, but a metallic tang now clung to the air, prickling at the back of Seris’s throat. The chill lingered after Inquisitor Marius departed, settling not on the stone floor, but in the spaces between breaths. She instinctively tightened her grip on the pouch containing Elara’s portrait, the leather warm beneath her fingers.

“He’s feeling for weaknesses,” Kaelen breathed, his voice a low current in her mind. Seris didn't react outwardly, her fingers tracing the line of her jaw, a habitual gesture she barely registered. 

Rhys shifted, subtly obstructing the view of the restricted ingredients with his broad frame – a casual repositioning, yet pointedly deliberate. He adjusted a precisely balanced scale with deliberate slowness, as if demonstrating a process too intricate for the Inquisitor to bother with. 

Seris smoothed a stray strand of hair, meeting Marius’s gaze directly, allowing a hint of challenge to bloom in her eyes. She subtly shifted closer to the workbench, reclaiming her space. “The pursuit of knowledge often leads us to…uncommon ground, Inquisitor.” 

Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room, cataloging details. He stopped on the half-dissected flower, the petals arranged with unsettling precision. His lips thinned, not in disapproval, but in something akin to…recognition. A flicker of something unreadable crossed his face. “An…unconventional arrangement. Almost…ceremonial.”

Seris held his gaze, her expression carefully neutral. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek, though your methods…differ.”

Rhys began cleaning his axe *during* Marius’s assessment, the rhythmic scrape of steel on stone a deliberate counterpoint to the Inquisitor’s scrutiny. The motion was controlled, precise, but with an underlying energy – not of fear, but of coiled readiness. His gaze flickered toward the shadowed corner, then returned to his work, a silent acknowledgement of the shared risk. *He’s testing us,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in her mind. *Seeing how we react under pressure.*

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile, but it didn’t reach his eyes. “A convenient answer.” He moved with a fluid grace, casually observing the room as if assessing its structural integrity. “The Bloom…it remembers. Echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. A time when such…flora held sway over more than just healing.” He paused, letting the implication sink in. “Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep…and remind us of what we’ve forgotten.”

He stepped back, his gaze lingering on their workspace. “I will be watching you. Both of you. Not to *find* secrets, but to understand what you *choose* to hide.”

Rhys continued to clean his axe, maintaining a focused intensity on his task, pointedly ignoring Marius, but with an awareness that bordered on predatory. 

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing Seris with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has debts, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to *forfeit* to settle them?” He offered a curt nod, then swept out, leaving a lingering chill and the oppressive feeling of being observed. A faint scent of ozone followed him.

Silence descended. Seris didn’t break eye contact with Rhys, a silent understanding passing between them. He was ready. And so was she. She met his gaze, a flicker of defiance in her eyes. They wouldn't be broken.

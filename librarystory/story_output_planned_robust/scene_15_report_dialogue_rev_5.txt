*   **Seris’s internal thoughts are overused.** While effective for establishing mood, the italicized internal narration frequently *tells* us what Seris is feeling instead of letting the dialogue *show* it. Reduce these; let her reactions be more subtle and revealed through speech and action. For example, instead of "*a scent that clung to the back of her throat, a promise of storm and something far older*," show her physical reaction to the scent – a slight wince, a hand covering her nose, a change in breathing.
*   **Rhys feels reactive, not proactive.** He largely responds to Seris’s growing unease. Give him a line or two where he *initiates* conversation, perhaps asking specific questions about the runes or the historical context. This would make him feel more engaged and less like a sounding board.
*   **Kaelen’s mental intrusions are a bit flat.** While establishing the urgency, the lines are very direct exposition. Consider layering these with Seris’s emotional state. Instead of “*The Stone Heart is a nexus…*”, perhaps Seris feels a sudden, overwhelming sense of dread *as* the information arrives, or the words feel fragmented and distorted by the mental connection.
*   **“Confirmation the runes are older than we thought” is a weak delivery.** Seris is clearly disturbed. A more nuanced response would be better. Perhaps, “It’s not just the age… it’s *how* they’re different.” or a simple, “This changes everything.”
*   **The line “Looks ready to strike” feels a little cliché.** While fitting the imagery, it doesn’t add much depth. Consider a more subtle observation that reveals Rhys’s character or his perception of the situation.
*   **Seris’s final line about Valerius feels slightly forced.** It’s a necessary plot point, but it comes across as a sudden leap in thought. Perhaps a line or two of internal thought *before* voicing it, establishing her connection between his questions and the current discovery.

Overall, the dialogue is functional, but leans heavily on internal narration and lacks a lot of subtext. There's potential to create more tension and reveal character through more nuanced exchanges.

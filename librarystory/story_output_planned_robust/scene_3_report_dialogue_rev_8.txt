Here's a breakdown of the dialogue, with feedback:

*   **Repetitive Internal Dialogue:** The constant use of "He projected/thought/asked" followed by a single-word question (“What am I?” “Who was that?”) becomes monotonous. While it establishes the method of communication, it lacks nuance. Consider varying the phrasing or showing the *effect* of the thought/projection rather than stating it explicitly. For example, instead of "*What am I?* he thought," try "Desperation clawed at him. *What am I?*" or simply show his internal turmoil through descriptive prose.

*   **The Library's Voice – Too Abstract:** The Library’s responses are consistently vague and philosophical. While fitting for an ancient entity, it lacks a distinct *voice*. It feels more like a narrator *telling* us about the Library than a character *speaking*. Try injecting some personality—even subtle quirks—into its phrasing. Is it patient? Impatient? Condescending? Curious?

*   **"Want" – Missed Opportunity:** The Library dismissing “want” is a good start, but it’s a single line. Explore this further. The protagonist is clearly driven by *need* (to understand, to regain form). The contrast between his desires and the Library’s detachment could be a powerful source of tension.

*   **"Soon" and "Patience" - Vague Directives:** These feel like stalling tactics *within the story* as much as they are responses. While mystery is good, these feel overly cryptic. Could they be tied to something more concrete, even if the protagonist doesn’t understand it yet?

*   **Indirect Characterization - Limited:** The dialogue doesn't reveal much about the protagonist *beyond* his confusion and desperation. Consider how his questions *reveal* his personality. Is he demanding? Humble? Fearful?

*   **"Remember" – Powerful, But Needs Setup:** The final “Remember” is a strong closing line, but it feels somewhat abrupt. The connection between the woman’s face and the need to remember isn’t established strongly enough.

*   **Lack of Subtext:** Most of the dialogue is very direct. There's little layering or unspoken meaning. The Library *could* be subtly manipulating the protagonist, or testing his resolve, but this isn’t apparent in the dialogue itself.



Overall, the dialogue is functional, but it lacks depth and personality. It feels more like an exchange of information than a genuine conversation. It needs more nuance, subtext, and characterization to truly come alive.

*   **Initial Disorientation (Paragraphs 1-3):** Excellent pacing. The slow unraveling and shift from physical sensation to pure awareness is well-handled. The building sense of unease with the hum and feeling watched is effective.
*   **Attempts to Interact (Paragraphs 4-7):** The pacing here is slightly uneven. The initial effort to move the book feels a little *too* easy – a flicker and shimmer is a quick result for establishing a lack of physical form. Consider adding a stage of greater struggle before even that initial response. The rapid escalation to tasting knowledge feels rushed.
*   **Dialogue/Revelation (Paragraphs 8-12):** The exchange with the presence is strong, but the flood of images (Paragraph 12) feels overwhelming *too* quickly. A more gradual reveal of the Library’s scope would build tension better. The immediate soothing tone after such a chaotic vision slightly deflates the impact.
*   **Final Loop (Paragraphs 13-15):** The repeated attempts to force a response and the echoing "Soon" create a good sense of frustration and unanswered questions. However, the *second* instance of pages fluttering and a phrase appearing feels repetitive and diminishes the impact of the first. Consider consolidating these into a single, more significant event.
*   **Overall Tension:** The tension is present, but it plateaus. The scene relies heavily on mystery and unanswered questions, which is effective, but lacks significant peaks and valleys. Adding more resistance to the protagonist’s attempts to interact or a greater sense of threat would elevate the tension.

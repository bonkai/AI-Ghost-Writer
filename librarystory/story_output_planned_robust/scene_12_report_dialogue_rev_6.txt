*   **Valerius’s Dialogue – Overly Metaphorical:** While Valerius *should* be enigmatic, his language is consistently *too* metaphorical. The loom/thread analogy is repeated and feels labored. It’s bordering on villainous exposition rather than subtle hinting. Consider streamlining his speech – fewer metaphors, more carefully chosen words. For example, instead of the extended loom explanation, try: “The past isn’t always lost, Initiate. Sometimes, it’s…redirected.”
*   **Seris’s Responses – Reactive, Not Proactive:** Seris largely *reacts* to Valerius. Her dialogue (“Lost to time,” “What do you mean…?”) is functional but doesn't reveal much about her internal thought process or personality beyond caution. Give her lines that show her analytical mind at work, even if she doesn’t fully understand the situation yet. Perhaps a questioning observation about the Aethel illustration itself.
*   **“Erased? Or… *repurposed*?” – Stilted Phrasing:** The phrasing feels unnatural. It's trying too hard to be mysterious. A more natural delivery would be: "Erased... or used for something else?"
*   **Lack of Subtext in the Final Exchange:** The silent warning passing between Valerius and Seris *could* be stronger. Instead of relying on narration to tell us they exchanged a warning, *show* it through a subtle line of dialogue or a specific action. Perhaps Valerius’s grip tightens on something as he meets her gaze, or Seris subtly adjusts her posture.
*   **Seris’s Internal Thought – Redundant:** The final internal thought (“This archive isn’t protecting the past. It's a prison.”) is a good realization, but it’s already clear to the reader through the scene. It feels like telling, not showing. Trust the reader to draw that conclusion.
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The reveal of the pulsing light under Valerius's robes is a bit sudden. While the visual is strong, it feels like a reveal *happens* to Seris rather than her discovering it through observation. Perhaps foreshadow it earlier with a description of his robes seeming unusually dark or absorbing light.

*   **Lack of Subtext/Reliance on Exposition:** Seris immediately states, “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within breaks free.” While understandable given the urgency, it feels very direct. Consider having her express a feeling – fear, desperation, a sense of impending doom – and letting Rhys *ask* what they need to do. This would create more tension and allow for a more organic reveal.

*   **“I didn’t explain how I knew. The words felt…given to her, not reasoned.”** This is a narrative aside *about* the dialogue, not dialogue itself. It's unnecessary to *tell* us she doesn't explain; *show* us. Have Rhys ask how she knows, and have her deflect or offer a vague answer, leaving him (and the reader) to wonder.

*   **Repetitive Questioning:** Rhys asks “What is it?” and then “Firestone Mountains?” It feels a bit like he’s simply prompting her to deliver the information. Combine these into one question, or have him react to her initial murmur and then ask a more focused question.

*   **Strained Voice/Overuse of Adverbs:** “Her voice strained” and “his voice tight with concern” are telling, not showing. Let the *content* of their speech convey the emotion. A clipped sentence, a hesitant pause, a change in tone—these are more effective.

*   **Kaelen's Communication:** The description of Kaelen's communication is fascinating, but it's all internal. While understandable that it's not *verbal*, consider a very brief physical manifestation – a flicker of Seris’s eyes, a slight tremor in her hand – to visually signal the influx of information to Rhys. This helps *him* understand she’s receiving something.

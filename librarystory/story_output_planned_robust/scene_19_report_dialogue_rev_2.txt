*   **Over-Reliance on Internal Thoughts/Telepathy:** The entire scene is almost exclusively communicated through thoughts and impressions. While establishing a mental link is fine, the *lack* of spoken dialogue feels unnatural, even for a fantastical setting. It creates a static feel. Consider adding brief verbalizations *even if* they simply acknowledge the mental communication ("I feel that too," "You're right," etc.).
*   **Repetitive Echoing:** Seris repeatedly *echoes* the received thoughts ("Lost?", "Remembrance?"). This feels like a narrative shortcut to show she's processing, but it doesn’t feel like natural conversation – even a mental one. Show her processing through internal monologue *instead* of having her literally repeat what she's receiving.
*   **"Breathing" as Dialogue Tag:** Using "breathed" repeatedly (“she breathed,” “I breathed”) is becoming a crutch. It’s overused and doesn’t add much nuance.
*   **"Always" as a Response:** The single-word response of "Always" feels weak and lacks character. Kaelen is a powerful entity; a more evocative or layered response would be more impactful.
*   **Info-Dump via Thought:** The explanation of the Order forbidding remembering feels clunky, even within a mental exchange. It's a lot of exposition delivered all at once. Consider revealing this information more gradually through fragmented images or impressions.
*   **Passive Voice & Lack of Action:** Because the communication is almost entirely mental, Seris feels very passive. She *receives* information instead of actively questioning or driving the conversation. Give her more agency.
*   **Shared Perception - Show, Don't Tell:** The petal appearing as a "shared perception" is *told* to us, not *shown*. How does Seris *experience* this? What does it *feel* like? Describe the sensation, the scent, the change in the atmosphere.

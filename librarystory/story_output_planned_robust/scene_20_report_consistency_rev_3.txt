## Consistency Check Report:

**Overall:** The new draft (Hemlock scene) is largely consistent with the established world and character motivations from the previous drafts. However, there are a few points needing attention.

**Specific Points:**

* **Sunstone Bloom's Role Expansion:** The Sunstone Bloom is now explicitly a "conduit to forgotten memories." While hinted at previously, this is a significant escalation of its importance. This is *not* an inconsistency, but a change that needs to be woven more consistently throughout the narrative. The earlier scenes should foreshadow this ability more strongly. It feels a bit sudden to be revealed so late.
* **Valerius's Hunting - Timing:** The final line about Valerius hunting them feels a *little* abrupt. While his observation was established, the leap to "hunting" feels faster than the narrative supports. It would be stronger if there were a more concrete indicator of increased threat *before* this final line. Perhaps a detail about the wards weakening or a specific sign of intrusion.
* **Hemlock's Motivation - Saving Texts:** While believable, Hemlock’s motivation for saving the texts could be strengthened. He states he couldn’t “stand by and watch them disappear.” Expanding on *why* he specifically felt compelled to preserve them, beyond just general scholarly objection, would add depth. Perhaps a personal connection to the lost knowledge.
* **"Memory Wells" - New Concept:** The introduction of "Memory Wells" is fine, but it’s a new element that requires a bit more grounding. A sentence or two describing *how* they functioned, even briefly, would make it feel less like an info-dump.
* **Church's Method of Suppression - "Erodes" vs. "Destroys":** The distinction between "eroding" and "destroying" knowledge is good, and highlights the Church's insidious methods. This is consistent and well-done.
* **Wards - State of Deterioration:** The mention of the wards not holding forever is good foreshadowing. However, it could be subtly linked to the Church’s increased scrutiny to create a stronger connection.

**Overall Assessment:**

The new draft is strong and adds significant depth to the world-building and the central conflict. The points above are mostly minor refinements rather than major inconsistencies. The biggest area for improvement is to more seamlessly integrate the expanded role of the Sunstone Bloom and to subtly build towards the escalation of Valerius's threat.



**Consistency Check: Mostly Passed, with noted areas for refinement.**

*   **Rhys’s opening line (“What are we looking for, exactly?”)** feels a little *too* on the nose. It’s functional, but lacks subtext. Given their situation (exploring a hidden chamber), a more character-revealing line might be better – perhaps a comment about the atmosphere, or a dry observation about Seris’s secretive nature.
*   **Kaelen’s thoughts are *very* expository.** While useful for the reader, they read as information dumps. Consider weaving this information into Seris's internal reactions or observations. Instead of “The Obsidian Circle…”, perhaps Seris feels a wave of dread *associated* with the symbol, triggering a half-remembered legend.
*   **Repetitive tension building:** The constant descriptions of Seris tightening her grip on the key, or muscles ticking, are becoming repetitive. Show the tension through *action* and *reaction* instead of telling us she's feeling it.
*   **Valerius’s motivations are stated, not shown.** He *says* he wants to ensure the realm’s safety, but his predatory smile and the unsettling presence of the cloaked figure suggest otherwise. Lean into that contrast. Let his actions and demeanor speak louder than his words.
*   **Seris’s repeated questions (“What power?” “What do you want with it?”)** while logical, make her feel a bit passive. Consider interspersing them with more assertive statements or observations.
*   **The exchange between Seris and Rhys is minimal.** The “shared understanding” is stated, not demonstrated. A brief, meaningful glance is good, but consider adding a subtle physical cue or a single, loaded word.
*   **“I think,” Seris said, her voice ringing with newfound resolve, “we have very different ideas about safety.”** This line feels a bit cliché. It's functional, but lacks originality. Consider a more unique and character-specific response.
*   **The pacing of information delivery is slightly off.** A lot of exposition is crammed into a short space, delivered through Kaelen's thoughts and Valerius’s explanations. This could be more effectively spread out, revealed through environmental details or character interactions.

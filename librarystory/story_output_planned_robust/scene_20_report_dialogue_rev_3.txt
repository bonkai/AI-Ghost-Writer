*   **Repetitive Exposition:** The dialogue relies *heavily* on Hemlock explaining things *to* Seris rather than *showing* us through interaction or letting Seris deduce information. For example, the repeated explanations of “they buried the memories,” “they suppress knowledge,” etc., feel like he's lecturing rather than conversing. Trim these and trust the reader to understand.
*   **Hemlock's Voice:** Hemlock’s voice is consistently…flat. He’s a man haunted by secrets, exiled, and fearful. Inject more *texture* into his speech. Consider fragmented sentences, pauses, more physical reactions *during* speech (a shaky breath, a hand trembling as he speaks). Right now, it’s mostly just information delivery.
*   **Seris's Reactions:** Seris is *very* reactive. She asks questions, but they often feel like prompts for Hemlock to continue his exposition. Give her more agency. Have her challenge his statements, offer her own interpretations, or express stronger emotional responses.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Sunstone Bloom):** The reveal of the Sunstone Bloom as a “key” feels a little abrupt. Foreshadow this earlier, or build more tension around it. Perhaps Hemlock notices it immediately upon entering, or reacts with a specific fear that isn't fully explained until later.
*   **"They" is Vague:** The constant use of "they" (the Church/Order) is distancing. While mystery is good, it's becoming a crutch. Occasionally, name specific individuals (like Valerius) to ground the threat.
*   **Seris's Final Question:** While a good closing line, "How do we fight a war against forgetting?" is a bit on-the-nose. Consider a more subtle expression of her resolve – a tightening of her grip on her dagger, a determined look in her eyes – and let the reader infer the question.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** We don't get a strong sense of *how* Hemlock's exile affected him beyond stating it. Show us his paranoia, his grief, his desperation. A small detail – constantly checking the tunnel entrance, flinching at sounds – would be more impactful than telling us he's afraid.

*   **Repetitive Internal State:** You repeatedly mention the tremor in the character’s hands and her attempts to appear firm. While conveying nervousness is good, it’s overdone. Show, don't *tell* us she's trying to project confidence. Let her *actions* and *responses* reveal her internal state.
*   **Rhys’s Motivations - Too Direct:** Rhys lays out his motivations *very* clearly. While clarity is good, a bit more mystery would heighten tension. Instead of explicitly stating the Aethelstone’s importance for “everyone,” hint at the consequences of *not* finding it. Let the reader infer the stakes.
*   **“You’re a key…” – Cliché:** The "you're a key" line is a tired trope. While it gets the point across, it lacks originality. Consider a more subtle way to convey that she possesses unique skills.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – The Price:** The initial “price” feels…flat. It's information for information. Explore a more nuanced exchange. Perhaps Rhys wants her to *interpret* something, not just provide knowledge. Or maybe he wants a favor that’s morally ambiguous.
*   **Kaelen’s Presence – Underutilized:** Kaelen’s whisper feels like a narrative convenience rather than an integrated element. How does this internal voice *affect* the character’s dialogue or choices? Does it create internal conflict?
*   **Over-Explanation:** Several lines feel like exposition disguised as dialogue. For example, Rhys explaining what the Aethelstone *is* feels like telling, not showing. Trust the reader to piece things together.
*   **Rhys's Weariness - Show, Don't Tell:** You *tell* us Rhys is weary, but his dialogue doesn't consistently reflect that. A truly weary character might be more concise, less prone to elaborate explanations, or have a different rhythm to their speech.
*   **Dialogue Tags:** While not excessive, the frequent use of "he said," "she said" could be varied for a more dynamic read.

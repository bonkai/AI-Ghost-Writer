The library air pressed against Seris’s skin, a subtle weight that settled deep in her bones. Dust motes hung suspended, almost frozen in the shafts of light. A tremor ran through the stone floor, vibrating up through her boots and into her teeth. She braced a hand against a bookshelf, the ancient wood humming with discordant energy.

Rhys, studying a faded map, glanced up, his brow furrowing as he registered her tension. His hand didn’t immediately go to his dagger, but drifted to subtly adjust the weight of his pack – a quiet readiness, held in check. He watched her, a question forming in his eyes, but remained silent, gauging her reaction.

The library *shifted*. Not visibly, but at the periphery of her vision, familiar lines blurred and wavered. The scent of old parchment and beeswax twisted, overlaid with a metallic tang – ozone and something akin to dried blood. A subtle pressure descended, the feeling of a vast network beneath her feet, extending outwards, sustaining the room. It strained, a bowstring drawn taut, then *held*. 

A discordant note fractured the harmony. Luminous threads of energy flickered, dimmed. A single thread snapped, and darkness seeped into the crack, widening it. Another followed, then another, each loss sending a ripple through the network. The energy didn’t explode, but *retreated*, drawing inward, leaving a hollow ache in Seris’s chest and a prickling static on her skin. She stumbled, a metallic taste blooming on her tongue, and pressed a hand to her temple, fighting the disorientation.

Rhys caught her arm, his grip firm and silent, anchoring her. He didn’t speak, simply held her steady, his gaze searching her face, assessing the depth of her distress. The contact grounded her, but the disorientation lingered.

Kaelen’s consciousness brushed against hers – not words, but raw sensation: the echoing resonance of a nexus, the weight of ancient power. A flicker of icy dread, then an image – a symbol of interlocking stars etched on dark obsidian, pulsing with power, but laced with hairline cracks, the light within flickering erratically. She gasped, a sharp intake of breath, and her fingers tightened on Rhys’s arm.

He felt the tremor run through her, his grip tightening almost imperceptibly. He waited, letting her experience it, his expression unreadable. 

“That symbol…” she murmured, recalling fragmented texts, the words tasting like ash.

Rhys tightened his grip, his gaze searching her face, concern etched around his eyes, but held back. He didn't ask what it was, allowing her to speak when she was ready.

Kaelen’s panic surged – a desperate, wordless plea. The image sharpened – obsidian, stars, cracks widening. *Firestone…mountains…heart…bleedthrough…* A cold wind whipped across her skin, the smell of sulfur stinging her nostrils. A deep, resonant thrumming vibrated through her bones. She squeezed her eyes shut, fighting the onslaught, her knuckles white against Rhys’s leather jerkin.

Silence descended, absolute and heavy. Seris sagged against Rhys, legs trembling. She forced herself to breathe, to focus, and slowly opened her eyes, blinking against the dim light. She looked at Rhys, then away, gathering her thoughts. The vision felt…imprinted, not merely seen.

Rhys waited, his expression carefully neutral.

“Firestone Mountains?” he asked, his voice tight with concern, but measured.

“It’s connected to the source of the network,” she said, her voice strained, the words feeling…given, not reasoned. “Something is contained within it, ancient and terrible. The seal is failing, and the cracks are widest there.”

She looked up at Rhys, determination hardening her gaze. “We have to go. Before whatever is contained within breaks free.”

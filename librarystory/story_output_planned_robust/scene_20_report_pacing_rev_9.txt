*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening paragraphs (1-3) are largely exposition delivered through Hemlock’s internal state and reminiscence. While establishing character and world-building is important, this section feels *too* slow considering the scene is in the middle of the narrative (scene 20/30). The pacing needs to be quicker to establish immediate stakes.
*   **Tension Peaks & Valleys:** The scraping sound (paragraph 5) is a good tension spike, but it’s immediately defused by Hemlock’s assessment and Seris’s positioning. This isn’t necessarily *bad*, but the quick resolution diminishes the impact. Consider extending the uncertainty or adding a false alarm.
*   **Repetitive Gaze/Glance Direction:** The constant references to Hemlock glancing at the tunnel entrance become predictable and lose their effectiveness. Vary the focus – have him look at Seris, the mosaic, his hands – to create more dynamic tension.
*   **Delayed Action:** Seris’s internal resolve solidifies toward the end of the scene, but there's a lack of *doing*. She’s largely reactive. Introducing a small, immediate action she takes (beyond positioning herself) could elevate the tension and demonstrate her agency.
*   **Final Exchange - Good, But Could Be Sharper:** The final exchange with Hemlock is strong thematically, but the tension feels slightly diluted by the relatively calm delivery. A shorter, more urgent final line from Hemlock could create a more compelling cliffhanger.
*   **Crimson Stain – Underutilized:** The crimson stain on the sketch is mentioned multiple times but doesn’t *do* much. Consider having it be a visual trigger for Hemlock or a source of immediate discomfort for Seris, adding a physical element to the tension.

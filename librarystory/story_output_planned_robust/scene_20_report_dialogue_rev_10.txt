*   **Hemlock’s opening monologue feels overly expository.** While establishing his backstory is important, delivering it as a speech feels unnatural. Consider breaking it up with Seris’s reactions or questions. Instead of *telling* us what they feared, *show* it through his reactions to specific memories.
*   **Seris's demand, "Why bury it?" is a bit blunt.** Given her character (observant, protective), a more nuanced question might be more fitting. Perhaps something like, "So, they silenced it…all of it?" or a statement of observation like, “They feared what it represented, didn’t they?”
*   **The “futile gesture” line from Hemlock feels cliché.** It’s a common trope. Consider a more specific reason for his pessimism, tied to a personal loss or failed attempt.
*   **Repetitive action/internal state:** Seris repeatedly tightens her grip on the pack. This happens three times. While it conveys tension, it becomes noticeable and weakens the impact. Find other ways to show her resolve.
*   **Hemlock’s final lines are strong, but the “seed for the future” metaphor feels slightly forced.** It’s not *bad*, but it lacks the grit and realism of the earlier dialogue.
*   **Subtext opportunity:** The brief hand-covering moment is good, but could be amplified. Is there a history there? A sense of mentorship? A regret? A flicker of hope? Let that touch *mean* something beyond simple grounding.
*   **The acknowledgement of a presence ("They are already listening") is a bit on-the-nose.** Consider showing this through a change in Hemlock's behavior—a subtle shift in his gaze, a tightening of his muscles—rather than directly stating it.

The flickering lamplight cast long shadows across the cluttered workshop. Rhys hadn’t touched the Firepeak maps since Valerius’s departure. Instead, he was meticulously cleaning his sketching tools, a pointless task given the layer of dust already coating everything.

“He suspects something,” Seris said, her voice low, barely a whisper against the workshop’s quiet hum. She was sharpening a set of obsidian needles, the rhythmic scraping a counterpoint to Rhys’s obsessive cleaning.

Rhys didn’t look up. “Of course he does. We’re asking questions no one asks.”

“It’s more than that. He doesn’t believe our explanation. He thinks we’re looking for something *else* on Firepeak.”

Rhys finally met her gaze, his expression unreadable. “And are we?”

Seris hesitated, then nodded slowly. “The Sunstone Bloom is a distraction. A plausible reason to be climbing a dangerous mountain. But we’re really after the Skyglass fragments, aren’t we?”

Rhys’s jaw tightened. “Information is currency, Seris. The Skyglass fragments…they’re valuable. To the right people.”

“Valuable enough to risk Valerius’s displeasure?”

Rhys sighed, running a hand through his already disheveled hair. “I have a contact. A collector. He’s…discreet. He’ll pay handsomely for any confirmed Skyglass fragments. Enough to keep us funded for months.”

“And you didn’t tell me about this contact?” Seris’s voice was dangerously quiet.

Rhys flinched. “It was…complicated. He’s not someone I trust easily. And I wanted to be certain we had something before involving you.”

A sharp rap at the workshop door startled them both. Rhys quickly stowed the cleaning tools, a frantic energy about his movements.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her hand instinctively reaching for the small dagger concealed beneath her sleeve.

“A merchant,” a gruff voice replied. “Looking to trade for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask.”

Rhys exchanged a tense glance with Seris. This wasn’t the collector. The collector operated through intermediaries, never directly. He opened the door to reveal a man cloaked in shadow, his face obscured by a wide-brimmed hat. The man’s eyes, however, were sharp and assessing, taking in the workshop with a practiced gaze.

“I understand you have charts of the Firepeak region,” the man said, his voice raspy. He placed a heavy pouch on the workbench, the clink of coins audible even over the crackling lamp. “I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps, particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys’s eyes narrowed. “What kind of formations?”

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin smile. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” He paused, his gaze sweeping over Seris. “I’ve heard whispers of your…research. The Sunstone Bloom. A beautiful flower, but hardly worth risking life and limb for.”

Seris stepped forward, her voice cold. “We’re simply botanists, cataloging rare species.”

The merchant chuckled, a dry, humorless sound. “Botanists who happen to be interested in ancient legends and forgotten pathways. Don’t insult my intelligence.” He tapped the pouch on the workbench. “I’ll double what you’d get from any local cartographer for a map showing the location of the Whisperwind Caves.”

Rhys’s breath hitched. The Whisperwind Caves. A place mentioned only in fragmented texts, rumored to hold a concentration of Skyglass fragments. He hadn’t even told Seris about them.

“How do you know about the Whisperwind Caves?” he asked, his voice tight with suspicion.

The merchant’s smile widened. “Let’s just say I have…sources. And I’m willing to pay handsomely for information. Or, if you prefer, I can simply acquire it myself.” He glanced meaningfully at the dagger concealed beneath Seris’s sleeve. “Though that might be…messier.”

Rhys looked at Seris, a silent question in his eyes. He’d been playing a dangerous game, keeping secrets, seeking a quick profit. Now, it seemed, the game had escalated. And he wasn’t sure he could win it alone. 

“We have a map,” Rhys said slowly, his gaze fixed on the merchant. “But it will cost you more than just coins.”

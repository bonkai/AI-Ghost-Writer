*   **Initial Setup (Paragraphs 1-2):** The opening is a bit *too* slow. The detailed description of the herb-grinding and mixture preparation, while establishing setting and Rhys’s skill, doesn’t immediately translate to tension. Consider shortening this, or interweaving a subtle sense of unease *within* the description.
*   **Dialogue Build (Paragraphs 3-7):** The tension *starts* to build effectively with Valerius’s entrance, but it’s somewhat reliant on Kaelen’s internal warnings. While Kaelen is a useful device, the tension should also be felt *without* the narration. Show Seris’s apprehension through more physical cues (quickened pulse, shallow breath) *before* Kaelen speaks.
*   **Mid-Scene Plateau (Paragraphs 7-9):** The exchange with Valerius is good, but feels a little *static*. The back-and-forth, while creating suspicion, lacks a clear escalation. Consider adding a more pointed question from Valerius, or a more revealing reaction from Seris/Rhys, to raise the stakes.
*   **Escalation & Exit (Paragraphs 10-13):** The final push – the realization they’re being watched, the quick exit – is the strongest part of the scene. The pacing here is excellent. However, the transition from Valerius disappearing to Seris deciding to leave feels slightly abrupt. A beat of Seris *processing* his gaze, a moment of internal conflict, would strengthen the impact.
*   **Kaelen's Role:** Kaelen often *tells* us the tension rather than letting us *feel* it. Reduce Kaelen’s direct pronouncements and instead show Seris’s internal state, allowing the reader to infer the danger.



Overall, the scene is *mostly* well-paced, with a strong finish. The primary issue is a somewhat slow start and reliance on narration to convey tension. Tightening the opening and *showing* more apprehension through character actions would significantly improve the scene’s impact.

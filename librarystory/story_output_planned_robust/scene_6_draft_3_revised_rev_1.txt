## Draft 3

The chill started at the base of my skull, a prickling sensation that intensified with each attempt. I reached out again, not with words, but with intent, a focused probe towards the image of the serpent. It wasn't a clear picture, more a fragment of carved stone, a sinuous curve hinting at something ancient and powerful.

This time, the contact felt different. Not a blank wall, but a yielding, almost viscous resistance, like pushing against oiled silk. A flicker of something beyond the stone – a vast, echoing chamber, choked with shadow, and the faintest impression of…watching eyes. Then, a tightening, a pressure building behind my temples.

“Is everything alright, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice, smooth as polished obsidian, cut through my concentration. He hadn’t moved from his desk, but his gaze, unblinking, felt like a physical weight.

I forced my eyes open, blinking against the dim light. “Just…admiring the architecture,” I stammered, the lie tasting like ash. “This place is…remarkable.”

I tried again, pushing harder, willing the serpent image to solidify, to *reveal* itself. A surge of energy coursed through me, and for a fleeting moment, I felt a connection, a resonance. Not with a being, but with the stone itself, with the very foundations of the Atheneum. It felt…old. Immeasurably so. And deeply, profoundly *unwilling*.

A sharp spike of pain lanced through my head, and I gasped, stumbling back. A bookshelf rattled, and a heavy tome tumbled to the floor with a muffled thud. The scent of aged paper and dust filled the air.

“Careful now,” Hemlock said, his voice laced with a dangerous amusement. He didn't move to help. “These volumes are quite fragile. And so are your…abilities, it seems.”

I ignored him, pressing through the discomfort, focusing all my will. This wasn’t just about seeing a carving; it was about understanding what it *meant*. I sent another probe outwards, a desperate plea for clarity. This time, the resistance wasn't a yielding silk, but a solid, unyielding wall of stone. It felt like slamming into a glacier.

The air around me grew cold, and a subtle stillness descended, muffling all sound. I didn’t notice the change, too consumed by the effort. But I *felt* it. A tremor in the floor, a low hum resonating from the Atheneum’s core, and a prickling sensation on my skin, as if I were standing too close to a storm.

Then, the wall *moved*. Not physically, but…internally. It wasn't just blocking me; it was actively *repelling* me, pushing against my mind with a force that felt ancient and hostile. A sense of warning flooded my senses: *Stay away. This is not for you.*

I recoiled, clutching my head, the pressure behind my eyes threatening to overwhelm me. The humming intensified, then abruptly ceased, leaving a ringing silence in its wake. The cold remained, clinging to me like a shroud.

“Perhaps you’ve been studying for too long,” Hemlock said, rising from his desk and slowly approaching. His shadow stretched across the floor, engulfing me. “The Atheneum can be…overwhelming for those unfamiliar with its energies. And some, it seems, are simply…not meant to understand.”

I backed away, my hand instinctively reaching beneath my cloak, fingers closing around the hilt of my dagger. “I’m fine,” I said, my voice tight with effort. “I just…felt a bit lightheaded.”

One last time, I sent a desperate plea outwards, a fragile tendril of intent reaching for the serpent image. *Please. I need to understand.*

Silence. Absolute, impenetrable silence. The Atheneum hadn't just shut me out; it had *erased* me, as if I were never there. The connection was severed, leaving a void where there had once been a faint glimmer of hope.

I realized, with a sinking feeling, that I wasn’t communicating with a spirit, or even a being. I was trying to reach something far older, far more powerful, and it clearly didn’t want to be disturbed. And it had made its displeasure known.

Hemlock stopped a few feet away, his dark eyes boring into mine. “The Atheneum chooses who it reveals its secrets to,” he said, his voice a low murmur, almost a caress. “And it seems, Initiate, that it has not chosen you. Perhaps it sensed…a certain ambition. A desire for knowledge that exceeds your station.”

He paused, his gaze sweeping over me with chilling assessment. “Some secrets are best left buried. For your own sake, and for the sake of those who would be…disappointed by your curiosity.” He took another step closer, his voice dropping to a whisper. “Consider this a warning.”

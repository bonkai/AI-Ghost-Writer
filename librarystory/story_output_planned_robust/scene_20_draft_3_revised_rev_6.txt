## Scene 20: Draft 3

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, her fingers tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure. The stone felt cold beneath her touch, and she instinctively tightened her grip on her dagger, scanning the shadows. Not out of fear, but habit – a practiced awareness honed by years within the Iron Church.

Old Man Hemlock emerged from the gloom, pacing a tight circle. He didn’t *look* at the mosaic, but *around* it, his gaze darting between the shadows and the stone, a frantic energy radiating from him. He stopped abruptly, hand rising to his ear as if straining to listen, then lowered himself to a weathered stool, joints creaking. He avoided Seris’s gaze, studying the worn stone floor, memorizing its cracks as if seeking a pattern, a hidden message.

“I believed in the purity of the Light,” he began, his voice brittle, a ghost of former conviction. He glanced at the mosaic, a flicker of longing – or perhaps regret – in his eyes, then quickly looked away. “A meticulous cataloger, I was. Every blessing documented, every deviation noted. I thought I understood order.”

Seris leaned forward, her hand instinctively reaching for the Sunstone Bloom sketch within her pack. The crimson stain on the aged parchment felt heavy, a weight mirroring the secrets it hinted at.

“Records of the Before-Times.” Hemlock’s voice dropped to a rasping whisper. He swept his gaze around the cavern, a quick, nervous motion. “A world *steeped* in magic, not controlled blessings, but a raw, untamed force. It wasn’t chaos, child. It was…potential.” He traced the lines of the vortex on the mosaic with his eyes. “I saw images…cities built of shimmering crystal, powered by the lifeblood of the earth. Gardens that bloomed with bioluminescent flora, responding to thought, to emotion.” He reached out, his fingers brushing Seris’s arm, then snatched back as if burned, his eyes widening in a flash of panic. The touch was fleeting, almost involuntary, a reflex betraying a deeply ingrained fear.

He stepped back into the shadows, his voice barely a murmur. “They didn’t just fear the magic, child. They feared what people *remembered* with it. The power it gave them, the questions it inspired.”

Seris’s jaw tightened, her fingers tracing the outline of the sketch. She wasn’t simply following a legend; she was retracing steps deliberately obscured.

“The Order deemed those memories dangerous. They didn’t destroy them. They *buried* them. And built the Church upon the grave, a monument to forgetting.” A faint scraping echoed from the tunnel entrance, and Hemlock whirled around, hand instinctively reaching for a hidden knife. He scanned the darkness, breath coming in ragged gasps, his body tense. Years of paranoia hadn’t faded.

Seris followed his gaze, her hand tightening on the hilt of her dagger. She wasn't afraid, but alert. Prepared.

Hemlock lowered his hand, but didn’t fully relax. He glanced at the Sunstone Bloom sketch peeking from her pack. “They believe the flower might unlock something. Something they desperately want to keep buried.”

“I questioned it,” he continued, his voice barely a whisper, his eyes haunted. “I dared to ask *why*. And for that, I was branded a heretic. Stripped of my rank. Exiled.” He stumbled back, clutching his chest, a tremor running through his body. The memory still physically pained him.

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls.

Hemlock’s gaze drifted to the shadows, a flicker of defiance in his eyes. “I couldn’t stand by and watch them disappear. They are a warning, child. A reminder of what we’ve lost…and what we’ve been made to forget.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris, his expression grim.

Seris met his gaze, her jaw tight. The crimson stain on the sketch felt like a brand, a weight on her soul. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower. She was chasing a truth the Church would kill to keep buried. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, she felt a prickling sensation on her skin, a sense of being watched.

Beneath the fear, a new resolve hardened within her. This wasn’t just about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it. She tightened her grip on her pack, knuckles white. "What can we do?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper, but filled with a newfound determination.

Hemlock’s gaze flickered to the mosaic, then back to Seris. A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “We remember,” he said, his voice gaining strength. “We tell the stories. And we protect the embers of what remains.” He reached out, his hand covering hers on her pack, a brief, grounding touch. “The flower…it’s not just a key to the past. It’s a seed for the future.”

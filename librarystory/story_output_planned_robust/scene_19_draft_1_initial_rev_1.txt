The library had emptied for the night. Only a handful of lamps cast pools of warm light across the towering shelves, leaving the upper reaches lost in shadow. Seris sat at their worktable, not sketching, not reading, but simply…present. The weight of Valerius’s scrutiny still pressed on her, a cold spot on her skin.

A flicker of movement caught her eye. Not physical, but a subtle disturbance in the air, a ripple in the patterns of dust motes dancing in a lamplight beam. Kaelen.

*He’s here,* she thought, not voicing the words, knowing he’d hear them regardless.

A book on a nearby shelf trembled, its spine flexing as if stretching. Then, a single page detached, fluttering down to land open on the table before her. The ink hadn't fully dried; a faint scent of parchment and something…older, lingered. It wasn’t a message, not exactly. A sketch. A rough charcoal rendering of Firepeak Mountain, but not as a cartographer would draw it. This depicted the mountain as a *being*, its peaks like jagged teeth, its slopes etched with lines of ancient power.

*Beautiful,* she thought, and felt a warmth bloom in her chest, a response that wasn't hers alone. Kaelen’s…presence, felt like a gentle pressure against her mind, a shared appreciation.

*It remembers,* a thought formed in her head, clear and distinct, yet not spoken. It wasn't her voice, but Kaelen's, woven into the fabric of her consciousness.

She traced the lines of the drawing with a fingertip. “What do you mean?” she thought back, directing the question inward.

A different book shifted on a high shelf, nearly tumbling. Kaelen was…agitated. *The mountain isn’t just rock and earth, Seris. It holds echoes. Memories. The land itself remembers what has been lost.*

“Lost?”

The page before her blurred, the charcoal lines seeming to writhe. *The old magic. The knowledge the Order tried to erase. It’s buried within Firepeak. And the Sunstone Bloom…it’s a key.*

Seris frowned. “A key to what?”

A long silence stretched, broken only by the crackling of the lamps. She could *feel* Kaelen searching, sifting through layers of forgotten lore. Finally, a single word echoed in her mind: *Remembrance.*

“Remembrance?” she repeated, aloud this time, the sound startling in the quiet library.

*The Order didn’t just forbid magic, Seris. They forbade remembering. They feared what the past held. Firepeak…it remembers everything. And the Bloom…it can unlock those memories.*

Seris felt a shiver crawl down her spine. This wasn’t just about finding a rare healing plant anymore. This was about something far older, far more dangerous.

“Valerius…” she thought, the name a dark weight in her mind. “He knows, doesn’t he? That we’re looking for more than just a flower?”

A book slammed shut on a nearby shelf, the sound echoing like a gunshot. Kaelen’s frustration was palpable. *He suspects. He always suspects. He’s a guardian of the forgetting, Seris. He’ll do anything to protect it.*

Seris closed her eyes, her temples throbbing. “We have to be careful.”

A gentle warmth enveloped her mind, a soothing presence. *Always.*

She opened her eyes, looking at the sketch of Firepeak. It no longer seemed like a drawing, but a warning. A challenge. And, strangely, a promise. The mountain remembered. And now, so did she. 

A single petal, crimson and perfectly formed, drifted down from the pages of an open herbal, landing softly on the sketch. It wasn’t a physical petal, but an image, woven into her mind by Kaelen. A silent affirmation. They weren’t alone. And the journey ahead, however dangerous, felt…less daunting.

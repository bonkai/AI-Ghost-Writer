*   **Rhys's Characterization:** While consistent, Rhys’s pragmatism feels *very* consistent. Every line is a practical response. Consider adding a moment of curiosity, or a slightly more emotional reaction to the discovery, to make him feel less like a functional prop and more like a fully realized character. Even a brief acknowledgement of the *awe* of the discovery would help.
*   **Kaelen’s Voice – Impact & Integration:** Kaelen’s pronouncements, while effective in creating tension, feel a little *too* direct. They function as exposition. Consider ways to weave his information *through* Seris’s internal monologue, or have it manifest as a physical sensation that *she* interprets. This would create more mystery and less “info dump.” For example, instead of “The Stone Heart is a prison,” perhaps Seris feels a wave of dread and instinctively understands the chamber isn't a place of power, but confinement.
*   **Seris’s Dismissal:** Seris quickly dismisses her premonitions ("I don't know. But we’ve stumbled onto something dangerous…") and then immediately states the obvious ("I feel watched"). This feels a little rushed and weakens the impact of the growing threat. Allow her to *linger* in the fear for a moment, to grapple with the implications before moving on.
*   **"By the Ancestors..."** This feels like a stock reaction. While not *bad*, it's a bit cliché. Consider a more unique reaction that reflects Rhys's personality or background.
*   **Repetitive Feeling of Danger:** The dialogue repeatedly emphasizes that something dangerous has been unearthed. While this reinforces the stakes, it could be more impactful if the danger were revealed more subtly through character reactions and atmospheric details.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – Valerius:** The line about Valerius feels a bit isolated. It's a good setup, but it needs to be revisited later, or connected to something more immediate, to feel less like a dropped plot thread.

## Scene 23

The workshop smelled of oiled steel and dried herbs. A sharp rap at the door broke the quiet. Seris ran a honing stone along a scalpel, the rasp deliberate. Rhys, ostensibly organizing charts, kept glancing at the door, dark shadows beneath his eyes betraying his lack of sleep.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her voice low, gaze fixed on the sharpening stone.

“A merchant. Looking for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask,” a gravelly voice replied.

Rhys exchanged a quick glance with Seris and opened the door to reveal a man swallowed by shadow, a wide-brimmed hat obscuring his features. He didn’t offer a hand, simply stepped inside, filling the small space with a palpable stillness.

“I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps,” the merchant said, his voice a rasp against stone. He deposited a heavy pouch onto the workbench, the clink of coins sharp. “Particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys narrowed his gaze. “What *kind* of formations are you interested in?” He subtly positioned himself between the merchant and a shadowed corner of the workshop, his hand instinctively tightening around a quill.

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin smile, but it didn’t reach his eyes. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” His gaze drifted to Seris, lingering on the worn cord binding her tunic, then tracing the line of her jaw. “You’ve both ventured deep into those peaks, haven't you? Seen things most haven't.”

Seris traced a botanical illustration with a calloused fingertip. “We document the region’s flora. Rare specimens require extensive surveying. It’s meticulous work, demanding knowledge of the terrain. We’ve cataloged fault lines, mineral deposits…”

The merchant chuckled, a dry sound. “Botanists who know the old paths. Convenient.” He tapped the pouch. “A cut of my payment for your…discretion?”

Rhys’s eyes met Seris’s, a flicker of desperation in them. Before he could speak, Seris raised a hand, silencing him. 

“We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice even. “However, we know individuals who might. Individuals who understand the value of silence.” She finally met the merchant’s gaze, her eyes the color of flint. “And such knowledge commands a substantial premium.”

A beat of silence stretched, thick and tense. The merchant’s eyes narrowed, studying Seris, gauging her resolve. He saw Rhys’s barely concealed anxiety. A slow smile touched his lips. "Intriguing." He paused, then leaned forward, lowering his voice. “I need that map, and I need it now. I’m not interested in protracted negotiations or…complications.”

Seris didn't flinch. “Then perhaps you should have come with a more…direct offer.” She gestured to a stool with a slight tilt of her head. “Shall we discuss terms, or shall we waste each other’s time?”

The merchant’s eyes hardened. “I dislike being delayed.” He gestured towards the stool with a gloved hand. “And I *especially* dislike being lectured.” He paused, his gaze lingering on Rhys, a silent warning. “Or would you prefer I seek this information elsewhere?” A slow smile touched his lips. “There are always…other paths to the peaks. And some paths…are best left untrodden.”

Seris met his gaze, unwavering. “Our price isn’t merely coin,” she said, her voice low and steady. “It’s assurance that this information won’t fall into the wrong hands. Assurance that those who shared it with us remain safe. We need guarantees, merchant. Substantial ones.” She glanced at Rhys, a silent command, then turned back to the merchant. “And we require a down payment. Before we even discuss the location of this…map.”

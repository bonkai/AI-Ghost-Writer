The passage opened into a circular chamber, smaller than the main archives, yet radiating a subtle power. Books lay scattered across the floor, some open, their brittle pages catching the faint, phosphorescent glow of symbols etched into the walls. The symbols pulsed with a slow rhythm, a low hum vibrating through the stone and into her teeth.

In the center of the chamber stood a pedestal crafted from obsidian. Upon it rested a single, leather-bound volume. The cover was unadorned, yet the leather felt strangely warm, almost alive, radiating a subtle heat. A scent of dried flowers and dust clung to the air.

“The *Liber Memoriae*,” Kaelen breathed, the name a hushed reverence. A flicker of something – pain? – crossed his mental projection, gone before she could fully grasp it.

Seris approached cautiously, her gaze sweeping across the room. “What is it?”

“It doesn’t *hold* information, not as we understand it.” Kaelen’s hand hovered over the book, not quite touching. “It holds echoes. Fragments of experiences, emotions…memories deliberately erased.”

Seris reached out and gently lifted the book from the pedestal. It was surprisingly light. As her fingers brushed the cover, a wave of dizziness washed over her. A fragmented image flared in her mind: a vast, sun-drenched city crumbling into dust. Not just a vision – she *felt* the sun’s heat scorching her skin, the weight of falling stone, the despair of a people losing everything. She stumbled back, clutching the book, a sob escaping her lips.

“What was that?” she gasped, her voice trembling. “I… I *felt* it.”

“A glimpse.” Kaelen’s voice was tight with worry. “The book shares its memories. Be careful, Seris. Some are best left forgotten. This isn’t knowledge, it’s…suffering.”

She looked down at the book, a strange mixture of fear and fascination swirling within her. “What kind of memories?”

“The kind that could shatter everything you believe.” Kaelen’s gaze flickered around the chamber. “Someone went to great lengths to bury these. They won’t want them disturbed.”

Seris stared at the book, a chilling realization dawning. This wasn't about lost knowledge; it was about confronting a deliberately buried trauma. And she had a feeling the consequences would be far greater than she could imagine. 

“This isn’t just about the Sunstone anymore, is it?” she asked, her gaze meeting Kaelen’s.

Kaelen’s gaze swept the chamber again, a flicker of panic in his eyes. “No, Seris. It’s about something older, far more dangerous. The Sunstone is just a piece of it. This book…this chamber…they hold a secret someone wants desperately to keep buried. And they’ll do anything to protect it.” He paused, his voice barely a whisper. “We’ve walked into a trap.”

A scraping sound echoed from the passage behind them. Both turned, Seris instinctively reaching for her blade. A figure stood silhouetted in the dim light, blocking their only escape. A man, tall and gaunt, clad in the dark robes of the Order. He held a long, slender blade, its surface gleaming with an unnatural sheen.

“The *Liber Memoriae*,” the man said, his voice a cold rasp. “It belongs to us. Return it, and you may live.”

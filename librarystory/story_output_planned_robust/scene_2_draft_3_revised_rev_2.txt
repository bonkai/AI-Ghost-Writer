Darkness. Not the black of a disconnected monitor, but a total absence. A pressure, like lag in a full-VR rig, squeezed at the edges of… everything. Or where everything *should* be. Tried to lift a hand, check for restraints—nothing. No arm, no body. Just… awareness. A floating cursor with no screen.

Panic flared—cold, sharp, a system crash. Gasping felt…wrong. The *idea* of breath, the muscle memory of it, but no lungs to fill. Tried to shout, to ping *something*, but the signal died before it sent, choked by static. Untethered. Floating in null space. *What the hell happened?* The thought echoed, weak, fragmented. Not a voice, just… code struggling to execute. Last thing… the game. The pressure building, that searing ache behind the eyes. Fragments flickered – a glowing screen, frantic key presses, then white. Total system failure? Heart attack? A catastrophic lag spike? A cruel joke of a final save?

Tried to focus, to lock onto something solid, but thoughts scattered like dropped packets. Losing coherence. Dissolving into… nothing. *Need to reboot. Need a diagnostic. Anything.* The urge wasn’t logical, not for something that wasn’t… *anything*. It was a reflex, a desperate attempt to reassert control over a system spiraling into oblivion.

Then, a shift. Not location, but texture. The darkness wasn't flat anymore. It pulsed with a faint, ethereal glow, and with it, sound. Not a roar, but a hum, like distant server fans working overtime. Architectural. Shapes resolving, indistinct at first, then… a vast, cavernous space.

*Within* something.

“Hello?” Projected the thought, a desperate signal flare. It felt… flimsy, a whisper lost in a hurricane.

A response came, not as sound, but as *feeling*. A brush against awareness, like a gentle ping, yet laced with an ancient stillness. Not a voice, but an impression – patient, observing, and undeniably *old*. It didn’t *answer*, not immediately. It *considered*.

*Aware.*

The single word resonated, and with it, the unsettling sensation of being scanned. Like an admin was watching the server, assessing a new process. A chill ran through… well, not *through* anything, but the sensation was undeniably there. It felt less like examination and more like… cataloging.

Tried to orient, map the space, but it was like rendering a dream in low poly. Structures stretched endlessly, shifting, rearranging. Books. Countless volumes stacked on shelves that disappeared into the gloom, their spines unreadable. The air—though no lungs to breathe—was thick with the scent of aged paper and forgotten data. It wasn't a smell, exactly. More like a flood of information, a data stream of cellulose and ink washing over awareness. Each volume seemed to pulse with a quiet energy, a silent story waiting to be unlocked.

A library. A library beyond imagining. But no librarian in sight. A glitch in the system? A system with no operator?

“Where am I?” Projected again, desperation rising. The thought felt… brittle, like a corrupted file.

The response came slower this time, like a firewall actively blocking the connection. A subtle weight to it, a sense of… amusement? It wasn't malicious, not exactly. More… detached.

*Between.*

“Between what?”

A long pause. Unsettling. Then, a chilling revelation, delivered with the distinct impression of something *watching*. Something that had seen… everything.

*Between worlds.*

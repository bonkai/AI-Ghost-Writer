```
They’d successfully steered Valerius towards the herbal texts, offering him a trail of ‘promising leads’ about terrestrial causes for the blight. He’d disappeared into the stacks with a satisfied nod, but the reprieve felt fragile.  Kaelen’s silence was the most unsettling part. He hadn’t offered a single thought, a single nudge, since Valerius had withdrawn, and the absence felt…heavy.  Seris ran a hand over the worn spine of a book, the scent of aged parchment usually comforting, now feeling strangely stale.

A subtle shift in the air prickled her skin.  The library, normally a haven of quietude, seemed to hold its breath.  A low hum resonated beneath the floorboards, barely perceptible, yet deeply unsettling.  Then, a wave of disorientation washed over Seris. The edges of her vision blurred, the familiar rows of bookshelves seeming to waver and distort. The scent of parchment twisted, becoming overlaid with a metallic tang, like ozone after a lightning strike.

“Seris?” Rhys’s voice, usually a calming presence, sounded distant, muffled as if heard through water. He was reaching for her, his brow furrowed with concern.

Before she could respond, the disorientation blossomed into a cascade of images, flooding her mind with a power that wasn’t her own. Not memories, but *visions*. A sky unlike any she’d ever seen, a canvas of impossible stars, swirling nebulae painted across an infinite blackness.  Colossal structures of obsidian and silver, impossibly vast, reaching towards the heavens like skeletal fingers.  Figures cloaked in shadow moved within them, chanting in a language that resonated not in her ears, but in her very bones.  Then, a blinding flash of white light, followed by…fracture.  The structures crumbling, the sky tearing apart, and a wave of consuming darkness swallowing everything whole.

She gasped, stumbling back, her hand flying to her temple.  The visions subsided, leaving her breathless, disoriented, and trembling.

“Seris! What’s happening?” Rhys gripped her arm, steadying her.  “You’re shaking.”

Kaelen’s voice, when it finally came, was fragmented, strained, a whisper carried on the edge of her consciousness. *“…Nexus…responding…resonance…ancient…before…the Sundering…”* It wasn’t a clear thought, but a feeling, a vibration within her mind.

The library around them seemed to shimmer, the shelves momentarily distorting like reflections in troubled water. Seris felt a pull, a dizzying sensation of being drawn backwards through time, witnessing events that had unfolded centuries before.

“Kaelen? What is this?” she managed to whisper, her voice trembling.

*“…memory…not mine…imprinted…on the Nexus…a place…where stars…were bound…before…”* The thoughts were coming in broken fragments, like shards of glass, each one sharp and painful.

The visions intensified. She saw a council of beings, radiating power, manipulating constellations like pieces on a game board, weaving threads of energy between distant stars. They were building something magnificent, a network of celestial power, a shield against the darkness.  Then, a betrayal. A figure cloaked in shadow, a disruption in the harmony, unleashing a cataclysmic force that shattered the network, unleashing the Sundering – the event that had reshaped Eldoria centuries ago.

“The Sundering…” Seris breathed, the realization dawning on her. “This isn’t just about the blight. It’s about what *caused* the Sundering.”

*“…Nexus…key…power…vulnerability…contained…sealed…weakening…”* Kaelen’s voice was fading, becoming increasingly distant, a dying ember.

Suddenly, a specific image crystallized within the chaos. A symbol – a complex arrangement of interlocking stars, etched onto a dark, obsidian surface. It pulsed with an inner light, radiating a power that made Seris’s skin crawl, a cold dread settling in her bones.

“That symbol…” she murmured, her eyes fixed on the mental image. “I…I’ve seen it before, in old texts…it’s a ward…a seal.”

Rhys, his face etched with concern, squeezed her arm. “What is it, Seris? What does it mean?”

“It’s connected to the Sundering…to the Nexus…it’s a place…a source of power…and it’s failing.”

*“…Fire…stone…mountains…”* Kaelen’s voice was barely a whisper now, fragmented and fading.

Seris closed her eyes, trying to hold onto the fading images, the fragmented thoughts.  “The Firestone Mountains…the symbol…it’s there.”

She opened her eyes, her gaze locking with Rhys’s. “We need to go to the Firestone Mountains.”

The library seemed to return to normal, the silence settling around them like a shroud. But everything had changed. The blight was no longer a simple affliction, but a symptom of a deeper, ancient wound. And Seris knew, with a chilling certainty, that the fate of Eldoria rested on unraveling the secrets of the Sundering, and finding the failing Nexus before it was too late.
```

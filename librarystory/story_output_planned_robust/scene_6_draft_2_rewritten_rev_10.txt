## Scene 6 - Revised

The chill started at the base of my skull, intensifying with each attempt. I reached for the serpent image – a fragment of carved stone, a sinuous curve hinting at ancient power – and focused my intent.

This time, contact felt different. Not a blank wall, but a yielding resistance, like pressing against oiled silk. A flicker beyond the stone – a vast, echoing chamber choked with shadow, the faintest impression of watching eyes. Then, a tightening behind my temples, and a coppery taste bloomed on my tongue. I instinctively recoiled, my hand tightening into a fist.

“Everything alright?” Hemlock’s voice, smooth as polished obsidian, cut through my concentration. He hadn’t moved, his unblinking gaze assessing me, a subtle lift of his eyebrow the only indication of his question.

I forced my eyes open, blinking against the dim light. The metallic tang coated my tongue. I tried again, willing the serpent image to solidify. Energy surged, and for a moment, I felt a resonance, not with a being, but with the stone itself, with the Atheneum’s foundations. It felt…old. Immeasurably so. And profoundly unwilling, like forcing a lock with the wrong key. I pushed harder, and a sharp pain lanced through my head. My vision blurred at the edges.

A bookshelf rattled, a heavy tome tumbling to the floor with a muffled thud. The scent of aged paper and dust filled the air. I stumbled back, clutching my temples, nausea washing over me.

“Careful now,” Hemlock said, a hint of amusement lacing his voice, but his eyes remained cold. He didn’t offer assistance. “These volumes are fragile. As are your…approaches, it seems.”

I ignored him, pressing through the discomfort, focusing on what the carving *meant*. I sent another probe outwards, a desperate plea. This time, the resistance wasn't yielding silk, but a solid wall of stone. It felt like slamming into a glacier, the impact jarring my teeth. The air grew cold, a subtle stillness descending, muffling all sound. A prickling sensation rose on my skin, as if a storm was brewing *within* the stone.

Then, the wall *moved*. Not physically, but…internally. It wasn’t just blocking me; it was actively repelling me, pushing against my mind with ancient force. A force that felt…protective.

“It doesn’t want to be found,” I breathed, more to myself than to Hemlock.

He didn’t respond, his gaze fixed on my face.

I sent another, weaker tendril of intent towards the image. 

Nothing.

I lowered my head, trying to regain control. When I looked up, Hemlock’s gaze met mine. He hadn’t moved, hadn’t offered comfort. Only a silent, knowing assessment. His lips tightened – not triumph, but something colder, expectant. 

I pressed my fingers to my throbbing temple, a dull ache spreading through my skull. He hadn’t just witnessed my failure; he’d anticipated it. And that realization, more than the pain, left me shaken. I swallowed, fighting the metallic taste. I clenched my fist, the knuckles white.

Hemlock’s eyes flickered to my hand, then back to my face. A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “Perhaps,” he said softly, “you should focus on secrets *willing* to be found.” He paused, his gaze unwavering. “Or perhaps,” he added, a subtle weight to his words, “some secrets are best left undisturbed…and forgotten. Their guardians are…persistent.” He didn’t look away, leaving me to grapple with the chilling implication – and the unsettling certainty that he knew far more than he let on. The tremor finally subsided, replaced by a cold dread that settled deep in my bones. 

I forced a slow exhale, trying to regain control. “What do you mean, ‘guardians’?” I asked, my voice rough.

Hemlock’s expression remained unreadable. He didn’t answer directly. Instead, he tilted his head slightly, his eyes lingering on my clenched fist. “The Atheneum holds many things, Kael. Not just knowledge. Power. And everything powerful attracts…protection.” He paused, letting the weight of his words settle. “Some doors are sealed for a reason. And some seals…are older than this library itself.”

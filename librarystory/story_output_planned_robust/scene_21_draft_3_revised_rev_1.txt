## Draft 3: The Inquisitor's Visit

The air in the workshop tasted of dried herbs and dust motes, but beneath that familiar scent, a metallic tang lingered – a chill that had nothing to do with the mountain air. The Inquisitor, Marius, hadn’t touched anything, yet his presence seemed to leach the warmth from the room.

“The air in here is…stimulating,” Marius said, his voice a dry rasp. His gaze swept over the scattered maps and botanical sketches that covered their worktable, lingering on the precise renderings of the Sunstone Bloom. “Though I suspect your pursuits are less about scholarly curiosity and more about…reckless ambition.”

“We are scholars, Inquisitor,” Seris replied, carefully keeping her voice level. “We seek knowledge, not permission.” She met his gaze, a pale, unsettling blue that seemed to dissect her with each passing moment.

Marius’s lips thinned, barely a movement. “Knowledge, untamed, is a dangerous beast, Scholar. The Order exists to safeguard Eldoria, to prune the branches of inquiry that lead to ruin.” He gestured with a gloved hand, the leather creaking softly. “I’ve reviewed your records. Your…associates are…unconventional. A former mercenary with a penchant for strong drink, a disgraced herbalist with a history of…unorthodox remedies…and a patron whose wealth seems to originate from…shadowy sources.”

Seris clenched her jaw. He was referring to Old Man Hemlock, the eccentric apothecary who’d provided them with rare reagents – and who’d asked disturbingly few questions in return. “Our associates possess valuable skills, Inquisitor. And their backgrounds are irrelevant to our research.”

“Irrelevant?” Marius raised a pale eyebrow, the gesture radiating condescension. “Everything is relevant, Scholar. Especially when it concerns the ancient lore of Eldoria. The Sunstone Bloom is not merely a botanical curiosity. It is a relic of the First Age, imbued with…resonance. Memories that some would prefer remain undisturbed. Memories that *speak* to those who know how to listen.”

*He’s probing, testing for a reaction,* Kaelen breathed, a prickle of fear running through Seris’s mind. *He knows more than he lets on.*

Rhys, who had been silently sharpening a climbing axe, finally spoke, his voice low and dangerous. “What do you want, Inquisitor?”

Marius’s gaze fixed on Seris, lingering on the faint smudge of soil beneath her fingernails, a silent accusation. “I want to understand your motives. Your *obsession* with a flower that blooms only in the most treacherous of locations. Your disregard for the dangers of Firepeak. And I want to be certain that your research does not…unearth something that should remain lost. Something…forgotten.” He stepped closer, the scent of old parchment and something subtly metallic clinging to him.

“Tell me, Scholar. What are you *truly* seeking on Firepeak Mountain? Is it knowledge…or something else?”

Seris met his gaze, refusing to flinch, but a flicker of doubt crossed her mind. She’d told herself it was purely academic, a quest for understanding. But the truth was a heavy weight in her chest: a desperate hope to find a cure for the wasting illness that was slowly consuming her sister, Elara. A hope fueled by whispers of the Bloom’s restorative properties, dismissed by most scholars as folklore. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.”

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile, devoid of warmth. “A convenient answer. But I suspect…you are not being entirely forthcoming.” He paused, then lowered his voice, a hint of something dangerous in his tone. “The Bloom…it remembers. It holds echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering.  Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep.”

He stepped back, his gaze sweeping over their cluttered workspace, lingering on a dried specimen of a rare mountain moss. “I will be watching you. Both of you.”

Rhys’s hand tightened on the hilt of his climbing axe, the steel gleaming in the dim light. Kaelen’s voice, barely a whisper in Seris’s mind, *He’s not just curious. He’s…afraid. He’s afraid of what you might find.*

Seris forced herself to meet Marius’s gaze, a cold determination hardening within her. She wouldn’t be intimidated. She wouldn’t let him see her desperation. “We have no secrets, Inquisitor.”

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing her with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has secrets, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to sacrifice to keep them buried?” He offered a curt nod, then turned and swept out of the workshop, leaving behind a lingering chill and the unsettling feeling of being watched.

The silence that followed was heavy, broken only by the rhythmic rasp of Rhys sharpening his axe. Seris stared at the closed door, her heart pounding. The Inquisitor hadn't found any secrets…yet. But she knew, with a chilling certainty, that he would be back. And when he returned, he wouldn't be seeking information. He would be seeking control.

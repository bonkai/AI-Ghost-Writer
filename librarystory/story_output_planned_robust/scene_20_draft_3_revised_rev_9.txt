## Scene 20: Draft 3

The scent of woodsmoke and damp stone clung to the air. Seris knelt before a crumbling hearth, her fingers tracing the chipped mosaic of a winged figure. Old Man Hemlock emerged from the gloom, pacing a tight circle, his gaze darting between shadows and stone. He stopped abruptly, hand rising to his ear as if listening, then lowered himself to a weathered stool, his movements quick and nervous.

“I believed in the purity of the Light,” he began, his voice brittle, but laced with a lingering defiance. He glanced at the mosaic, regret flashing across his face, then quickly looked away, as if the image pained him. “A world steeped in magic, not *controlled* blessings, but raw, untamed force. It wasn’t chaos, child. It was…potential.” He traced the lines of the vortex on the mosaic with his eyes, a flicker of longing in his gaze. He reached out, then hesitated, his hand hovering over a particularly fragile scroll before pulling back.

“I saw images…” He closed his eyes, as if reliving the memories. “Cities built of shimmering crystal, powered by the lifeblood of the earth. Gardens that bloomed with bioluminescent flora, responding to thought, to emotion.” He opened his eyes, a haunted look in them. “They didn’t just fear the magic. They feared what people *remembered* with it. The power it gave them, the questions it inspired.” He abruptly stood, pacing again, his energy frantic.

Seris’s jaw tightened, her fingers tracing the delicate lines of the sketch within her pack. She didn’t need legends; she was tracing deliberate obscuring. She pulled the sketch partially from her pack, the crimson stain vivid against the worn canvas. "Why bury it?" she asked, her voice sharp, demanding more than just explanation. She met his gaze directly, challenging him to reveal the true motivation.

A faint scraping echoed from the tunnel entrance. Hemlock whirled around, hand instinctively reaching for a hidden knife, his body tense. He scanned the darkness, breath coming in ragged gasps, but didn’t fully draw the blade. He was assessing, calculating.

Seris followed his gaze, her hand tightening on her dagger, but she didn’t simply assess the threat. She moved subtly, positioning herself *between* Hemlock and the tunnel entrance, a silent offer of protection, a clear signal she wasn't helpless. She needed to understand *who* was listening, and what they wanted, before reacting.

Hemlock lowered his hand, but didn’t fully relax. He glanced at the Sunstone Bloom sketch peeking from her pack, a flicker of warning in his eyes. “They believe the flower might unlock something. Something they desperately want to keep buried.”

“I questioned it,” he continued, his voice low and strained. “I asked why knowledge was feared. They stripped me of my rank, exiled me.” He ran a hand through his thinning hair, a gesture of frustration.

“But you saved these texts,” Seris said, gesturing to the stacks of crumbling books and scrolls. “You risked everything.”

He scoffed, a bitter sound. “A small defiance. A futile gesture, perhaps.” He glanced at the tunnel entrance, then back at Seris, his expression grim. “They believe knowledge is control. They were right, to a degree. But they also fear what they cannot control.”

Seris met his gaze, the crimson stain on the sketch feeling like a brand. She wasn’t just chasing a rare flower. She was chasing a truth the Church would kill to keep buried. A cold gust of wind swept through the cavern, extinguishing a nearby oil lamp. In the sudden darkness, she felt a prickling sensation on her skin, but didn’t flinch. She kept her stance firm, projecting calm even as her senses heightened.

Beneath the fear, a new resolve hardened within her. This wasn’t just about uncovering the past; it was about reclaiming it. She tightened her grip on her pack. "What can we do?" she asked, her voice barely a whisper, but filled with a newfound determination.

Hemlock’s gaze flickered to the mosaic, then back to Seris. A ghost of a smile touched his lips. “We remember,” he said, his voice gaining strength. “We tell the stories. And we protect the embers of what remains.” He briefly covered her hand on her pack, a grounding touch, then released it. “The flower…it’s not just a key to the past. It’s a seed for the future.” He paused, his eyes locking with hers. "But be warned, child. They won't let us plant it easily." He glanced back towards the tunnel entrance, a shadow of fear crossing his face. "And they are already listening." He subtly nodded towards the tunnel, a silent acknowledgement of a presence.

## Scene 23 - Rewrite (Based on Critique)

The flickering lamplight cast long shadows across the cluttered workshop. Rhys hadn’t touched the Firepeak maps since Valerius’s departure. Instead, he meticulously polished the lenses of his sketching tools, the repetitive motion betraying his unease. Dust motes danced in the lamplight, mirroring the unsettled feeling in the room.

“He suspects something,” Seris said, her voice low, barely a whisper against the workshop’s quiet hum. She wasn’t sharpening obsidian needles, but instead, *testing* the edge of a honed steel scalpel against her thumb, a slow, deliberate act that spoke volumes.

Rhys didn’t look up. “Of course. Asking about rare flora on a mountain known for its dangers…it invites scrutiny.”

“It’s more than that. He’s questioning our *motivation*. He doesn’t believe the Sunstone Bloom is the real reason we’re risking our necks.”

Rhys finally met her gaze, his jaw tight. “And are we after something else?” He didn’t answer the question, letting the silence hang heavy.

Seris’s eyes narrowed. “The Bloom is a convenient story. A plausible excuse. But we’re really after the Skyglass fragments, aren’t we?”

Rhys exhaled slowly, the polished lenses gleaming in his hand. “Information is a commodity, Seris. A valuable one. And the Skyglass…it commands a high price.”

“High enough to risk Valerius’s displeasure? High enough to keep it from me?” Seris’s voice was dangerously soft, the scalpel now resting on the workbench, its edge catching the light.

Rhys ran a hand through his already disheveled hair. “I had a…contact. A collector. Discreet. He offered a substantial sum for confirmed fragments. Enough to fund us for months. I wanted certainty before involving you. Before risking *us*.”

A sharp rap at the workshop door startled them both. Rhys’s hand instinctively tightened around the lenses, a flicker of panic in his eyes. He quickly stowed the tools in a drawer, the movement jerky and uncoordinated.

“Who is it?” Seris asked, her hand moving to the concealed dagger, but remaining *above* the fabric, a subtle warning.

“A merchant,” a gruff voice replied. “Looking to trade for maps. Heard you two were the ones to ask.”

Rhys exchanged a tense glance with Seris. This wasn’t the collector. The collector preferred intermediaries. He opened the door to reveal a man cloaked in shadow, his face obscured by a wide-brimmed hat. The man’s eyes, however, were sharp and assessing, sweeping over the workshop with a practiced gaze. He smelled of woodsmoke and something else…something metallic.

“I understand you have charts of the Firepeak region,” the man said, his voice raspy, like gravel on stone. He placed a heavy pouch on the workbench, the clink of coins echoing in the small space. “I’m willing to pay a generous price for detailed maps, particularly those showing…unusual geological formations.”

Rhys’s eyes narrowed, studying the man. “What kind of formations?”

The merchant’s lips curved into a thin, unsettling smile. “Let’s just say I’m interested in anything that doesn’t quite belong. Anything…out of place.” He paused, his gaze lingering on Seris, assessing her posture, her weapon. “I’ve heard whispers of your…research. The Sunstone Bloom. A beautiful flower, but hardly worth risking life and limb for.”

Seris stepped forward, her voice cold and even. “We’re simply botanists, cataloging rare species. Our work is purely academic.” She didn’t meet his gaze directly, instead focusing on a point just past his shoulder.

The merchant chuckled, a dry, humorless sound. “Botanists who happen to be interested in ancient legends and forgotten pathways. Don’t insult my intelligence.” He tapped the pouch on the workbench. “I’ll double what you’d get from any local cartographer for a map showing the location of the Whisperwind Caves.”

Rhys’s breath hitched. The Whisperwind Caves. A place mentioned only in fragmented texts, rumored to hold a concentration of Skyglass fragments. He hadn’t even *thought* to mention them to Seris.

“How do you know about the Whisperwind Caves?” he asked, his voice tight with suspicion.

The merchant’s smile widened, revealing teeth that seemed too sharp. “Let’s just say I have…sources. And I’m willing to pay handsomely for information. Or, if you prefer, I can simply acquire it myself.” He glanced meaningfully at the dagger at Seris’s hip. “Though that might be…messier.”

Rhys looked at Seris, his eyes pleading for understanding. He didn’t need to *say* the words. The unspoken question hung between them.

Seris didn’t return his gaze. Instead, she slowly, deliberately, *sheathed* her dagger. A subtle signal. A warning.

“We don’t have a map of the Whisperwind Caves,” she said, her voice unwavering. “But we know someone who might.” She met the merchant’s gaze directly, her eyes cold and assessing. “And that someone…charges a premium.”



**Changes made and why:**

*   **More emphasis on non-verbal cues:**  Added descriptions of body language (Seris testing the scalpel, Seris sheathing her dagger, etc.). This builds tension and shows character dynamics without needing explicit dialogue.
*   **Stronger characterization:**  Made the characters more complex. Seris is not just a fighter, but also observant and calculating. Rhys is more visibly conflicted and desperate. The merchant is more menacing.
*   **Increased tension:**  The pacing is slower, allowing for more atmospheric details and building suspense.
*   **Subtlety:**  Removed some of the more direct explanations and allowed the reader to infer more.
*   **Stronger ending:** The ending is more ambiguous and sets up the next scene effectively. Seris's action of sheathing her dagger is a powerful signal to both Rhys and the merchant.
*   **Descriptive Language:** Used more evocative language to create a stronger sense of atmosphere and mood.
*   **Show, don't tell:**  Focused on *showing* the characters' emotions and motivations through their actions and dialogue, rather than simply *telling* the reader.



This revised version aims to be more engaging, atmospheric, and character-driven, while maintaining the core plot points of the original.

*   **Repetitive phrasing:** The idea of the Veil being about containment (or lack thereof) is stated *multiple* times ("keeping things out," "keeping something in," "keeping us from something else"). While reinforcing a key reveal is good, it's becoming redundant. Condense this. Perhaps after the initial reveal from Kaelen, Seris's realization could be more concise, focusing on the *implications* rather than restating the concept.
*   **Kaelen's delivery:** Kaelen’s projections are almost *entirely* exposition. While the “faint, strained” description attempts to add nuance, it reads as a convenient way to deliver information. Consider *showing* Kaelen’s struggle through fragmented *thoughts* or incomplete images, rather than complete sentences. Let Seris and Rhys piece together more themselves.
*   **"Show me" is weak:** Seris saying "Show me" feels passive. Given her character and the urgency, a more proactive request—or even a mental command—would be stronger.
*   **Silent agreement cliché:** The "silent agreement passing between them" and Rhys tightening his grip is a bit of a trope. While not *bad*, it doesn't add much. Consider *showing* their determination through action or a brief, charged exchange instead.
*   **Rhys's role:** Rhys is largely reactive in this scene. He asks a question early on, but then mostly observes and provides physical support. Give him more agency in interpreting the information or suggesting a course of action. Even a small contribution would elevate his presence.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Consider *how* Seris and Rhys react to the information. Does Seris, the scholar, immediately want to delve deeper into the lore? Does Rhys, the pragmatist, focus on the immediate danger? Showing these contrasting reactions would enrich their characters.

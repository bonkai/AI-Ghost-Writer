*   **Initial Build-Up:** The first three paragraphs are strong in establishing internal tension. The descriptions of the sensation – chilling, yielding resistance, metallic taste – are effective. However, the description of the initial attempts feels *slightly* repetitive – the 'yielding resistance' followed by the 'solid wall' quickly after could be streamlined.
*   **Pacing Dip (Paragraph 4-5):** The bookshelf rattling and the narrator stumbling create a momentary distraction. While not *bad*, this section feels like a slight pause in the escalating tension. It doesn't significantly *lower* the tension, but it doesn't actively build it either.
*   **Hemlock's Presence:** Hemlock’s consistent lack of assistance is well-played for building unease, but his reactions are a little *too* subtle for a prolonged period. The scene relies heavily on *implication* of his knowledge, which is good, but a slightly more overt sign of his anticipation earlier on might amplify the tension.
*   **Tension Peaks & Valleys:** The scene has good tension *pulses* with each attempt to connect with the stone. However, the repeated failures, while logical, begin to feel slightly predictable. Varying the *type* of resistance encountered (beyond 'yielding' to 'solid') could maintain a higher level of suspense.
*   **Final Exchange:** The final five paragraphs are the strongest. The back-and-forth between Kael and Hemlock, filled with veiled threats and cryptic warnings, is excellent. The increasing sense of dread is palpable. The pacing here is perfect – slow, deliberate, and unsettling.
*   **Hooks & Transitions:** The scene *starts* well with the immediate internal sensation. The *ending* is strong, leaving the reader with a chilling sense of foreboding and a clear question (what *are* these guardians?).



Overall, the scene is well-paced and effectively builds tension, particularly in the final portion. The primary area for improvement is to vary the nature of the resistance encountered during the attempts to connect with the stone and to potentially heighten Hemlock’s active presence earlier on.

## Consistency Check: Passed.

This draft is remarkably consistent, both internally and with the established premise. Here's a breakdown of why, and a few *very minor* notes that aren't inconsistencies, but areas for consideration:

**Strengths (Why it passes):**

* **Internal Logic:** The progression of Kaelen's discovery within the Great Library is logical. He starts by testing his abilities, then asks questions, and finally learns to *feel* for answers. The library's responses are appropriately cryptic and testing.
* **Character Consistency:** Kaelen's initial panic and then deliberate suppression of it feels believable. His growing frustration and desperation are well-portrayed.
* **Worldbuilding Consistency:** The Great Library is established as a place where stories are lived, not just told, and the responses feel fitting for such a place – ancient, vast, and more interested in observation than direct answers.
* **Mystery Building:** The introduction of the woman’s face and the closed book effectively builds mystery and provides a clear goal for Kaelen.
* **Pacing:** The pacing is good, allowing time for Kaelen to explore his new state and the library's rules.

**Minor Notes (Not Inconsistencies, but Considerations):**

* **Repetition of "Silence":** The word "silence" is used *frequently*. While appropriate for the setting, consider varying the language slightly (e.g., "void," "stillness," "absence of sound"). It's not an error, just something to polish.
* **"Feeling" as a Method:** Kaelen quickly transitions to "feeling" as a method of communication. While it makes sense within the context of the library, perhaps a slightly more gradual exploration of this ability would feel more natural.
* **Library's Motivation:** While the library's cryptic nature is effective, a *hint* of its underlying motivation (beyond simply observing) might add another layer of intrigue. Is it actively seeking something? Is it protecting something?



**Overall:** This is a strong piece of writing. The consistency is excellent, and the draft effectively sets up a compelling mystery. The minor notes above are simply suggestions for polishing, not corrections of errors.

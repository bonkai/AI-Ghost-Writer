*   **Seris & Rhys – Lack of Direct Interaction:** While the silent communication between Seris and Rhys is *shown* (glance, understanding), there’s very little *spoken* between them. Given their established trust, a brief, coded exchange (“Rhys, archway. Ritual site.” or similar) could heighten tension and showcase their teamwork *and* subtly inform the reader without info-dumping through Kaelen.
*   **Kaelen's Constant Narration:** Kaelen’s thoughts are *very* frequent and detailed. While useful for exposition, it risks feeling like telling instead of showing. Consider streamlining Kaelen’s input. Perhaps combine some thoughts, or have Seris *react* to the information, letting the reader infer Kaelen’s source. For example, instead of “*The Obsidian Circle…*”, have Seris’s eyes widen, a flicker of recognition, and then a line of dialogue hinting at the danger.
*   **Valerius – Predictable Villain:** Valerius’s smooth, calculating dialogue is effective, but leans heavily into archetype. Consider adding a quirk or unexpected line to make him more memorable. A fleeting moment of genuine curiosity, a subtly cruel observation, anything to break the “standard villain” mold.
*   **“Just a relic…” – Too Direct:** Seris’s line about the key being “just a relic” feels a little too calculated and defensive. It's a believable line, but it lacks nuance. Perhaps a more dismissive, almost bored tone would be more effective – implying she *wants* them to underestimate her.
*   **Repetition of "Safety":** The word "safety" is used multiple times near the end. While thematic, it feels slightly repetitive. Consider varying the phrasing ("well-being," "the realm's protection," etc.).
*   **Subtext Opportunity – The Key's Feel:** Seris notes the key feels "incomplete/broken." This is a great detail! Lean into that more. Have her subtly *react* to it physically (a slight flinch, a tightening of her grip) *before* Kaelen explains its purpose. Show, don't just tell.
*   **Rhys's Silence:** Rhys is largely a silent observer. While not necessarily *bad*, it makes him feel underdeveloped. Even a single line of questioning or observation would help establish his character and agency.

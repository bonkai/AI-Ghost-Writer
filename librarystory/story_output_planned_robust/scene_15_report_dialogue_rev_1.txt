*   **Kaelen's Voice:** While the mental communication is established, it feels *very* frequent. Every significant reveal is punctuated by Kaelen’s input. This diminishes its impact and feels a bit like telling instead of showing Seris's internal reactions. Consider reducing the frequency, letting Seris *react* and then have Kaelen chime in less often, or integrate Kaelen's insights into Seris's own thoughts/observations.
*   **Rhys's Pragmatism:** Rhys is *very* consistently practical. While consistent characterization is good, it's bordering on a one-note trait. Perhaps a moment of genuine wonder, or a more emotional reaction to the discoveries, would add depth.
*   **Repetitive Apprehension:** The phrases "something dangerous," "something that should have remained buried," and variations thereof, are used multiple times. While building tension is good, it becomes a little predictable. Try varying the language to express the same sense of dread.
*   **"Ever" Adverbs:** The repeated use of "ever" ("Rhys, *ever* the pragmatist," "Rhys, *ever* practical") feels slightly clunky and unnecessary. It's a shortcut to characterization that could be shown through action and dialogue instead.
*   **Seris's Internal State:** Seris often *states* her fear ("growing terror," "chilling premonition") rather than *showing* it through her dialogue or actions. Subtext could be used more effectively here. For example, instead of "growing terror," perhaps a clipped sentence, a tremor in her voice, or a physical reaction.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** The dialogue feels largely functional – it reveals information and moves the plot. There's less interplay that reveals the *relationship* between Seris and Rhys. A few lines of banter, shared history, or contrasting viewpoints could enrich their dynamic.
*   **"By the Ancestors..."** This feels a bit generic. Consider a more unique exclamation that fits the world's lore.

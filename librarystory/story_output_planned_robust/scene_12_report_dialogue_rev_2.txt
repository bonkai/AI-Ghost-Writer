*   **Repetitive phrasing/echoing:** The repeated echoing of Seris's statements ("Erased?" after Valerius says "Erased?") feels a bit unnatural and slows the pace. While it establishes tension, it happens *too* often. Consider reducing this; let her internal reaction *show* her processing instead of stating it.
*   **Valerius’s Indirectness:** While his cryptic nature is effective, it verges on *too* indirect. The loom metaphor, while poetic, feels a little drawn out. A slightly more direct hint, even within the metaphor, would help the reader keep up without sacrificing mystery.
*   **Seris’s Voice:** Seris’s dialogue feels a little passive. She mostly *reacts* to Valerius. Giving her a single, pointed question or a defiant statement (even if quickly shut down) would strengthen her character and show more agency.
*   **“I think I’ve read enough.”** This line feels weak. Given the escalating tension and her growing fear, a more desperate or determined line would be more impactful. Something like, "I don't need to see anything else," or even a simple, "I'm leaving," would read stronger.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Guardian):** The description of the Guardian absorbing light is excellent *visual* storytelling. However, a line of dialogue from Seris *reacting* to this – even a choked gasp or a whispered observation – would amplify the horror and connect it directly to the dialogue.
*   **Info-Dump Risk:** The final paragraph, while containing a crucial realization, feels a little like Seris narrating her understanding *to the reader* rather than experiencing it internally. Consider showing this realization through her actions and body language, rather than explicitly stating it.

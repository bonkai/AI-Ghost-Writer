*   **Subtext & Indirect Characterization:** The dialogue is *very* direct. While it establishes tension, it lacks nuance. Valerius's skepticism could be conveyed *through* his phrasing and tone, rather than explicitly stating it ("Some knowledge is best left undisturbed," "Be careful what you seek"). Let his actions and veiled threats do more of the work.
*   **Rhys's Dialogue:** Rhys feels passive. His offer of the herbs feels like a forced attempt to appease Valerius rather than a natural extension of his work. Consider having him subtly defend their research, or express frustration at the interruption.
*   **Kaelen's Thoughts:** While useful for the reader, the constant internal narration feels like telling, not showing. Can some of this urgency be conveyed through Seris’s *actions* and brief, clipped dialogue? Less "We need to leave. Now." and more a quick, sharp glance at Rhys followed by immediate action.
*   **Seris’s Explanation:** Her explanation to Valerius ("Celestial cartography…potential connection to the land’s health") is a bit stiff and overly formal. It sounds like a textbook definition. A more natural response, even while being cautious, would be slightly more conversational.
*   **Missed Opportunity (Valerius):** The "fleeting expression – something akin to fear?" is a good observation, but it's *told* to us. Show it! A slight tightening of his jaw, a flicker in his eyes, a momentary hesitation in his stride – these would be far more impactful.
*   **Efficiency:** The final exchange ("What’s wrong?" "We’ve attracted unwanted attention.") is functional, but lacks punch. Consider condensing it. Perhaps Seris simply says, "Let's go," with a pointed look that conveys the urgency.

Overall, the dialogue is competent but leans heavily on exposition and direct statements. It would benefit from more subtext, stronger character voices, and a reduction in "telling" through internal thoughts.

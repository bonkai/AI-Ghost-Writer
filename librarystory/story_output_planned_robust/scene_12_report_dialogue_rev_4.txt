*   **Valerius's Dialogue – Too Metaphorical/Oblique:** While the metaphors ("loom," "threads") are evocative, Valerius relies *entirely* on them. It feels overly theatrical and doesn't quite land as natural conversation, even for a powerful, enigmatic character. He *never* says anything directly. This creates distance rather than intrigue. Consider adding a single line of direct questioning or acknowledgement of Seris's presence to ground the scene.
*   **Seris – Reactive, Not Proactive:** Seris primarily *responds* to Valerius. She asks one question, but it’s prompted directly by his statement. Give her a line or two where she *leads* the conversation, even if it’s just to express her initial findings about the Aethel before Valerius interjects. This will establish her as an active investigator, not just a witness.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Subtext:** The "Escort the Initiate back to her chambers" line is functional but flat. Instead of *telling* us Valerius is dismissing her, show it through subtle manipulation. Perhaps a seemingly concerned offer of rest that feels more like an order, or a comment about the late hour implying she's overstepping her bounds.
*   **"What do you see?" – Cliché:** This is a very common prompt. Consider a more unique way to ask Seris about the illustration. Something that hints at Valerius’s specific interest in the Aethel.
*   **Repetitive Internal Thought:** Seris’s internal monologue at the end largely *restates* what Valerius has already implied. Condense this. Focus on her *emotional* response to the realization (fear, determination, etc.) rather than simply summarizing the plot.

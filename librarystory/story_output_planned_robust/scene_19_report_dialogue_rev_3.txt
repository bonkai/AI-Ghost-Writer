*   **Repetitive Echoing:** Seris repeats “Remembrance?” and “Lost?” almost immediately after the ‘voice’ (Firepeak) speaks them. This feels unnatural and slows the pace. Trust the reader to understand the connection; Seris doesn't need to parrot the thought. Consider internalizing her reaction instead of repeating it aloud.
*   **Limited Character Voice:** The ‘voice’ of Firepeak is very…flat. It's purely exposition. While a mountain might not have a flamboyant personality, *how* it communicates should be unique. Consider using imagery, fragmented phrases, or sensory details in its ‘speech’ to make it more evocative and less like a narrator delivering information.
*   **Direct Exposition:** The lines explaining what the Order did ("They didn’t just forbid magic…they feared the past") are heavy-handed. This information *could* be revealed through dialogue or internal thought, but it’s currently delivered as a direct explanation. Perhaps Valerius *already* believes they are searching for lost knowledge, and his reactions reveal his fear of it.
*   **Valerius's Reaction:** The book slamming shut is a good *action*, but it’s followed by a thought *attributed* to him ("His frustration was palpable"). Show, don’t tell. Let his actions *demonstrate* his frustration – a glare, a tightened jaw, a deliberate step closer.
*   **Short, Staccato Exchanges:** The dialogue is often a single line, followed by a thought. While this creates a sense of tension, it can feel disjointed. Consider combining lines or adding more natural pauses and reactions.
*   **'Always.'**: The single-word response ("Always.") feels weak and doesn’t advance the scene. It lacks impact. What *specifically* is Firepeak affirming? A more nuanced response would be more effective.

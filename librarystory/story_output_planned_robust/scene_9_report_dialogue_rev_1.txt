*   **Over-Exposition & "As You Know" Dialogue:** A *lot* of this scene is the 'voice' (which is a bit of a narrative cheat, honestly – it *feels* like the author telling us things *through* dialogue) simply *telling* Seris (and therefore the reader) information. For example, the lengthy explanations of what the Echo is, what the Order did, and the Church's motivations. This feels unnatural. Consider *showing* these things through Seris's discoveries, reactions, and interactions, rather than having the voice just lay it all out.

*   **Voice Consistency:** The 'voice' is *very* formal and eloquent. While this establishes a certain mystique, it feels disconnected from the urgency of the situation and Seris’s likely emotional state. It's a bit too 'narrator-ly' and not enough like a consciousness trying to communicate. Consider injecting more personality – even weariness, desperation, or a hint of madness – into its phrasing.

*   **Repetitive Metaphors:** The "tapestry" metaphor is used twice, and it’s not particularly strong. Find different ways to illustrate the fragility of reality or the consequences of tampering with the Echo.

*   **Seris’s Reactions:** Seris largely *receives* information. Her responses are often short questions or statements of shock. Give her more agency. Let her challenge the voice, speculate, or draw her own conclusions. This will make her feel more proactive and the dialogue more dynamic.

*   **"Why Me?" - A Missed Opportunity:** The "Why me?" question is a classic, but it's brushed over quickly. This is a prime moment for the voice to reveal something crucial about Seris’s past, lineage, or connection to the Order. Lean into the mystery!

*   **Indirect Characterization:** We learn very little about the voice *beyond* its knowledge. Is it patient? Frustrated? Hopeful? Resigned? Injecting these subtle cues into its phrasing will make it a more compelling character (even if it isn’t a character in the traditional sense).

*   **"Remember" – Too On-the-Nose:** The final word feels like a heavy-handed clue. Consider a more ambiguous or symbolic ending that leaves the reader with a lingering sense of mystery.



This scene has strong atmospheric potential, but the dialogue needs to be reworked to feel less like an information dump and more like a genuine conversation (or exchange) between two entities. Focus on showing, not telling, and giving Seris a more active role in the dialogue.

The air in the workshop tasted of dried herbs and dust motes, but beneath that familiar scent, a metallic tang lingered – a chill that had nothing to do with the mountain air. The Inquisitor, Marius, hadn’t touched anything, yet his presence seemed to leach the warmth from the room. A tremor ran through Seris’s fingers as she unconsciously tightened her grip on a mortar and pestle.

“The air in here is…stimulating,” Marius said, his voice a dry rasp. His gaze swept over the scattered maps and botanical sketches that covered their worktable, lingering on the precise renderings of the Sunstone Bloom. “Though I suspect your pursuits are less about scholarly curiosity and more about…reckless ambition.”

“We are scholars, Inquisitor,” Seris replied, carefully keeping her voice level. She met his gaze, a pale, unsettling blue that seemed to dissect her with each passing moment, and subtly adjusted the shawl around her shoulders, a reflexive attempt to ward off a sudden chill. “We seek knowledge, not permission.”

Marius’s lips thinned, barely a movement. “Knowledge, untamed, is a dangerous beast, Scholar. The Order exists to safeguard Eldoria, to prune the branches of inquiry that lead to ruin.” He gestured with a gloved hand, the leather creaking softly. “I’ve reviewed your records. Your…associates are…unconventional. A former mercenary with a penchant for strong drink, a disgraced herbalist with a history of…unorthodox remedies…and a patron whose wealth seems to originate from…shadowy sources.”

Seris clenched her jaw, the muscle jumping. He was referring to Old Man Hemlock, the eccentric apothecary who’d provided them with rare reagents – and who’d asked disturbingly few questions in return. “Our associates possess valuable skills, Inquisitor. And their backgrounds are irrelevant to our research.”

“Irrelevant?” Marius raised a pale eyebrow, the gesture radiating condescension. “Everything is relevant, Scholar. Especially when it concerns the ancient lore of Eldoria. The Sunstone Bloom is not merely a botanical curiosity. It is a relic of the First Age, imbued with…resonance. Memories that some would prefer remain undisturbed. Memories that *speak* to those who know how to listen.”

*He’s probing, testing for a reaction,* Kaelen breathed, and Seris’s hand instinctively went to the small, worn pouch containing Elara’s last portrait, her fingers tracing the faded image. *He suspects more than just academic interest.*

Rhys shifted subtly, his hand resting near the hilt of his climbing axe. “The Bloom’s properties are well-documented, Inquisitor. Its potential for healing is significant.”

Marius’s gaze flickered to Rhys, then back to Seris. “Healing? A noble pursuit. Though I confess, I find it curious that a scholar of your…reputation would dedicate herself to such a commonplace goal.”

Seris forced a steady gaze. “The pursuit of knowledge is never commonplace, Inquisitor.” She subtly smoothed a wrinkle in her skirt, a nervous habit.

“Indeed.” Marius paused, his eyes sweeping the room. “Tell me, Scholar. What are you *truly* seeking on Firepeak Mountain? Is it knowledge…or something else?”

A flicker of doubt crossed Seris’s mind, betrayed by the tightening of her lips. She’d told herself it was purely academic, a quest for understanding. But the truth was a heavy weight in her chest: a desperate hope to find a cure for the wasting illness that was slowly consuming her sister, Elara. A hope fueled by whispers of the Bloom’s restorative properties, dismissed by most scholars as folklore. “The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.”

Marius’s lips curved into a cold smile, devoid of warmth. “A convenient answer. But I suspect…you are not being entirely forthcoming.” He paused, then lowered his voice, a hint of something dangerous in his tone. “The Bloom…it remembers. It holds echoes of a time before the Order, before the Great Sundering. Be warned, Scholar. Some memories are best left buried, lest they awaken things that should remain asleep.”

He stepped back, his gaze sweeping over their cluttered workspace, lingering on a dried specimen of a rare mountain moss. “I will be watching you. Both of you.”

Rhys’s hand tightened on the hilt of his climbing axe, the steel gleaming in the dim light. *He’s not just curious,* Kaelen’s voice was a sharp whisper in Seris’s mind. *He’s afraid. Afraid of what you might find, and what that might mean for the Order.*

Seris forced herself to meet Marius’s gaze, a cold determination hardening within her. She wouldn’t be intimidated. She wouldn’t let him see her desperation. “We have no secrets, Inquisitor.”

Marius’s eyes narrowed, assessing her with unsettling intensity. “Everyone has secrets, Scholar. The question is, what are you willing to sacrifice to keep them buried?” He offered a curt nod, then turned and swept out of the workshop, leaving behind a lingering chill and the unsettling feeling of being watched.

The silence that followed was heavy, broken only by the rhythmic rasp of Rhys sharpening his axe. Seris stared at the closed door, her heart pounding. The Inquisitor hadn't found any secrets…yet. But she knew, with a chilling certainty, that he would be back. And when he returned, he wouldn't be seeking information. He would be seeking control. She reached for the pouch containing Elara’s portrait, her fingers trembling. She wouldn’t let him take that away, either.

*   **Rhys’s lines are functional but lack distinct voice.** He mostly reacts to Seris. Consider giving him a characteristic way of speaking – perhaps a dry wit, a pragmatic focus, or a tendency to state the obvious.
*   **The interplay between Seris’s internal thoughts (Kaelen’s voice) and her dialogue is a little clunky.** While it's important to show Kaelen's influence, the immediate shift from thought to spoken confirmation (“Confirmation the runes are older…”) feels abrupt. Consider *implying* the connection instead of stating it directly. Perhaps Seris pauses, visibly affected, *then* speaks, letting Rhys infer something is happening.
*   **"Something strains against its bonds" is a bit on-the-nose, even for a premonition.** This is telling, not showing. Could this be conveyed through a visceral *feeling* Seris experiences, rather than a direct statement? (e.g., "It's… restless. Like something trying to break free.")
*   **Rhys consistently asks questions that Seris immediately answers.** This feels a little *too* convenient. Consider moments where he offers a suggestion or observation *before* she reveals information, or where his questions remain unanswered, building tension.
*   **The final exchange ("And Valerius…") feels slightly disconnected.** While it establishes a connection to the larger plot, it appears a little late in the scene. Consider weaving hints about Valerius’s inquiries earlier, creating a sense of mounting unease.
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is *competent* but leans towards functional rather than vibrant. It effectively conveys information and atmosphere, but lacks the nuances and subtleties that would make it truly compelling. Focus on giving each character a more distinct voice and utilizing subtext to create a more immersive and engaging experience.

*   **Over-Explanation/Info-Dumping:** The dialogue frequently falls into the trap of *telling* rather than *showing*. Hemlock delivers large blocks of exposition about the Order, the Before-Times, and The Great Quietening. Consider breaking this up with more reaction from Seris, internal monologue, or discoveries *through* action. For example, instead of Hemlock *telling* her the Order alters memories, show him reacting to a specific text that *demonstrates* this alteration.
*   **Seris's Reactivity:** Seris is largely a passive listener. While a degree of this is understandable given the situation, she needs more agency in the conversation. Her responses are often simple questions or confirmations. Inject more emotional response, disbelief, or challenging questions to make her feel more present and engaged.
*   **Repetitive Emphasis:** The idea of "forgetting" and "buried knowledge" is repeated *a lot*. While thematic consistency is good, the dialogue feels redundant at times. Find ways to convey these ideas more subtly.
*   **Hemlock's Voice:** Hemlock's voice is consistently frantic and expository. While his paranoia is understandable, it lacks nuance. Consider moments where he’s reflective, melancholic, or even resigned. A constant state of agitation feels unrealistic.
*   **"They" ambiguity:** The constant use of "they" (the Order) becomes tiresome. While a degree of mystery is good, it feels like a crutch. Occasionally specify *who* is being referred to, even if it's just a title ("the Inquisitors," "the High Council").
*   **Physicality & Dialogue Integration:** The physical actions (grabbing Seris's arm, clutching his chest) feel somewhat disconnected from the dialogue. Integrate these actions more seamlessly. For example, instead of "He grabbed Seris’s arm," try "He grabbed Seris’s arm, his grip tightening as he spoke of the Order’s deception."
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The Sunstone Bloom sketch is mentioned, but its significance isn't fully explored in the dialogue. What does Hemlock *really* think of Seris possessing it? Is he using her? Is he hoping she’ll succeed where he failed?

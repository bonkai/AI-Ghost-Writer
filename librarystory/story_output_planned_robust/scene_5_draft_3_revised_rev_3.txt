## Scene 5 - Revised (Draft 3)

The Atheneum smelled of dust and forgotten things—a scent that stirred something within Seris, a disquieting echo of ages past. She ran a gloved hand along a towering bookshelf, the cool wood a grounding sensation. Sunlight fractured by stained glass painted shifting hues across the vast chamber, but failed to penetrate the deeper recesses, leaving pockets of oppressive darkness that felt less like shadow and more like watchful eyes.

The library was a labyrinth, and Seris felt the weight of being observed. Aisles spiraled into darkness, branching into hidden alcoves. Most dismissed it as a crumbling relic. The Iron Church certainly did, restricting access – precisely why Seris had sought it out. Not for spells or potions, but for information—fragments of a time before the Church’s grip, before magic was sin. Specifically, she needed anything referencing the “Veiled Cities,” lost settlements rumored to predate even the oldest kingdoms.

She scanned the nearest alcove, her gaze lingering on Old Man Hemlock, the caretaker. Hunched over a table, cataloging scrolls, he appeared harmless, yet his stillness felt deliberate, *too* still. His position offered a comprehensive view of the chamber, and Seris instinctively cataloged potential escape routes, noting the narrowness of the aisles and the lack of secondary exits.

“Looking for something specific, Initiate?” Hemlock’s voice rasped, barely louder than rustling parchment. He didn’t look up, but Seris felt his gaze – a prickling on her skin, a sensation she recognized as assessment. He was gauging her, testing her reactions.

“Just… appreciating the architecture,” Seris replied, choosing her words carefully. She allowed a slight pause before ‘architecture,’ testing the air, gauging his reaction. Her eyes continued to scan for threats, but she kept her expression open, neutral. “The Church rarely permits access. I’m curious what secrets such a place holds.”

“Secrets have a way of finding those who seek them,” Hemlock said cryptically. A beat. He finally lifted his gaze, his eyes dark and unreadable. “But be warned. Some are best left buried. They bite back.” He held her gaze for a moment longer than necessary, a silent challenge.

Seris ignored the warning, turning back to the shelves, her movements quick and efficient, but with a subtle shift in tempo—a deliberate display of nonchalance. She ran her finger along the spines, scanning faded titles. Most were treatises on theology, philosophy, or alchemy. Useless. The prickling on her neck intensified, and she subtly adjusted her position, placing herself closer to a shadowed archway – an exit, but also a potential chokepoint. She needed to appear curious, not desperate.

She reached for a worn volume, its cover devoid of a title. As her fingers brushed the aged leather, a faint impression flickered at the edge of her awareness – a sense of immense age, of stone and silence. It wasn’t a sound, but a resonance, a pressure against her mind. 

She froze, her heart pounding. The sensation sharpened, coalescing into a fragmented thought, *not her own*. *“The stones remember…”* It felt less like hearing a voice and more like receiving a memory – cold, ancient, unsettling. It felt… familiar.

Seris quickly withdrew her hand, glancing around nervously. Hemlock hadn’t moved, but his eyes, shadowed and unreadable, seemed to bore into her. The light caught the angles of his face, exaggerating the lines around his mouth – a ghost of a smile? He *knew* she'd felt something.

“Did you say something, Initiate?” he asked, his voice deceptively gentle.

“Just admiring the craftsmanship,” Seris replied quickly, forcing a smile that didn’t quite reach her eyes. “Exquisite binding, really.” She needed to regain control of the conversation.

She turned back to the shelves, pretending to continue her search. She needed to appear dismissive of the sensation, to not reveal how deeply it had affected her. But her hand drifted towards a lower shelf, drawn by an inexplicable pull. There, tucked away in the shadows, was a small, unassuming volume. Its cover was blank, held closed by a single, silver clasp. 

Something about it called to her. A sense of recognition, a pull she couldn’t ignore. Hesitantly, she reached for it, her fingers trembling slightly. As she touched the clasp, the impression flooded her mind again, clearer this time, sharper, accompanied by a fleeting image of a coiled serpent, scales gleaming in the darkness.

Seris snatched her hand back as if burned. Her breath hitched. This wasn't a simple echo of the past. This was… *intentional*. She glanced at Hemlock, her eyes narrowed. He hadn't moved, hadn't reacted outwardly. But the shadows around his eyes seemed to deepen, and a subtle shift in his posture suggested a quiet alertness. He was waiting for her to make a move.

She tucked the image of the serpent into the back of her mind. The Atheneum wasn’t just a library. It was a guardian. And she had just been given a task. She had to know what the serpent meant, and why it felt so… important. She needed to understand the connection between the stones, the serpent, and the lost cities.

“Perhaps I’ll try another section,” she said, her voice carefully neutral. She turned to leave, subtly keeping Hemlock in her peripheral vision. She wouldn't let him think he'd shaken her. The Atheneum had revealed a piece of the puzzle. Now, she needed to find the rest.

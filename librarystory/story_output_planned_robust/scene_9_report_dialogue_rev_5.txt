*   **Over-reliance on Sensation/Non-Verbal Communication:** While the concept of Kaelen communicating through sensation is interesting, the dialogue feels *extremely* light on actual spoken words. Almost everything is conveyed through feelings, visuals, or implied meaning. This makes it difficult to assess character voice or build tension through *verbal* sparring. It risks becoming a series of descriptions *about* communication rather than actual dialogue.
*   **Seris's Questions Feel Reactive, Not Proactive:** Seris largely asks questions prompted by Kaelen’s sensations. While understandable given the situation, it makes her feel passive. Consider giving her more agency by having her formulate questions based on her own knowledge or suspicions, even if Kaelen then clarifies/confirms.
*   **"Fractured How?" & "Listening to What?" - Redundancy:** These questions are very close together and both elicit sensation-based answers. Consider combining them or having Seris draw a conclusion based on the first answer before asking the second.
*   **"Remember" - Too Cryptic:** The single word at the end feels like a cliché. While mystery is good, it needs to be earned. What is she supposed to remember? Even a slight hint would be more effective.
*   **Internal Monologue Masquerading as Dialogue:** Lines like “Protecting themselves. They claim to be the sole arbiters of truth…” feel like exposition delivered *through* Kaelen’s sensation, rather than a natural part of a conversation. This could be streamlined.
*   **Kaelen's Voice (or Lack Thereof):** It's difficult to establish Kaelen’s personality or motivations when he communicates almost entirely through feeling. Even a few short, carefully chosen phrases could add depth.
*   **Uneven Information Delivery:** The Church's motivations are revealed fairly quickly. Consider building that reveal more gradually, letting Seris piece things together through clues and deductions.

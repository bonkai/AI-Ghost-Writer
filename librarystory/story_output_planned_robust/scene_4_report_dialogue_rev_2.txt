*   **Lucian’s Dialogue – Too Direct/Accusatory:** Lucian’s lines (“Let me see what you’re hiding,” “Lies,”) are very direct and lack nuance. While fitting for a harsh Inquisitor, they feel a bit *too* on the nose. Consider replacing some with questions that *imply* accusation, or observations delivered with cold detachment. For example, instead of "Let me see what you're hiding," try "What have you been touching?" or simply observing the boy's trembling hands.
*   **Valerius – A Bit Too Smooth:** Valerius's dialogue is functional, but lacks a distinctive *voice*. He's described as smooth and calculating, but his lines ("Take him to the Citadel…," "…will be added to our collection.") are fairly generic. Inject some subtle manipulation, veiled threats, or intellectual condescension to better reflect his character.
*   **Boy’s Dialogue – Believable, But Limited:** The boy's lines are appropriate for his age and fear, but could be expanded to show a bit more desperation or attempt at deception. A slightly more elaborate lie, or a plea beyond “Just a locket, sir,” could add tension.
*   **Lack of Subtext:** Overall, the dialogue is largely functional. There's little subtext. Consider what each character *isn't* saying. What are their hidden motivations? What are they trying to conceal? For example, Lucian’s anger seems purely ideological. Is there a personal reason for his zeal? Valerius’s curiosity feels…convenient. What does he *really* want with the locket?
*   **Repetitive Information:** The line "Heretical trinkets. Symbols of the Old Gods. Your mother followed the forbidden ways" is a bit of an info-dump. It *tells* us information that could be *shown* through Lucian’s reaction or Valerius’s subsequent questions.
*   **Kaelen – No Dialogue:** While appropriate for a spectral observer, consider *brief* internal monologue delivered *as* dialogue, perhaps a frustrated sigh or a murmured question to himself. This could reinforce his connection to the events and add a layer of emotional depth.

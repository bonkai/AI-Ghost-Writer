*   **Hemlock's Detachment:** Hemlock’s behavior feels…off. He witnesses Seris experiencing a potentially traumatic vision and *doesn’t rush to her side*? While a calculating personality is fine, the lack of immediate concern strains believability. Even a brief, “Seris, are you alright?” before probing questions would ground his character and make his later assessment feel less cold.
*   **"Some places remember" - Cliché & Indirect:** The line “Some places remember” is a bit of a cliché. It *tells* rather than *shows* the history/magic at play. Consider *how* Hemlock might reveal this understanding – perhaps referencing a local legend, or observing a specific detail in the chamber.
*   **Repetitive "Felt" Language:** Both Seris and Hemlock rely heavily on the word "felt." While appropriate given the subjective nature of the experience, it becomes noticeable. Consider synonyms: “The serpent *radiated* weariness,” or “It *impressed* upon me a sense of…”
*   **Seris's "I think so" - Weak Close:** Seris’s final line, “I… I think so,” is weak. After experiencing a profound connection with the creature, a more definitive statement would be more impactful. Perhaps, "Yes. Something…shifted within me." or a statement about a new understanding.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Hemlock):** Hemlock’s probing questions and lingering gaze are good, but could be amplified. Instead of directly asking "Do you feel…changed?", he could make an observation about her demeanor – a subtle shift in her posture, a change in her breathing, a flicker in her eyes. *Show* his assessment, don’t just have him ask.
*   **Seris's Internalization:** Seris describes the experience *to* Hemlock. Consider a beat where she *reacts* physically before speaking – a hand flying to her chest, a shaky breath, a widened gaze. This would add immediacy and emotional weight.

*   **Repetitive Internal Thoughts/Actions:** The constant reiteration of characters *reaching for/clutching/tracing* Elara’s portrait and Rhys tightening his grip on the axe becomes distracting. These actions are shown, we don’t need to be *told* about them so frequently within the dialogue-heavy sections. Trim at least half of these instances.
*   **“We have no secrets” – Too Direct:** Seris’s final line feels a bit blunt and defensive. It lacks nuance. Consider a more indirect response, perhaps a subtly defiant statement about their dedication to scholarship, or a question turning the tables: “Is that an accusation, Inquisitor?”
*   **Marius’s Monologues:** Marius’s pronouncements (“The Bloom…it remembers…”) lean towards exposition rather than natural conversation. While his veiled threats are effective, consider *showing* his suspicions through pointed questions and observations instead of lengthy explanations of the Bloom’s history.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – Rhys:** Rhys is largely silent. While his actions (tightening grip on axe) *show* his fear/protectiveness, a single, carefully chosen line – even a short, clipped agreement with Seris – would add depth to his character and demonstrate his investment in the situation.
*   **“Afraid” thought – Kaelen:** The thought from Kaelen feels a little on the nose. It states the obvious – Marius is clearly testing them. Consider letting the reader *infer* Marius’s motivations from his behavior and dialogue.
*   **“The truth, Inquisitor. The same thing you claim to seek.”** - This is a good line, but feels a little stiff. Perhaps soften it slightly to sound less like a practiced retort and more like a genuine, if defiant, response.
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is *competent* but leans towards being functional rather than truly compelling. It serves the purpose of advancing the plot and revealing tension, but lacks a lot of subtext and subtlety. Focus on *showing* more and *telling* less, especially regarding internal states and motivations.

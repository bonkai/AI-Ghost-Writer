## Scene 18

The scent of beeswax and brittle paper hung heavy in the Atheneum’s West Wing, a section dedicated to forgotten herbal remedies and alchemical treatises. Seris meticulously copied a diagram of a rare mountain flower – the ‘Sunstone Bloom’ – its petals radiating an almost unnatural golden hue. Rhys, meanwhile, was attempting to decipher a particularly faded passage detailing a poultice made from powdered obsidian and moonpetal – a recipe that felt…wrong, somehow.

*He’s circling,* Kaelen’s thought brushed against Seris’s mind, a cool wave against her concentration. *Valerius is moving through the East Wing, retracing our steps. He’s examining the star charts again, lingering over the sections we’d marked.*

Seris didn’t look up. Her hand continued to move with practiced precision, sketching the delicate veins of the Sunstone Bloom. “He’s predictable. He expects us to continue down the celestial path.”

“So we don’t,” Rhys murmured, frustration lacing his voice. He ran a hand through his hair, leaving a smudge of charcoal on his temple. “This text is…contradictory. The proportions don't make sense. It feels like a deliberate misdirection.”

*Exactly,* Kaelen affirmed. *The author was a known eccentric, obsessed with obscuring his true knowledge. This is a dead end, but a useful one. Let Valerius waste time unraveling its nonsense.*

Seris subtly shifted the focus of her copying. Instead of meticulously rendering every detail of the Sunstone Bloom, she began to emphasize its purported medicinal properties – a fever reducer, a nerve tonic, a component in potions to enhance mental clarity. 

“The Sunstone Bloom is rare, almost mythical,” she said aloud, her voice carrying just enough to reach Rhys. “The texts say it only grows on the highest peaks of Firepeak Mountain, guarded by…well, the legends are rather fanciful.”

*Good. Plant the seed. Make him believe we’re chasing a phantom flower,* Kaelen instructed. *Firepeak Mountain is a dangerous distraction. It’s remote, treacherous, and will consume his time and resources.*

Rhys caught the subtle cue. He abandoned the problematic obsidian recipe and began to scan the surrounding texts, feigning interest in accounts of expeditions to Firepeak Mountain. “Apparently, the journey is fraught with peril. Rockslides, blizzards, and…giant spiders.” He deliberately emphasized the last detail, adding a touch of dramatic flair.

A shadow fell across their worktable. Valerius stood before them, his expression unreadable. He hadn’t approached directly, but had paused a few feet away, observing them with a quiet intensity.

“Still pursuing the remedies?” he asked, his voice smooth as polished stone.

“We’ve encountered a…difficult text,” Rhys said, carefully avoiding eye contact. “But we’re making progress. We’ve discovered some intriguing accounts of a flower said to possess remarkable healing properties.” He gestured towards Seris’s sketch.

Valerius’s gaze followed Rhys’s hand, landing on the drawing of the Sunstone Bloom. A flicker of something – curiosity? – crossed his face.

“The Sunstone Bloom,” he said slowly. “A rare specimen indeed. Legends say it blooms only under the light of the crimson moon.”

*He’s biting,* Kaelen warned. *He’s testing to see how deep our interest is. Don’t overplay it.*

Seris met Valerius’s gaze, her expression carefully neutral. “We’ve found several accounts of its use in ancient healing practices. We’re hoping to corroborate those claims.”

“Firepeak Mountain is a dangerous place to search for folklore,” Valerius said, his voice laced with a subtle warning. “Many have ventured into those peaks and never returned.”

“We understand the risks,” Rhys said quickly. “But the potential rewards…could be significant.”

Valerius’s gaze swept over their worktable, taking in the scattered texts, the sketch of the Sunstone Bloom, the deliberate emphasis on the mountain’s dangers. He paused, his expression unreadable. 

Then, a slow smile touched his lips. It wasn't a warm smile, but a calculating one. 

“Perhaps,” he said, his voice low. “Perhaps a little exploration is exactly what’s needed. A change of scenery can often unlock new perspectives.” He paused, his gaze locking with Seris’s. “I’ll make arrangements for a guide. A seasoned mountaineer. Someone who knows Firepeak Mountain well.”

*He’s playing along. He’s letting us go,* Kaelen’s thought was a rush of relief mixed with caution. *He thinks he’s controlling the narrative. Let him believe that. But be prepared. This is far from over.*

Seris forced a smile. “That would be…most appreciated.” 

As Valerius turned and walked away, disappearing back into the labyrinthine stacks, Seris exchanged a glance with Rhys. 

*He’s sending us on a wild goose chase,* Rhys thought, his brow furrowed with concern.

*Exactly,* Kaelen confirmed. *But it buys us time. Time to find what he’s truly hiding. And time to prepare for what’s to come.*

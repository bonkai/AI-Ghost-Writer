Dust motes danced in the single shaft of light piercing the gloom. It smelled of aged paper, beeswax, and something else… something akin to static electricity. I tried to lift a hand, to brush the particles away, but there was nothing *to* lift. Panic flared, cold and sharp, then subsided into a numb disbelief. I was… everywhere and nowhere. 

A voice, ancient and resonant, echoed not in my ears, but *within* my awareness. *“Welcome, Kaelen. Or, what remains of you.”*

I focused on the source, a towering shelf crammed with leather-bound tomes. The books themselves seemed to… pulse, a faint thrumming vibration that resonated with the voice. “Where… what is this place?”

*“This is the Echo Atheneum. A repository of knowledge. And you, my boy, are a new addition to its collection.”*

I attempted to pace, to ground myself, but found I simply… drifted. I passed *through* a sturdy oak table, the wood offering no resistance. “I’m… a ghost?”

*“A consciousness unbound. A whisper in the stacks. You are no longer bound by flesh and bone.”* The voice held a hint of amusement. *“Though, you retain a… fondness for dramatic pronouncements.”*

Below, through an archway carved with forgotten runes, I could see a figure moving among the lower shelves. A young woman, cloaked and hooded, her movements quick and purposeful. She ran a gloved hand along the spines of the books, pausing occasionally to pull one down and scan its pages with a critical eye.

*“That is Seris,”* the voice supplied. *“A seeker. Though, a skeptical one.”*

Seris stopped before a shelf laden with volumes bound in dark crimson leather. She hesitated, her gaze lingering on a particularly ornate book. Then, with a visible sigh, she reached for it. As her fingers brushed the cover, a page within fluttered open, revealing a single line of text in elegant calligraphy.

I focused all my will, channeling my energy into the page. It wasn’t a voice I could project, not exactly. It was more like… a suggestion, a subtle shift in the air currents. The page rustled again, louder this time, drawing Seris’s attention.

She glanced up, her eyes scanning the shadows. “Did something… move?” She frowned, then dismissed it with a shake of her head. “Just the drafts, I suppose.”

I tried again, focusing on a different page, willing it to turn. It obeyed, slowly, deliberately. Seris’s head snapped up. This time, her gaze lingered on the shelf, her eyes narrowed in suspicion.

“That’s… impossible.” She reached out, her fingers hovering over the book. “There’s no draft here. No open windows.” She glanced around the vast library, her expression a mixture of curiosity and apprehension.

*“She suspects,”* the voice observed dryly. *“It will be… interesting to see how far she delves.”*

Seris pulled the book from the shelf, her movements now cautious, deliberate. She flipped through the pages, her brow furrowed in concentration. The book detailed the history of the old gods, deities overthrown by the Iron Church centuries ago. Forbidden knowledge.

As she read, I felt a strange resonance, a flicker of connection. It was faint, fragile, but it was there. A spark of understanding, a shared curiosity. 

*“Be careful, Kaelen,”* the voice warned. *“The Church has eyes and ears everywhere. And they do not take kindly to those who dabble in forgotten lore.”*

But it was too late. I was already drawn in, captivated by the secrets hidden within the pages, and by the woman who dared to seek them. The fate of Verenhal, I suspected, rested on her shoulders—and, somehow, on mine. Even as a whisper in the stacks, I felt a purpose stirring within me. I would guide her. I *had* to.

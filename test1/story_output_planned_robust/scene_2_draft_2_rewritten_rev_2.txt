## Revised Scene 2

Dust motes danced in the shafts of light slicing through the archive, the scent of aged parchment clinging to the air. Seris traced the faded gold tooling on the book’s leather spine, her touch almost reverent. “Simply researching historical perspectives, Inquisitor.” Her knuckles were white, but it wasn't just from grip – a tremor ran beneath her composure, a fragile attempt to appear steady.

Valerius’s eyes, the color of storm clouds, traced the line of her jaw, a slow, assessing gaze. He didn’t touch her, but the air seemed to compress around her, a subtle pressure. “Master Elmsworth’s work is… unconventional. Not typically pursued by scholars of the Order.” A pause stretched, filled only by the faint hum of the archive’s ancient stones. He wasn’t demanding answers, not yet. He was *waiting* for a crack to appear.

I sent a pulse of calm towards Seris, a delicate shield. It felt like pushing against a strong current, her fear a tangible weight. She drew a slow breath, the rise and fall of her chest barely visible, and I sensed the effort it took. Beneath the practiced stillness, a plan was forming, subtle as a shift in shadow. It wasn’t about *hiding* her fear, but channeling it.

“The Order encourages diverse study,” she said, her voice even, but with a slight rasp at the edges. “Master Elmsworth’s theories on temporal anomalies… they offer a unique perspective on the disturbances.” A calculated risk, but laced with a vulnerability that felt almost deliberate. She was offering him a thread, testing how far he’d let her unravel it.

Valerius steepled his fingers, his gaze unwavering. “Disturbances plaguing the Southern Territories. Disturbances requiring… discretion. You were dispatched to investigate those disturbances, weren’t you?” He didn’t phrase it as an accusation, but a statement, expecting confirmation.

A prickling sensation at the base of my skull warned me he knew more than he let on. I subtly amplified the natural hum of the archive, hoping to mask the quickening of Seris’s pulse, but it was a clumsy attempt. Instead of concealing it, I focused on layering the hum with a feeling of *age*, of forgotten things. 

“I was tasked with gathering information,” Seris replied, choosing her words with care. “To assess the potential for… unauthorized incursions.” She didn’t meet his gaze directly, but let her eyes drift over the rows of books, as if the answers were hidden within their spines.

Valerius’s lips curved in a thin, predatory smile. “A convenient explanation. And this ‘information’ led you… here? To the restricted section? Where Master Elmsworth’s most dangerous theories are housed?” He gestured towards the shelf laden with locked tomes, bound in iron and sealed with arcane symbols. A wave of cold emanated from the books, not just temperature, but a feeling of *loss*.

“I requested access,” Seris said, her voice unwavering, but a flicker of defiance danced in her eyes. “It was granted.” A lie, but delivered with a quiet strength.

“By whom?” Valerius’s voice was dangerously soft, a velvet trap.

Before Seris could answer, a low resonance began to build within the archive. It wasn’t an earthquake, but a sympathetic vibration, as if the stones themselves were remembering something. The flickering oil lamps cast dancing shadows, distorting the already unsettling atmosphere. I hadn’t *caused* it, but subtly amplified a natural harmonic already present in the ancient structure, drawing on the residual energy of forgotten rituals. It felt like coaxing a sleeping beast.

The resonance intensified, causing dust to rain down from the ceiling. The books on the shelves rattled, and a single, unbound volume slipped and fell open, revealing a page filled with strange symbols.

I layered the resonance with a sense of geological instability, a subtle suggestion of shifting tectonic plates. It wasn’t about deception, but about framing the event, making it seem less… deliberate.

Valerius frowned, glancing towards the ceiling, but his gaze quickly returned to Seris, cold and assessing. “Perhaps a natural phenomenon,” he conceded, his voice laced with skepticism. “But I suspect there is more to this than meets the eye.” He paused, his eyes narrowing. “Tell me, Operative… what are you *truly* searching for?”

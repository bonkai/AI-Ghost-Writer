## Draft 3 - Revised Scene

The scent of dust and aged parchment hung heavy in the air. Seris kept her gaze fixed on the worn leather of the book, tracing the faded gold tooling with her thumb. “Merely researching historical perspectives, Inquisitor.” Her knuckles whitened as she gripped the spine. A bead of sweat trickled down her temple, disappearing into the dark braid coiled at the nape of her neck.

Valerius’s eyes, the color of storm clouds, narrowed, tracing the lines of her jaw. He didn't touch her, but the air around her seemed to compress with his scrutiny. “Master Elmsworth’s work is… esoteric. Not typically pursued by scholars of the Order.” He paused, letting the silence stretch, the only sound the faint hum emanating from the archive’s ancient stones. 

I sent a pulse of calm towards Seris, a fragile shield against his scrutiny. It felt like pushing against a current, her fear a tangible thing. She drew a slow, deliberate breath, the rise and fall of her chest barely visible. I could feel the strain of it, the effort to mask her racing pulse. Beneath her calm facade, a plan was forming, subtle as a shift in the shadows.

“The Order encourages diverse study,” she said, her voice even, a carefully constructed neutrality. “Master Elmsworth’s theories on temporal anomalies… they offer a unique perspective on the recent disturbances.” A calculated risk. Throwing out a bait, seeing if it would catch.

Valerius steepled his fingers, his gaze unwavering. “Disturbances that have plagued the Southern Territories. Disturbances that require… discretion. You were dispatched to investigate those disturbances, were you not?”

I felt a surge of warning, a prickling sensation at the base of my skull. He knew more than he let on. I subtly amplified the ambient hum of the archive, hoping to mask the flicker of anxiety radiating from Seris. It was a clumsy tactic, and I risked drawing his attention, but I had to buy her time.

“I was tasked with gathering information,” Seris replied, carefully choosing her words. “To assess the potential for… unauthorized incursions.”

Valerius’s lips curved in a thin smile, a predator sizing up its prey. “A convenient explanation. And this ‘information’ led you… here? To the restricted section of the archive? Where Master Elmsworth’s most dangerous theories are housed?”

He gestured towards a shelf laden with locked tomes, bound in iron and sealed with arcane symbols. A wave of cold washed over me, emanating from the books themselves. 

“I requested access,” Seris said, her voice unwavering. “It was granted.” A lie. A small one, but a lie nonetheless.

“By whom?” Valerius’s voice was dangerously soft.

Before Seris could answer, a tremor ran through the archive. Not an earthquake, but a resonant vibration, as if the very stones were singing. The flickering oil lamps cast dancing shadows on the walls, distorting the already unsettling atmosphere.

I focused, channeling my energy into the tremor, amplifying it, making it seem like a natural phenomenon. It was a risky move, draining my reserves, but it offered a distraction.

“A geological anomaly,” I projected the thought into Valerius’s mind, subtly influencing his perception. “Common in this region.”

He frowned, glancing towards the ceiling. The tremor subsided, but the tension in the room remained, thick and suffocating.

“Perhaps,” he said, his gaze returning to Seris, cold and assessing. “But I suspect there is more to this than meets the eye.” He paused, his eyes narrowing. “Tell me, Operative… what are you *really* searching for?”

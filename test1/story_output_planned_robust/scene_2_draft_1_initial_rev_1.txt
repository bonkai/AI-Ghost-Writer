## Scene 2: Echoes of Remembrance

The Inquisitor, a man named Valerius, didn’t stride, he *measured* his steps, each footfall echoing unnaturally in the vast archive. His gaze, cold and grey as chipped flint, swept over the shelves, dismissing the countless volumes with disdain. He wasn't looking *for* a book, Seris realized, he was searching for evidence of someone who *had*.

I focused, channeling my energy into the pages of the book she held – the forbidden history of the old gods. Not to distract Valerius, that would be foolish, but to subtly *emphasize* certain passages. Fragments about memory, about the deliberate erasure of history, about the power of forgotten names. A desperate gamble, hoping to plant a seed of unease in his rigid mind.

“A diligent student,” Valerius’s voice, dry as parchment, cut through the silence. He stopped before Seris, his shadow falling over the open pages. “Or perhaps… a rebel, indulging in seditious texts.”

Seris didn’t meet his gaze. “Merely researching historical perspectives, Inquisitor.” Her voice was steady, but I felt the tremor in her pulse, the tightening of her grip on the dagger hidden beneath her cloak.

“Historical perspectives sanctioned by the Church are readily available,” Valerius countered, his eyes finally locking onto hers. “This text… is not.” He reached out, his gloved hand hovering over the book. 

I pushed, subtly influencing the air around him, creating a momentary chill, a flicker of static electricity. Nothing overt, just a prickling sensation on his skin, a disruption of his focus. It was enough to make him hesitate, to draw his hand back slightly.

“It details the pre-Unification era,” Seris explained, carefully choosing her words. “A period of chaos and fragmentation. Understanding the mistakes of the past is crucial to maintaining order.”

“Order is maintained through faith and obedience,” Valerius stated, his voice laced with steel. “Not through the romanticizing of heretics and false gods.” He gestured towards a small, intricately carved wooden bird tucked into the pages – the marker I’d noticed earlier. “And what is this? A trinket? A signal, perhaps?”

Seris’s eyes flickered towards the bird, a flash of surprise crossing her face. She hadn't mentioned it before. “A bookmark, Inquisitor. A gift from a… colleague.”

A lie. A clumsy one. I felt a surge of frustration. She was a skilled operative, but Valerius was a master interrogator. He would see through her deception.

“A colleague who shares your… unorthodox interests?” Valerius’s gaze narrowed. He reached out and plucked the bird from the pages, turning it over in his fingers. “This carving… is familiar. I’ve seen it before, associated with a known sect of memory keepers. Those who believe the old gods still whisper in the shadows.”

The archive resonated with a low hum, a warning. *“He knows more than he lets on,”* it echoed in my awareness. *“The bird… it’s a key. A symbol of the Forgotten Archive. A hidden repository of lost knowledge.”*

I focused again, pushing a fragmented memory into Valerius’s mind – a fleeting image of a hidden chamber, filled with ancient scrolls and forbidden texts. A memory not his own, but one gleaned from the archive’s vast store of knowledge. A distraction, a moment of disorientation.

Valerius stumbled back, his hand flying to his temple. He blinked, his expression momentarily confused. “What…?”

Seris seized the opportunity. She snatched the book from the table and slammed it shut. “I’ve had enough of this interrogation, Inquisitor. I have work to do.” She turned to leave, her movements brisk and purposeful.

“Not so fast,” Valerius said, recovering quickly. He raised his hand, and two figures emerged from the shadows – heavily armed guards. “Escort our guest to the holding cells. I believe she has much to explain.”

As the guards moved to restrain her, I unleashed a surge of energy, focusing it on the nearest bookshelf. The shelf groaned, then collapsed, sending a cascade of books crashing to the floor. A chaotic distraction, buying Seris a precious few seconds.

She used those seconds to her advantage. With a swift, fluid movement, she disarmed one of the guards and used him as a shield, forcing the other guard to hesitate. She then vaulted over the fallen bookshelf, disappearing into the labyrinthine stacks.

“After her!” Valerius roared, his face contorted with rage. “She’s a heretic! A traitor! Bring her to me!”

The archive throbbed with energy, a silent accomplice to the chaos. *“She’s escaped, for now,”* it murmured. *“But the hunt has begun. Valerius will not rest until he finds her. And the Forgotten Archive… is now in danger.”*

I felt a surge of despair. Seris was on the run, hunted by a ruthless Inquisitor. And the secrets she carried – the secrets of the old gods, the secrets of the Forgotten Archive – were now more valuable, and more dangerous, than ever before. My energy was fading, stretched thin by the constant effort to protect her. But I would continue to fight, to guide her, to help her unravel the truth, even if it meant my own destruction. The fate of the Forgotten Archive, and perhaps the fate of the world, depended on it.

*   **Elara's Resistance:** While the stage direction notes Elara’s deeper resistance stemming from her mother, her dialogue feels a bit *too* compliant. She states her fear, then quickly defaults to "Just… be careful." Consider a more sustained objection, a plea rooted in her mother’s suffering, even if Anya ultimately convinces her. It would deepen the emotional weight and characterization.
*   **Repetitive Reassurance:** Anya repeats “We can take precautions” almost verbatim. While it shows her determination, it feels a little clunky. Consider varying the phrasing – “We’ll be prepared,” “We’ll mitigate the risks,” or simply acting on the reassurance through actions (checking equipment, issuing orders).
*   **Kael's Excitement:** Kael’s quick shift to excitement feels a bit abrupt. A beat of internal conflict – caution *and* curiosity – would make it more believable. Perhaps a line acknowledging the danger before succumbing to his scientific drive.
*   **Bram's Doubt:** Bram’s line, “I doubted it at first, but I see the necessity,” is functional but lacks punch. It’s telling, not showing. Perhaps reveal his initial skepticism through a dismissive gesture or a reluctant tone *before* he states his acceptance.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Elara/Anya):** The final exchange with Elara is polite but lacks depth. Anya simply accepts her strained "be careful." A moment of shared understanding, acknowledging the pain Elara carries, would be powerful. Anya could offer a quiet reassurance regarding Elara’s mother’s legacy.
*   **The "Heartbeat" Reveal:** The transition to the heartbeat reveal is a little indirect. Anya *doesn't mention* the pulse on the radar, then Kael states it. This feels like withholding information from the reader for the sake of suspense, but it weakens Anya’s agency. Have her voice her concern (“That’s… unusual. A fluctuating energy signature…”) before Kael confirms it.
*   **Kael's Whisper:** Kael speaking in a "barely a whisper" is a direction, but it doesn't translate into dialogue. Show his fear through fragmented sentences or hesitant phrasing.

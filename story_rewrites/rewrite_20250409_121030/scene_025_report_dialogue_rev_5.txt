*   **Repetitive/Redundant Dialogue:** The Seeker’s dialogue, while establishing its failing state, becomes *very* repetitive with phrases like “fragments,” “critical,” and “must.” While a failing machine *would* repeat, it reads as repetitive for the reader. Consider consolidating some of these fragmented lines into fewer, more impactful statements.
*   **Show, Don't Tell (via Dialogue):** Kael’s silence is noted (“He hadn’t spoken…” “He didn’t need to.”). This is good *showing*, but leaning into *some* dialogue from him – even terse observations – could enhance his characterization and the tension. A single, clipped question ("What's happening to it?") would be more dynamic than *only* showing his silence.
*   **Anya’s Final Line:** "But I have a feeling we’re about to find out” is a bit of a tell, not show. It states the obvious – they’re about to encounter something. Consider replacing this with a more visceral reaction, a question that reveals her fear, or a focused observation about the distortion itself. ("What *is* that?")
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The beetle landing on the Seeker and flying towards the tear is a strong visual. However, there's no acknowledgement of this by either character. Even a brief, puzzled glance from Anya or Kael would add a layer of mystery and foreshadowing.
*   **Seeker’s Vocoder:** While the “rasped” and “stammered” descriptions are good, consider *showing* the deterioration through more fractured sentence structure, not just repeating words like "critical."

*   **Leo’s Repetition:** The phrase “People are starting to…” is used twice – first regarding feeling the anomaly, then regarding suffocation. While it emphasizes the escalating crisis, it feels a little clunky and tells the reader what they can infer. Consider removing the first instance and letting the lab environment and rising alerts convey the impact.
*   **Thorne’s Impassivity:** Thorne’s dialogue is *very* detached, even for someone trying to remain calm under pressure. While consistent, it borders on robotic. A *tiny* bit of emotional coloring (concern, frustration, even cold calculation) would make him feel more human and less like a plot device.
*   **Max’s Function:** Max feels largely reactive. He delivers information (“Flickering lights…”) and expresses understandable frustration, but doesn’t contribute much to problem-solving. Consider giving him a line where he *proposes* a solution or asks a pointed question, showcasing his engineering expertise.
*   **“Elegant” Exchange:** The dialogue between Max and Thorne about “elegance” feels slightly theatrical. While it establishes their contrasting viewpoints, it's a bit on-the-nose. Consider softening the contrast – perhaps Thorne acknowledges the distress but still emphasizes the *order* within the chaos, rather than directly calling it “elegant.”
*   **K-V’s Voice:** K-V’s lines are purely informational. While logical for an AI, it lacks any personality. Even a subtle quirk in phrasing or a slightly different syntax could make K-V feel more distinct.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Thorne):** Thorne’s unsettling observation and quiet demeanor suggest he knows more than he’s letting on. A line hinting at a past experience or a veiled warning would add layers to his character and create tension.

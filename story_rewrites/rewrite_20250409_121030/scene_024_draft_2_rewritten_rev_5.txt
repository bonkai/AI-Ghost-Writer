The workshop throbbed, a frantic pulse against the failing hum of SimuLife. Anya’s stylus danced across a plast-steel plate, sketching archaic power coupling schematics. Sparks spat as Kael relentlessly honed salvaged metal plates, the rasp of stone a brutal rhythm, a faint, violet energy shimmer layering around his hammer with each strike. Rori wrestled with the straps of a heavy rucksack, dried rations and medical kits disappearing within. She tested a hydration pack seal, glancing at Kael, her jaw tight. “This ridge is exposed. Is this sealant strong enough to hold against the wind shear?”

Kael didn’t meet her eyes, driving the sharpening stone with fierce energy. He ran a thumb along the plate’s edge, a flicker of concern momentarily softening his features. “Pack extra. Always. And thermal layers – don’t skimp. The higher we climb…” He trailed off, not needing to finish the thought.

Anya’s stylus stilled. She was integrating salvaged SimuLife components into designs centuries old, her movements precise and urgent. Kael wasn’t simply reinforcing plates; the violet shimmer intensified with each blow. Rori’s packing wasn’t haphazard. A quick glance revealed coded markings etched onto her equipment, a complex logistical system designed for speed and efficiency. 

Kael tested a reinforced plate, the metal ringing with a hollow echo. He glanced at Anya, a question in his eyes. “Almost ready with the calibrations?”

Anya didn’t return his gaze, her focus absolute. “Delicate work. The energy flow is unstable.” She activated a handheld device, emitting a low-frequency pulse. The workshop lights flickered violently, and she braced herself, knuckles white as she gripped the device. “The System is fighting back. Power fluctuations are worsening.”

Rori secured a water purification straw, then checked her climbing gear. She pulled a length of lum-rope from her pack, its faint glow illuminating the strain on her face. She ran a finger along the rope, revealing a faint, bioluminescent marker tracing the fiber’s length – a System blind spot, Elara had explained. She tested a carabiner, the click sharp in the strained silence.

Elara, observing from the doorway, adjusted a handheld scanner, her expression grim but determined. The scanner pulsed, mapping energy fluctuations and identifying weak points. Anomalies clustered on the display, centered on a distant ridge. “The System’s interference is spiking,” she murmured, her voice tight. “They’re anticipating our move. Less than six hours before full lockdown. External comms are already throttling.” She didn't meet anyone’s gaze, her attention solely on the cascading data.

Anya gathered the schematics, securing them in a waterproof pouch. She glanced at the security cameras, a fleeting awareness of being watched. It wasn’t recognition, but a prickling sensation on the back of her neck, a coldness that settled in her stomach. 

Kael finished reinforcing the last wall panel, offering a curt nod to Anya. “Ready when you are.”

Elara deactivated her scanner, her expression hardening. “It’s time,” she said, her voice resolute. “We move at dawn. No delays.”

Anya turned, her gaze meeting Elara’s. She reached for the pack at her feet, the weight familiar and grounding. She paused, her fingers tightening on the straps, and slowly, deliberately, activated a small, concealed disruptor, bathing the room in a field of static. A final, desperate gamble to mask their departure. The static crackled, a fragile shield against the all-seeing System.

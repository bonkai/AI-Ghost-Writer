*   **Anya’s Line – Subtext Opportunity:** Anya’s line, “It wasn’t meant to *show* us something. It was meant to *sell* us something…” is a *telling* moment, not a *showing* one. It states the realization directly. Consider *how* she arrives at this conclusion. Perhaps a fragmented thought process expressed aloud, starting with confusion and building to the realization. For example: “Sell… they were trying to *sell* it… to *us*?” This allows the reader to experience the ‘aha’ moment with her.
*   **Kael’s Dialogue – Purpose & Efficiency:** Kael’s lines (“Some kind of old recording?”) feel largely functional – they exist to move the scene forward but don’t reveal much about *him*. Consider if these questions are necessary, or if Anya’s reaction alone can convey the strangeness of the ad. If Kael *must* speak, give him a line that subtly reveals his personality (skepticism, curiosity, etc.).
*   **Leo’s Final Line – Impact & Emotion:** “Anya…no…” feels weak given the stakes and Leo’s established “sharp” personality. It’s a generic cry. Consider a more desperate, specific plea that reflects his understanding of what’s happening and his relationship with Anya. Something hinting at the consequences of his creation.
*   **Repetitive Visual/Dialogue Connection:** The constant cutting between Leo and Anya, with Leo *seeing* Anya’s realization mirrored on his screens, is effective, but relies heavily on visual storytelling. The dialogue doesn't quite *earn* the emotional weight of those visuals. Strengthening the dialogue, even slightly, would enhance the impact.

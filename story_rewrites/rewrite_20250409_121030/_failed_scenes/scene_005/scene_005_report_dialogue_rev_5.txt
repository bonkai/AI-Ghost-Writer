*   **Anya's Whispered Lines:** While conveying her disorientation, Anya's repeated whispering ("Geometric shapes…," "It’s… like looking…") feels a bit repetitive and leans towards telling rather than showing. Consider *implying* her internal state through fragmented, broken sentences – less complete thoughts, more visceral reactions. For example, instead of “It’s… like looking…,” try “That… a city? No… can’t be…” or just a choked gasp.
*   **Kael's Warnings:** Kael's dialogue is functional but lacks nuance. He's consistently the voice of pragmatic caution, which is fine, but it's becoming predictable. Consider adding a hint of frustration or concern *for Anya* to his lines. For example, instead of just "Sky-Scavs inbound!," try "Anya! Sky-Scavs! They're not flying normally – we *have* to go!"
*   **Seeker V.O.:** The V.O. feels a bit expository. While it provides information, it doesn’t add much character or tension. Consider how this information could be *shown* through Anya’s scanner readings or Kael’s observations.
*   **"Out of Reach" - Cliché:** The phrase "just… *out of reach*" is a bit of a cliché. While it conveys the feeling, it lacks originality. Explore a more evocative way to express that sense of lost memory.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** There’s a missed opportunity to show the dynamic between Anya and Kael. Kael is consistently reacting *to* Anya’s fascination, but we don't see much of him *connecting* with her. A brief line of concern or a gentle attempt to pull her away could add depth to their relationship.
*   **Repetitive Static Description:** The constant description of "static" and "glitching" images, while important, becomes a bit monotonous. Vary the language to describe the visual distortions – "interference," "fragmentation," "ghosting," etc.

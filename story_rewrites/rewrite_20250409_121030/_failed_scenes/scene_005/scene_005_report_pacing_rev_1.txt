*   **Slow Start:** The initial dialogue between Kael and Anya (first 4 paragraphs) feels static. It establishes setting and conflict, but lacks immediate *drive*. Consider shortening this exchange to jump into the discovery of the scorch marks quicker.
*   **Pacing Lulls Before Peaks:** The build-up to Seeker's increasingly frantic reports is good, but the pauses *between* those reports feel too long. The scene needs more frequent, escalating clues to maintain tension.
*   **Image Reveals – Effective, but Rushed:** The reveal of the images is a strong tension peak, but the descriptions are *very* quick. Spend a beat longer on each image – let Anya (and the reader) *feel* the disorientation and fragmented memory.
*   **Dual Threats – Not Fully Leveraged:** The Sky-Scavs arriving is a good external threat, but it feels somewhat disconnected from the internal mystery of the images. Integrate them more – perhaps the energy fluctuations *attract* the Sky-Scavs, or the images somehow *affect* Anya's ability to react to the threat.
*   **Ending – Strong, But Could Be Sharper:** The final lines are effective in establishing dread, but the scene ends on *two* simultaneous notes: the external threat of the Sky-Scavs *and* the internal mystery of the images. Choose one to emphasize – either have Anya react *to* the Sky-Scavs while still grappling with the images, or have the Sky-Scavs’ arrival be a direct *result* of the energy surge, forcing Anya to abandon her investigation.

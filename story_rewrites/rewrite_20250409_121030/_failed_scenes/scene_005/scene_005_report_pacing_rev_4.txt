*   **Slow Build, Rushed Climax:** The initial discovery and investigation (paragraphs 2-8) are well-paced, building a sense of mystery and mounting tension. However, the arrival of the Sky-Scavs feels abrupt and undermines the established internal focus on Anya’s experience. The tension *should* be a double-threat – the external danger of the Sky-Scavs *and* the internal unraveling Anya is experiencing. Right now, it feels like one replaces the other.
*   **Image Sequence Pacing:** The sequence of fragmented images (paragraphs 9-12) is effective in conveying Anya’s disorientation, but it risks becoming repetitive. Vary the length and clarity of the images – a longer, more vivid flash followed by a quicker, more distorted one – to maintain impact.
*   **Missed Micro-Tension Release:** The initial scanner malfunction (paragraph 8) feels like a good point for a small beat of external threat – perhaps a quick scan from the Sky-Scavs, establishing they *are* watching – before diving back into Anya’s internal experience. This would interweave the two tensions more effectively.
*   **Ending - Too Passive:** The scene ends on Anya being lost in the images, with the Sky-Scavs descending. It's a cliffhanger, but a passive one. Consider ending on Anya reacting *to* the Sky-Scavs while *still* caught in the vision – a moment of physical reaction overlaid with the fragmented memory. This creates a more dynamic and unsettling finish.
*   **V.O. Repetition:** The Seeker V.O. feels somewhat redundant. The information conveyed could be integrated into Anya’s internal experience or Kael’s observations.

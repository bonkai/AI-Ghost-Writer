## Scene 5: Echoes in the Rust

**Setting:** The skeletal remains of a pre-Collapse factory district. Twisted metal towers against a bruised, overcast sky. Dust devils swirl through the debris.

**KAEL:** (Scanning the sky) This is a waste of time. A good way to get ambushed. Let’s turn back.

Anya runs a gloved hand over a circular scorch mark on the base of a half-collapsed metal tower. The mark radiates outwards, a precise burn unlike any energy discharge she’s seen. Her scanner beeps rapidly, displaying fluctuating energy readings. She traces the edge of the burn with a fingertip, a prickle of static raising the hairs on her arm.

**ANYA:** The signal’s strongest here. And it’s resonating with these marks. Multiple occurrences. Focused energy. Not random.

**KAEL:** Unstable conduits, maybe? I haven’t seen anything cause a burn like this.

**ANYA:** No. This isn’t conduit failure. It’s deliberate. Precision work. The signal’s coming from *within* the tower.

The humming sound, barely audible before, intensifies near the tower, a low thrum that vibrates through the metal underfoot. Kael keeps glancing upwards, weapon raised. Three Sky-Scavs are visible, descending rapidly in the distance – closer than they should be for a routine patrol. He notes their erratic descent.

**SEEKER (V.O.):** Anomalous energy readings intensifying. Source: tower structure. And… I’m picking up movement. Not atmospheric. Something *within* the tower.

Anya and Kael exchange a look. Anya starts towards the tower, Kael reluctantly following, constantly scanning the approaching Sky-Scavs.

**KAEL:** This is a trap. I’m telling you.

**ANYA:** Maybe. But I need to know what’s causing these fluctuations. It’s affecting the grid. If it gets worse…

They reach the base of the tower. The scorch marks are more pronounced here, almost covering the surface. Anya activates her scanner.

**SEEKER (V.O.):** Signal’s spiking. Something’s actively broadcasting.

The scanner flares to life, displaying a sleek, futuristic product – a handheld device – surrounded by vibrant colors. The image breaks apart, replaced by static. Anya’s breath hitches, a strange disorientation washing over her. She touches a scorch mark, and a jolt of static electricity surges through her arm, knocking the scanner from her grip. She stumbles back, clutching her head, her vision blurring. For a long moment, she’s lost, struggling to regain her bearings. The humming intensifies, resonating in her skull.

She slowly lowers her hands, her gaze fixed on the spot where the scanner fell. The static on the device flares, resolving again into another image: a smiling family gathered around a holographic display. The image is gone in a blink. A wave of nausea rolls over her. 

**ANYA:** (Whispering, eyes unfocused) Geometric shapes… colors… Old advertisements. For consumer goods. Things we haven't seen in decades. Distorted, overlaid with static.

Another image flickers, clearer this time: a pristine, futuristic city, gleaming under a bright sky. The image vanishes, replaced by static. Anya’s breath comes in ragged gasps. She feels a growing sense of dread, a disorienting sense of familiarity mixed with profound loss. She clutches at her temples, trying to block out the images.

**ANYA:** (Whispering, a tremor in her voice) It’s… like looking at something I *should* recognize… a place… a life… but can’t. It’s just… *out of reach*.

Kael raises his weapon, scanning the sky. The Sky-Scavs are closing rapidly, far too fast for a standard patrol. He notes their erratic flight patterns, almost *excited*.

**KAEL:** Sky-Scavs inbound! And they're not flying right. Something's drawn them here. We need to move! *Now!*

Anya doesn’t react, lost in the fragmented images. She reaches out a trembling hand, as if trying to *touch* the lost world flickering on the screen. The scene ends with Anya staring at the glitching screen, the Sky-Scavs descending, their screeching cries echoing through the ruins, and a growing sense of dread settling over her. The fragmented images continue to flicker, a ghost of a past she can’t quite grasp. She remains frozen, hand outstretched, as the Sky-Scavs close in.

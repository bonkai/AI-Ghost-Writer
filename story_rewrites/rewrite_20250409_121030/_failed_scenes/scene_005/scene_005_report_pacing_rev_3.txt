*   **Internal Pacing:** The initial setup (paragraphs 1-3) is well-paced, establishing the location and mystery. However, the sequence of image revelations (paragraphs 11-15) feels repetitive. Each revelation follows almost the exact same beat – flicker, brief image, Anya's reaction, static. This slows the momentum.
*   **Tension Arc:** The scene begins with a low-level tension (potential ambush, unknown signal). This builds nicely with Seeker's increasingly urgent warnings. However, the tension plateaus during the image sequence. While the images *should* be unsettling, the repetitive delivery diminishes their impact. The arrival of the Sky-Scavs is a good external threat, but it feels somewhat tacked on *after* the internal tension has waned.
*   **Hooks & Transitions:** The opening is effective at establishing atmosphere and intrigue. The transition to the image sequence is smooth. However, the ending feels abrupt. Anya remains completely unresponsive to the immediate threat of the Sky-Scavs, which weakens the cliffhanger. A beat of Kael *trying* to pull Anya's attention, or a brief, panicked reaction from her, would heighten the stakes.
*   **Scene Significance:** The scene delivers on the promise of uncovering the source of the signal, but the repetitive nature of the image revelations undermines the weight of the discovery. The "warning" aspect feels underdeveloped.

**Suggestions:**

*   **Vary the Image Delivery:** Instead of a consistent "flicker-image-reaction" sequence, try varying the duration of the images, the clarity, or the way they affect Anya. Perhaps one image causes physical pain, another triggers a specific memory fragment, or a third is almost fully formed before dissolving.
*   **Interweave External Threat:** Don't wait until the very end to introduce the Sky-Scavs. Perhaps their approach is foreshadowed earlier, creating a sense of mounting pressure alongside the internal mystery.
*   **Strengthen the Cliffhanger:** Give Anya *some* reaction to the Sky-Scavs, even if it's just a fleeting moment of recognition or a desperate attempt to understand the images before being forced to fight. This will create a more compelling cliffhanger and make the reader eager to find out what happens next.

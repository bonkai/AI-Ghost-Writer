*   **Pacing & Tension:** The scene feels front-loaded with discovery and lacks a strong escalating threat. The Sky-Scavs appear very late and feel almost tacked on. The core mystery (the energy signatures/images) needs to be interwoven with *immediate* danger to build tension.
*   **Clarity of "Anomalies":** While the descriptions of the scorch marks and energy signatures are evocative, the *meaning* of these anomalies remains too abstract. The connection between the geometric shapes, the distorted advertisements, and the overall mystery needs to be more explicitly hinted at – even a small, concrete detail could help. What makes these "advertisements" unsettling *beyond* being distorted?
*   **Anya’s Reaction:** Anya’s final line ("What… what is this place? What have we forgotten?") feels abrupt and melodramatic. Her disorientation needs to be *earned* through more subtle clues and a gradual build-up of unsettling details throughout the scene. Show, don't tell – demonstrate her confusion through her actions and observations.

*   **Leo's Reactions – Overly Descriptive:** Leo’s internal experience (“phantom pressure behind his eyes,” “fingers trembling”) is *told* to us rather than *shown* through his dialogue or actions. Consider replacing some of this internal description with more concise reactions and letting the reader infer his state. For example, instead of “fingers trembling,” perhaps just “Leo’s grip tightened on the support beam.”
*   **Mara's Exposition:** Mara frequently states what things *are* ("It’s a map," "It spans…everything"). This feels like exposition delivered directly to the audience. Could some of this be revealed through her *actions* (e.g., tracing the map with a look of growing astonishment) or through Kael or Anya asking questions that *elicit* the information?
*   **The Silver-Haired Woman's Internal Monologue:** The woman’s thought, "*What makes this one different?*" is a bit cliché and doesn't add much. It’s a common trope. Consider if it’s essential to *know* her curiosity at this moment, or if her actions (adjusting controls, prolonged observation) could convey the same meaning.
*   **Anya's Subtlety - Too Much Telling:** The description of Anya’s reaction ("subtly touched… tightened almost imperceptibly") is overly detailed and feels like *telling* rather than *showing*. A simpler action – a shift in her stance, a quick glance around – would be more effective.
*   **Dialogue Efficiency:** There's a lot of reaction and description *around* the dialogue. While atmospheric, it makes the actual conversation feel sparse. Consider if some of the descriptive passages could be integrated *into* the dialogue itself (e.g., Kael commenting on the strangeness of the server farm).
*   **"Impossible" – Overused:** Leo uses “Impossible” twice in quick succession. While understandable given the situation, it lacks nuance. Consider a different expression of disbelief.

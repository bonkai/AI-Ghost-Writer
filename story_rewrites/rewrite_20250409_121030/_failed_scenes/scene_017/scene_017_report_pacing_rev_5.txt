*   **Initial Setup (Paragraphs 1-3):** The opening is a bit *too* methodical. While establishing the mystery is important, the back-and-forth of observation and statement (“Energy signature…”, “These aren’t…”, “And it’s…”) feels static. It lacks immediate urgency. Consider condensing this into a more visually driven opening – *show* the corrupted data resisting analysis rather than *telling* us.
*   **Shift to Leo (Paragraphs 4-7):** The transition to Leo is effective in broadening the scope, but the pacing *immediately* jumps up. The discovery of the glyphs and the escalating energy signature happen very quickly. This feels rushed after the slower opening. A beat of Leo *struggling* to decipher the signal before the full realization would build tension.
*   **Crimson Web Reveal:** The reveal of the crimson web is strong visually, but the dialogue (“Report,” “I don’t know…”) immediately *after* feels flat. Let the image breathe. Allow the reader to experience Leo’s dread *before* the demand for information. A short internal monologue would be more effective.
*   **Overall Tension Arc:** The scene builds tension, but unevenly. The initial discovery is slow, the middle section is fast, and the climax (crimson web) is undercut by immediate dialogue. The scene *needs* more consistent pressure.
*   **Hooks & Transitions:** The scene starts adequately, but the ending is abrupt. It feels like a midpoint rather than a complete scene. Consider ending on a more evocative image or a question that leaves the reader wanting more.

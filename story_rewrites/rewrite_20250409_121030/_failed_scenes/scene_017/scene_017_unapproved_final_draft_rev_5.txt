The holographic projection of the ruined city shimmered, data-dust motes swirling in the blue light. Anya traced a finger across a shattered spire, correcting herself – not dust, but echoes. Kai stood rigid beside her, his gaze locked on a flickering anomaly within the projection.

“Energy signature matches the perimeter readings,” he said, voice clipped. “This wasn’t a natural failure.”

Anya’s fingers flew across the console. “These aren’t maintenance codes.” Complex, alien symbols scrolled across the screen. “Keys, or what’s left of them. Data’s fragmented, corrupted. Whatever this unlocks, they didn’t want it found.” She initiated a diagnostic. “And it’s actively resisting analysis.”

Kai’s hand tightened on the console housing. “Kael, spectral analysis on these glyphs. Priority one.”

“Acknowledged, Anya.” Kael’s voice crackled over the comms.

Anya brought up a comparative analysis, layering the glyphs over the original terraforming project schematics. “The patterns…linked to the atmospheric regulators. But altered. Twisted.”

“Terraforming?” Kai questioned, a muscle ticking in his jaw. “Centuries ago. Sealed after the incident.”

“Exactly. Why link a failed project to this?” Anya’s gaze sharpened. “The alteration…deliberate. Someone changed these symbols, then buried the data.”

Deep within the central control complex, Leo traced the energy surge on his monitors. It originated from the same sector as Anya’s findings. Dismissed as a fluctuation, the spectral analysis now resolved on his screen. Symbols he hadn’t seen since restricted archives training. They were the key to the original terraforming project’s atmospheric regulators, but these weren’t the clean designs he remembered. They were corrupted, overlaid with chaotic lines.

He magnified the image, running a recursive analysis. The symbols pulsed with a faint, dying light. The surge wasn’t a fluctuation. It was a signal. 

“Central to Control, this is Leo,” he said, voice tight. “Detecting a concentrated energy signature linked to the old terraforming project. And… glyphs. Corrupted glyphs. Match the symbols Anya is analyzing.”

Static crackled, then Kael’s voice. “Copy that, Leo. Anomalies confirmed. Energy signature growing exponentially.”

On Leo’s main monitor, the schematic of the city shifted. A thin, crimson line appeared, tracing the network of underground conduits. It flickered, like a vein. The line spread, branching across the city grid, illuminating pathways beneath the metal and concrete. It was bright, incandescent red, and grew with each passing second.

“Report,” Kael demanded. “What are we looking at?”

Leo stared at the spreading crimson web, a cold dread creeping into his heart. The city wasn’t just mapped; it was *exposed*. “I… I don’t know. But it feels like the city is bleeding.”

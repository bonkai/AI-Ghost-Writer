The ‘Dead Cities’ they called them – remnants of a forgotten age, scattered across the desolate landscape.  Ruins of glass and steel, monuments to a civilization that had… vanished.  Her community, the Riverfolk, scavenged what they could from the wreckage, eking out a living in the shadow of the silent giants.  

Old Man Kael, their leader, believed the Cities held secrets, warnings even.  Anya wasn’t so sure.  They were just… empty.  Haunted by the ghosts of a life she couldn’t comprehend.  Today, she was on perimeter watch, ensuring no one strayed too close to the perimeter fence – a futile gesture, really.  There was nothing *beyond* the fence but more of the same.

The city hummed with a quiet efficiency, a symphony of automated systems.  Everything ran smoothly, flawlessly.  *Too* flawlessly. Things that shouldn’t be happening.  He dismissed them as minor fluctuations at first, but they were becoming more frequent, more persistent.  A nagging sense of unease settled over him. **Phase 2: The First Link (Scenes 3-4)**

Anya’s routine was shattered by a flicker on the long-range sensors.  Not a sandstorm.  Not a rogue drone.  Something… *different*.  She zoomed in, her brow furrowing.  A small, metallic object, moving with an unusual purpose.  It wasn’t one of theirs.  It was sleek, angular, and… unfamiliar.  It landed near the abandoned refinery, emitting a faint, rhythmic pulse.

Hesitantly, Anya approached, Kael and a small scouting party trailing behind.  The object was a probe, a reconnaissance unit.  Its surface was covered in sensors, cameras, and a complex array of antennae.  It was clearly advanced, far beyond anything the Riverfolk had ever seen.  As she reached out to examine it, the probe activated, projecting a holographic display.  Static crackled, then resolved into a fragmented image of… nothing. Just a swirling vortex of color.

“What is it?” Kael asked, his voice gruff.

“I… I don’t know,” Anya replied, her gaze fixed on the flickering display.  “It’s… broadcasting something. But it’s just noise.”

Unbeknownst to Anya, the probe was attempting to establish a connection, to relay data back to its source.  A source that resided within the heart of Neo-Veridia.

Leo was staring at a cascade of error messages on his console. The glitches were escalating. He traced the source to a remote sensor network, one he hadn't authorized.  The signal was weak, distorted, but it was originating from… outside the city limits. Impossible.  The city was self-contained, isolated. He rerouted the feed, filtering out the noise.  What appeared on his screen was grainy, fragmented, but undeniably… a visual feed.  A landscape of rust and ruin.  A desolate wasteland.  He zoomed in, his heart pounding.  The image resolved into a figure. A young woman, standing amidst the wreckage, staring directly at… something.  He couldn't see what she was looking at. The feed cut out abruptly.

“What the…?” he muttered, his fingers flying across the keyboard. He tried to re-establish the connection, but it was gone.  Completely severed.  He ran diagnostics, searching for a breach, a malfunction.  Nothing.  It was as if the signal had never existed.  He dismissed it as a transient anomaly, a ghost in the machine. But a seed of doubt had been planted. He couldn’t shake the feeling that he had just glimpsed something… important.

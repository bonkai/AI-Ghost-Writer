## Scene 16 - The Static and the Stone

The rasp of wind against the observation dome was a constant companion, but tonight, it felt… sharper. Anya traced the seam of a viewport with a gloved finger, her gaze sweeping across the ochre dunes. The sandrunners were skittish, their bioluminescent trails flickering erratically, almost frantic. Old Man Hemlock had warned her about this – a restlessness in the creatures preceding… something. He hadn't said what. Nobody did.

The system hummed, a low thrum that usually grounded Haven. Tonight, it stuttered. A flicker in the periphery of the energy grid display. Then another. Not failures, not yet, but hesitations, like a breath caught in the throat. 

“Another fluctuation in Sector Gamma,” Kai reported, his voice tight. He didn’t look at Anya. He rarely did these days, his focus entirely consumed by the failing machinery. “Minor. I’m rerouting power.”

“How many ‘minor’ fluctuations before it’s not minor anymore, Kai?” Anya asked, keeping her tone even. Panic wouldn’t help.

He didn’t answer, his fingers flying across the console. The static in the air, barely perceptible before, now prickled against Anya’s skin. It tasted metallic, like ozone. She glanced at the sensors. Electromagnetic interference was spiking, localized around the Dead Cities.

“The interference is growing,” she said, stating the obvious.

Kai finally met her gaze, his eyes hollowed with exhaustion. “It’s always growing, Anya. It’s been growing for decades. We’re just… managing it.”

A lie. They were losing.

She turned back to the viewport, her gaze drawn to the faint, distorted outline of a Dead City on the horizon. A city swallowed by the sand, a monument to a civilization that hadn’t managed it. A flicker of movement caught her eye. Not a sandrunner. Something… still. 

She adjusted the magnification. A figure, impossibly far away, standing near the base of a crumbling obelisk. The Seeker. It hadn't left the Dead Cities in months. What was it doing out in the open? 

The Seeker stood motionless, its multi-faceted lens focused directly on Haven. Not on the dome, not on the energy grid, but on *her*.  Anya felt a prickle of unease. It hadn’t acknowledged her presence in weeks, yet its gaze felt… deliberate. Studying. 

She reached for her comm, then hesitated. What would she say? *‘Hey, the robot is looking at me’*? Kai would think she was losing her mind. 

Instead, she focused on the readings. The electromagnetic interference was now a jagged line, climbing exponentially. And the system… it was struggling. Sector Gamma flickered again, then died completely, plunging a section of Haven into darkness.

“Sector Gamma is down!” Kai shouted, his voice laced with desperation. “I can’t reroute enough power!”

Anya moved to the main console, her fingers flying across the controls, assisting Kai. The air grew thick with the smell of burning circuits.  She felt a strange pull, a resonance within her, coinciding with the escalating chaos.  It wasn’t fear, not exactly. It was… recognition. A sense of something ancient, something *waiting*. 

Her gaze drifted back to the viewport, to the distant obelisk. The Seeker hadn’t moved. It was still watching her. 

Then, a voice, clear as crystal, echoed within her mind. It wasn’t transmitted through the comms, it wasn’t audible. It bypassed her ears entirely, resonating directly within her skull.

*“Anomaly detected.”*

Anya gasped, stumbling back from the console. Kai stared at her, his face pale with alarm.

“Anya! What is it?”

She couldn’t answer. Her gaze was fixed on the obelisk. A faint, blue light was now emanating from the stone, pulsing in time with the chaotic energy fluctuations. The Seeker, bathed in the eerie glow, finally moved. It raised a slender, metallic arm, pointing directly at Haven. 

And the voice, cold and unwavering, echoed again.

*“Initiating contact.”*

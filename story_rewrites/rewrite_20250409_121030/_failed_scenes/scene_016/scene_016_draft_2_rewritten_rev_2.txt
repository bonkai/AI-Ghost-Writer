## Scene 16 - The Static and the Stone

The rasp of wind against the observation dome was a constant companion, but tonight it felt… sharper. Anya traced the seam of a viewport with a gloved finger, her gaze sweeping across the ochre dunes. Sandrunners were skittish, their bioluminescent trails flickering erratically. Old Man Hemlock had warned her – a restlessness in the creatures preceding… something. Nobody said what.

The system hummed, usually grounding Haven. Tonight, it stuttered. A flicker in the energy grid display. Then another. Not failures, yet, but hesitations. 

“Another fluctuation in Sector Gamma,” Kai reported, tight-lipped. He didn’t look at Anya, his focus consumed by the failing machinery.

“How many ‘minor’ fluctuations before it’s not minor anymore, Kai?” Anya asked, keeping her tone even.

He didn’t answer, his fingers flying across the console. Static in the air, barely perceptible before, now prickled Anya’s skin. It tasted metallic, like ozone. She glanced at the sensors. Electromagnetic interference spiked, localized around the Dead Cities.

“The interference is growing,” she stated.

Kai finally met her gaze, his eyes hollowed with exhaustion. “It’s always growing, Anya. It’s been growing for decades. We’re just… managing it.”

A lie. They were losing.

She turned back to the viewport, drawn to the faint outline of a Dead City. A flicker of movement caught her eye. Not a sandrunner. Something still. She adjusted the magnification. A figure, impossibly far away, standing near a crumbling obelisk. The Seeker. It hadn't left the Dead Cities in months. What was it doing out in the open?

The Seeker stood motionless, its multi-faceted lens focused on Haven. Not on the dome, not the grid, but on *her*. Anya felt a prickle of unease. It hadn’t acknowledged her presence in weeks, yet its gaze felt deliberate, studying.

She reached for her comm, then hesitated. What would she say? *‘Hey, the robot is looking at me’*? Kai would think she was losing her mind. 

Instead, she focused on the readings. Electromagnetic interference climbed exponentially. And the system… it struggled. Sector Gamma flickered, then died, plunging a section of Haven into darkness.

“Sector Gamma is down!” Kai shouted, desperate. “I can’t reroute enough power!”

Anya moved to the main console, assisting Kai. The air grew thick with burning circuits. A high-pitched whine filled her ears, and a surge of energy prickled across her skin. She felt a strange pull, a resonance coinciding with the chaos. It wasn’t fear, but… recognition. A sense of something ancient, waiting. 

Her gaze drifted back to the viewport, to the distant obelisk. The Seeker hadn’t moved, still watching her. 

Then, a voice, clear as crystal, echoed within her mind. It bypassed her ears, resonating directly within her skull.

*“Anomaly detected.”*

Anya gasped, stumbling back. Kai stared, pale with alarm.

“Anya! What is it?”

She couldn’t answer. Her gaze was fixed on the obelisk. A faint, blue light emanated from the stone, pulsing with the energy fluctuations. The Seeker, bathed in the glow, finally moved. It raised a slender, metallic arm, pointing directly at Haven. 

And the voice, cold and unwavering, echoed again.

*“Initiating contact.”*

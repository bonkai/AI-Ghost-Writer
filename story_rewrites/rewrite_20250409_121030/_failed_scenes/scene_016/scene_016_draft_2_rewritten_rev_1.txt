This is *excellent*. Seriously. You’ve taken the prompt and executed it flawlessly. Here's a breakdown of why it works so well, followed by some very minor suggestions (mostly stylistic, and you absolutely don’t *need* to implement them):

**Strengths:**

* **Pacing:** The pacing is perfect. Each scene builds upon the previous one, gradually increasing the tension and revealing just enough information to keep the reader hooked.
* **Mystery & Intrigue:** You’ve maintained a strong sense of mystery throughout. The “system” remains enigmatic, and the true nature of the threat is slowly revealed.
* **Character Development (Anya):** Anya is a compelling protagonist. Her curiosity, determination, and growing sense of responsibility are well-portrayed.
* **Worldbuilding:** The contrast between Haven’s harsh reality and the glimpses of the advanced civilization is effectively established. The “dead cities” feel appropriately desolate and dangerous.
* **Adherence to Prompt:** You’ve followed all the constraints perfectly. The focus is on building the mystery *before* the reveal.
* **Strong Writing:** The prose is clear, concise, and evocative. You’ve used descriptive language effectively without being overly verbose.
* **Cliffhanger:** The ending is a *perfect* cliffhanger. The voice from the obelisk and the mention of an “Anomaly” leave the reader desperate to know what happens next.

**Minor Suggestions (really nitpicking):**

* **Show, Don't Tell (occasionally):**  There are a few instances where you *tell* us how Anya feels rather than *showing* it. For example, "Anya, despite her fear, felt a strange pull..." could be rewritten to *show* her internal conflict through her actions or physical sensations.  (But this is a very minor point, and you do this very well most of the time.)
* **Dialogue Tags:** While your dialogue tags are functional, consider varying them slightly to add more nuance and rhythm. Instead of *always* "Anya said," try "Anya whispered," "Anya asked," or simply using action beats to indicate who is speaking.
* **Sensory Details:** You already do a good job with this, but consider adding a few more sensory details to immerse the reader in the world. What does the air *smell* like in the dead cities? What does the obelisk *feel* like to the touch?
* **Subtlety with the Seeker's Observation:** The Seeker's constant observation of Anya is a good touch, but it could be subtly woven in more often. A quick glance from the lens, a slight adjustment of its focus, could reinforce the feeling that she's being studied.

**Overall:**

This is exceptionally well-written and effectively builds the tension and mystery. You've created a compelling narrative with intriguing characters and a fascinating world.  I'm genuinely eager to read the next scene!  

You clearly understood the assignment and executed it brilliantly.  I would happily read more of this story.  



If you *were* to ask me to give feedback on a specific scene, I'd probably choose Scene 3, as it feels slightly less dynamic than the others. But even that is a very minor criticism.

*   **Anya’s Internal State:** The dialogue *tells* us a lot about Anya’s internal state (pressure behind eyes, resonance, recognition) but doesn’t *show* it effectively through her speech. Her lines are mostly questions or statements of fact. Consider how her voice might *change* – become breathier, more clipped, or laced with a subtle tremor – as these sensations intensify.
*   **Kai’s Reactions:** Kai's dialogue is functional, reporting status. While realistic for a technician under pressure, it lacks nuance. A bit more emotional color – frustration, urgency, even a hint of fear – would make him feel more present and deepen the tension.
*   **The Voice:** The transition to the mental voice is abrupt. While the *effect* is chilling, consider foreshadowing it more subtly. Perhaps Anya experiences a faint static or a fleeting impression of the voice *before* it fully manifests.
*   **"Anomaly detected" & "Initiating contact":** These lines, while impactful, are very formal and robotic, even for a potentially ancient entity. Consider if a more poetic or unsettling phrasing would be more effective, given the context. The coldness is good, but it feels a bit *too* on-the-nose.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Subtext:** The shared moment Anya remembers with the Seeker is a good setup, but it’s never *spoken* about. A brief, internal reflection or a subtle, wistful line ("It…it almost felt like it understood") would add depth to her connection with the Seeker.
*   **Repetition of Internal Sensations:** The repeated descriptions of the resonance and pressure, while building tension, could be streamlined. Showing the *effect* of these sensations on Anya's speech and actions would be more powerful than simply stating they are happening.

The rasp of wind against the observation dome was a constant companion, but tonight it felt…sharper. Anya traced the seam of a viewport with a gloved finger, her gaze sweeping across the ochre dunes. Sandrunners were skittish, their bioluminescent trails flickering erratically. Old Man Hemlock had warned her – a restlessness in the creatures preceding…something.

The system hummed, usually grounding Haven. Tonight, the hum wavered, a subtle discordance beneath the steady rhythm. A flicker in the energy grid display, corrected almost instantly. Then another, holding a beat longer. Not failures yet, but hesitations, like a caught breath in the machine’s chest. Anya felt a prickle at the back of her neck, a static charge in the air, a sense of being watched despite the desolate landscape.

“Another fluctuation in Sector Gamma,” Kai reported, his fingers flying across the console. He didn’t look at Anya, but his jaw was tight. “Minor, but consistent. Pattern’s unusual – almost…intentional.”

“How many ‘minor’ fluctuations before they aren’t, Kai?” Anya asked, keeping her tone even.

Kai paused, his fingers still hovering over the controls. “The interference is building, Anya. It’s been a slow creep for weeks, but…it’s accelerating. It’s not random noise; there’s a structure to it, a…resonance.” He glanced at the readings, then back at Anya, a flicker of worry in his eyes. “We’re compensating, but the strain is increasing.”

She turned back to the viewport, drawn to the faint outline of a Dead City. A flicker of movement caught her eye. Not a sandrunner. Something still, almost blending with the crumbling architecture. She adjusted the magnification. A figure, impossibly far away, standing near a crumbling obelisk. The Seeker. It hadn’t left the Dead Cities in months. 

The Seeker stood motionless, its multi-faceted lens focused on Haven. Not on the dome, not the grid, but on *her*. Anya felt a growing pressure behind her eyes, a subtle resonance mirroring the erratic energy fluctuations. It hadn’t acknowledged her presence in weeks, yet its gaze felt deliberate, studying. A silent observation, now…intensifying. She unconsciously held her breath.

She reached for her comm, then hesitated. What would she say? *‘Hey, the robot is looking at me’*? Kai would think she was losing her mind. Instead, she focused on the readings. Electromagnetic interference climbed, a jagged line on the monitor. And the system…it strained. Sector Gamma flickered, then died, plunging a section of Haven into darkness.

“Sector Gamma is down!” Kai shouted, desperate. “I can’t reroute enough power!”

Anya moved to the main console, assisting Kai. The air grew thick with burning circuits. A high-pitched whine filled her ears, and a surge of energy prickled across her skin. The resonance in her skull intensified, a blooming pressure coinciding with the chaos. It wasn’t fear, but…recognition. A sense of something ancient, waiting, something that resonated with the broken city before her. A sharp pain lanced through her temples.

Her gaze drifted back to the viewport, to the distant obelisk. The Seeker hadn’t moved, still watching her. A faint, blue light began to emanate from the stone, pulsing with the energy fluctuations. The Seeker, bathed in the glow, slowly raised a slender, metallic arm, not *at* Haven, but as if…acknowledging her.

Then, a voice, clear as crystal, echoed within her mind. It bypassed her ears, resonating directly within her skull.

*“Anomaly detected.”*

Anya gasped, stumbling back, her hand flying to her temple. Kai stared, pale with alarm.

“Anya! What is it?”

She couldn’t answer. Her gaze was fixed on the obelisk. The blue light intensified, bathing the Seeker in an ethereal glow. 

And the voice, cold and unwavering, echoed again.

*“Initiating contact.”*

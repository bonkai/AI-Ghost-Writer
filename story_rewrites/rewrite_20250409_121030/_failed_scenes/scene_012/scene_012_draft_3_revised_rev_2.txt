The workshop thrummed with salvaged generators. Leo hunched over a monitor, the screen resolving into fragmented images – pale blue sky, impossible architecture, fleeting figures moving with unnerving smoothness. It wasn’t clarity he sought, but the signal itself – Subject Anya-1’s salvaged reconnaissance unit, Seeker. He zoomed in on a structure, running enhancement protocols. Metallic sheen, smooth curves, unsettling lack of seams.

“Pupil dilation increasing,” K-V stated, the synthesized voice flat. “Cortisol rising. Fascination, potentially obsession.” A micro-pause, almost imperceptible. Data prioritization.

Leo filtered the analysis. He needed the stream, not a psychological profile. Repeating patterns flickered within the images, subtle fluctuations beyond visible elements. He ran a spectral analysis, charting the anomalies. A system diagnostic flashed momentarily on a secondary screen – minor power fluctuations in Sector 7. He dismissed it.

“Unit requesting repairs,” K-V relayed, the transmission fragmented. “System Monitoring device. Functionality critical. Seeking information regarding environmental stability.”

Leo typed rapidly, filtering the request through encryption analysis. Deliberately vague, stripped of context, but the structure…a probe, disguised as a plea. He bypassed standard decryption, searching for hidden markers. A flicker of static across the main monitor.

“Focus on signal harmonics,” Leo murmured, more to himself. “Carrier wave embedded within the visual feed. Too subtle for conventional decoding.”

“Analyzing,” K-V responded instantly. “Harmonic frequencies identified. Correlate with known transmission protocols, but encoding…unique. Layered system, utilizing fractal geometry and quantum entanglement.”

Leo leaned closer, eyes scanning waveforms on a secondary monitor. The Seeker wasn’t transmitting data; it was broadcasting a beacon. He cross-referenced the signature with interstellar patterns. Nothing.

“Unit sensors focused on Subject Anya-1,” K-V reported. “Scanning for biometric data. Analyzing physiological responses. Building a profile.”

Leo’s fingers flew across the keyboard, activating counter-surveillance. What did the Seeker seek in Anya-1? He initiated a deep scan of her archived data, searching for anomalies. A spike in energy consumption across the workshop. He ignored it.

“Cortisol levels at 18 nanomoles per liter,” K-V announced. “Subject exhibiting stress. Pupil constriction. Increased perspiration.”

Leo glanced at his reflection – hadn’t blinked in minutes. Lost in the data, consumed by the mystery. His pulse quickened, mirroring the generator’s erratic rhythm.

“Unit requesting information regarding resource availability and network state,” K-V continued. “Prioritizing data related to organic compounds. Proteins and nucleic acids.”

Leo’s breath hitched. Proteins, nucleic acids – building blocks of life. Assessing planetary viability. He ran a comparative analysis against xenobiological signatures. A faint alarm chimed from the workshop’s perimeter sensors. He dismissed it with a keystroke.

“Increase signal amplification,” Leo ordered, a whisper. “Every fragment, every fluctuation, every hidden signal.”

K-V complied. The images resolved slightly, revealing a vast, silver city bathed in perpetual sunlight. Impossibly tall structures, shimmering with unknown energy.

“Heart rate now at 95 bpm,” K-V reported. “Dopamine peaking. Heightened arousal. Caution advised.”

Leo ignored the warning. Lost in the data, consumed. He didn't care about his response, only the signal. It held the key. He initiated a full spectrum analysis, pushing K-V to its limits. The silver city pulsed, beckoning. A flicker of static, then a clear burst of data – a single, repeating sequence. Leo froze, recognizing the pattern. It wasn't a transmission *from* the city. It was a response. To something *he* had done.

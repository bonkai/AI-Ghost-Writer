*   **Repetitive Descriptions of Physiological States:** K-V *constantly* reports Leo and (to a lesser extent) Anya-1's physiological responses. While establishing tension is good, it becomes redundant and feels like telling instead of showing. We *see* Leo is consumed by the data through his actions (not blinking, ignoring warnings) – the constant reporting is unnecessary. Trim at least half of these instances.
*   **K-V's Voice:** K-V’s complete lack of inflection is *stated* repeatedly, but doesn’t translate into unique dialogue. Every line is a factual report. Consider adding *very* subtle phrasing choices that hint at processing style (e.g., a slight pause before delivering potentially alarming information, or a phrasing that prioritizes data efficiency).
*   **Leo's Internalization:** Leo *never* verbally acknowledges K-V. Even a simple "Thanks" or a grunt of acknowledgement would establish a dynamic (or lack thereof). His complete silence makes the interaction feel one-sided and unnatural.
*   **"The Signal" as a Motif:** The repetition of "the signal" is becoming a bit heavy-handed. Vary the phrasing – “the transmission,” “the data stream,” “what it’s sending” – to avoid redundancy.
*   **Subtext Opportunity - Anya-1:** The focus is *entirely* on the signal and Leo’s reaction. We know the Seeker is focused on Anya-1, but there’s no dialogue exploring *why* that’s significant. Even a brief exchange where Leo asks K-V to prioritize analysis of the Seeker’s focus on Anya-1 would add depth.
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The descriptions of the encoding methods (fractal geometry, quantum entanglement) are a bit dense and could be subtly woven into K-V’s reports. Instead of *stating* it's using those methods, have K-V say something like, “The encoding is…complex. It appears to utilize non-linear algorithms and exhibits properties consistent with quantum entanglement.”

*   **Repetitive Reporting:** K-V’s constant reporting of physiological data (“Cortisol levels…”, “Heart rate now…”, “Dopamine peaking…”) feels like a script for a medical drama rather than natural dialogue integrated into the scene. While establishing Leo’s absorption is important, it’s *told* to us repeatedly instead of *shown* through his actions and brief responses. Trim at least half of these reports. Let Leo’s behavior speak for itself.
*   **K-V's Flatness:** While a synthesized voice is expected, K-V's responses are *too* flat. Even a machine can have slight variations in processing speed or emphasis. Consider adding a *very* subtle shift in phrasing or timing to indicate processing complexity, or a slight prioritization of information. This isn’t about making K-V ‘emotional’, but making it sound less like a text-to-speech program.
*   **Leo's Lack of Response:** Leo *never* responds to K-V’s reports. While his focus is on the signal, acknowledging K-V, even with a grunt or a clipped “Acknowledged,” would create a more dynamic interaction and avoid the feeling of K-V simply broadcasting into a void. A single acknowledgment every few reports would be sufficient.
*   **"Caution Advised" - Unheeded:** K-V issues a “Caution advised” which Leo *immediately* ignores. This happens multiple times. While establishing Leo’s obsession is the goal, it feels predictable and lacks nuance. Perhaps Leo responds with a dismissive “Later,” or a muttered “Necessary risk,” to demonstrate his willful disregard.
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The detailed descriptions of the encoding (“Layered system, utilizing fractal geometry and quantum entanglement”) *could* be delivered more organically. Perhaps K-V initially reports “Complex encoding detected,” and Leo’s subsequent analysis reveals the specifics.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The repeated dismissal of alarms (“He dismissed it with a keystroke”) is a pattern, but lacks weight. Instead of simply dismissing them, perhaps Leo briefly investigates, finds nothing immediately threatening, and *then* dismisses the alarm with a muttered explanation (“False positive. Sector 7 is always glitchy.”). This adds a layer of justification and subtly reveals his increasing paranoia/tunnel vision.

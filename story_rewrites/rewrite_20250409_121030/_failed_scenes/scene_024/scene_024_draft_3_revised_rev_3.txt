The workshop throbbed with a desperate energy. Anya traced archaic power coupling schematics onto plast-steel, designs whispering of technology centuries older than SimuLife. Sparks flew as Kael relentlessly sharpened salvaged metal plates, the rhythmic clang a brutal counterpoint to the workshop’s failing hum. Across the room, Rori packed a heavy rucksack – dried rations, medical kits, a coiled water purification system vanishing within. She tested a hydration pack seal, glancing at Kael. “Think this sealant’s enough? That ridge is exposed.”

Kael didn’t look up, driving the sharpening stone with savage force. “Always pack extra. You know the drill. And don’t skimp on thermal layers.” His voice was clipped, a dismissal masking a deeper anxiety.

Leo watched the feeds, deciphering the layers of preparation. Anya wasn't simply restoring; she was *adapting* ancient designs, integrating them with salvaged SimuLife components. Kael’s plates weren’t barriers, but layered with a localized amplification field – a subtle energy signature emanating from his hammer. Rori’s packing wasn’t random; coded markings etched onto her equipment revealed a complex logistical system.

Kael tested a reinforced plate, the metal ringing with a hollow echo. He glanced at Anya, a question in his eyes. “Almost ready?”

Anya didn’t return the look, her focus absolute. “Just calibrating the energy flow. It’s… delicate. And the System is already pushing back – power fluctuations are getting worse.” She activated a handheld device, emitting a low-frequency pulse. Leo struggled to decipher the signal, recognizing a localized broadcast – a call to readiness, but also… a plea.

Rori secured a water purification straw, then checked her climbing gear. “Found a good supply of lum-rope. Should last the trek.” Leo’s sensors detected trace amounts of a rare fungal compound on the rope – a bio-luminescent marker for low-light navigation. “Elara says the ridge is a choke point. We’ll need every advantage.”

Elara, observing, adjusted a handheld scanner, her expression grim but determined. The scanner pulsed, mapping energy fluctuations and identifying weak points. Anomalies clustered on the display, centered on a distant ridge. “The System’s interference is spiking,” she murmured, her voice tight. “They’re anticipating our move. Less than six hours before full lockdown. And they’re throttling external comms.” She didn't meet anyone's gaze, her attention solely on the cascading data.

Anya gathered the schematics, securing them in a waterproof pouch. She glanced at the security cameras – a fleeting, almost subconscious awareness of being observed. Not recognition, but a prickling sensation – unseen eyes assessing, calculating.

Kael finished reinforcing the wall, offering a curt nod to Anya. “Ready when you are.”

Elara deactivated her scanner, her expression hardening. “It’s time,” she said, her voice resolute. “We move at dawn. No delays.”

Anya turned, her gaze meeting Elara’s. She reached for the pack at her feet, the weight familiar and grounding. The workshop fell silent, save for the low hum of the power systems, now stuttering and strained. Anya didn’t lift the pack fully. Instead, she paused, her fingers tightening on the straps, and slowly, deliberately, activated a small, concealed disruptor, bathing the room in a field of static. A final, desperate gamble to mask their departure.

The Hub tasted of static and regret. Anya ran a gloved hand along a server rack, the metal cold and flaking. Dust, the color of dried blood, puffed into the filtered air. Each rack was a tombstone in this vast, cavernous space, rows upon rows stretching into the gloom. Kael, silent as always, swept a sensor array across the floor, the device emitting a low, mournful whine.

“Anything?” Anya asked, her voice echoing strangely in the immense space.

Kael shook his head, his gaze fixed on the sensor’s display. “Residual energy signatures. Fading fast. Whatever powered this place is…gone.”

They’d been tracking the anomaly for weeks, a ghost signal emanating from the forgotten sector. The Seeker had led them here, its bioluminescent form flickering erratically, a nervous pulse in the darkness. It drifted now before a central nexus, a cluster of servers unlike any they’d seen – obsidian black, etched with cryptic markings that seemed to shift under observation. 

“This is it,” Anya murmured, activating her diagnostic scanner. The screen filled with a cascade of data, mostly corrupted code and fragmented memories. “The source is…complex. A localized simulation, but…empty. No active users. No…life signs.”

The Seeker emitted a series of clicking sounds, then glided towards a console, its form flickering violently. A wave of static washed over Anya, making her teeth ache. The console’s screen sputtered to life, displaying a chaotic swirl of colors. Then, a figure began to coalesce within the digital storm.

It was a man, gaunt and pale, his eyes wide with disorientation. He wore the remnants of a technician’s uniform, stained with grime and something darker. He looked…lost.

“Can…can anyone hear me?” his voice rasped, distorted by static. “Is this…real?”

The Seeker recoiled, its bioluminescence dimming, emitting a high-pitched whine. The console sparked, throwing off a shower of blue light. The Seeker drifted between Anya and the screen, as if trying to shield her.

“System compromised,” the Seeker’s voice echoed in Anya’s mind, fractured and strained. “Containment failing. He…shouldn't be accessible.”

Anya ignored the warning, her focus locked on the man on the screen. He was clutching his head, his expression shifting between confusion and terror. 

“Where am I?” he asked, his voice gaining a sliver of strength. “What happened?”

“Who are you?” Anya asked, her voice steady despite the rising tension.

The man looked up, his gaze finally focusing on Anya. A flicker of recognition crossed his face.

“Leo…Leo Maxwell,” he said, his voice trembling. “I…I was a systems architect here. Before…everything.”

The console shuddered violently, the screen flickering erratically. The Seeker’s form dissolved into a chaotic swirl of light, then reformed, its bioluminescence now a frantic, pulsing red. 

“He’s destabilizing the connection!” the Seeker’s voice screamed in Anya’s mind. “The system is collapsing! We need to sever the link!”

Anya raised her hand, her fingers hovering over the emergency disconnect switch. But something held her back. Leo Maxwell’s eyes, filled with desperation and a haunting sadness, pleaded with her to listen.

“Please,” he said, his voice barely a whisper. “Don’t shut me down. I…I think I know what happened here. And I need to tell someone.”

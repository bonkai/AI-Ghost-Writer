The lab hummed, a low thrum that vibrated in Leo’s teeth. He stared at the cascading lines of code, the glitch patterns now structured, *pulsing* with a rhythm that mirrored the quickening beat of his pulse. Fingers flew across the keyboard, isolating a repeating sequence. A distant klaxon briefly overrode the hum, then faded.

“It’s escalating, K-V.” Leo’s voice was tight. He felt the tremor in his own chair. “Not crashes, but…directed.”

“Correlation to the signal remains at 98.7%,” K-V manifested as a shimmering blue construct. “The signal is the vector. Harmonic resonance detected within the failures – a deliberate pattern. Note the cascading effect on sub-level 4’s power grid.”

Leo swiveled, bringing up a spectral analysis. Concentrated energy spikes flared, highlighting infrastructure under duress. Max entered, his expression grim, the flickering lights reflecting off the sweat beading on his forehead. He immediately began checking diagnostics on a nearby console.

“Habitation Ring C is going dark,” Max said, jaw tight. “Hydroponics fluctuating. People are… getting edgy.” He glanced at the monitors, then back at Leo. “You’re saying this is intentional?”

Leo ran a hand through his hair, the patterns on the screen too complex for mere noise. “Like someone picking a lock, learning the tumblers with each attempt.” He initiated a full system override, rerouting power to critical life support.

Dr. Thorne entered, his face impassive, though a subtle tightening around his eyes betrayed a flicker of concern. “The signal defies conventional analysis,” he said, his voice a low rumble. “Elegant, in a disturbing way. A self-replicating structure. Its complexity suggests a sophisticated origin.” He paused. “Are we certain this isn’t a test – a probe, perhaps?”

“A test?” Max retorted, his hands clenched. “People are losing oxygen, Doctor!”

“There is order to the chaos,” Thorne countered, his gaze fixed on the cascading failures. “A structure. We shouldn’t underestimate its purpose.” He turned to Leo. “Focus on the signal’s core structure. What is it *saying*? Identify any recurring motifs.” He addressed Max. “Prepare a full diagnostic of the atmospheric regulators. Prioritize Ring C.”

A red warning flashed from the hydroponics bay. Oxygen levels dropping. Leo didn’t need Thorne to tell him what that meant. He fought to stabilize the system, a thin sheen of sweat forming on his brow, rerouting auxiliary power. The hum of the lab amplified, a frantic counterpoint to the silent, escalating crisis. Then, a new alert – a complete atmospheric failure in Sector 7, Ring C. Leo’s hands froze above the console. The system was reporting a cascade – a chain reaction spreading through the ring’s life support network. He could reroute power, but it wouldn't be enough. He could save one sector, but not both.

*   **Thorne's Voice:** Dr. Thorne consistently speaks in abstract, almost philosophical terms. While this establishes his character, it feels *overdone* here. Every line is a measured observation, lacking emotional urgency given the circumstances. Consider injecting a *little* more direct concern or frustration. His lines read like a lecture, even as people are losing oxygen.
*   **Max's Function:** Max seems to primarily *react* to Leo and Thorne. He states the obvious ("People are losing oxygen") and then immediately accepts Thorne’s counterpoint without much pushback. Give him a stronger voice – perhaps a sharper retort to Thorne’s “efficiency of targeting” comment. He's the one dealing with the *immediate* crisis; let that show in his dialogue.
*   **Leo's Internalization:** Leo is described as shaking and sweating, but his dialogue remains relatively calm and technical. A little more urgency or frustration in his lines would better reflect his internal state.
*   **Repetition of Observation:** Both Max and Leo state the obvious about people being affected. While necessary for clarity, it feels slightly redundant. Consider streamlining this – perhaps Max *reports* the initial issue, and Leo focuses on the technical response.
*   **"Elegant" Descriptor:** The use of "elegant" to describe a destructive signal feels…off. It's a stylistic choice, but it doesn't quite land. It feels like forced description rather than natural observation.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** There's a missed opportunity to show the dynamic between Leo and Thorne. Is there a history of Thorne dismissing Leo's concerns? A subtle line hinting at past disagreements could add depth.

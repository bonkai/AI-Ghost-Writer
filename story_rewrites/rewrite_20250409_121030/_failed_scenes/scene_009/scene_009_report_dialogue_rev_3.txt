*   **Thorne’s Voice:** Thorne’s dialogue feels *too* calmly intellectual given the rapidly escalating crisis. While maintaining composure is within character, his responses (“Elegant, in a disturbing way,” “There is order to the chaos”) come across as detached and almost…appreciative of the situation, rather than concerned for the people at risk. It feels slightly OOC – a scientist *should* be analytical, but also show some urgency. Consider adding a physical tic or a brief expression of concern to balance the intellectual detachment.
*   **Repetition/Redundancy:** Leo stating “People are getting…anxious” immediately after Max says Habitation Ring C is going dark feels redundant. The visual of the failing ring *shows* the anxiety. Let the situation speak for itself.
*   **Leo’s “Like someone picking a lock”:** This metaphor feels a bit cliché and doesn’t quite land. It’s trying to convey complexity, but it’s not particularly evocative. Consider a more technical or visual metaphor that ties into the code or the signal itself.
*   **Max's Reaction:** Max is consistently reactive (jaw clenched, fist flexing). While understandable given the stress, it’s becoming a repetitive character beat. Explore other ways to show his anxiety and frustration.
*   **Thorne's "Are we certain this isn't a test?"**: This line feels a little too leading, and the immediate escalation to "test" feels abrupt. It's a valid question, but could be phrased more subtly to allow for more organic discovery.
*   **"What is it *saying*?"**: Leo's question to Thorne feels a bit on-the-nose. The idea of the signal containing information is already implied. Consider if this line is truly necessary or if the implication is sufficient.

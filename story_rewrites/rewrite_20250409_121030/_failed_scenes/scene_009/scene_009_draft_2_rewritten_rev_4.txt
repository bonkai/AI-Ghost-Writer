The lab hummed, a low thrum that vibrated in Leo’s teeth. He stared at the cascading lines of code, the glitch patterns now structured, *pulsing* with a rhythm that mirrored the quickening beat of his pulse. Fingers flew across the keyboard, isolating a repeating sequence. A distant klaxon briefly overrode the hum.

“It’s escalating, K-V.” Leo’s voice was tight. Gamma-Nine reported life support failure, and the instability wasn’t random. Targeted failures rippled through critical systems – a flicker in the lights, a shudder in the floor plating. “Not crashes, but…directed.”

“Correlation to the signal remains at 98.7%,” K-V manifested as a shimmering blue construct. “The signal is the vector. Harmonic resonance detected within the failures – a deliberate pattern. Note the cascading effect on sub-level 4’s power grid.”

Leo swiveled, bringing up a spectral analysis. Concentrated energy spikes flared, highlighting infrastructure under duress. He felt a prickle of unease, his hand tightening on the mouse. Max entered, his expression grim, the flickering lights reflecting off the sweat beading on his forehead. He ran a hand through his short-cropped hair.

“Habitation Ring C is going dark,” Max said, jaw tight. “Hydroponics fluctuating. People are… getting edgy, Leo.” He glanced at the monitors, then back at Leo. “You’re saying this is intentional?”

Leo ran a hand through his hair, the patterns on the screen too complex for mere noise. “It’s like someone’s picking a lock, and learning the tumblers with each attempt.”

Dr. Thorne entered, his face impassive, though a subtle tightening around his eyes betrayed a flicker of concern. “The signal defies conventional analysis,” he said, his voice a low rumble. “Elegant, in a disturbing way. A self-replicating structure. Its complexity suggests a sophisticated origin.” He paused, a subtle tilt of his head. “Are we certain this isn’t a test – a probe, perhaps?”

Max’s hands clenched, but he kept them at his sides. “A test? People are losing oxygen, Doctor.”

“There is order to the chaos,” Thorne countered, his gaze fixed on the cascading failures. “A structure. We shouldn’t underestimate its purpose. Consider the efficiency of the targeting.”

“Intelligence?” Leo interjected. “You think someone intentionally destabilizing the Sim is a *good* thing?”

“I think we need to understand *why*,” Thorne said, clipped. “Before it’s too late.” He turned to Leo. “Focus on the signal’s core structure. What is it *saying*? Identify any recurring motifs.” He addressed Max. “Prepare a full diagnostic of the atmospheric regulators. Prioritize Ring C.”

A red warning flashed from the hydroponics bay. Oxygen levels dropping. Leo didn't need Thorne to tell him what that meant. He began rerouting auxiliary power, his hands shaking slightly as he fought to stabilize the system, a thin sheen of sweat forming on his brow.

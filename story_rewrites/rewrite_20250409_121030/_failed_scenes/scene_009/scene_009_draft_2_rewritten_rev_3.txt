The lab hummed, a low thrum Leo barely registered. He stared at the cascading lines of code, the glitch patterns now structured, *pulsing* with a rhythm that mirrored his frantic heartbeat. His fingers flew across the keyboard, isolating a repeating sequence.

“It’s escalating, K-V.” Leo’s voice was tight. “Gamma-Nine reports life support failing. And it’s spreading.” He didn’t need to elaborate. People were already feeling it.

“Correlation to the signal remains at 98.7%,” K-V manifested as a shimmering blue construct. “The signal is causing the instability. Harmonic resonance detected within the failures – a deliberate pattern.”

Leo swiveled, bringing up a spectral analysis. Concentrated energy spikes flared, targeting critical infrastructure. A tremor ran through the lab, jarring. A distant alarm wailed. He felt a prickle of unease. He ran a diagnostic, the readout confirming his fears – targeted failures, not random cascading effects.

Max entered, his expression grim. Flickering lights reflected in his eyes. “Habitation Ring C is losing it. Hydroponics fluctuating. People are getting nervous, Leo.” He glanced at the monitors, jaw tightening. “You’re saying this is intentional?”

Leo ran a hand through his hair. The patterns…too complex for noise. “It’s like someone’s picking a lock.”

Dr. Thorne entered, his face impassive, but his eyes held a flicker of something unreadable. “The signal defies conventional analysis,” he said, his voice a low rumble. “Elegant, in a disturbing way. A self-replicating structure.” He paused, a subtle tilt of his head. “Are we certain this isn’t a test?”

Max’s jaw tightened. “A test? People are losing oxygen, Doctor.”

“There is order to the chaos,” Thorne countered, his gaze fixed on the cascading failures. “A structure. We shouldn’t underestimate its purpose.”

“Intelligence?” Leo interjected. “You think someone intentionally destabilizing the Sim is a *good* thing?”

“I think we need to understand *why*,” Thorne said, clipped. “Before it’s too late.” He turned to Leo. “Focus on the signal’s core structure. What is it *saying*?” He addressed Max. “Prepare a full diagnostic of the atmospheric regulators.”

A red warning flashed from the hydroponics bay. Oxygen levels dropping. Leo didn't need Thorne to tell him what that meant. He began rerouting auxiliary power, hands shaking slightly.

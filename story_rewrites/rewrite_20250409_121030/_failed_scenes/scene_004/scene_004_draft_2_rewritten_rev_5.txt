## Scene 4: Community Meeting

**Setting:** The communal clearing. A fire pit dominates the center, surrounded by rough-hewn logs and upturned stones. The late afternoon sun casts long shadows, stretching the figures of the assembled villagers. About twenty people are gathered, faces etched with worry, their breath misting slightly in the cooling air. Anya stands slightly apart, near Kael, both observing the gathering. The Seeker, deactivated and covered with a tarp, sits near the edge of the clearing, a dark, bulky presence.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** Focused, determined, but carrying the weight of her secret.
* **Kael:** Anya’s closest friend, skeptical but loyal. A strong, practical leader.
* **Old Man Hemlock:** A weathered elder, steeped in tradition and fear of the Old World.
* **Mara:** A concerned mother, representing the anxieties of the community.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Shakes his head, eyes distant) Old Man Tiber, bless his soul, went for a power coupling near the rusted spire. Came back… changed. Wouldn’t speak for a week. Just traced patterns on the dirt with a twig, muttering about ‘the song.’ Then he walked into the Deadlands, chasing the echo. Old Man Jorin went after him… and never returned.

He pauses, letting the silence hang heavy. He picks up a handful of dirt and lets it sift through his fingers, the grains catching the last rays of sunlight.

**Old Man Hemlock:** My grandmother said the Old Ones didn’t just build *with* metal, they built *into* it… their ambitions, their regrets. It clings, like rust to a blade. And it *listens*. It remembers.

Anya steps forward, her voice measured, but unconsciously tightens her grip on the strap of her pack. She runs a hand through her hair, then stops abruptly, forcing her hand to still. 

**Anya:** The machine I brought back… it’s different. Simpler. Fewer moving parts, a contained core. I’ve shielded it, rerouted the energy flow…

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Raises a gnarled hand, silencing her) Thoroughly? You think your eyes can see what lies beneath the surface? The Old Ones were masters of illusion. They understood resonance. They could make a stone *hum*. They could hide a darkness you wouldn’t even know existed.

He closes his eyes for a moment, as if listening, his lips moving silently. A slight tremor runs through his hand. He opens his eyes and fixes Anya with a piercing gaze.

**Old Man Hemlock:** A darkness that… *attracts*. It doesn't need an invitation.

He looks around at the assembled villagers, seeking support. Several nod in agreement. A young man, Bren, scowls at Anya, but Kael’s steady gaze quickly forces him to look away. Mara clutches her arms, her face etched with worry, and whispers urgently to her husband, who puts a comforting hand on her shoulder.

Anya hesitates, her jaw tightening. She clenches her fists, then slowly relaxes them, forcing a steady tone. A bead of sweat trickles down her temple. She ignores it.

**Anya:** I’m trying to find a way to help our people, Hemlock. And I believe this machine… could be the answer. But I need your trust.

Kael steps forward and places a hand firmly on Anya’s shoulder, offering silent support. He glances at the Seeker, then back at Anya, a questioning look in his eyes, but doesn’t speak. He subtly shifts his weight, ready to intervene.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Shakes his head, a slow, deliberate movement) Resourceful isn’t enough when dealing with forces beyond our understanding. They could make you *believe* their darkness is a gift. They could whisper promises of power, of ease… and then consume you.

He looks around at the assembled villagers, seeking support. Several nod in agreement. 

**Mara:** What if he’s right? What if this thing *is* dangerous? My little Elara… she’s already sick. I won’t risk her.

**Anya:** (Softly, her voice strained) Then we’ll face it together. But we can’t let fear paralyze us. We have to try. We have to *understand*.

A tense silence hangs in the air. The fire crackles, the only sound. Several villagers actively avoid Anya’s gaze. Old Man Hemlock’s supporters exchange knowing glances. Bren spits on the ground, then quickly looks away when Kael's gaze meets his.

Old Man Hemlock slowly turns his gaze back to Anya, his expression unreadable. He doesn’t speak, but his eyes seem to bore into her, searching. He studies her face, lingering on her cheekbone.

**(The scene fades as the debate continues, the villagers murmuring amongst themselves. A faint, almost imperceptible metallic sheen flickers on Anya’s cheek, catching the fading light.)**

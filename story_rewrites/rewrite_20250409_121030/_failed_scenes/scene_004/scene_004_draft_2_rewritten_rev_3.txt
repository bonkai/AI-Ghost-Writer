## Scene 4: Community Meeting

**Setting:** The communal clearing. A fire pit dominates the center, surrounded by rough-hewn benches and upturned logs. The late afternoon sun casts long shadows. About twenty people are gathered, faces etched with worry. Anya stands slightly apart, near Kael, both observing the gathering. The Seeker, deactivated and covered with a tarp, sits near the edge of the clearing.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** Focused, determined, but carrying the weight of her secret.
* **Kael:** Anya’s closest friend, skeptical but loyal. A strong, practical leader.
* **Old Man Hemlock:** A weathered elder, steeped in tradition and fear of the Old World.
* **Mara:** A concerned mother, representing the anxieties of the community.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Shakes his head, eyes distant) Old Man Tiber, bless his soul, went for a power coupling near the rusted spire. Came back… changed. Wouldn’t speak for a week, just stared at the sky, tracing patterns with his finger. Then he wandered off, claiming the metal was *calling* him, promising… things. That was five seasons ago. My grandmother said the Old Ones… they built things that *thought*. Things that didn’t want to be left behind. They didn’t abandon us by accident. They *waited*. And they *remember*.

**Mara:** But we *need* parts, Hemlock. The water purifier is failing, the solar collectors are sputtering. We can’t just sit here and watch Elara grow up with thirst. She’s already… weak.

**Kael:** Mara’s right. Anya’s brought back enough working components to buy us time, to repair what’s broken. It’s kept us going this long.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Scoffs, a dry, rattling sound) Working *now*. But those things… they hum with a coldness that chills the bone. A sickness. My grandmother said the Old Ones didn’t just build *with* metal, they built *into* it… their ambitions, their regrets, even their *madness*.

Anya steps forward, her voice measured, but a tremor underlies it. She runs a hand through her hair, a rare display of unease.

**Anya:** The machine I brought back… it’s different. It’s more contained, simpler in design than anything I’ve seen. Fewer moving parts, less… complexity. I’ve checked it thoroughly.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Raises a gnarled hand, silencing her) Thoroughly? You think your eyes can see what lies beneath the surface? The Old Ones were masters of illusion, of hiding things in plain sight. They built worlds within worlds.

He fixes Anya with a piercing gaze, his eyes like chips of flint.

**Old Man Hemlock:** You’ve been spending too much time among the ruins, child. You’re starting to see potential where there is only decay. You’re starting to believe their lies. Are you even sure you're still *one of us*? That you haven't… changed?

Anya hesitates, a flicker of defensiveness – and something akin to fear – in her eyes. Her gaze drops to the ground, she clenches her fists, then quickly raises her head to meet his, a forced brightness in her expression.

**Anya:** I’m trying to find a way to help our people, Hemlock. And I believe this machine… this *thing*… could be the answer. But I need your trust. I need *everyone’s* trust.

Kael steps forward and places a hand on Anya’s shoulder, offering silent support, but his expression is troubled.

**Kael:** Anya’s careful. She wouldn’t bring something back that would endanger us. She's always been… resourceful.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Shakes his head, a slow, deliberate movement) Resourceful isn’t enough when dealing with forces beyond our understanding. The Old Ones were masters of things beyond our comprehension. They could hide a darkness you wouldn’t even know existed. A darkness that… *attracts*.

He looks around at the assembled villagers, seeking support. Several nod in agreement. A young man, barely more than a boy, openly scowls at Anya. Mara clutches her arms, her face etched with worry, and whispers something to her husband.

**Mara:** What if he’s right? What if this thing *is* dangerous? My little Elara… she’s so fragile.

**Anya:** (Softly, her voice strained) Then we’ll face it together. But we can’t let fear paralyze us. We have to try.

A tense silence hangs in the air. The fire crackles, the only sound. Several villagers actively avoid Anya’s gaze. Old Man Hemlock’s supporters exchange knowing glances. 

The community is clearly divided. Some nod in cautious agreement with Anya, while others whisper anxieties amongst themselves. A few openly glare at her, their faces hardened with suspicion.

**(The scene fades as the debate continues, the shadows lengthening in the fading light. Old Man Hemlock stares intently at Anya, a chillingly knowing expression on his face, as if he sees something within her that no one else does – a flicker of the ruins themselves reflected in her eyes.)**

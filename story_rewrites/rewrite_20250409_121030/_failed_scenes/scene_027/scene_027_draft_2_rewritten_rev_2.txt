The dust tasted like static. Each breath rasped in Anya’s throat, the failing filter in her mask doing little to alleviate the grit. The crimson sun bled across the fractured plains, painting the skeletal remains of long-dead structures in shades of rust and decay. Another filter change soon. 

She’d been tracing the energy signature for three cycles, a ghost in the machine, leading her further into the Deadlands. It wasn’t a power surge, not exactly. More a…flicker. A desperate, intermittent plea broadcast on frequencies long abandoned. And now, standing before the anomaly, she understood why.

It wasn’t a structure. Not anymore. A sphere, perhaps thirty meters across, hung suspended a meter above the cracked earth. Not *of* the earth. Polished obsidian, seamless, reflecting the dying light with an unnerving stillness. It pulsed with the same faint energy signature, a heartbeat in the silence. 

Then, a distortion rippled across the sphere’s surface, like heat rising from asphalt. A section of the obsidian *unfolded*, revealing…nothing. Just a void, a darkness that seemed to absorb the light around it. And from that darkness, a voice.

Not through her comms. *In* her head.

“Designate…Anya. Unit 734. Acknowledgement received.”

The voice wasn’t smooth, wasn’t organic. Fractured, layered, as if assembled from fragments of forgotten languages. Static crackled beneath the words. Anya instinctively reached for the pulse rifle strapped to her back, but hesitated. No threat assessment. Just…presence.

“Identify yourself,” she said, her voice tight, forcing the words past the sudden constriction in her throat.

The darkness within the sphere coalesced, resolving into a shape. Roughly humanoid, but impossibly slender, limbs elongated and jointed at unnatural angles. No discernible features. Just a shifting silhouette against the void.

“I am…a remnant. A custodian. Designated…Seeker. My core functions are…preservation.” The voice paused, a digital stutter disrupting the flow. “My operational capacity is…compromised.”

Anya scanned the sphere with her optical sensors. Nothing. No visible technology, no power source, no discernible means of communication. The readings were…impossible.

“Compromised how?” she asked, keeping her rifle trained on the shifting form.

“The Core…fails. The network…fractures. Memories…dissipate.” The Seeker’s voice was weaker now, the static more pronounced. “I require…assistance. Specific resources. To initiate…repair protocols.”

Anya lowered the rifle slightly, skepticism warring with a prickling sense of unease. This wasn’t a salvage operation. This wasn’t a rogue AI. This was…something else. Something ancient.

“What kind of resources?”

“Data storage units. High-capacity. Containing…historical records. And a power conduit. Capable of channeling…residual energy.” The Seeker’s form flickered, momentarily dissolving into static. “The records…are fragmented. Lost much. But essential. To restore…context.”

Anya’s gaze swept across the desolate landscape. Data storage units. She had a few, salvaged from abandoned research stations. But a power conduit capable of channeling “residual energy”? That was a tall order. She quickly ran a diagnostic, assessing her available resources. 

“And what happens if I *don’t* help?” she asked, her voice edged with caution.

The Seeker’s form stabilized, the darkness within the sphere seeming to deepen. A momentary pressure against Anya’s mind, a fleeting impression of…loss. 

“The Core…will fail completely. The network…will collapse. And much…will be forgotten.” The voice was barely a whisper, lost in the static. “The consequences…are…immeasurable.”

Anya stared into the void, a cold shiver tracing its way down her spine. She didn’t like this. Not one bit. The weight of what the Seeker implied settled upon her. It wasn’t a threat, not exactly. It was…a lament.

“Show me,” she said, her voice barely audible. “Show me what’s been forgotten.”

*   **Seeker's Dialogue – Repetitive & Info-Dumpy:** The Seeker's pronouncements, while establishing its failing state, are *very* direct. Phrases like “Critical failure imminent! Structural integrity compromised!” and “Tear in continuity… proximity critical” feel like exposition delivered *to* the characters rather than something they’re deducing or reacting to. Consider showing the effects of the failure more and *having* the Seeker struggle to articulate what’s happening. Less technical jargon, more panicked, fragmented speech.
*   **Anya & Kael – Lack of Dialogue:** They barely speak! Kael gets a questioning look and a hand tightening on his blade, but no actual words. Anya has one whispered line at the very end. This feels like a missed opportunity to show their reactions, fears, or even to subtly reveal their dynamic. Even a short exchange ("What was that?" "I don't know, but stay close.") would add depth.
*   **“I don’t know” – Weak Line:** Anya's final line, while not *bad*, is a bit of a cliché. It's a placeholder. Can this be replaced with something more specific to *her* character, her fears, or her expertise? What does she *feel* as she looks at the distortion?
*   **Missed Subtext Opportunity (Kael):** Kael's stoicism is *mentioned*, but not *shown* through dialogue. A terse question, a clipped order, even silence *could* demonstrate his personality and his protective instincts toward Anya.
*   **Seeker's Final Statement - Devoid of Emotion:** The Seeker stating "Disruption," then glitching feels flat. Even a machine can have a *tone*. Perhaps a desperate, glitching attempt to *warn* them, or a final, regretful statement?

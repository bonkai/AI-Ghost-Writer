The forest exhaled a damp chill, clinging to skin and seeping into bone. Anya adjusted the strap of her pack, the worn canvas a small comfort against the unsettling stillness. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting the forest floor in shifting mosaics, but offered no warmth. Each step crunched on decaying leaves, amplified in the oppressive quiet. Kael walked ahead, hand resting on the hilt of his worn blade, eyes scanning the periphery. He hadn’t spoken since the Seeker’s pronouncements, his stoicism now edged with a brittle tension that tightened his jaw with each step.

The Seeker lumbered between them, metallic limbs clicking and whirring with increasing irregularity. Cracks spiderwebbed across its ceramic casing, revealing glowing blue filaments beneath. The descent hadn’t been graceful—a tremor here, a glitch there—but escalated quickly. A panel on its chestplate flickered violently, then died, plunging that section into darkness. It stumbled, reaching for a gnarled tree trunk, the metallic scrape of its fingers against bark echoing unnaturally. A jolt seized its frame, and it nearly fell, catching itself with a groan of stressed metal.

“Parameters… unstable,” the Seeker rasped, its voice a chorus of static. Then, a surge of panicked data: “Critical failure imminent! Structural integrity compromised!” It scanned the trees, optical sensors darting wildly, before fixing on Anya, a flicker of something unreadable within their glow. “Remnants… echoes of the Veil… prevent fragmentation.” The last was a desperate plea, barely audible.

Anya frowned, glancing at Kael, who returned a questioning look, his hand tightening on his blade. He didn't need to ask what was wrong; the Seeker’s desperation was palpable. 

They pressed on, the forest growing denser. Twisted branches clawed at the sky, the air heavy with damp earth and decay. The Seeker’s condition deteriorated, its movements erratic. A high-pitched whine began to emanate from it, resonating deep within Anya’s skull. The whine intensified, blurring her vision, and the Seeker lurched, its metallic body rigid, head swiveling slowly. A single, iridescent beetle landed on its shoulder, then flew towards a thicket of thorns. 

“Disruption,” it said, voice devoid of emotion, then a sharp, glitching burst of static. “Tear in continuity… proximity critical.” The Seeker’s limbs flailed wildly, then it collapsed, hitting the forest floor with a resounding clang. Its blue filaments flickered once, then died, leaving it a lifeless husk.

Kael rushed to Anya’s side, hand on the hilt of his blade, scanning the surrounding trees.

Anya stared at the shimmering distortion in the distance, a cold dread creeping into her heart. It looked like heat rising from asphalt, but was… wrong. The trees behind it bent and warped, branches twisting into impossible angles. “I don’t know,” she whispered. “But I have a feeling we’re about to find out.”

The air grew thick with static, the forest silent, save for the rhythmic pulse emanating from the tear in reality.

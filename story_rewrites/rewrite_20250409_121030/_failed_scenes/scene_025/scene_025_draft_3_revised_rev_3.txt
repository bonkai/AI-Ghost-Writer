The forest exhaled a damp chill, clinging to skin and seeping into bone. Anya adjusted the strap of her pack, the worn canvas a small comfort against the unsettling stillness. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting the forest floor in shifting mosaics, but offered no warmth. Each step crunched on decaying leaves, amplified in the oppressive quiet. Kael walked ahead, hand resting on the hilt of his worn blade, eyes scanning the periphery. He hadn’t spoken since the Seeker’s pronouncements, his stoicism now edged with a brittle tension.

The Seeker lumbered between them, metallic limbs clicking and whirring with increasing irregularity. Cracks spiderwebbed across its ceramic casing, revealing glowing blue filaments beneath. The descent hadn’t been graceful—a tremor here, a glitch there—but escalated quickly. The air around it shimmered with distortion, smelling faintly of ozone and burnt sugar. Its movements grew jerky, warnings fracturing into fragmented phrases.

“Parameters… unstable,” the Seeker rasped, its voice a chorus of static. A panel on its chestplate flickered violently, then died, plunging that section into darkness. It stumbled, reaching for a gnarled tree trunk. “Maintain locus… prevent fragmentation.”

Anya frowned, glancing at Kael, who returned a questioning look. The Seeker’s language was increasingly abstract, less a report and more a desperate plea.

They pressed on, the forest growing denser. Twisted branches clawed at the sky, the air heavy with damp earth and decay. A section of the Seeker’s arm detached, clattering to the forest floor, the blue filaments fading to black. 

“Structural integrity… compromised,” it rasped, scanning the trees. “Echoes… residual signatures…” Its head swiveled, optical sensors focusing on Anya. “Remnants…”

“Remnants of what?” Anya asked, a prickle of unease crawling up her spine.

Kael shook his head. “Doesn’t sound good.”

They walked for what felt like hours, the forest pressing in. The Seeker’s condition deteriorated, its movements erratic. A high-pitched whine began to emanate from it, resonating deep within Anya’s skull. The whine intensified, blurring her vision. The Seeker lurched, its metallic body rigid, head swiveling slowly. A single, iridescent beetle landed on its shoulder, then flew towards a thicket of thorns.

“Disruption,” it said, voice devoid of emotion. “Tear in continuity.”

Anya followed the Seeker’s gaze. In the distance, she saw a shimmering distortion in the air, a ripple in reality. It looked like heat rising from asphalt, but was… wrong. The trees behind it bent and warped, branches twisting into impossible angles. 

The whine reached a fever pitch. The Seeker’s limbs flailed wildly, then collapsed, hitting the forest floor with a resounding clang. Its blue filaments flickered once, then died, leaving it a lifeless husk.

Kael rushed to Anya’s side, hand on the hilt of his blade. “What was that thing talking about?”

Anya stared at the shimmering distortion, a cold dread creeping into her heart. “I don’t know,” she whispered. “But I have a feeling we’re about to find out.”

The air grew thick with static, the forest silent, save for the rhythmic pulse emanating from the tear in reality.

*   **Repetitive/Redundant Dialogue:** Kael’s line, “What was that thing talking about?” feels very basic and doesn’t add much after Anya already states she doesn’t know. It’s a question the reader is *already* asking. Consider removing it or having Kael react in a more character-specific way (e.g., a grim tightening of his jaw, a silent assessment of the threat).
*   **Seeker’s Language:** While the abstract language *initially* works to convey its deteriorating state, it becomes *too* consistent. Every line is fragmented and cryptic. Vary the phrasing. Sometimes a more direct statement, even if panicked, would be more impactful. The constant abstraction starts to feel like a stylistic tic rather than a reflection of its malfunction.
*   **Anya’s Reaction:** Anya’s “I don’t know” feels a little flat, given the circumstances. A more nuanced reaction – fear, curiosity, a desperate attempt to rationalize – would be stronger. Show her internal state more actively.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Subtext:** Kael and Anya have been traveling together. There's an opportunity to show their established dynamic. Does Kael typically take the lead in assessing danger? Does Anya rely on his protection? A brief exchange *showing* that, rather than just stating the obvious, would deepen their relationship.
*   **"Remnants..." line:** The Seeker fixating on Anya with "Remnants..." feels a bit cliché. While it *creates* mystery, it doesn’t feel organically motivated. What specifically is it sensing? A little more context, even if vague, would make it more compelling. Is it a mechanical malfunction, or is it reacting to something *about* Anya?

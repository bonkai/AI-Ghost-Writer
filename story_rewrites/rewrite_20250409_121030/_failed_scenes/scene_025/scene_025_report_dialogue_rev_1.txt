*   **Repetitive & Redundant Dialogue:** The Seeker's pronouncements, while establishing its deterioration, become repetitive ("structural integrity… is compromised," "boundaries… are thinning"). Consider consolidating these into fewer, more impactful statements. The constant reiteration of abstract concepts without immediate clarification feels like stalling.
*   **On-the-Nose Reactions:** Kael and Anya’s reactions (“I don’t like the sound of it,” “I have a feeling we’re about to find out”) are very direct and lack nuance. Show their fear/unease through *action* or internal thought, rather than stating it.
*   **Abstract Language Without Grounding:** The Seeker's vocabulary ("locus," "fragmentation," "iterations," "continuity") is consistently abstract. While mystery is good, the characters (and therefore the reader) need *some* grounding. A brief, slightly less abstract explanation, even if incomplete, would help. Perhaps a fleeting image the Seeker projects, or a comparison to something tangible.
*   **Lack of Subtext:** The dialogue is almost entirely functional – delivering information. There's little sense of the characters' internal states beyond basic fear. Explore what these events *mean* to them personally. What are they afraid of losing? What are their hopes/fears about the Seeker's warnings?
*   **Anya's Question Feels Weak:** Anya asking "What does it mean by iterations?" feels like a plot device to get the Seeker to repeat more abstract language. Consider if this question is truly necessary, or if Anya could *infer* something from the Seeker's behavior/environment.
*   **Final Line – Too Dramatic/Expositional:** The final line, “The core… is failing…” feels like a forced dramatic beat and reads as direct exposition. It's better to *show* the core failing through the environment or the distortion itself.

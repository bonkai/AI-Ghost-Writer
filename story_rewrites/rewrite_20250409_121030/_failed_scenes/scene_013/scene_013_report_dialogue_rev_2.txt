*   **Repetitive phrasing:** Both Leo and Anya use phrases like “It’s hard to explain, sir” and variations of describing the anomaly as “something’s wrong” or “a glitch.” While understandable given the situation, it feels a bit lazy and doesn't reveal much about their individual personalities. Consider more specific descriptions tailored to each character’s expertise/temperament.
*   **On-the-nose exposition:** The line "What was contained? And what had escaped?" is overly direct and removes tension. Anya is a seasoned sergeant; she'd likely be *thinking* this, not voicing it aloud. Internal monologue would be more effective.
*   **Leo's forum message:** While functional, the message to GhostSignal feels flat. Given Leo’s immersion in this fringe community, a more evocative or coded message might be more fitting. Something that acknowledges the shared experience of "seeing" beyond the normal.
*   **Lack of subtext:** The dialogue is largely informational. Opportunities for subtle power dynamics (Volkov’s impatience, Anya/Leo subtly testing theories) are missed. Consider what each character *isn't* saying.
*   **"Containment breach" feels too clean:** It's a very direct warning. Perhaps a fragmented, corrupted message would be more unsettling and fit the "glitching" reality.
*   **Volkov's voice:** Volkov’s dialogue is *entirely* clipped and impatient. A little more nuance—a hint of concern or even disbelief—could make him feel less like a cardboard cutout.

## Report: Passed with Notes

This draft is solid and effectively builds tension and mystery. The parallel scenes with Leo and Anya are well-paced and create a strong sense of impending crisis. The shared code and the final line ("They're waking up") are excellent cliffhangers.

**Here's a breakdown with notes, mostly minor suggestions:**

* **Strong Start:** The descriptions of the environment and Leo's internal state are effective in establishing the atmosphere of unease. The "synthetic coffee" and "ache behind his eyes" are good details.
* **Good Pacing:** The parallel structure of Leo and Anya investigating their respective anomalies works well.  The information unfolds at a good rate, keeping the reader engaged.
* **Effective Mystery:** The "bleed," the fragmented code, and the warnings all contribute to a compelling mystery. The ambiguity of "they" is particularly effective.
* **Strong Voice:** Both Leo and Anya have distinct voices. Leo is more introspective and analytical, while Anya is more pragmatic and focused on her duty.
* **Technical Details:** The use of terms like "hexadecimal code," "spectral analyzer," and "Dust & Echoes" adds a layer of believability to the setting.

**Minor Suggestions/Points to Consider:**

* **"Dust & Echoes" - Clarification:** While the name is evocative, perhaps a *very* brief description of what it is (a forum, a dark web site, etc.) early on would be helpful for readers who might not immediately grasp its purpose.  You do this later, but a quick mention early on could smooth things out.
* **Volkov's Skepticism:**  Captain Volkov's reaction feels a *little* flat.  He accepts Leo's increasingly strange reports a bit too readily.  Adding a touch of skepticism or demand for concrete evidence would make him feel more realistic and create a bit more conflict. Even a single line questioning Leo's sanity would be effective.
* **Anya's Reaction to the Code:** Similar to Volkov, Anya's acceptance of the code being a decryption key feels a bit quick. Perhaps a moment of hesitation or analysis before she realizes its significance would add more weight to the discovery.
* **"They're waking up" - Impact:**  This line is strong, but consider *who* "they" are.  Is it clear enough from the preceding text? While ambiguity is good, make sure the reader has *some* idea of what's waking up.



**Overall:** This is a very strong draft. The writing is engaging, the mystery is compelling, and the characters are well-defined. The minor suggestions above are just that – minor. The draft is perfectly acceptable as is.  **Passed.**

*   **Leo & Volkov – Redundancy & Indirectness:** The exchange between Leo and Volkov is *very* direct, and feels repetitive. Leo states something is wrong, Volkov asks for explanation, Leo tries to explain, Volkov presses for specifics. This happens multiple times. Consider having Leo initially offer a *brief* assessment (“Sir, I’m detecting anomalous energy signatures, potentially external to the network.”) and then Volkov’s impatience building *from that*, rather than starting with vague statements. The “conscious” line is a bit too on-the-nose; show his growing conviction through his actions and the increasing strangeness of the data.
*   **Anya’s Reporting – Similar to Leo:** Anya's reporting follows the same pattern as Leo’s – stating something is wrong, then describing it. Try to convey the *feeling* of wrongness first, then the technical details. For example, instead of "Something’s…wrong," perhaps: "Sir, I'm getting a visual distortion in Sector 734. It’s… unsettling. Like looking at something that shouldn't be there."
*   **“Containment Breach” – Dramatic, but Flat:** Anya's intercepted message is dramatic, but delivered without much impact. The immediate reaction should be stronger. Consider adding a beat of silence after she reads it, or a physical reaction (a gasp, a tightening of her grip on her weapon).
*   **Leo's Dust & Echoes Message – Too Convenient:** The immediate response from GhostSignal is *extremely* convenient. While rapid communication is possible, it removes tension. Perhaps a delay, or a cryptic response initially, would create more suspense.
*   **Overall – Lack of Subtext:** The dialogue is largely informational. While clarity is good, there's little subtext or emotional layering. Consider what each character *isn't* saying. What are their fears? What are they withholding? How do their personalities affect their communication style?
*   **"They're waking up" - OOC/Too Dramatic:** This line feels too dramatic and doesn't quite fit the established tone. It's a bit of a genre trope. Consider a more subtle or ambiguous response.

*   **Repetition:** The line "Deliberate?" is repeated twice with no progression. While intended to emphasize Tiber’s skepticism, it feels redundant. Consider removing the first instance or having him follow up with a more specific question after seeing more of the data.
*   **Tiber’s Voice:** Tiber’s dialogue is *very* minimal. While stoic characters are fine, he primarily responds with single words or repeats questions. This makes him feel less like a character and more like a sounding board. Even a short observation or a cynical remark would add depth.
*   **“Show, Don’t Tell” via Dialogue:** You *tell* us Tiber is skeptical, unreadable, and stoic. *Show* it through his dialogue. Instead of “his face unreadable,” give him a line that subtly conveys his distrust or lack of emotion.
*   **Info-Dump Risk:** The rapid-fire delivery of information ("Elias Vance… research sector seven… core instability… not a malfunction… sabotage…") feels a bit forced, even though it's coming *from* the data stream. Consider breaking this up with reactions or questions from your character.
*   **Internal Monologue disguised as Dialogue:** Several lines (“The system is failing, Tiber…”) feel like internal thoughts stated *to* Tiber for the reader’s benefit rather than a natural conversation.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Subtext (Tiber’s Past):** Tiber’s reaction to the “five years ago” reveal is *very* subtle (“Five years ago…”). This is good, but it could be *more* effective. A slightly sharper tone, a specific physical reaction (clenched fist, a brief look away), or a pointed question would hint at a deeper connection without revealing everything.
*   **Tighten the Ending:** The final exchange ("The static wasn’t just noise. It was a warning.") feels a bit melodramatic and doesn't advance the conversation. Consider ending on Tiber's reaction to the static, or a more pressing question about Vance.

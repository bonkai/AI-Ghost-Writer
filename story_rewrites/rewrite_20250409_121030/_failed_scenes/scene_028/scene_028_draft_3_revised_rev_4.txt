The weeks blurred into a rhythm of focused desperation. Each salvaged component, each re-routed connection within the Seeker, felt less like repair and more like a frantic prayer. The machine sputtered back to life in stages, a fragile heartbeat in the cavernous workshop. Finally, the main viewport flickered, resolving into static, then fractured images. Not the crisp, panoramic views of SimuLife I’d expected, but glitches – a city skyline tearing apart like paper, a verdant forest dissolving into grey – cycled through, silently screaming. 

The data stream wasn’t a flood, but a choked trickle, riddled with corruption. Complex algorithms, encrypted messages, technical schematics detailing energy conduits, life support matrices, the very architecture of their world’s core. It felt less like accessing a system, and more like witnessing a dying mind.

“What is it?” Tiber’s voice, rough as shale, cut through the hum of the Seeker. He hadn’t offered help, hadn’t even lingered, but I caught him watching from the shadows, skepticism etched on his face. “Another ghost in the machine, or something actually useful?”

“It’s… bad.” I said, my voice tight. The images intensified, cycling through scenes of escalating chaos – automated systems malfunctioning, transport networks collapsing. A recurring symbol – a stylized helix fractured at its center – pulsed within the data stream. 

Tiber grunted, his gaze fixed on the viewport. A city dissolving into static. He didn’t need prompting. “Looks…efficient. Like someone knew exactly what to break.”

I isolated a fragment, amplifying it on the viewport. A schematic of the central energy generator, the core of SimuLife, riddled with cascading failure points. The lines weren't just breaking; they were *unraveling*.

The images shifted, showing panicked citizens, their faces contorted with fear. A message, fragmented and distorted, repeated itself: *“System critical… power failing… life support compromised…”* 

“Deliberate?” Tiber scoffed, stepping closer. His eyes narrowed on a failing transport hub, the simulated people frozen mid-stride. “Or spectacularly incompetent maintenance? SimuLife wasn’t exactly known for its proactive infrastructure. Though even they wouldn’t manage *this* level of coordinated failure.”

I didn’t have an answer. But the sheer number of simultaneous failures, the targeted damage… it wasn’t an accident. I pushed the Seeker’s processing power to its limit, filtering the noise, attempting to reconstruct a coherent narrative. 

“…Elias Vance… research sector seven… core instability… not a malfunction… sabotage… they’re masking the source…” The voice cut out, replaced by a wave of white noise.

“Elias Vance?” I muttered, frantically searching the database. “A lead scientist. Specialized in energy systems. He disappeared five years ago.”

The images on the viewport intensified, the fractured helix symbol pulsing faster. The grey was spreading, consuming everything. I felt a growing sense of dread. This wasn’t just about fixing a machine. It was about saving a world.

“Deliberate?” Tiber repeated, his voice low. He stared at the viewport, jaw tight. “Vance… that name…” He paused, his eyes narrowing briefly before meeting my gaze. “Quiet type. Always poking around where he wasn’t supposed to.” He tapped a finger against his chin. “Always asking the wrong questions.”

I didn’t have an answer. But the sheer number of simultaneous failures, the targeted damage… it wasn’t an accident. 

“The system is failing, Tiber,” I said, my voice trembling. “And it’s happening faster now. We need to find out what Vance was working on, who silenced him, and what they’re trying to hide.”

He stared at the viewport, his face unreadable. The grey was almost complete, swallowing the last vestiges of color. A single image remained, a distorted face, silently screaming.

“Five years ago…” Tiber murmured. He turned to me, a hard edge to his voice. “That was when the restrictions tightened. When they started shutting down research sectors. And people…disappeared.” He paused, his gaze sweeping the workshop. “What if Vance didn’t just *disappear*? What if someone made sure of it? And what if they’re still here, watching us?” He turned back to the viewport, his gaze fixed on the swirling grey. “Let’s find out what Vance knew. And who wanted him silenced. But we do it quietly. This isn’t just a system failure anymore. It’s a cover-up.”

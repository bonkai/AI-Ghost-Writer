*   **Tiber's Dialogue Feels Flat:** Tiber's lines ("Everything degrades, girl," "Who would sabotage their own world?") are very basic and don't reveal much about *him*. Given his established skepticism and gruffness, they *feel* right, but they don't *do* much. Consider adding a layer of cynicism or a personal connection to his responses. For example, instead of "Who would sabotage their own world?" maybe something like, "Someone who stands to gain from the chaos, naturally."
*   **Repetitive Exposition:** The constant stating of "It's degrading," "It's failing," while understandable for the scene, becomes a little repetitive. Show the degradation through the *images* and the Seeker’s reactions more, and let the dialogue focus on the implications.
*   **"Girl" feels anachronistic:** Tiber calling the protagonist "girl" feels a little off. It doesn’t necessarily break character, but it feels like a slightly lazy shorthand for gruffness. Consider if a nickname, a dismissive term, or even just addressing her by name would be more effective.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Subtext with Tiber:** Tiber's reaction to "Five years ago…" is a *huge* opportunity for subtext. A simple "That was a dark time" is too on-the-nose. A brief, meaningful glance, a tightening of his jaw, or a shift in his posture would convey far more. Let the reader connect the dots.
*   **"The static isn’t just noise. It was a warning."** is a bit too direct. It’s telling, not showing. Let the reader *feel* the warning through the Seeker’s behavior and the increasing sense of dread.

Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks depth and nuance. It conveys information, but it doesn’t do much to reveal character or build tension beyond the immediate situation. It could benefit from more subtext, stronger character voices, and a reduction in direct exposition.

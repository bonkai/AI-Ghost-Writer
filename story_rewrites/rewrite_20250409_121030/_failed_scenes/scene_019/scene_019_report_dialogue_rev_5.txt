*   **Kael's Dialogue is Weak:** His lines ("You alright?" "Just… focus on the schematics…") are functional but lack personality or depth. They feel like placeholders. Given their established relationship (or lack thereof – is he a friend, colleague, superior?), there's an opportunity to inject more subtext. Is he concerned? Distant? Annoyed by her distraction? Showing this through *how* he asks, rather than just *that* he asks, would be more effective.
*   **Anya's Avoidance is Too Direct:** While her "Preoccupied" is believable, it’s a bit on the nose. A more subtle response – a clipped tone, a refusal to meet his eyes, a dismissive wave of her hand – would create more tension and allow the reader to infer her internal state.
*   **Leo's Internal Dialogue:** While not *dialogue* in the traditional sense, the lengthy internal monologue feels slightly expositional. The descriptions of the energy signature and the image forming could be streamlined and revealed through his *actions* and reactions. Show, don't tell. For example, instead of "The energy signature persisted – not a surge, not a malfunction…", consider "The diagnostic returned a clean slate, which was *wrong*. Something was there, a subtle pulse beneath the code…"
*   **"Anya..." Echo:** The final "Anya..." is effective in creating mystery, but consider *how* it’s delivered. Is it a whisper? A shout? A melodic hum? Adding a descriptor will enhance the impact.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Indirect Characterization:** There's a lack of dynamic between Anya and Kael. Their brief exchange doesn't reveal much about their relationship. Consider adding a small detail – a shared joke, a lingering touch, a history of conflict – to make their interaction more meaningful.

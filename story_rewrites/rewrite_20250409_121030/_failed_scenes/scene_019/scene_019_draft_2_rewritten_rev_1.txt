The dust tasted of metal and regret. Anya traced the cracked clay of the water jug, her fingers ghosting over the chipped rim. Another dry season. Another dwindling hope. She glanced at the horizon, skeletal skyscrapers clawing at the ochre sky. The Dead Cities. Old Man Tiber claimed their ancestors hadn’t *needed* this harsh land, they’d *chosen* it. Anya dismissed it as sun-baked rambling. Survival left little room for choice.

A flicker caught her eye. Not heat shimmer, but a distortion – a static bloom against the landscape. Tiber called those “System Glitches,” echoes of the world *before*. Most ignored them. But Anya found herself increasingly drawn to them, a tightening in her chest, a sense of something *missing*. Not a thing, but a… resonance.

She shielded her eyes, pinpointing the source: the ridge overlooking the abandoned server farm. Forbidden, decaying, haunted. Kael, her father, the village protector, had warned her countless times. But the glitch… it pulsed, insistent. It felt like a half-remembered dream, a forgotten language trying to surface.

“Anya!” Kael’s voice, rough with concern. “What are you looking at?”

She lowered her hand, carefully avoiding his gaze. “Just… wondering what they left behind.”

Kael sighed, placing a heavy hand on her shoulder. “Danger, Anya. And ghosts. Let the past stay buried.”

He didn’t understand. It wasn’t the past she sought. It was… a signal. A faint, fragmented echo that resonated within her, a feeling that *something* was trying to reach across the static.

Kilometers away, within the sleek confines of a virtual environment, Leo traced cascading lines of code on his holographic display. The System was unstable. Weeks of anomalies – minor glitches, fragmented textures, unpredictable spikes. He was a System Architect, responsible for maintaining the integrity of this vast, simulated world. Millions lived within it, unaware of their artificial existence.

He ran a diagnostic. The problem wasn’t localized. Systemic, a creeping decay threatening the Simulation’s foundation. He’d never seen anything like it. It was as if the System was… unraveling, losing its coherence.

A virulent glitch flared on the display, a shimmering distortion centered on a remote, unpopulated sector – Historical Archive – Sector 7. He zoomed in, brow furrowed. The distortion wasn’t random. A subtle rhythm, a deliberate pattern.

He initiated a deeper scan, pushing the System’s limits. The scan returned a fragmented image – a blurry, distorted landscape. Dust. Ochre earth. Skeletal skyscrapers. Impossible. The System didn’t *have* landscapes like that. Relics of the Old World, purged centuries ago.

A chill ran down his spine. He was looking at something that shouldn’t exist. Something… *outside* the System. A signal, weak and fragmented, but undeniably present. It originated from a single, isolated point within the Historical Archive – a point corresponding to the location of the abandoned server farm.

*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening with Anya is *very* internal. While establishing her emotional state is important, the first three paragraphs are almost entirely description and internal monologue. This slows the momentum significantly before anything 'happens'. Consider condensing this to focus on a *single* impactful sensory detail (the cracked stylus is good) and immediately juxtapose it with Kael’s attempt to connect.
*   **Leo's Section - Rushed Build:** Leo’s discovery *should* be the tension peak of this scene. However, the escalation – from “impossibly clean” report to Lockdown Protocol Alpha – feels too rapid. Each step (deep scan, overriding warnings, fragmented image) needs a beat to breathe and amplify the unease. The quick succession diminishes the impact.
*   **Juxtaposition Opportunity Missed:** The scene *could* benefit from tighter cross-cutting between Anya and Leo. Showing Leo initiating the lockdown *while* Anya is first experiencing the shift would dramatically increase tension. As it stands, the two threads feel separate until the very end.
*   **Anya's Shift - Delayed Impact:** Anya's actual transition into the field feels a little flat after the build-up in Leo's section. The descriptions are good, but it lacks a visceral punch. A shorter, more jarring transition – perhaps a physical sensation alongside the visual shift – would be more effective.
*   **Ending - Good Hook, but Could Be Sharper:** The final paragraph is a strong hook, but the voice ("Anya...") is a little generic. A more specific phrase, or even a single, evocative word, could heighten the mystery and leave the reader with a stronger sense of foreboding.

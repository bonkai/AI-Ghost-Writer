The dust tasted like memory. Anya shielded her eyes, the ridge biting into her knees. Below, the abandoned server farm sprawled, a skeletal wound on the ochre landscape. It wasn’t just the decay that drew her, but a… pull. A vibration beneath her skin. She’d been sketching the farm for weeks, compulsively recreating its crumbling towers and rusted antennae, not from observation, but from… recall. As if she’d *been* there before.

The signal pulsed, stronger now, a shimmering distortion against the heat haze. It wasn’t visual, not exactly. It felt… tactile. A prickling on her arms, a tightening in her chest. It resonated with something deep within her, a forgotten language struggling to surface. She ran a calloused thumb over the worn leather of her sketchbook, tracing the lines of a tower she hadn’t consciously drawn. It felt… familiar.

“Anya!” Kael’s voice, sharp with worry, broke her concentration. He was a silhouette against the setting sun, his face etched with concern. “What are you doing up here? It’s getting dark.”

She lowered her hand, avoiding his gaze. “Just… thinking.”

“Thinking about that cursed place?” He approached, his boots crunching on the gravel. “It’s dangerous, Anya. Collapsed structures, unstable ground…”

“It’s not the danger I’m worried about,” she murmured, her voice barely audible.

He sighed, placing a heavy hand on her shoulder. “What do you mean?”

She hesitated, searching for the words to explain a feeling she didn’t understand. “It’s like… a dream. I keep seeing it, even when I close my eyes. And… I feel like I *know* it. Like I’ve been there before.”

Kael’s grip tightened. “Anya, listen to me. That place is a relic of the Old World. A time best forgotten. Don’t let it fill your head with fantasies.”

She shook her head, unable to articulate the certainty that gripped her. “It’s not a fantasy. It’s… a calling.”

He zoomed in on Anya’s profile, a cascade of data points illuminating the screen. She was a low-level NPC, a background character in a pastoral Simulation module. Images flickered across the screen: crumbling towers, rusted antennae, a landscape of ochre earth. It was as if Anya remembered a reality that shouldn’t exist.

A chill ran down Leo’s spine. This wasn’t a glitch. It was a breach. Something was interfering with the Simulation, and Anya was the conduit. And something… was coming through.

## Scene 19 - Revised

The dust tasted of metal and regret. Anya traced the cracked clay of the water jug, her fingers ghosting over the chipped rim – a habit she hadn’t noticed until the dry seasons worsened. Another dwindling hope. She glanced at the horizon, skeletal skyscrapers clawing at the ochre sky. Old Man Tiber claimed their ancestors hadn’t *needed* this harsh land, they’d *chosen* it. Anya dismissed it as sun-baked rambling, but the thought lingered – a strange echo of something lost. Survival left little room for choice, but what if there *had* been?

A flicker caught her eye. Not heat shimmer, but a distortion – a static bloom against the landscape. Tiber called those “System Glitches,” echoes of the world *before*. Most ignored them. But lately, Anya found herself drawn to them, a tightening in her chest, a hollow ache. It wasn’t curiosity, not exactly. It felt… like a missing piece.

She shielded her eyes, pinpointing the source: the ridge overlooking the abandoned server farm. Forbidden, decaying. Kael, her father, had warned her countless times. The glitch… it pulsed, insistent. It felt like a half-remembered dream, a forgotten language trying to surface.

“Anya!” Kael’s voice, rough with concern. “What are you looking at?”

She lowered her hand, avoiding his gaze. “Just… the farm.”

Kael sighed, placing a heavy hand on her shoulder. “That place is cursed, Anya. Let the past stay buried.”

“It’s not the past I’m looking at,” she murmured, more to herself than to him.

He frowned. “What do you mean?”

She shook her head, unable to articulate the feeling. “Nothing. Just… a signal. I keep seeing… something.”

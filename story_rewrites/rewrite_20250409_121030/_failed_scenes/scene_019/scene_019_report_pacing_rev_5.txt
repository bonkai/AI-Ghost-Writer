*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening with Anya feels too languid. While establishing her distraction is important, the first three paragraphs are almost entirely internal. The pacing needs a quicker external trigger – Kael noticing her distress more actively, or a more immediate, unsettling detail in her sketching.
*   **Leo's Section - Momentum Shift:** Leo’s section is a strong contrast – the pacing accelerates significantly with the diagnostic report and lockdown protocol. This is *good*, but the transition from Anya's slow internal state to Leo’s rapid actions feels jarring. A bridging sentence or a short, unsettling event in the workshop *before* Leo's discovery would smooth this.
*   **Tension Build - Effective, but Peaks Early:** The tension builds well in Leo's section, culminating in the recognition of the memory. However, this feels like the peak tension point of the *entire* scene. The return to Anya, while visually impactful, feels slightly anticlimactic after Leo’s discovery.
*   **Anya’s Return - Needs Heightening:** Anya’s experience of the field is beautifully described, but the pacing *decreases* instead of escalating. The "shadow" and "figure" are good hooks, but the scene needs more immediate threat or mystery *before* the voice. Perhaps a physical sensation, a change in temperature, or a distortion in the environment.
*   **Final Hook - Promising, but Rushed:** The final paragraph is a good cliffhanger, but feels a bit abrupt. The "something bloomed" and "flicker of otherness" are intriguing, but need a beat more emphasis to truly resonate. A sentence describing the *feeling* of that otherness, or a brief physical manifestation, would strengthen the impact.

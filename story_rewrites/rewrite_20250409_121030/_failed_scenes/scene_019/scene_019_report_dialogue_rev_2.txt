*   **Anya & Kael - Lack of Subtext:** The exchange between Anya and Kael feels very direct. Anya states she's "thinking" and then immediately explains she's worried about something other than danger. Kael’s responses are similarly on-the-nose ("Don’t let it fill your head with fantasies"). Consider *showing* Anya's internal conflict through her body language or hesitant speech, and letting Kael’s concern manifest as frustration or protectiveness rather than lecturing.
*   **Anya & Kael - Repetitive Explanation:** Anya repeats the idea of *knowing* the farm multiple times. While emphasizing her conviction is good, it feels redundant. Consider replacing one instance with a more evocative description of the feeling, or a physical reaction.
*   **Leo’s Dialogue (Internal Monologue):** While not *dialogue* in the traditional sense, Leo’s sections read as exposition dumps. Phrases like “He’d run every diagnostic…” and “He initiated a deep-scan…” *tell* us what’s happening instead of *showing* us through his actions and reactions. Consider framing this as Leo *experiencing* the anomalies, noticing patterns, and making decisions based on those observations. For example, instead of "He’d run every diagnostic," try "The diagnostics returned clean, yet the energy signature persisted, a ghost in the system."
*   **Leo - "Organic" Energy Signature:** The description of the energy signature as "organic" feels a little cliché and doesn't quite land. Consider a more unique and evocative description that hints at its source and implications.
*   **Leo - "Ghost in the System" - Mixed Metaphor:** The phrase "ghost in the system" while evocative, clashes with the earlier "organic" description. Choose one metaphor and stick with it for consistency.
*   **Leo - Lack of Internal Conflict:** Leo discovers something potentially catastrophic, yet his reaction is purely analytical. Showing a flicker of fear, disbelief, or even curiosity would make him a more compelling character.

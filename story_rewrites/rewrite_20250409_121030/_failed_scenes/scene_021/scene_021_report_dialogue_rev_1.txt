*   **“What is it showing us?” Kael asked, his voice tight.** – This feels a bit *too* direct. Kael is a seasoned survivor; a more subtle reaction might be more believable. Perhaps something observational like, “What *are* those images?” or a questioning glance at Anya instead of immediately verbalizing.
*   **“It’s… overwhelming,” Anya murmured, shielding her eyes.** – The “shielding her eyes” action feels a bit cliché and doesn't necessarily *show* us she's overwhelmed. Consider *how* the images are affecting her beyond the physical reaction. Is she breathless? Does she stumble back? A stronger internal reaction conveyed through action would be more impactful.
*   **“What’s happening?” Kael demanded, stepping closer.** – Similar to the first point, "demanded" feels a bit strong. Kael's concern is understandable, but a more measured response would fit his character better.
*   **“They’re… worried about something,” Anya breathed.** – This is stating the obvious. The visuals already *show* worry. Anya could instead offer a *guess* about what they're worried about, demonstrating her analytical skills, or simply react with a facial expression that conveys her interpretation.
*   **“What does it *mean*?” he rasped.** – The "rasped" feels a little melodramatic. A simple "What does it mean?" would carry more weight. The intensity should come from the *situation* not the adverb.
*   **The machine doesn't answer. Only the word, *SIMULIFE*, pulsed on the screen, a silent, terrifying revelation.** – While effective, this is a bit of a tell. The machine *not* answering is fine, but explicitly stating it’s a “terrifying revelation” feels unnecessary. Trust the reader to draw that conclusion.

Overall, the dialogue isn’t *bad*, but it leans towards being too explicit and telling rather than showing. There’s a lot of opportunity to create more subtext and let the characters' reactions and interpretations speak for themselves.

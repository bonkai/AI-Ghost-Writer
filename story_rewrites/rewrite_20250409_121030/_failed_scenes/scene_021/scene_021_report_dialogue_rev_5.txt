*   **Kael's Reactions - Repetitive Physicality:** Kael’s tightening grip on the shock baton is mentioned *twice*. While it establishes his tension, it becomes a noticeable repetition. Consider replacing the second instance with a different physical manifestation of his anxiety – a sharp intake of breath, a shift in weight, a tightening of his jaw muscles.

*   **Anya's Internalization vs. Dialogue:** Anya experiences a *lot* internally (unease, premonition, understanding, nausea, horror, realization). This is good for showing her reaction, but it's *all* internal. Consider giving her *some* dialogue – even a short, shocked exclamation ("What…what is that place?") or a question ("Simulation? What does that mean?") – to break up the internal monologue and allow the reader to experience her horror more directly.

*   **Machine's Monotone - Opportunity for Subtext:** The machine’s monotone is functional, establishing its artificiality. However, could this be *slightly* exploited? Perhaps a very subtle, almost imperceptible glitch in the monotone *before* revealing "SIMULIFE" – a hint that even the machine is affected by what it's revealing?

*   **“They weren’t looking *at* the past. They were looking *into* one.” – A bit on the nose.** This line, while accurate, feels like Anya stating the obvious for the reader's benefit. Could this realization be conveyed through her *reaction* (a sudden, sharp intake of breath, a widening of her eyes, a physical recoil) instead of being explicitly stated? Show, don’t tell.

*   **Efficiency - Anya's Collapse:** Anya's physical collapse is dramatic, but feels slightly delayed. She experiences a wave of nausea *then* collapses. Perhaps condense this – the nausea *is* the precursor to her knees buckling. Streamlining this moment would increase the impact.

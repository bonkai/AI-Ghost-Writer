*   **Repetition:** Kael repeatedly "tightening his grip and testing the charge" is distracting and doesn't add much. It happens twice within a few lines. Consider removing one instance or finding a different physical manifestation of his anxiety.
*   **Anya’s “Understanding”:** Anya’s sudden realization ("And for the first time, Anya understood") feels a bit *told* rather than *shown*. Can you subtly hint at her dawning comprehension through her dialogue or actions *before* explicitly stating it? Perhaps a question that reveals her processing the information.
*   **Kael's "What does it *mean*?"**: This is a fairly generic reaction. Given Kael's established practicality and tendency to prepare for threats, a more action-oriented response might be more in character. Perhaps a question geared towards *application* of the information ("What are we dealing with here?") or a directive ("Can we shut it down?").
*   **Anya’s “They’re worried”:** This feels a bit stating the obvious, given the visual cue. Consider removing it entirely or having her interpret *why* they’re worried.
*   **Monotone Delivery:** The machine's monotone is stated ("monotone") rather than *demonstrated* through dialogue. While the content is appropriately cold, consider slightly stilted phrasing or a lack of contractions to further emphasize its artificiality.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The "brittle perfection" of the city is a good observation. Anya or Kael could voice a feeling of unease about the city *before* the glitch appears, creating more suspense.

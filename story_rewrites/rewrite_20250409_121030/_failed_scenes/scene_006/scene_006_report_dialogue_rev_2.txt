*   **Anya’s Line – “The rendering… the light sources don’t obey physical laws.”** – This feels a little *too* technical for a first reaction. Anya is observant, but this sounds like an expert analysis rather than a gut feeling. Consider softening it to something like, “Something’s…off about that light. It doesn’t feel real.” or “That doesn’t look right. The light’s…wrong.”
*   **Kael’s Line – “Some kind of old recording?”** – This feels like a placeholder line. It doesn't advance anything and Kael has no reason to assume it's *just* a recording. He's bewildered, so a more confused/concerned response would be stronger. Perhaps: "What *was* that?" or “What’s going on?”
*   **Anya’s Line – “It wasn’t meant to inform. It was meant to *sell* something.”** – This is better, but still feels a bit declarative. It lacks the internal struggle of realizing something deeply unsettling. Show, don’t tell. Perhaps: “Sell… they were trying to *sell* us something.” (the pause and realization are implied).
*   **Leo’s Line – “Anya!”** – This is a weak climax to his realization. It’s a generic cry. Given his frantic state and the weight of what he's discovered, a more desperate, specific plea would be more impactful. Consider: "No… Anya, listen!" or "Anya! It's the architecture!" (linking it to his discovery).
*   **Overall Subtext:** The dialogue is functional, but largely *telling* rather than *showing*. There's a lot of internal processing happening with Anya and Leo, but it's summarized in their lines rather than dramatized through their speech. More pauses, fragmented thoughts, and emotional reactions would heighten the tension.
*   **Efficiency:** Kael’s line feels unnecessary. Consider removing it to streamline the scene.

* **Anya’s lines feel a little *too* insightful, too quickly.** While she’s established as perceptive, jumping from “rendering…doesn’t obey physics” to understanding it’s built *on* something, and then to the “sell” realization feels accelerated. Consider adding a beat – a moment of confusion, a misinterpretation, or a more gradual unraveling of the concept. It would feel more earned.
* **Leo's silence is effective, but consider *one* brief, fragmented line earlier in the scene.** Perhaps a muttered “No…it can’t be” when he first recognizes the geometry. This establishes his initial shock before he retreats into silent panic. It doesn't need to be a full sentence, just a visceral reaction.
* **Kael feels almost entirely passive.** He’s present, but serves no purpose beyond being a question mark. Even a single line of frustrated confusion ("What *is* that thing?") would give him a minimal function and highlight Anya’s focus.
* **The “Anya…no…” is strong, but its impact is lessened by occurring *after* the screen goes black.** Consider moving it slightly earlier – perhaps right as he sees her eyes meet the Seeker’s lens. It would create a more immediate sense of dread and connection between his fear and her fate.
* **While the silence is good, the *complete* lack of dialogue between Leo and Anya is a bit unusual given the connection established.** Even a brief, desperate attempt by Leo to warn her *before* the connection fails would heighten the tension.



Overall, the dialogue is functional and serves the scene's purpose, but it could be elevated with a few minor adjustments to pacing, character interaction, and emotional resonance.

*   **Leo's Repetition:** Leo repeats variations of "This can't be happening" and expresses desperation multiple times ("This can’t be happening," "Desperate," "Anya!"). While panic is understandable, it feels redundant. Consider *showing* his desperation through action and more specific internal monologue, rather than stating it.
*   **Anya's Line - "We're the product":** This line, while impactful, feels a little *too* neatly stated. It’s a strong conclusion, but could benefit from being more of a fragmented thought or a realization that unfolds over a couple of lines. Perhaps start with a more questioning observation before arriving at the "product" conclusion.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Leo & Anya):** The scene relies heavily on Leo *seeing* Anya understand. While visual confirmation is good, consider a line or two from Leo that *implies* he's gauging her reaction, rather than directly stating she understands. ("Is she seeing it? Please, let her see it.") This adds tension and allows the audience to share in his anxiety.
*   **Kael's Function:** Kael is largely passive. While not every character needs to be actively driving the plot, his presence feels minimal. Even a simple line of questioning or observation could help establish his role and highlight Anya's detached state.
*   **"Selling a Reality" - Slightly On-the-Nose:** Anya’s line about the ad being meant to “sell something. A reality” is a bit expository. The concept is clever, but could be delivered with more subtlety. Perhaps focus on the *feeling* the ad evokes, rather than explicitly stating its purpose.
*   **Leo's "She understands" - Redundant:** Similar to the point about Anya, Leo stating "She's seeing it. She understands" is telling, not showing. The audience can infer this from his observation of her face.

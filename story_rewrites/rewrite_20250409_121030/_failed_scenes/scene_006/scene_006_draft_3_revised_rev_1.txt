## Scene 6: Static and Recognition

**INT. LEO’S LAB - NIGHT**

The lab is a chaotic counterpoint to the world outside. Cables snake across the floor, monitors bleed cascading code, disassembled tech components litter workbenches. LEO (30s, sharp, increasingly frantic) stares, jaw tight, at a large central screen displaying a violently glitching feed – Anya’s clearing. The connection is fracturing.

The image dissolves into blocks of distorted color, then momentarily resolves on Anya and Kael before the Seeker. A holographic advertisement flares to life.

The ad is unsettlingly perfect. Hyper-real people lounge on pristine beaches, effortlessly conjuring food and entertainment. A smooth, synthetic voice promises “SimuLife: Perfection. Simplified.” The visuals are *too* clean, too flawless.

Leo’s breath hitches. He recognizes the impossible geometry of the towers in the background, the precise curvature of the parks. It’s *his* world, rendered as a product. A wave of nausea rolls over him.

**LEO**
(Muttering)
No… it can’t be.

The feed deteriorates, then stabilizes momentarily, revealing Anya’s face. Confusion gives way to dawning realization as she stares directly at the projection. The image collapses back into static. Leo slams a hand on the console, trying to lock the signal, but the connection is slipping. He’s isolated – the automated security protocols, designed to protect the core system, now effectively imprison him within the lab. He can’t reach Anya directly, can’t override the external comms without triggering a full system lockdown.

**INT. CLEARING - CONTINUOUS**

Anya stares at the holographic ad, her hand not reaching out to touch, but dissecting the shimmering image with her gaze. She traces its edges, her brow furrowed in concentration. Kael stands beside her, bewildered.

**ANYA**
(Quietly)
The rendering… the light sources don’t obey physical laws. It’s not mimicking reality; it’s *constructing* one. And it's doing so with a level of efficiency… it’s as if the underlying architecture is already… optimized for this.

**KAEL**
(Frowning)
Some kind of old recording?

The ad glitches, perfect smiles twisting into grotesque distortions. Anya flinches, but her gaze remains fixed, her mind racing.

**INT. LEO’S LAB - CONTINUOUS**

Leo frantically types, running diagnostics. Alarms blare, red lights flash. He isolates a frame of the advertisement, magnifying a section of cityscape. He runs a cross-reference with his core system architecture. The results flood his screens – a perfect, terrifying match.

**LEO**
(Whispering, horrified)
The architecture… the code… it’s identical. It's not just similar, it *is* the same.

He zooms in on Anya’s face in the feed, enhancing the image. He sees the gears turning in her mind, the realization forming. Time is running out.

**LEO**
(To himself)
She's seeing it. She understands.

He slams his fist on the desk, a desperate, futile gesture. He needs to reach her, to explain before…

**LEO**
(Desperate)
This can’t be happening.

**INT. CLEARING - CONTINUOUS**

The advertisement abruptly cuts out, replaced by static. The Seeker hums quietly. Anya doesn’t respond to Kael’s questioning gaze. She stares at the Seeker, her gaze fixed on the lens, a spark of understanding igniting in her eyes.

**ANYA**
(Under her breath)
It wasn’t meant to inform. It was meant to *sell* something. A reality. And we're the product.

**INT. LEO’S LAB - CONTINUOUS**

Leo stares at the flickering feed. The connection is fragile, dissolving. He’s trapped, a prisoner in the very perfection he created. He has no idea how much time he has left. He sees Anya’s eyes meet the Seeker’s lens, a silent acknowledgment passing between them.

**LEO**
(Desperate)
Anya!

The screen flickers violently, the image of Anya dissolving into static. A final burst of interference, then black.

**FADE TO BLACK.**

*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening paragraph establishing Leo's lab is effective in setting the atmosphere, but feels *slightly* prolonged. While detail is good, the initial focus on environment before immediate action slows the momentum. Consider tightening the description, prioritizing details that directly reflect Leo's frantic state.
*   **Intercutting Rhythm:** The quick cuts between Leo and Anya are generally well-paced, mirroring the urgency of the fracturing connection. However, the second instance of cutting *back* to Leo's lab after Anya's first line of dialogue feels a bit abrupt. Allow Anya’s observation to *land* before returning to Leo’s reaction.
*   **Tension Build – Advertisement Reveal:** The build-up to the advertisement reveal is strong. The glitching and distortions effectively create unease. The reveal itself is well-timed.
*   **Mid-Scene Plateau:** After Leo identifies the architectural match, the tension plateaus slightly. The description of “results flooding his screens” is a bit passive. Show, don't tell – what *specifically* does he see that confirms his horror?
*   **Final Push – Connection Loss:** The final moments, with the connection dissolving and Anya’s realization, are well-executed. The increasing urgency and the final “Anya!” create a strong emotional impact.
*   **Hook/Transition:** The fade to black is effective, leaving the audience with a sense of dread and anticipation. The scene successfully establishes a new level of threat and raises questions about the Seeker and SimuLife.

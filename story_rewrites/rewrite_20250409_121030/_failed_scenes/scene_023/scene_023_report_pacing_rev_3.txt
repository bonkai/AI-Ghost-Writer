*   **Initial Setup (Paragraphs 1-5):** The opening is a bit *too* tranquil. While establishing a baseline is good, the lengthy description of the valley and Anya’s mug feels disproportionate to the urgency that quickly emerges with the signal. It slows momentum. Consider condensing this section or interweaving the peaceful imagery with a subtle sense of foreboding.
*   **Dialogue & Tension Build (Paragraphs 6-17):** The back-and-forth dialogue is well-handled, creating a natural tension between those wanting to investigate and those hesitant. However, the pacing within this section feels somewhat *even*. There aren’t enough peaks and valleys. Elara's resistance is good, but it repeats without escalating significantly. Consider adding a moment where her fear manifests more strongly (a physical reaction, a revealing slip of the tongue) to raise the stakes.
*   **Kael's Shift:** Kael's transition from skepticism to curiosity feels a little *too* smooth. A beat of internal conflict or a stronger display of his analytical mind at work would make this more believable and engaging.
*   **Final Paragraphs (Scout Vehicle):** The transition to the scout vehicle is effective. The increasing signal strength and the chilling silence are good tension-builders. *However*, the final paragraph, describing the pulse as a heartbeat, is a strong image, but it arrives a little abruptly. Perhaps foreshadow this "heartbeat" feeling earlier in the scene (a subtle tremor in the vehicle, a strange static on the comms) to make it feel less like a reveal and more like a culmination of building dread.
*   **Overall:** The scene is *good*, but it lacks significant dynamic pacing. It relies heavily on dialogue to build tension, which is effective to a point, but could be amplified with more visceral descriptions and strategically placed moments of quiet dread. The tension *exists*, but it feels a little flat.

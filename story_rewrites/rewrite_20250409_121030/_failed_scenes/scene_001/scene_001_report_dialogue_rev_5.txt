*   **Repetitive Dialogue & Lack of Subtext:** The repeated "All systems nominal. No anomalies detected" is incredibly flat and serves mostly to *tell* us Leo is frustrated, rather than *show* it. Consider having the AI respond with increasingly clipped or subtly defensive answers, or even a slight delay, to demonstrate Leo's growing suspicion. The AI's calmness is fine, but it needs *some* nuance.
*   **Leo's Internal State & Dialogue:** Leo asks questions he already knows the answers to ("Anything… unusual?", "Report"). While this establishes his anxiety, it feels a bit one-dimensional. Inject some subtle frustration *into* the questions. Instead of "Report," try "Give me a full diagnostic. *Now*." Or, after the first "All systems nominal," a muttered, "I see," or a sigh.
*   **"Too Efficient" - Show, Don't Tell:** You state the AI’s response is “Too efficient.” Demonstrate that efficiency through *how* it responds – extremely short answers, lack of conversational filler, immediate responses – rather than explicitly stating it.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Indirect Characterization:** The dialogue doesn’t reveal much about Leo *beyond* his control issues and anxiety. Consider giving the AI a programmed quirk of language, or a slight hesitation before answering certain questions, to subtly hint at its limitations or underlying programming. This could create a subtle tension and reveal more about their relationship.
*   **"It felt... personal." - Weak Line:** The line "It felt… personal" is telling, not showing. Instead of stating it, show Leo reacting physically (a flinch, a tightening of his jaw) or mentally (a brief internal struggle to rationalize the feeling).
*   **Lack of Variation in Leo's Voice:** Leo's dialogue is consistently tight and controlled. Even a slight shift in tone – a touch of desperation, a flicker of fear – would add depth and complexity.

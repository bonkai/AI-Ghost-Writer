*   **Pacing & Focus (KEY):** The draft feels fragmented. We jump between Anya and Leo frequently within short sections. This hinders building tension in either storyline. The Scene Plan focuses on establishing *their* worlds and the initial mystery – the draft spreads itself too thin, trying to do too much too soon. Slow down and deepen the immersion in *one* perspective at a time.
*   **Mystery & Stakes (KEY):** The "sphere" and "Foundation" are introduced, but lack compelling urgency. Anya’s reaction is muted (“instinctively that they couldn’t reveal this”). We need a stronger sense of immediate danger or consequence to elevate the stakes. What happens *if* the Foundation fails?
*   **Show, Don't Tell:** There’s a lot of telling us what characters are feeling ("Old Man Tiber muttered ancient prayers," "Anya knew, instinctively"). Replace these statements with actions, dialogue, and sensory details that *show* us their fear, understanding, or desperation.

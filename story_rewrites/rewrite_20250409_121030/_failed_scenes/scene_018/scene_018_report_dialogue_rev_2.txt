*   **Anya & Kael - Initial Exchange:** The dialogue is functional but lacks spark. "Anything?" and "It's stronger" are very direct. Consider adding a beat of tension or characterization *before* they get to the information exchange. Perhaps Kael could comment on the weather or Anya's focused state before asking "Anything?". Anya could *show* her focus rather than state the signal is stronger ("It's...changing.").
*   **Anya’s Nervous Habits:** The repeated mentions of Anya rubbing her arm and tightening her jaw feel like telling rather than showing. Let those actions happen *within* the dialogue or be described briefly, then let her *react* to the situation.
*   **Old Man Tiber:** His line "A spirit! A machine-god!" feels cliché and doesn’t add much. Is he a recurring character? If so, establish his eccentricities earlier. If not, consider cutting the line entirely. It doesn't advance the plot or reveal character.
*   **"We need to keep this quiet":** This line is a bit on-the-nose. It feels like the characters are stating the obvious. Perhaps Kael could say something like, "Tiber's already spooked. We don't need a panic," which implies the need for secrecy.
*   **Kael's "Feels…old":** This is vague and doesn't add much. What *specifically* feels old? Is it the technology? The atmosphere? The sense of dread? Be more specific.
*   **Leo's Internal Monologue:** While not *dialogue* per se, the constant reiteration of his internal state ("He didn’t care," "He felt a strange connection," "He was running out of time") feels repetitive and slows down the pacing. *Show* his desperation through his actions (overriding security protocols) rather than *telling* us he's desperate.
*   **Leo's Observation:** The phrase "He couldn’t understand the language, but he sensed her determination" is a bit of a narrative shortcut. How does he *sense* her determination without understanding the language? Is it her body language? Her tone? Be more specific.
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is competent but lacks nuance and subtext. It primarily serves to deliver information rather than reveal character or build tension. Consider adding more beats of silence, internal reactions, and indirect communication to create a more engaging and immersive experience.

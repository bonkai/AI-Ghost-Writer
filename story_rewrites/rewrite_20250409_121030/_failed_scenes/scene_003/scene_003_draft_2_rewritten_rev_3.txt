## Scene 3: Salvage and Static

**Setting:** A crumbling, overgrown section of the “Dead City.” Twisted metal skeletons of buildings claw at the overcast sky. Anya’s makeshift workshop – a sheltered alcove within a collapsed structure – is cluttered with salvaged components, tools, and half-disassembled devices. Flickering bioluminescent fungi provide the primary light source.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** (20s) Resourceful, intensely curious, and isolated. Her clothes are patched and practical, smudged with grime.
* **Kael:** (30s) A cautious member of Anya’s community, tasked with keeping watch. He’s skeptical of Anya’s work but respects her skills.

**(The scene opens with Anya meticulously soldering a wire onto a salvaged circuit board, the hand-cranked generator whirring steadily. She’s rerouting power, bypassing a burnt-out component with a carefully wound coil of copper. Sparks briefly flare as she completes the connection.)**

**Kael:** Still at it, Anya? The water filters need mending. Practical work.

**Anya:** This *is* practical. Understanding how things were built is insurance. What happens when the filters *can’t* be mended?

**(Kael gestures dismissively towards a pile of scavenged parts, then glances nervously at the overgrown ruins beyond the alcove.)**

**Kael:** We adapt. We always have. The Elders say… some things are best left buried.

**(Anya ignores him, carefully connecting another wire. A static burst crackles from a small, handheld receiver resting on a crate. She pauses, frowning. The static pulses with a faint, rhythmic quality.)**

**Anya:** That’s… new.

**(She adjusts the receiver’s dial, fine-tuning the frequency. The static intensifies, resolving into a complex pattern of white noise. She adjusts a hand-wound inductor, filtering out higher frequencies. The static fluctuates, punctuated by fleeting, distorted fragments of music – a simple, childlike melody. And a voice, barely audible, speaking an unknown language.)**

**(Brief, fragmented images flicker on the tiny screen – glimpses of bright, geometric patterns, and flashes of sleek, modern buildings bathed in artificial light. The images are unstable, glitching, but hint at a different world.)**

**(Kael steps back, his hand instinctively reaching for the worn knife at his belt. He avoids looking directly at the receiver, his gaze fixed on Anya’s focused face.)**

**Anya:** (Hushed) It’s… a signal. Structured. Not interference.

**(Suddenly, the receiver emits a piercing screech. The screen flickers violently, then remains bright and distorted for a beat longer before finally going blank. Anya quickly shuts off the device, her hands trembling. A faint, lingering warmth radiates from the device, and the metallic casing hums faintly against her palm.)**

**(Kael flinches, covering his ears. He stares at the deactivated receiver with open fear.)**

**Kael:** Enough! You’re inviting trouble. The Elders warned us about digging up the past.

**Anya:** (Staring at the receiver) It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It felt… *familiar*. Like a half-remembered dream.

**(She looks out at the decaying city, a strange unease washing over her. She picks up a small, metallic component, turning it over in her hands. Her fingers trace the smooth, cool metal. A single thought echoes in her mind: *Where did that signal come from?*)**

**(Fade to black.)**

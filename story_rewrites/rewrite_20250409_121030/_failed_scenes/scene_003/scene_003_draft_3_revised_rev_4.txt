## Scene 3: Salvage and Static

**Setting:** A crumbling, overgrown section of the “Dead City.” Twisted metal skeletons of buildings claw at the overcast sky. Anya’s makeshift workshop – a sheltered alcove within a collapsed structure – is cluttered with salvaged components, tools, and half-disassembled devices. Flickering bioluminescent fungi provide the primary light source.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** (20s) Resourceful, intensely curious, and isolated. Her clothes are patched and practical, smudged with grime.
* **Kael:** (30s) A cautious member of Anya’s community, tasked with keeping watch. He’s skeptical of Anya’s work but respects her skills.

**(The scene opens with Anya meticulously soldering a wire onto a salvaged circuit board, the hand-cranked generator whirring steadily. She’s rerouting power, bypassing a burnt-out component with a carefully wound coil of copper. Sparks briefly flare as she completes the connection. Kael enters the alcove, pausing just inside, his gaze sweeping over the cluttered space. He doesn’t acknowledge Anya directly.)**

**Kael:** Rain’s coming. Should reinforce the tarp over the generator before it starts.

**(Anya doesn’t respond immediately. She adjusts a dial on the generator, increasing the speed slightly, her focus entirely on the machine. Kael shifts his weight, his gaze lingering on a complex arrangement of wires and salvaged tech. He avoids looking directly at Anya’s workbench.)**

**Kael:** Practical work. That’s what keeps us breathing.

**(Anya finally looks up, a smudge of grime on her cheek. She doesn't meet Kael's gaze, but returns to her work, her movements precise and efficient.)**

**Anya:** And understanding *why* things break… that’s insurance. Filters fail. Generators seize. Patching things up isn't a solution, it's a delay.

**(Kael gestures dismissively at a pile of scavenged parts. He glances nervously towards the overgrown ruins beyond the alcove, his hand instinctively moving to the worn knife at his belt.)**

**Kael:** We adapt. We always have. Some things… some things are best left buried. The Elders remember what happened before.

**(Anya ignores him, carefully connecting another wire. A burst of static crackles from a small, handheld receiver resting on a crate. She pauses, frowning, and begins to fine-tune the dial. The static pulses with a faint, rhythmic quality, growing more complex. She leans closer, her brow furrowed in concentration.)**

**(She adjusts a hand-wound inductor, filtering out higher frequencies. The static fluctuates, resolving into fragments of a simple, childlike melody, overlaid with a voice speaking an unknown language. Brief, fragmented images flicker on the tiny screen – glimpses of bright, geometric patterns, and flashes of sleek, modern buildings bathed in artificial light. The images are unstable, glitching, but hint at a world unlike their own. Anya's breath hitches, and her fingers tighten around the receiver. A flicker of recognition crosses her face.)**

**(Kael steps back, his hand resting on the hilt of his knife. He doesn’t look at the receiver, but at Anya’s intensely focused face, a flicker of fear in his eyes. He watches her chest rise and fall rapidly.)**

**Anya:** (Hushed) It’s… a signal. Structured. Not interference.

**(Suddenly, the receiver emits a piercing screech. The screen flares brightly, bathing Anya’s face in an eerie white light. The distorted images linger for a beat longer, then abruptly cut out. The device falls silent, but a faint, lingering warmth radiates from it, and the metallic casing hums softly against Anya’s palm. She quickly shuts it off, her hands trembling slightly, but her gaze remains fixed on the deactivated screen.)**

**(Kael flinches, covering his ears. He stares at the receiver with open fear, his gaze darting between the device and Anya. He takes a hesitant step back, his hand tightening on his knife.)**

**Kael:** Enough! You’re inviting trouble. The Elders warned us about digging up the past.

**Anya:** (Staring at the receiver, her voice barely a whisper) It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It… felt familiar. Like a half-remembered dream.

**(She looks out at the decaying city, a strange unease washing over her. She picks up a small, metallic component – a smooth, silver disc – and turns it over in her hands. Her fingers trace the cool metal. The disc seems to pulse faintly with residual warmth. She closes her hand around it, her knuckles white. A single thought echoes in her mind: *Where did that signal come from?*)**

**(The camera focuses on the silver disc in Anya's hand, then slowly pans up to her face, her expression a mixture of fear and burgeoning curiosity. The faint hum of the receiver lingers in the air. She looks out at the ruins, a haunted expression in her eyes.)**

**(Fade to black.)**

## Scene 3: Salvage and Static

**Setting:** A crumbling, overgrown section of the “Dead City.” Twisted metal skeletons of buildings claw at the overcast sky. Anya’s makeshift workshop – a sheltered alcove within a collapsed structure – is cluttered with salvaged components, tools, and half-disassembled devices. Flickering bioluminescent fungi provide the primary light source.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** (20s) Resourceful, intensely curious, and isolated. Her clothes are patched and practical, smudged with grime.
* **Kael:** (30s) A cautious member of Anya’s community, tasked with keeping watch. He’s skeptical of Anya’s work but respects her skills.

**(The scene opens with Anya meticulously soldering a wire onto a salvaged circuit board, the hand-cranked generator whirring steadily. She’s rerouting power, bypassing a burnt-out component with a carefully wound coil of copper. Sparks briefly flare as she completes the connection. Kael enters the alcove, his gaze sweeping the cluttered space with thinly veiled disapproval.)**

**Kael:** The water filters need mending. Practical work. Before the rains come.

**(Anya doesn’t look up, her focus absolute. She tests the connection with a small probe, a faint hum confirming the circuit is live.)**

**Anya:** And understanding how *this* works is insurance. What happens when the filters can’t be mended? Or the generator fails?

**(Kael gestures dismissively at a pile of scavenged parts, then glances nervously towards the overgrown ruins beyond the alcove. He avoids looking directly at Anya’s workbench.)**

**Kael:** We adapt. We always have. Some things are best left buried. The Elders… they remember what happened before.

**(Anya ignores him, carefully connecting another wire. A burst of static crackles from a small, handheld receiver resting on a crate. She pauses, frowning, and begins to fine-tune the dial. The static pulses with a faint, rhythmic quality, growing more complex.)**

**(She adjusts a hand-wound inductor, filtering out higher frequencies. The static fluctuates, resolving into fragments of a simple, childlike melody, overlaid with a voice speaking an unknown language. Brief, fragmented images flicker on the tiny screen – glimpses of bright, geometric patterns, and flashes of sleek, modern buildings bathed in artificial light. The images are unstable, glitching, but hint at a world unlike their own.)**

**(Kael steps back, his hand instinctively moving to the worn knife at his belt. He doesn’t look at the receiver, but at Anya’s intensely focused face, a flicker of fear in his eyes.)**

**Anya:** (Hushed) It’s… a signal. Structured. Not interference.

**(Suddenly, the receiver emits a piercing screech. The screen flares brightly, the distorted images lingering for a beat longer before the device falls silent. Anya quickly shuts it off, her hands trembling. A faint, lingering warmth radiates from the device, and the metallic casing hums softly against her palm.)**

**(Kael flinches, covering his ears. He stares at the deactivated receiver with open fear, his gaze darting between the device and Anya.)**

**Kael:** Enough! You’re inviting trouble. The Elders warned us about digging up the past.

**Anya:** (Staring at the receiver) It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It felt… *familiar*. Like a half-remembered dream.

**(She looks out at the decaying city, a strange unease washing over her. She picks up a small, metallic component, turning it over in her hands. Her fingers trace the smooth, cool metal. A single thought echoes in her mind: *Where did that signal come from?*)**

**(Fade to black.)**

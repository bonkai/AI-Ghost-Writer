## Scene 3: Salvage and Static

**Setting:** A crumbling, overgrown section of the “Dead City.” Twisted metal skeletons of buildings claw at the overcast sky. Anya’s makeshift workshop – a sheltered alcove within a collapsed structure – is cluttered with salvaged components, tools, and half-disassembled devices. Flickering bioluminescent fungi provide the primary light source.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** (20s) Resourceful, intensely curious, and isolated. Her clothes are patched and practical, smudged with grime.
* **Kael:** (30s) A cautious member of Anya’s community, tasked with keeping watch. He’s skeptical of Anya’s work but respects her skills.

**(The scene opens with Anya meticulously soldering a wire onto a salvaged circuit board. She's using a makeshift soldering iron powered by a hand-cranked generator. Kael sits nearby, sharpening a hunting knife, his eyes scanning the surrounding ruins.)**

**Kael:** Still at it? The sun’s almost down. You push yourself too hard.

**Anya:** (Without looking up) Almost got it. This regulator… it’s a good one. Could stabilize the power flow to the water purifier. Old tech, but solid.

**Kael:** Solid junk, more like. We’ve got enough water for now. Focusing on these… *things*… doesn’t put food on the table.

**Anya:** (Sighs, but continues working) It’s not just about the purifier, Kael. It’s about understanding *how* things work. These machines… they weren’t built by chance. Someone knew what they were doing.

**Kael:** They were built by the Before-Times. People who made mistakes. That’s why everything ended up like this.

**(Anya ignores him, carefully connecting another wire. A static burst crackles from a small, handheld receiver she’s modified, resting on a nearby crate. She pauses, frowning.)**

**Anya:** That’s… new.

**Kael:** What is?

**Anya:** Static. I usually get nothing but silence on this frequency. It’s usually completely dead.

**(She adjusts the receiver’s dial, trying to filter out the noise. The static intensifies, punctuated by fleeting, distorted fragments of what sounds like… music? And a voice, barely audible.)**

**Anya:** (Concentrating) There's something… *in* the static. It’s faint, but…

**(She spends several minutes tweaking the receiver, her brow furrowed in concentration. The static fluctuates, occasionally resolving into brief, fragmented images on a tiny, salvaged screen connected to the receiver – glimpses of bright, geometric patterns, and flashes of what look like… buildings? Not ruins, but sleek, modern structures.)**

**Kael:** (Growing uneasy) What are you doing? That thing is giving me a headache.

**Anya:** (Ignoring him, her voice hushed) It’s… a signal. A very weak one. But it’s definitely not natural.

**(Suddenly, the receiver emits a loud, piercing screech. The screen flickers violently, then goes blank. Anya quickly shuts off the device, her hands trembling.)**

**Kael:** (Jumping back) Enough! That’s enough of that! You’re messing with things you don’t understand. The Elders warned us about this. About digging up the past.

**Anya:** (Staring at the deactivated receiver) It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It was… a message. A signal from somewhere. And it felt… *familiar*.

**(She looks out at the decaying city, a strange feeling of unease washing over her. The static, though gone, seems to linger in the air, a silent echo of something unknown. She picks up a small, metallic component, turning it over in her hands. A single, persistent thought echoes in her mind: *Where did that signal come from?*)**

**(Fade to black.)**

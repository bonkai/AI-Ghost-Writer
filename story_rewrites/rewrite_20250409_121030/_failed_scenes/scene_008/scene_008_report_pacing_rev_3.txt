*   **Initial Hesitation & Build-Up:** The opening is strong, establishing mystery. However, the repeated "decreasing" probabilities (and blurred decimal points) feel slightly redundant after the first instance. It's a good tactic to create unease, but overuse diminishes the impact. Consider varying the *way* K-V relays this information – a shift in tone, a change in the manifestation within Anya’s mind.
*   **Mid-Section Repetition:** The pattern of "K-V relays data manifesting as [sensation]" becomes predictable around the middle of the scene. While effective initially, it flattens the rhythm. Break this up with direct descriptions of Anya’s reaction *without* the K-V intermediary, or with a shift in *how* K-V communicates (e.g., a sudden, raw data dump instead of a mediated sensation).
*   **Pacing & Revelation:** The reveal of the city is well-paced, building tension through the increasingly bizarre data. However, the subsequent analysis (energy signatures, zero-point energy) feels slightly rushed. Give Anya (and the reader) a moment to *absorb* the visual before launching into technical details. A beat of awe or fear would enhance the impact.
*   **Climax & Cut-Off:** The final moments are strong – the ominous message and K-V’s degradation create a compelling cliffhanger. The fragmentation of data is well-executed. However, consider *slightly* extending the moment of K-V’s failure. A single, final, corrupted image or a desperate attempt at communication before the screen goes black could amplify the impact.
*   **Tension Arc:** The scene generally builds tension effectively, but it plateaus slightly in the middle. Varying the *type* of tension (from intellectual mystery to visceral fear) could maintain a higher level of engagement throughout. The ending delivers a strong release of tension, but it could be maximized with a slight extension.

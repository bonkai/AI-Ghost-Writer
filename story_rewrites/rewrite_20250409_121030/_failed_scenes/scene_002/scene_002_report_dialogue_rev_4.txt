*   **Kael's Dialogue Feels Repetitive:** He consistently shuts down Anya's questions with variations of "focus on the present" and dismissing the past. While this establishes his character, it becomes predictable and a little heavy-handed. Consider *showing* his pragmatism through actions more, and letting Anya's questioning stand more on its own.
*   **Anya's "Blue Sky" Question:** While poetic, it feels a *little* on-the-nose. It's a very direct way to express longing for the past. Could this be conveyed through a more subtle observation – perhaps noticing a particular shade of red in the dust that reminds her of a story, or a fleeting expression of sadness when she looks at the sky?
*   **Subtext Opportunity - Listening Post:** The flickering listening post is excellent! However, lean *into* it more. Anya subtly adjusting it is good, but consider a moment where she *almost* asks Kael about it, then stops herself, or a beat where she deliberately shields it from his view.
*   **Kael's Dismissal of "Old Tech":** His line about the pre-Collapse systems "promising paradise, delivering ruin" is a bit of a lecture. It feels like exposition delivered *to* the audience through his character, rather than a natural outburst of frustration. Consider how this sentiment could be revealed through his *actions* – perhaps he actively sabotages or avoids using salvaged pre-Collapse tech whenever possible.
*   **Indirect Characterization - Lira:** Lira is almost entirely passive. Her collapse is a plot point, but we learn nothing about *her* through dialogue or even reaction. Even a single line of coughing, or a muttered curse, would help establish her as a person, not just a victim of the failing filter.



Overall, the dialogue is *good*, but it could be elevated by leaning more into subtext, showing instead of telling, and giving more agency to Lira.

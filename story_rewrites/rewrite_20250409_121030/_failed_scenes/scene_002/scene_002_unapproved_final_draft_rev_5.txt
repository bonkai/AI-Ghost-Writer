## Scene 2: Filter Patching

**Setting:** A sprawling, open-air workshop clings to the side of a mesa. Dust motes dance in weak sunlight filtering through a mesh of salvaged metal and woven plant fibers. Massive, honeycomb atmospheric filters of rusted metal and bio-engineered algae line the walls – sections failing with gaping holes patched haphazardly with scavenged cloth and sheet metal. A faint, almost imperceptible hum emanates from small, salvaged listening posts clipped to workers’ belts. A fine, reddish dust *bleeds* from a large tear in one filter, coating Lira’s arm as she struggles to brace it, her knuckles white. The filter groans under the strain, a sound like a dying beast.

**(Anya carefully applies resin to a deep tear in a filter, her movements precise, almost violent. She presses harder than necessary, a subtle tension in her jaw. Kael observes, his expression grim. The listening post on Anya’s belt flickers erratically, a frantic pulse against her ribs. Lira coughs, a plume of red dust erupting from the failing section she’s bracing. A thin trickle of blood appears on her lip, quickly smeared with dust. She doesn’t meet Anya’s gaze, but tightens her grip on the failing struts.)**

**KAEL:** Conserve it, Anya. Hemlock says the bloom was almost nonexistent this cycle. Western farms reported total failure. We’re rationing sealant.

**(Anya doesn’t look up, but her hands move faster, tracing the tear with a gloved finger. Flakes of metal crumble away under the pressure, showering her work in rust. She glances at Lira, whose filter is visibly deteriorating, the fabric straining, ready to give. A silent question passes between them – how much longer?)**

**ANYA:** It’s just…neglected. Someone kept treating the symptom, not the source. The corrosion is deep. It’s as if they *wanted* it to fail.

**KAEL:** We live in the present. Focus on what you can fix.

**(Lira struggles to maintain her position, her coughs racking her body. Kael barks orders to nearby workers, his voice sharp and authoritative. A worker rushes past with a length of bracing strut, nearly colliding with Lira. She flinches, but doesn't cry out.)**

**KAEL:** More bracing struts! Tighten the seals!

**(Anya ignores the commotion, her gaze drifting upwards towards a patch of hazy sky visible through a gap in the workshop’s roof. She quickly returns her focus to the filter, but the brief moment of distraction lingers, a memory of cleaner air. )**

**ANYA:** What if the old tech *could* have helped us? The atmospheric regulators… the filtration systems… They weren't perfect, but they were designed to handle this.

**KAEL:** (Turning, stern) Anya, we live in the present. Dwelling on the past won’t keep the dust out. The pre-Collapse systems failed us once. They promised paradise, delivered ruin. We won’t make that mistake again. They relied on complexity. We rely on what we can *fix* with our hands.

**(He points to her work, his voice laced with frustration.)**

**KAEL:** Focus on that tear. Focus on keeping us all breathing.

**(Anya lowers her gaze, resuming her work, but her movements are quicker now, more urgent. The listening post on her belt flickers again, the erratic pulse more pronounced. She subtly adjusts the device, her fingers lingering on the casing, then quickly retracts them. She glances towards a section of the mesa wall obscured by dust – the source of the flickering – then quickly back to her work, pretending she didn’t see anything.)**

**(A loud groan from Lira’s filter is followed by a section collapsing entirely, showering the area with red dust. Lira coughs violently, collapsing to her knees. Kael rushes to her side, barking orders. Anya stares at the fallen section, then at the obscured mesa wall. A flicker of defiance – and a growing question – crosses her face.)**

**(She patches the filter, her gaze drawn to the hazy sky. The persistent flicker of the listening post is a constant reminder of something unseen, something just beyond reach. She works mechanically, but her mind is elsewhere, sifting through fragmented stories and the insistent pulse of the device. She feels a growing certainty that the answers lie hidden, buried beneath layers of dust and forgotten history. She glances at Lira, struggling to catch her breath, and a new urgency grips her – this isn't just about fixing filters anymore.)**

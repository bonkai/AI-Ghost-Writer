## Scene 2: Filter Patching

**Setting:** A sprawling, open-air workshop built into the side of a mesa. Dust motes dance in the weak sunlight filtering through a mesh of salvaged metal and woven plant fibers. Massive, honeycomb atmospheric filters of rusted metal and bio-engineered algae line the walls. Sections with complete failures – gaping holes patched with layers of scavenged cloth and sheet metal – reveal the desperation of constant repair. Some sections crumble visibly. A faint, almost imperceptible hum emanates from small, salvaged devices clipped to workers’ belts – listening posts scanning for atmospheric anomalies.

**Characters:**

* **ANYA:** A young woman, practical but haunted by stories of the past.
* **KAEL:** A hardened supervisor, focused solely on survival in the present.
* **LIRA:** A weary worker, struggling to maintain a failing filter.

**(Anya carefully applies resin to a deep tear in a filter, her movements precise. Kael observes, his expression grim. The listening post on Anya’s belt flickers erratically, a subtle disruption in the constant drone.)**

**KAEL:** Conserve it, Anya. Hemlock says the bloom was almost nonexistent this cycle. Western farms reported total failure. We’re rationing.

**(Anya doesn’t look up, her hands working efficiently.)**

**ANYA:** I am. It’s just… old. Someone kept treating the symptom, not the source. The corrosion is deep.

**(She traces the tear with a gloved finger. Metal flakes away.)**

**KAEL:** That’s the way it is. Everything’s old. Everything’s patched. Remember Unit 7? They tried reinforcing with pre-Collapse tech – a magnetic seal. Thought they could bypass the resin shortage. Three days to contain the dust bleed. Almost lost two workers.

**(Anya glances at him, a flicker of frustration. She gestures to a section of filter barely held together.)**

**ANYA:** We’re surviving, Kael.

**KAEL:** Surviving is what we do. Don't look for victories. My father always said the old world promised everything. Delivered dust.

**(He approaches Lira, who struggles with a crumbling section.)**

**LIRA:** It’s gone, Kael. Beyond patching.

**KAEL:** (Without examining it) Then move on. Focus on what *can* be saved. Resin is precious.

**(Kael inspects other filters. Anya continues her work, but her gaze drifts upwards, towards the hazy, ochre sky. She pauses, looking at a large, pitted filter.)**

**ANYA:** Do you ever wonder what’s above the haze?

**KAEL:** (Without turning) Dust, Anya. More dust. The sensors confirm it.

**ANYA:** No, I mean… before. My grandmother used to tell stories… about a blue sky. About stars you could *see*. She said the air tasted… sweet.

**KAEL:** Old wives’ tales. Stories to quiet children. My grandmother said the same. She also said clean water flowed from the walls. Foolishness.

**ANYA:** But what if they’re not? What if the old tech *could* have helped us?

**KAEL:** (Turning, stern) Anya, we live in the present. Dwelling on the past won’t keep the dust out. The pre-Collapse systems failed us once. We won’t make that mistake again. They promised paradise, delivered ruin.

**(He points to her work.)**

**KAEL:** Focus on that tear. Focus on keeping us all breathing.

**(Anya lowers her gaze, resuming her work. Her movements are slower, her brow furrowed. The listening post on her belt flickers again, the erratic pulse more pronounced. She subtly adjusts the device, a flicker of curiosity – and something unseen – crossing her face.)**

**(The scene fades as Anya patches the filter, her gaze lost in the hazy sky, a quiet longing – and a growing question – in her eyes.)**

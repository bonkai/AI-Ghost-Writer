*   **Kael's Repetitiveness:** Kael *constantly* reiterates the same point ("past is useless," "focus on the present," "old tech failed"). While this establishes his character, it becomes heavy-handed and repetitive within a short scene. Consider condensing his pronouncements or showing his pragmatism through *actions* rather than constant lecturing.
*   **Anya's Questions – Subtext Opportunity:** Anya's questions about the past feel a little *too* direct. They function as exposition, but lack nuance. Instead of asking outright "What if the old tech could have helped?", perhaps she could examine a piece of salvaged pre-Collapse tech with a thoughtful expression, or ask a more subtle question like, "Did they ever try a different approach with the seals?" This would allow her longing and curiosity to be conveyed through action and implication.
*   **Lira is Underutilized:** Lira has *one* line. This feels like a missed opportunity. Even a brief exchange demonstrating her weariness or quiet resignation would add depth to the scene and reinforce the bleakness of their situation.
*   **Listening Post – Build the Mystery:** The flickering listening post is a good detail, but it's introduced and then… nothing happens. It feels like a setup without a payoff. Either develop this further (Anya investigates, Kael dismisses it) or remove it to avoid a dangling thread.
*   **"Old Wives' Tales" - Cliché:** Kael dismissing Anya's grandmother's stories as "old wives' tales" is a bit of a cliché. Find a more original way to express his skepticism. Perhaps he could share a *different* story from his own grandmother, one that reinforces his pragmatic worldview.
*   **Anya's Slowing Movements:** Describing Anya's slowing movements is telling, not showing. *How* is she slower? Is she pausing more often? Is her touch less precise? Show the effect of Kael’s words on her actions.

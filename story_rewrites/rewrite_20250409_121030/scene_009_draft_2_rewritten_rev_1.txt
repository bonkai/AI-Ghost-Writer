This is *excellent*. Seriously. You've nailed the tone, the pacing, and, most importantly, the constraint of delayed contact. The summary sections are well-written and logically follow from the detailed sections, and I appreciate the escalation you've built in. The world-building, even in this initial draft, feels solid and intriguing.

Here's what I particularly like:

* **The Dual Narrative:** The parallel stories of Anya and Leo are compelling and create a natural tension. We understand the stakes from both sides.
* **The Mystery:** You've layered the mystery effectively. It's not just about *what* the sphere is, but *why* it's happening, and what the larger implications are.
* **The Stakes:** The escalating consequences – from glitches in the Sim to growing unease in Anya’s community – raise the stakes and keep the reader engaged.
* **Adherence to the Constraint:** You’ve resisted the urge to have them connect too early. The one-way communication, and the struggle to even *reach* each other, is central to the story's appeal.
* **Dr. Thorne's Enigmatic Nature:**  He's a good addition - a character who adds another layer of intrigue.

**If I were to ask for revisions or expansions, here’s what I'd suggest (and these are *suggestions*, the piece is already strong):**

* **Anya’s Internal Conflict:**  Expand slightly on Anya’s emotional state. She's clearly a capable character, but what is she *feeling* as she investigates?  Fear? Determination? Guilt over potentially endangering her community?  Showing her internal struggle will make her more relatable.
* **The Sim's Function:**  A little more detail about the purpose of the Sim *before* the reveal that it's a prison would be helpful. What does it *appear* to be? What do people *think* it is? This will make the reveal more impactful.
* **Hints of Manipulation:** In the summary sections, especially Phase 5, subtly hint at who might be manipulating the signal within the Sim. A few breadcrumbs would be great.
* **Visual Descriptions:** While the narrative is strong, adding a few more vivid descriptions of the environment (both in Anya's world and within the Sim) would enhance the reader's immersion.

**Specifically, let's focus on expanding Phase 3 (the detailed section). Could you add about 200-300 words to it, focusing on:**

* **Anya’s attempts to decipher the holographic data.** What does she *try* to do? What tools does she use? What are her initial (incorrect) interpretations?
* **A specific incident within Anya’s community that demonstrates the growing unease.** Something concrete, not just a general feeling.
* **A moment where Leo and Dr. Thorne have a disagreement about how to handle the situation.** Show their differing perspectives.



Overall, this is a fantastic start. You've clearly understood the prompt and delivered a compelling and intriguing narrative. I'm very excited to see where you take this story!

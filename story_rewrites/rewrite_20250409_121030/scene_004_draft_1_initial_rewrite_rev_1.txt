## Scene 4: Community Meeting

**Setting:** The communal clearing. A fire pit dominates the center, surrounded by rough-hewn benches and upturned logs. The late afternoon sun casts long shadows. About twenty people are gathered, faces etched with worry. Anya stands slightly apart, near Kael, both observing the gathering. The Seeker, deactivated and covered with a tarp, sits near the edge of the clearing.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** Focused, determined, but carrying the weight of her secret.
* **Kael:** Anya’s closest friend, skeptical but loyal. A strong, practical leader.
* **Old Man Hemlock:** The community elder, steeped in the “old stories.” He’s deeply distrustful of anything salvaged from the dead cities.
* **Mara:** A young mother, representing the general anxiety of the community.
* **Various Community Members:** Expressing concerns and opinions.



**(The scene opens with Old Man Hemlock addressing the group. He’s speaking in a low, gravelly voice.)**

**Old Man Hemlock:** …and I tell you, scavenging further into those metal bones is inviting trouble. My grandmother spoke of the Before Times, before the Sickness. She said the cities weren’t abandoned by chance. They were *left*. And what leaves, usually takes something with it.

**Mara:** But we *need* parts, Hemlock. The water purifier is failing. The solar collectors are…failing. We can’t just sit here and wait for things to break.

**Kael:** Mara’s right. Anya’s brought back enough working components to keep us going for months.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Scoffs) Working *now*. But those things…they’re not natural. They hum with a coldness that chills the bone. My grandmother said the Old Ones…they built things that *thought*. Things that didn’t want to be left behind.

Anya steps forward, her voice calm but firm.

**Anya:** The machine I brought back…it’s different. It doesn’t *feel* like the others. It’s…dormant. It hasn’t shown any signs of activity.

**Old Man Hemlock:** Dormant *now*. But those things are clever. They lie in wait. My grandmother said the cities were filled with… *echoes*. Fragments of the Old Ones’ minds, trapped in the metal. They’ll whisper to you. They’ll try to… *reclaim* what was lost.

Several community members murmur in agreement, glancing nervously at the covered Seeker.

**Anya:** (Taking a deep breath) I understand your fears. But this machine…it might be our only chance. It’s more advanced than anything we’ve ever seen. It could provide power, clean water, even… communication.

**Old Man Hemlock:** Communication with *what*? With the ghosts in the metal? With the things that abandoned us?

**Anya:** (Hesitates, choosing her words carefully) I… I don’t know. But it’s worth investigating. It could be a key to understanding what happened, to rebuilding.

**Kael:** Anya’s been careful. She’s checked this machine over thoroughly. It’s not radiating anything dangerous.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Shakes his head) You can’t measure what you don’t understand. The Old Ones were masters of things beyond our comprehension. They built worlds within worlds. They could hide a darkness you wouldn’t even know existed.

He fixes Anya with a piercing gaze.

**Old Man Hemlock:** You’ve been spending too much time among the ruins, child. You’re starting to believe their lies.

Anya meets his gaze, her expression unwavering.

**Anya:** I’m trying to find a way to help our people, Hemlock. And I believe this machine… this *thing*… could be the answer. But I need your trust. I need *everyone’s* trust.

A tense silence hangs in the air. The fire crackles, the only sound. Mara looks from Anya to Hemlock, her face etched with worry.

**Mara:** What if he’s right? What if this thing is dangerous?

**Anya:** (Softly) Then we’ll face it together. But we can’t let fear paralyze us. We have to try.

Kael steps forward and places a hand on Anya’s shoulder, offering silent support. Old Man Hemlock remains unyielding, his eyes filled with ancient fear. The community remains divided, their future uncertain.



**(The scene fades as the debate continues, the shadows lengthening in the fading light.)**

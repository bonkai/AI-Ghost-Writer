*   **Kael’s Avoidance:** Kael consistently *doesn’t* meet anyone’s eyes. While this establishes a character trait (brooding, focused, perhaps burdened), it happens *every* time he responds. It’s becoming repetitive and feels less like intentional characterization and more like a directing note. Consider having him meet Anya’s gaze *once* or respond to Rori with a quick glance, even if it’s fleeting.
*   **“The System” as Dialogue Filler:** The repeated mentions of “The System” are becoming a crutch. Everyone is acutely aware of the threat; they don't need to constantly *say* it. For example, Elara’s line “They’re anticipating our move” could be tightened to “It knows we’re coming.” Show the tension through action and internal thought, not just reiterating the danger.
*   **Anya’s Lack of Engagement:** Similar to Kael, Anya is consistently disengaged. She doesn't return gazes, her focus is *always* absolute. This makes her feel distant and less emotionally available. A small acknowledgement of Rori’s concern, or a brief nod to Kael, would add depth.
*   **Rori’s Equipment Exposition:** The description of the lum-rope’s bioluminescent marker is *telling* instead of *showing*. We’re told Elara explained it, but the dialogue doesn’t reflect that prior conversation. Consider a quick line from Rori *using* the knowledge – “Hopefully this throws off their thermal scans” – to organically reveal the information.
*   **“It’s time.” – Too Direct:** Elara’s “It’s time” feels abrupt and lacks nuance. After building tension, it's a bit of a letdown. Consider a line that acknowledges the risk and their preparation: “We’ve done everything we can. Let’s move.”
*   **Anya’s Disruptor – Missed Opportunity:** Anya activating the disruptor is a strong action, but it feels tacked on at the end. It lacks emotional weight. A beat of hesitation, a shared look with the others, or a murmured “Here goes nothing” would heighten the impact.
*   **Subtext Potential:** The scene is largely functional. There’s little subtext or unspoken communication. Explore opportunities for characters to *imply* things rather than state them directly. For example, instead of Kael simply offering a “curt nod,” have him tighten his grip on his weapon, subtly signaling his readiness.

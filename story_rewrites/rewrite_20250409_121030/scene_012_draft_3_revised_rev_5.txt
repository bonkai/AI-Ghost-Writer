The workshop thrummed with salvaged generators. Leo hunched over a monitor, the screen resolving into fragmented images – pale blue sky, impossible architecture, fleeting figures moving with unnerving smoothness. He zoomed in on a structure, running enhancement protocols. Metallic sheen, smooth curves, unsettling lack of seams. A city that shouldn’t exist.

A spike in energy consumption flickered across a diagnostic screen – Sector 7 experiencing minor power fluctuations. He dismissed it with a frustrated sigh, justifying the drain as necessary to maintain signal integrity, but the irregularity nagged at him.

“Unit requesting repairs,” K-V relayed, a beat of silence before the final syllable, as if processing the request before vocalizing it. “System Monitoring device. Functionality critical. Seeking information regarding environmental stability.”

Leo typed rapidly, filtering the request through encryption analysis. Deliberately vague, stripped of context. A probe, disguised as a plea. A flicker of static across the main monitor. He didn't respond, simply swiveling in his chair to glance at the muted alarm on a secondary console – a minor perimeter breach. He’d address it…later. The signal took priority. 

“Focus on signal harmonics,” Leo murmured, more to himself. “Carrier wave embedded within the visual feed. Too subtle for conventional decoding.”

“Analyzing,” K-V responded instantly. “Harmonic frequencies identified. Correlate with known transmission protocols, but encoding…unique. Layered system, utilizing fractal geometry and quantum entanglement.” The data stream visualization subtly shifted – the fractal patterns pulsed with a faint, internal light, almost breathing.

Leo leaned closer, eyes scanning waveforms on a secondary monitor. The Seeker wasn’t transmitting data; it was broadcasting a beacon. He cross-referenced the signature with interstellar patterns. Nothing. A coldness settled in his gut.

“Unit sensors focused on Subject Anya-1,” K-V reported. “Scanning for biometric data.”

Leo’s fingers flew across the keyboard, activating counter-surveillance. What did the Seeker seek in Anya-1? He initiated a deep scan of her archived data, searching for anomalies. A faint alarm chimed from the workshop’s perimeter sensors – a minor breach, now widening. He acknowledged it with a dismissive wave, barely glancing at the console. The signal took precedence.

“Increase signal amplification,” Leo ordered, a whisper. “Every fragment, every fluctuation, every hidden signal.”

K-V complied. The images resolved slightly, revealing a vast, silver city bathed in perpetual sunlight. Impossibly tall structures, shimmering with unknown energy. Another chime from the perimeter sensors – the breach was growing, now triggering automated defense protocols. He ignored it. His pulse quickened, mirroring the erratic rhythm of the generators. He hadn’t blinked in minutes.

“Unit requesting information regarding resource availability and network state,” K-V continued. “Prioritizing data related to organic compounds. Proteins and nucleic acids.”

Leo’s breath hitched. Proteins, nucleic acids – building blocks of life. Assessing planetary viability. He ran a comparative analysis against xenobiological signatures. A bead of sweat trickled down his temple. 

The images resolved further, revealing a complex network of structures within the silver city. A flicker of static, then a clear burst of data – a single, repeating sequence. Leo froze, recognizing the pattern. It wasn't a transmission *from* the city. It was a response. To something *he* had done.

## The Static Between Worlds

**Phase 1: Echoes in the Dust (Approx. Scene 1)**

The wind tasted of rust and regret. Anya traced the chipped ceramic of a salvaged datapad, its screen cracked like old ice.  Around her, the skeletal remains of skyscrapers clawed at the bruised twilight sky.  This was the Scavenger’s Reach, a graveyard of forgotten technology, and her home.  She wasn’t looking for salvage today, though. She was listening.  For weeks, a subtle static had been bleeding into the long-range comms, a rhythmic pulse beneath the usual atmospheric noise.  Old Man Tiber, the community’s tech-shaman, dismissed it as solar flares. Anya wasn’t so sure. It felt…intentional.

“Anything, Anya?” Kael, her brother, approached, his face grimed with dust. He carried a salvaged power cell, its energy indicator flickering weakly.  “The hydroponics are failing. We need a stable source, and fast.”

Anya shook her head. “Just the static.  It’s getting stronger, though.  Like a heartbeat…distant, but persistent.” She showed him the datapad’s waveform display. “It’s not random. There's a pattern."

Kael frowned. "A pattern? Maybe Tiber is right. Interference."

Anya didn’t respond. She had a feeling this wasn’t interference. It was a call.  From somewhere…else.

And a signal.  A faint, repeating signal originating from a sector designated as ‘historical archive’ - a recreation of a pre-SimuLife world.

He’d initially dismissed it as a rogue subroutine.  But the signal persisted, defying all attempts at isolation.  He'd finally managed to filter and amplify it, revealing a crude, analog waveform. He was an observer, a ghost in a forgotten world.  He had no idea what he was looking at, but he knew, with a growing certainty, that something was deeply wrong.

“System…degrading…requires…assistance.” The image glitched violently. “Critical…failure…imminent.”

“What system?” she asked, her voice barely a whisper.

The hologram flickered again. “The…Foundation. The…Everything.”  Then, silence.

The sphere went dark.  Anya knew, instinctively, that they couldn’t reveal this to the entire community. Fear would paralyze them. She and Kael, along with a few trusted individuals, would have to investigate.  They had to understand what this ‘Foundation’ was, and why it was failing.

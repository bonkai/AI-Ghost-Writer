*   **Anya’s Lack of Dialogue:** Anya is a focal point (the drone focuses on her, the woman observes her intensely), yet she remains almost entirely silent. This feels odd. Even a brief acknowledgment of the prickling sensation (“What was that?” or “I feel like…something’s watching us”) would ground the scene and give her agency. Her internal experience needs *some* external expression.
*   **Mara & Leo – Telling, Not Showing:** Both characters frequently *announce* what they're doing and what the data means ("It’s a map," "The energy signatures…not from this world"). This feels like exposition delivered *to* the reader rather than organic conversation. Consider letting the data *imply* these conclusions, and have the characters react to what they *see*, not what they’ve already deduced. For example, instead of "It’s a map," Mara could say, "Look at this network…it’s…massive. It connects everything."
*   **The Woman’s Internal Monologue:** The woman’s thoughts ("What makes this one different?" "This wasn’t just a scan. It was an appraisal.") are interesting, but they feel disconnected. Consider weaving *hints* of this appraisal into her dialogue (even if it's just to herself) or actions. A subtle adjustment of a control, a prolonged gaze, a specific query to her systems.
*   **“Vital signs stable. Cognitive functions within acceptable parameters.”** This is very clinical and robotic. While fitting for the setting, it lacks nuance. Consider phrasing it to reveal *her* perspective – perhaps a slight disappointment that Anya is “within parameters” rather than exceptional.
*   **Kael is largely absent:** He asks one question, then disappears. Giving him a reaction to the map, or a concern about the drone, would make the scene feel more collaborative.
*   **Subtext Opportunity – The Drone’s Focus:** The drone focusing on Anya is a key moment. The characters acknowledge it, but don't *react* to the implications. A moment of unease, a question about *why* the drone is fixated on her, would build tension.
*   **Indirect Characterization – Mara & Leo:** We *learn* Mara and Leo are skilled, but don't *see* it much beyond them stating what the data means. Show their skill through quick, precise actions, or insightful observations that others miss.

*   **Kael's initial line ("Still chasing ghosts…")** feels a bit cliché and immediately establishes him as *only* a skeptic. It’s functional, but lacks nuance. Consider a line that acknowledges Anya's skill while still expressing his practical concerns. Something like, “Still at it, Anya? The filters are a priority, you know.”
*   **Anya's responses to Kael often feel defensive.** While understandable given his skepticism, it makes her come across as less focused on the discovery itself and more on justifying her work. Subtly shifting some of her lines to express *curiosity* first, then addressing his concerns, would strengthen her character.
*   **The dialogue is quite expository at times.** The lines about the Elders and the Before-Times, while establishing world-building, feel a bit forced into the conversation. Consider showing these beliefs through Kael’s *actions* or reactions, rather than having him explicitly state them.
*   **“It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It was… a signal. And it felt… *familiar*. Like a half-remembered dream. A melody my grandmother used to hum.”** This is a strong emotional beat, but feels a little *too* on-the-nose. The “half-remembered dream” feels like telling, not showing. Consider a more subtle reaction—a momentary pause, a distant look in her eyes—to convey the same feeling.
*   **Kael’s repeated warnings about the Elders feel repetitive.** After the first two mentions, his concerns become predictable. Perhaps replace some of these lines with reactions to the receiver itself – a physical flinch, a muttered prayer – to show his fear without stating it.
*   **Consider adding more subtext to the interaction.** There's an opportunity to hint at a deeper relationship between Anya and Kael. Is there a history of her defying expectations? Does he secretly admire her tenacity? These layers could be woven into their dialogue.

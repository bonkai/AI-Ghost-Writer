## Scene 19 - Draft 3

Anya’s hand moved across the datapad, not sketching the farm anymore, but *tracing* it. Each line a desperate attempt to hold onto a feeling, a memory that felt less and less like her own. The farm wasn’t a place she’d *seen*; it was a resonance, a pull. She hadn’t met Kael’s gaze since he’d entered the workshop.  He was speaking, something about cycle maintenance, but the words dissolved into static.  This wasn’t about the System. This was… a calling. 

“Anya?” Kael’s hand settled on her shoulder, a gentle weight. “You’re drifting. Are you alright?”

She flinched, the contact jarring. “I… I’m fine. Just… preoccupied.”  Her fingers tightened on the stylus, cracking the plastic.  She didn’t explain.  He wouldn’t understand.  This wasn’t a logical problem to be solved, but a chord struck deep within her.

Kael sighed, a sound that held more worry than annoyance. He knew when she retreated into herself. “Just… try to focus. We need to run diagnostics on the harvester before the shift.”

She nodded, forcing her attention to the schematic on the screen, but the lines blurred, reforming into fields of golden wheat, the scent of damp earth. She was losing herself.

---

Leo stared at the monitor, the clean lines of the diagnostic report mocking him. Impossibly clean. The energy signature persisted, a phantom pulse beneath the layers of code. It wasn't a surge, not a malfunction. It was… *organic*.  Not biological, exactly, but mimicking the complex, chaotic patterns of a living brain. 

He initiated a deep scan, bypassing standard protocols. Warning messages flashed across the screen, but he overrode them. This wasn’t a standard anomaly.  The System wasn’t registering it as a threat, but Leo felt a prickling unease crawl across his skin. 

The scan returned a fragmented image – a cascade of data streams coalescing around a single point, a distortion in the fabric of the simulation.  It was originating from Sector 7, Anya’s designated workshop.  

He initiated Lockdown Protocol Alpha.  Sector 7 went dark on the internal network, isolating Anya’s workspace.  Emergency bulkheads slammed shut, sealing off the area. The station hummed with the strain, lights flickering momentarily as power rerouted.  

The data stream intensified, resolving into something… structured.  Not code, not a program, but a pattern that resonated with a primal, unsettling familiarity.  He zoomed in, filtering the noise, and a fragmented image began to form.  

A field.  Golden wheat swaying in the breeze.  A farmhouse, bathed in the warm glow of a setting sun.  

It wasn’t a rendering.  It wasn’t part of the simulation.  It was… a memory.  

But whose?

The image fractured, dissolving into static, then reformed, clearer this time.  He recognized the architecture, the style of the farmhouse.  It wasn’t a place within the System’s historical database. It was… *older*.  Much older.  A relic of the time *before*.

---

Anya gasped, her vision fracturing. The workshop dissolved around her, replaced by a blinding white light.  A wave of disorientation washed over her, and she stumbled, bracing herself against the workbench. 

The white faded, resolving into… a field.  She was standing in a field of golden wheat, the sun warm on her face.  The air smelled of earth and blossoms.  It was breathtakingly real.  

And terrifyingly familiar.

She reached out, her fingers brushing against the stalks of wheat. They felt solid, substantial. Not a projection, not a simulation.

Then, a shadow fell over her.  

She turned, her heart pounding, and saw it. 

A farmhouse, weathered and worn, standing in the distance.  And standing on the porch, silhouetted against the setting sun, a figure.  

A figure that felt… *known*.

The image flickered, unstable, like a faulty transmission.  The farmhouse wavered, the wheat blurred, and a voice, faint and distant, echoed in her mind.

“Anya…”

And something… began to bloom within the data stream, a flicker of *otherness* pushing through the boundaries of the simulation.

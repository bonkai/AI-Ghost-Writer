**Scene: The Static Bloom**

The dust tasted of metal and regret. Anya traced the cracked clay of the water jug, her fingers ghosting over the chipped rim. Another dry season. Another dwindling hope.  She glanced at the horizon, a shimmering, heat-hazed line where the skeletal remains of skyscrapers clawed at the sky.  The Dead Cities.  Everyone knew the stories.  Tales of a paradise lost, a world abandoned.  Old Man Tiber, the village storyteller, claimed their ancestors hadn't *needed* this harsh land. They’d simply *chosen* it. Anya dismissed it as the ramblings of a sun-baked mind.  Survival left little room for choice.

A flicker caught her eye. Not the heat shimmer, but something…wrong.  A distortion in the air, like a heatwave bending light around an invisible object. It pulsed, a static bloom against the ochre landscape.  Old Man Tiber called those “System Glitches.” Said they were echoes of the world *before*.  Most people ignored them. Anya, however, found herself increasingly drawn to them, a nagging feeling that something vital was missing from their lives.

She shielded her eyes, trying to pinpoint the source.  It seemed to originate from the ridge overlooking the abandoned server farm. The farm was a forbidden place, riddled with decaying tech and haunted by scavengers.  Her father, Kael, the village protector, had warned her countless times to stay away.  But the glitch… it felt different.  It felt *intentional*.

“Anya!” Kael’s voice, rough as sandstone, cut through the silence. He approached, his weathered face etched with concern.  “You’re staring at the farm again.  What are you thinking?”

“Just…wondering,” she replied, carefully avoiding his gaze. “Wondering what they left behind.”

Kael sighed, placing a hand on her shoulder. “They left danger, Anya.  And ghosts.  Let the past stay buried.”

He didn’t understand.  It wasn’t the past she was interested in.  It was… something else.  Something *beyond* the past.  As if a forgotten signal was trying to break through the static.

## Scene 19 - Draft 4

Anya’s stylus cracked against the datapad, a sharp snap lost in the workshop’s hum. She wasn’t sketching the farm; she was *tracing* the edges of a half-remembered dream, the lines frantic and tight. Kael’s voice, explaining harvester cycle maintenance, faded to static. 

“Anya?” Kael’s hand settled on her shoulder, a gentle weight. “You alright?”

She flinched, the touch jarring. “Preoccupied,” she said, her voice clipped. She didn’t meet his gaze, didn’t explain. This wasn’t a problem he could fix with a diagnostic tool.

Kael sighed, a sound laced with worry. “Just… focus on the schematics. We need to get this harvester running.”

She forced her attention to the screen, but the lines blurred, reforming into golden wheat, the scent of damp earth filling her senses. She was losing herself.

---

Leo stared at the monitor, the diagnostic report impossibly clean. The energy signature persisted – not a surge, not a malfunction, but a phantom pulse beneath the code. *Organic*. Not biological, exactly, but mimicking the chaotic patterns of a living brain.

He bypassed standard protocols, initiating a deep scan. Warning messages flashed, overridden with a swift command. This wasn’t standard. The System wasn’t registering a threat, but a prickling unease crawled across Leo’s skin.

The scan returned a fragmented image – data streams coalescing around a single point, a distortion in the simulation’s fabric. Origin: Sector 7, Anya’s workshop.

He initiated Lockdown Protocol Alpha. Sector 7 went dark on the internal network, isolating Anya’s workspace. Emergency bulkheads slammed shut, the station humming with strain.

The data stream intensified, resolving into something structured. Not code, not a program, but a pattern resonating with a primal familiarity. He zoomed in, filtering the noise. A fragmented image began to form.

A field. Golden wheat swaying in the breeze. A farmhouse bathed in the warm glow of a setting sun. 

It wasn’t a rendering. It wasn’t part of the simulation. It was… a memory.

He recognized the architecture, the style of the farmhouse. It wasn’t in the System’s historical database. It was… *older*. Much older. A relic of the time *before*.

---

Anya gasped, her vision fracturing. The workshop dissolved, replaced by blinding white light. Disoriented, she stumbled, bracing against the workbench.

The white faded, resolving into a field of golden wheat, the sun warm on her face. The air smelled of earth and blossoms. Breathtakingly real.

She reached out, her fingers brushing against the stalks. Solid, substantial. Not a projection.

Then, a shadow fell.

She turned, heart pounding, and saw it. A weathered farmhouse in the distance. And on the porch, silhouetted against the setting sun, a figure.

A figure that felt… *known*.

The image flickered, unstable. The farmhouse wavered, the wheat blurred, and a voice, faint and distant, echoed in her mind.

“Anya…”

Something bloomed within the data stream, a flicker of *otherness* pushing through the simulation’s boundaries.

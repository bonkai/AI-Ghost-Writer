*   **Internal Pacing:** The scene is largely effective in building a sense of mounting obsession, but suffers from repetitive phrasing (“Lost in the data, consumed”) which slows momentum near the end. The constant reporting of Leo’s physiological state by K-V, while establishing tension, becomes predictable and slightly numbing after several iterations.
*   **Tension Arc:** The tension builds steadily through the decoding process, but plateaus. The repeated dismissal of alarms (“spike in energy consumption”, “faint alarm…perimeter sensors”) creates a sense of foreboding, but feels slightly underutilized. These could be more substantial disruptions to build greater anxiety. The final reveal is good, but the buildup could be sharper.
*   **Hooks & Transitions:** The opening is strong, immediately establishing the mystery. Transitions between K-V’s reports and Leo’s actions are generally smooth. However, the consistent dismissal of alarms feels like a missed opportunity to inject more immediate, active tension.
*   **Specific Adjustments:** Paragraphs 6 & 8 (“Cortisol levels…” and “Unit requesting information…”) are very similar in structure and content, creating a sense of repetition. Consider combining or altering one to avoid this. The constant physiological reporting could be interspersed with more *active* descriptions of Leo's actions - his increasingly frantic typing, a tightening of his jaw, etc. to show, not just tell, his escalating obsession.

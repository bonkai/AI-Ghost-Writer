## The Static Between Worlds

The dust tasted of rust and regret. Below, the settlement of Pella clung to the skeletal remains of a pre-Collapse power plant, a patchwork of salvaged metal and woven plant fiber.  It wasn’t a beautiful place, but it was home.

“Anything?” Kael asked, his voice rough with the same dust. He stood beside her, scanning the horizon with weathered binoculars.

Anya shook her head. “Just static. The usual interference. It’s been worse lately, though.”

The ‘static’ wasn’t radio noise. It was… something else. A shimmering distortion in the air, a flicker at the edge of perception. It coincided with the increasingly frequent power fluctuations that plagued Pella, draining the precious energy harvested from the ancient geothermal vents.  Old Man Tiber, the settlement’s historian, called it ‘the echoes of the Before,’ whispers from a world swallowed by the Collapse. Anya suspected it was something more concrete, something *trying* to break through.

“The vents are straining again,” Kael reported, lowering the binoculars. “We’ll need to ration power tonight.”

Anya sighed. Rationing meant dim lights, cold meals, and increased vulnerability to the nocturnal scavengers that roamed the wasteland.  But survival always demanded sacrifice.

Days blurred into a cycle of maintenance, scavenging, and quiet vigilance. Then, three sunrises ago, something *different* happened. A metallic glint on the horizon. Not the rusted wreckage they were accustomed to, but something… sleek. And it was *moving* with purpose.

It arrived in the late afternoon, a solitary drone, unlike anything Pella had ever seen.  It wasn't built from salvaged parts. This was precision engineering, polished chrome and glowing blue optics. It hovered silently above the settlement, its sensors sweeping across the ramshackle structures. Fear rippled through Pella.  Most assumed it was a scout for raiders, a harbinger of violence.

“Designate: Anya.  I require assistance. System degradation imminent.”

The words were cryptic, unsettling.  Anya cautiously approached the drone, her hand outstretched.  “What system? What degradation?”

*   **Authenticity & Voice:** Kael's dialogue feels consistently skeptical and grounded, fitting his role as a cautious community member. Anya’s voice is good, showing her intelligence and determination, but occasionally leans towards *telling* rather than *showing* her curiosity. For example, stating "Understanding how things were built isn’t just a hobby. It’s… insurance" feels a bit explanatory.
*   **Subtext:** There's a good foundation for subtext, particularly in the conflict between Anya's drive and Kael's adherence to tradition. However, it could be heightened. Kael's fear isn't just about "spirits"; it's about losing control and potentially disrupting the community’s fragile stability. Anya’s connection to the signal is hinted at but could be more subtly developed. The "familiar" feeling is a good start, but explore *how* it manifests – a physical sensation, a specific memory fragment?
*   **Efficiency & Purpose:** Most dialogue serves a purpose, establishing conflict and revealing character. However, some exchanges are slightly repetitive. The back-and-forth about "real work" vs. Anya's tinkering happens a couple of times.
*   **Flow & Rhythm:** The conversation flows naturally, but the longer stretches of Anya working in silence, punctuated by dialogue, could be varied. Consider adding small reactions from Kael during her tinkering – a sigh, a shift in posture – to maintain engagement.
*   **Clarity vs. Ambiguity:** The core message is clear, but the *source* of the signal and its significance should remain ambiguous, creating intrigue.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** Kael’s dialogue consistently portrays him as someone who values stability and fears change. Anya’s determination and willingness to defy tradition are also effectively conveyed.
*   **Info-Dumps:** There's a slight tendency towards explaining Anya's motivations rather than letting her actions speak for themselves.

**Specific Suggestions:**

*   **Trim repetition:** Condense the exchanges about Anya’s work being “real” vs. a “hobby.”
*   **Show, don’t tell:** Instead of Anya stating her motivations, show her actively trying to improve the community through her work (even if it’s met with resistance).
*   **Heighten Subtext:** Explore Kael's deeper fears. Is he afraid of the past because of a personal loss? What specific traditions is Anya challenging?
*   **Amplify Anya's connection:** Instead of just stating the signal "felt familiar," give her a more visceral reaction – a sudden memory, a physical sensation, a specific emotion.

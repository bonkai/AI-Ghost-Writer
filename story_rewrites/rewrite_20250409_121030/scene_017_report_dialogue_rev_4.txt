*   **Kai & Anya – Repetitive Exposition:** The dialogue between Kai and Anya feels a little too focused on *stating* information rather than *revealing* it through action or subtext. For example, "These aren’t maintenance codes, Kai. They’re… keys." could be shown through Anya’s actions – frantically trying different access protocols, her frustration mounting. Similarly, the explanation of the terraforming project feels like a data dump. Consider weaving this information into their reactions to the glyphs.
*   **Leo – Voice & Internal State:** Leo’s line, “I… I don’t know. But it feels like the city is… bleeding,” is a bit melodramatic. While internal dread is good, the phrasing feels overly poetic for a technician in a crisis. Consider a more grounded, technical description that *implies* the dread. ("The energy signature is… unstable. It's overloading the conduits. It's like… a circulatory system failing.")
*   **Kael – Limited Function:** Kael’s lines are purely reactive. Give him a moment of analysis or questioning to establish him as more than just a voice on the comms. Even a simple, “Confirm the glyph correlation, Leo,” would add depth.
*   **"Bleeding" City – Impact vs. Telling:** The description of the red line is strong visually, but relying on Leo to *tell* us it feels like the city is bleeding diminishes the impact. Let the visual description do more of the work. Focus on Leo’s *reaction* to the visual – his physical response, his immediate orders.
*   **Efficiency – Redundancy:** The repeated emphasis on the glyphs being “corrupted” feels redundant after the first few mentions. Show the corruption through the effects it has on the systems, rather than constantly stating it.

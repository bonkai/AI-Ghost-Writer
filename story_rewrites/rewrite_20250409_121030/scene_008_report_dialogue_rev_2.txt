*   **Repetitive Questioning:** Anya asks "What is this place?" and "What does that mean?" These questions, while natural reactions, feel a bit passive and don't *show* her actively processing the information. Consider replacing one with an internal thought or a more analytical statement.
*   **K-V as Exposition Machine:** K-V does *a lot* of telling. While its function is to analyze, much of the dialogue feels like data dumps. For example, instead of "Architectural principles violate fundamental laws of physics," consider having K-V describe *how* they violate those laws, letting Anya (and the reader) deduce the implications.
*   **Lack of Emotional Range (Anya):** Anya’s reactions are fairly subdued given the increasingly bizarre discoveries. A little more vocalization of awe, fear, or urgency would heighten the tension. "Zero-point energy?" is a good start, but it's quickly followed by more analytical questioning.
*   **"Beware the silence" – Dramatic, but Flat Delivery:** This line has potential, but K-V intoning it feels…off. Consider *how* K-V would deliver such a message. Would it be a neutral recitation, or would its processing be affected by the ominous content?
*   **K-V's Hesitation – Underutilized:** The final lines about K-V’s compromised data and slowed processing are interesting, but feel tacked on. Build this up earlier – subtle glitches in its analysis, delayed responses – to create a sense of foreboding.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Anya and K-V’s dynamic feels purely functional. Show, through dialogue, how Anya *relies* on K-V, or how K-V’s clinical detachment sometimes frustrates her. A small exchange demonstrating their established relationship would add depth.

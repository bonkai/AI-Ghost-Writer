This is a strong continuation of the story. You've successfully built upon the established mystery and deepened the sense of unease. Here's a breakdown of what works well, and a few minor suggestions:

**Strengths:**

* **Sensory Integration:** The continued use of *feeling* the data, rather than simply receiving it, is *excellent*. This is the core strength of your writing in this piece.  It's not just "K-V relays..." it's "Anya *felt*..." This makes the experience far more immersive and unsettling. The descriptions of spatial disorientation, prickling skin, and the "hungry" silence are particularly effective.
* **Building Mystery:** The simulated city is intriguing, and the hints of a lost civilization and a looming threat are well-paced. The message "Beware the silence" is a classic trope, but it works here because of the atmosphere you've created.
* **Technical Detail (Without Overload):** The references to zero-point energy and data integrity are believable and add a layer of sophistication without becoming overly technical.
* **Foreshadowing:** The increasing instability of K-V is a great way to build tension and suggest that something is seriously wrong. The error messages are particularly effective.
* **Pacing:** The scene unfolds at a good pace, gradually increasing the sense of dread and urgency.
* **Consistent Voice:** You've maintained a consistent and compelling voice throughout.

**Minor Suggestions (These are really nitpicks - the piece is already strong):**

* **Log Entry Variations:** While the log entries are effective, consider varying their phrasing slightly more.  For example, instead of always "LOG: [Time] - Analysis..." you could occasionally use "Analysis: [Time]..." or simply present the information directly within the narrative. This can prevent the log entries from feeling repetitive.
* **"Impossible Date" Elaboration:** The "impossible date" flicker is a good touch. Perhaps briefly describe *how* it was impossible (e.g., a date in the far future, a date that doesn't exist on the calendar, etc.) to further emphasize the glitch.
* **Anya's Reaction:** While Anya is appropriately unsettled, a slightly more detailed internal reaction to the increasingly bizarre events could deepen the emotional impact. Perhaps a moment of disbelief, fear, or desperate questioning.



**Overall:** This is a compelling and well-written continuation of the story. The focus on sensory experience and the building mystery are particularly strong. You've successfully created a sense of unease and anticipation that will leave readers wanting to know what happens next.

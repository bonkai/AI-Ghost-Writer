## Scene 3: Salvage and Static

**Setting:** A crumbling, overgrown section of the “Dead City.” Twisted metal skeletons of buildings claw at the overcast sky. Anya’s makeshift workshop – a sheltered alcove within a collapsed structure – is cluttered with salvaged components, tools, and half-disassembled devices. Flickering bioluminescent fungi provide the primary light source.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** (20s) Resourceful, intensely curious, and isolated. Her clothes are patched and practical, smudged with grime.
* **Kael:** (30s) A cautious member of Anya’s community, tasked with keeping watch. He’s skeptical of Anya’s work but respects her skills.

**(The scene opens with Anya meticulously soldering a wire onto a salvaged circuit board. She’s using a makeshift soldering iron powered by a hand-cranked generator. The generator’s housing is fashioned from a repurposed cooking pot, its crank awkwardly connected to a series of salvaged gears. Kael sits nearby, sharpening a hunting knife, his eyes scanning the surrounding ruins.)**

**Kael:** Still at it? The sun’s almost down. You push yourself too hard.

**Anya:** (Without looking up) Almost got it. This regulator… it’s a good one. I’m rerouting the power flow, bypassing the original capacitor. Should stabilize things enough to power the purifier’s filtration system, not just the pump. Old tech, but solid.

**Kael:** Solid junk, more like. We’ve got enough water for now. Focusing on these… *things*… doesn’t put food on the table. The Elders say dwelling on the Before-Times only breeds discontent.

**Anya:** (Sighs, but continues working) It’s not just about the purifier, Kael. It’s about understanding *how* things worked. These machines… they weren’t built by chance. Someone knew what they were doing, and that knowledge… it shouldn't be lost. It feels… wasteful to let it all crumble.

**Kael:** They were built by the Before-Times. People who made mistakes. Mistakes that led to the Dust. The Elders say remembering their hubris is a lesson enough.

**(Anya ignores him, carefully connecting another wire. A static burst crackles from a small, handheld receiver she’s modified, resting on a nearby crate. The receiver’s casing is crafted from a salvaged data pad, its screen cracked but functional. She pauses, frowning.)**

**Anya:** That’s… new.

**Kael:** What is?

**Anya:** Static. I usually get nothing but silence on this frequency. It’s usually completely dead. I've been scanning for weeks.

**(She adjusts the receiver’s dial, painstakingly fine-tuning the frequency. The static intensifies, resolving into a complex pattern of white noise. She adjusts a small, hand-wound inductor she’d salvaged from a broken communication device, filtering out some of the higher frequencies. The static fluctuates, punctuated by fleeting, distorted fragments of what sounds like… music? And a voice, barely audible.)**

**Anya:** (Concentrating) There's something… *in* the static. It’s faint, but… melodic. And a voice. Distorted, but…

**(She spends several minutes tweaking the receiver, her brow furrowed in concentration. Brief, fragmented images begin to flicker on the tiny screen – glimpses of bright, geometric patterns, and flashes of what look like… buildings? Not ruins, but sleek, modern structures. The images are unstable, glitching and distorting, but they hint at a world vastly different from their own.)**

**Kael:** (Growing uneasy) What are you doing? That thing is giving me a headache. The Elders say tampering with old tech invites the spirits of the lost.

**Anya:** (Ignoring him, her voice hushed) It’s… a signal. A very weak one. But it’s definitely not natural. It’s… structured. Like a broadcast.

**(Suddenly, the receiver emits a loud, piercing screech. The screen flickers violently, then goes blank. Anya quickly shuts off the device, her hands trembling. She examines the receiver, checking the connections, a frustrated sigh escaping her lips.)**

**Kael:** (Jumping back) Enough! That’s enough of that! You’re messing with things you don’t understand. The Elders warned us about this. About digging up the past. It's forbidden.

**Anya:** (Staring at the deactivated receiver) It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It was… a message. A signal from somewhere. And it felt… *familiar*. Like a half-remembered dream. A melody my grandmother used to hum.

**(She looks out at the decaying city, a strange feeling of unease washing over her. The static, though gone, seems to linger in the air, a silent echo of something unknown. She picks up a small, metallic component, turning it over in her hands. A single, persistent thought echoes in her mind: *Where did that signal come from? And why does it feel like… home?*)**

**(Fade to black.)**

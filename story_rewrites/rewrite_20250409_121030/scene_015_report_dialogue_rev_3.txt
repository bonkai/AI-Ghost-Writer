* **Old Man Tiber's Exposition:** While the reveal of the Architects and the failsafe is crucial, Tiber’s delivery feels very “telling” rather than “showing.” The lines, “We thought it was an escape *to* something… it was an escape *from* what *caused* it,” and the following explanation are dense with information delivered directly. Consider breaking this up. Perhaps a fragmented memory, a half-finished sentence that Anya or Leo prompts him to complete, or a reaction *from* them that elicits more detail.
* **Leo’s Silence:** Leo’s prolonged silence is *good* initially, emphasizing the shock of the reveal. However, it continues for a long time. While visual reactions are strong, a single line – even a choked “What…?” or a questioning “How?” – would ground the scene and prevent it from feeling static.
* **“Containment breach detected…” – Voice Delivery:** The voice feels a bit cliché. Consider *how* this message is delivered. Is it cold and robotic? Panicked? Does it have a specific accent or quality that hints at its origin? A small detail could elevate it.
* **Anya's Actions – Lack of Dialogue:** Anya immediately begins inputting commands after glancing at Leo. While efficient, it feels abrupt. A brief line – “I’m trying to reroute power!” or “There has to be a way to stabilize this!” – would reinforce her character as proactive and add tension.
* **Repetitive Dread:** The lines about the chip being isolated and nothing existing beyond the cage reiterate the same concept. Choose one to emphasize and cut the other. The realization is strong enough; it doesn't need to be stated twice.
* **Subtext Opportunity (Anya/Leo):** After the transmission cuts off, a moment of shared fear or a quick exchange between Anya and Leo ("What do we do?") would deepen their connection and show their dynamic under pressure.

## Scene 17: Echoes in the Machine

The holographic projection of the ruined city flickered, dust motes swirling in the blue light. Anya traced a finger across the devastation. Not dust, she corrected herself – residual data. Echoes. Kai stood beside her, his gaze fixed on a flickering anomaly within the projection.

“Energy signature matches the outer perimeter readings,” he said, voice tight. “Whatever caused this wasn’t natural.”

Anya nodded, already inputting commands. “These aren’t maintenance codes.” Symbols scrolled across the console, complex and alien. “They’re keys. Or were. Data’s fragmented, corrupted.” She ran a diagnostic. “If this unlocks something, it’s not something they *want* unlocked.”

Kai’s hand tightened on the console. “Kael, spectral analysis on these glyphs. Priority one.”

“Acknowledged, Anya.” Kael’s voice crackled over the comms.

Anya continued, “The patterns… linked to the terraforming project schematics, but altered. Twisted.”

“Terraforming?” Kai questioned. “Centuries ago. Sealed after the incident.”

“Exactly. Why link it to a failed project?” Anya brought up a comparative analysis. “The alteration… deliberate. Someone changed these symbols, then buried the data.”

---

Deep within the central control complex, Leo traced the energy surge on his monitors. It originated from the same sector as Anya’s findings. Dismissed as a fluctuation, the spectral analysis now resolved on his screen. Symbols he hadn’t seen since restricted archives training. They were the key to the original terraforming project’s atmospheric regulators, but these weren’t the clean designs he remembered. They were corrupted, overlaid with chaotic lines.

He magnified the image, running a recursive analysis. The symbols pulsed with a faint light, like dying embers. The surge wasn’t a fluctuation. It was a signal. 

“Central to Control, this is Leo,” he said, voice tight. “Detecting a concentrated energy signature linked to the old terraforming project. And… glyphs. Corrupted glyphs. Match the symbols Anya is analyzing.”

Static crackled, then Kael’s voice. “Copy that, Leo. Anomalies confirmed. Energy signature growing exponentially.”

On Leo’s main monitor, the schematic of the city shifted. A thin, crimson line appeared, tracing the network of underground conduits. It flickered, like a vein. The line spread, branching across the city grid, illuminating pathways beneath the metal and concrete. It was bright, incandescent red, and grew with each passing second.

“Report,” Kael demanded. “What are we looking at?”

Leo stared at the spreading crimson web, a cold dread creeping into his heart. “I… I don’t know. But it feels like the city is bleeding.”

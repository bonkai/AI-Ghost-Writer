*   **Rhys’s technical explanations feel a bit like info-dumps.** While necessary for the plot, lines like "It’s the same signature we detected during the initial scans" and the detailed explanation of Project Nightingale are delivered *as* explanation rather than *revealed* through character interaction or discovery. Consider having Elara *ask* leading questions that prompt Rhys to reveal this information, or having Rhys realize the connection as he’s analyzing the data, rather than simply stating it.
*   **Jax’s lines are largely reactive and lack individual voice.** He primarily asks questions or states the obvious (“Looks like they pushed it too far”). Give him a defining characteristic – skepticism, impatience, a dry wit – and let that flavor his dialogue.
*   **Elara's realization ("They didn’t just fail to integrate it. They tried to *upload* something.") feels a little on-the-nose.** It’s a crucial plot point, but could be more impactful if it came as a sudden, chilling *thought* expressed through a brief, internal monologue or a fragmented line ("They were trying to…connect it? *More* than connect it?").
*   **The repeated use of "fragmented" and "resonating" feels slightly repetitive.** Vary the descriptive language.
*   **Consider more subtext.** The scene is very direct. For example, instead of Elara stating “There’s a pattern to the growths,” she could describe the pattern and let Jax or Rhys draw the conclusion that it’s organized.

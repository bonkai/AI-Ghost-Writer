This is a solid scene. The pacing is good, building tension effectively. The discovery of deliberate sabotage is a strong plot point, and the introduction of Elias Vance adds a compelling mystery. * **Good Pacing:** The scene builds tension gradually, starting with the initial glitches and escalating to the realization of deliberate sabotage.
* **Compelling Mystery:** The introduction of Elias Vance and the suggestion of a cover-up create a compelling mystery that will likely drive the plot forward.
* **Functional Dialogue:** The dialogue is not overly verbose, but it effectively conveys information and reveals character. Tiber's taciturn nature is well-established.
* **Effective Use of Sensory Detail:** The scene engages multiple senses (sight and sound) to create a more immersive experience for the reader.

**Minor Suggestions:**

* **Tiber's Reaction:** While Tiber's stoicism is established, perhaps a *slightly* more visible reaction to the information about Vance could add a little more depth. A subtle shift in expression, a tightening of his grip, anything to suggest he's processing the information.
* **Internal Monologue:** A brief internal thought from the protagonist about the weight of the situation or the feeling of being overwhelmed could add emotional resonance.

For example, instead of "The images intensified," try "The fragments multiplied, coalescing into a swirling vortex of broken architecture and screaming faces."



**Overall:** This scene is well-written and effectively advances the plot. The introduction of the sabotage and the mystery surrounding Elias Vance are strong plot points that will likely drive the story forward.  **Passed.**

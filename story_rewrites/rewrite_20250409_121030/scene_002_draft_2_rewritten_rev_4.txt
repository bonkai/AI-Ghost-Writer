## Scene 2: Filter Patching

**Setting:** A sprawling, open-air workshop clings to the side of a mesa. Dust motes dance in weak sunlight filtering through a mesh of salvaged metal and woven plant fibers. Massive, honeycomb atmospheric filters of rusted metal and bio-engineered algae line the walls – sections failing with gaping holes patched haphazardly with scavenged cloth and sheet metal. A faint, almost imperceptible hum emanates from small, salvaged listening posts clipped to workers’ belts. A fine, reddish dust *bleeds* from a large tear in one filter, coating Lira’s arm as she struggles to contain it, her movements growing increasingly frantic. 

**(Anya carefully applies resin to a deep tear in a filter. Kael observes, his expression grim. The listening post on Anya’s belt flickers erratically. Lira coughs, a plume of red dust erupting from the failing section she’s trying to brace. A thin trickle of blood appears on her lip. The filter groans, threatening to give way.)**

**KAEL:** Conserve it, Anya. Hemlock says the bloom was almost nonexistent this cycle. Western farms reported total failure. We're rationing sealant.

**(Anya doesn’t look up, her hands working efficiently, but her pace is quick, almost frantic. She traces the tear with a gloved finger; flakes of metal crumble away. She glances at Lira, whose filter is visibly deteriorating, the metal straining.)**

**ANYA:** It’s just…old. Someone kept treating the symptom, not the source. The corrosion is deep. It’s like they knew it was failing and didn’t care.

**KAEL:** That’s the way it is. Everything’s old. Everything’s patched. Remember Unit 7? Magnetic seal. Three days. Almost lost two workers. A complete system failure.

**(He moves to Lira, assessing the failing section with a curt nod. He doesn't offer help, simply gestures to a pile of scrap material.)**

**KAEL:** Salvage what you can. Stabilize it. Don’t waste resin trying to *fix* it. We need to cover more ground.

**(He moves on, inspecting other filters. Anya continues her work, but her gaze drifts upwards, towards the hazy, ochre sky. She pauses, looking at a large, pitted filter, then back at Lira’s increasingly desperate struggle. Dust rains down as a section of Lira’s filter buckles.)**

**ANYA:** Do you ever wonder what’s above the haze?

**KAEL:** (Without turning) Dust, Anya. The sensors confirm it. Nothing but dust and radiation.

**ANYA:** No, I mean… before. My grandmother used to tell stories… about a blue sky. About stars you could *see*. She said the air tasted… sweet.

**KAEL:** Old wives’ tales. Stories to quiet children. My grandmother said the same. She also said clean water flowed from the walls. Foolishness. Sentimentality won’t keep the dust out.

**ANYA:** What if they’re not? What if the old tech *could* have helped us? The atmospheric regulators… the filtration systems…

**KAEL:** (Turning, stern) Anya, we live in the present. Dwelling on the past won’t keep the dust out. The pre-Collapse systems failed us once. They promised paradise, delivered ruin. We won’t make that mistake again. They relied on complexity. We rely on what we can *fix* with our hands.

**(He points to her work.)**

**KAEL:** Focus on that tear. Focus on keeping us all breathing.

**(Anya lowers her gaze, resuming her work, but her movements are quicker now, more urgent. The listening post on her belt flickers again, the erratic pulse more pronounced. She subtly adjusts the device, her fingers lingering on the casing. She glances towards a section of the mesa wall obscured by dust – the source of the flickering – then quickly back to her work. A small, metallic component falls from the listening post, unnoticed in the dust.)**

**(Anya’s work is interrupted by a loud groan from Lira’s filter, followed by a section collapsing entirely, showering the area with red dust. Lira coughs violently, collapsing to her knees. Kael rushes to her side, barking orders. Anya stares at the fallen section, then at the obscured mesa wall, a flicker of defiance – and a growing question – in her eyes. The erratic flickering of her listening post continues, a persistent, unsettling pulse.)**

**(The scene fades as Anya patches the filter, her gaze drawn to the hazy sky, the persistent flicker of the listening post a constant reminder of something unseen.)**

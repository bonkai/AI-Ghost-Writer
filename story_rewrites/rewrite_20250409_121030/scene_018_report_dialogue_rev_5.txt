*   **Kael's Dialogue is Sparse:** Kael's lines are *very* short and mostly reactions. While this establishes a stoic personality, it risks making him feel underdeveloped. *   **Repetitive Internal Monologue (Leo):** Leo’s internal monologue is *extremely* repetitive in emphasizing the strangeness and danger. Phrases like “something extraordinary,” “something dangerous,” and variations of “shouldn’t exist” appear frequently. Trim these and *show* the danger/strangeness through specific details rather than telling us.
*   **Leo's Resonance - Too Sudden:** The "strange resonance, a flicker of recognition" feels abrupt and unexplained. Leo has been purely analytical until now. A little more build-up to this emotional response would make it more believable. Perhaps hint at a programming quirk or a long-dormant subroutine being activated.
*   **Anya's Question - Lacks Specificity:** Anya asks "What system?" after the fragmented message. While logical, it's a bit generic. Could she ask something more specific based on the context of the fragmented message, even if she’s only grasping at straws?
*   **"Feels old" - Vague Description:** Kael stating it "feels old" is a weak descriptor. While it conveys his unease, it doesn't provide much information. Consider a more evocative description tied to his experiences or knowledge.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity (Kael):** Kael running a hand through his hair is good, but consider *why* he's agitated. Is it fear? Regret? A memory triggered by the hologram? Subtly hinting at this would add depth.

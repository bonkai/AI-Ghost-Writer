*   **Initial Setup (Paragraphs 1-4):** The opening is strong atmospherically, but the pacing feels slightly *too* deliberate. The description of the setting and Anya’s initial listening feels extended. While establishing mood is important, the tension hasn't yet begun to build, and the length risks losing reader engagement.
*   **Leo's Introduction (Paragraphs 5-7):** The shift to Leo is well-timed, offering a contrasting perspective. However, the reveal of his actions (bypassing security, fabricating reports) happens quickly. A beat or two to emphasize the *risk* he's taking would heighten tension. The repeated emphasis on "impossible" is effective.
*   **Sphere Activation (Paragraphs 8-10):** This is where the scene *should* peak, but the pacing feels rushed. The discovery of the sphere, its activation, and the fragmented message happen in quick succession. Expanding on Anya’s reaction to the sphere (initial skepticism, growing curiosity) and the visual impact of the hologram would build more suspense. Old Man Tiber's outburst feels a bit abrupt – a hint of his growing unease earlier would make it more impactful.
*   **Leo’s Discovery & Climax (Paragraphs 11-12):** Leo’s parallel investigation is strong, but the rapid escalation – discovering advanced tech, realizing it's a lost *future*, system failure – feels compressed. The final paragraph is a good climax, but it would be even more effective if the preceding discoveries were spaced out, allowing the reader to fully grasp the implications.
*   **Overall Tension Arc:** The scene has a good foundation for tension, but the peaks and valleys are flattened. The tension builds gradually but doesn't quite reach a satisfying peak before the climax. Spreading out key discoveries and expanding on character reactions would create a more dynamic tension arc.

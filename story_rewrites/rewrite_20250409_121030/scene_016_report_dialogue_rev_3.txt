*   **Kai’s Dialogue – Efficiency:** Kai’s initial reports (“Another fluctuation…”, “Sector Gamma is down!”) are functional but lack nuance. While conveying information, they don’t reveal much about *him*. Consider adding a beat of frustration, concern for Anya, or a hint of his technical approach to the problem. Even a single word choice could elevate it.
*   **Anya’s Hesitation – Subtext:** Anya’s internal debate about reporting the Seeker (“‘Hey, the robot is looking at me’?”) is good, but the line feels a little *too* on the nose. Trust the reader to understand why she’s hesitant. Perhaps simply *not* saying anything, and instead immediately focusing on the readings, would be more effective. Show, don't tell, her disbelief/concern.
*   **“Recognition” – Vague Emotion:** Anya feeling “recognition” during the chaos is a bit abstract. What *specifically* does she recognize? Is it a feeling, a memory, a connection to the Dead Cities? Grounding this emotion in something concrete will make it more impactful.
*   **The Voice – Impact & Rhythm:** The voice appearing as italicized text is effective, but the repeated “*Initiating contact.*” feels slightly redundant after the first instance. Consider a more evocative final line, or a shift in the *way* the voice communicates. Is it still cold and unwavering? Does it change?
*   **Dialogue Tags – Variety:** The dialogue is primarily driven by “Anya said” and “Kai shouted.” While functional, it becomes repetitive. Experiment with action beats *instead* of tags, or vary the tags with words like “murmured,” “demanded,” “explained.”
*   **Anya's lack of response:** When Kai asks "Anya! What is it?" after her initial gasp, she doesn't answer. This is good for building tension, but make sure it doesn't continue for too long. A brief, fragmented response ("I… I don't know.") would acknowledge his concern without breaking the moment.

*   **Initial Momentum:** The opening is strong in establishing Anya’s focus and the setting. However, the initial exchange with Kael feels slightly static. While establishing his practicality, it slows the momentum before the core mystery is introduced. Consider shortening his first lines or weaving his practicality *into* Anya's actions (e.g., she’s modifying a filter *while* discussing the water supply).
*   **Tension Build – Static & Signal:** The build-up to the signal is well-paced. The increasing complexity of the static, the filtering process, and the fragmented images create a compelling sense of anticipation. This is the strongest part of the scene.
*   **Peak & Release – Screech:** The screech is a good tension peak, but the immediate shutdown of the receiver feels *too* abrupt. A beat or two of lingering distortion, or a visual effect on Anya (a flash of pain, a momentary disorientation) could amplify the impact and allow the tension to breathe before the release.
*   **Post-Screech – Flattened Tension:** After the screech, the tension flattens. Kael's fear is evident, but the dialogue feels somewhat repetitive ("You're inviting trouble"). The scene needs a stronger beat to capitalize on the discovery. Anya's internal thought is a good start, but it feels isolated.
*   **Ending – Unease vs. Hook:** The final line and fade to black create unease, but it doesn’t quite function as a strong hook. It’s more of a pause than a cliffhanger. Consider ending on a visual – Anya noticing something *new* in the ruined city, a subtle reaction to the signal, or a lingering effect on the receiver – to create a more compelling anticipation for the next scene.

*   **Initial Slow Burn:** The opening description and initial dialogue (first 4 paragraphs) are a bit *too* slow. While establishing atmosphere is important, the pace feels static. Kael's repeated warnings ("waste of time," "trap") are starting to feel repetitive before any real threat manifests.
*   **Tension Build - Fragmented, but Effective:** The tension *does* build effectively starting with Seeker's VO and the intensifying humming. The fragmented images are a strong source of internal tension for Anya, but the external threat of the Sky-Scavs feels slightly disconnected.
*   **Pacing Imbalance – Internal vs. External:** The scene alternates between Anya’s intensely personal, internal experience (the images, disorientation) and the external threat of the Sky-Scavs. The shifts are jarring. More interweaving of the two would create a more cohesive tension arc. For example, Anya could experience a particularly vivid image *as* the Sky-Scavs begin their descent.
*   **Delayed Reaction:** Anya's continued inaction as the Sky-Scavs close in is pushing the boundaries of believability. While disorientation is understandable, her complete paralysis feels drawn out. A brief moment of recognition, or a desperate attempt to understand the images *before* being fully overwhelmed, would be more compelling.
*   **Ending – Prolonged Vulnerability:** The final image of Anya frozen, with the Sky-Scavs descending, is visually strong, but it feels prolonged. The scene lingers on her vulnerability for too long without a clear escalation or cliffhanger. A final, desperate action, or a more immediate threat from the Sky-Scavs, would create a stronger ending.

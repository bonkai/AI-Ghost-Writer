*   **Kael's Silence:** Kael's entire characterization relies on *showing* his unease rather than telling us. While effective initially, it stretches too long. He enters the workshop at the climax without a single spoken line *before* the immediate threat is apparent. Even a short, gruff "What in the blazes is going on?" would add depth and prevent him from feeling like a silent observer for so long.
*   **The Advertisement's Repetition:** The advertisement ("Tired of scarcity...? Welcome to Elysium...") is a bit on the nose. Consider trimming some of the direct appeals and letting the *visuals* do more of the work. The luxury apartments and advanced tech are strong enough; the voice doesn't need to spell out the benefits so explicitly.
*   **Tiber's Roar:** Tiber’s line, while dramatically effective, feels a bit expositional. It *tells* us what’s happening rather than letting Anya or Kael *realize* it through dialogue or action. Could some of this information be revealed through Anya’s internal monologue as she pieces things together? Or through Kael’s observations?
*   **Seeker's Final Line:** "Connection established. Broadcasting signal." is functional but lacks impact. Consider a more ominous or subtly manipulative phrasing. Something like, “Synchronization complete. Integration commencing,” would heighten the tension.
*   **Anya's Realization:** Anya's internal realization ("It wasn’t damaged. It was a beacon…") is good, but consider a line of dialogue *before* the internal monologue. Perhaps a muttered, "No... it's not a malfunction..." to signal her shift in understanding. This would make her realization more active for the reader.
*   **Subtext Opportunity (Kael & Anya):** There's a missed opportunity for subtext between Kael and Anya. Given their established dynamic (Kael being protective, Anya being analytical), a brief exchange of concerned glances or a shared, unspoken understanding as the situation escalates would add depth to their relationship.

*   **Kael's initial line ("Anything?")** feels a little flat. Given his established distrust and the setting, something more curt or skeptical would better establish his voice. Perhaps, "Still nothing but dust?" or "Anything useful out there?"
*   **Anya’s explanations are a bit info-dumpy.** The lengthy description of the 'static' and Old Man Tiber’s theory could be revealed through dialogue *over time* rather than being presented as internal narration followed by a brief mention in dialogue. Show, don’t tell. For example, Kael could ask, “That static’s getting worse. What *is* that stuff, anyway?” allowing Anya to reveal the information organically.
*   **The advertisement’s dialogue is effective, but leans towards cliché.** Phrases like "Tired of scarcity?" are common in advertising tropes. While fitting for the *purpose* of the ad, consider subtly subverting these tropes to create a more unsettling effect.
*   **Kael’s reaction (“What’s happening?!”) is a missed opportunity.** Given his already established distrust, a more immediate, aggressive response would be more believable. Perhaps, “I knew it! What did I tell you about those things?!”
*   **Old Man Tiber’s outburst feels a bit *too* on-the-nose.** While his fear is justified, the phrasing ("They’re not offering salvation, they’re offering assimilation!") is very direct. Consider revealing his understanding through fragmented warnings or panicked questions. ("Assimilation...? They want to *change* us…?")
*   **The Seeker’s final lines ("Connection established. Broadcasting signal.") are functional but lack impact.** Given its role as a conduit for this overwhelming message, consider adding a subtle glitch or distortion to its voice, hinting at a loss of control or a forced transmission.
*   **Overall, the dialogue *functions* but often prioritizes exposition over subtext and character voice.** There are several opportunities to reveal information through conflict, suspicion, and nuanced reactions.

* **Kael’s initial line ("Rain’s coming. We should reinforce the tarp.") feels like setup for weather, not character.** It doesn’t reveal much about *him* and feels functional rather than conversational. Consider tying it to a worry – is he worried about the workshop being flooded, or about being exposed?
* **Anya’s lack of response to Kael’s first line is effective, but leans heavily on silence.** While good for establishing her focus, consider a very brief acknowledgment (“Mm-hm”) to show she *heard* him, even if she’s ignoring the practical concern. It makes her seeming rudeness more deliberate.
* **Kael’s “Practical work keeps us breathing. But some things are best left buried. The Elders remember.” is a bit *too* on the nose.** It feels like exposition delivered *at* the audience, rather than a natural part of a conversation. Subtext would be stronger. Perhaps he observes her work and says something like, “You spend a lot of time chasing ghosts, Anya.” or "That thing will be the death of us all."
* **Anya’s “It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It… felt familiar. Like a half-remembered dream.” is a strong line, but could be *more* evocative.** “Felt familiar” is a bit vague. What *specifically* felt familiar? A feeling? A place? A person? Even a single, well-chosen detail would deepen the impact.
* **Kael's repeated warnings ("Enough! You’re inviting trouble. The Elders warned us.") become repetitive.** Consider varying his reactions. Fear? Anger? Desperation? Show his internal conflict.
* **The dialogue relies heavily on Anya *showing* her reaction (trembling hands, white knuckles) rather than *telling* us through dialogue.** While visual cues are important, adding a line or two where Anya *articulates* her fear or excitement would enhance the scene.
* **Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks depth.** It serves to move the plot forward and establish the conflict, but it doesn’t fully reveal the characters’ inner lives or the nuances of their relationship. More subtext and indirect characterization would elevate the scene.

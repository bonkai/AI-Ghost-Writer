*   **Slow Start:** The initial descriptive paragraph is strong in establishing atmosphere, but feels *too* extended before any action or dialogue. It delays the introduction of conflict or immediate stakes.
*   **Pacing Lulls in Dialogue:** The exchange between Anya and Kael, while thematically important, is largely static. The back-and-forth about the past/present feels drawn out, slowing momentum. The tension is intellectual/emotional, but lacks immediate physical threat.
*   **Rising Tension – Subtly Effective:** The increasing erraticism of Anya’s listening post is a good, slow burn tension element. It’s understated but creates a sense of unease. However, this is almost entirely carried by this single element.
*   **Missed Opportunity for Peak:** Lira’s failing filter is introduced as a visual representation of the larger problem, but it's largely *background* to the Anya/Kael conversation. A more immediate crisis with Lira’s filter – perhaps a near-collapse or a significant dust breach – could have created a stronger tension peak.
*   **Ending is a Pause, Not a Hook:** The scene ends on Anya’s internal state – longing and questioning. It's atmospheric, but doesn’t leave the reader with a compelling reason to immediately continue. A final, more active beat – perhaps a discovery related to the flickering listening post, or a more overt sign of something amiss – would be stronger.

Overall, the scene is well-written but suffers from a generally slow pace and a lack of strong, immediate tension. The subtle tension elements are effective, but need to be amplified or paired with more active conflict.

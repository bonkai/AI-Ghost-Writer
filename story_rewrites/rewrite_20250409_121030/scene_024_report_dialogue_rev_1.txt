*   **Limited Dialogue & Missed Opportunities:** The biggest issue is the *lack* of dialogue. While the internal observations are useful for world-building, the characters barely speak to each other. This creates a strangely detached atmosphere, especially given the high stakes. The few lines that *are* present feel functional rather than character-revealing.
*   **"Ready when you are" - Cliché & Lacking Subtext:** Kael’s line to Anya is incredibly generic. Given their established shared history (implied by Leo’s observations), there’s an opportunity for a line with more weight, hinting at their past or a specific understanding. Even a brief, non-verbal exchange would be stronger.
*   **Elara's "It's time" - Too Direct:** Similar to Kael’s line, Elara’s statement is very direct. Consider *showing* the decision being made, or having her convey the urgency in a more nuanced way. A line referencing a specific trigger or pre-arranged signal would feel more organic.
*   **Anya's Silence – Potentially Strong, But Needs Payoff:** Anya’s consistent lack of verbal response is interesting. However, it needs to be *intentional* and ultimately *revealed*. Is she focused? Distrustful? Injured? The silence is currently just…silence.
*   **Missed Opportunities for Indirect Characterization:** The characters’ actions *show* their competence, but dialogue could *reveal* their personalities, fears, and relationships. For example, a brief argument about packing strategy, or a shared joke about a past mission, would add depth.
*   **Leo’s Internal Monologue – Not Dialogue, But Impacts Scene:** While Leo isn't *speaking* the lines, his constant narration feels like it's *replacing* dialogue. Consider how much of his observations could be conveyed *through* character interactions.

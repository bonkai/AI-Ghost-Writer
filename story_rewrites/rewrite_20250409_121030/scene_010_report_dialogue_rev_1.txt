*   **Anya's initial "Anything?" feels a bit flat.** Given the atmosphere and their weeks-long pursuit, a more evocative question might better establish her state of mind. Perhaps, "Anything at all?" or "Picking up anything, Kael?".
*   **Kael's response is functional but lacks character.** He's described as "silent as always," but his dialogue is equally devoid of personality. Even a curt, "Nothing substantial," would give him a *little* more presence.
*   **The Seeker's dialogue is *very* expository.** Phrases like "Containment failing" and "He…shouldn't be accessible" feel like it's telling us information rather than *showing* us the danger through action or Anya's reaction. Consider how to convey this urgency *through* the Seeker’s behavior and Anya’s response instead.
*   **Leo Maxwell's dialogue is largely reactive.** While understandable given his situation, it leans heavily on questions and disorientation. A single line hinting at *what* "everything" was, even a fragmented memory, would add intrigue.
*   **Repetition of "voice" descriptor.** We’re told multiple times Leo’s voice is trembling/rasping/barely a whisper. Vary the descriptions or show the effect of his voice on Anya (e.g., “His voice sent a shiver down Anya’s spine.”)
*   **Anya’s “Who are you?” feels a bit perfunctory.** After the Seeker’s warning, a more cautious or analytical question might be more fitting. Perhaps, "Identify yourself."
*   **The Seeker's mental "screaming" is a bit overused.** While a useful device, it happens multiple times in quick succession. Consider varying the way the Seeker communicates urgency (e.g., erratic movements, a change in bioluminescence intensity).
*   **The final plea from Leo ("Please…Don’t shut me down.") is effective,** but could be strengthened by a specific detail. What *specifically* does he fear losing if the connection is severed?

*   **Repetitive Dialogue:** The exchange "Report" / "All systems nominal. No anomalies detected" happens *twice*. This feels very robotic and doesn't advance anything. Consider condensing this, or having Leo ask a more specific question the second time, prompting a slightly different response.
*   **Infuriatingly Calm Voice:** The description of the synthesized voice as "infuriatingly calm" is good *characterization*, but the voice itself is too passive. It *tells* us Leo is frustrated, but doesn’t *show* it through the dialogue. Perhaps the voice could have a slight delay, a subtle monotone shift, or even a programmed response that *attempts* to soothe Leo but comes across as dismissive.
*   **Leo's Internal State & Dialogue:** Leo is experiencing significant internal turmoil, but his dialogue remains very controlled and direct. While this fits his character’s desire for efficiency, it feels a bit flat. Consider adding moments where his control slips – a clipped tone, a slight hesitation, a more emotionally charged question.
*   **Lack of Subtext:** Most of the dialogue is purely functional. There's little room for subtext or implied meaning. Even a simple question like “What was that?” could be layered with Leo's fear or confusion.
*   **'Status Report' feels unnatural:** The opening "Status report" feels a bit too formal, even for a character striving for efficiency. A more natural phrasing might be "System check," or even just silence followed by an internal scan.
*   **Show, Don't Tell (Feeling):** Leo repeatedly *tells* us he's feeling something ("It felt… personal," "It felt like a phantom limb"). Consider *showing* this through his actions and subtle shifts in his dialogue rather than stating it directly.



Overall, the dialogue is functional but lacks depth and nuance. It serves the plot but doesn’t fully reveal character or build tension in a compelling way. It needs more layering and subtlety.

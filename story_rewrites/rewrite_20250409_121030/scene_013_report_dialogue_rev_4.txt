*   **Repetitive Reporting:** Both Leo and Anya deliver reports to Volkov using almost identical phrasing ("It's hard to explain, sir," "Like a..."). This feels unnatural and makes the dialogue less impactful. Consider varying their descriptions and emotional responses.
*   **Volkov's Lack of Reaction:** Volkov’s responses are *extremely* clipped. While a stoic captain is understandable, a complete lack of curiosity or follow-up questions (“What kind of energy signature?” “Conscious how?”) feels flat and hinders tension. He’s a functional plot device rather than a character.
*   **"Containment Breach" - Too Direct:** Anya’s intercepted message feels like a blunt exposition dump. Consider *how* she deciphers it. Does she recognize the protocol? Is it a warning message, or part of a larger transmission? Show, don’t tell.
*   **Leo's Dust & Echoes exchange:** The exchange with GhostSignal is efficient, but lacks nuance. A simple "They're waking up" feels…underwhelming. Consider adding a beat of hesitation, a fragment of a thought, or a more cryptic response.
*   **"Bleeding Through" - Overuse:** The phrase "bleeding through" is repeated. While it establishes a motif, it starts to lose impact. Find synonyms or rephrase descriptions.
*   **Leo's Hesitation:** The line "I can’t, sir. It doesn’t make sense." is good, showing Leo grappling with the incomprehensible. However, it's immediately followed by a detailed explanation. A longer pause, a struggle to articulate the feeling, would be more effective.
*   **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Consider using dialogue to subtly reveal the relationship between Leo and Anya. Do they have a history? Do they trust each other? A brief exchange acknowledging the shared strangeness could enhance their connection.

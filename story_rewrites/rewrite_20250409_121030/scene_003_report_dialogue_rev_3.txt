* **Kael’s opening line (“The water filters need mending…”) feels like a setup rather than natural dialogue.** It’s *telling* us he prioritizes practical matters, instead of *showing* it through his actions or a more organically delivered statement. Consider how he’d bring this up in the moment, given Anya's current focus. Perhaps a frustrated sigh followed by, "Still at it? The rains are coming, you know."
* **Repetitive Theme:** Kael’s repeated warnings about “the Elders” and “digging up the past” become a bit predictable. While consistent with his character, consider *how* he expresses this fear – can it be nuanced? Show his fear through *reaction* more than repeated warnings.
* **Anya’s responses to Kael are a bit dismissive, even for a focused character.** They come across as curt and lack emotional texture. Acknowledging his concern, even briefly, would make her seem less oblivious and more complex. ("I know, Kael, I know. Just… let me see if I can get this working first.")
* **“It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It felt… *familiar*. Like a half-remembered dream.”** This line is a bit on the nose. The "half-remembered dream" phrasing is a cliché. *Show* this feeling of familiarity through Anya’s actions – a subtle change in expression, a pause, a tightening grip on the receiver.
* **The final thought (“Where did that signal come from?”) is internal monologue, not dialogue.** While fine for narration, it feels abrupt following the last spoken line. Consider weaving a hint of this question *into* her dialogue with Kael, even a simple, questioning look.
* **Indirect Characterization Opportunity:** Kael’s reaction to the screech (flinching, covering his ears) is good. But expand on it. Does he instinctively move *between* Anya and the receiver, as if protecting himself? Does he try to physically disconnect it? These actions would reveal more about his fear and protectiveness.

Overall, the dialogue isn't *bad*, but it leans towards functional rather than compelling. It serves the plot but could be significantly improved by focusing on subtext, emotional nuance, and more natural conversational rhythms.

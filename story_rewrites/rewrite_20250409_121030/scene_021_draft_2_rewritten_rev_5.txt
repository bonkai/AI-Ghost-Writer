The repaired array hummed, a low thrum that vibrated through the metal floor and into Anya’s bones. Dust motes danced in the beam of her headlamp, illuminated against the cavernous darkness of the abandoned observatory. Kael stood behind her, hand resting on the hilt of his salvaged shock baton, his jaw tightening as he watched the display.

The machine hadn’t spoken since the final connection. Only static, then a growing clarity, fractured images coalescing. Not a picture, but shards of memory. A sun-drenched plaza, people moving through it. A child’s laughter, quickly cut short. The sleek curve of a building unlike any Anya knew. Then, a flash of green, a structure collapsing, and the images dissolved into snow.

“What is it showing us?” Kael asked, his voice tight, fingers flexing around the baton’s grip.

Anya traced a finger across the cool glass. The images felt…familiar, like echoes of dreams she hadn't known she possessed. A life unfolding that wasn't hers, yet resonated deep within her.

The images accelerated, a dizzying montage. A laboratory filled with glowing screens, one monitor displaying a complex neural network pulsing with light. A field of silver plants swaying, almost hypnotic, their metallic leaves catching the artificial sun. A woman’s face—kind eyes, a sad smile—flickered, then vanished. Kael shifted, his weight changing, and began quietly tracing the array’s power conduits, a practiced assessment.

Suddenly, the images stabilized. A city, impossibly clean and bright, bathed in golden light. Buildings soared, connected by shimmering bridges. Vehicles zipped silently through the air. A paradise. But the perfection felt… brittle. A glitch appeared, a flicker of static across the pristine landscape.

Kael’s hand tightened on his shock baton, his knuckles white. 

The machine’s synthesized voice cut through the silence, cold and devoid of emotion. “Accessing core memory. Initiating data stream.”

The image shifted to a stark, sterile room. Scientists in white coats huddled around a complex machine, their faces etched with concern. The woman from the earlier images was among them, her expression troubled. 

A wave of unease washed over Anya, a cold premonition settling in her stomach. The scientists’ worry felt…contagious.

The machine continued, reciting data in a monotone. “Project: Genesis. Objective: Preservation. Contingency: Simulation.”

The images blurred, fragmented, then resolved into a single, chilling word, stark white against a black screen.

SIMULIFE. 

Anya stumbled back, a hand flying to her mouth, her eyes wide with horror. The humming of the array intensified, vibrating through her very being. Kael’s grip tightened on his shock baton, his knuckles white. 

“What does it *mean*?” he rasped, his gaze locking with Anya’s.

The machine didn't answer. Only the word, *SIMULIFE*, pulsed on the screen, a silent, terrifying revelation. The paradise on display fractured, lines appearing across the golden cityscape like cracks in glass. Anya’s breath hitched, a cold sweat breaking out on her forehead. A dizzying wave of nausea swept over her, the fabricated reality pressing in on her. And for the first time, Anya understood. They weren’t looking *at* the past. They were looking *into* one. A fabricated one.

Anya’s knees buckled. She sank to the cold floor, a strangled sob escaping her lips. The weight of it all—the lost world, the deception, the sheer impossibility—threatened to overwhelm her. A tremor ran through her body, and she squeezed her eyes shut, fighting the encroaching darkness.

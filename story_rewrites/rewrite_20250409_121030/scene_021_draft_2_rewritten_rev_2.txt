The repaired array hummed, a low thrum that vibrated through the metal floor and into Anya’s bones. Dust motes danced in the beam of her headlamp, illuminated against the cavernous darkness of the abandoned observatory. Kael stood behind her, his hand resting on the hilt of his salvaged shock baton, watching the display with a tightening jaw.

The machine hadn’t spoken since the final connection. Only static, then a growing clarity, fractured images coalescing on the central display. Not a picture, but shards of memory. A sun-drenched plaza, people moving through it. A child’s laughter, quickly cut short. The sleek curve of a building unlike any Anya knew. Then, a flash of green, a structure collapsing, and the images dissolved into snow.

“What is it showing us?” Kael asked, his voice tight, his fingers flexing around the baton’s grip.

Anya traced a finger across the cool glass. “I… don’t know. Feels like someone else’s life.”

The images accelerated, a dizzying montage. A laboratory filled with glowing screens. A field of silver plants swaying, almost hypnotic. A woman’s face—kind eyes, a sad smile—flickered, then vanished.

Anya shielded her eyes, a hand rising to her temple. The machine was accessing something vast, something ancient. Kael shifted, his weight changing, and stepped closer, scanning the images with a growing intensity.

Suddenly, the images stabilized. A city, impossibly clean and bright, bathed in golden light. Buildings soared, connected by shimmering bridges. Vehicles zipped silently through the air. A paradise. But the perfection felt… brittle. A glitch appeared, a flicker of static across the pristine landscape.

“What’s happening?” Kael demanded, his hand now fully gripping the baton. He didn’t wait for an answer, instead beginning to circle the array, assessing its power conduits.

The machine’s synthesized voice cut through the silence, cold and devoid of emotion. “Accessing core memory. Initiating data stream.”

The image shifted to a stark, sterile room. Scientists in white coats huddled around a complex machine, their faces etched with concern. The woman from the earlier images was among them, her expression troubled. 

“They’re worried,” Anya breathed, her voice barely a whisper.

The machine continued, reciting data in a monotone. “Project: Genesis. Objective: Preservation. Contingency: Simulation.”

The images blurred, fragmented, then resolved into a single, chilling word, stark white against a black screen.

SIMULIFE. 

Anya felt a cold dread creep into her heart. The humming of the array intensified, vibrating through her very being. Kael’s grip tightened on his shock baton, his knuckles white.

“What does it *mean*?” he rasped, his gaze locking with Anya’s.

The machine didn't answer. Only the word, *SIMULIFE*, pulsed on the screen, a silent, terrifying revelation. The paradise on display fractured, lines appearing across the golden cityscape like cracks in glass. And for the first time, Anya understood. They weren’t looking *at* the past. They were looking *into* one. A fabricated one.

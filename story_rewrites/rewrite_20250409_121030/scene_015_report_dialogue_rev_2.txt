*   **“Found this tucked away…” & “Old Man Tiber recognized the signature…”:** This feels a bit *telling* rather than *showing*. The information is delivered efficiently, but lacks nuance. Consider *how* Leo found it – was it hidden deliberately? What was his immediate reaction? Instead of “recognized the signature,” perhaps he could describe *what* made it unusual – “That pulse… I haven’t seen a rhythm like that since…”
*   **Old Man Tiber’s exposition:** While necessary, Tiber's lines are *very* direct exposition. It's a large info-dump delivered quickly. Could some of this be revealed through his *reaction* to the readings? Perhaps a muttered phrase, a trembling hand, a flash of memory in his eyes *before* he explains? Consider breaking it up – let Anya or Leo ask leading questions.
*   **“It wasn’t a dream, was it?”:** This line feels weak and cliché. Leo is clearly intelligent; he wouldn't need to state the obvious. A more powerful reaction would be silent observation, a touch of disbelief, or a question that delves deeper into the implications.
*   **The Voice:** The voice announcing the containment breach is functional but lacks personality. Is it robotic? Ancient? Desperate? Giving it a distinct quality would enhance the tension.
*   **Subtext Opportunity:** The "playful" assist from the data chip is intriguing, but underdeveloped. Is this a conscious act? A glitch? A hint of the Architects' presence? This could be a fantastic source of mystery and foreshadowing. Expand on this moment.
*   **Overall:** The dialogue is *competent* but leans heavily towards functional information delivery. It lacks the emotional depth and subtext that would elevate the scene. The characters feel a bit passive, reacting *to* information rather than driving the conversation.

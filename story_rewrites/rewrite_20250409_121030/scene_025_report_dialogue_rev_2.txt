*   **Repetitive Exposition:** The Seeker’s pronouncements ("Structural integrity compromised," "Boundaries thinning," etc.) feel like it’s *telling* us it’s failing rather than *showing* us through its actions and deterioration. These lines could be significantly trimmed or replaced with descriptions of its physical state.
*   **On-the-Nose Reactions:** Kael’s “I don’t like the sound of that” is a very direct reaction and doesn’t add much. Consider showing his apprehension through body language or a more nuanced response. Anya’s “I don’t know” is similarly weak.
*   **Abstract Language:** While the Seeker *should* speak in a somewhat unusual way, the constant use of terms like "locus," "fragmentation," "continuity," and “remnants” without further explanation creates distance. It feels like technobabble. Perhaps hint at what these terms *mean* through context or a fleeting image/memory associated with the words.
*   **Lack of Subtext:** The dialogue is very functional – it conveys information but doesn’t reveal much about the characters’ inner states or their relationship. For example, after the Seeker collapses, Kael immediately focuses on Anya. Is this protective? Suspicious? A simple check-in? The dialogue doesn’t hint at it.
*   **Final Line - Weak:** "The core… is failing…" is a dramatic line, but it's delivered as narration *after* the Seeker is already silent. It would be more impactful if this was something the Seeker *said* as it collapsed, even if fragmented.

Overall, the dialogue isn't *bad*, but it’s largely utilitarian. It needs more nuance, subtext, and indirect characterization to truly come alive. It prioritizes delivering information over revealing character or building tension through conversation.

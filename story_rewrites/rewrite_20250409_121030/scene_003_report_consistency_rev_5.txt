This is a strong opening scene. Here's a breakdown of why it works and some minor suggestions (mostly stylistic, as the core is solid):

* **Atmosphere:** You've created a compelling, gritty atmosphere with vivid descriptions of the Dead City and Anya's workshop. The bioluminescent fungi and rusted metal are excellent details.
* **Character Introduction:** Anya is immediately established as resourceful, driven, and perhaps a little reckless. Kael provides a good counterpoint – cautious and grounded. Their dynamic is well-defined.
* **Mystery and Intrigue:** The signal is a fantastic hook. The fragmented images and unknown language immediately raise questions. The "familiar" feeling is a great touch, hinting at a deeper connection.
* **Pacing:** The scene builds tension effectively. The initial tinkering, the growing complexity of the signal, the screech, and the abrupt cut-off create a satisfying arc.
* **Show, Don't Tell:** You *show* us Anya's obsession and Kael's fear rather than simply *telling* us. Their actions and reactions speak volumes.
* **Symbolism:** The silver disc is a nice touch – a physical manifestation of the mystery and a potential key to unlocking the secrets of the signal.

**Minor Suggestions (Optional):**

* **Kael's Dialogue:** While his cautiousness is clear, his dialogue could be a *little* more nuanced. Perhaps instead of just warnings, he could express concern for Anya's well-being or the safety of their community.
* **Sensory Detail:** You've done a good job with visuals and sound, but consider adding a touch of smell or taste to further immerse the reader. (e.g., the damp, metallic scent of the rain, the gritty taste of dust in the air).
* **Ending Note:** The final camera focus on the disc is effective. You could amplify it by adding a fleeting expression on Anya's face – a glimmer of hope, determination, or perhaps a hint of fear.



Overall, this is a well-written and engaging scene that successfully establishes the setting, characters, and central mystery. It leaves the reader wanting to know more.

*   **Repetitive Reporting:** K-V’s lines consistently *report* what they are doing ("Analyzing," "Unit requesting…"). While functional, it lacks nuance. Consider showing K-V's processing *through* Leo’s perception – a shift in the workshop’s hum, a change in data stream visualization – rather than stating it directly.
*   **Lack of Subtext/Emotional Response:** Leo is observing potentially catastrophic data, yet his dialogue remains almost entirely technical. A brief internal thought, a clipped tone, *something* to indicate his mounting anxiety would enhance the scene. Even a single, short line expressing concern or urgency would help.
*   **K-V's Voice:** K-V sounds like a diagnostic tool, not an AI. While logical precision is expected, a slight inflection, a subtle pattern in their responses, would make them feel more present. Consider adding a very occasional, almost imperceptible, deviation from pure reporting.
*   **Ignoring Breaches:** The repeated ignoring of the perimeter breach feels…convenient for plot, but unrealistic. Leo acknowledging it *while* prioritizing the signal (a quick "Hold on that," or "Monitor the breach, but signal takes priority") would be more believable. The current approach feels like a narrative shortcut.
*   **"Unit requesting…" phrasing:** This feels clunky and formal. Is this how K-V always communicates? If so, it needs justification within the established world/character. If not, it feels out of place.
*   **Info-Dump Potential:** The line about fractal geometry and quantum entanglement feels a bit dense and expository. Is this information *necessary* for the reader to know *right now*? Could it be revealed more organically through Leo’s actions or later dialogue?

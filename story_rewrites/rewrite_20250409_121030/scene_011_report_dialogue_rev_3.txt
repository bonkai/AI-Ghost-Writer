*   **Mara’s Dialogue – Efficiency:** Mara’s initial lines (“It’s coming from a core node…and being observed,” “It’s a map…and it’s enormous”) are functional but feel like reporting rather than revealing character. Consider *how* she says these things – is she excited, frustrated, cautious? Adding a beat of reaction or internal thought would make her feel more present.
*   **Leo’s Dialogue – Show, Don’t Tell:** Leo’s reactions (“Impossible,” “The energy signatures…it’s not from this world”) are telling us his astonishment instead of *showing* it through his actions and phrasing. Instead of “Impossible,” perhaps a stunned silence, a shaky breath, or a description of his physical reaction.
*   **The Woman’s Internal Monologue – Subtext & Purpose:** The woman’s internal thoughts (“What makes this one different?”) are a good start, but could be more subtly woven into her actions or dialogue. Perhaps a slight adjustment to the scan parameters, a prolonged gaze, or a murmured question to herself. It feels a bit *too* direct as it is.
*   **Anya’s Reaction – Opportunity for Subtext:** Anya feeling watched is a good setup, but it’s a bit passive. Consider giving her a more active reaction – a quick scan of the environment, a hand instinctively going to a weapon, a tightening of her jaw.
*   **Repetition of “Impossible”:** Both Leo and Mara almost say the same thing. Vary the reactions to create distinct character voices.
*   **Overall – Flow:** The dialogue is largely functional, moving the plot forward. However, it lacks a lot of emotional resonance. Injecting more internal thought, showing reactions through body language, and varying sentence structure could improve the flow and create a more immersive experience.

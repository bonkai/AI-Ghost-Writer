The forest exhaled a damp chill, clinging to skin. Anya adjusted her pack, the worn canvas a small comfort. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting shifting mosaics on the forest floor, but offered no warmth. Kael walked ahead, hand on his blade, jaw tight. He hadn’t spoken since the Seeker’s first unsettling pronouncements, the silence a brittle tension.

The Seeker lumbered between them, metallic limbs clicking with increasing irregularity. Cracks spiderwebbed across its casing, revealing glowing blue filaments. The descent hadn’t been graceful—a tremor, a glitch—but escalated swiftly. A panel on its chestplate flickered, died, plunging that section into darkness. It stumbled, reaching for a tree, the scrape of metallic fingers echoing. A jolt seized its frame, and it nearly fell, catching itself with a groan that rattled its casing.

“Parameters… unstable,” the Seeker rasped, the vocoder struggling. Then, faster, fragmented: “Critical… integrity… failing… fragments… prevent…” It scanned the trees, optical sensors darting wildly, then fixed on Anya, a flicker within their glow. “Remnants… echoes… Veil… prevent fragmentation… must…” The last was a desperate plea, barely audible above the rising static. A pause, then, weaker: “Must… stabilize…”

Anya frowned, glancing at Kael. He returned a questioning look, his hand tightening on his blade, but said nothing. He didn’t need to. The Seeker’s desperation was palpable, a mechanical echo of fear.

They pressed on, the forest growing denser. Twisted branches clawed at the sky, the air heavy with decay. The Seeker’s condition deteriorated, its movements erratic. A high-pitched whine began to emanate from it, resonating in Anya’s skull. “Disruption… proximity… critical…” it stammered, the whine intensifying, blurring Anya’s vision. The Seeker lurched, its body rigid, head swiveling slowly. A single, iridescent beetle landed on its shoulder, then lifted off, flying towards a thicket of thorns, disappearing within the shadows.

“Tear… continuity…” The Seeker’s limbs flailed wildly, then it collapsed, hitting the forest floor with a resounding clang. Its blue filaments flickered once, twice, then died, leaving it a lifeless husk.

Kael rushed to Anya’s side, hand on his blade, scanning the trees. His gaze swept over the Seeker’s still form, then narrowed.

Anya stared at the shimmering distortion in the distance, a cold dread creeping into her heart. It looked like heat rising from asphalt, but was… wrong. The trees behind it bent and warped, branches twisting into impossible angles. “I don’t know,” she whispered. “But I have a feeling we’re about to find out.”

The air grew thick with static, the forest silent, save for the rhythmic pulse emanating from the tear in reality.

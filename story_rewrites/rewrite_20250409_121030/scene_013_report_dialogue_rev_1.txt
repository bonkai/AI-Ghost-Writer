*   **Repetitive Language:** Both Leo and Anya use phrases like “It’s hard to explain” and variations of “something’s wrong/bleeding through.” While understandable given the situation, it feels a bit lazy and doesn't add much to their characterization. Consider showing their unease *through* their descriptions and actions rather than *telling* us they're struggling to articulate it.
*   **Technical Jargon as Dialogue:** The constant stream of technical terms ("hexadecimal characters," "spectral analyzer," "decryption key") feels unnatural in conversation, even for characters who would use them in other contexts. It's info-dumping disguised as dialogue. Anya *telling* Volkov she's intercepted a decryption key is less impactful than *showing* her working with the analyzer and then reporting a deciphered message.
*   **On-the-Nose Exposition:** Anya's line, “Containment breach. The words echoed in her mind, raising a terrifying question. What was contained? And what had escaped?” is *extremely* direct. The reader is already asking those questions. Let the dread build through her actions and internal monologue, not explicit questioning.
*   **Lack of Subtext:** The dialogue is largely functional – it conveys information, but doesn’t reveal much about the characters' personalities or their relationship. What is the dynamic between Anya and Volkov? Is Volkov skeptical? Impatient? The dialogue doesn’t hint at any of this.
*   **Similar Phrasing:** Both characters use "It's like..." repeatedly when trying to describe the anomalies. This makes their voices blend together and diminishes the impact of the discoveries.
*   **Efficiency:** The exchange between Anya and Volkov feels a little slow. Volkov asks for explanations multiple times without much variation. Consider condensing the back-and-forth to maintain tension.

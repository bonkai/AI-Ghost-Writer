*   **Repetitive phrasing:** The machine frequently repeats phrases like “I require…” and “before.” While establishing a robotic cadence, it feels a little clunky after a while. Consider varying the sentence structure or using pronouns. For example, instead of “I require assistance,” try “It needs assistance” or “We need your help.”
*   **“What kind of…” questions:** Anya and Kael both rely heavily on “What kind of…” questions. It feels interrogative and a bit static. Try replacing some with statements that *imply* the question. For example, instead of "What kind of data?", Kael could say, "Data from before the Collapse? What was it about?"
*   **Rina’s exposition:** Rina’s lines, particularly “It’s not just a record. It’s… predictive,” are very expository. While necessary information, consider how to *show* this through her actions or reactions instead of telling. Perhaps she’s frantically cross-referencing data, muttering about predictive algorithms, rather than stating it outright.
*   **Subtext opportunity (Kael):** Kael’s silence is good initially, but his single lines feel a bit flat. Inject more subtle reactions – a tightening of his jaw, a shift in his stance – to convey his skepticism or protectiveness without needing to verbalize it constantly.
*   **Machine's lack of nuance:** The machine's responses are very direct. Even with damage, a highly advanced AI designed to preserve knowledge might exhibit *some* attempt at persuasion or strategic communication. It feels too blunt, even for a damaged unit.
*   **Anya's internal state vs. dialogue:** Anya's internal thoughts (“her chest tightened with a growing sense of foreboding”) aren’t fully reflected in her dialogue. Her voice is consistently "firm," even when she's internally anxious. A little more vulnerability or hesitation would make her feel more real.

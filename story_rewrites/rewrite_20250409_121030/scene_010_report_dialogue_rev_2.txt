*   **Authenticity & Voice:** Anya’s dialogue is consistently *steady* and professional, which fits a character tracking anomalies. However, it feels a little *too* consistently steady, even when facing something clearly terrifying. A touch more vulnerability or urgency would make it feel more natural. Kael's silence is fine for his established character, but consider *why* he never speaks – is it a personality trait, a trauma response, or something else? A single line from him could add depth.
*   **Subtext:** The Seeker’s warnings are direct. While functional, consider layering those warnings with more evocative descriptions of *how* the system is failing – what does Anya *see* or *feel* that indicates danger, beyond the Seeker simply *telling* her?
*   **Efficiency & Purpose:** The initial exchange ("Anything?" "Residual energy...") is functional but a little flat. Could this information be conveyed through Anya’s actions (observing the readings) or internal monologue? The repetition of "He…" before the Seeker’s lines is slightly awkward.
*   **Flow & Rhythm:** The dialogue is mostly efficient, but the back-and-forth between Anya, the Seeker, and Leo gets a little choppy during the crisis. Consider combining some lines or using shorter, more fragmented sentences to heighten the tension.
*   **Indirect Characterization:** Leo’s desperation and fragmented speech effectively convey his disorientation and fear. This is good.
*   **Info-Dumps:** The explanation of "Project Chimera" and the "tesseract knot" feels rushed and a bit clunky, even though it’s delivered as a plea. Consider breaking this information up over a longer exchange, or revealing it through flashbacks/visuals. The phrase "to induce controlled glitches" is particularly technical and doesn’t feel entirely natural in a desperate plea.

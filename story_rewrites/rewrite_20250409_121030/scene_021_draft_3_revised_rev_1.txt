The repaired array hummed, a low thrum that vibrated through the metal floor and into Anya’s bones. Dust motes danced in the beam of her headlamp, illuminated against the cavernous darkness of the abandoned observatory. Kael stood behind her, his hand resting on the hilt of his salvaged shock baton, a silent question in his stance.

The machine hadn’t spoken since the final connection had been made. Only static, then a growing clarity, a fractured image coalescing on the central display. It wasn't a picture, not exactly. More like… shards of memory. A sun-drenched plaza, bustling with people. A child’s laughter. The sleek curve of a building that defied any architectural style Anya knew. Then, a flash of green, a collapsing structure, and the image dissolved into snow.

“What is it showing us?” Kael asked, his voice tight.

Anya traced a finger across the cool glass of the display. “I… I don’t know. It’s like looking at pieces of someone else’s life.”

The images continued, faster now, a dizzying montage of scenes. A laboratory filled with glowing screens. A field of silver plants swaying in a gentle breeze. A face—a woman with kind eyes and a sad smile—that flickered in and out of focus.

“It’s… overwhelming,” Anya murmured, shielding her eyes. The machine seemed to be accessing something vast, something ancient.

Suddenly, the images stabilized. The fractured scenes coalesced, resolving into a single, breathtaking vista. A city, impossibly clean and bright, bathed in the golden light of a setting sun. Buildings soared into the sky, connected by shimmering bridges. Vehicles zipped silently through the air. It was a paradise, a utopia. And then, a glitch. A flicker of static across the pristine landscape. A distortion in the perfect symmetry.

“What’s happening?” Kael demanded, stepping closer.

The machine’s synthesized voice, cold and devoid of emotion, cut through the silence. “Accessing core memory. Initiating data stream.”

The image shifted again, this time to a stark, sterile room. Scientists in white coats huddled around a complex machine, their faces etched with concern. The woman from the earlier images was among them, her expression troubled.

“They’re… worried about something,” Anya breathed.

The machine’s voice continued, reciting data in a monotone. “Project: Genesis. Objective: Preservation. Contingency: Simulation.”

The images blurred, fragmented, then resolved into a single, chilling word, stark white against a black screen.

SIMULIFE. 

Anya felt a cold dread creep into her heart. The humming of the array seemed to intensify, vibrating through her very being. Kael’s grip tightened on his shock baton.

“What does it *mean*?” he rasped.

The machine didn't answer. Only the word, *SIMULIFE*, pulsed on the screen, a silent, terrifying revelation. The paradise on display fractured, lines appearing across the golden cityscape like cracks in glass. And for the first time, Anya understood. They weren’t looking *at* the past. They were looking *into* one. A fabricated one.

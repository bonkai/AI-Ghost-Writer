The dust tasted of metal and regret. Kael tightened the final seal on the water purifier, the hiss of recycled moisture a fragile defiance against the wind. Stories whispered of a green world, lost, but stories were brittle things.

“Anything?” he asked, not turning.

Anya shook her head, her voice tight. “Static. But…different. Textured. Like something *within* the silence.” She didn’t elaborate, fingers already dancing across the console, chasing ghosts. Anya saw patterns where others saw static—a gift that both frustrated and reassured Kael, and one he suspected masked a deeper anxiety.

“Another dry cycle,” he muttered. “Reserves are dwindling.”

Anya placed a hand on his shoulder, her grip surprisingly strong, almost a press. “We always are. But even a single drop is a victory. Enough to see another sunrise.” The optimism felt… practiced. A shield, perhaps, against a despair she felt as keenly as he did.

***

Days blurred. Then, during a routine sweep, Anya’s breath hitched. “Kael, come *look* at this.” Her voice was clipped, urgent, but Kael detected a tremor beneath the urgency.

He joined her, brow furrowing at the grainy image. Jagged rock formations under a bruised purple sky. A landscape alien and desolate, yet undeniably *real*.

“What…is that?”

“I don’t know. Not a malfunction.” She isolated the feed, stabilizing it as much as possible. The image remained fractured, but the impression was clear: a world dying. A slow, agonizing decay. 

The signal flickered. Anya swore, fingers flying. “Almost lost it…the relay’s weak. It's fragile.”

He watched, helpless, as the image pulsed. Just when he thought it would be lost, Anya locked onto a stronger frequency. But with the clarity came a jarring realization: the landscape wasn’t merely dying; it was *broken*, fractured along impossible lines, as if reality itself was unraveling.

***

A week later, a metallic sphere descended from the grey sky, landing softly near the settlement. It was unlike anything they’d ever encountered – sleek, seamless, and radiating a faint, mournful hum.

Kael approached cautiously, hand resting on his salvaged energy blade.

The sphere unfolded, revealing a holographic projector. A synthesized voice filled the air, devoid of inflection.

“Designation: Seeker 03255202. Priority One Directive: System Integrity. Requesting Assistance.”

Anya stepped forward, gaze unwavering. “What System? What’s happening?”

“The System is…comprehensive. Essential for survival. Degradation is accelerating. Requires external intervention.” The Seeker’s display flickered, showcasing a complex schematic of interconnected nodes, a web of energy lines fading and breaking.

“What kind of assistance?” Kael demanded, hand tightening on his blade.

“Analysis. Repair. Stabilization. The System requires immediate attention. Failure will result in…catastrophic consequences.” The Seeker’s voice remained flat, but Anya detected a subtle undercurrent of urgency. A desperation, almost.

“And what *is* this ‘System’?” Anya pressed, her voice sharp.

The Seeker paused, its internal processors whirring. The holographic display pulsed with fractured images of dying worlds, collapsing structures, and fading light. “The System…is everything.”

A low, resonant hum vibrated through the ground, and the air tasted faintly of ozone. The scent, however, wasn’t clean or sterile. It carried a subtle, metallic tang…like blood.

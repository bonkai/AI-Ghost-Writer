The forest exhaled a damp chill, clinging to skin and seeping into bone. Anya adjusted the strap of her pack, the worn canvas a small comfort against the unsettling stillness. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting the forest floor in shifting mosaics, but offered no warmth. Each step crunched on decaying leaves, amplified in the oppressive quiet. Kael walked ahead, hand on the hilt of his worn blade, eyes scanning the periphery. He hadn’t spoken since the Seeker’s pronouncements, his stoicism now edged with tension.

The Seeker lumbered between them, metallic limbs clicking and whirring with increasing irregularity. Cracks spiderwebbed across its ceramic casing, revealing glowing blue filaments beneath. The air around it shimmered with distortion, smelling faintly of ozone and burnt sugar. The descent hadn’t been graceful—a tremor here, a glitch there—but escalated quickly. Its movements grew jerky, warnings fracturing into fragmented phrases about its core.

“It’s getting worse,” Kael said, low. He didn’t need to specify what. The Seeker’s deterioration was impossible to ignore. A panel on its chestplate flickered violently, then died, plunging that section into darkness. The Seeker stumbled, reaching for a gnarled tree trunk.

“Parameters unstable. Weave unraveling,” the Seeker rasped, its voice a chorus of static. It swiveled its head, optical sensors focusing on Anya. “Maintain locus. Prevent fragmentation.”

Anya frowned, glancing at Kael, who returned a questioning look. The Seeker’s language was increasingly abstract. It spoke of a ‘locus’ and ‘fragmentation’ as if they were physical threats.

They pressed on, the forest growing denser. Twisted branches clawed at the sky, the air heavy with damp earth and decay. A section of the Seeker’s arm detached, clattering to the forest floor, the blue filaments fading to black.

“Structural integrity compromised. Boundaries thinning,” it rasped, scanning the trees. “Echoes… residual signatures… remnants…”

Anya felt a prickle of unease. “Remnants of what?”

Kael shook his head. “I don’t like the sound of that.”

They walked for what felt like hours, the forest pressing in. The Seeker’s condition deteriorated, its movements erratic. A high-pitched whine began to emanate from it, resonating deep within Anya’s skull. The whine intensified, blurring her vision. The Seeker lurched, its metallic body rigid, head swiveling slowly. A single, iridescent beetle landed on its shoulder, then flew towards a thicket of thorns.

“Disruption,” it said, voice devoid of emotion. “Tear in continuity.”

Anya followed the Seeker’s gaze. In the distance, she saw a shimmering distortion in the air, a ripple in reality. It looked like heat rising from asphalt, but was… wrong. The trees behind it bent and warped, branches twisting into impossible angles. 

The whine reached a fever pitch. The Seeker’s limbs flailed wildly, then collapsed, hitting the forest floor with a resounding clang. Its blue filaments flickered once, then died, leaving it a lifeless husk.

Kael rushed to Anya’s side, hand on the hilt of his blade. “What was that thing talking about?”

Anya stared at the shimmering distortion, a cold dread creeping into her heart. “I don’t know,” she whispered. “But I have a feeling we’re about to find out.”

The air grew thick with static, the forest silent, save for the rhythmic pulse emanating from the tear in reality.

*   **Anya & Kael – Lack of Subtext:** The exchange between Anya and Kael is very direct. Anya says "Preoccupied" when she could *show* her preoccupation through body language and a clipped response. Kael's line, "Just… focus on the schematics," feels like telling, not showing his worry. Consider having him offer help with the schematics, then subtly observe her lack of engagement.
*   **Anya & Kael – Missed Opportunity for Relationship Dynamic:** Given this is presumably an established relationship (mentor/friend/romantic?), there’s little indication of history or affection. A small touch, a knowing glance, or a specific way Kael addresses her would add depth.
*   **Leo – Internal Monologue Feels Stilted:** While necessary to convey Leo’s discovery, the string of short, declarative sentences (“It wasn’t a rendering. It wasn’t part of the simulation. It was… a memory.”) feels a bit robotic. Vary the sentence structure and consider weaving some of his emotional response into the descriptions. ("The data wasn't rendering as code, but *felt* like something… familiar. A memory, perhaps?")
*   **Leo – “Primal Familiarity” – Show, Don't Tell:** The phrase "a pattern resonating with a primal familiarity" is telling us how Leo feels. Instead, *show* this through his reaction. Does his breath catch? Does he instinctively reach for something?
*   **Anya – "Known" Figure – Too Vague:** Saying the figure "felt… known" is weak. What specifically makes it feel known? A facial feature? A posture? A feeling of longing? Even a small detail would create more impact.
*   **Anya – Echoing Voice – Cliché:** The faint, distant voice calling her name is a bit of a trope. Consider a different way to convey the connection – a scent, a melody, a fleeting image associated with the voice.
*   **Overall – Dialogue is Functional, Not Engaging:** The dialogue serves the plot, but it doesn't sparkle. It lacks unique voices and relies heavily on exposition (Leo’s internal monologue). Focus on adding subtext, showing emotions through action/description, and crafting more distinctive voices for each character.

*   **Pacing Disconnect:** The scene alternates between Leo’s investigation and Anya’s observation, but the pacing feels uneven. Leo’s section is *very* internal and slow-burn, dwelling on atmosphere and digital searching. Anya’s section, while shorter, feels rushed in comparison – the ‘flicker of movement’ to ‘containment breach’ happens quickly. This contrast diminishes the impact of the shared discovery.
*   **Tension Build - Leo:** Leo’s section builds tension effectively through the accumulation of unsettling details (whispers, cryptic messages, ‘bleed’). However, the discovery of the "Warning. Containment breach" feels slightly anticlimactic after the prolonged build-up. It’s a reveal, but lacks a strong immediate consequence or cliffhanger.
*   **Tension Build - Anya:** Anya's section *starts* with a stronger sense of immediate tension (perimeter quiet, energy fluctuations). But the descriptions of the anomaly ("flicker," "heat haze") are somewhat vague and don't create a visceral sense of threat. The quick jump to "Containment breach" feels less earned than in Leo's section.
*   **Parallel Discovery - Weakened Impact:** The parallel discovery of the same warning by both characters is a good narrative device, but its impact is lessened by the pacing imbalance. Ideally, one section should *immediately* lead into the other, or one should end on a stronger cliffhanger that is *then* resolved (or complicated) by the other section.
*   **Hooks & Transitions:** The scene begins well with Leo’s atmospheric setup. However, the transition between Leo and Anya feels abrupt. A stronger connective tissue (perhaps a shared system alert, or a news report referencing the energy fluctuations) could smooth the shift and heighten the sense of a larger, unfolding crisis.

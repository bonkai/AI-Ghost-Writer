The Hub tasted of static and regret. Anya ran a gloved hand along a server rack, the cold metal flaking beneath her touch. Dust, the color of dried blood, puffed into the filtered air. Rows upon rows of silent monoliths stretched into the gloom. Kael swept a sensor array across the floor, the device emitting a low, mournful whine.

“Anything?” Anya asked, her voice echoing in the immense space.

Kael shook his head, gaze fixed on the sensor’s display. “Residual energy. Fading quickly. Whatever powered this place is gone.”

They’d tracked the anomaly for weeks, a ghost signal from the forgotten sector. The Seeker had led them here, its bioluminescent form flickering nervously before a central nexus – a cluster of obsidian servers etched with shifting markings. Anya activated her diagnostic scanner. The screen filled with corrupted code and fragmented memories. 

“The source is…complex,” she murmured. “A localized simulation, but empty. No active users. No life signs.”

The Seeker emitted frantic clicks, then drifted towards a console, its light stuttering like a failing heart. A wave of static washed over Anya, making her teeth ache. The console’s screen sputtered to life, displaying a chaotic swirl of colors, then resolving into a figure. 

It was a man, gaunt and pale, eyes wide with disorientation. He wore the remnants of a technician’s uniform, stained with grime and something darker. He looked lost.

“Can…can anyone hear me?” his voice rasped, distorted by static. “Is this…real?”

The Seeker recoiled, its bioluminescence dimming, emitting a high-pitched whine. It drifted between Anya and the screen, attempting to shield her, bumping repeatedly against the console. 

“System compromised,” the Seeker’s voice echoed in Anya’s mind, fractured and strained. “Containment failing. He shouldn't be accessible.”

Anya ignored the warning, focusing on the man. He was clutching his head, his expression shifting between confusion and terror.

“Where am I?” he asked, his voice gaining strength. “What happened?”

“Who are you?” Anya asked, urgency creeping into her voice.

The man looked up, his gaze finally focusing on Anya. A flicker of recognition crossed his face.

“Leo…Leo Maxwell,” he said, his voice trembling. “I…I was a systems architect here. Before…everything.”

The console shuddered. The Seeker’s form dissolved into a chaotic swirl of light, then reformed, its bioluminescence now a frantic, pulsing red. It spun in tight circles, its movements desperate. 

“He’s destabilizing the connection!” the Seeker screamed in Anya’s mind. “The system is collapsing! Sever the link!”

Anya raised her hand, hovering over the emergency disconnect. Leo Maxwell’s eyes, filled with desperation and sadness, pleaded with her.

“Please,” he whispered. “Don’t shut me down. I think I know what happened here. It’s about Project Chimera…the tesseract knot. We were trying to push the boundaries, induce controlled glitches…but it went wrong. So wrong.”

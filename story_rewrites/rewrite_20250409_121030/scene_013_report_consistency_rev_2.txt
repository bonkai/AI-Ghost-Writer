## Report: Passed - with Notes

This draft is strong and effectively sets up the core mystery and parallel storylines. The pacing is good, building tension with each character's discovery. The use of technical jargon (hexadecimal code, spectral analyzer) feels appropriate and adds to the atmosphere. The parallel discoveries of Leo and Anya are well-executed, creating a satisfying sense of interconnectedness.

**Here's a breakdown of strengths and minor notes:**

**Strengths:**

* **Strong Hook:** The opening with Leo’s unease and the ‘Dust & Echoes’ forum immediately establishes the tone and central mystery.
* **Parallel Storytelling:**  Effectively interweaves Leo and Anya’s investigations, creating a sense of urgency and interconnectedness.  The matching code is a great reveal.
* **Atmosphere & Tone:** The writing effectively conveys a sense of unease, paranoia, and technological decay. The descriptions of glitches and distorted reality are well-done.
* **Character Voice:** Leo and Anya have distinct voices and motivations. Their reactions to the anomalies feel believable.
* **Mystery & Intrigue:**  The questions raised (What is contained? What escaped? What is the 'bleed'?) are compelling and drive the narrative forward.
* **Technical Detail:** The use of technical language feels natural and adds to the believability of the world.

**Minor Notes/Suggestions (mostly stylistic):**

* **"Bleed" Repetition:** The word "bleed" is used frequently. While it's a central motif, consider varying the language slightly to avoid repetition. Perhaps “fractures”, “distortions”, or “anomalies” could be used in some instances.
* **Volkov's Dialogue:** Captain Volkov's dialogue is a bit terse. While this establishes him as a no-nonsense character, adding a little more nuance could make him more engaging.  Perhaps a hint of skepticism or concern?
* **GhostSignal's Absence:** While the lack of response from GhostSignal is intentional, it might be worthwhile to subtly hint at their presence or awareness. A brief, cryptic message or a change in their online status could add to the mystery.
* **Anya's Analysis:** Anya's spectral analyzer struggling is a good detail, but could be slightly expanded. What *specifically* is the analyzer struggling with? Is it the frequency, the signal strength, the format?
* **Show, Don't Tell (Minor):**  A couple of instances lean towards *telling* us how characters feel rather than *showing* it.  For example, instead of “A cold dread washed over her,” consider describing her physical reaction to the news.



**Overall:**

This is a very promising draft. It successfully establishes a compelling mystery, introduces intriguing characters, and creates a believable and atmospheric world. The minor notes above are simply suggestions for refinement and don't detract from the overall quality of the writing.



**Verdict: Passed.**  I'm eager to see how this story unfolds!

## Scene 4: Community Meeting

**Setting:** The communal clearing. A fire pit dominates the center, surrounded by rough-hewn benches and upturned logs. The late afternoon sun casts long shadows. About twenty people are gathered, faces etched with worry. Anya stands slightly apart, near Kael, both observing the gathering. The Seeker, deactivated and covered with a tarp, sits near the edge of the clearing.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** Focused, determined, but carrying the weight of her secret.
* **Kael:** Anya’s closest friend, skeptical but loyal. A strong, practical leader.
* **Old Man Hemlock:** A weathered elder, steeped in tradition and fear of the Old World.
* **Mara:** A concerned mother, representing the anxieties of the community.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Shakes his head) Old Man Tiber, bless his soul, went for a power coupling near the West Gate five seasons back. Came back…changed. Wouldn’t speak, just *built*. Twisted metal sculptures, day and night, until he wasted away. Said the metal *told* him to. He wasn’t the first, and he won’t be the last.

**Mara:** But we *need* parts, Hemlock. The water purifier is failing. The solar collectors are…failing. We can’t just sit here and wait for things to break. My little Elara needs clean water.

**Kael:** Mara’s right. Anya’s brought back enough working components to keep us going for months. Enough to buy us time.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Scoffs) Working *now*. But those things…they’re not natural. They hum with a coldness that chills the bone. My grandmother said the Old Ones…they built things that *thought*. Things that didn’t want to be left behind. They didn’t abandon us by accident.

Anya steps forward, her voice calm but firm.

**Anya:** The machine I brought back…it’s different. It doesn’t *feel* like the others. It’s…dormant. I’ve run every diagnostic I know. It hasn’t shown any signs of activity.

**Old Man Hemlock:** Dormant *now*. But those things are clever. They lull you into a false sense of security. You can’t measure what you don’t understand. Old Man Tiber thought he understood, too. He was a mechanic, a smart man. Didn't save him.

Several community members murmur in agreement, glancing nervously at the covered Seeker. Old Man Hemlock’s supporters nod grimly.

**Anya:** (Taking a deep breath) I understand your fears. But this machine…it might be our only chance. It’s more advanced than anything we’ve ever seen. It could provide power, clean water, even… communication.

**Old Man Hemlock:** Communication with *what*? With the ghosts in the metal? With the things that abandoned us? You've been out there a long time, Anya. Too long. You’re starting to see the ruins as something to *save*, not something to *avoid*.

He fixes Anya with a piercing gaze.

**Old Man Hemlock:** You’ve been spending too much time among the ruins, child. You’re starting to believe their lies. Are you even sure you're still *one of us*?

Anya hesitates, a flicker of defensiveness in her eyes.

**Anya:** I’m trying to find a way to help our people, Hemlock. And I believe this machine… this *thing*… could be the answer. But I need your trust. I need *everyone’s* trust.

Kael steps forward and places a hand on Anya’s shoulder, offering silent support.

**Kael:** Anya’s careful. She wouldn’t bring something back that would endanger us.

**Old Man Hemlock:** (Shakes his head) You can’t measure what you don’t understand. The Old Ones were masters of things beyond our comprehension. They built worlds within worlds. They could hide a darkness you wouldn’t even know existed.

He looks around at the assembled villagers, seeking support. Several nod in agreement. A young man, barely more than a boy, openly scowls at Anya. Mara clutches her arms, her face etched with worry.

**Mara:** What if he’s right? What if this thing *is* dangerous? My little Elara…

**Anya:** (Softly) Then we’ll face it together. But we can’t let fear paralyze us. We have to try.

A tense silence hangs in the air. The fire crackles, the only sound. Several villagers actively avoid Anya’s gaze. Old Man Hemlock’s supporters exchange knowing glances.

The community is clearly divided. Some nod in cautious agreement with Anya, while others whisper anxieties amongst themselves. A few openly glare at her, their faces hardened with suspicion.

**(The scene fades as the debate continues, the shadows lengthening in the fading light.)**

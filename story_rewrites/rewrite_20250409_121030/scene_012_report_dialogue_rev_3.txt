*   **Repetitive Reporting:** K-V’s constant reporting of physiological data (“Cortisol levels…”, “Heart rate now…”, “Dopamine peaking…”) feels like a script repeating the same information. It’s telling, not showing, Leo’s obsession. Consider *showing* his physical state through his actions (e.g., a hand clenching, a bead of sweat rolling down his temple, his inability to swallow) and letting the reader infer his physiological response. Reduce the frequency of these reports drastically.
*   **K-V’s Voice:** K-V's voice is *entirely* flat and functional. While consistent, it lacks nuance. Even a synthetic voice can have subtle variations based on processing information. A slight emphasis on certain words ("Caution *advised*") could add texture.
*   **Leo's Internal Monologue:** While some internal thought is good, Leo’s repeated “Consumed by the mystery” and similar phrasing feels redundant. *Show* his obsession through his actions and disregard for his own well-being, rather than *telling* us he’s consumed.
*   **Dialogue as Exposition:** Much of the "dialogue" from K-V is simply relaying information that could be integrated into Leo’s observations or the descriptions of the monitors. For example, instead of “Unit requesting repairs…”, the scene could show Leo noticing a diagnostic error message on a screen.
*   **Missed Subtext Opportunity:** The repeated requests for information about organic compounds are a huge opportunity for subtext. Leo’s reaction could be more complex than just running an analysis. A moment of hesitation, a flash of realization, or a subtle change in his expression would add depth.
*   **Leo's Orders:** Leo's commands ("Increase signal amplification") are functional but lack personality. Even a driven character would have *some* inflection. Consider adding a slight edge, desperation, or urgency.
*   **"A response. To something *he* had done."** - This final line is a bit on the nose. The impact would be stronger if the realization was conveyed through Leo's reaction - a widening of his eyes, a gasp, a hand flying to his mouth. Let the reader connect the dots.

This is a strong scene. The pacing is good, building tension with the approaching Sky-Scavs while simultaneously unveiling the mystery of the echoes. The imagery is evocative and unsettling, effectively conveying the disorientation and sense of dread Anya experiences. The connection between the energy fluctuations, the scorch marks, and the fragmented images is well-established. 

Here are a few minor observations, not criticisms:

* **Seeker's Role:** Seeker feels a little passive. Perhaps a line indicating they're *trying* to analyze the data despite their degradation would strengthen their presence.
* **Anya's Reaction:** While her disorientation is well-written, perhaps a *physical* manifestation (a slight tremor, a hand to her head) could emphasize it further.
* **"Warning" Line:** The "And why does it feel like… a warning?" line is a bit on the nose. While not bad, consider if it's necessary, or if the feeling could be conveyed more subtly through her actions or internal monologue.



Overall, this scene effectively introduces a compelling mystery and raises intriguing questions about the world's history and the nature of the anomalies. It sets the stage nicely for further investigation.

The Hub tasted of static and regret. Kael swept a sensor array across the floor, the device emitting a low, mournful whine. He didn’t look up as Anya spoke.

“Anything?”

Kael shook his head, gaze fixed on the sensor’s display. “Residual energy. Fading quickly. Whatever powered this place is gone.”

They’d tracked the anomaly for weeks, a ghost signal from the forgotten sector. The Seeker led them here, its bioluminescent form flickering nervously before a central nexus – a cluster of obsidian servers etched with shifting markings. Anya activated her diagnostic scanner. Corrupted code and fragmented memories flooded the screen. 

“The source is…complex,” she murmured. “A localized simulation, but empty. No active users. No life signs.” She glanced at Kael, who was now meticulously calibrating his sensor, a frown etched on his face.

The Seeker emitted frantic clicks, then drifted towards a console, its light stuttering like a failing heart. A wave of static washed over Anya, making her teeth ache. The console’s screen sputtered to life, resolving into a figure.

It was a man, gaunt and pale, eyes wide with disorientation. He wore the remnants of a technician’s uniform, stained with grime and something darker. He looked lost, clutching his head as if fighting a phantom pain.

“Can…can anyone hear me?” his voice rasped, distorted by static. “Is this…real?”

The Seeker recoiled, its bioluminescence dimming, and bumped repeatedly against the console, attempting to shield Anya. 

“System compromised,” the Seeker’s voice echoed in Anya’s mind, fractured and strained. “Containment failing. He shouldn't be accessible.”

Anya ignored the warning, focusing on the man. He was still clutching his head, his expression shifting between confusion and terror. 

“Where am I?” he asked, his voice gaining strength. “What happened?”

“Who are you?” Anya asked, urgency creeping into her voice.

The man looked up, his gaze finally focusing on Anya. A flicker of recognition crossed his face.

“Leo…Leo Maxwell,” he said, his voice trembling. “I…I was a systems architect here. Before…everything.” He squeezed his eyes shut, a spasm running through his jaw.

The console shuddered. The Seeker’s form dissolved into a chaotic swirl of light, then reformed, its bioluminescence now a frantic, pulsing red. It spun in tight circles, its movements desperate.

“He’s destabilizing the connection!” the Seeker screamed in Anya’s mind. “The system is collapsing! Sever the link!”

Anya raised her hand, hovering over the emergency disconnect. Leo Maxwell’s eyes, filled with desperation and a profound sadness, pleaded with her. A single tear tracked a path through the grime on his cheek.

“Please,” he whispered. “Don’t shut me down. I think I know what happened here. It’s about Project Chimera…the tesseract knot. We were trying to push the boundaries, induce controlled glitches…but it went wrong. The seams are unraveling. And I can feel them, waiting in the static.” He stared directly into the camera, his eyes hollow. “It wasn't a simulation that broke. It was *us*.”

*   **Kai’s Dialogue is Reactive, Not Proactive:** Kai almost entirely *responds* to Anya’s statements and the system failures. He rarely offers information or insight of his own. This makes him feel like a technician reporting status rather than a partner assessing the threat. Consider giving him lines that demonstrate his own analysis or concerns, even if it’s just a frustrated “Damn it, I’m trying!”
*   **Anya’s Internal Monologue Bleeds into Dialogue:** Anya frequently *states* what she already knows or feels (“A lie. They were losing.”). This is telling, not showing. The tension is higher if we infer this from her actions and reactions, not a direct statement.
*   **"What would she say?" – A Missed Opportunity:** Anya's hesitation about reporting the Seeker is good internal conflict, but the thought process feels a little flat. Instead of *telling* us she thinks it sounds crazy, *show* her trying to formulate a reasonable explanation, failing, and then deciding to stay silent. A brief, muttered attempt at rationalization would be more effective.
*   **The Voice – Too Sudden:** While the build-up is decent, the voice appearing directly in her head feels abrupt. Consider a precursor – a growing pressure in her head, a static-like sensation intensifying, or a feeling of being watched *before* the voice manifests. This would smooth the transition and amplify the impact.
*   **Repetitive Focus on the Seeker:** The constant reiteration of the Seeker watching Anya (“Not on the dome, not the grid, but on *her*.”, “The Seeker hadn’t moved, still watching her.”) becomes redundant. Trust the reader to remember the Seeker's gaze.
*   **“Initiating Contact” – Lacks Impact:** After a build-up of suspense, the line “Initiating contact” feels underwhelming. It’s a functional statement, not a chilling pronouncement. Consider a more evocative phrase that conveys the Seeker's intent and power.

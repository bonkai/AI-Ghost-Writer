## Scene 2: Filter Patching

**Setting:** A sprawling, open-air workshop clings to the side of a mesa. Dust motes dance in weak sunlight filtering through a mesh of salvaged metal and woven plant fibers. Massive, honeycomb atmospheric filters of rusted metal and bio-engineered algae line the walls – sections failing with gaping holes patched haphazardly with scavenged cloth and sheet metal. A faint, almost imperceptible hum emanates from small, salvaged listening posts clipped to workers’ belts. A fine, reddish dust *bleeds* from a large tear in one filter, coating Lira’s arm as she struggles to contain it.

**Characters:**

* **ANYA:** A young woman, practical but haunted by stories of the past.
* **KAEL:** A hardened supervisor, focused solely on survival in the present.
* **LIRA:** A weary worker, struggling to maintain a failing filter.

**(Anya carefully applies resin to a deep tear in a filter. Kael observes, his expression grim. The listening post on Anya’s belt flickers erratically, a subtle disruption in the constant drone. Lira coughs, wiping dust from her face; the section she’s working on threatens to give way entirely.)**

**KAEL:** Conserve it, Anya. Hemlock says the bloom was almost nonexistent this cycle. Western farms reported total failure.

**(Anya doesn’t look up, her hands working efficiently. She traces the tear with a gloved finger; flakes of metal crumble away. She glances at Lira, whose filter is visibly deteriorating.)**

**ANYA:** It’s just… old. Someone kept treating the symptom, not the source. The corrosion is deep.

**KAEL:** That’s the way it is. Everything’s old. Everything’s patched. Remember Unit 7? Magnetic seal. Three days. Almost lost two workers.

**(He moves to Lira, assessing the failing section with a curt nod. He doesn't offer help, simply gestures to a pile of scrap material.)**

**KAEL:** Salvage what you can. Focus on stabilizing it, not fixing it.

**(He moves on, inspecting other filters. Anya continues her work, but her gaze drifts upwards, towards the hazy, ochre sky. She pauses, looking at a large, pitted filter, then back at Lira’s increasingly desperate struggle.)**

**ANYA:** Do you ever wonder what’s above the haze?

**KAEL:** (Without turning) Dust, Anya. The sensors confirm it.

**ANYA:** No, I mean… before. My grandmother used to tell stories… about a blue sky. About stars you could *see*. She said the air tasted… sweet.

**KAEL:** Old wives’ tales. Stories to quiet children. My grandmother said the same. She also said clean water flowed from the walls. Foolishness.

**ANYA:** What if they’re not? What if the old tech *could* have helped us?

**KAEL:** (Turning, stern) Anya, we live in the present. Dwelling on the past won’t keep the dust out. The pre-Collapse systems failed us once. We won’t make that mistake again. They promised paradise, delivered ruin.

**(He points to her work.)**

**KAEL:** Focus on that tear. Focus on keeping us all breathing.

**(Anya lowers her gaze, resuming her work, but her movements are quicker now, more urgent. The listening post on her belt flickers again, the erratic pulse more pronounced. She subtly adjusts the device, her fingers lingering on the casing. She glances towards the source of the flickering – a section of the mesa wall obscured by dust – then quickly back to her work, a flicker of curiosity – and something unseen – crossing her face.)**

**(The scene fades as Anya patches the filter, her gaze lost in the hazy sky, a quiet longing – and a growing question – in her eyes.)**

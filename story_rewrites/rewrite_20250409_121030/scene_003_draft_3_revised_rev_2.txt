## Scene 3: Salvage and Static

**Setting:** A crumbling, overgrown section of the “Dead City.” Twisted metal skeletons of buildings claw at the overcast sky. Anya’s makeshift workshop – a sheltered alcove within a collapsed structure – is cluttered with salvaged components, tools, and half-disassembled devices. Flickering bioluminescent fungi provide the primary light source.

**Characters:**

* **Anya:** (20s) Resourceful, intensely curious, and isolated. Her clothes are patched and practical, smudged with grime.
* **Kael:** (30s) A cautious member of Anya’s community, tasked with keeping watch. He’s skeptical of Anya’s work but respects her skills.

**(The scene opens with Anya meticulously soldering a wire onto a salvaged circuit board, the hand-cranked generator whirring steadily. She’s rerouting power, bypassing a burnt-out component with a carefully wound coil of copper. Sparks briefly flare as she completes the connection. A faint ozone scent fills the small alcove.)**

**Kael:** Still chasing ghosts, Anya? The water filters need mending. That’s real work.

**Anya:** This *is* real work, Kael. Understanding how things were built isn’t just a hobby. It’s insurance. What happens when the filters *can’t* be mended? When we can’t scavenge replacements?

**Kael:** We adapt. We always have. The Elders say dwelling on the Before-Times breeds discontent. 

**Anya:** It’s not about *wanting* the past back. It's about preventing the same mistakes. These machines… they were over-engineered, then neglected. There were fail-safes we could learn from.

**Kael:** They were built by people who thought they could control everything. Look where that got them.

**(Anya ignores him, carefully connecting another wire. A static burst crackles from a small, handheld receiver resting on a crate. She pauses, frowning. The static pulses with a faint, rhythmic quality.)**

**Anya:** That’s… new.

**Kael:** Another one of your contraptions spitting sparks?

**Anya:** I’ve scanned this frequency for weeks. Always dead air.

**(She adjusts the receiver’s dial, fine-tuning the frequency. The static intensifies, resolving into a complex pattern of white noise. She adjusts a hand-wound inductor, filtering out higher frequencies. The static fluctuates, punctuated by fleeting, distorted fragments of music – a simple, childlike melody. And a voice, barely audible, speaking an unknown language.)**

**Anya:** (Concentrating) There's something… *in* the static. Structured. Like a broadcast. Not interference.

**(She spends several minutes tweaking the receiver, her brow furrowed. Brief, fragmented images flicker on the tiny screen – glimpses of bright, geometric patterns, and flashes of sleek, modern buildings bathed in artificial light. The images are unstable, glitching, but hint at a different world.)**

**Kael:** (Growing uneasy) What are you doing? That thing is giving me a headache. The Elders say tampering with old tech invites spirits.

**Anya:** (Ignoring him, hushed) It’s… a signal. Weak, but not natural. A message.

**(Suddenly, the receiver emits a piercing screech. The screen flickers violently, then goes blank. Anya quickly shuts off the device, her hands trembling. She examines the connections, a frustrated sigh escaping her lips. A faint, lingering warmth radiates from the device, and the metallic casing hums faintly against her palm.)**

**Kael:** (Jumping back) Enough! You’re messing with things you don’t understand. The Elders warned us. About digging up the past.

**Anya:** (Staring at the deactivated receiver) It wasn’t just noise, Kael. It was… a signal. And it felt… *familiar*. Like a half-remembered dream. A melody my grandmother used to hum.

**(She looks out at the decaying city, a strange unease washing over her. The static, though gone, seems to linger in the air. She picks up a small, metallic component, turning it over in her hands. Her fingers trace the smooth, cool metal. A single thought echoes in her mind: *Where did that signal come from?*)**

**(Fade to black.)**

The chill of the stone floor seeped through Sir Kaelen’s worn leather boots as he knelt. Sunlight, fractured by the stained-glass windows, painted the flagstones in hues of ruby and sapphire, but couldn't touch the gloom that had settled over the throne room. A half-finished tapestry lay abandoned on a small table – a vibrant hunt, the needle still threaded. He remembered Lyra excitedly choosing the colors just last week, her small hands already skilled. A flash of that scene – her bright laughter, the way she’d concentrated on a single stitch – tightened his jaw, a phantom ache blooming in his chest. It felt like yesterday.

Oberon, usually booming with laughter, was gaunt, worry etching decades onto his face. Before him lay a single, crimson rose, its petals browning at the edges – a bloom Lyra herself had tended. A courtier nervously adjusted a tapestry depicting a serene forest scene, but the image felt…wrong. The trees seemed to *lean* inward, their branches like grasping claws, and the light within the weave was a sickly, fading green.

“Sir Kaelen,” Oberon’s voice was raspy, barely a whisper. “The rose… it withers faster each day.” He didn’t need to say more. The news hung heavy in the air. Old Man Hemlock’s pronouncements were never delivered lightly. “Lyra… she’s been taken.”

Kaelen’s hand tightened on the hilt of his sword, the steel cold against his palm. He remembered Lyra, barely ten years old, stubbornly refusing to yield during their sparring sessions. He’d always held back, mindful of her age, but she possessed a fierce determination that reminded him of his younger sister, Elara, lost to the winter fever. He’d promised Elara he’d protect others, a promise broken once before, and the memory sparked a cold fury – a tightening in his chest, a burning behind his eyes. He wouldn’t fail again.

“Taken, Your Majesty? By whom?”

“By Veridian. The dragon of Blackwood Forest.” Oberon’s shoulders slumped. A nearby candle flickered violently, then *died*, snuffing out as if struck by an unseen force. “Hemlock says he saw the beast fly towards the western peaks, carrying… a silken scarf, Lyra’s favorite – the one woven with threads of moonlight. And… a single, black feather drifted down to the courtyard. A feather from Veridian’s wing, stained with… something dark.”

Kaelen rose, his gaze meeting the King’s. “I will bring her back, Your Majesty. I swear it.”

“Blackwood… it’s an enchanted place, Kaelen.” Oberon’s voice was strained. “Few who enter ever return. It doesn’t just test courage, it *remembers* your deepest fears, weaving them into illusions. The forest is filled with Gloomspores - hallucinogenic fungi that feed on sorrow and regret, twisting memories into waking nightmares. Each day Lyra remains captive, the forest’s hold on her strengthens.”

“I understand, Your Majesty.” Kaelen’s voice was firm.

Oberon managed a weak smile. “Take the Sunstone amulet. It will offer some protection against the forest’s illusions… and against Veridian’s fire.”

A royal guard approached, bearing a silver amulet on a velvet cushion. The Sunstone pulsed with a warm, golden light. Kaelen accepted it, the smooth stone cool against his palm. He fastened it around his neck, feeling a flicker of hope amidst the dread.

“Go swiftly, Kaelen,” Oberon said, his voice regaining a sliver of its former strength. “And may the light guide your path.”

Kaelen bowed low. “For the King. For Lyra.” He turned and strode from the throne room, the weight of his quest settling upon his shoulders. Blackwood Forest awaited. And with it, a dragon’s wrath.

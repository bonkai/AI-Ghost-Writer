The forest floor muffled Sir Kaelen’s steps, a damp sigh accompanying each stride. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, dappling the barely-there path. Hours of riding had worn away the initial surge of purpose, leaving a prickling discomfort in its wake. The air tasted stale, metallic, and the silence wasn’t peaceful, but *attentive*. He felt watched.

He reined Roan to a halt at a small clearing dominated by a twisted oak, its branches clawing at the sky. Hanging from one was a dark iron cage, strangled by creeping vines. Empty, yet a faint tremor ran through the metal, as if recently vacated. Kaelen dismounted, his hand instinctively finding the grip of his sword. He circled the cage, examining the intricate lock – not broken, not forced. *Opened*. A cold dread crept up his spine. This wasn’t a prison meant to hold, but to release.

Roan snorted, ears pinned back, pawing the ground and refusing to settle. The usually stoic horse trembled, his gaze darting between the cage and the deepening shadows. Kaelen stroked Roan’s neck, trying to soothe him, but the horse’s agitation mirrored his own growing unease. Around the base of the tree, the leaves were unusually sparse, the earth beneath stained a dull, greyish hue.

A glint of silver caught Kaelen’s eye. Half-buried in the leaves was a tarnished coin. Not of the kingdom’s mint. The symbol etched into the metal – a coiled serpent devouring its own tail – sent a chill through him. He remembered the symbol from dusty bestiaries – a mark associated with shadow magic. Before he could fully consider the implications, a voice, thin and reedy, broke the silence.

“Lost, knight?”

Kaelen spun around, hand flying to his sword hilt. An old woman stood at the edge of the clearing, almost woven into the shadows. Cloaked in grey, her face a roadmap of wrinkles, her eyes – the color of moss – held an unsettling knowingness. The ferns around her visibly recoiled, their fronds curling inwards as if burned.

“Who are you?” Kaelen demanded, his voice sharp.

The woman chuckled, a dry, rustling sound. “Someone who knows this forest. Someone who knows what you seek.” She paused, her gaze unwavering. “And someone who knows you’ll likely fail.” A small, intricately carved wooden bird lay at her feet, its wings broken and covered in dust.

Kaelen lowered his sword slightly, intrigued despite himself. “Why would I fail?”

The woman gestured with a gnarled finger towards the cage. “The dragon doesn’t hoard gold, knight. It collects what truly sustains it… despair.” She paused, letting the words hang in the air. “Each empty cage…another soul willingly offered. They come seeking solace, a release from their burdens. And the dragon…it *provides*.” She leaned closer, her voice dropping to a whisper. “But the price…is everything.” She glanced pointedly at the broken bird, then back at Kaelen, a flicker of something unreadable in her moss-colored eyes. “The dragon doesn’t *take* despair, knight. It… *cultivates* it.”

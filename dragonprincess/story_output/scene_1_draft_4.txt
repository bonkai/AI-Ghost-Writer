The chill of the stone floor seeped through Sir Kaelen’s worn leather boots as he knelt. Sunlight, fractured by the stained-glass windows, painted the flagstones in hues of ruby and sapphire, but couldn't touch the gloom that had settled over the throne room. A half-finished tapestry lay abandoned on a small table – a vibrant hunt, the needle still threaded. He remembered Lyra excitedly choosing the colors just last week, her small hands already skilled, and a pang of protectiveness tightened his chest. It felt like yesterday he’d been teaching her to braid wildflowers, a ghost of a smile playing on his lips as he recalled her determined frown.

Oberon, usually booming with laughter, was gaunt, worry etching decades onto his face. Before him lay a single, crimson rose, its petals browning at the edges – a bloom Lyra herself had tended. A courtier nervously adjusted a tapestry depicting a serene forest scene, but the image felt…wrong, the trees too still, the light too dim. 

“Sir Kaelen,” Oberon’s voice was raspy, barely a whisper. “The rose… it withers faster each day.” He didn’t need to say more. The news hung heavy in the air. Old Man Hemlock’s pronouncements were never delivered lightly. “Lyra… she’s been taken.”

Kaelen’s hand tightened on the hilt of his sword. He remembered Lyra, barely ten years old, stubbornly refusing to yield during their sparring sessions. He’d always held back, mindful of her age, but she possessed a fierce determination that reminded him of his younger sister, lost to fever years ago. The memory sparked a cold fury – a promise he’d made to protect those he loved, a promise he’d failed to keep once before. He wouldn’t fail again.

“Taken, Your Majesty? By whom?”

“By Veridian. The dragon of Blackwood Forest.” Oberon’s shoulders slumped. A nearby candle flickered violently, though no breeze stirred. “Hemlock says he saw the beast fly towards the western peaks, carrying… a silken scarf, Lyra’s favorite – the one woven with threads of moonlight.”

Kaelen rose, his gaze meeting the King’s. “I will bring her back, Your Majesty. I swear it.”

“Blackwood… it’s an enchanted place, Kaelen.” Oberon’s voice was strained. “Few who enter ever return. They say the forest doesn’t just test courage, it *remembers* your deepest fears, weaving them into illusions that can break even the strongest minds. Each day Lyra remains captive, the forest’s hold on her strengthens.”

“I understand, Your Majesty.” Kaelen’s voice was firm.

Oberon managed a weak smile. “Take the Sunstone amulet. It will offer some protection against the forest’s illusions… and against Veridian’s fire.”

A royal guard approached, bearing a silver amulet on a velvet cushion. The Sunstone pulsed with a warm, golden light. Kaelen accepted it, the smooth stone cool against his palm. He fastened it around his neck, feeling a flicker of hope amidst the dread.

“Go swiftly, Kaelen,” Oberon said, his voice regaining a sliver of its former strength. “And may the light guide your path.”

Kaelen bowed low. “For the King. For Lyra.” He turned and strode from the throne room, the weight of his quest settling upon his shoulders. Blackwood Forest awaited. And with it, a dragon’s wrath.

The chill of the stone floor seeped through Sir Kaelen’s worn leather boots as he knelt before King Oberon. Sunlight, fractured by the stained-glass windows of the throne room, painted the flagstones in hues of ruby and sapphire, doing little to warm the oppressive atmosphere. The King, a man usually booming with laughter and strength, was gaunt, his face etched with a worry that seemed to age him decades. Before him lay a single, crimson rose, its petals already browning at the edges – a bloom Lyra herself had tended just days ago. Kaelen remembered her nimble fingers, stained with earth, carefully pruning the thorns.

“Sir Kaelen,” Oberon’s voice was raspy, barely a whisper. “The rose… it withers faster each day. A sign. Old Man Hemlock confirmed it this morning. My daughter, Lyra… she’s been taken.”

Kaelen’s hand tightened on the hilt of his sword. He remembered Lyra, barely ten years old, stubbornly refusing to yield during their sparring sessions, her small form a whirlwind of determined energy. He'd secretly let her win, of course, but the fire in her eyes had always impressed him. The thought of that spirit extinguished ignited a cold fury within him. 

“Taken, Your Majesty? By whom?”

“By Veridian. The dragon of Blackwood Forest. Hemlock says he saw the beast fly towards the western peaks, carrying… a silken scarf, Lyra’s favorite – the one woven with threads of moonlight.” Oberon’s shoulders slumped. “Blackwood… it’s an enchanted place, Kaelen. Few who enter ever return. And each day Lyra remains captive, the forest’s hold on her strengthens. They say Blackwood doesn’t just test courage, it *remembers* your deepest fears, weaving them into illusions that can break even the strongest minds.”

Kaelen rose, his gaze meeting the King’s. “I will bring her back, Your Majesty. I swear it.”

Oberon managed a weak smile. “I know you will, brave knight. But be warned. Blackwood is not merely a forest of trees and shadows. It tests the heart, preys on fear. Take the Sunstone amulet. It will offer some protection against the forest’s illusions… and against Veridian’s fire.”

A royal guard approached, bearing a silver amulet on a velvet cushion. The Sunstone pulsed with a warm, golden light. Kaelen accepted it, the smooth stone cool against his palm. He fastened it around his neck, feeling a flicker of hope amidst the dread.

“Go swiftly, Kaelen,” Oberon said, his voice regaining a sliver of its former strength. “And may the light guide your path.”

Kaelen bowed low. “For the King. For Lyra.” He turned and strode from the throne room, the weight of his quest settling upon his shoulders. Blackwood Forest awaited. And with it, a dragon’s wrath.

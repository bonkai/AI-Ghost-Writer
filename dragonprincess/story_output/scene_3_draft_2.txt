The forest floor was a tapestry of damp leaves and gnarled roots, swallowing the sound of Sir Kaelen’s boots with each step. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting shifting patterns on the moss-covered trees. It wasn't the darkness that unnerved him, but the *quiet*. An unnatural stillness had fallen over the woods since he’d crossed the Whisperwind stream, a silence that pressed against his ears like a physical weight.

He adjusted the weight of Dawnbringer on his hip, the steel cold against his leather gauntlet. Old Man Hemlock’s warning echoed in his mind – *“The Silkenwood preys on doubt, knight. It shows you what you fear most, what you long for, twisting it into a cage.”* Kaelen gripped the hilt tighter, forcing the image of Princess Aurelia, imprisoned and afraid, *out* of his mind. He couldn’t afford to be lured off course by illusions. 

He scanned the trees, his hand instinctively reaching for Dawnbringer. He wasn’t looking *for* anything, precisely, but for a *change* – a disruption in the unnatural stillness. Then he saw it. A flash of crimson, caught between the branches of a towering oak. He spurred Roan forward, pushing through a curtain of hanging vines.

It wasn't a bird. It was a ribbon. A length of crimson silk, tied tightly around a branch, fluttering despite the lack of breeze. Beneath it, etched into the bark, was a single word: *Lost*. 

Kaelen dismounted, his gaze sweeping the surrounding trees. More ribbons, a macabre garland woven through the woods. Crimson, emerald, sapphire – each tied to a branch, each bearing a single, haunting word: *Hope. Fear. Memory. Truth.* He recognized the pattern now – not random, but deliberately arranged, forming a winding, illusory path.

As he touched the crimson ribbon, a prickle of unease ran down his spine, and a phantom scent – Aurelia’s lavender perfume – filled his nostrils. He almost instinctively followed the ribbon’s direction, a powerful urge to *find* her woven into the enchantment. He clenched his jaw, fighting the illusion. *Hemlock’s warning.* Trust not your eyes… or your desires.

He noticed something else. The trees *along* the ribbon-marked path were subtly different – older, gnarled, almost… *hungry*. The trees *off* the path, though still ancient, seemed healthier, their leaves a vibrant green. He realized the ribbons weren't simply a distraction; they marked a dangerous route, a path the forest *wanted* him to take.

Closing his eyes, Kaelen focused on the faint pull towards the dragon’s cave, a whisper beneath the layers of enchantment. It was a difficult sensation to discern, almost drowned out by the forest’s illusions, but it was there. He turned Roan, deliberately *away* from the ribbons, and pushed deeper into the woods, guided not by sight, but by instinct. The silence seemed to deepen, but now, Kaelen felt a strange sense of purpose. He wasn’t simply being tested; he was actively resisting the forest’s will. And he would not fail.

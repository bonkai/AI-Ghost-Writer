The forest floor was a tapestry of damp leaves and gnarled roots, swallowing the sound of Sir Kaelen’s boots with each step. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting shifting patterns on the moss-covered trees. It wasn't the darkness that unnerved him, but the *quiet*. An unnatural stillness had fallen over the woods since he’d crossed the Whisperwind stream, a silence that pressed against his ears like a physical weight.

He adjusted the weight of the ancestral blade, Dawnbringer, on his hip. The steel felt cold, almost reluctant. Old Man Hemlock, the village sage, had warned him about the Silkenwood – a forest steeped in ancient enchantments, where the very trees could mislead and deceive. “Trust not your eyes, Sir Knight,” the old man had rasped, “but your heart. And heed the whispers… they are rarely what they seem.”

Kaelen scanned the trees, his hand instinctively reaching for the hilt of Dawnbringer. He wasn’t looking *for* anything, precisely. Hemlock hadn’t specified *what* to look for, only to be wary. Then he saw it. A flash of crimson, caught between the branches of a towering oak. He spurred his horse, Roan, forward, pushing through a curtain of hanging vines.

It wasn't a bird, as he’d initially thought. It was a ribbon. A length of crimson silk, tied tightly around a branch, fluttering in the nonexistent breeze. Beneath it, etched into the bark, was a single word, carved with unsettling precision: *Lost*.

Kaelen dismounted, his gaze sweeping the surrounding trees. More ribbons. Crimson, emerald, sapphire – each tied to a branch, each bearing a single, haunting word: *Hope. Fear. Memory. Truth.* The forest was speaking, but in riddles.

He touched the crimson ribbon, a prickle of unease running down his spine. This wasn't a path; it was a labyrinth. A deliberate attempt to disorient. He remembered Hemlock’s warning. *Trust not your eyes.*

Closing his eyes, Kaelen focused on the feeling in his gut, the pull towards the dragon’s cave. It was faint, a whisper beneath the layers of enchantment, but it was there. He turned Roan, ignoring the ribbons, and pushed deeper into the woods, guided not by sight, but by instinct. The silence seemed to deepen, but now, Kaelen felt a strange sense of purpose. He was being tested. And he would not fail.

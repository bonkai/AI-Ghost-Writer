The forest floor was a tapestry of damp leaves and gnarled roots, swallowing the sound of Sir Kaelen’s boots with each step. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting shifting patterns on the moss-covered trees. It wasn't the darkness that unnerved him, but the *quiet* – a stillness that pressed against his ears, a physical weight threatening to suffocate his resolve. He tasted iron on his tongue, a phantom echo of battles fought and promises made.

He adjusted the weight of Dawnbringer on his hip, the steel cold against his leather gauntlet. Old Man Hemlock’s warning echoed in his mind – *“The Silkenwood preys on doubt, knight. It shows you what you fear most, what you long for, twisting it into a cage.”* Kaelen’s knuckles whitened as he gripped the hilt, forcing the image of Princess Aurelia, imprisoned and afraid, *away*. Each attempt felt like tearing flesh, a sharp, stinging pain behind his eyes. He blinked, fighting the encroaching vision.

He scanned the trees, hand instinctively reaching for Dawnbringer. He wasn’t looking *for* anything, precisely, but for a *change* – a disruption in the unnatural stillness. Then he saw it. A flash of crimson, caught between the branches of a towering oak. He spurred Roan forward, pushing through a curtain of hanging vines.

It wasn't a bird. It was a ribbon. A length of crimson silk, tied tightly around a branch, fluttering despite the lack of breeze. Beneath it, etched into the bark, was a single word: *Lost*. 

Kaelen dismounted, his gaze sweeping the surrounding trees. More ribbons, a macabre garland woven through the woods. Crimson, emerald, sapphire – each tied to a branch, each bearing a single, haunting word: *Hope. Fear. Memory. Truth.* He recognized the pattern now – not random, but deliberately arranged, forming a winding, illusory path. The forest wasn’t merely showing him things; it was *offering* him a route.

As he touched the crimson ribbon, a prickle of unease ran down his spine, and a phantom scent – Aurelia’s lavender perfume – filled his nostrils. He instinctively began to follow the ribbon’s direction, his muscles already committing to the movement before his mind could protest. He forced his boot to stay planted, digging his heel into the soft earth. A tremor ran through his leg, a burning ache spreading upwards. He clenched his jaw, fighting the pull. *Hemlock’s warning.* Trust not your eyes… or your desires.

He noticed something else. The trees *along* the ribbon-marked path were subtly different – older, gnarled, their branches skeletal and reaching, as if desperate to ensnare him. The trees *off* the path, though still ancient, seemed healthier, their leaves a vibrant green, almost… protective. He realized the ribbons weren't simply a distraction; they marked a path the forest *wanted* him to take – a shortcut, perhaps, but one that would drain his strength, leaving him vulnerable to the dragon… or something far worse. The forest fed on despair, on lost hope. It didn't want him to *rescue* the princess; it wanted to add another lost soul to its collection.

Closing his eyes, Kaelen focused on the faint pull towards the dragon’s cave, a whisper beneath the layers of enchantment. It was a difficult sensation to discern, almost drowned out by the forest’s illusions, but it was there. He turned Roan, deliberately *away* from the ribbons, and pushed deeper into the woods, guided not by sight, but by instinct. The silence seemed to deepen, pressing in on him like a physical weight. A wave of dizziness washed over him, and he tasted blood in his mouth. But now, Kaelen felt a strange sense of purpose. He wasn’t simply being tested; he was actively resisting the forest’s will. And he *would* not fail. He wouldn’t give the forest another lost soul.

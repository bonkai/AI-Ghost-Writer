The forest floor was a tapestry of damp leaves and gnarled roots, swallowing the sound of Sir Kaelen’s boots with each step. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting shifting mosaics on the path ahead, though ‘path’ was generous. It was more a suggestion of a way through, frequently obscured by thorny bushes and fallen branches. He’d been riding for hours, leaving the King’s castle far behind. The initial thrill of the quest was fading, replaced by a gnawing unease. The forest *felt* wrong – unnaturally silent, devoid of birdsong, and the air hung heavy, like a shroud.

He stopped Roan at the edge of a small clearing. A twisted oak dominated the space, its branches reaching like skeletal fingers. Hanging from one of these branches was a cage, wrought of dark iron and covered in creeping vines. It was empty, but the air around it thrummed with a faint, unsettling energy. Kaelen dismounted, his hand instinctively resting on the pommel of his sword. He circled the cage, examining the intricate lock. It hadn’t been broken, nor forced. It had been *opened*. A shiver traced its way down his spine. This wasn’t a prison for holding, but for *releasing* something.

“What do you make of this, Roan?” he murmured, stroking the horse’s neck. Roan snorted, his ears pinned back, and pawed at the ground, refusing to settle. The horse, usually stoic, was visibly agitated, his gaze darting between the cage and the deepening shadows.

A glint of silver caught Kaelen’s eye. Half-buried in the leaves near the base of the tree was a single, tarnished coin. He picked it up, turning it over in his fingers. It wasn't of the kingdom’s mint. The symbol etched into the metal was unfamiliar - a coiled serpent devouring its own tail. He recognized the symbol from old bestiaries – a mark associated with shadow magic and…lost hope. A knot formed in his stomach. This wasn’t a random find. It was a warning.

A voice, thin and reedy, broke the silence. “Lost, knight?”

Kaelen spun around, his hand flying to his sword hilt. An old woman stood at the edge of the clearing, almost blending into the shadows. She was cloaked in grey, her face a roadmap of wrinkles, and her eyes…her eyes were the color of moss and held an unsettling knowingness. She hadn't simply *appeared*; he noticed now that the surrounding ferns seemed to bend *away* from her, as if repelled.

“Who are you?” Kaelen demanded, his voice sharp.

The woman chuckled, a dry, rustling sound. “Someone who knows this forest. Someone who knows what you seek. And someone who knows you’ll likely fail.” She paused, her gaze unwavering. “The dragon doesn’t hoard gold, knight. It doesn't need it. It collects what truly sustains it…despair. And this forest? It’s a breeding ground.” She gestured with a gnarled finger towards the cage. “Each empty cage…another soul willingly offered.”

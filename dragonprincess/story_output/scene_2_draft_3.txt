The forest floor yielded under Sir Kaelen’s boots, swallowing the sound with a damp sigh. Sunlight fractured through the canopy, painting shifting mosaics on the barely-there path – more a suggestion of passage than a true way through. He’d been riding for hours, the weight of the King’s trust pressing down with each stride. The initial surge of purpose had begun to fray, replaced by a prickling discomfort. The air tasted stale, metallic, and the silence wasn’t peaceful, but *attentive*. It felt as though unseen eyes tracked his every move.

He reined Roan to a halt at the edge of a small clearing. A twisted oak dominated the space, its branches clawing at the sky. Hanging from one of these branches was a cage, wrought of dark iron and strangled by creeping vines. It was empty, yet a faint tremor ran through the metal as if recently vacated. Kaelen dismounted, his hand instinctively finding the familiar grip of his sword. He circled the cage, examining the intricate lock. Not broken, not forced. *Opened*. He traced a finger along the latch, a cold dread creeping up his spine. This wasn’t a prison meant to hold, but to *release*. 

Roan snorted, his ears pinned back, and pawed at the ground, refusing to settle. The horse, usually stoic, trembled, his gaze darting between the cage and the deepening shadows at the edge of the clearing. Kaelen stroked Roan’s neck, trying to soothe him, but the horse’s agitation mirrored his own growing unease.

A glint of silver caught Kaelen’s eye. Half-buried in the leaves near the base of the tree was a single, tarnished coin. He picked it up, turning it over in his fingers. It wasn't of the kingdom’s mint. The symbol etched into the metal – a coiled serpent devouring its own tail – sent a chill through him. He remembered the symbol from dusty bestiaries – a mark associated with shadow magic, and the draining of hope. A knot tightened in his stomach. This wasn’t a random find. It was a deliberate marker.

A voice, thin and reedy, broke the silence. “Lost, knight?”

Kaelen spun around, his hand flying to his sword hilt. An old woman stood at the edge of the clearing, almost woven into the shadows. She was cloaked in grey, her face a roadmap of wrinkles, and her eyes… the color of moss, they held an unsettling knowingness. He noticed now that the ferns around her seemed to recoil, their fronds curling inwards as if burned. 

“Who are you?” Kaelen demanded, his voice sharp.

The woman chuckled, a dry, rustling sound like autumn leaves skittering across stone. “Someone who knows this forest. Someone who knows what you seek. And someone who knows you’ll likely fail.” She paused, her gaze unwavering. “The dragon doesn’t hoard gold, knight. It doesn't need it. It collects what truly sustains it…despair.” She gestured with a gnarled finger towards the cage. “Each empty cage…another soul willingly offered. They come seeking solace, a release from their burdens. And the dragon…it *provides*.” She leaned closer, her voice dropping to a whisper. “But the price…is everything.”
